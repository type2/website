In aircraft, they have what are called "annunciator lights" that come
on when various aircraft parameters (exotic things like "oil pressure"
and such) are out of the safe range.  These let the pilot know that
things are amiss before things go completely haywire.

Pilots don't call them "idiot lights" and we shouldn't either.  You
are far more likely to notice a lit warning light that isn't normally
lit than a thin gauge needle that is not where it normally is.  Some
folks even wire a buzzer into the circuit, so the light is accompanied
by a audable alert.

Guages are good for diagnosis, warning lights are good for warnings. 
There's no reason why you shouldn't have both on your vehicle.
