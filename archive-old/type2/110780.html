Subject: Re: [T2] Dynamic vs. Static Timing Adjustment

On Fri, May 23, 2003 at 12:36:04PM -0700, El Senor Capitan wrote:
> A buddy of mine told me that the best way adjust timing on a Volks is to do a
> static timing adjustment, because the dist is already advancing even at idle
> speed, so I gave it a try.

The 009 is mechanical only so it relies on engine speed to advance the
timing. The best method to time a 009 is by making sure your total
advance is in the 28-32 degree range. That will help you keep from
cooking your engine. Trying to set it from a static point will not
always tell you what this max advance is. For instance what if someone
has gone in & monkeyed w/ the distributor and the advance is much
greater than a stock one suddently you set it at 7.5 degrees & it
advances to over 40 degrees. That much advance really can't be good
for a VW engine.

But I will say this. since a 009 is mechanical only & doesn't rely on
vacuum signal it can be static timed if the real total advance is
known. But like I said 009s are supposed to be timed at total advance.

> When I check this dynamically with the timing light, it reads about 9 deg
