Subject: '62 clutch weirdness

And the MOT Saga [sic] continues, each installment more thrilling
than the next...

My clutch never behaved itself since I bought my bus. The cable
was on max, but it felt like it wasn't disengaging fully. So I put a
new cable in there, but still had to wind it up to max adjustment. It 
was a big improvement, but I still had to floor the pedal for a smooth
shift.

Since I had the engine out last weekend I decided to put a new 
clutch plate (friction plate) in there, see if that improved things.

Well, this evening after getting the brake lights to work (a frayed
wire up front), I tried moving the bus out of the garage. With the
engine was running, I could only shift into reverse, and then with
a horrible noise. I lifted my clutch-foot and rolled back. When I
pushed the pedal down it disengaged OK (i.e. I stopped moving).
Equally, if I put it in gear *before* starting the engine, everything
was OK, although it's not too happy about coming out of gear
with the engine running.

My guess: either
a) I've got the wrong cable
b) The pressure plate is worn (I don't fully understand the latter -
this is a hypothesis of my boyfriend's)

BUT  I am mystified and stupified at why everything is *worse* with the
new clutch plate than with the old (which, incidentally, wasn't worn
much).

AAAGGHHH. Help?
