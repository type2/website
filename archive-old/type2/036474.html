In answer to your question; no, it's not supposed to
come off easily (I wish) and unless you are lucky
enough to have the VW shop-tool to pull it, I wouldn't
waste time/money trying to find a puller that will
work (I've never seen one that will). I suppose you
could fab one, but it'd be some work.

The only way I've been able to get mine off was to
*loosen* the nut, sit in the driver's seat, and pull
upward on the wheel with all your might while a friend
smacks the holy-hell out of the steering rod (hit it
on the end only) with a 5lb sledge-hammer!

Remember...don't pull the nut off all of the way, or
you may lose a few teeth when the steering wheel
finally comes flying off into your face;-) Also,
pulling the nut *after* will clean up any threads that
were bunged-up by the hammer. Also, make sure to stuff
the horn wire down into the steering shaft, or it'll
get smooshed. 

I was shown this method by a bonafied VW mechanic who
didn't have, nor had he ever seen, the official VW
wheel-removal tool! It's scary-as-hell the first time
(at least if you want to keep the steering wheel),
'cause that wheel will *FLEX* like a mother before it
finally pops off!!!

Have fun ;-)
