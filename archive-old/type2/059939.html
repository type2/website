(Since this was posted to the list, I'll offer my two cents as well...)  
The easiest way to troubleshoot is to disconnect the servo hose at 
the engine and cap the vacuum port.  If the engine runs 
better/different then you've got a leak somewhere, most likely at 
the servo, though it could be the hose or (unlikely) the metal tube.  
Another symptom of a bad servo is dying/rough running when  
pushing down on the pedal while stopped at idle.  As for the one 
way valve, if you can blow through it one way and not the other, it's 
okay.  (It should be installed so the air flows toward the engine, of 
course.)

Hope this helps!
