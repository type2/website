all I can say right now is we run a very tight deck, about .030". The piston
shape is designed to create a very nice (detonation proof) chamber, with
lots of swirl. I'll also add two things.

1) the mixture is confined to a very small space, which reduces the amount
of heat transfer from the head to the A/F mixture (less surface area).

2) The instantaeous load on the engine (cylinder pressure) is LOWER than
with a conventional engine, just extended thru a much longer period of time,
for more push on the crank and at better crank/rod angles. More power on
less air/fuel. since more work is done, there is less leftover heat to deal
with, and the engine runs cooler.
