Man.  I deleted this message because I did not want to remember my
experiences with exhaust bolts but I felt like you might need the
forewarning.  I bought an Empi header and upon removing the old, snapped the
exhaust manifold stud.  I thought that was no big deal and would be a great
opportunity to replace the rusty studs.  Well, all the old studs came out
just fine with a small pipe wrench ($20 from Home Depot) except the last one
snapped flush with the head surface.  I bought an expensive drill bit and an
easy out and began to drill away.  The drill bit snapped, yes children,
that's right....flush with the stud and flush with the head.  I tried to
drill around it....no luck  Damn!!!  It was machine shop time.  I spent
hours, mind you attempting to take this one tiny bastard stud out.  When I
got the heads off to take to the machine shop after yanking the motor, I
found that it was cracked and needed to be replaced anyway.  This is what I
refer to as "Justin luck".

Justin
