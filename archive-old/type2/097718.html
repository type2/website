Speis Hecker is excellent paint--BUT--be sure to over reduce it a bit--

Otherwise it has a tendancy to bubble all over---don't ask why--it's
just the nature of the beast.

Two of my buddies bought a cab over Peterbuilt--and they were going to
paint it-- they got 3 gallons of Black Speis for zip--I advise against
it because of the inherent problems with it---they went and layed it on
heavy anyway---
I got another phone call----"what now?"

"Let it set up, sand it all down with 320 and lay two light coats on it
again"---it bubbled again---

Another phone call--"now what"

Ahh--sand it all down--lay a light coat of Vari Prime on it, scuff it
down and go get a gallon of Deltron--and lay a couple of coats of that
on her"

No bubbles.

The trucks payload must have dropped considerably just because of the
weight of the paint on it--and mind you this is a CAB OVER semi I'm
talking about--like aptment # 9--these guys must have liked the absue
big time.

And this is not the first time I have heard horror stories anout Speis
Hecker--

Good long lasting paint--but hell to apply.

I prefer PPG Concept-- a blind, crippled, and crazy man, smoking pot
could get a deep, smooth, long lasting, finish for half the price, and
half the effort.

And if I was painting a car--I would use base / clearcoat---deeper
finish--longer lasting protection against the UV evil stuff--
