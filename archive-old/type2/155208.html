Subject: Clutch Pedal Doesn't Return

You could be dealing with a number of problems, but pulling the clutch cable is 
a good place to start.  Since the pedal won't return without your assistance, 
you can check for binding/corrosion in the clutch pedal axis when you remove 
the cable.  You may also notice something about the cable such as fraying... 
but then you may choose to pull the engine and look for problems in the clutch 
release shaft axis or the clutch cover springing.  If one of the release forks 
has come un-welded, you would have different symptoms; but when you are 
underneath removing the clutch cable, why not soak the release bearing axis 
with plenty of penetrating oil.  Good luck!
