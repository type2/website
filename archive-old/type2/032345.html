-->  Yes, but this would also be symptomatic of a loose Temp. Sensor
2...it's high resistance when the engine's cold, low when it's hot.  When
it rattles around, it intermittently looks like a high resistance, causing
the engine to go full rich momentarily and miss.  This drove me nuts for a
while, since on the FI engine I had the P.O. had stripped the threads the
sensor screws into.  (see below)

> 
> A couple of questions - if there is an erratic problem like the
> grounding of the #2 temp sensor, will I be able to spot it when the
> problem is not actually happening? 

-->  Maybe not, but all you need to do is put a wrench on it and see if
it's snug.  Don't tighten it too hard -- those threads are aluminum and
strip easy.  Just "snug and a tug".  While you're at it, you can check the
resistance with an ohmmeter and make sure it's not defective.  They do go
bad on occasion, and it's a cheap part...$15, last I needed one.
