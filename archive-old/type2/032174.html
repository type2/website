--> The torque *probably* isn't critical, but be careful.  On my '77
engine (where the sensor is in a little different location, so you can get
at it with a normal box-end wrench) someone over-tightened this and
stripped the threads in the head.  This caused all sorts of fun problems
with the sensor working itself loose and making the engine miss, which I
tried various ways of preventing until the rest of the FI died from
unrelated causes, making the point moot. ;) 

When you put the thing in, make it just snug, so it won't work loose.
Don't overtighten it.  No lock-tite, either, it has to make electrical
contact.  I wouldn't remove it at all unless you have to, but I'm assuming
you wouldn't be trying it it hadn't shorted out or something.
