George:
I agree heartily. If Jack's flywheel came off, either it wasn't torqued
enough or the dowels or holes are damaged. I'd guess they certainly are
by now.
I've built a few 356 motors that were in the 110 horspower range and Ray
Litz, one of the old-time Porsche machining authorities published his
opinions about gland nuts. He recommended pitching the spring washer and
using a machined heavy duty washer and removing an equivilent amount
>from the underside of the bolt. He made his own chrome- moly gland nuts
and recommended torquing them to fearsome specs: seems like 350-400 ft
lbs range. (VW says 217 ft lbs) I'd err on the high side and use lube on
the threads!
