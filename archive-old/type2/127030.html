I'm trying to get a recently purchased SVDA to run correctly. I am
installing into a 1972 VW bus engine (type4) with a Weber 32/36 carb.
The SVDA is replacing a 009. Aircooled_dot_net installed the Compufire
electronic ignition, and I've installed a new blue Bosch coil (also
from Aircooled). I have a Sprint aircleaner pulling exclusively from
the pre-heat system, so there is warm air going to the carb.
 
After installing, the engine will fire, but runs extremely rough, and
without much power. I've tried fiddling with the carb according to the
included documentation, but I'm not getting very far. 
 
I thought I might have the timing off. According to the Idiot's book

it ran funny (sounded real quiet, not too responsive, stalled out). A

be at 7.5 degrees BTDC. I reset to 7.5BTDC and started adjusting the
carb as the docs indicated would be necessary. Neither setting ran
well, but I didn't try tuning the carb at 5ATDC. Which setting is
correct for static timing?

carb. Carb adjustments seem to be making matters worse anyway. The
symptoms I'm seeing: 
1- hard to maintain idle at cold startup
2- gasping, gagging when pedal pressed under a load
3- no power at any point in the RPM cycle
4- occasional backfire (too lean?), some misfires
