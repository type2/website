well I drove a bus for many years (not a VW, but Greyhound type) and I
disagree with the prochedures below (but YMMV) 

first thing we were taught is to not turn around, learn to trust your
mirrors.  

grab the bottom of your steering wheel (not the top) look forward and
drive back using just your outside rearview mirrors, (make sure they are
adjusted properly, you should just be able to see your rear bumper in the
bottom corner.)  A blind spot mirror on the near side (passenger side)
mirror is an huge help.

remember you are driving from what would normally be the front bumper of a
sedan or type 3

DON'T hang out the door, what happens if you fall out (don't forget your
seatbelt) your nice new van drives away without you.

also turning around and looking out the rear window is a waste of time and
confuses your spatial orientation from looking in the mirrors.  you can
get away with it in a sedan but not a bus.  

in a bus the interior rearview mirror is for keeping an eye on the kids in
the back, not looking out of the bus. and the rear window is for the light
to keep the back of the bus from being too dark to read in the rear seat.

the outside mirrors are your friends, learn to use em to the fullest
