I seem to think that they first referred to westy's as 
campmobiles around 1967. I know that my 1971 westy had a silver 
sticker with black lettering that said "campmobile" on the pass. 
side of the cold box. Basically I think the term "Campmobile was 
a term originated by VW and WEsty to make their camper conversion 
more "appealing" to the N. American audience. I don't know that 
many N. Americans would necessarily co-realte "Westfalia" with a 
"Camper", so VW and WEsty decided to add a more self-evident 
term. Oddly in the late 70's the term seems to have been chucked 
and then went back to calling them "Westfalia's". In 1979 they 
even took to putting the "Westfalia" name on the back of the pop 
top.
