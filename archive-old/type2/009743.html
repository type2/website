> George wrote:
> >The heat of vaporization provides a hysteresis loop that should allow

> >from hot things to cooler things, the opening outlet temperature has
> >to be a few degrees higher than the closing air outlet temperature.
> >If the air runs a bit warm, the thermostat opens a bit.  If the air
> >cools off a bit then the thermostat closes a bit.

> I don't understand why this has anything to do with it except that the

alcohol--I
> still maintain that it will be either open, closed, or in process of

> 
> In order for the thermostat to sustain a state of being half open, the

> doesn't seem very likely.
> 
> I was not able sustain a half-open bellows using a pot of water,
which is a
> much more controlled environment than the ass of a moving bus.

Two things are working against you in the pot of water.  

First, the heat capacity of water is large, meaning that there is a
large reservoir of heat to expand the bellows as soon as there is a
delta-T to move the heat.  Same thing works the other way as well.

Second, in the pot of water there isn't any mechanical friction to
retard the motion of the bellows.
