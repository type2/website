>Main consideration will be electricity, I'm assuming.
>Lights for sure, but I'd like to run a little TV/VCR
>combo to entertain peoples every now-and-again...and a
>blender sure wouldn't hurt. Century makes a nice
>little 'Electri-pac" that's only $99 but it's also
>only 300 watts. 

Go with 12V appliances wherever possible.  Truck stops have the 
best selection.  You should be okay with a second battery and 
driving every couple of days.  Get a deep cycle battery or if you 
can spare $100 get two 6V golf cart batteries in series which will 
handle deep discharging much better than the 12V RV/Marine variety.

>I was going to buy a little portable
>electric ac/dc generator...600 watts at about $189.
>Good call, or am I a fool? 

Unless you're parked way away from everyone, it will draw 
attention to you.  I'd avoid this route if I were you.

>What about if I simply
>bought a good size (I don't even know what that would
>be) deep cycle 12v RV battery and hooked up an
>inverter of an appropriate size which y'all suggest?

Again, use 12V appliances wherever possible as they are much more 
efficient than using 110V ones with an inverter.  With only one 
battery, I'd keep the inverter to no more than 300 watts and use 
it sparingly.

>This would be in addition to the installed battery. I
>could leave the whole thing outside, cover it and run
>a cord to the outside wall outlet of The Bus, huh? 

As long as you can keep it away from weather and theives.

>If I did this would it be worth gettin' a solar panel
>charger to limit the runs to a nearby buddy's house
>for a recharge?

Depending on how much you use the 2nd battery, probably not.  You 
would have to spend several hundred dollars to get a panel big 
enough to do any good, and it would be cumbersome and prone to 
theft.  If you use the second battery only a couple of hours each 
evening to power a light bulb, you might break even with a $100 
solar panel.  Those little $20 panels that you set on your 
dashboard are nearly worthless as they only put out a few 
milliamps.

>I've used a Coleman Black Cat catalytic heater (about
>$70) in roomy boat cabins with good results. What
>about for The Bus, am I a dreamer? 

Most people will recommend against it.  Even if you crack the 
windows you will likely wake up with a headache due to oxygen 
deprivation.  You might not wake up at all.  Don't risk it - 
instead buy a good sleeping bag!  If you must use the catalytic 
heater, do so only when you are awake and keep the windows cracked.

>Also, what about an
>electric space heater plugged into whatever electric
>system I end up with.

Forget it - won't work.  They draw way too much power.  You can 
only use them if you can plug in to wall power somewhere.  Rule of 
thumb - nothing that makes significant heat can run very long off 
of 12V.
