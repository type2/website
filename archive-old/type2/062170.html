John Jensen wrote:
> 
> I bought a 2000 type iv engine out of an '82 vanagon at the junk yard this week
> and stripped it down for parts.  I got most of what I wanted.  In the process of
> breaking it down, I noticed that the tops of the pistons and parts of the heads
> were badly damaged.  Pitted is actually a gross understatement.  In fact it
> looked like someone had been at them with a hammer and chisel.  The plugs looked
> OK...maybe a little on the black side.  So any one know what caused this to
> happen. What ever it is I sure don't want that to happen to my engine.
> 
> I noticed that there were a lot of loose and broken hoses and vacuum lines in
> the Fuel Injection System, Which may have some bearing on the state of the
> pistons and heads
> 
> Anyone have any Ideas or have you seen this before????    John Jensen in
> Annapolis.

At a first guess, it sounds like detonation. If there are vaccuum leaks, 
this may have caused an excessive amount of air to enter. A lean mixture 
can cause these symptoms, as well as running too low an octane of fuel, 
timing advanced too far, etc.

This is assuming, of course, that it's not just corrosion. I have seen a 
couple engines left in the weather. Water standing on the heads caused 
them to pit. If this were the case, you'd probably see water in the oil 
and other signs of corrosion.
