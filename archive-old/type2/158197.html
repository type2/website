Yes, you can, but you don't need to (probably). I have
replaced a dozen elbows and never needed any of the
others. The elbow is rubber and gets stretched every
time you stick a nozzle in there. The other hoses are
reinforced plus they never get stressed.

If you decide to go ahead anyway, don't forget the two
screws at the outboard ends that come up from
underneath (one on each end.) It also helps if you
have elbows that bend the wrong way and you only weigh
about 40 pounds! (Kinda hard access) -BaH
