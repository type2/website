Hi Randall,

I respect your experiece. This is mine. 

When I picked up my new (first owner) bus with just 
26km on the clock, I drove it 200 miles on main roads 
at about 50mph to my first stop off point. Directly on 
stopping I applied the 'dip stick test' and yes I could 
hold it; but I wouldn't have liked it had it been much 
hotter. I can still apply the dip skick test without burning 
my hand.

Off couse if I lived somewhere hotter (eg South USA, 
Mexico, Brazil...) rather than the temperate UK my 
experience may be different.

Over advanced ignition, over lean mixture, improper 
choking, inadequate manifold preheat, worn AFM electric 
tracks (amongst other things) can all cause the van to 
run poorly when cold and over hot after warm up.

Yes I do have a completely different FI system to 
anyone else on this list, but my van now with 25000km 
runs smashing straight from cold. Just as my previous 
bus (1600, 34Pict3, 009) did.

Good luck
