Subject: Re: using ether in air cleaner to start bus

We did this in a 75 bus that did not have hoses to airbox clamped.  It 
backfired and blew the afm flapper in half.  Can't say now if she had it 
floored after I yelled "Try it!"  I feel using starter fluid just proves 
your ignition works, and if it runs a few seconds, proves 
valves/timing/compression is adequate.  Better to check for spark to 
test ignition, verify fuel pressure, and verify injectors get current.
If you must use it, it's safer to squirt it down a hose to the airbox 
AFTER the throttle plate (and don't floor it to start!).
