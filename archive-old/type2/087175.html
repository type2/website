Billy,

The rear rod goes thru a "tunnel"(a pipe).  The 2 bellows are on each end 
of the pipe and seal the tunnel from the elements while the rod goes in at 
the front and out in the rear.

This tunnel/pipe is what the bushings ride inside of to help keep the 
rod centered in the tunnel.

Front and rear couple together in the front and then the rear goes 
straight thru to the rear tranny coupler.

If you are careful when you pull the rear shift rod out, you can probably 
save the bushings for inspection....and *then* decide whether to replace 
them or not....
