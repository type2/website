hydro-electricity doesn't pollute in a traditional way, but the noise, the
disruption of the ecosystem, and the materials needed to build it do
pollute.  there is also a lot of pollution from inside (the lubrication of
the turbines, and the hydraulics associated with making it all work).  i
studied hydrology in college and researched dams. alot of times they aren't
as great as we think.  they are better in alot of ways than traditional
electricity plants.  i am also a pessimist and tend to think that nothing is
really good enough, except for my buses!!

It depends on the part of the country you're in. Many of the folks in the
Northwest, as long as they're not shipping it all off to California, get all
their electricity from hydroelectric generators--dams. This doesn't pollute
at all; the dam either has a pipe coming out the bottom of it or flows over
a roller, the water turns turbines, the turbines turn alternators, the
alternators turn out enough electricity to keep you going. Some places have
big windmills, and they work fairly well too.
