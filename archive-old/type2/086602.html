The brakes on a 71 are essentially the same ones on a 73. Both use disc up
front and drum in the rear. They may have used different calipers in the
front, but they are not significantly different. There must have been
something definately wrong with the 71 you drove. I had a 71 that stopped
on a dime and gave me nine cents change. My 78 stops equally as well.
Collapsed brake hoses, leaking wheel cylinders, frozen or sticking
calipers, worn out un adjusted rear shoes, vacuum assist or booster
problems, the list goes on but the bottom line is that the brakes on a 71
are just as poweful as a 78. Now engines are another story. 73 has pancake
motor with more hp and a diferent geared tranny. Many other differences but
those 2 make the  biggest difference.

Hello Everyone,

I own and drive daily a 73 bus that I purchased last July.  This is my
first
bus although I've driven bugs for the last 10 years.  On Saturday, I test
drove a 71 camper as I want to do some camping this summer.  I was

I guess I'm really just curious as to how a 73 should compare to a 71 when
