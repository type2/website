Your bus originally came with a dual-vacuum distributor designed to be set
at 5 degrees ATDC, and the pulley notch (if it is the original pulley)
reflects 5 ATDC.  To the right of the notch and on the opposit (rear) side
of the pulley) is an oval indentation with the TDC mark.

The 009 distributor is designed for a static timing of 7.5 degrees BTDC,
which means that neither of the existing marks is correct.

Measure from the existing notch along the curve of the pulley to the point
opposite the TDC mark.  multiply this figure buy 2.5 and measure clockwise
from the existing 5 ATDC notch.  this will be the 7.5BTDC mark.  Make a
mark here on the pulley with a file.

This will be your static timing mark.
