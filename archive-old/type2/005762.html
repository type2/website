> I spent today stripping the engine of all the sheet metal etc. I noticed
> the difference in the pressure plate and fly wheel. Can I use the 78
> flywheel or should I use the flywheel off the engine that came with the
> engine? I assume the flywheel that came with the bus is a 1975 unit
> because of the size of the clutch disk (215mm). Both the 75 and 78 units
> are in good shape. Does either assembly have advantages over the other?
> I hope to avoid problems with the starter gear engaging the flywheel.

The 78 228mm flywheel uses a different starter. The teeth on the pinion
stick out 1/2" further. Looking at both starters: the 1.7 [210mm flywheel]
and 1.8 [215mm flywheel] teeth start at the drive sprague. On the 2.0
starter drive, there is a 1/2" blank space between the teeth and the
sprague. You cannot use the self-supporting auto trans starter on a 2.0L
because of this.
