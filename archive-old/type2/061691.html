Yep, and like another listmember mentioned, it's terrible to add that kind
of weight to the roof of a bus, not to mention the clearance problems.  I

>
>I would be very interested in hearing how your friend installed ac into
>his '70 westy.

He didn't do it himself, but basically there was an under dash
evaporator/blower, hoses routed underneath the bus to a mid-chassis mounted
condensor with a forward scoop to pick up air (also a fan for idle
conditions), and a rear evap/blower unit where the shelf over the bed in a
Westy would be.  Very slick if you have back seat passengers.  The
compressor is the standard Sankyo rotary, a very good compressor from what
I've seen.  Mounted opposite the generator so the (longer) fan belt makes a
triangle.  Don't remember how the compressor was mounted.  He has a big
motor (1835?) in it so he can handle the extra drag.  Cooling?  Dunno, he
hasn't had any problems though.  He installed it after most of the hot
weather was over this year - we'll see how it holds up next year.  Same for
mine for that matter.  I've gotta get out there and take some pictures of
all of this.
