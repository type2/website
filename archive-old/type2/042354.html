Sorry, George, but I am very much with Sue on this one.  There are a lot
of nuts and bolts on a VW with a torque spec of "moderate hork" and a
couple with a torque spec of "mondo hork" (big rear axle nut), but there
are also many that demand a torque wrench.  Clutch bolts are definitely
among these.  More importantly, *spark plugs* are high on the list of
"bolts" with a very demanding torque spec (first reply that mentions
horking the plugs into an engine with a cast-iron head gets a wet carrot
up side the head!) , and once you have the wrench for them, you ought to
use it for everything else as well :-)

I have a Sears click-style torque wrench and I use it a *lot*.  Never
regretted doing so, either!  Yes, they are pricy (~USD60-70) but compare
it to the effort of pulling a T4 engine *again* to replace the warped
pressure plate ($130 from Bus Depot, last I checked) and it begins to look
cheap.  Comare it to pulling the heads to helicoil a stripped spark plug
hole and it is a pittance.
