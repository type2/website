I'll add a couple of comments here. First of all, when checking the engine
code, always look at the top of the block, not on the fan shroud.  The
engine code is stamped on both. But since fan shrouds are interchangeable
between years, all that you can tell if you look at the stamping on the
shroud is the year of the _fan shroud_, not the engine.  More than once I
have seen someone replace their engine with one of a different year, but use
the shroud from their original engine.  The code on the top of the block,
near the oil breather, is the place to look.

Also, looking at the heat exchanger flange will very likely, but not
definately, determine the year of the engine.  If the heat exchanger has a
triangular flange to the exhaust, then it is indeed a '75-78 heat exchanger,
hence a '75-78 engine. But if it has a trapezoidal flange, it could be a '79
heat exchanger (likely), or a '72-74 one (less likely, but possible as they
will fit onto '75-78 heads).
