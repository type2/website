I too am interested in the metrology of what my engine is doing, I just lack 
the resources to fully instrument with calibrated sensors/readouts etc.  I 
don't have head temp gauges though I have thought about it.

There is a difference between oil temp and case temp.  The sump plate is 
insulated by the 2 paper gaskets but connected by the sump plate studs/nuts. 
  There is a correlation, but loose. Also, the gauge will reflect 
temperature changed more rapidly than the case temp should change.  If case 
temp cools 20 degrees as quickly as I see oil temp drops I'd worry anout the 
clearances due to the different coefficients of expansion of the materials 
in the engine.

I have noticed between my 2 busses that the oil will cool faster than the 
case.  Case as solid mass holds the heat longer and the mass of the case 
exceeds the mass of the oil.  The oil running through the cooler then the 
oil gallery and heads etc cools the case.  The oil temp per gauge was, for 
example 195 wheras the infrared thingy measured the case temp by the 
distributor at 205.  It is indeed possinble the disparity could be the temp 
sending unit, but the case remains warm long after the oil's cooled.  I can 
grab the dipstick long before I can hold my hand against the case.
