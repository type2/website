On Sat, 6 Dec 2003, Sage Green wrote:

> For the manifold, I have   (1) four metal sandwich gaskets (with heat shield
> material in the sandwich, full donut rush);   (2)  two 'Lone Ranger mask'
> black 3/8" spacers;  (3)  four thin metal crush gaskets (thin metal, no
> filler, but 1/2 donut crush)

For dual carburetors you should use the thin metal gaskets, either #1 or
#3 on your list depending on whether you have a 1700 or 1800cc engine.
The diameter of the interior holes on these gaskets should differ to match
the different intake holes on the heads.

The "Lone Ranger mask" bakelite gaskets are for fuel-injected type 4
engines.  Do not use these on a dual-carb engine.
