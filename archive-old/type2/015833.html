Hello Tristan,

	If you own an old vacuum cleaner, the kind that can be reversed so it
blows instead of sucking you are lucky. During the welding let the cleaner
blow into the drained tank to vent all vapours out. The Shade three
mechanic I use for complicated welding jobs do this all the time, even
welding on the tanks themselves. He got one of those ball shaped Hoovers
>from the sixties......He does car painting with it too, yes he got a paint
gun made by Hoover for that. If you have a large air compressor you can use
it instead. It's the amount of air that does it not the pressure!

	Another way is to fill the tank with water but you can never be sure you
get all the water out afterwards.  

And Tristan, keep a fire extinquisher handy!
