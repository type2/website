It IS a pressed-in plug. It's in the end of the main oil gallery.  There's
another one just like it behind the flywheel.  The one behind the flywheel is
the one that typically pops out at around 150k miles.  If your going to take
out the motor, do both of them.  If your anal about it do all of them! 

To fix it so it never leaks again, you need to remove it, (drill a small hole
in it, insert a sheet metal screw and pull it out) tap the hole and insert a
pipe plug well slathered with loc-tite 571.  If interference with the fan
housing might be a problem, you'll need to either find a flush fitting plug or
make the pipe plug flush fitting.

Welding IS NOT the solution!!!  JB Weld IS NOT the solution!!!

Another thing to check while the fan housing is off is the condition of the
rear motor mounts.  If at all questionable, replace them now.  The fan housing
HAS to come off to get to them. Then NEVER jack the bus up by the motor! It
tears them every time.
