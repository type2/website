Thanks for the quick replies everyone!!
Jim's reply really got me thinking.  Since it is only a little out of 
spec and the shims do wear, this sounds quite logical, and 
wallet-friendly even!
I pulled the 3 shims from my engine ( H-case) to measure them.  I came 
up with .0133, .0134, and .0125 to give a total of .0392" of shims.  
With my end play being off .0075 this would mean that my total end play 
would be .0467".  What is the shim-less end play spec?  Does anyone know 
it or was it ever even published?  Bentley just says to put 2 shims in, 
measure, and then add an appropriate 3rd shim to bring it within spec.  
Shims are available in .0134" and .0126" so two of my shims are only 
worn .0001" each.  That is if they were in that size when NEW.  These 
would have 50,000 miles on them, but I don't know how much they wear.  
They have oil on them constantly so I would assume very very little. 

This still leaves my end play out of spec, I think.  I wish I knew what 
the shimless end play tolerances were.

So, if I put in 3 shims of .0142 (.36mm) that would bring my shimmed end 
play to .0041.  That is in the dead center of the Bentley spec.  I think 
that is the route that I will go.  3 bucks for 3 shims which could mean 
another 10-20K or more (depending on how I drive it) out of this engine, 
why not?
