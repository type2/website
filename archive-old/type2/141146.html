Another $00.02 worth!
    3M yellow  weather-striping glue is by far the way to go. IMHO

 Following these rules:
1) Clean, clean, and clean again the valve cover and head.
2) Valve covers straight
3) A tight bail.
4) Good quality cork gasket

    When the valve covers and heads are clean and dry. Apply a thin bead of
yellow glue around the valve cover. "Pat" the gasket in and out of the glue
to aerate till it gets tacky. Set the gaskets in place and set valve covers
aside to dry (about the time it takes to adjust the valves will do). Rub a
very thin coat of oil, (not grease) on the head side of the gasket. This is
to allow the gasket/cover to "walk" a little and settle in place.
    Yes this a little more work to clean the glue the next time. But in over
30 years of VW maintenance I've never had one leak from the gasket "sucking
in". I've had leaks from a misplaced a valve cover from time to time, come
on guys, who hasn't.
    Last rule....... Do your valve adjustments/oil changes on time, make
sure your engine isn't over heating, and your gaskets will stay soft, and
most of all......seal, during that time. Its the two years 20,000 mile
services that cause the hard gaskets, leaks and extra work to clean the old
ones off.
