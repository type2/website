The shop was not going by sound, but by using a propane test.  When they
sprayed at the plenum itself, they could measure a noticeable change in
the engine.  The fishy part (and this is what has them stumped): the shop
says after they spray near the plenum, the see the engine react ~10-15
sec later.  I tell them: maybe the propane drifts to another location
during that time and gets sucked into the air system at another point.
The shop says: that's what you'd think, but we sprayed everywhere else
and see no change in the engine; the only other suspect area is near
throttle body bushings, but this was minor.

Today, the shop owner started musing about the valves/guides or something,
wondering if there is a leak in that area.  But, I just put AVP refurb
heads on...  Is this plausible?

> Is that 6% with a cat on it?

Yes, after the cat (but with the engine running cold, so the cat had not
heated up in order to work yet anyway).

> What is your timing set at, at idle?

Here's my experience: the timing can't be set properly since the vac is
out of whack.  The bus _barely_ idles to begin with, only after it has
been warmed up a bit.  The bus will run with the timing around 7BTDC but
as I turn the dizzy to go for 5ATDC, the engine stalls.

his theory.  Maybe I'll need a new plenum, maybe not.
