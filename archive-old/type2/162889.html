>  The engine seal had caught fire.

	This very important part is needlessly expensive compared 
with Armathane (Armstrong corp of Canada) closed-cell foam crammed in 
all around the gap.  This material is sold as exterior thermal 
insulation for hot-water pipes.
	The cooling system of these vehicles primarily depends on 
maximising the pressure surplus in the engine compartment so that the 
fan can pass the most air per s down past the cooling fins.  The 
gasket around the engine perimeter can develop large holes, degrading 
this precious pressure surplus.  Stopping leaks from that engine 
compartment is essential to good cooling of the engine.  But the 
official gasket is needlessly expensive compared with a yard or so of 
Armathane.
