bill anymore. And unless we all become proficient welders, metalurgists, and

remember... the parts people, Volkswagen of America, etc.. are not owned by
hippies. They're owned by business oriented investors.

Hi Folks,

I just had to jump in on this one.  Even though I'm not old enough to have 
been alive during the "heyday" of the hippies back in the late 60's, I 
remember the counterculture ideas were still very much alive in the early 
70's.  Though I never got the chance to enjoy those days firsthand from 
behind the wheel of a brand-new '67 Kombi, I grew up in a VW Beetle (a '74 
Std), and have owned various VW's over the years.  I like the idea of 
community, of grass-roots movements, and of being counter to the corporate 
and governmental greed and control.  I try to follow a pretty simple and 
earth-friendly lifestyle, and I really love my Bus.  Now that a new 
generation of Bus owners is coming to the fore, I think it's our 
responsibility to keep those ideas alive.  Perhaps if we keep pushing for 
more environmental, personal, and spiritual awareness, someday the greedy 
ones will figure it out.  I enjoy passing a plastic minivan with its plastic

occupants in my "original" minivan, complete with all of its 
user-serviceable parts.  I enjoy the smiles and waves from strangers, and I 
especially enjoy the love of the VW community.

Keep the faith, all.  Heck, we even have VWoA trying to get friendly with us

again...
