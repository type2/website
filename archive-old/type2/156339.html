Back to agreed value.  The value of your vehicle
is the value of your vehicle.  For sure, the
value of your damages is subjective and what that
means to the other guy, the other guy's insurer,
you, your insurer, or a jury could be many
different things.  But the value of your vehicle
is the value of your vehicle.  You will need
evidence to back up that value, either at the
time of the claim or when you are trying to pay
more for an agreed value.  

There is no reason to pay more for agreed value. 
Either your vehicle is worth a certain amount or
it is not.  EVEN if you have an agreed value
policy of say $20,000 on a $2000 bus, YOU WILL
ONLY GET $2000.  Period.  I posted in the
archives about the concept of "insurable
interest".  Check it out if you have any
questions.  If you bus is worth $20000 and you
buy a normal policy, you will get the value of
the vehicle.  

Agreed value is the "whole life" of auto
insurance.  Insurers sell it, but no educated
consumer would buy it.  

Shark, the fact that you have an "agreed value"
vis-a-vis your insurer has nothing to do with
damages that a tortfeasor owes to you.
