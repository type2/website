GD is the correct engine for a 76-77 USA bus.
BUT if it is a USA bus, it should have fuel injection.

Where do you live?
Where did the bus come from, country wise..... we all know it originally came from Germany :)

When you say the engine number came from 'on the engine just infront of the distributor', this tells me you
are reading off the fan housing to the right of the ignition coil. WRONG place to read the correct engine
number. Fan housings get swapped all the time. The real engine number is stamped in the case, between the fan
housing and the square oil breather.

But keep in mind, all the engine number denotes is the identification of that case when it left the factory 30
years ago. What is in it now is anyones guess, without disassembly and inspection.

1976 is a strange year as far as the distributor goes. Up to build date 12/31/75 and vin # 2x6 2077 583, the
engine ignition and FI were essentially 1975 components. From build date 01/01/76 and vin # 2x6 2077 584, the
components are the same as 1977.

You will need identify your distributor by removing it and looking at the VW and Bosch numbers stamped in the
body.
