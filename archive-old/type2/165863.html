The air cleaner on the dual carb engine has thermostatic control box  
just before the air cleaner:

http://www.ratwell_dot_com/technical/VacuumHosesCarb/ControlBox.jpg

Its purpose is to let it in warm air to help the engine idle during  
the warmup period. This supply of warmed air comes from below the  
cylinder heads via preheat tin:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/AVP2/46-PreheatTube.jpg
http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/Parts72Bus1/02- 
PreheaterTin.jpg

The valve in the box is activated by a vacuum signal which comes from  
the brake booster line that feeds off the intake manifold where you  
found that 3-way rubber tee. The vacuum signal to the box is  
controlled by a temperature switch in the air intake for the right  
carb which cuts off the vacuum signal at the designated temperature:

1. http://www.ratwell_dot_com/technical/VacuumHosesCarb/RightCarb.jpg
2. http://www.ratwell_dot_com/technical/VacuumHosesCarb/AirHornRight.jpg
3. http://www.ratwell_dot_com/technical/VacuumHosesCarb/ControlBox.jpg

See my article for more photos of a dual carb setup:

http://www.ratwell_dot_com/technical/VacuumHosesCarb.html

last (76.5-79) FI configuration made that system obsolete.
