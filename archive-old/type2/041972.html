Any time we changed our gasket covers, we took off the old covers, put
some weatherstrip cement on the gasket and the cover, let it get tacky
and then put the gasket in the cover.  Don't put any cement on the part
that touches the engine though.  Then just put the cover back on and you
shouldn't have any problems.  The cement holds the gasket in place so
it's not slipping out of the cover when you're movin the cover around
trying to get the spring over the cover.  If you still have a leak, it's
probably because the spring that holds the cover on is loose and you'll
need to get a tighter one.
