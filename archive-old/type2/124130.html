No, that's the cooling fan for the engine and has no connection to the
heating system.  It pulls cool air in from the engine compartment; the air
circulates past the heads and cylinders and exits below the engine.  The
metal fan inside the plastic housing attached to the alternator is driven
"coordinated" with the cooling fan since there's a belt connecting the two.
As engine speed picks up the heating fan also speeds up, sending more warm
air up front.
