> The usual rule of thumb for 009 distributors on type IV motors is that they
> should achieve a maximum of 28 - 30 degrees advance at about 2800 RPM.  If
> you're seeing 34 degrees, you're a little past the safety zone and I'd
> recommend retarding it about 4 degrees.  It will give you a little poorer

     Not so -- that's more like what it should be for a T1 upright engine, not

rpm.  34 degrees is not even near enough, and not even remotely dangerous.
