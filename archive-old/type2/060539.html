Jack,

There is a connection between the bundle of wires and the high beam.
When you turn on your high beams, a contact is made in the switch and
the signal goes to the dimmer relay via the single brown and white wire
in that bundle of wires, this signal activates a switch in the relay for
your highbeams.  If you remove the 2 screws that hold the turn signal
switch to the steering column you should be able to pinpoint the
location of the short that is causing the erratic behaviour of your
lights.  The brown and white wire has become loose or is somehow frayed
and is likely shorting out. If you can't see any signs of obvious damage
then perhaps the wire has become loose where it joins to the switch or
at the point where the wire connects to the dimmer relay.
Check these connections and you should find the fault.
