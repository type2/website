Could be gummed up needle valve in the carb.  I don't know the history,
but if it sat for a while that is a definite possibility.  It happened
to me, and once the fuel pump got up enough pressure, which it wouldn't
do during cranking, it would force the needle valve open, filling the
float bowl, then it would work OK until it sat for a while, gumming up
again.  Cleaning out the carb did it for me.  

Also could be that the carb is emptying out for some reason, like too
hot or a leak around the drain plug on the left (FIF) side.  You might
never see a leak if it's slow and the gas evaporates as fast as it
leaks.
