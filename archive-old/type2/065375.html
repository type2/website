The following is my experience....

I have an early AD engine (1600 twin port) probably a year or two older than my 
van (1973) the timing from the Bentley/Haynes manuals should be 5 degrees ATDC 
(yes 5 ATDC).

When I had my van first I knew little/nothing about timing/tuning so I let my 
Dad, who said he knew what to do, put a vacume gauge on the input manifold and 
advance the ignition to maximize the vacume.... It will come as no suprise to 
most of you that my engine became "seriously cooked" after 50 miles of my next 
long trip.

I learnt four things -
    1.   Dont let my Dad change the timing :-)
    2.   I didn't have a foam seal
    3.   VW engines can be quickly rebuilt (2 heads, 4 pistons/barrels)
    4.   Advancing the ignition creates heat, retarding the ignition reduces
         the heat. Measured indirectly by an oil temperature gauge that I 
         later fitted.

Last year I changed to a 009 distributor. Muir says 7.5 degrees BTDC for 009 on
any engine. Given the range of settings with standard equipment (10BTDC to 
5ATDC) I decided to be cautious.

The 009 went in with the same dynamic timing as the old distributor. Knowning 
that if it was a bit 'flat' and sluggish I could advance it a bit.

How ever everything works fine. I have more power, so can hang on better on 
the hills. So my 009 remains at 5 degrees ATDC.

The unexpected added bonus is that my van now runs with the oil temperature 10 
degrees centigade cooler.

I cann't justify this by computations of the theoretical advance/retard 
position. But postings on this seem to confirm my original gut feeling that 7.5
degrees BTDC would not have been right for my engine.
