Subject: wood screws

Elmer's glue in the hole. Stick a tootpick in and
break it off flush. Screw in the wood screw and let
sit
for a while.
 Use the flat wood toothpicks. It partially fills the
hole and gives the screw something to bite into and
the
elmers will act like loctite on a metal to metal
screw.

If this fails, get some wood putty.. fill the hole
with the putty and then install the screw. Make sure
no stress is put on it untill the putty setup time has
passed.

Hardcore fix... 2 part epoxy in the hole and install
the screw... dont plan on getting it out though.
