Russell,

I believe that we are talking about a weight transfer thing here as
well.

Having all the weight transferred on the back wheels and tire's and
having the front en lite, can't be a safe situation, and most certainly
can't be good for the tires, or whatever suspension there is left---

I put Air shocks in the back of my 74 Westy, and the top studs bent,
almost as soon as I installed them----this heavy duty shock thing is
superseding the the intended suspension component's, the torsion bar's,
and I don't belive a shock was intended, or should be expected to carry
the whole load-- 

A shock should be used to supplement the springs, or in this case the
torsion bar's---

I had my bar's adjusted, and added new shocks all the way around----and
the bus handle's, and rides as new---gives me the option of loading up
the Bus to it's intended capacity, without dragging the back bumper on
the asphalt, and prevents me from performing coitus interuptus with my
headlamps at night, on the neighbor's bedroom window's---:>)

John, 
i asked this same question a while back. i think it depends on each bus
on a case-by-case basis. to determine if you really _need_ to do this, i
might suggest looking at tire wear. are the treads wearing evenly, etc.?
also, do your CV joints seem to be wearing out quickly, etc.? 
if the sag isn't causing apparent problems, then i would be inclined to
say you do not need to fix the sag.
