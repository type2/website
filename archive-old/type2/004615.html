Beth and Jon:

Was it running bettter before the tune up?  If so, take a few minutes and
look at every thing that you touched and/or replaced to see if it's in
correctly.  For example, a badly connected or disconnected spark plug wire
on one of the cylinders (ay the plug or at the dizzy) would cause the
problem you described.   It's more likely that something that  you worked on
is not quite right then some new problem just occured.  This is one of the
first things I was told by a buddy when I started to work on my first volks
back in college, and this advice has served me well for many years....  ;^)

There is quite a bit in the archives about finding vacuum leaks;  I'm
partial to the spraying carb cleaner or  WD-40 method;   however,  you need
to be careful to not overdo it because of the fire potential.

I'd suggest that you think  (once you get it running)  about replacing the
points with a petronix (sp?) ignition - definitly (IMHO)  worth the bucks -
never mess with those points again....

"Don't give up!"  If you've figured out how to use the e-mail program on
your computer, you can figure out  20+ year old car technology.  8^)
