On Tue, 4 Jul 2000, McComas_Glenn wrote:
> Hey Marcus --
> I second the recommendation on bench bleeding.  What this means:
> 1) Buy a length of brake line [say 12"-18"] with metric end fittings
> suitable for the master cylinder output ports, i.e., the same as your
> VW brake tube fittings.  Should be a buck or two at discount parts
> store.
> 2) Cut the new brake line in half.  Clean burrs/chips from the cut
> ends.
> 3) Bend each piece into a broad 'C' shape -- you want the cut end to
> be near the bottom of the reservoir and the fitting end in the output
> port.  Tighten the fittings.
> 4) Clamp this assembly on your bench [gently if you use a vise]; fill
> with clean/fresh brake fluid.  Then use a dowel to depress the piston.
> You will see bubbles of air rising in the reservoir.
> 5) Press and release repeatedly, until you see no more bubbles in the
> reservoir.  At this point your master cylinder is free of trapped air
> and ready to install.  Take out the 'C' lines and save them in a clean
> plastic bag in your tool cabinet!
