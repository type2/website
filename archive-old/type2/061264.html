Mike,

A very interesting point, however I believe it has a fatal flaw.  There is
much greater positive pressure generated in the exhaust system than the
vacuum (HE -> Blow -> Cabin), or the pressure (blow -> HE -> cabin)
generated by any of these fans, so I believe that in either case exhaust
gas that can enter the 'fresh air' system will do so.  Maybe there is some
truth to the argument you present, but I would guess it accounts for a
single digit percentage change when the engine is running at speed.

>                                  HEAT EXCHANGERS -> BLOWER(S) -> CABIN
> 
> Is there a reason for this?
> 
> I do not like this approach because the heat exchanger tin is not air tight. 
> Pulling air through the heat exchanger can also suck in fumes.  Pushing air
> through the heat exchangers reduces the changes/volume of fumes (blower->heat
> exchangers->cabin).  Maybe this is why Type 4 engines have blowers set up this
> way?
>                                  BLOWER -> HEAT EXCHANGERS -> CABIN
