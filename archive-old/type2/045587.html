On Thu, 7 Sep 2000, Mark Schantz wrote:

 > What gives w/the cloth "net" behind the metal lining of the heat 
exchangers?

Well... if it is what I think it is (the mat that sits between the outer
shell and the aluminum fins on the OEM VW heater boxes) then it is an
asbestos pad that VW installed back in the bad old days before asbestos
caused cancer.  It was installed to keep the heat from radiuating off the
box and improve the heat flow to the front.

I do not know (but doubt highly) if the Dansk replacement boxes have this
pad, or at least if it is still asbestos.

 >   I was considering trying to use some sort of cleaning solvent to wash 
out
 > the exchanger & eliminate any oily/exhaust particle buildup that might be
 > contributing to my smelly, smoky dilemma.  Would this backing (& 
baffling,
 > possibly insulative, I guess...) be harmed by rinsing the exchanger?

I would say leave it alone.  DO NOT clean it!  If it is intact and oil
soaked and hard as a rock, that is *GREAT*... it means that it can't shed
any fibers!  If it is nice and clean and fuzzy, then it can and will shed
fibers and they have a bad rep.  If you have removed the outer shell and
can remove this pad intact, I would do so. Replace it with a piece of the
hard high-temp fiberglass board.  Or just leave it out entirely.  The air
you get from the heating system will not be quite as hot, but it was never
very hot anyway :-)
