The tech issue aside, I'm with Glenn. I bought my first Vanagon ('80
aircooled) a few months ago and drove it 800 kilometres to my place, mostly
on freeways. I wanted to baby it a bit being an unknown, so I slowed down
for the hills and avoided putting the pedal to the floorboards to keep up.
Most of the time I did the speed limit or a few kPH below it, and I swear
sometimes it seemed like I was parked judging by the speeds of those passing
me -including tractor trailers. There is little doubt in my mind that people
are driving much faster these days and these old VW rigs are an anachronism
-designed for an era when going a few miles slower than max didn't mean
someone was going to drive through your rear window. The positive side is it
makes me drive slower, which by and large is a lot more peaceful and
relaxing. Like sailing, riding one of these rigs is about the journey not
the destination. It's great watching the tailights of the maniacs
disappearing over the hill ahead of me ;)

And recent research bears out what Glenn is saying -vehicles are much safer
than they used to be, but this improved engineering is almost being
nullified by people's overconfidence and taking more risks while driving.
Design a safer car and some people will push it until it is no longer safe.
Human nature.

Nathaniel
