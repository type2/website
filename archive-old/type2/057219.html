Any fuel pressure gauge that is to be read in the cab would have to be 
remote as you do not want pressurized fuel inside the cab. I didn't see a need 
for constant FP readings in the cab but it is very helpful in troubleshooting 
engine problems.  I installed a cheap ($6 _at_ sears) mechanical water gauge on the 
engine. It is connected to the fuel pressure test outlet on the left fuel 
manifold and attached to the air manifold with a large tie wrap which eliminates 
vibration. with the gauge installed on the engine, I have checked the fuel 
pump and pressure, fuel pressure relief valve,  and injector leak down time. 
Works for me!
