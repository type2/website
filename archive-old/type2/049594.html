They are "C" load rating, which is what is specked by VW.  That's 1435 lbs
per tire, which is enough for a vehicle with a GVW of 5,000 lbs.  A "D"
rating is around 1700 lbs per tire, and I'm not sure I know of any proper
fitting tires in the "D" rating, other than the Michelin Agelis 61, which
doesn't seem to be available this side of the Atlantic.
http://www.michelin_dot_com/us/eng/news/agilis/1.htm

Note also that Truck tires are loaded at 91% of actually carrying load,
whereas car tires are rated at 100% of carrying load.  So a "C" rated truck
tire, actually has 10% reserve, which is about another 140 lbs capacity.
Put another way, a truck tire (ie LT) at 1435 lbs capacity is equivelant to
a car tire at 1575, approx.
http://www.tirerack_dot_com/tires/tiretech/tirespecskey.html#maxload



Reinforced?  Yes, its a 6 ply.  Passenger car tires are 3 or sometime ply.

Basically the same specs as either:

The Michelin LTX M/S
http://www.michelin_dot_com/us/eng/tire/catalog/ltxms.htm

or the Uniroyal Laredo:
