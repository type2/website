wiggling is the trick, and you may have to use some pliers to pinch them a little,
but they can be repaired if you find a socket that fits thru a good one, and tap it
thru the bent one with a ratchet extension.

 I'd rather not have to go out and purchase a special
>tool for this either. I'd like to get this job done so I can enjoy the rest
>of the weekend!

 there is no special tool for this job. use  small vise-grip pliers with curved jaws,
only tight enough to grip, twist, and pull . use a light touch of grease ( not sealant)
to lubricate the new seals. use viton seals ( wierd color of which i cannot even name )
they will leak a lot less for a lot longer.....
