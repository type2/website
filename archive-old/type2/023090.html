I was somewhat over served last night so I crashed at my cousin's house as 
opposed to becoming DWI ticket fodder.

This morning, when I fired Casper up to go home, he started immediately, 
just as he always does, hardly even letting me hear the sound of the 
starter motor.
Then, after a few seconds, Casper sort of died down. I figured it was just 
the early morning cold.

Upon re-start, there was a loud backfire, but Casper again fired right up.
Backfire ?? Hmmm ?? Why ?? Oh well !

accelerating up to full speed. Casper would do full speed, but the 
acceleration curve was long and drawn out.

saw that the "plug" in my carburetor was missing. You know, that metal disc 
that seems to be on there for whatever reason, held in by whatever holds it.
I can actually look inside my carburetor and see the innards.

OK, so how does one go about getting a replacement plug, and how is same 
held in place in that hole ?
To fill this hole in my carburetor, do I need to buy a whole new carb ?

I have a spare carb, but you guessed it........... That same plug is missing.

What eats these things, anyway ?
