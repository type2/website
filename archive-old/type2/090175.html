On Mon, 10 Jun 2002, Richard Atwell wrote:

> When I press the brakes, the brake booster seems to pull the engine
> idle down quite a bit.  Does this mean the shoes aren't close enough
> to the drum?

Probably not.  It's time to break out (or buy) the vacuum gauge.  Attach
it to the air dist box, then have someone step on the brakes while you
watch the gauge (bus at idle).  If the vacuum dips, then comes right back
up, the booster is fine.  If it dips and stays down, then your booster is
shot.
