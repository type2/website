I commute on an electric scooter made by Currie Technologies and sold by
Mongoose Bicycles.  Similar scooters are also branded as Schwinn and GT.
The same company also makes electric bicycles.

They are a handy little device, but take the mileage and hill-climbing
claims with a big grain of salt.  Battery technology just isn't that
terrific unless you want to spend a _lot_ of money.  I have a 1-mile
commute and I could probably do it 3 or 4 times before the batteries
need recharging for a total of six or eight miles.  Add a hill and the
power consumption goes way up and they do not regenerate going downhill.

The scooter is also a handy thing to have at VW meets, making it easy to
run errands without having to move my bus.

It works for me, giving me the functionality of the ubertech Segway
scooter at a tenth of the cost.  No, it won't sit there balancing like a
Segway, but there is this handy little kickstand that holds it upright
even when the power is off!  It also doesn't make me do a faceplant when
the software has a glitch!  :)
