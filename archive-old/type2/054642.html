Hi Andy --
  you wrote:
"My much messier process is to install the reservoir, fill, and
submerge all of the open
ports in a bucket of brake fluid, pump manually until no bubbles, wipe
and install. the bucket of fluid is still fresh so i dump it back into
the containers and use it for the bleeding......"

Some purists on brake fluid will argue against this, especially if
there is some kind of contamination on the outer surfaces of cylinder
and/or reservoir..... but I have done the same thing when I couldn't
find my bench bleeder tubes.  I did, however, scrounge a couple of
coffee filters from the kitchen, put the filters in a funnel, and then
filtered the fluid before putting it into the system.  Also I like to
run the cylinder casting and the reservoir [previously cleaned
internally with BBs and detergent] through the dishwasher before
reassembly......  when the Frau is not around, of course.

I am a big fan of Dow Corning silicone grease.  But that is another
topic.  Good luck with your brake projects.
