mine when they go bad.  they usually give a short warning by breaking a
few strands, which causes low pedal.  If you catch it then, you can
replace it in your driveway and be able to pull the whole thing out at
once.

> >I put them back in with plenty of grease, I like to use CV joint grease. 
> I used grease but can't figure out why everyone says to.  They slid in 
> just fine without grease during the trial fit.

The grease isn't to help them slide in.  It's to lubricate the cable's
motion while in operation and to prevent the cable and housing from
corroding.

> >spare.  I compare the newly replaced cable with the one I'm carrying as a 
> >spare and keep the best of the 2 as my spare.

This sounds like a really good way to end up with two marginal cables!  I
prefer to carry a new one as a spare, first making sure that it is the
correct length and then packaging it in something a bit more secure than
the normal little plastic bag.  When a cable gets replaced, the parts box
one goes in and another new cable takes its place.
