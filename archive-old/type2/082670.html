 Don't know about the fuel pump, chrome trim, paint job, or gearbox but I do know that putting a bigger engine, if aircooled is you desire, could be rather expensive.
If you go for a Type 4 style 2.0l you'll have to put an upright conversion kit on it to fit it in the back of the 71.
You can kiss about a grand goodbye for that alone & rebuilding that sort of engine at the is going to be quite pricey....belive me, I know!
If you choose to keep a Type 1 style 1600 case & bore &/or stroke it then there are other probs.
For instance, if you like driving in the desert be ready to fit an external oil cooler because going larger than a 1641cc means machining the block & that will begin to take the strength out of it & shorten the engine life & will also mean that the engine runs hotter.
The truth is, that for an everyday driver a 1600, while not being a speedy proposition, is a very reliable one.
I'm sure there are people who will tell you that they can build a 1776 or 1835 that will run cool & have a long life & they may be right but more power for a bus that age is often expensive.
Of course you can always go the route I intend to with my 67 Panel.........a after-market fuel injected 1640 with a low boost supercharger to give it extra torque.
It'll be a long lasting engine, much more fuel efficient & reliable sources have told me the engine actually runs cooler with the supercharger although I'll be intercooling the s'charger.
Anyway, check out the prices for an upright converted 2.0l......it may be cheaper where you are but I bet it will still be expensive in comparison to other builds.
