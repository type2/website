From Steve:
 
When manifold vacuum rises to over 20" (when you take your foot off the
gas) the vacuum coming thru the small hose pulls a diaphram in the can
open and allows air to flow from the big hose around the throttle to the
intake air distribution box.  This prevents an ultra-lean condition on
deceleration and lowers certain pollutants (NOx, I believe).  It also
accounts for the lack of "engine brake" on FI buses.
