	Stephen,

	It's allways fun to read about someone doing the same misstakes as
oneself:-) The second time I "flanged" my exhaust system I did it the
following way:
 I welded the flanges straight on the unshortened heat exchangers. This way
you can even go back to donuts (God forbid) and clear the engine support
bar. And it's easier to weld this way if you have the heat exchangers on
the bus. I shortened the mufflers tubes instead, mufflers have a shorter
lifespan than heat exchangers and they are cheaper to do misstakes on.......  

To make the welding process easier I made the holes in the flanges big
enough so the tubes would fit into them. I slid the tube halfway into the
flange and welded on the inside. This way you don't have the problem with
the nuts and welding seam. And it really looks proffessional with no
visible welding seams. You just have to grind them with a rotating file so
they don't resrict the exhaust flow too much.
