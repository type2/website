Jan Willem Brier got it 100% right:

	"it's never safe for 16 years olds to go on a mega-tour alone,
	no matter what vehicle or how many gauges you give them."

But you might consider taking the tour with them. If you've done
that already and they really have to go somewhere alone, they
should be making a phone call at each arrival and have a defined 
place to stay at each step. An AAA or other road-service card
is a good idea, as is a cell phone, dual band digital AND 
analog. But they should really travel with someone, if a friend,
at least an older friend. 16 is YOUNG, and shouldn't be otherwise.

	Look, I was 16 myself once. When I was 15 I did a drive-a-day 
and camp for 3 days  trip with an older friend, the next summer I 
was 16 and went off with two other barely-older pals on a drive-a-
day-and-a-half trip with a week's camping, and a week-long drive 
with three friends from San Jose to Portland and back. Two summers 
later I drove with yet another friend from San Jose to LA for a week, 
staying with various friends. I wouldn't have made any of those trips 
by myself.

	The thought of a 16 year old with a new license and an old bus 
1500 miles from home just isn't comforting.

	Bill, who's son will be 16 in 12 years, 4 months!
