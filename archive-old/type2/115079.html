That engine isn't a spry youngster anymore but it seems to be coping all the
same. The book implies that 100-135 is drivable and you need to rebuild at
85.

> Question 2 - Noisy valves
> ===================
> Ever since I have owned this Westy I have struggled with noisy valves. I have
> found that
> if I overfill (at least looks like it on the dipstick) by 1/2 to 3/4 quart
> then the valves will pump

The low and high mark is about 1/2 quart so you are really overfilling the
engine doing that.

Overfilling is bad because it increases the crankcase pressure and this
leads to unnecessary oil leaks.

Also, any blow-by you experience as a result of low compression due to rings
and cylinders also increases crankcase pressure -> oil leaks.

> up and quite down. If I fill exactly to full line on dipstick then I can never

> year, and

> Synthetic.
