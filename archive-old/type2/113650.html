I have not been following this thread so I may repeat something someone 
else has already said...

I had a 1970 Westy when I lived in Naples, Italy that went through 4 
throw-out bearings in 2 days. We could not figure it out for a while.

Turns out it was a combination of two things: the throw-out arm had a 
cracked spot weld and one of the arms was bending (rotating on the shaft so 
it was not pushing as far as the other side). The other problem was that 
the bushing in the bellhousing on the driver's side was worn and the arm 
was shifting towards the driver's side causing what you mention above - the 
bearing to rotate against the pressure plate but off center so they rubbed 
instead of just mating up and being quiet. In our case the problems were 
severe enough that the bus would only make it about 4 or 5 miles before it 
self destructed. The heat built up would cook the bearing and the bearing 
would explode.

We towed it home each time with my little 40 HP Beetle and fixed it again 
and again before we got "educated". The Italian parts guy at the 
autoricambi thought it was funny though...
