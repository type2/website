On Sat, 13 Aug 2005, Karl wrote:

> > is it ok to use a beetle style muffler on a 70 bus?
>
> Sure, if you want to risk dying from carbon monoxide poisoning.
>
> Bus mufflers exit at the corner where the slip-stream is.
>
> Buses have a vortex at the rear. No air flow away, it comes back to the bus . Ever see the back of a bus all
> spattered with oil spots when the engine has a small leak? That is because of the vortex. No matter how good
> you think the rear hatch seal is, it leaks! Exhaust gas will enter the inside.

OTOH, thousands of folks have used beetle mufflers with no apparent
mortality.  I used to run one with an "iffy" rear hatch seal and my CO
detector card didn't detect any excess CO.

In fact, the only time I smell exhaust is when reversing with the heater
on, and this only happened with the correct bus muffler!

The best advice is always to run the correct "bus" part, but if you happen
to have a beetle muffler around it will serve well.  You could even get
creative and have a muffler shop fabricate two "bus style" exhaust tubes
for it.  You could pass it off as a 1-year-only South African dual
exhaust!  :)
