I tend to agree.  I've never had long life from an RV or marine battery in
a camper application.  Two years seems to be about max which, oddly
enough, is the length of the warranty on these expensive beasts.

I went to using standard starting batteries from Pep Boys and usually get
about four years from them.  Do the math:

Deep cycle battery: $120/2yr = $60 per year
Starting battery: $50/4yr = $12.50 per year

Plus, the warranty is better on starting batteries!

The only time I'd recommend a deep cycle battery is if the total capacity
of the battery was an important consideration.  For normal 1-evening
camping, a starting battery is fine.

Plus, you can give yourself a jump from a starting battery, an act which
will destroy a normal deep cycle battery.
