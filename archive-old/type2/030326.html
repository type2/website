You've got half the picture!  You're absolutely correct about the
thermostat not opening until 145F.  Cold outside air travels throught he
vents, through the fan, past the cylinders and finally past the
thermostat.  The thermostat regulates the outlet temperature by throttling
the airflow.

The whole idea is to keep the cylinders at a set temperature.  The system
is set up so that the cylinders get sufficient cooling at 100F OAT with
the flaps wide open.  However, what happens when the OAT is cooler?  IF
the cylinders are cooled to 250F at 100F OAT they will only get up to 150F
when the OAT is 0F, since the heat transfer is a function of the
difference in temperature between the incoming air and the cylinders.

Obviously, 150F is too cold for good engine operation.  The solution to
this is to limit airflow and allow the the cylinders to warm up to 250F or
so.  The thermostat "throttles" air flow to make sure that the cylinder
temperature stays high enough.  If the outside air is cold, the flaps are
kept more closed to maintain the same exit air temperature.
