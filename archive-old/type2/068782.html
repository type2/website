When I put a '78 engine into my '73 chassis, the State forced me to go to
a State inspection station for my smog certificate.  I had performed an
"engine swap" and the inspector would check to insure that all the emissions
equipment that goes with the '78 is present and functional.  I passed all
inspections and received a 3x5" white sticker that says smog to '78 standards.

You can legally swap in an engine from any year _after_ the chassis year.

In your case, you'll have to get a State inspection for the '79 "engine swap".
In the future you may swap back from a '79 engine to the original '76; that
would constitute another "engine swap".  Inspection time again.
