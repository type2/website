I think that if that breaker goes it means that the heater has nor
re-ignited for a certain period of time while fuel is flowing (safety
feature to prevent explosion or backfire). Mine would backfire if I reset
the switch without letting the heater purge the fumes first. I don't know
why yours wouldn't reignite after a half hour of continuous operation. Mine
would start, but then not re-light because the spark part of the glow plug
was no good.

I believe these heaters were built for the North, they seem to be standard
equipment on Canadian Buses. Mine is absolutely necessary up here in
Saskatoon, even though temps are not bad here now (around -15C overnight),
its going to get a workout when temps hit -35C.
