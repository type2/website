Billy-
There are two large copper contacts inside the solenoid cap.
They complete the power circuit to the starter, and are often arc-corroded.
This repair will require a soldering iron to R+R the solenoid cap.
While the starter is out, you can disassemble it for cleaning the
commutator with sandpaper, and a little lube at the bearings.
You can't electrically test the starter unless you have a spare
trans bellhousing to support the nose bushing, or you have the
self-supporting automatic-style starter.
Altogether, a very educational experience, you will "think starter",
And you gain a fresh starter service life, for just a little time.
