My parents used to drive their 1963 split and later 68 bay in all kinds
of evil Midwestern snow and ice.  Never had a problem with it.  Real,
skinny snow tires are necessary, and back then we always studded them as
soon as the law allowed.

My experience with winter driving in Pennsylvania, which is much milder
but more prone to icing, is that even a little bit of ice on a hill will
spell disaster if you don't have chains/studs all around.  The buses
like to slide front-first on slick hills.

Either way, it looks like "traction enhancement" is the order of the
day.  But then you'll have no troubles at all.
