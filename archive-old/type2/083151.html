The problem as I see it is that there are three possible outcomes, only
one of which would count as a "win."  In that area there are three places
from which oil can come:

the forward crank seal;
the cam plug;
a crack in the case.

If the leak is from the forward crank seal, no sweat. Pull engine,
measure end play, remove flywheel, replace seal, reset end play, button
it up.

If it is the cam plug, well, we could RTV it as a temporary fix, but to
really fix it, you have to split the case.  I suppose with as many people
as we would have there, a complete overhaul could be done if we could find
a machine shop with saturday hours.  We'd have to split into teams and
could do it as a race, but I have no doubt it could be done :-)

If it's the case, well, it's the case.  You need a new one.  Same
scenario as the cam plug, but we don't ned the machine shop, just a new
case.

It is very probable that your problem is the bad crank seal, but you
should understand that the other two possibilities exist.
