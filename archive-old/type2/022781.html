If you don't want to drive 55 MPH put a CHT gauge on your engine.  Do it 
properly, put the sending unit on the hottest part of the head you can find, 
but not under the plug because it tends to leak and erode the sealing 
surface.  Before you install it check how accurate it is.  An easy way is to 
put the sending unit in boiling water.  This isn't the best way to calibrate 
but it's within the means of the "common man".

300 to 350 degrees F is a good safe temp range and your engine will last a 
long time at these temps.  400 degrees or even a bit higher is "safe" for 
short periods of time or even long periods of time, it will just shorten the 
life of the engine.
I've done this over the years and have gotten well over 100,000 miles of 
trouble free transportation by doing it.

This only applies to a type I engine.
