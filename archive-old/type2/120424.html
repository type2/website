On Mon, 16 Feb 2004, Richard Atwell wrote:

> I took off the engine tin to do some painting and I stripped one of the screws. I should have had a small impact driver present but I manged to get all of the tough ones off that make a loud crack when I broke torque.
>
> The originals are the ubiquitous metric phillips cheese head style (the worst design). I see several people sell kits of shiny slotted engine tin screws. I assume the bug and bus screws are the same except for a couple of places where length is a concern. The part numbers seem to be the same.
>
> I'd prefer another style of screw that either of those two. Any recomendations?

The slotted cheese head screws are original, and seem to work fine.  I
don't recall any phillips screws in type I engine tin application.

There are a few places where you want to use hex-head bolts instead of the
cheese-head screws, just in case you need to take various screws out when
you cannot get a screwdiver on them.

The places I remember are:

Doghouse tin on front of shroud.
generator tin (lower two screws).
fan shroud on both ends (blocked by DP manifold).
Heat riser covers (one of the screws is blocked by the riser).

I recommend using anti-sieze on the tin screws.  Prevents much cussing
when you have to take them out again.
