Ron, 
     I think you are right about this.  One more thing to add, the retro appeal 
is very volatile.  Anyone who works in the consumer food business 
(supermarketing, food processing) will tell you that the dessert category is 
known as 'flavor du jour' -- something new sells for awhile, then drops 
quickly.  A product that took lots of development time/cost can be quickly 
eclipsed.  I would suggest that the 'retro' theme is much the same.  Chrysler 
cobbled the PT Cruiser together out of Neon parts -- keeping costs low -- and 
absorbed much of the nostalgia market.  I would bet many non-VW-traditionalist 
prospects opted for a PT instead of the NB.

As far as another minivan is concerned, the market has moved elsewhere.  Moms 
who listen to their kids don't want to be seen dead in a minivan.  The kids 
like SUVs and have no knowledge of, or interest in, the Type 2 history.  Except 
for a few diehard VW enthusiasts, the sales potential for the new Type 2 is 
questionable, and when you layer-on the poor styling job [my opinion here] and 
forecast high prices, even folks on this List will start looking at 
alternatives.  Just my 2".......  

Glenn
