>
>Another indespensible object for pulling vehicles is a 
>Come-a-Long, especially if it is wet or muddy.  Can be hitched to 
>rescuer vehicle or other suitably fixed object.
>
>The gear oil, 90 weight, can be obtained with a self contained pump 
>included, in the gallon size.  Check at your 'merican FLAPS type 
>place.
>
>Rope, for tying things down.  There is one type (polypropylene maybe) 
>that is resistant to battery acid.
>
>Platform for the floorjack, stout enough to support vehicle.  3/4" 
>minimum thickness plywood.
>
>Ice pick and misc crud pickers such as dental tools for cleaning out 
>hex head bolts. Also a small paint scraper comes in hand.
>
>Spray carb cleaner for cleaning out hex heads, and cleaning off 
>regular hex bolt heads.  Not only does the solvent clean, but the 
>pressure blasts stuff out of the way.
>
>Cardboard is a cheap substitute for carpet for laying upon.
>
>Supply of those cardboard *trays* that are used to deliver 24 cans of 
>ones favorite beverage.  Great for containing tools and parts, get 
>greasy then recycle them.  Can be stocked WITH favorite beverage too.
>
>As for fuel lines, get a variety of small sizes, no telling what PO 
>has in there, along with SMALL hose clamps for when the fitting is 
>smaller that your smallest hose.

>wabbott_at_mtest.teradyne_dot_com (William Abbott)
>
>
>       No CVs or boots- swing axle if its a Splittie, unless hopped up.
>
>       If its really running regularly, then you don't need a can
>to dump the bad gas into, but a real salvage operation you'd want one.
>
>       I'd bring a hose, sponges, squeegee, bucket & soap, and
>a scrub-brush. I dunno, maybe some people would prefer it to be
>dusty when first fired up... but driving home clean is fun too.
>
>       Battery postive and ground straps and a new tranny ground braid?
>
>       I'd bring a brake bleeding tool like the one I've described
>too many times AND a vaccuum pump. Use the vaccuum pump to draw
>fluid out of the system, rather than pumping, unless you already
>plan to replace the master cylinder or its seals- old master
>cylinders tend to tear when pumped vigorously after a lot of
>crud has settled in the bore... if the system's solid but the
>fluid's filthy, this would make a difference.
>
>       Bill
