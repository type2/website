Well, I did my first partial tune-up!  Gretel, my poor german pack mule, was
having a real bad time, at stop lights he'd idle down, oil pressure light 
blinking furiously, until she shut off (shaking and shimmying the whole while) 
and she had NO power.  

I bought some diagnostic equipment, and set my valves (.006"), my timing (7.5 
degrees BTDC ('77 FI camper)) and my idle (850 rpm's), and now, joy of joy's, 
it's like she's been reborn! I can't believe how much power there seems to be 
now..  it feels like a v8!  Unfortunately, now I'm running into new problems.

Old Gretel has always had an exhaust leak, and she eats a lot of oil.  The 
problem is, today driving to work, and riding at 55 for a while, caused a LOT
of white smoke to emanate from the fan housing when I stopped.  Also some
white smoke appeared to be coming from the large square part of the engine that
the coil is mounted on.  I havne't figured out what this is called yet, but
it's the big lumpy square right to the left of the fan housing.  Am I just 
burning off oil that got splashed around when she was running poorly (and
cooler, I'm guessing?) or is something else possibly wrong?  Any advice in
this regard would help a lot, especially whether I should stop driving it
until I get this new problem fixed.
