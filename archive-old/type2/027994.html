Jack,
  I would guess that it is a 12VDC square wave (of sorts).  Duration/frequency of the wave will be dependent on operating speed/conditions (hence the need for syncronization).  There may be some "spikey" edges to the wave but it shouldn't be that far off.  I'd suggest making up a "T" jumper that goes in between the harness and the injector to provide a connection point (rather than trying to reach into the existing plug with the boot pulled back).  Shouldn't be a problem with polarity either - hooking it up backwards would show a negative-going wave versus positive.  Just reverse the scope leads.
