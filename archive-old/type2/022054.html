Hi Andy,

I would say 35mpg is exceptional. I used to get 25mpg from my 1600cc '73 and 
now get around 26mpg from my 1998 1600 with FI (although thats at 55 to 60 
mph rather than 50 to 55mph [slower on hills] in the '73). all mpg in 
imperial gallons.

I've met people who claim slightly more than 25mpg but not much. I believed 
the guy who said he got 30mpg from a 1645cc type 1 engine but I think he 
would have been at the top end of possible.

So unless every thing in the engine is immaculately balanced, the head has 
larger ports, he has a large bore extractor exhaust, and everything else is 
in the running gear is better than new, I would think 35mpg was at best 
optimistic.

I did once get 28 mpg between fill-ups; but at next fill-up I only got 20 
mpg. I put this down to the middle fill-up being not so full as the two on 
each side because driving conditions were the same throughout.

If you are looking for an FI system for a 1600 upright dont overlook the 
pulsed injection system fitted by VW to current production 1600 engines in 
Mexican Beetles - works well.

Good luck
