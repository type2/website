That configuration is screwed up. You don't have any crankcase
ventilation like that. I'll bet the back of the engine has oil all over
it.

Here's how to fix it. Take the box off the fan housing. Take the
fittings out of the box. Throw the box away. Drill a hole in the top of
one of the air cleaner on the right side big enough to install the
fitting you saved from the box and install it. Run a hose from beside
the oil filler to the fitting on top of the air cleaner. Plug the hole
where the fuel pump used to be. Clean up the back of the engine.

A hose clamp on the connection on the carb is a good idea, just to be
sure the fitting doesn't vibrate loose and fall into the carb. If there
are holes in the fan housing, those need to be plugged too.

The point is: you need air flow from the crankcase to the air cleaner
for the ventilation system to work right. You don't have that with the
present setup.

Hope that helps.
