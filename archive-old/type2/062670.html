> I have a good sturdy cross bar that allows me to pull up on one end while I
> stomp on the opposite end.  This gives an impact effect that I have found much
> more useful in getting stubborn lug bolts and nuts off than sockets and breaker
> bars.  Only failed to work once when the bolt was sloppy to start and rounded
> out when I tried to take it off.  Then I tried a 12 point socket and breaker bar
> - big mistake, rounded it even worse.  Finally used a cold chisel to mangle the
> bolt, hammered my cross bar onto it and got it of.  The cross bar show no signs
> of distress after all this, so I re-iterate, that a good, sturdy cross bar is
> the best tool for removing lugs when you have no impact wrench.  (I have seen
> cross bars that were springy and wimpy that would have cringed at the abuse mine
> has seen).

The owner of the VW shop I used to work at showed me a trick. Take the biggest hammer

to break it loose.  This always worked for me.
