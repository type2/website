Hi Russell,

See my other post on testing vacuum leaks in response to Andy.  Get a pint
squeeze bottle and a 2 ft hose.  Jet in the end of the hose to stream the
flow.  Got it?  More control that way.

(flame suit on) Ditch the 10w40 Castrol.  If using conventional oil up to 68
degrees, use SAE 30 and I recommend Valvoline.  Above 68-degree ambient air
temp, I use SAE 40.  Without FF filtering I cannot recommend synthetics in
T1 engines unless you are rich...but if you are, you drive a Lexus or
something.

Some builders leave out the under-cylinder tin that I call "forget me nots"
because if you put the heads on and forget that tin, you have to take it
apart again. Cool tin is either late T3 or aftermarket under-cylinder
tin...it works better but requires some modification to surrounding tin.
You can put that in when you do a valve job.

You could surface the tops of the RV pistons with successively finer grades
of wet or dry with solvent on a plate of glass if you're AR like me.
