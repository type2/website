My suspect is a worn throttle shaft that lets air past.  A (very)
temporary fix for this is to take the nuts off of both sides and put some
thick grease (like Bosch distributor point grease) where the shaft enters
the carb body.  If the carb tunes better you might be able to get it past
smog before the grease goes away.

The real fix is a new carb or having the old carb rebushed.

The -2 and -3 carbs are a lot more susceptable to being dirty than the -1
versions.  A good cleaning will often cure their ills.
