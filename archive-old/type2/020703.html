Steve,

It's not as difficult as I wanted to make it out to be.  When I got the
Adventurewagen a year ago, I went through a series of brake problems.  The lines
were the last thing I replaced, I wish now I had done them at the start.  I've
also replaced them on the single cab.

The most difficult issue is forming the new line to "match" the old ones you're
removing, but the good news is they don't have to be exact ( I've also heard
that you can get replacement brake lines that are pre shaped - although I don't
know from where - it would be interesting to see how the center brake line is
packaged for shipping!).  I just taped one end of the new line to the old and
then bent it to match the old one.

I bought mine from Bus Depot.  I made the mistake of just ordering one of each
listed on the web site for my year range.  I ended up with one very short line
(5-6 inches) that I could find anywhere on my bus.  If it's not a daily driver,
I'd be inclined to pull all the lines off (marking them as you go), measure
them, then place an order based on mm length.  I replaced five lines, the front
ones from the master cylinder to the flexible hoses (2), the long line from the
master cylinder to the rear "T" (1), and the two lines running from the "T" to
the rear flexible hoses (2).  I would recommend replacing them all, they're
cheap.

Tools?  The only special tool I recall using was a brake tube bender (2-3$ from
your FLAPS).  Good luck.
