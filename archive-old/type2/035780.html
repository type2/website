Nick,

Hey, no offense intended, but... Are you closing the
slider properly??? Hey, I've had 3 busses and only
recently learned the "proper" way to close it ;-)

Instead of "slamming" the door, you slide it just hard
enough to engage the front latch, then pull the outer
handle up. This locks the back latch and prevents
unecessary stress-damage from "slamming" it shut.

If your handle will not pull "up" after the front
latch is engaged, try checking the little lock-switch
inside the door. It should be white and tear-drop
shaped. Make sure it's perfectly level (try lifting it
"up") and try again. This should work.

As far as the rear latch not opening properly....LUBE,
LUBE, LUBE, BABY! Make sure that all
channels/mechanisms are free of loose crud/debris
first, then.... LUBE! I use wheel-bearing grease,
combined with a liberal dose of WD-40. 

I found the wheel-bearing grease alone was too thick,
causing it to stick on colder days. Likewise, the
WD-40 was too thin and didn't last long enough. You
might also try white-lithium grease, but I'm dubious
about it lasting very long. 

Also, visualy inspect the doors bearings while sliding
the door back and forth....make sure they are  all
(there are 4 of them)turning as they are supposed to.
If not, try freeing them with WD-40 (or other
penetrant) and see if this works. If not, replace
them; the door will not slide properly otherwise. If
you gotta replace, call a bearing-supply house...you'd
be surprised what they have and much cheaper/less
frustrating than trying to find the O.E.M. stuff, too
(you'll need the I.D., O.D. and width specs before
calling, so dig out the old micrometer;-).


Good luck!
