On Sat, 18 Nov 2000, Doktor Tim wrote:

> A German gland nut can be reused, but only if you know for certain that it
> has never been overtorqued. That is rarely the case, hence, only a new
> gland nut can insure against failure.

And make SURE that its a VW or equivalent gland nut.

The gland nut is made softer than the crank so if overtorqued the gland
nut deforms, if you get a high strenth CHRO-MOLLY gland nut and overtorque
it, it will strip out the end of your crank.

This is not a place to put in a stronger than stock fastener!
