Hi Joel

On 10/30/03, you wrote:

>>> Brake fluid makes a really good rubber conditioner -
> and after you wash your van you can rub some on the
> tires to give them a like-new, but not glossy look.
> Works great actually.<<

> WARNING - brake fluid will strip paint!

Definitely.....brake fluid oxidizes paint which means its reactive.

If you read the bottle it tells you to keep it sealed.  Old brake
fluid shouldn't be used in a brake system because of this.
Once oxidized it compresses more than new fluid.

While rubber may appear to look better I'd be hesitant about using
brake fluid on it.

UV light in sunlight hastens oxidation and aging of rubber so I
question using a product that will oxidize paint.

I'd stick with the products that state they are formulated for rubber
and preferably include  some sort of UV screening ingredient.

Its a little messy but I've used Vaseline on non automotive rubber in
the past....apply while doing washes, etc. and wipe off the excess.
