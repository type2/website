This is an interesting point. If you want to see something really scary,
take a ride in a new O*ds Aur*ra. These are equipped with a 'control center'
that is capable of displaying current oil pressure. After warm-up, they
consistently display an oil pressure of 7 PSI at idle. Normally.

Reciprocating Air Compressors use splash lubrication (0PSI) with electric
drive motors putting in the equivalent of 50HP and give an amazing service
life on the rod bearings, which are basically identical to automotive
bearings. With similar bearing loads.

is working. We live in the highest peaks of the Appalachians, and some of
the grades are considerable. We have even run the van at almost full
throttle (75MPH-probably had a tailwind) coming home from Missouri for
several (two) hours at a time. Not a problem.
