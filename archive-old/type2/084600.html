Within the last month I saw a Vanagon come in running very poor and putting 
off a lot of smoke. It was at least a full quart over full from gas 
dilution from constant short commuting. It took months for this to occur, 
meaning the oil level had not been checked, even at the garage it got towed 
from to me 'cause they couldn't make it run right. Even the "experts" at 
the prior shop didn't check the oil level. They are certified by ASC and 
AAA, just paper hanging on the wall, a testiment to their skills.  In 
addition, the oil being used was 5w-30. Thank God they did the right thing 
and sent them to me.

I also saw a BIGSIXBIMMER in the last month that had been serviced at the 
dealer to about 80K miles. My wizbang on-board, real time, Euro specific 
computer interface diagnostics system pinpointed the running problem as the 
oxy sensor. It was called for replacement by standard procedure at 60K. Not 
even the dealers will add a $250 part to the invoice if they gave a quote

And just last week had another Vanagon come in running very poor right 
after a "tune-up" at a "reputable" metropolitan shop. Gas mileage was half 
what it was before, and before the "tune-up" the motor was running quite 
good. Plugs were carbon fouled. It took me over an hour of validating and 
testing till I saw while monitoring the oxy feedback drops to 0 volts 
intermittently. I found the copper static shielding for the oxy wire was 
grounding the circuit at the terminal. First time I've seen that in 
hundreds of these Bosch harnesses. An anomaly, caught in the coarse of 
standard given procedures. I trimmed and insulated things, replaced the oxy 
sensor, then verified the timing/idle/mixture and all is well. I noted in 
the history of invoices on this vehicle that it had never, since new, had a 
proper cooling system service. Even when the motor was replaced, they 
pinched the hoses and reused half the filthy coolant to enhance the 
deterioration of the new motor right out of the box. Not a single invoice 
since new showed a charge for new coolant, except for the motor replacement 
which charged for one gallon, half that required by a whole system 
service.  This is a common occurrence with Wasserboxers. The cause of 
failure here is bone head simple. Lack of reasonable maintenance procedure.
