Steve (Leslie's husband):

The air cooled Vanagons don't really have a "head gasket" in the same 
sense as the typical water cooled engine.  There may or may not be a 
"shim" between the top of the cylinder and the head, but that's there to 
adjust compression ratio and provide a sealing surface for the 
cylinder/comprssion chamber.  Since there shouldn't be a lot of oil in 
the cylinder, getting signifant oil from this area really isn't possible, 
absent a BIG head problem that you'd probably notice first as a loss of 
power.  

Most likely the leak is coming from the pushrod tubes or, more precisely, 
>from the pushrod seals at either end.  Since the pushrod tubes go in 
place after the head's in place, you can actually replace these tubes 
and/or seals without dropping the engine or removing the heads.
