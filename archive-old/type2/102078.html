The key phrase here is "from the factory." Apparently the factory did
the engineering needed to integrate the siping into the basic design of
the tire. 
This is a long way from having Bobo the Tire Guy slice a set of tires
that were not designed for it! 
I'm not saying that siping a tire won't aid traction on ice and snow,
but there has to be a downside in wear or dry traction. I used to do a
lot of artistic things with razor blades to motorcycle tires when I
raced, but with the sure knowledge that the tires would wear rather
rapidly. I certainly wouldn't want to use a sliced tire on dry pavement,
as the same flexibility that aids traction on loose surfaces will ruin
it on dry pavement.
