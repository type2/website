      Around 1965, a friend and I went to a Pittsburgh (Pa.) auction where we 
bought a non-running VW microbus.  Seemed a bargain too good to pass up.  We 
towed the bus ~25 miles using his Beetle; and in the process we burned two

kept the revs up.  The combined mass and some hilly conditions, despite the 
cool weather, were clearly too much for the engine.  It marked the beginning of 
an end to our friendship, and though we both understood the physics of the 
problem, it clearly represented bad judgment in several respects.... Glenn
