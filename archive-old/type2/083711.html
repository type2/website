What we call "vacuum advance" distributors are't really advancing with
vacuum, at least not manifold vacuum.  The vacuum signal to the
distributor comes from the carburator and is controlled by the throttle
opening and airflow through the carburator.

You're right in that it is hard to get good readings in the unloaded
condition.  You can run the engine at a speed where all the centrifugal
advance is in and then blip the throttle open while watching the timing
marks.  The vacuum advance comes into play and you can pick out the
maximum.  It helps to have the marks highlighted with white paint ("Liquid
paper" works well).
