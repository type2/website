 Err, from where did these specs come, out of interest?

 Sean Bartnik's scanned '78 owners manual (http://www.type2_dot_com/bartnik/tech.htm) lists acceptable tyres as:

 185R14C 6PR Load range C
 185SR14 Reinforced

 and indicates that you shouldn't mix these specs on the same axle. It explicitly indicates that these are European standards (C indicates European commercial vehicle load range C and reinforced indicates a strengthened passanger car tyre in the European system of rating).

 My European '77 kombi manual (for a camper) has the European specs for the various models of type2 of that year. It makes no reference to Reinforced tyres but recommends 185R14C 6PR tyres for L model busses, ambulances and firetrucks and 7.00 14 8PR for delivery vans (I presume these are crossplies!).
 
 The Haynes manual for pancake type-2s says 185SR14 Reinforced tyres are OK on '72-on Campmobiles but others should use 185R14C commercial vehicle tyres.
 Radials were fitted from '71-on (and if you do fit crossplies they need to be 8-ply-rated).

 The conclusion I draw from this is that a reinforced passanger car tyre is marginal for use on a type2,  unreinforced tyres are wholly inappropriate  and that a European commercial vehicle spec. load range "C" tyre is a sensible miminum fitting for acceptable handling on modern roads.
 My experience, and reading of the list, indicates that load range "D" tyres are stiffer (=better handling) and wear better than load range "C"s. They probably don't warrant the extra cost in purely commercial terms (and are technically overspecified) but some individuals think the extra cost is worth it. Much in the same way as many drivers of sporty saloons feel that it's worth spending money on top of the line performance tyres, even though they are overspec, as they may improve handling and roadholding.
