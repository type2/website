valve - it is a little round brass plug that fits into hole - so that it is
always closed.  What I'm not sure of is whether the resistor and bimetallic
strip causes the plug to close the hole when warm or open it when warm.  I
have gambled on it closing when warm, but a bimetallic strip can operate
either way depending on design.  To decide whether I have gambled correctly,
I need to know how it works.  I assume that air flows through the enrichment
valve, not fuel and not mixture.  If so, why would the valve close when
warm, cutting off the extra air which would otherwise make the idle mixture
leaner when warm?  On this view of things it would make more sense to open
when warm allowing the air through?
If disconnected, wouldn't the engine idle fine when cold but not when warm?

>choke operates, allowing the choke butterfly to open up.  The engine will
