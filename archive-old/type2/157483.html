"Kevin H. Roberts" <bopsax_at_yahoo_dot_com> wrote:I  had this problem on one of my Jags. The tank had a sizeable drain  easily accessible behind the rear fender. Don't know if the bus has a  drain on the bottom, how easy it is to get to, etc. But what I did was  completely drain the tank and flush it with a water hose really good.  Then I filled it about half full of Kerosene and stuck an air hose down  in it to really slosh it up good. Drained the Kerosene and filled it  with Gasoline. Water is heavier than Kerosene and it pushes it to the  bottom. It is lighter than Gasoline. So, pump some out through the fuel  line into a glass container until you don't see any more water floating  to the top. Since I didn't fill the tank with Kerosene I didn't  completely solve the problem. But I go much longer between having to  change fuel filters. Maybe once every couple of months as opposed to  days.
