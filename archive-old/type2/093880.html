You know, I got to thinking:  With this into you could verify your VDO oil
temp gauge by using a variable potentiometer ($2 from Radio Shack) and your
multi-meter (set to Ohms) or an Ohm-meter.  

Ground one side of the pot to your bus and hook the other side to your
Ohm-meter and connect the other lead of your Ohm-meter to the bus.  Using
your meter, set the pot to one of the Ohm values in the chart (for instance,
64 Ohms).  Then disconnect your Ohm-meter from the pot and hook the pot to
the input wire for your temp gauge.  If you chose 64 Ohms, your gauge should
be reading 210 degrees.
