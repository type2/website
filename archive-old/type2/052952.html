>Subject: Re: j-tubes or heat exchangers?
>
>J-tubes do take the place of heat exchangers, eliminating the
>heat-transfer boxes and therefore your heat.
>
>I'd recommend that you take another look at the old boxes.  Original >boxes
>are the best and are very difficult to find.  I've found that the >boxes
>are easily patched by brazing patches over the holes and reinforcing >where
>the exhaust pipe enters/exits with a metal sleeve.
>The condition of the heater boxes has little bearing on engine >overheating
>except that leaky boxes can divert a bit more air from the fan.
>
>For heaven's sake, don't throw the old boxes away, even if you don't >use
>them.  Someone will find them useful down the road!
>
>George Lyle
