Often the cable failures can be traced to wear in the hole through
which the clevis pin passes in the clutch arm.  The pin wears the hole
oval and also wears a groove in itself.  The two pieces wear to each
other and the cable clevis stops pivoting on the pin while the clutch
operates.  This causes stress where the cable attaches to the clevis
as the attachment is flexed back and forth under the pull of the cable.

The solution is to repair the oval hole back to its original round
form (I simply fill the hole with a gas welder, grind the arm back
flat on both sides, and redrill the hole.

I replaced the clevis pin with a standard bolt, making sure that the
pivoting occurs on the shoulder of the bolt, not the threads.

This fix won't last forever, but the original lasted 250K miles and
this one should do about as well :)
