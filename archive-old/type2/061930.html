>
> >As to a new pedal, it does not look good.  VWofA reports
> >that accelerator pedals for busses are no longer available.
> >BusBoys does not have one.  RMMW does not have one.  And
> >with the bracket welded down...
>
> I watched David Easterwood do this to my 68 last January. (This ain't too
> elegant, but it's held up fine).
>
> Go to the hardware store, get a barn door hinge. A little less wide than
> the pedal. Take the broken pedal and the hinge to a muffler shop. Ask them
> to cut the hinge about in half (take the points off it).
>
> Then, have them weld one side of the hinge to the pedal such that the
> geometry stays as close to the original as possible. Show them, don't tell
> them. Those folks ain't too bright sometimes.
>
> Take the "restored" pedal back to the bus and bolt the free side of the
> hinge to the floor. Mark it and drill holes, then bolt straight through the
> floor. My floor is rusty, too, but there was enough there to bolt to.
>
> I think this all cost about twelve dollars including the welding.
>
As an even cheaper, easier alternative, just bolt the hinge onto the pedal as
well as the floor. Ive done this in my last 2 busses with no problems...
