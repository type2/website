If you can find a good head without cracks that will incure high welding repair costs, it's a good way to go.

GoWesty! and other engine rebuilders are upgrading the AMC heads:

http://www.gowesty_dot_com/cylinderhead.html

They naturally have engine warranties to protect and while they can probably get a replacement head, they lose too much money to R&R the engine again for the customer so they improve the head and pass the a smaller cost along to them.

AMC produces a head casting and does some basic machining to it. It is the US importer that grinds the valve seats and installs the valves, springs and other hardware. The parts chosen vary in quality and cost as time goes by and occasionally as evidenced by the GoWesty! article one of those parts causes a head failure. Is it any greater a failure rate than any other mass produced part? Who knows. Those are just the facts. Many people are driving around with stock AMC heads without issue.
