Wow, a working A/C!.  How lucky some people are!  First of all your unit is
dealer installed rather than an actual factory unit and your unit may not be
exactly like mine.  However, if it is like the one on my 76 Westy deluxe
then you can get to the belt in about an hour or so.  Removing the
compressor is usually the first thing I have to do whenever I remove the
engine.  The long bolt across the top of the bracket is removed and a couple
of 13mm nuts at the bottom of the compressor are removed and it slides out.
I lay it in the auxillary battery area to get it out of the way.  DO NOT
remove the hoses.  You then simply need to remove the engine tin, front of
the fan shroud, and fan.  You may have to remove or unbolt the alternator,
but I simply can't remember off the top of my head. Even if you do, it is no
big deal.  There is a belt pulley behind the fan that you loop your belt
over then thread the belt over to the compressor.  You then begin to
reninstall everything.  The bottom of the compressor has shims that allow
for enough slack to install the belt then replace the shims to tighten the
belt.  Most of the dealer installed A/C units I have seen look very similar
and I can't imagine a VW mechanic that couldn't do this job in 2 hours.
Good luck.
