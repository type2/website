Craig,
I did this in my 75 (with a 79 engine), with the
engine in, and it took me an afternoon, probably 5
hours,  including trips to 3 auto parts places to try
to find the right size of hose and elbow.  I had
ordered a new rubber piece that goes at the body at
the filler cap, but the other rubber bent hose at the
tank was cracked, and I replaced it with a metal elbow
and two pieces of rubber hose (fuel rated). 
My son helped me by leaning over the back seat and
helping take out the firewall.
Don't forget the 2 screws that are accessible under
the bus that fasted in the bottom of the firewall.
I took out the air cleaner, fuel injection stuff, and
the relays and voltage regulator off the firewall.

I also corresponded with a couple of other folks on
the list that sounded like they were going to attempt
this but haven't heard how they did.  Maybe they will
also respond.

Good luck.
