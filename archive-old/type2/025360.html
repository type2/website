Actually, Mr. Lyle will probably need to make several measurements: there
should be *negative gradients* extending from the various boudaries with
still air to the fan (lowest pressure).  Pressure will decrease (per
Bournoulli's law) in areas where air flows faster. (example: the pressure
between the sparetire bulge and the body will be 0, but between the fan
housing and the firewall will be lower. (assuming an upright engine)).

There will be a second (negative) gradient begining at the circumference of
the fan and extending to free air.

(Of course there's a positive gradient from the center of the fan to its
circumference, but if George can devise an experiment to measure it he'll be
able to take his place along side Franklin, Michleson/Morley, Ruthford and
Millikan. :) )

The point is to think of them as "continuous functions"
