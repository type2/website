When the heater tube rusted out of my 69 about 20 years ago, I replaced
it with a piece of 2" schedule 40 plastic dvw pipe. I just made it a
little long wiggled it in and then caulked all around it to glue it in.
Seems to me you could do the same thing with the fan, just add some 3"
pipe with two 2-3 adapters and put the 3" fan in the middle. Actually,
probably closer to one end so as to make support easier. I don't think
you'd gain much with the 4" fan because an old bus's duct system is
approx. 2" anyway. Somewhere either 72 or 73 they changed to 3" which
they stayed with thru Vanagons. The factory Eberspacher replaces most of
this tube in the 74-79 models. When my gas heater in my 78 died due to a
leaky heat exchanger, I just replaced it with a  piece of 3" dvw pipe-
it fit on with the same clamps that held the heater! Incidently, I think
that a booster fan is a really good idea. I had fitted a weld in one
under the front floor of a 70 once- sure cleared the windscreen well and
gave some relief from the cold. Sometimes in Iowa it dips down to 15 -25
below F., so for you guys and gals in the moss belt that might be all
the heater you need! In Iowa you know it's cold in the morning when you
jump into the car and the seat feels like it's made of cement!
Apparently the cold affects the foam padding about the same as
everything else- makes it stiff and brittle! I've noticed this
phenomenon mostly below 0 F., but it gets really noticeable around -15
F.
It just all depends on what you're used to.
