Regarding "parking in the sun", I whole heartedly agree.  Again, it 
depends on your application.  When I travel to Burning Man, we're camped 
out in the middle of a dry lakebed -- no shade in sight -- for 7-10 days.  
In this case, a solar powered refridgeration system works well.  

Similarly, if you're "on the road", you're probably getting some 
additional charging while you travel -- be that to charge your depleted 
batteries or power your fridge which is running.  There was a trip back to 
San Diego from the Grand Canyon one memorial day that I would have 
*loved* to have a fridge on board for...and I was certainly getting 
plenty of sun.

Bottom line: the trick is to either design something robust in *most* 
applications or to pick a particular application and design towards it.  
Then you get to factor in the money part.
