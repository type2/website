> A couple of suggestions on your symptom:
> Check to see if the throttle valve is fully closing after you have run it
> for a while. Crud can build up in the valve throat and impede it's return
to
> full closure. Just turn it by hand. If it slows down, then remove the
valve
> and clean the passageway. Get a new gasket (valve to air distribution
> manifold) before you start, however.
> Check for leaks in the air hose and its connections that supplies the
> auxiliary air valve from the s-shaped air hose. That valve is the
> thermostatically controlled equivalent of a choke. If you have a crack in
> the hose or a leak of air at any fitting in the pathway, then unmonitored
> air can get into the distribution manifold which causes a too lean
condition
> when cold, which one then mistakenly compensates for by opening the idle
air
> screw so it will idle when cold, but then when the temperature shuts dcwn
> the auxiliary air valve the engine speeds up because that closes off the
air
> leak, and now you are running with the idle screw too far open. Let me
know
> if that helps!
