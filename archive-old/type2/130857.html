> 3. What's best (durable and aesthetic) route to repairing the hole in 
> the yellow part?

There are people who can reweave any cloth, but you would have to take 
the canvas off. If you are only interested in how the repair looks from 
the outside, you'd do best to use an iron-on patch on the inside. Have 
someone stand on the inside with the patch taped to a piece of wood, 
with the glue facing the repair. You will be on the outside with an 
iron (no steam). Bring the torn parts together as closely as you can 
and iron until the glue bonds. Then, for a first rate repair, put in a 
stitch with stout thread of matching color, crossing back and forth 
across the tear. It will look like a repair, but that's a badge of 
honor. Most people will never notice it.
