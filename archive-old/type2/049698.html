All you do is build a modified front housing for a
VW transmission that
handles an output shaft bearing next to the shift
rod. You extend the
pinion shaft out of the front of the tranny right
through this new bearing.

It's been done and there is a web page somewhere
with detailed pictures.

The next challenge is to create a front
differential with a matching gear
ratio - maybe existing pinion shaft and
differential out of a matching
transmission?

It would take a long driveshaft with a carrier
bearing too like my CR-V has.

Might want to add a viscous coupler to minimize
binding in turns.

Been thinking about this for a long time...
