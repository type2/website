Subject: Re: T2] Oil Temp Sender Wire Length

The wire length (within reason) should not matter for oil temp. This sender
changes it's resistance with temperature by a fairly large amount, and the
gauge measures the voltage drop relative to bus voltage (12 ish). A little
extra wire would add a very small resistance, which would be negligible
compared to the sender's resistance.

The CHT sensor actually generates it's own tiny voltage, which the gauge
measures.  A small change in resistance by adding wire might have a
significant effect on the reading. With the CHT you'd have to try it and see
what happens.
