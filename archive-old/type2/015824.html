	Hi,

 The Finnish authorities are pretty strict when it comes to trailer weight.
They allow the 1971 bus to tow 850 kg if the trailer itself has brakes. A
trailer witout brakes is allowed to have a total weight of 480 kg. I don't
think theese rules has anything to do with engine power or cooling, they
are rather an outcome of the braking and handeling capacities of the
vehicle. For example the 1972 bus has much higher ratings but was sold with
the 1600cc upright engine. Don't know if the brakes on the later busses was
that much better.

My advice is not to go much over the 480 kg anyway. And mabye take it a
little easier with cruising speed, definetivly no uphill speeding! If you
have a oil temp gauge it will tell you all you need.

My own experience is from towing my boat twice a year, a 60km road trip.
The total weight of boat and trailer is about 550kg. The oil temp gauge use
to tell me to keep a speed of 85 km/h to not run too hot. The boat  is not
very aerodynamic so a low light trailer vould not give the same effect.

Have a nice camping summer!
