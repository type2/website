>Buckminster Fuller's dymaxion car. This has become my automotive
>obsession. Back in the early thirties, the guy who gave us the geodesic
>dome designed a car that could carry eight adults, cruise at 140 mph,
>and get 30 miles per gallon. He actually built several working
>prototypes. The dymaxion car looks like a cross between a VW and a DC-3.
>Imagine stretching out a split-window bus into more a teardrop shape.
>The engine was in the back, where it drove a single, large wheel (It was
>a three wheeler.). The rear wheel also was the steering wheel; it could
>actually turn completely sideways, allowing the car to spin in circles
>while the nose stayed in the same place.
>
>I've only seen pictures of the dymaxion car, though there are a few in
>museums around the country. I'm dying to see the interior of one.
>
>The reason I bring this up is because the dymaxion car was totally
>unlike any car that existed at the time. Is it possible that the VW
>engineers were tyring to achieve a slightly more conservative
>interpretation of the dymaxion car? I thought maybe some folks on the
>list might have seen info on VW's inspiration for the transporter.
