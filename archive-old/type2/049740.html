Scot Schuckert wrote:
> One of
> the key elements of safe winter driving is to avoid sudden actions, ie.
> sudden braking. If one can, they should avoid braking by using the braking
> effect of their own engine to stop; shift down, take your foot off the gas,
> and let the engine do the work.

I agree about not braking suddenly, BUT: if things get really
hairy, you'll be better of braking with the brakes than with
the engine.  The brakes act on all four wheels, the engine on
just two (normally).  This can and does leave people spinning,
especially so with a rear-engined vehicle like an old VW.
Been there, done that, seen others do it too.
