On 2/15/04 8:10 AM, "Sage Green" <honey_bus_at_hotmail_dot_com> wrote:

 
> And since I am looking for a "best value" purchase next time, which wrench
> (21mm or 22mm) would find the most uses on a 68 - 79 bay?  I mean, is there
> ANY PLACE other than the alternator nut to use one of these wrenches??

22mm.

The other place you need this size for sure is the reverse lights switch on
a manual tranny. Not sure of any others.

For the alternator, I find the best wrench to use is a bent ratchet type. I
mean the old school ratchet wrench, the big ones with a toggle on the side
to change directions. This tool is very good for turning the motor over when
doing a valve adjustment. You need the bent style to get at the nut.
