Subject: Re: [T2] How to tell when the heat riser is unplugged

On Fri, 11 Nov 2005, Bronsema, Max wrote:

> I am cleaning some final parts before engine assembly. I turned my
> attention to the heat risers on my 1600dp manifold and knocked lots and
> lots of carbon out. How do I tell if it is unplugged though? I blow into
> it but I cannot really determine if it is unplugged or not. Should I be
> able to put a piece of wire in one end and take it out the other?

You should be able to blow easily through the heat riser.

If you can, this would be a good time for the "speedo cable in the drill"
cleaning trick.  This doesn't work so well on a completely blocked riser,
but it is a good way to clean out a tube that is just a bit dirty with
carbon deposits.
