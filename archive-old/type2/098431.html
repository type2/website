On Fri, 8 Nov 2002 Vwmike78_at_aol_dot_com wrote:

> To continue this topic, I am about to install either one or both new CV
> joints on my '78 Westy. One side is in desperate need of replacing (making
> lots of clunking noise), while the other has its boots intact and is not
> (yet) giving me any trouble. I have ordered two *new* axles with CV joints
> already installed. Should I swap both axles out or just replace the offending
> side (and carry the new one as a spare)?

I don't think that you should swap out the good axle.  Life of CV joints
varies widely, and the failure of one does not mean the others will fail.
I recently replaced an original joint on my '71 which went 270,000 miles
and was still quiet even though it was galling badly.  I just watch the
boots, and when one goes bad inspect the CVs while replacing the boots.
Of course, this doesn't work well with the "replace the whole axle"
philosophy, so you'll have to take your own path.
