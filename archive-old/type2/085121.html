I wouldn't tackle the body without a lot of practice first.
Get some scrap sheet metal and try some butt welds, lap welds and spot welds
with you mig. I took welding in highschool but needed a lot of practice just
to get up to speed. Sheet metal is tricky and usually involves a lot of
individual tack welds rather than a bead. Another technique involves
drilling holes in an over lapping piece and filling the holes as spot welds.
I have two concurrent, ongoing projects right now that both require regular
welding so I easily justified the purchase of an inexpensive mig welder. I
already had a stick welder and learned less #1 while trying to use it for
sheet metal.
 
I recommend having. . .
 
-          Wire Welder - gasless is okay
-          Dremel Rotary Tool - These things are indispensable for real fine
finish grinding and cutting in tight places.
-          Angle Grinder  - You can get a cheapy  one at harbor tools for
less than $20. You will also need grinding wheels, cup brush, cutoff wheels
and flap disks.

through all of MetWiz's site at. . .
