Check the pulleys for any burrs or roughness on the surfaces where the belt
rides.  It's fairly easy to ding up a pulley when a wrench slips or a tool
gets dropped.  It doesn't take much of a defect to eat belts alive.

A well worn pulley that's operated a bit misaligned for a long time can wear
a sharp edge on the rim - sharp enough to cut your fingers like a knife.  If
the pulley alignment is disturbed, and a new belt installed, this sharp
flange can destroy belts.
