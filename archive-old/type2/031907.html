Tom:

On one end of the thermostat is a brass "nut" that screws into the 
thermostat.  One side of this "nut" is closed off, except for a small hole 
in the middle.  Unscrew this "nut" and pass the thermostat cable through 
this hole from the threaded side of the "nut" such that the small 
molded-in barrel on one end of the cable is captured inside the nut.  
Then screw the nut back on the thermostat. 

The thermosat basically "floats" inside the bracket, that is the 
thermostat and bracket are not attached one to the other.  The bracket 
attaches to the engine with the 2 small studs that are there for that 
purpose, trapping the thermostat inside.
