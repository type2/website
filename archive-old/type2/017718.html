I have done this job a couple of times.  They can be really hard to get
out.  Part of the problem seems to be that there is no room to swing a
really big hammer in there.  On one job, I wound up using the floor
jack, an appropriately sized socket and a 6" extension. I started with
the socket on the lip of the bushing, put the floor jack under it (I
believe I took the round thing off, and used just the flat part under
it).  Then jack up so the weight of the bus presses it upward.  When it
almost disappears, you can add the extension or another socket or two
and keep pushing.  Both bushings should come out the top.  I used a
similar technique to install them easily, then reamed them out to size.

HTH . . . YMMV,  let me know if it works for you !
