In the spirit of never assume anything, I went out to my bus, got out what I
believed to be the correct socket and popped off the rear hubcap to size it up
to insure it was the right one.  You need a 46mm or a 1 13/16".  I have a
Craftsman 1 13/16", which has served me well for over twenty years.  Don't get
the solid 3/4" breaker bar with the sliding head from Craftsman - they bend.  I
guess that Craftsman doesn't understand that a 3/4" breaker bar needs to be able
to handle the cheater bar that you slide over it (I use the handle from my
Craftsman floor jack that I bought at the same time as the socket).  The breaker
bar I do use is the 1/2" drive pivot head, which looks a lot wimpier than the
3/4", but has survived many operations, and if it breaks, after I wipe myself
off the floor, since I would have been standing on the end of the cheater bar
when it broke, I'll go to Sears, get a free replacement and buy the 3/4" version
at the same time.  The sliding head on the solid bars also is kind of clumsy.

Just my $.02,
good luck,
terry
