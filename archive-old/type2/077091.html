Adam,
Look at it this way, it's just like opening the throttle valve a little
more, which in turn will let more air in, which will deflect the AFM
flap and give more fuel.  As that valve closes, less air is allowed to
bypass the throttle valve, and the engine slows down.. but because it's
warm, it's able to run at the lower air setting.  The air that goes
through the aux air regulator and the air that go through the throttle
valve both come from the same place.

The aux air regulator is just like opening your idle screw when the
engine's cold.  But the Aux air regulator is a little more advanced that
it has a built in heater that closes itself after a given time/temp.  In
addition, there is the cold-start valve to give more gas at startup,
which also has it's own internal heater to turn it off at a certain
temp/time.

Lastly, there is a temperature sensor built inside the AFM and there is
also the temp sensor on the heads.  Now, these are both on the 76-79
models, so I'm guessing the same or similar with the later models.
