Thank's for reading.  Here is a free pointer to start

Well, I got the engine back in and all together, and
went to drain the oil. (It has not been run yet.) 
Brass shavings.  Lots of 'em. 

They are chunky stepped spirals, some tiny(50 pieces),
some the size of a  small ant (10 pieces), smooth on
one side and chunky-stepped on the other.  Under a
magnifying glass, one of them looks a bit like Sy
Sperling.  Then there's the  grandaddy, a piece about
1/2" long x 1/16" square. It looks like it might have
been from a collar, but I haven't seen a bearing up
close, so I don't know.

Other than low compression, the engine ran fine
before.  I didn't notice any odd noise, and other than
some small endplay movement in the flywheel crankshaft
bearing, I didn't notice any problems. Endplay was
.01", I got it down to .005". The bearing was a doing
a little in-out dance, but not alot.  The rod bearings
seemed O.k.

I no longer have the facility to tear the motor down
again, so I'm in a pinch.  But I don't want to court
disaster, either.
