A few years ago I had a CHT and an oil temp sender that replaces the plug in 
the pressure relief valve hooked up at the same time.  The sender for the CHT 
was attached under a head stud nut.  They read the exact same temp during all 
phases of engine warm-up cool-down.
The moral of my experience is: if you want to read the case temp use the 
sender that replaces the plug for the pressure relief valve and place your 
head temp sender under a head stud.  If you want to read oil temp use the dip 
stick sender (I'm not sure how well this will work on a type IV however) or 
thermally insulate your oil temp sender from the case.  For the CHT sender 
either place the sender under the spark plug (be sure you get a good seal) or 
some other location similar to the spark plug location, e.g. not under a head 
nut (the studs act as heat sinks) and not right on the exhaust port.
One more thing, VDO gauges are extremely inaccurate.  I just bought a 
replacement for my dip stick sender and when I checked its accuracy it was 
reading 30 degrees too low at 250 degrees.  I also just installed a CHT gauge 
in my sons bug a few weeks ago and when I checked it's accuracy it was 
reading 35 degrees too low at 350 degrees.  When I checked accuracy of the 
CHT gauge I installed on my '79 Adventurewagen it read 50 degrees too low at 
350 degrees.
