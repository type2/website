I have just cleaned the poptop quite succesfully, with liquid all
fabric bleach. I used the cheaper store brand from the local Tops
supermarket. You may use any brand, just be sure it has the warning on
the label, "Caution, contains hydrogen peroxide". The hydrogen peroxide
is the magic ingredient. Put the all fabric bleach in a spray bottle.I
wetted down the top, and scrubbed off with just water and a stiff scrub
brush first. Rinse, then spray liberaly with the all fabric bleach. It
will foam up. Let is sit a couple of minutes, scrub, and rinse (be sure
to rinse it off of the body too). Repeat. It took a little more elbow
grease than X-14 would have, but the all fabric bleach is a less
caustic cleaner. It's action is self limiting also. It will stop
foaming after a couple of minutes. I did 1/2 a side at a time (length
of a picinc bench I had to stand on). But I kept spaying on the all
fabric bleach on the whole side as it stopped foaming. Now the top
looks respectable again. And I didn't have to worry about damaging the
paint. X-14 has warnings not to use on painted surfaces.
