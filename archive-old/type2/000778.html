You may have a slave cylinder that's dying, or perhaps a bad seal in the
clutch master cylinder.  Check for a hydraulic fluid leak under the bus
where the slave cylinder lives.

When you start up in the morning after the bus has sat for a while, try
pumping the clutch pedal a few times before engaging a gear.  If that makes
a difference, the problem is definitely in the clutch's hydraulic system.

In that case, keep the fluid topped up, watch for leaks, remember to pump
the pedal before shifting, and plan for an overhaul of the clutch hydraulics
ASAP...
