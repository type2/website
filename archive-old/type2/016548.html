> Our buses don't have this feature, so, despite what the oil companies
> would like us to believe, increased octane won't improve performance.

        Actually, performance should decrease. Reason being that higher
octane just means that they put stuff in it (lead, whatever) to make it
harder to ignite -- so that it won't pre-ignite from higher compression
ratios -- so ounce for ounce, there is less intrinsic power in higher
octane. It might not be really noticable, of course, probably would have to
be measured in a lab, but there are more power in lower octane.
       Same thing in diesels -- #2 diesel fuel in significantly more
powerful than the more expensive #1, and you don't have to measure this in
any lab, it's easily seen.
