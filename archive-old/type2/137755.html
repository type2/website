Congratulations! 

The only point I see in moving the wire to the starter is because that
is where future mechanics / owners will expect it to be. Myself, I
always thought it was kind of indirect to have the current go to the
starter, and then from there go to the battery for charging. I guess
since the bus really runs off alternator current when running, it
creates a more direct route for the current to go more directly from
the alternator to the rest of the bus electricals.
