The 3 notch pulley is correct for a 1970 engine WITH a single unit vacuum advance.

The number on the case means nothing, except in 1971 it came in another vehicle. I believe the X on the end
means it was rebuilt?

Timing a stock engine must be done by the distributor that is in it.
If you go by the AE and want to use the 5 after figure, then you would need this dist. :

Bus & Pickup 1971 * 1600
Distributor: VW 211-905-205Q, Bosch 0231 167 055 or 211-905-205S, 0231 180 001 > 043-905-205D, 0231 176 033
Can Use: VW 043-905-205D, Bosch 0231 176 033, 113-905-205AH, 0231 167 053
Vacuum Can: 07 092
Timing Set At:: 5deg ATDC _at_ 800-950rpm w/strobe, vacuum hose(s) connected
Advance/Retard Range: Vacuum: 2-5deg Adv, 11-13deg Ret; Centrifugal: 12-16deg _at_ 2200rpm, 22-25deg _at_ 3800rpm

You will notice that it has vacuum advance and vacuum retard, hence it is a dual vacuum unit distrbutor.

If you have a single vacuum unit you should have this dist:

Bus & Pickup 1969-1970 * 1600
Distributor: VW 113-905-205T, Bosch 0231 137 035 or 036
Can Use: VW 113-905-205M, Bosch 0231 137 021
Replacement: VW 111-905-205AA, Bosch 0231 137 039
Vacuum Can: 07 024 (Replacement uses 1237 121 400)
Timing Set At:: 0deg TDC Static or _at_ 800-950rpm w/strobe w/vacuum hose disconnected and plugged
Advance/Retard Range: Vacuum (Advance Only): 17-19deg _at_ 1.3 In. Hg, 32-35deg _at_ 3.2 In. Hg

This is the correct 70 dist. But all it has is vacuum advance.

Look real close around the body of your distributor and try to find the numbers on it.
Another question: does it have a vacuum unit on it?
