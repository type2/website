Muir's advice on oil is a pretty dated. A single weight oil has some
advantages, but it is recommended to use only in a fairly narrow ambient
temperature range. Our owners manuals (late bays) allow both 30W and
20W50, but 20W50 covers a wider temp range. I would think 30W might be
good only in a place like Hawaii, where the ambient temps are really
pretty stable. There are some folks who will change oil weights on
demand prior to long trips or in response to unseasonable weather. This
allows them the advantages of single weight oil without getting outside
the temp limits for a given weight. If you don't have the owners manual
I can email you the oil data from my '77.
You can mix various weight oils of the same brand without any problem,
but avoid mixing different brands. If you switch brands, just change the
filter at the same time, that will get out most of the old oil out, and
what's left won't be significant. Use detergent oil. Unless you've been
going way out of your way to find non-detergent oil, most modern
available oils are detergent type.
BTW, I've been using Castrol for years on all my vehicles, but
apparently there was a recent problem with Castrol destroying the cam
lobes in T4 engines. This was suspected (not proven) by a reputable
engine shop. Better safe than sorry, I switched brands on my new
rebuild...the archives are full of stuff on oil.
