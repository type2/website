> which is next to (if not completely) impossible without a torque wrench--
> and when you're doing a clutch, "even" is more important in terms of warp-
> prevention than is "torqued to VW specs." You probably wouldn't hurt the
> clutch even if it was 50 lb-ft all the way around, but 51/52/61/55/53/49 is
> definitely going to screw up the works.

Considering that the pressure plate is screwed solidly to the massive
flywheel, I don't see how uneven torques can make any difference as long
as they stay tight.

Now, _while_ you are tightening them down, you can warp things.  Snug them
up until the springs start to compress, then go round and round and
tighten each bolt a quarter turn at a time.  When the pressure plate
bottoms against the flywheel then get out the torque wrench and tighten
away.
