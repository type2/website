Rebuild them yourself by all means. Just remember that everything must be
antiseptically clean and don't get any hydrocarbons in the works!  I like
using copious amounts of Girling Brake Rubber grease on all the rubber parts
and believe it's one of the reasons I have 20 years on the last rebuild and
the calipers are still free as can be. It prevents water from entering the
area beneath the dust seal and rusting the pistons. We get lot o' wet in the
NW.  I separate the caliper halves so I can scotch brite the cylinders,
clean and reassemble easily, but I've only ever found the caliper half seals
at the dealer.
