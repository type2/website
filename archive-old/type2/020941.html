This talk about bus engines only making it to 160K!  Mine will hopefully
make it to Everybus with 221,000 mi on never rebuilt status. 
I just pulled it to change a bad friction disk.
Well, after much cursing about the fill pipe being in the way (bent it
in with the ass end of a tie rod fork) , I got the 78 engine back in
after changing muffler, heater flapper boxes, clutch, pressure plate,
front/rear seals, trans seal, and setting endplay.  I was given a 4 ton
hoist that I tried to use above the upper hatch to lift the engine after
sliding it under the bus, but you still have to tip it up 5" to get the
hoist legs under- so it's just as easy to use 2x4 shims and a lever
under the heater boxes to tip it up to where the jack will roll under. 
I did use the hoist to lift the engine onto a work surface and lift it
back down.  This time I tied the board tied under the engine so it would
stay put as I centered the jack by tapping with a sledge. Worked fine;
tomorrow I hook everything back up.
