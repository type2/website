Bob Lee wrote:

> I was not aware until I had to troubleshoot them that
> the horn and the brake lights on the 68-71 series, at least,
> are linked. A while back the horn stopped working. I just
> thought that it had rusted up again. I would get around to
> fixing it eventually. Then, a few days ago, a very angry
> driver informed me that my brake lights did not work.
> I thanked him for letting me know.
>
> To fix the problem, I traced the wiring and found that
> the 12 volts that feeds the brake light switch on the
> master cylinder is the same 12 volts that powers the
> horn. After a few more minutes of poking around, I
> found that the wire had somehow slipped off the fuse
> box. I reconnected it and both the horn and the brake
> lights now work just fine.
>
> Moral: if your horn stops working, your brake lights
> might also be dead and you might make other drivers
> angry. Fix it soon.
