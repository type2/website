 I also ran a VW powered sawmill
>for many years -- only minimal cooling tin.
>And that, BTW, was like having your car sitting in one spot all day
>long, no airstream from forward movement like you do in a vehicle.

I suspect that the VW sawmill used a Type 1 industrial engine, not a 
Type 4 engine (the engine in question in the '79-'81 swap).  The Type 1 
does have the cooling fan in a location more conducive to this type of 
operation.  The Type 4 has the cooling fan located directly above the 
exhaust.  Not as good for stationary engines.  And the fact that there 
is no air flow invalidates this comparison also as air flow becomes a 
significant factor in why you need all the cooling tin!
