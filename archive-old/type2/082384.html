Hi John,

	From what I have pieced together, the .009 was originally favored as
a "performance upgrade" by the racing crowd.  Since most of these guys were
building engines that were far from stock, the stock dizzy wasn't
necessarily the perfect choice.  My research indicates that for instance the
vacuum signal from many large Webers is far from ideal for the stock dizzy.

	For streetability and extended engine life on a stock or near stock
engine, the vac advance is the way to go.  
This is another classic case where the VW  engineers would have rather saved
the money and kept things simple if it worked as well or better for the
engine they designed.  
	It seems nowadays that the .009 is used whenever people need a cheap
distributor that will get their engine to run well enough to get them from
point A to point B.  The fact that the .009 has no vacuum port attachments
(relies on mechanical advance only) makes it a simple and straightforward
install.   In my opinion this is the reason for being the choice for so many
shadetree mechanics over the years.  The .009 will get most aircooled
VWengines to run somewhat, even the Type IV's,
	The availability of cheap parts is also another reason that they
still enjoy popularity.  You would probably be hard pressed to find a FLAPS
without a cap, points and condenser for the .009 in any location.  Trying to
find these parts for a stock vacuum dizzy can be a much more trying, and
often more expensive process.

							Of course I could be
wrong...
