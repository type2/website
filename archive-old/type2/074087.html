Hold on,

The rear suspension consist of a few more parts. First there is the radius
arm that allows the wheel to travel in a vertical direction. This is
controlled and guided by the spring plate. The spring plate and the radius
keep the wheel bearing housing in the vertical plane. The only way that this
could get out of the vertical plane is if you slid into a curb "hard". The
spring plate is tension or holds the weight or suspends the bus via a
torsion bar. These bars can sag after a while and can be adjusted. Check you
Bentley manual for info on how to do this.
