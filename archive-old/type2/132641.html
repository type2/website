Hi Corbett,

Here's a pic of my Left dual solex. you can see the throttle cable 
running through the right of the pic. Unfortunately the smog hardware 
is blocking sight of the tin at the point where it passes through, but 
you can see that the hole is very close to the transmission.

http://www.everybus_dot_com/greg_potts/pictures/dscn1051.jpg

If the tin is in original condition there will be a socket on the back 
to keep the cable housing in place. Without that fitting in place the 
cable will not take long to  saw through the tinwork. AMHIK. The tin 
will also make a mess of the cable, and the whole works will eventually 
jam or break.

If the socket fitting is gone (Mine broke away from the tin) you can 
create a workable replacement using plastic plumbing fittings available 
at Home Depot.
