I have three thoughts on this ...

1) Anti-squeal compound - yet another great use for copper paste. Works
great behind all my brake pads and you should also never install a muffler,
manifold - or anything else were a bolt or stud gets both hot and rusty -
without it. Works great for rear brake adjuster stars, too.

2) Caliper grease - this is more of a theory than experience ... Some
calipers are of the floating type (my '80 Ford Fiesta had these): Pistons
are on one side of the rotor only and part of the caliper moves - both to
bring the non-piston-side pad to the rotor when you apply the brakes and to
compensate for wear. For this kind of caliper it is neccesary to keep the
two caliper parts moving freely. I would assume that caliper grease is meant
to facilitate this process. My Fiesta would always wear down the piston-side
pad a lot quicker than the other - precisely because the caliper part was
stuck.

3) There is a special tool for resetting pistons - never had one though.
