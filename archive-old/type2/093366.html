>Off the specific topic, but related to an earlier bus...........
>
>Just the other day, my friend Trey came over with a problem of his right 
>rear wheel being locked up on his '71 Westy.
>I pulled the hub and started to inspect, replacing minor items from my 
>emergency stash of  "brake box" bits and pieces that I keep with me for 
>roadside needs, etc.
>
>What I discovered on Trey's bus was.........
>The wheel cylinder piston has a wire ring going around an outer groove 
>that is machined into the piston.
>This wire ring has a "gap" in it, since it cannot be a continuous wire.
>It is "snapped" into place on the piston when assembly occurs.
>
>This wire ring "gap" had managed to route itself to where it was "open" in 
>perfect alignment with the slots in the piston where the brake shoe fits.
>The brake shoe would then push outward in fine form, but then "hang up" as 
>it was pushed outward, then not properly retract so as to come off the drum.
>It would remain pushed against the drum, locked (hung up) in improper 
>position, and continue to drag right on down the road.
>
>Conclusion note:
>The wire ring on the wheel cylinder piston must be oriented so that is 
>does not align it's open gap in the piston's brake shoe slotted area.
>The wire ring must be oriented so as to place it's "gap" off to the side 
>area of the solid metal area of the piston.
>Reference:
>Bentley, section Brakes and Wheels page 27
>Figure 8-8
