>Details please! I've been chasing charging demons since I got my bus. Last

You didn't say anything about voltage, as in what voltage you are seeing at the battery during charging.  Maybe you just didn't include this in your post, but I don't see how you can hope to diagnose your problem without spending a lot of time with the voltmeter.

The solution to your problem is probably as simple as swapping in a newer, solid-state regulator.  But I would hesitate to recommend that until I know what voltages you are seeing from your current system.  I assume you don't want to simply throw money at the problem.  :-)

>P.S. Thanks everyone for the input on the FRS radios. Seems to be the best bet
>is to buy the cheapest set I can find. Avoid GMRS.

You won't hear that from me.  GMRS radios have a longer range than FRS and the prices on them have dropped to where they are affordable.  In my experience, FRS in motor vehicles doesn't work as well as even a poorly set up CB.  GMRS radios can also communicate with FRS radios so I see no reason to consider the lower power FRS, unless you absolutely must have the lowest-priced option.

If you must have FRS, I've seen them for as little as $0/pair after rebate from places like Frys.
