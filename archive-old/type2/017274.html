9 November 1998
Olinda, Brazil

Greetings Pedro,
We have completed a loop of some of the sights of Venezuela and are now in
the process of working our way down the Northeast coast of Brazil.

Thank you for your suggestions of the sights to see in Venezuela. We saw most
of them and really enjoyed the variety and beauty of Venezuela.

 I thought the drive between Merida and Barinas was especially scenic. Much
more so than the drive into Merida from the opposite direction. We took the
cable car ride and walked between a couple of the stations through the trees.
Really nice but I think we were the only ones that did it. Nobody else on the
trail.

A tour group owner told us to go with Tomas Bernol to see Angel Falls. He is
a Peruvian Indian who has lived for 30 years next to the falls and knows them
better than about anyone else. The location of his overnight camps are in the
best locations. We could see Angel Falls from our hammocks.

Because it was the rainy season, we could take a boat up to the foot of the
falls. Very impressive, so much water shooting off the top of the falls. A
photograph does not do the falls justice. You could place two photographs of
waterfalls next to one another and they would look similar even though one
falls may be 3 times as high.

We also enjoyed the Gran Sabana area. Our favorite waterfalls were not the
highest or those with the most power or flow. Jasper Falls is only about 3
meters high and 50 meters wide but the creek bed is a sheet of red jasper --
absolutely beautiful in the sunlight. Another favorite formed a great swimming
hole under the falls.

The road between Venezuela and the Amazon River is now completely paved so we
had a lot easier trip than we anticipated.

We hope all is well with you and your family.
Wayne and Jean Andreen

I forgot to mention that we spent a couple nights at Henri Pettier National
Park. We really enjoyed the variety of birds. We even had hummingbirds perch
on our fingers as they fed from the feeder.
