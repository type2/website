personally I would pull the old head which can be done with engine still
in the bus but much easier with engine out. your call.

having just gone thru head hell what I would do is measure the old one if
you can, and the new one. if you don't have the skills or tools then for a
"Rough" quick and dirty job (I've had em last 5-8 years like that, but no
warranties either)

if the old head comes off and it has gaskets, I would use gaskets on the
new one.
if the old head has a step and the new one doesn't I would use a gasket.
if the old head has no gasket and looks like the new one I would put it
back on the same way,
if there is a shim under the cyl I would use a shim under the cyl.

unless you take the other head off you won't be able to balance the engine
anyway. the VW type 4 engine is pretty tough.

my opinon is getting the head torqued down exactly right is critical. so
if you don't have a torque wrench buy beg borrow or steal one(well skip
the steal part)
