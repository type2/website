Cheer up, my southern compadre. If all you need is an engine, there is 
no cheaper car in the world. I've seen engines, in running condition, 
selling for $200-$300... and sometimes they come with a battered bug to 
boot. The same engine fits in the beetle, so there are quite a few 
around. A friend of mine at work had his son-in-law's engine rebuilt. 
The engine had been run w/o oil, and threw a rod through the block. 
Since the trade-in was unbuildable, the whole sheebang cost him $875, 
and included installation (it would have been $825 w/ a rebuildable 
core).

I'm willing to bet that there are a couple folks here that have a spare 
engine or two lying around. With a bit of luck, you'll find one w/ all 
the sheet metal still attached. 

One bit of advice. Be sure you seal the engine when you put it in. Lots 
of folks plop a VW engine into the chasis, but scrimp on the extra 
time/money needed to put the tin and seals back into place. Your van was 
designed to create a positive pressure in the engine compartment. This 
is extremely valuable in keeping the engine cool.
