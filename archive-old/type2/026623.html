Condition of the Engine and My Analysis

	On tear down I found the following conditions and I want to see if I
am reading the engine's demise properly.

	The number 2 piston rod was totally disconnected from the
crankshaft.  Both rod nuts, the rod bearing insert and the lower part of the
rod were in the bottom of the case.  The rest of the rod was wedged in the
right half of the case casting where it broke a lifter and some to the
casting that retains the lifter.  I had to pry the rod out with a screw
driver.  Big and small pieces of magnesium were scattered throughout.  

	The rod itself and the crank are discolored (dark blue) which I
believe is from the excess heat that was generated by friction between the
rod and the crankshaft.  The rod insert bearing was significantly chewed and
scored and partially gone.

The number 2 piston barrel had pieces missing where the barrel meets the
case from the rod banging against it .  The pieces were found in the sump.

	All 4 barrels had metal shavings on top of the pistons, I did not
measure but I would say a tablespoon or so of fine (probably steel) shavings
in each.

	I suspect that the number two rod was not properly torque when
installed and that there was no 300 mile oil change.  The suspended
particles from piston/barrel wear in helped to plug the oil passages on the
number 2 rod which caused friction to overheat and eventually to loosen the
rods nuts and fly off.  I also suspect that the rod must have been knocking
for some time based on the discoloration and that the engine was driven
until catastrophic failure.

	Based on this information does my conclusion sound reasonable?  Are
there any other things that I should look for in this engine.  This engine
case is not rebuildable but I want to make sure I understand what happened
so I don't have the next engine suffer a similar fate.
