  Craig . . there is a post called "balancing the CR, long 'n short
of it" which leads to a couple others that get into details . .

 David Raistrick has up on his site . .  or archives 

 Basically it amounts to the fact that all these parts, including
the case have tolerances for the machinist to work to . .
Say plus or minus .005 inches . .

 You will have parts in spec even tho they are 5 mils short or 
5 mils long . . from one to the other tho, they can be .010" diff.

 So you have a deck face at the spigot opening, a rod length, 
a piston from pin to top, a jug length that could add up to .040"
(1 mm) from your shortest to your longest . .

 So the idea is to get these all labeled and put the long rod with 
the short piston and the long piston/rod in long hole of case and
juggle the jug in there too . .

 You shift these around until you have less than .005" difference.

 Normally you can achieve this without machining . .
