Or that the FI System is not sensing any spark at the ignition, or 
there's a hose off somewhere, or the cable on the back of the air flow 
meter is not connected properly.

The engine will start on the cold start valve and last a couple of 
seconds with any of the above scenarios.  the injectors will not fire as 
the computer is not sensing enough air coming through or a spark at the 
ignition.

>>It turns over, fires, runs for two seconds
>
>This sounds like an inadequate fuel pressure problem.  Check the fuel
>pressure relief valve that returns excess fuel to the tank.
>
>Or, the double relay - the circuitry that activates the electric fuel
>pump.
>
>The circuitry design is such that the fuel pump will run under two, and
>only two, circumstances - cranking and running.  It may be that your
>pump is running during cranking, putting sufficient pressure into the
>lines to start and run for a few seconds, but is failing to keep running
>once the engine starts and you stop cranking.
