Set your timing at zero and then do your advance tests. You will find them
right on spec.

> With the vacuum reconnected the timing is 20 BTDC _at_ 1600 RPM and 36 _at_
> 2000 RPM and 36BTDC. It tops out at 2200 RPM where it is 36 BTDC at
> 2200 RPM.
>
> Isn't this way too high?

No, when you add up all the advances together you will find it runs around
43 total. Usually the timing mark will be at the O of VOR, past the number
marks! This is the way it is designed. If it was a upright 1600, yes that is
too much. But not for a type4 engine.
This is one reason why a 009 is not a good choice for these engines = not
enough advance.


> The bus has very little power when I hit the accellerator. Could my

No.
Check your cylinder head temp sensor. An overrich engine will lack power.
