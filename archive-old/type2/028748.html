> are the extractors much better? give better performance, more
> power, better fuel economy? or is that just a salesman pitch?


A true "header" system can slightly improve performance and fuel economy.
But the extractor systems typcally sold are not true header systems.  The
additional performance and economy that they will provide (partcularly on a
stock engine) is negligable, and, in my opinion, more than offset by the
fact that they are typically louder and rust out _much_ sooner than a good
European stock exhaust system.  One possible exception is the '75-79 bus
system, which originally used a convoluted system of elbows and pipes and
may benefit somewhat from the simpler path of an extractor system.
