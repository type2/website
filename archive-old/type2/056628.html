You'd think so, but it doesn't work out that way.  In fact, the 
injectors all fire simultaneously and they fire once per crankshaft 
revolution.  Additionally, half the total amount of fuel needed for one 
firing of a cylinder is injected in each injection.  (This info comes 
from the Bosch FI book by Probst.)

But since the brain only gets a
>signal when the points close due to distrib. cam action, how does the 
ECU
>know which cylinder injector to ground?  

It doesn't, which is why it grounds them all at once :-)  -- the signal 
from the coil to the ECU is basically just an rpm signal to let the ECU 
know how often to trigger the injectors (once per crankshaft revolution)

 Since the fuel ring is under
>pressure and the injectors fire gas at each grounding, wouldn't
>simultaneous injection waste gas???
>                                        
Apparently not...I think that even at 900 rpm (idle) things are moving 
fast enough in there that grounding all the injectors simultaneously 
doesn't cause any problems as far as fuel delivery goes.  It must not, 
the thing works :-)
