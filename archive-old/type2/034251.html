I get about the same figures but for mainly city driving and imperial
gallons.  I haven't got so much data for summer driving though because I
tend to do at least one longer trip with each tankful, which pushes the
average up.

There are other factors which must come into play too, like increased use of
lights and heater, more effort charging the battery, turning the engine and
trans in cold oil, etc.

I don't think carbs will draw in more fuel if the air is cold - the amount
is mainly determined by the volume of air, is it not?

live in the UK.
