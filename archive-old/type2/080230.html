> Browsing recently through the electrical circuit diagrams, in my Robert 
> Bentley and Haynes manuals for the 1968~79 VW Type 2 and 1968~75 VW Type 4, 
> I noticed that the headlamp dimming-relay for the USA (and also Canadian 
> presumably) specification vehicles, only has 4 terminals.
> 
> In Great Britain and Europe, the headlamp dimmer-relay has an extra terminal 
> labelled 30, which is connected by a 2.5 mm2 cable, to an unfused battery 
> supply (same fusebox terminal as the red/white 6.0 mm2 cable from the 
> battery).
> 
> This enables we British to flash our headlamps, without first turning on the 
> headlamps, at the main dashboard lighting switch!
> 
> Formally, flashing headlamps has the same meaning as sounding the horn; 
> warning people of one's presence. Informally, it is used to acknowledge a 
> courtesy from other drivers and lorry drivers commonly flash their headlamps 
> to say it's okay to pull in front of them, following an overtaking 
> manouevre. Owners of air-cooled VWs, also do it in greeting to one another, 
> in Great Britain!
> 
> I discovered this difference, whilst sorting out a revised headlamp circuit, 
> for a fellow VW Type 2 listee, in California, USA. I devised the circuit 
> sometime ago, to enable the use of H4 100/55W, 100/80W, 130/90W or 160/100W 
> quartz halogen headlamp bulbs (both p43t and P45t flanges), on my British 
> specification 1973 VW 1600 Type 2, without suffering excessive voltage 
> losses or overloading the standard wiring cables, ignition switch, dashboard 
> lighting switch or headlamp dimming-relay.
> 
> The British & European specification, 5-terminal headlamp dimmer-relay, used 
> for 1971~79 VW Type 2, post-1967 VW Type 1 and post-1970 VW Types 3 & 4, has 
> VW Part No. 411 941 583B.
> 
> I have a few spares, if anyone wants to trade for some USA specification, 
> featherweight second-hand, air-cooled VW components, which I seek for my 
> 1973 Westfalia Continental.
