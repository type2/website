WRT the continuity from ground to the alternator.
This one stumped me as well.
It has to do with the diodes that are in the alternator and the tiny
amount of voltage in your multimeter.
If you check the alternator w/ the positive lead on the body of the
alternator (ground)  and the negative lead to the alternator cable, you
should get 0 continuity.
If you connect the positive multimeter lead to the alt cable, and the
negative multimeter lead to the body of the alternator, then you should
get a blip on the multimeter that will go to 0 continuity (may depend on
the strength of your multimeter?)  

This confused the hell out of me until I realized I was not consistantly
testing the alternator w/ the same leads to the same points.
