The best way to test for a bad wire is with the Ohm test.  If you have the
resistor end on your plug wire, you will have to add in for that.  BTW, I
have found a number of those ends loose where they screw onto the wires.

As far as checking the plug for not firing under extreme conditions, the
only check I know of is to replace with a plug that you know is working
well.  The ignition system must be checked and bad components replaced
regular.

An engine out of tune not only burns more fuel, but runs hotter and with
much less power.  Don't get cheap when replacing parts, you'll pay for it!!
