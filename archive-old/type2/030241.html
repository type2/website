Hylomar works well too,  Grease isn't such a good idea, since it aids the
gasket in slipping out of position.  BTDT.

After years of trying to find a good multi-use gasket, I finally came to
the conclusion that the stock cork rubber-impregnated gaskets work best if
you replace them every time.  They are cheap and I buy them by the dozen
at the local VWFLAPS.  FelPro also makes excellent rubber gaskets if you
want to experiment.

Of course, the best thing between the gasket and head is nothing.
