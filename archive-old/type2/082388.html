Hi Dan,

	There is a possibility that you may have lock mechanisms that are
not keyed alike, but my first inclination would be that your locks are just
mucked up.  Try spraying some WD-40 in them and give them a good wiggle.
When I got my bus none of the locks worked, but with a little bit of time
and patience they all work smooth as silk now.  Be sure to finish up with
some good quality graphite lock lubricant or Tri-Flow to keep them working
smoothly.  
	Give this approach a shot first, hopefully you won't have to go any
further.  If this doesn't do the trick there are a few other likely
possibilities.
	There is a decent chance that your key is a copy of a copy of a copy
(you get the idea), or is an original key that is simply so worn down that
it will not operate anything but the most often used lock cylinders.  Every
time you get in your bus to go for a drive you unlock the drivers side door
and turn the ignition key.  Now add up how many times your drivers side door
lock and ignition have been turned since 1971 (over 11,000 days x number of
trips taken) and you can easily see that there is plenty of opportunity for
wear and tear on your lock cylinders and keys.
	You can have a new key code cut to original specs by Steve Sandlin
from http://www.steveslockshop_dot_com.  He is a really nice guy and charges
fair prices, I can't recommend him highly enough to anyone in need of keys
for their classic VW.  Get some spares to keep with you in strategic
locations just in case you drop your keychain off the face of El Capitan or
something.
	Since you will be using your bus for climbing and camping, you want
to be sure that the sliding door lock works.  You can pull the handle by
undoing the screw on the inside and take the code off the shaft in less than
5 minutes.  This will at least afford you easy access to your gear and
sleeping quarters.
	If you are leaving your gear in the bus while you are away from it,
be sure to hide it as well as you can, no matter how good your door locks
are, a rock through a window will get a theif into your bus in about 2
seconds.   Expensive gear left in plain sight can make a target out of any
vehicle.  Pull your curtains closed when you are away from the bus to keep
would-be theives guessing...
