> > > Me too!  I took a BFA, (sculpture and art history mostly), from U. of Colo. ...ancient
> > > history now.  I'm doing the CVs on my Rabbit at the moment (one badly galled and
> > > will be replaced).  If you have the tools and the inclination, there are two ways to
> > > renew the boot.
> > >
> > > 1) Get the correct split boot kit which is available complete with grease, boot,
> > > bolts, nuts, instructions, and glue (I have one of these split boots on my Rabbit
> > > for the last 25k miles).  These let you replace the damaged boot without removing
> > > the driveshaft.  If there is grit, likely since the boot is not tight...
> > >
> > > 2) you'll want to take the axle off, remove the CVs (I'd do both once I had the axle
> > > off), clean/inspect them for wear/galling, regrease, and fit with new boots if
> > > required.  The original boots last well, and I reuse them if they aren't cracked or
> > > ripped.
> > >
> > > If you have a repair manual (Haynes or Bentley) and the tools, maybe a pair of
> > > jackstands this is a very doable operation. If not, a trustworthy mechanic is in
> > > order.  If he removes the shaft, removes the joints, cleans/inspects, repacks with
> > > grease, renew boots...easy 3 hours per side...shop rate say $40/hr....plus $10 ea.
> > > for good German boots...total around $150-$200 per side if the joints don't need
> > > replacement.  New joints: $100-$150/pair.
