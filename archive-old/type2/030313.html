> Ever smelled rat pee that has been inside a closed up vehicle for any

I don't know if it was rat pee, but I know what you are talking of.  And
that is without the risk of hantavirus in the SW of the U.S.  I am not
saying that keeping them out is not a good idea... just that I don't agree
on the ethylene glycol.  Use traps, use moth balls, use rat poison in
proper feeding stations, no problem.  

The best way of all is don't let it sit for long periods of time!  No pest
control system is going to work unattended for a year.  Get out there once
a week and do something (crank the engine, start it up, push it forward
and backward, shove a broom handle into the heater tube, etc.) and the
problem will not occur. Rodentia need peace and quiet... disturb them
frequently and they will move on.  Trap or bait them and it's really a
non-issue (tho these need weekly checking as well), but please use a
purpose-designed system, not a shotgun.
