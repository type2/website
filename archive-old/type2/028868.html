Some materials, called Diamagnits, do not have intrinsic magnetic dipoles,
(that is they are not paramagnetic), but dipole moments may be induced in
them by the action of an external magnetic field. If a sample of such a
material (screw driver) is placed in the nonuniform magnetic field near one
pole of a strong magnet(speaker magnet), a (very weak) magnetic force will
act on the sample (screw driver). Diamagnetism is a property of all
materials.

You can set up these dipoles and they will stay until you shack the out.
Just place the tip on a magnet pull it straight away and place the screw on
it. You could tap the tip on the magnet to help set up the dipole, but then
don't shack it.
