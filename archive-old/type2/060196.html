>Really!? Wow, that's good to know! <smile> Uh, can I get a second opinion

Here's a second opinion:  Whoever told you that normal was 125-158 was dead
wrong.  "Normal" is a matter of much debate but it is generally accepted
that your oil temperature should be at least 180 in order to lubricate the
engine properly, and temps up to 230 are not extreme.  I have seen 250 on
my bus in very hot conditions with no observed problems, although I start
to get nervous at that temperature.  If you're seeing 275, your engine is
running too hot!  My advice is to do what you have to do (slow down,
downshift, whatever) to keep it under 250.  With respect to Mr. Seaver's
statement, 25 more degrees is probably not going to kill your engine, at
least in the short term, but I would argue that 275 *is* extremely hot, and
I wouldn't ignore it.
