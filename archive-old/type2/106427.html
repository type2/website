On Thu, 10 Apr 2003, andy wrote:

> Im sorry Fay or Scott but you have seriously failed physics.
> The deal with a lever (fulcrum) is that you trade off distance moved and
> force needed. The work force x distance is still the same.
> A bigger tire moving at the same rpm  requires more power input.

Correct, but you also get more work output!

think of a tire as a lever extending from the axle to the ground.  If you
make the lever longer you will increase the torque required to get the
same force (that pushes the car)  This will require greater torque from
the engine.

However, you also need less RPM from the engine since the axle is turning
slower at the given speed.

The result is that you are pulling the same power from the engine.

If the engine runs cooler at lower RPMS, this is a good thing.
