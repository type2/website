Regarding the lack of full return: IIRC, the master
cylinder cannot physically restrain the actuating rod
from the pedal/booster when the rod returns
itself...so I think you have a mechanical problem with
the pedal, linkage, or booster. Lack of lube?
Broken/worn pedal spring? Bad booster?

Regarding the soft braking:

1.  I have a 77, but I doubt a 70 is much different...
I would expect to use several small bottles, or maybe
one big bottle of fluid.

2. I doubt the ramp would make much difference, but
generally you want the vehicle flat when bleeding. 
You might have trapped a bubble in the master cyl?

3. I would definitely try bleeding it again. Reference
the manual and make sure you do it in the correct
order.  Also, if you didn't the first time you should
have a helper to push the pedal...those one-man
brake-bleeder check valve thingies suck.

If that doesn't work, check out the master cyclinder.
(they are pretty cheap at the FLAPS).
