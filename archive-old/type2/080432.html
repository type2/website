Wonder about the VW Bus?

likely to either burst into flames or kill you in an accident b/c people 
ignored that the VW s were not properly maintained and not always owned by 
folks who were careful drivers. That means folks who did not wear seat 
belts, who were too cheap to properly care for their bus (repair worn or 
rusty safety equipment, bald tires, incorrect tires), or some were the 
stereotypical hippie with drug or alcohol problems without enough sense to 
be sober when they were moving down the road.

A question: for the times was the VW considered a "cheap" vehicle like the 
current run of Kia cars and SUVs? I've noticed locally that alot of the Kia 
cars aren't aging well due to neglect and abuse. They aren't being kept 
clean, many look like they have been run through the woods (dents and body 
damage) and This was the same back when the K-cars were roaming the roads. 
Folks didn't take care of them, they also had a rep for being a cheap 
quality automobile and this created a general opinion that they were junk 
and built with terrible quality.

Is this what has happened to the VW Bus?

For the record I feel safe in mine as long as the brake system (rubber 
brake lines included) is in good (recently new) condition and all the fuel 
lines are recently new (not 25 years old). I would also through in there a 
good inspection of the steering and the steering column coupler. This is 
just generic antique car inspection stuff.
