> You are very lucky you didn't destroy the engine.  But now you have found
> out that even with a Type 4 engine you still have to stop immediately and
> check when the gen light comes on, as several people on this list have had
> the fan bolts shear or the fan otherwise come loose as you experienced.
> 
> The fan doesn't have to be in any particular position for engine operation,
> but for timing it is important.  The fan should only seat fully one way.
> Behind the fan there is a fan hub which should have a little pin sticking
> out of it which seats in a hole on the back of the fan (or the fan has the
> pin and the hub has the hole, I can't remember).  Once you get that seated
> you're good to go.  If this pin has sheared off (seems likely if the fan
> loosened up) then it's anyone's guess.  You would have to find TDC manually
> (by removing spark plug for #1 and inserting a dowel to identify when the
> piston reaches TDC) and then you could reattach the fan based on that.
