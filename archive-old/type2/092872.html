The Type II bus sold in Mexico, which, most recently, had a water-cooled =
engine and a radiator on the front, cost about US $21,000 when I looked =
at one four years ago. It was manufactured in Brazil. VW de Mexico, =
unfortunately, no longer offers the Brazilian Type II bus, either in the =
water-cooled or air-cooled version. They now sell only the Eurovan, =
which comes from Germany, I think.

I imagine that there would be a healthy demand for gray market =
air-cooled Type II buses in Mexico now, as the Kombi is so popular down =
there as a collective taxi, delivery vehicle, etc. I don't know if =
they're available through the gray market there, though.

Any used vehicle in Mexico is expensive. The Mexicans drive them, patch =
em up and generally just drive them into the ground. If you were able to =
find a 25+ year old VW bus down there, it would probably be very =
expensive or completely worn out.
