with just advance.  I am ever thankful that I have that 36 inch fillister 
driver; works perfectly for tin screws and the long reach to the idle screw.

from a flea market vendor, with a 5/16 (equates to a 8mm)  small combination

phillips screws on my cooling tin, similar to the fillisters.  Man, it is so 
NICE to hear the 'crack' when those fillister screws give up their grip!!  
and brush with a wire brush and a dip in the phosphoric acid and they are 
ready to go back in...  (Yes, I have new cooling tin screws, but I only 
bought a small bag of 50 screws/washers last summer.)
