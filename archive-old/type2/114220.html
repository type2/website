Rob, Dave & The Rootin' Tootin' List:

Dave suggested using the POR Fireseal product, but had no experience with
it.  It's a great product, but my own personal experience using it in a vain
attempt to plug the more corroded heater box tin where it contacts the down
pipes on T4 heater boxes was a semi-dismal failure :-(  I believe that it is
best used in EXACTLY the manner proscribed by the manufacturers.  In spite
of following the directions to a "U", I came to the conclusion that when it
is laid on in anything other than very small quantities in a narrow crevass,
it dries and cures in a very uneven manner, which results in large chunks of
it breaking off and falling out.  What did remain was also not very
resistant to mechanical abrasion - a fault I suspect due to improper curing.

I had high hopes for it and after several seasons of playing around with it,
I called it quits and used carefully fitted pieces from a can of beans
installed with a stainless pop-rivet or two, and high temp copper sillycone
sealant for the lesser gaps.  Now THAT worked!  FRREEEEEEEP!
