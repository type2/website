Subject: Re: [T2] How do valves work?

On Sun, 2 Sep 2001, Mike Soultanian wrote:

> I understand the basic concept of the cam pushing the pushrod which then
> pushes the rocker and then pushes the valve, but how does the hydraulic
> part work on the hydro valves?  I know that there's the little cylinder
> that rides on the cam, but how does it maintain the proper clearances
> instead of having to manually adjust the valves with solid lifters?

the hydraulic part keeps the pushrod in contact with the cam all the time
so it doesn't "tap" everytime there is an upstroke.  they are preloaded to
match the spring rate of the valves so they "softly" open the valves as
the presure builds.
 
> > also, how do modern car valves work?  Are they hydraulic?  Why don't
> they need as much adjustment.

they are all hydraulic of one sort or another

VW hydraulic don't need adjusting either.
