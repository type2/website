Muir, author of the "idiot" book, has a pretty detailed procedure for
evaluating a vehicle and negotiating a purchase. For me, simply getting a bus
became more important than getting a "good" bus. I resolved to have no
regrets.

Broken down, crawling under the bus in a parking lot on Lincoln Blvd. in Santa
Monica, hours from home yesterday, I had no regrets, no second thoughts.

The bigger question comes before you get on the plane. Will you be able to get
back on the plane empty-handed if your "spider sense" starts to tingle--either
that or drive your new bus home [or part way home] with no regrets?

My own decision was based less on "will this bus be reliable" than, "can I
live with its faults?" Like a foster child, it's been full of surprises, but
so far I'm happy.
