Sounds like what I'm going through - but so far, no
breakage! (Knock on wood!) I am anxious to read about
your observations w/wo/WI. Once I get the bugs tweaked
out of the "new" Subey, I'd like to add WI to it.

So far my woes have been a mis-timed new cam belt from
the people I bought the engine from - not a major
setback cuz as soon as I tried to start it I knew what
the matter was! Problem is they have too-short bolts
for the tensioning device and after one
removal/replacement they require heli-coils. I got
that all worked out as a matter of course. Then I
managed to mis-time at the crank sprocket after
carefully aligning the two cam sprockets. My bad!
Still no go - could not remember attaching the ground
for the ECU and all the sensors. That was because I
didn't! Then a few leaks at solder joints - hey, I
could see light at the end of the tunnel and I got a
bit sloppy! Paid for it, I sure enough did, in the
long run! Got some cooling issues now, after road
testing. I suspect it has a 190 thermostat and it is
sticking. When it's steady, it runs about 196 degrees.
It will suddenly climb to 220 then go back down to
296-200, so I hope that is the problem. It sure fits
the symptoms.

Good luck on your engine. As you know, it's tough for
them to sit around for years like that and then
suddenly be put to work. No telling what the condition
was - ring gum-seized in groove and unable to flex
enough for something on the cylider wall? You'll
figure it out. May be nothing of the sort, but I
suspect that a ring-related injury is highly likely.
Did you dump Marvel Mystery oil in it and cycle it?
Doesn't matter now - does it! -BaH
