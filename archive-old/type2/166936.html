> Bob, on my engine that was the hole for the temperature sender when

> describe.  I don't know its history, but I do know at one time, on  
> some type IV engines, the head temperature sender was buried  
> underneath the tin and in the head near the #3 sparkplug....a  
> design that was fixed in later years.  Is it possible that your bus

It was a design hold-over from the 411 engines. The early 72-75 bus  
tin used the same tin and used a rubber plug on the 72-74 dual carb  
models.

The 75 bus head needed a temp sender mount and re-used the earlier  
design. Karl took some photos of the 75 head a while back:
