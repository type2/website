I am restoring a '69 Westy and while doing the electrical system I 
constantly had similar problems.  Each time I would shake my head and 
begin to trace the problem only to have the lights come back on without 
me doing anything.  As it turned out I had a couple of BAD fuses, but 
not BLOWN fuses.  The metal strips in the fuses were so old and brittle 
they would sometimes carry current and sometimes they woudn't.  They 
looked fine, but when I removed them the metal strips would crumble at a 
touch.  For a few bucks I replaced all the fuses regardless of how they 
looked.  Now my lights work very reliably and they are also much 
brighter.  This may not fix your problem, but definitely won't hurt.
