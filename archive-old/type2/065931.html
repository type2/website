AS far as I learned in my smog tech class, the purpose of a smog pump is 
to deliver extra oxygen to the exhaust track so that when you have excess 
fuel, or high CO content exhaust mixed with HC, the extra oxygen combines 
with the extra CO from the motor exhaust to make CO2 and combines with the 
HCs to make H20 and CO2. The smog pump is not merely to there to dilute, 
If this were the case, the gas analyzer would still pick up the same CO 
and HC becuase that is what they are tuned for. SMOG PUMPS can and DO 
work, but at the detriment of a slight loss in performance (added back 
pressure).  IF you have any doubts, there are some great books out on 
Emissions systems you could look it up in
