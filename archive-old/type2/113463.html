At 08:20 PM 05/11/2003, you wrote:
>One final question.  Currently, with a fuel pressure gauge connected to my 
>fuel rail with a T fitting, the pressure drops to zero within a few 
>seconds after the engine is turned off. My reading of the archives tells 
>me that this should not happen.  Some people report that pressure holds at 
>35 psi overnight!


That's correct. Either the regulator is leaking down or the check valve in 
the pump is leaking down or you have a fuel leak in a hose or an injector 
is jambed or sticking open. If I've got 25 lbs + after 10 minutes rest, I'm 
not concerned as long as it's not hose fittings weeping. It won't create 
running or starting issues. If you are smelling gas fumes, it's not the 
regulator or check valve, those pressure leaks remain in the system. If you 
do smell gas fumes, it's a hose or injector leaking to atmosphere from 
pressure loop.
