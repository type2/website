At 10:18 AM 10/12/98 -0700, Russell Barr wrote:
>
>I went outside and pulled the top off the right carb.  Is the main jet the
>one that is at an angle to the horizontal.  If so, then its a 175, and big
>enough to drop a basketball down =:O  No wonder its running so fat.  Can
>these jets be obtained at a FLAPS?  

No, that's the air correction jet.  The main jet is mounted horizontally in
the bottom of the carb's float bowl.  There is a large bolt-plug thing in
the side of the bowl that you need to remove, then you stick a flat blade
screwdriver in through the hole to loosen the jet.  Don't remove the large
bolt-plug till you drain the fuel out of the carb!  It's easiest to remove
the top of the carb first so you can see what you're doing.  There are 5
machine screws holding the top on.
