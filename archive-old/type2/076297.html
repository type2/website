STARTER TEST


Well, that depends on how you're hooking it up


The solenoid has two places to hook up to.=20


If you apply 12V to the spade connector and ground the starter housing, =
the gear should pop out and nothing else should happen.

If you apply 12V to the big stud that the battery cable was attached to =
and ground the starter housing, nothing should happen.

If you apply 12V to both the big stud and the spade connector and then =
ground the starter housing, the gear should pop out and the motor should =
turn.

Finally, if you apply 12V to the fat wire that goes from the solenoid =
into the starter proper and then ground the starter housing the motor =
should turn without the gear popping out.

If the third test doesn't cause the motor to turn, then the problem is =
either bad contacts in the solenoid or bad windings in the starter.  The =
fourth test checks the starter windings.

 George Lyle
