Come on, guys! Waving has way more to do with the culture in which you're
traveling than with the type of vehicle you drive. Granted, you may get a
wave simply because you're driving a vehicle the waver identifies with.
Rorty (some anal-retentive guy who studies social groups and is
nearly worshipped in academe) might say that you share that same
worldview.

But try this simple experiment. Drive through a busy area...or better yet,
go some place where people fear each other. Try waving. (In some parts of
LA County I understand, this should not be done in an unarmored vehicle.) 
Now drive out to "the country." Try waving. 

In many parts of Arkansas, waving is mandatory. It's considered rude if
you don't wave...at EVERYBODY! **I'm serious!** You can get away with the
one-finger (*index finger*, guys!) wave if you don't know the person, but
still, you MUST wave...and if you miss a chance to wave, you're sure to
feel guilty...or go to hell, or something like that.

I don't know about all this waving exclusively between bus lovers. Why not
wave at everyone? Can't we all just get along? ;) Joe
