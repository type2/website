More expensive is probably correct, there are more parts in the FI
system & many are carried only by a few vendors. Here's the good part,
the diagnostic equipment isn't expensive. I would consider a good
diagnostic set to include:

pressure gauge for fi (local FLAPS has it for $35)
vacuum gauge
VOM
Test light (which you can eliminate w/ the VOM but it's nice)

As you can see, it's not a whole lot of stuff. Really the only thing
listed that is in addition to diagnostic tools you would need for any
engine (I didn't list them here) would be the pressure gauge that
measures higher pressures. You should have all the other thing anyway.
