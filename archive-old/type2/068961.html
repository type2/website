> cylinders fire intermittently, but it whuffles out.  When it stops
> after trying to start, it sometimes produces a very disturbing

It sounds to me like it could be fuel, as opposed to ignition problems.
In the morning, look at all the vacuum lines carfully.  Feel along them
and tug at them gently to make sure they're intact and in place.  One
line that's given me problems from time to time (with symptoms _somewhat_
like what you're describing) is the line from the distibutor to the
*front* of the throttle body.  You have to stick your head all the way
to the front of the engine and sight down between the tin and the throttle
body to see where this line attaches.  I've had this one come loose out
on the freeway, with interesting results!
