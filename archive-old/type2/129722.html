Craig,
        What Phil suggests is quite correct, but there could be other factors.  
I parked an aircooled engine [in this case in a garden tractor] in a friend's 
barn over a winter.  In the springtime, at first it ran OK; then suffered your 
symptoms.  Pulling off the shrouding, I found that mama mouse had built a nest 
in the fins near one exhaust valve, where she gave birth to her litter.  
Without cooling air, the valve burned, became stuck in its valveguide.  Result: 
zero compression in that cylinder.

Other guys on this List may comment on valvetrain factors.  I'm thinking, for 
example, of the stud holding the rocker arm shaft.  If one stud pulls out, you 
may lose valve motion on one cylinder, yet have "enough" valve motion on the 
other cylinder to have some compression.

All the same, it is tear-down time.  Good opportunity to put the engine in 
first class condition...... Glenn
