VW used so many different timing mark configurations that they finally
started selling replacement pulleys
with only a TDC mark, no timing marks at all.  The only consistent mark on
any of the pulleys is the TDC mark
on the later style pulley, I guess after about '73 or '73. If you find a
concave mark on the rear lip of the pulley,
that is a TDC mark.  Anything else is suspect unless you can reference it to
the TDC mark.  If you're in doubt,
Locate TDC for #1 by feeling through the plug hole with a dial indicator,
positive stop device, or just by feeling with a screwdriver or something.
In all cases the timing at idle RPM is a function of the distributor in use,
not the engine itself.  If in doubt, set the
maximum advance at about 30 Degrees and let the idle advance fall wherever
it winds up.
