At 11:35 PM 10/15/98 -0700, Robert S. Williams, MD wrote:
>I just got my 1982 aircooled Vanagon Westie back from the "surgeon" after
>having a tranny transplant, new CV boots installed, and viton pushrod tube
>seals installed. Anyway, I was talking to the mechanic about the valve
>adjustments he made. The book says: "tighten till they touch then two turns
>beyond that" is the proper way to adjust the valves with hydraulic lifters.
>My mechanic claims that this is too much preload on the lifters and
>typically tightens them 1/2 to 1 turn beyond touching. The question to the
>List is:
>
>1) is this dangerous? 

No.

>2)What would be the effect of not tightening them 2 turns over the long
>run?

Probably nothing.  It's a subject of debate amongst mechanics.  Personally
I'd rather have too little preload than too much, because the consequences
of bottoming out a lifter (tight valves) are much worse than having all of
your preload go away (noisy rockers).  I would go with your mechanic's
recommendation.
