     What a "proper installation" consists of for some people on the
list is clearly different for others. Depends on whether you are doing a
"restoration" or just looking for a ride. My bus is ride. When I'm able
to get a more pristine bus from some nice place like Ariz. I'll do a
restoration, but for this one it's all a hack. You're making the same
arguement that others do about "if the VW engineers in their infinite
wisdom designed it that way, we should follow suit." So therefore,
putting extractors on our buses, Weber carbs, whatever, is bad, bad,
bad. Not! 
    I'll agree that a sealed up engine compartment is a good thing -- it
keeps all the slush, water from puddles, mud, leaves, and other crap
out. Or maybe it's real purpose is to help you float when you hit the
water -- which is what they used to say about the bugs. 8-) 
    But for engine longevity, I'll worry more about the essentials. My
last engine wasn't in a sealed compartment, and never got hot all last
Summer here in Mobile, AL -- had no problem at all with it until the fan
hub sheared the key and I got it out of time.
