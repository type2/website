goes back to my original contention that the only thing one needs to use

full contraction at room temps = dead or dying thermostat.  No calipers,
manuals or other implements of destruction required.  Replace it and
drive on...

Dave, Chris - I've got a dead gennie Wahler T4 thermostat I'd be willing

Wahler also makes the later bug w/ FI thermostats (as supplied by my VW
FLAPS) - IIRC the last time I examined one, I "think" the cover end of
it that carries the bolt is soldered down around the perimeter, but it
would probably make more sense to drill a hole for the refill.  One of
the stories I have heard floated at one time regarding the "dry up" of
gennie T4 thermostats was that the manufacturer had gone toes-up.  My VW
FLAPS had supplied Wahler T4 thermostats for years, so imagine my
surprise when the late bug thermostat shows up in a Wahler box :-0  And
why, oh why, did Wahler make the late T1 thermostats with a slightly
different thread pitch on the little stud holding down the extension?
If you remove the extension for use in a T4, the same little threaded
stud is there, but just don't try to fit the T4 cap nut on it :-/  Makes
no sense, T4 was around for years before the bug went FI, so why'd they
make the stud a different thread pitch?  Seems like it would have made
more sense to have made the threaded extension the same thread pitch as
the T4 thermostat, and not a new thermostat end piece...
