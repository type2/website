I had the same problem with my 71.  Got some regular screws, nuts and
washers from the hardware store - 6-32 or 8-32 IIRC.  Buy the stainless if
they have them.  The screws are inserted through the holes in the lens and
housing (maybe I had to drill holes in the housing - it's been a long time
and I don't remember), then through the holes in the body of the bus (I did
not drill out the holes in the body).  While holding a screwdriver in the
screw head, reach inside the engine compartment (you'll probably have to
remove the battery on the right side), slip on a washer and thread on a nut,
tighten the screw, and you're done.  Yeah, it's a bit of a pain, and it may
take two people if your arms are short, but how often will you need to do
this?
