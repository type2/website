Too small could  reduce the response time of the booster.  Much too large
would create a large vacuum accumulator which may cause engine problems
during rapid throttle changes. I would guess that you could get away with
slightly larger than stock. I don't have any hard data on this, other than
experience with hydrualic & pneumatic systems.
