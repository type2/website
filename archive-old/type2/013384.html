I am a new member of the list and have just recently
purchased a '69 Westy Hardtop in need of plenty of
TLC.

Now as I bought my rig for camping as opposed to it's
collectability, I'm looking to make some improvements
on the original design.  (Purists may hit "DELETE"
now.)

One of the many things I'd like to do is improve the
braking.  As someone once said, "A bus that won't go,
can't kill you.  A bus that can't stop, can."  or
words to that effect.  Thumbing through the June 2001
issue of "dunebuggy's and Hot Something-or-other's" I
noticed many ads for disc brake conversions.  I said
to myself "Self, how about 4 wheel disc brakes on the
Ol' Girl?"  But much to my dismay, all I could find
were kits for Bugs and Sand Cars, and most without
provisions for E-Brakes.

After a look in the library and a search of the
archives, I am left with a number of questions:

Does anyone make a four wheel disc brake conversion
for the bus?

Can a bug conversion kit be used?

What about a competition kit?  (one manufacturer
claims that their product will stop a Drag car from
150 mph, but will it stop a bus from 75 mph?)

If I can use a bug kit, will it fit bus 14" rims? or
can I use bug 15" rims and adjust tire size
accordingly?

Do I really want four wheel disc brakes, or should I
leave the rear brakes drum?

Any advice and counsel is appreciated...
