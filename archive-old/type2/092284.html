Well...

I own a '77 ASI Camper, an '82 Vanagon Diesel Westy and an '83.5 
Wasserboxer GL.  Each has its quirks, but each has its good points as 
well.  From the top:

'77; best off-road traction.  leaks oil like the Exxon Valdez. COLD in 
the winter (it snows here). Lousy interior layout. Rock solid 
dependable. Gets driven the most of all in the stable. A Westy of the 
same vintage has a better interior layout.

'82 Stinkenklankencampenwagen; 30+ mpg. Westy interior is the best! 
Cheap, common parts. sssllllooooowwwwwwwww, you'll never get a speeding 
ticket. Did I mention slow? Motor will run underwater if necessary. Oh 
yeah, 50 hp makes it a tad on the lethargic side. Good easy candidate 
for a gas engine swap.

'83 Volksmover; Horsepower!  Comfort. Fairly dependable. Head reseal on 
one side at 148k miles. Now has 220k on it. Some cooling system parts 
are NLA. Weak point on wasserboxers is the cooling system.  Gotta keep 
the grounds clean and religously change coolant annually.

Some folks swear by each, some swear at each.  IMHO, the least desirable 
is the luftboxer Vanagon.  Most desirable? A Diesel Westy with a 1.9NA 
(naturally aspirated) motor. (We'll get there eventually...)

I'm an AIRS member and I, like many others, am here to help.  If you 
show up with an E-van, we might have some problems, but '51-'91 I can 
and will help with and the Eurovan we can find 'professional' help for....
