If you mean the ones that have a ball with a flat on its face, you
really don't want to use them.  They are a bear to adjust, as the flat
is always getting cocked sideways, preventing the feeler from moving
into its proper place even if the feeler is thin enough to pass the
gap. I ended up having to loosen several adjusters each time to adjust
the valves.  Not a good situation at all.

Even the Berg adjusters can be a problem, as they alter the valve
geometry.  The spacers and odd angles introduced into the system can
cause unusual wear patterns in the parts.

My personal preference is to stick with the stock adjusters and
replace them once in a while if they get flattened.  Unless you are
running a rapidly-opening cam or extra-heavy valve springs you don't
need this type of adjuster.  After my experience I went back to stock
and have been happy ever since.
