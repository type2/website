OK,

Compression ratios are a tricky thing.....I have my opinions, just like
everyone. I can say that I HAVE HAD, AND WILL CONTINUE TO HAVE good luck
with Higher Compression ratios. A bus is a very different matter than most
applications, as it gets super hot under up hill loads.

I try to share as much info as possible about my experiences with CR as I
possibly can, without letting the total cat out of the bag, and giving every
thing I have learned up for the internet world to "Cut" and "Paste" into
their own armory of knowledge. I'm more than willing to help, knowledge does
not come easy. I will never write a "Blue Book" that will tell everyone how
and why to do everything, and that it is the only way, and that every
product I do not sell is basically inferior.

Using Higher CR also requires some camshaft changes, even with a stock
cam( lobe centers).Ignition timing must be set differently, and you must use
good gas all the time..I have built several low CR engines, and most of them
were a pain to tune, and they actually created as much heat from
inefficiency as the engines with more CR did, especially when being worked
hard.

I'm not saying that every bus on the road should go above 8:1, or that a
1776 can live on 10:1, merely that stock, or more CR can be run without
problems..if everything else in the engine meets the criteria for the CR
change.

I know, this is undoubtedly going to cause some sense of "flame" as my view
is on the verge on defying conventional wisdom, it is what I have found, and
what I believe in.......My experiences with both low and high CR have led me
to a happy medium, of around 7.7:1 on virtually everything..And yes, several
stock 1600 engines had a 7.7:1 rating, from the factory.

With our engine failure rate of 3% it cannot harm very much..
