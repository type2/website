Please stop swishing gasoline around in there. Don't play with gas, it is
not a good tool, it is incredibly dangerous and unwholesome, it is not a
good solvent, it does not dissolve rust, it has no redeeming qualities at
all come to think of it. If not for our bovine idiocy we should be running
on propane or vegetable oil.

If it were me, and the tank was still in the bus, I would bung up the
outlet with some sort of cork or fid (see the Hoover CD for a delightful
essay on fids). Then I would slosh it out with detergent and water as best
I could by rocking the bus. Then I would drain it and immediately rinse
with water, then drain it and immediately rinse with about a gallon of
isopropyl alcohol (probably less than $10 at a local printers' supply),
which will dry it.

You could coat the inside with POR-15 or equivalent, but doing it with the
tank in by plugging the outlet would be an incredibly expensive project.
They sell that stuff by the ounce. I have never heard a report of POR-15
peeling off, I don't know what other coatings people put in gas tanks.

You could burn the rust out with acid, I would guess phosphoric or oxcalic
(sp?), if you had a mind to. This is probably the cheapest way to get the
inside clean, then it's probably good for another ten years. I expect the
detergent rinse would get you five years for a daily driver though.
