Darrin,
Sounds to me like your rear wheel bearings are knackered .In mine the hub
seems to have worn and so the bearing begins to turn wit the wheel. This
starts off as a chuff then a wscrape then a really bad creaking rumble.
It only makes this noise under load, so jacking it up is no use. This might
sound funny, but try adjusting your road position to alter ythe camber that
the wheels are at ( move closer to the kerb) If the noise decreases then
suspect the bearing. I had to drive a few miles in the gutter to make the
noise more bearable before I got to a a garage.
If i'm right and you replace the bearings make sure they are glued into
place. Otherwise before the year is out you'll be back to square one.
I also confused this problem with a transdmission one originally, because
the noise seemed worse on slowing down.

Hope this helps.
