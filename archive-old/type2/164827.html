Ray,

I am in the process of tracing down why this happens as well with my '68. I
have the remanufactured 9-pin relay and the wiring isn't the exactly the
same as the original and the instructions are for a '68 beetle. I'll forward
on a corrected wiring diagram to you if you need to replace the relay.

As for your specific issue, if the light is just lighting up and not
flashing, check the front turn signal bulb holders. One of mine popped loose
and was causing the exact problem which would also explain the popped fuse
if the hot wire touched the body. A little hockey tape fixed mine in place.

Also, the whole wiring system ('68/early'69) is a direct-to-body ground,
meaning there are no ground wires. Check that your housings are not corroded
at the hold-down screws as these are your grounds. If you have corrosion,
you can rivet/sheet metal screw a wire to the housing and run it to a clean
post you add in a similar way to the body.

Hope this helps,
