Well, folks, we're bus-less again.  :-(

Financial pressures dictated we had to sell Daisy, our '76 westy.  And,
realistically, I just haven't had the time to proceeed with my resto work
on her, what with my heavy work load, a two-year-old, and another due in a
couple months.  In a few years, Russell, our due-in-September one, and I
can find a bus to work on together; as it is now, Russell just wanders off
with wrenches, wants to bang a screwdriver against paint, or just gets
upset my attention isn't 100% focused on him.  Hey, life when you're two.

Christa and I agreed we needed the money and had to sell our bus.  We also
were resolute we wouldn't sell to just anyone-we had to find it a good home.

I'm happy to report I think we did find that good home in Shawn Soucy, who,
I hope, will be joining the list sometime soon.  He's new to VWs, but is an
ex-mechanic and was able to see Daisy for the solid bus she is, despite
being a little rough around the edges, and is keen to continue the project
work, doing a resto with an eye to registering her with "collector" plates
when she turns 25 years old in less than two years.  So I'm very lucky we
connected.  Must be all that good bus karma.

I think he'll show up at shows in the PNW, and may even go on parts-huntin'
road-trips down to California.  I hope he'll join this list, too.

Thanks for everyone's help over the years, and the encouragement Christa
and I have received from listees for our big road trips.  I'll still hang
around, I think, although I might have to drop down to digest mode.  And,
we promised ourselves, we'll be looking for another westy in a few years
when the kids are a little older and better able to help us work on the bus.

Happy camping, everyone, and see you all down the road!

Tobin
