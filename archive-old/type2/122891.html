Terry et al, The logistics of a PC and a laptop screen are fairly easy. Go
to the shop and get a cable made. In Oz it cost me 20 bux. My project was a
portable PC in an aluminium briefcase with an LCD in the lid.
When I wanted to Power it up in the van I used an uninterruptable power
supply, from an auction for 40 bux, replaced the dead battery with an
external one of large capacity, you should have one in the shed, and simply
plugged in the PC. Went like a real one. The real joy of the Power supply is
that as well as an inverter it has a charger for the battery, so plug it in
to the mains and charge up again. This works real well for the notebook. I
can  work for ages. So get a non useful supply for V little at an auction,
mount it somewhere and wire it in to your battery and your mains wiring in
the van . Bingo instant AC Volts. Barry Wright
