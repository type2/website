>You see it imprinted on any T4 case over 50k miles.  I doubt seriously
>though they will even catch your fingernail if you scrape it.  Fretting on 
>the
>T4 case is sort of subtle you see it on the faces of the webs and it
>starts where you no longer see the pattern of the grinding marks on
>the faces around the main through bolt holes.  They initially are sort
>of polished away and you will see the grinding marks adjacent in thin
>strips where the other case half doesn't touch.  Then they become a
>dull grey, and then the metal sort of gets rough.  In practicality as far
>as I can tell this coresponds say to 75k, 115k, 150k miles respectively.
>Over that the metal gets rougher and subtly "balled up looking."  What
>it means, well if you clean the case, put some cam bearings in and
>while plastigaging the cam, go and torque the case down without anything
>in the mains, chances are by about 100-125k you don't strictly meet the
>by the book spec.  On the T4 usually because of out of round.  But it
>won't be bad.  And as there is a lot of tolerance stack up between the
>size of the case, the bearings, the crank, you can have some loss of
>tolerances and still expect good life with good oil pressure.  If the
>case looks to the bad end of what I described and oil pressure was
>poor before tear down without extreme wear on the mains or rods
>on inspection, and you think the pump was OK, or if the thing has over
>200k on it, or if you know it is on its 3rd rebuild consider having it cut.
>$40-$50 at Rimco, work will be superb.  Not so far from you.  Also
>have the main galleys tapped for pipe plugs and the case pressure
>washed.  Peen whatever galleys you don't have tapped with a center
>punch in a circular pattern surrounding the factory aluminum plug but
>you should also replace the small one behind the fan area with a 1/8
>NPT plug.  If you know the thing is only on its 2nd rebuild or under 150k
>miles and it didn't suffer a real hot horrible end you can probably go
>ahead and rebuild it.  The T4 case isn't indestructable but it is pretty
>darn tough.  Align bore T4 bearing sets are getting sort of pricey BTW.
