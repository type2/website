> Dear List Friends,
>
> After reading all the posts about how terrible it is to install a push
> button for starting, and the resulting flames and anti-flames, I felt =
it
was
> time for a response from yours truly.
>
> After making a silly mistake with some under-dash wires, I fried my
ignition
> switch. Jiggling would get it to start but I couldn't find a place to =
make
> it keep running. Long story short, I decided to install a push button
system
> since I couldn't wait for a new stock switch to arrive by mail. First =
of
> all, I didn't have to "cut holes in my dashboard" since there were two
> factory holes covered only by the vinyl, though I did have to enlarge =
one
> slightly with the dremel.  They appearance is professional looking;
factory
> even. Only someone who KNOWS what a 74 T2 dash is supposed to look =
like
> would notice that something was different.
>
> Second, this isn't some "squeaky-cheep" work around. We're not talking
romex
> stuffed under the carpeting, here. I used quality switches, terminals, =
and
> wire. I used a fused, two-position pull switch for power and a heavy =
duty
> push button for starting; I soldered all connections (even the crimp
> terminals), covered all joints with shrink-tube insulation. I used the
> original wiring, only having to extend one or two to reach the push
button.
> One advantage to this system is that I now have an "Accessory" =
position on
> my ignition--something VW never saw fit to include. I also see it as
having
> some minor anti-theft properties since it isn't a common system and =
nobody
> can make it start from the key switch.
>
> Done correctly, I don't see why this is inferior to the factory =
system.
> Rather than being jury-rigged, it is simply a differently engineered
system
> which accomplishes the original builder's intent with reliability,
> durability, and a bit of style (if I do say so myself). Did anyone =
here
put
> in a new stock switch in June of this year? We can have a contest to =
see
> whose lasts longer. Chances are we'll both be far beyond the age of =
reason
> before either one of them fails.
>
> On a final note, I have to say that I understand and respect Dr. Tim's
> opinion on these things based on his status as a professional. My =
system
may
> give pause to the next professional  who works on it (doubtful that =
will
> happen but never say never), but anyone competent enough to be a
> professional could figure it out in five minutes with a multimeter and =
an
> open mind. In many regards, "stock ist best", especially in engine
> components, however when it comes to ordinary things like switches =
there
is
> nothing inherently superior in the VW system when compared to other
quality
> systems that accomplish the same function.  (Remember we're talking =
daily
