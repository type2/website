>Todd
>One of the things that the FI tank that the non-FI tank does not is 
>a small
>built in sump inside the tank.  This is needed in those tanks if you 
>want to get
>more than 11 gallons out of a 15 gallon tank.
>
>I used a FI tank in my DC.  The supply and return lines were too 
>small for my
>system, so I got some AN parts and welded a supply port just below 
>the sump and
>welded up the old return.  I then welded a new return port on the 
>top of the
>tank and above the sump.  I thought this would be good enough.
>
>I now have an early warning system (very irritating) when the tank 
>gets too low
>and I make a right turn.  In these tanks there is a lot of left to 
>right
>movement of the fuel.  Right turn and all the fuel is over on the 
>left side of
>the tank and away from the intake.  Left turn and you have all the 
>fuel you
>need.
>
>The reason for my welding ports rather than using the stock FI ports 
>is that I
>put a CIS FI system out of a '79 Volvo onto my Type IV motor that's 
>sitting in
>the '60 Double Cab.  The CIS system has a bit higher pressure than 
>the
>L-Jetronic and I wanted good thread on connections rather than press 
>on hoses.
>
>Since you have already cleaned, etched and sealed the existing tank, 
>it sounds
>like your best bet would be to fabricate yourself your own sump and 
>return the
>fuel through one of the vent ports in the top.  That way you don't 
>have to mess
>up the nicely restored tank.
>
>I have heard of marine systems using the T-fitting for the return 
>line, but this
>concerns me (maybe needlessly).  I'll explain why.
>
>In the FI system you have the high pressure supply and lower 
>pressure return.
>Your system pressure is regulated at a set value.  With pressure 
>system, if you
>restrict the return line, the system pressure will go up.  Depending 
>upon the
>design of the FI system, this could either do no harm or cause 
>mixture problems
>like running too rich.
>
>My concern about returning the fuel at the bottom of the tank with a 
>T, is that
>with a full tank of gas, you have that weight of fuel providing 
>resistance to
>the return. As the tank empties, the resistance reduces.  Coming 
>back the top
>and there is no resistance regardless of tank level.  The twin tanks 
>on my Jag,
>both have the return lines at the top of the tank.
>
>Hope this helps
>
>Scott
