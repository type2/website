to

soon after start up. I
have heard no "funny" noises before , during or after this
smoke screen occurs... scares the hell out of the people

White smoke may indicate ingestion of brake fluid through
a leaky servo diaphragm.  Worth checking servo assistance
if you have
one fitted.  You should retain enough air for three or more
power assists
when the engine is off and you push the pedal immediately. 
If there are
less it could be the diaphragm.
Ø Bobby.

Pop out the exhaust may be a leaking connection in the

My guess is that brake fluid is getting into your intake 
through the vacuum line that is connected to your booster. 
I bet it happens 
when your bus is going uphill or accelerating hard (well,
*relatively* hard 
:-).  This is caused by a seal going out in the master
cylinder and the 
booster filling up with brake fluid - it's very common in
older vehicles.
-David

White smoke is, rule of thumb, excess raw fuel. If your 
carburated, look for a sunken float or stuck float valve or
a jet that has 
come loose. If your fuel injected, look for leaking
injectors. Pull a 
spark plug. Heavy carbon or wetness will confirm. Also
check for a sticking 
choke on carbs. That's just a guess from the info given. Do
submit to the 
list for other ideas.
