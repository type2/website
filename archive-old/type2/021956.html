Hi Volks,

Having thought about and discussed this idea of Barry's about coping 
with too-low garage doors:
> Let me see.  Combining your top two ideas, if you remove the 'junk'
> rubber from the wheels by pulling each side OVER the rim (so that the
> rubber tire sort of flops around OUTSIDE of the rims), you could scootch
> the 'bus' into the garage without damage to the floor.

I found it would not work: the inner sidewall would interfere with 
suspension parts, brake lines etc., probably resulting in damage to 
at least the latter -- sorry :-)
 
> On bare rims, you coud use pieces of plywood, two per wheel, and keep
> switching the plywood as you drive into the garage.  Remember, this is
> only going to be done once in/once out per year, and it might be fun!!

But why not, said my pal, attach the wood to the rims?  Cut some 
pieces of 2x4 to a length slightly more than the inner width of the 
rims, give them slanted ends, and hammer them between the rims' 
"sidewalls".  Repeat until it looks like enough of them.  Maybe grind 
a "channel" into the outer circumfence of this "wooden wheel" and 
fasten the pieces of wood with a string of wire.  Mount on bus.  
Drive into garage.

Don't give praise to me -- but I'll forward it to its proper 
addressee if I get some anyway.  Check it out, and let me know.  And 
if it works, maybe we ought to forward the idea to the vintage bus 
list?
