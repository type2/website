Let me set the record straight on the silicone and paint deterioration
around glass mounting rubber--.

It is a well known fact that Mack R model, U model, and DM model trucks
have a severe water retention problem around the windshield and rear
window area--- the very same problem VW Buses have--- and if not
attacked with some sort of permanent sealer they will rust up bad and
quick in these area's-- I've painted thousands of them, and seen this
happen.

A good Silicone is the only answer--

It does not have the capacity to eat into or deteriorate the paint--
it'll just lay on top and adhere to it and the rubber--

Now---there is a problem with silicone and new paint application--this
is a fact--
The Paint will "fisheye" any where the silicone is--

This is why upon the sealing of any glass area , you would want to take
mineral spirits and wipe the access silicone back to the edge of the
rubber moulding--and do a good job using copious amount of the mineral
spirits and a bunch of clean rags--

If there is big gaps in the rubber--either mask off to the edge of the
area you want to replace with the silicone--or get a new moulding and
silicone it into the window frame--

I've got trucks on the road that I painted 20 years ago--and the
windshield and back window area's have not had a          reoccurring
corrosion or rust problem in those area's, because I use a good 30 year
black silicone--

EVERY truck I paint gets the same treatment---I don't want it comng
back--

The silicone seal is not even noticeable--it blends in with the black
rubber well.  

The BEST time to do this job is either right after a vehicle is
painted--OR--right after a leak is noticed on a good existing paint
job--

Roofing tar is NOT the answer

 This is from a professional standpoint, not hearsay--
