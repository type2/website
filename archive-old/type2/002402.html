As a reply to Paul's question about Jasco. I think that's the stuff I used to
prep for painting Otis, http://members.home_dot_net/jplusby/ for the resto story. If
that's the stuff, it was not a replacement for POR-15 because it was basically a
phosphoric acid product, greenish and runny like water. Anyone else know this?
It was fine as a prep between sanding, and/or media blasting but certainly not a
paint itself. You'll notice that after sanding, even high humidity will start
the rust process unless you use something like this. I think I even used it to
wet sand in the final stages.
