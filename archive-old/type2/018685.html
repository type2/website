> To the very good advice from Jeff S. I would add this: get a copy of Ron
> Fournier's book on metal fabricating (there are two; one of them is
> co-authored by his wife Sue).
> 
> As you learn to weld, also try hammerforming with body tools and a shot
> bag. Don't expect your early work the be worth much; this takes some time
> and practice.
> 
> There are very few body panels on a bus that can not be hand-fabricated
> if you have the patience to learn. By the time you can turn out a good
> replacement panel, you'll be ready to move on to the bus itself. Steel
> isn't cheap, but it is way less expensive than proprietary repair panels.
Mark:
Actually, steel IS cheap. Just get an air chisel, the right bits, and a
few elderly American car hoods (bonnets to you UK guys and Ozzies) or
doors. Cut em up. This product- called "tin" in the scrap metal trade is
nearly valueless($30/ ton or so) and most any recycler would gladly sell
you  a hood for a few dollars. In fact, they'd probably zip off the skin
for you so you didn't have to handle the scrap framework. Just stay away
>from late model cars that use high strength steel(don't know the years
that this came in, sometime in the 80's I'd guess).
