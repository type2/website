Women should apply acetone to their nails, not their skin.  Yes, nails 
are technically skin, but much more densely crosslinked.  PS; superglue 
is good for temp. binding of a badly ripped nail. Try using acetone to 
remove grease or auto paint (it is found in lacquers and car acrylics) 
from your fingers and it will turn the skin white as it easily 
penetrates and removes skin oils AND hydration.  Years of use gave me a 
"work induced psoriasis" on my fingertips, where the skin was thickened 
and cracking as though it had no collagen to bind between the ridges.

bolts or pushing on screwdrivers.  Now, two years of religious 
non-solvent contact and minimal push pull on engine work my fingertips 
are fairly normal.  You should also avoid direct contact with gasoline, 
benzene, or tolulene, as they are also carcinogenic and can cause the 
same "psoriasis".
