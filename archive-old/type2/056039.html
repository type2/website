At 10:34 AM -0700 7/8/98, Jbmorris_at_ra.rockwell_dot_com wrote:
>I rebuilt the rear brakes in my 73 bus (including wheel cylinder, pads
>etc.) and also hooked up the power booster to the vacuum on my single weber
>carb setup.  STOPS better than ever !!......... However, it doesn't seem to
>want to idle.  The PO had plugged the hole in the manifold and left the
>brake booster disconnected.  Now that I have hooked this all back up the
>bus does not want to run right.  Does the vacuum have that much control in
>the performance? What literally happened inside the engine when I hooked up
>this vacuum ?  Do I need to just do a major tune up and re-adjust
>everything?

Sounds like you have a problem with the booster or the oneway valve in the
line to the booster.  Take out the oneway valve and check it - it's a small
plastic piece just in front of the engine (in front of the tin).  Air
should pass through one way (towards the engine) and not the other way
(towards the booster).
