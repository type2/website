Hi Jaybird,

	For this purpose, in general activated carbon is activated carbon.  There
are different size pellets and some claim higher pourosity, but for the most
part anything that is good enough to work in a fish tank should be okay for
your bus.  Stay away from the really small pellets and fine crushed carbon,
as they will tend to compact more and turn to dust than good extruded
pellets will.  I would also stay away from coconut shell based carbon, as it
is usually pretty brittle and dusty.  	In my saltwater reef tanks and discus
breeding tanks, I used to use Kent Marine pelletized carbon with very good
results.  It is less dusty and seems to absorb more organics than other
brands I have tried.  It is a bit spendy, but it is what I will be using
when I finally get everything back to stock (relatively speaking, I have a
'73 code CD engine in my '72) on Gretta.  Since you will only be replacing
it every 30,000 miles or so, the 15 bucks for the good carbon probably won't
kill you (isn't your engine and your planet worth the extra $0.0005 per
mile?)
