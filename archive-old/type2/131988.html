On 5/4/04 7:02 pm, "Jack Dinan" <jdinan_at_cox_dot_net> wrote:

> Are warpy washers the VW equivalent of ordinary lock washers?
> Are there studs / bolts on the engine where the use of warpy washers
> is mandated?
> Places where they should not be used?

Wavy washers are generally good for non-safety critical applications where
vibration isn't too high.  I prefer them to star or spring washers as they
don't gouge surfaces.  They are no good for high torque or where you want
the load to spread out over a large area.  Just about any M6 or M8 fastener
on an engine can use them.  Heads and the main case bolts need bigger, thick
washers to work properly.
