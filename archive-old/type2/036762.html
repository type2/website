This very thing happened to me while on a 5000 mile vacation this Christmas.
I was able to get it starting many times by whacking on the solenoid.
Sometimes it wouldn't turn the engine over no matter how much I whacked it.
On those occasions I'd push start the bus.  When I got back I replaced the
starter solenoid and it works almost all the time now.  And when it doesn't
I just sit and try and again and after the 4 or 5th turn it spins over.
This makes me think it's just a bad spot on my starter and when the solenoid
kicks it and kicks it it finally turns past this bad spot and takes off.

> that activates it. this is the smaller cylinder attached to the big
> motor part. When it gets hot it's sticking and so there's never
> any current getting to the actual motor.
>
>  Try cleaning the solenoid, rather than the motor. You may even be
> able to replace just the solenoid, depending on which year bus you
> have (after the mid-seventies they don't appear to be available
