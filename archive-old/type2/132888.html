its been a while since i've done this but IIRC you'll need to get that
rubber bump stop off before you attempt to remove the arm.  you'll have to
get the tension/weight off the torsion arm before removing the stop.

can you slide a small jack in there & push up on the torsion arm? or maybe
have a friend use a prybar to push it up so you can remove the rubber stop?
WARNING: watch your fingers!!!!!

once the rubber stop is off, you *should* be able to get the torsion arm off
with a prybar by pushing up & the pulling towards you. again, be careful,
there is alot of tension on that spring/torsion leaves!

& BTW the real hard part is getting it back on.
