Mike, brazing works fine, and it is at a lower temp than true gas
welding. I think it is a dying art in terms of panel welding because
migs, although operating at higher temps when the juice flows, have
instant on and off, unlike a torch, so unless you're reall good, the mig
still imparts less total heat than a brazing torch. 
I read a car mag article about sheet metal welding in which a hoary old
body man said he always uses brazing wire in his mig with reversed
polarity. I presume the brazing wire is silicon-bronze wire. This would
be the best of both worlds. Trouble is, i can never find it.
