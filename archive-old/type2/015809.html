At 06:15 PM 3/16/99 -0600, Timothy E.  Woenker wrote:
>
>The recent FART thread regarding type I vs type IV engines raises a
>recurring curiousity for me.  Various discussiuons in past months infer
>that engine cases are not all created equal.  From campfire experiences I
>know that the typical (German) type I engine case is cast of an aluminum
>alloy that contains a significant percentage of magnesium.  I've also heard
>talk of other engine cases (type IV and possibly the beetlemex type I)
>being cast entirely of aluminum.  Now I know that magnesium is often used
>to create lighter alloys.  Is there a knowledgeable type out there who can
>explain the differences and relative advantages/disadvantages of aluminum
>vs magnesium alloy engine cases???

I know that the all-aluminum type IV case does not "fatigue" like the
magnesium-alloy type I case.  So, the type IV case can be re-used almost
indefinately, as long as it's not damaged.  A type I case will eventually
"wear out".  This is what I gathered from reading Tom Wilson's book.  I
think aluminum has a higher melting point as well, that knowledge also
gleaned from campfire experiences.
