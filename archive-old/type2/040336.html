Forget the Mazda rotary in a Bus, it'll fit very very easily but it's not a
torquey motor down low (Revs to about 10,000rpm) and not the most economical
around.  They're fine in a light weight like a Bug and the ones I've seen
are real sleepers and go like a rocket, but in a bus, you'd do some serious
clutch slipping to get it off the mark.  Now the SVX motor is another thing
altogether, great engine tonnes of horsepower and a lot of torque down low
as well.  Not sure if you can flip the ring gear but I'd be concerned about
loading the transaxle backwards with a lot of power, aren't most
transaxles/gearboxes designed to cope with higher stresses running forward,
in saying that though a subaru box should cope Ok, after all I'm running a
VW box (heavily modified) in front of a 230hp V8 in my Bus and after 2years
of work it's still fine (can't say the same for the clutch though)
