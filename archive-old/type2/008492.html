Over & above the stock gauges of speed and fuel:

1. Oil pressure - probably most useful for knowing when engine is in
trouble! Below 10 psi, start worrying.
2. Oil temp - ease off after 220, get worried at 250
3. Cyl head temp - Useful for showing short-term temp rises, such as when
doing a quick burst on a motorway or climbing hills. Can get quite high
without damage but prolonged high readings show trouble. Say, maybe, 450??
4. Ammeter - v. useful for showing charge/battery system (I've not got one
though)
5. Voltmeter - also good for battery performance (get worried below, say, 10
volts).
6. Voltmeter on 2nd battery
7. Rev counter - for over-revving, when to change gear, etc. not completely
useful but something to amuse oneself with.
8. Clock - err, to tell the time?
9. Compass - distraction?
10. Fuel pressure??? Difficult to install in rear engine car
11. Vacuum - see 10, but probably quite useful

PS I've always wondered what to do with the controls if you have low oil
pressure; revving up gives higher rpm = higher pressure, but then higher
revs are going to wear engine more. Should you switch off? Idle? Do nothing?
Also, shows low oil level by pressure drop in corners - but then you should
check the dipstick!!!!!
