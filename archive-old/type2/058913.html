Hmmm... well, different cars, different experiences.  I had continuing
problems with the copper rings between the exhaust manifold and head.
After a couple of go-rounds with replacing them, I ran out of time once
and brought the job to a shop.  The mechanic told me that (a) the reason
that the rings failed and (b) the reason that the studs were so out of
line was the missing hangers.  According to him, after a period of time,
the weight of the exhaust system pulling on the hot manifolds warps them. 
With the holes no longer parallel, the manifold becomes difficult to
remove and install and since the faces of the ports are no longer at 90
degrees to the heads, the rings don't seal like they ought and eventually
fail. He refaced the ends of the manifolds and then bored the holes for
the studs slightly larger and all held for a long time thereafter.  I have
since had similar experiences on other vans, so I figure he knew what he
was doing.

Another responder, Karl Von Saltzen (aufever_at_hotmail_dot_com), wrote of cracks
forming in the manifold, which has not been my experience, but which I can
well believe. I, like him, have seen many broken and welded manifolds in
junk yards, just never thought this was why.  The only time I have had to
deal with a broken manifold, it was on a van that had been backed into a
tree *hard*, which explained the damage for me :-)
