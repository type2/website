Some people were trying to get 1/8" plywood, and I don't know if they had any
luck or not, but evidently, it's hard to get.  I'm sure having the woody roof
helps on those long cold winter drives though.

My camper "We Made It!" ('69) has the following camping setup:

Stove   -       A kerosene Coleman 2-burner
Fridge  -       A 1960's model Coleman ice chest
Sink    -       A 5 gallon pickle bucket and a water bottle
Potty   -       A hand shovel, a squeeze bottle of bleach water, and a roll of paper.
Bed     -       A 1960's model Coleman sleeping bag

I find this setup a LOT more convenient, practical and economical than most
campers I've seen.
