The correct answer is that I had the #2 and #4 wires reversed on the
distributor (connected them in 1-2-3-4 order instead of 1-4-3-2 order).
This caused the spark for those 2 cylinders to occur during the exhaust
stroke of the piston instead of the compression stroke while #1 and #3
were working perfectly.  Here's the listees that responded with the
exact cause:

Here are some of the other suggested causes - many of which I, too,
thought of and tested for:
     Carburetor synchronization problems.
     "Weber chipmunks are too small."
     Inconsistent compression ratios among cylinders.
     Intermittently bad spark plugs.
     Bad cam lobe for the intake on #2, #4.
     Cam gear not properly aligned with the crank gear.
     Bent pushrods.
     Intake manifold leaks.
     Intake manifold not lined up with ports in head.
     Forgot to connect the balance tube between the carbs.
     Forget to remove the rags stuffed in the #2 and #4 intake 
          ports while the carbs where off.
     TOO MANY BEERS while installing engine/carbs (too true).

A few respondents had the right idea, but hadn't deciphered my story
properly and/or didn't realize the significance of the non-firing
cylinders being the 2 opposed ones (#2 and #4).  This may have been my
fault for not being more clear.  If so, my apologies.

I spent 2 hours testing different things (mostly gaining the data that I
stated in the original problem) and had spent way too much time trying
to figure out how a single barrel carb on a y-shaped manifold could be
supplying fuel to one barrel and not the other.  

I finally puzzled it out in my head as I was drifting off to sleep
around midnight at which point (and much to my wife's annoyance) I lept
out of bed and ran outside in my skivvies to confirm my mistake.  I'm
much impressed that so many list members came up with the exact
solution.

Hope you all enjoyed this session of 'Stump the Mechanic' - I know I
did!  Anyone else got any puzzles they'd like to pose?  If I come up
with any more good ones, I'll let you all know.
