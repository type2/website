I am thinking you have a 72-73 tranmisison, due to the E designation, which
was instated in 1972 to rid of the 8 tooth pinion.
This is a 5.429 R/P, not 4.12, which was some to most 1967 buses. And 67-72
sedans.

If the trans actually has AH on it:
The capital letter which identifies this gear set is supposed to be stamped
with the serial number of the transaxle on the outside of the case. It is
also the same letter that Volkswagen uses on the end of the ring & pinion
part number to identify that gear ratio. Example: P/n 002 517 143 M

Then it is a bug trans from 67-72, with a 4.12 R and P, very similar to 1967
bus.
