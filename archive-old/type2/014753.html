This is a two fold problem. If you move the tire out away from the Bus nose,
it wi require that those bolts support all the weight of the tire and absorb
all the bouncing and bumps of the tire. Having the tire firm up against the
nose makes it so the mount just pulls it against the body and the body helps
because it only has to keep the tire from slipping straight down. A stock
steel wheel and tire is not light, it will pull out the bolts if you move it
out and allow it to bounce and move. 
One solution would be to leave it as you have it, but to use some clear
contact paper on the body where the tire touches it. That way the contact
paper will protect the paint, when it gets nasty looking, peel it off and
apply new.
The other way is what I am doing. Make a mount that attaches to the bumper,
that way the bumper supports the weight and the body is ancored to only for
stability. A Wet Westies member gave me one from a 73 and I am converting it
for my 71, plus moving the mount out so I can put the hub cap on.

My bus came with a barrel nut (a long nut, about 1.5 inches, allows another
bolt to be screwed into it) on the spare tire mount that was holding the
spare on, as well as 2 lug nuts. I have been meaning to figure out what
thread is on the other end of the barrel nut as it isn't the stock lug bolt
thread, since I want to do the same as you. I need 2 more to do a decent
job of mounting the tire, but this is low priority. If you knew the thread
and pitch of the lug bolt you should be able to find these barrel nuts
somewhere, I have seen people reference online fastener companies as
sources of stainless hardware, I'm sure they could get barrel nuts as well.
