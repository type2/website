You have me there!  The '71 filter has an internal thermostat that
controls the flap that shuts the hot air inlet (the elbow under the
snout) off when the intake temperature gets too high.  The themostat
is contained in the fatter part of the snout near the canister and can
easily be seen after you remove the top of the filter.

My thermostat has long since ceased to be, so I simply install and
remove the air duct with the seasons.  Be sure to plug the hole in the
tin when the duct is out.  I use an expanding rubber freeze plug from
the local flaps

On the '71 there are two connections on the air cleaner.  The one
under the snout goes to the oil breather (as you suspected)  The one
that comes off the side of the filter canister goes to the charcoal
canister attached to the roof on the right side of the engine bay. 
Another hose goes from the top right side of the fan shroud to the
charcoal canister.  A third hose goes from the charcoal canister to
the fuel tank.
