Go to www.bostonengine_dot_com there are some break in tips there.......

Not to knock the shop I hope they truly are great, but look and ask while
they are working if they are installing the thermostat and flaps.  There are
a lot of people that think this stuff is not needed...........if they say
no, insist they put it on...if you have to lie, say that you travel in the
North a lot in the winter to avoid arguments.  Seen a lot of work from shops
that you would never think would take off that stuff and do..........

An engine is only as good as it's weakest link.  Be certain that a new pilot
bearing was installed in the end of the crank, a new clutch pressure plate
was installed, a rear seal in the tranny was put in (unless your tranny was
rebuilt.  Also, if you have any kind of shift linkage issues, or just want
to rebuild the shift rod toor change the coupler...now is the
time..............also, you may want to look at the gas tank while the
engine is out........have it cleaned/coated if you want, but at least
replace the vent lines on top of the tank.

before I went NY to cal, I would really check the brakes...brake stuff is
fairly reasonably priced........

Hope this helps
