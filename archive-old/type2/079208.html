> Dave,
>
> I am sorry to say that my experience with the copper nuts was not a happy
> one.  I found and bought a box of what were labeled "copper exhaust nuts"
> and used a bunch of them over time.  These nuts *looked* like they were
> copper and had a slit cut in from the side and were crimped to make them
> locking.  When I came back to one of those systems a year or so later, the
> nuts were quite rusty and not the joy to remove I expected :-(  When I
> took a file to one of the nuts I had in the box, they turned out to be
> copper *clad* nuts and once the copper burned off, they rusted like
> the steel they were.  VW recommends copper nuts and I would say it was a
> good idea, but make sure that they are copper thru and thru!
>
> As I said in my previous post, I have had good luck with stainless nuts
> and bolts on the exhaust for my '80 Vanagon.   Metric stainless fittings
> are not easy to find, but worth the effort!
>
> If I could find them (so far, no luck), I would say the ultimate in
> corrosion resistance in the situation we are dealing with would be
> silicon-bronze nuts.  Alas, I have not seen thes in metric thread here in
> the states :-(((
