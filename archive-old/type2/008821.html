Said Steve Blackham: (to message below)
Why is a EGR filter required at all.  I have three other VWs (a1 & A2s) and
a Ford Bronco which have EGR valves but no filters on them.  Why is the
Type 2 so special.  I wonder if this is just profit item only and not
really necessary.  Wouldn't a direct tube  be satisfactory?

No expert here, just common sense.......Do your other cars feed the exhaust
to the air intake before the air filter or after, into the throttle, like
VW does?  Are the passage tubes of similar size?  Apparently one does not
want these gasses fed into the idle curcuit, so in VW it's shut off at idle
by the throttle linkage.  Considering how the heat riser on type I engines
plug up, carbon must be a factor....perhaps it's to prevent narrowing of
the piping BEFORE the exhaust gets past the throttle?  One doesn't "need"
it for the system to work, but it may be needed to keep it working optimally!
