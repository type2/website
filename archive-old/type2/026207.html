The dull red light syndrome is very common in 6 volt VWs and will eventually
lead to a host of problems -- all of them expensive (regulator, generator,
battery, etc) -- by making your charging system work harder.  After forking
out $150 for a new ignition switch for my Type 3 Ghia I decided to get
proactive.  The problem is with resistance in the electrical circuits.  It
usually boils down to poor connections on either the hot or the ground side
of the circuit.  The ground is easy to fix by cleaning the contact surfaces
-- but there are a lot of them to fix (all over the car).  A poor connection
on the hot side can many times be traced to a butt connector that you or the
P/O installed.  This method of repair creates problems especially on the fat
red wires.  The correct repair method is to braid the strands together and
then solder (sp.) the braid and then put shrink wrap over it.  To braid you
divide the strands in half (on each wire), twist 1 half to the other wire
half, then wrap the other halves in the opposite direction from the first
halves.
