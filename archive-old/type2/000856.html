People have been travelling thoughout the southwest and Mexico for
centuries without A/C.  VWs were made specifically without air
conditioning and therefore are excellent vehicles for this trip.  "modern"
vehicles are designed with A/C required to keep normal temps in their
poorly-vented passenger compartments.  Buses have lots of vents to keep
the air flowing!

For the kids, a couple of spray bottles and a cooler full of beverages
will make all the difference.  Keep everyone well hydrated.  Tint on the
windows and a roof rack with a shade (sheet of 1/4 plywood) will also keep
temps down.

Get in the habit of starting the day's travels early and take it easy in
the midday sun.  The habit of the "siesta" was born of necessity, not
sloth!

don't try to put too much driving into one day.  I've spent a lot of time
in Sonora, Mexico, and have watched many folks blasting through in their
quest to get to Mazatlan in a 1-week vacation.  four days later they are
back, looking frazzled, but with that Mazatlan T-shirt!  I think that I
had more fun staying put and enjoying the local flavor.
