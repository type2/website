At 2:29 AM -0500 10/17/00, Mike Dwyer wrote:
>White smoke is usually steam from condesation formed in the exhaust system as
>it cools.  Blue smoke is usually oil, and black smoke is gas (running rich.)

My guess is that brake fluid is getting into your intake through the vacuum
line that is connected to your booster.  I bet it happens when your bus is
going uphill or accelerating hard (well, *relatively* hard :-).  This is
caused by a seal going out in the master cylinder and the booster filling
up with brake fluid - it's very common in older vehicles.
