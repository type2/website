I'll offer the following with the standard "this is what I did but follow my
example at your own risk" disclaimer.

I have a '79 Westy that I've used as a daily driver for about 12 years, so
it rarely sits for long.  But if I either a) leave it for a week or more, or
b) park it on a steep hill overnight, it will sometimes make the loud valve
noise for several minutes on starting up.  I believe (someone correct me if
I'm wrong) the oil has drained out of the hydrolic valves and it sometimes
takes them a while to refill.  Is this bad for the valves?  Probably a bit,
but the occasional occurance doesn't seem to have done mine any serious
harm.  So depending on how you feel about risk taking, you might try running
the engine a few minutes before tearing it down.

Good luck!
