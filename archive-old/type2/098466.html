Muriatic acid is another name for hydrochloric acid, which is another name
for the acid that it in your stomach right now.  While I wouldn't pour
undiluted muriatic on the ground it wouldn't mean the end of life as we
know it.  It would buffer with the minerals in the soil and pretty much be
neutralized with time and rain.  After all, we do pour the stuff into
swimming pools and then jump in ourselves!

To me, the objectionable stuff would be the gas tank leavings  That stuff
is petrolum-based and will probably be around longer.

If your community has "hazardous waste roundups" this is the place to take
this stuff.  Make the haz waste guy's job easier by marking the container
with its contents ("Muriatic acid + petroleum residue").

If you have to store the stuff for a while before disposal, you can make
it safer by adding baking soda to the acid to neutralize it.
