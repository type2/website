>
> Been there, done that! Twice! Don't even think about reusing the piston. Odds are it's out
> of shape in more ways than just the gouge. Depending on the miles on your engine, I would
> consider purchasing new pistons and cylinders. I know it seems like a lot of money, but
> consider that the P&Cs' are the lungs of your engine. If you must remove the heads you might
> as well replace the pistons and cylinders. Once the heads are off you don't even have to
> remove any more nuts or bolts to get the P&Cs off. The condition of the rods and crank can
> be checked without splitting the case. But you must remove the P&Cs to inspect the crank and
> rods. In reality you will not be able to tell if the crank is bent but if the crank can be
> rotated smoothly with no binding, things are likely OK. After removing the P&Cs you can
> remove the rods and inspect the journals and bearings. Probably best to replace the rod
> bearings even if they look ok. You don't get this deep in the engine very often and for a
> few bucks you might as well extend the life of your engine while your at it.
>
> >
> >                 4. If the head is trash, what are my options? Can I replace the one head
> > or do I
> >                need to do it in pairs? Are their different kinds of 1.7
> > heads?
> >
>
> Not real sure about the 1.7 heads. The trick is to find the part number on the head. It is
> on the rocker arm side of the head. May be covered in gunk if the inside of you engine is
> dirty. Find a good used head with the same part number as your dead head. (Oops, No pun
> intended) Some heads have a letter at the end of the part number. Pay attention to that! The
> letters refer to different revisions in the design of the head. If you can't find a head in
> useable or rebuildable condition With the same part number you should buy a set of heads.
> The performance and reliability of you engine depends on all 4 cylinders having the same
> compression ratio. Unmatched heads mean a short engine life. Check out a Bently or any other
> motor manual. Most of them describe in detail what you need to do to perform this repair
> without splitting the case.
> I hope my advice doesn't depress you but when a anything hits a piston you must be extremely
> careful to repair all the damage correctly or your efforts will not produce lasting results.
> Done right your engine should last thousands of happy miles!
