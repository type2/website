I don't agree you should buy all new pistons and cylinders. I've used

re-honed cylinders and new rings in high performance engines with no grief.

Sometimes I think people just like to get too anal about things. Make sure

it's a LIGHT rehoning to just break the glaze, that's all you have to do.

Too far and you'll end up with piston slap due to too much clearance. You

can even use some Wet and Dry 200 grit sandpaper and carefully remove the

glaze by hand, but make sure you do it as evenly as possible and not wear a

groove in the cylinder. Do it round and round, not up and down from top to

bottom. 45 deg. is even better. OR go buy a cheap hone from China and use it

to deglaze the cylinders, again at a 45 deg. angle in your drill.

Not everyone is Rockefeller, so used cylinders are fine as long as they're

not WAY to worn. Mike them with an inside micrometer. Have fun. :-)
