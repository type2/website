That's very interesting.  I used to work for TVA on environmental projects.  We worked closely with the Electric Power Research Institute in Palo Alto.  Cleaning up emissions at coal-fired plants.  Then I got transferred to the electric vehicle testing facility where my job was to drive battery powered VW Rabbits around a track and count the mileage that we got out of them.  The project was successful and the end result was that Chattanooga now has a fleet of electric-powered shuttle busses where tourists and anyone else can ride around downtown for free.  

But to have an electric powered bus would mean having an additional bus as it would be no good for long-distance trips like campouts.  And if everyone runs electric vehicles, that just means that the coal-fired power plants are going to be spewing out that much more CO2, particulates, etc.  It would be interesting to know the difference in the direct costs of gasoline powered versus indirect costs associated with electric vehicles.  Maybe it would be worth doing, maybe not.  The hybrids seem to make a lot more sense.  All power costs something to the environment.  If it could be done safely, we'd be better off with mini nuclear plants under our bench seats running our busses.  Ooops, there's that taboo 'N' word.  Cheers...KR
