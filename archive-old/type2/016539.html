Eric:
 Sorry to be the bearer of bad news, but it sounds like severe
mechanical  trauma inside your engine. Once you've assured yourself that
it's not valve gear related it's time to pull the motor. Don't despair,
not everything spent on the rebuild is wasted! The heads should be okay,
unless you've dropped a valve seat.
 My guesses are:
 1. Rod bearing. Cause- Debris in oil passages from inadequate cleaning
 during rebuild.
 2. Loose flywheel. Cause- Improper installation of shims or torque of
 bolts
 3. Melted pistons. Cause- Too high compression ratio, too much
lead(btdc) in timing, too lean mix caused by bad temp sensor in air flow
meter.
 Anyway it's probably something you did or didn't do, new motors don't
 fail after 2000 miles. You'll be surprised at how much faster you can
 assemble it the SECOND time! There's a learning curve here.
 Let us know what you find. It's well worth redoing- a late 2.0 fi loaf
 is a truely sweet thing when it's 100%, you can't go to the dealer and
 buy a new one! (even for $10,500)
