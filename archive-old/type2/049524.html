I figure it's better to be safe than sorry. Yes, change your fuel hoses
every year. Yes, run your fuel lines through a bulkhead fitting like Hoover
says to. And use a metal fuel filter bolted to the body so it won't shake
loose, double up your hose clamps, tap your brass fuel inlet pipe in
regularly--hell, thread the place it goes into and a pipe to fit, and screw
it in.

Given all that, remember that s*** still happens--fixed fire suppression
systems are still a good idea. If you have the thing 40 years and it never
discharges once, it was still a good investment.
