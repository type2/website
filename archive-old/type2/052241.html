Hey John,

I've got a '72 westy, and I just replaced the Front Wheel Bearing on the
right side.  Before I replaced it the noise was intermittent...then got
progressively worse...After changing out the bearings I took it for a spin
and everything seemed find...a little farther down the road though I heard
the noise again, more often when turning than going straight.  After pulling
off the hub again I inspected everything and it all looked fine.  How ever
the Nut had backed off a bit.  Jack up the car and tighten that down, then
barley back it off,  the book I have (I think ) said .03 -.05mm  Which is
pretty small...keep in mind that the washer technically might be able to
move, but with the grease, the guid slot on the spindle, and the pressure
from the tire it self it shouldn't be flopping around.  Then, and this is
the important part (and the reason the sound came back on mine I think)
Tighten down that Hex Bolt on the Nut Tight!  If you have a torque wrench
use it (can't remember the exact lbs needed)...but if not...you'll have to
wing it...but if it's not tight enough you'll reverse the nut off when you
back up the bus. and it will loosen on you...
