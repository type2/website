I did the same thing Bob,took the spare off the front,, added the emblem and had
good intentions to add the spare, and never did find a donut tire that would
fit, so I took a good donut that I got from another car, and redrilled the Vw
pattern inbetween the chev pattern, worked for me,,
I don't plan on driving at high speeds or hundres of miles on the homemade rim
http://www.globalserve_dot_net/~jrivers/spareVWtire.htm for pictures near the bottom

But , if I was adding my suggestion, which I plan to do,,, cut the center out of
a stock vw rim ,and weld it into the donut rim,,, 
I use a lathe to cut the centers out of both rims so that it is easy to weld in
and spin true!
The taper part that the wheel nut tightens to is very important, and the
homemade one is not a very good taper

[I switched centers plenty of time for OffRoad racing cars,, and they take quite
a pounding, high speed spinning too,,, and we balanced them, so that gives me
confidence in building my own spare]
