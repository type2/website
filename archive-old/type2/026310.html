> Well, alcohol still has a vapor pressure.  If the vapor pressure of
> the alcohol at a particular temperature is less than the pressure of
> the "failsafe" spring pressure divided by the thermostat area, then
> the thermostat will remain closed.  If the pressure is the same, then
> the thermostat opens.
> 
> Note that it takes a certain amount of energy to boil the alcohol into
> vapor at constant temperature.  This is called the latent heat of
> vaporization.  If the temperature of the thermostat is raised to the
> boiling temperature and no additional heat is put in, the thermostat
> will not open.  A bit more heat will open it up.  The same applies to
> closing.  The thermostat will not close until a certain amount of heat
> is removed from the open thermostat.
> 
> The heat of vaporization provides a hysteresis loop that should allow
> for intermediate positions of the thermostat.  Since heat only flows
> from hot things to cooler things, the opening outlet temperature has
> to be a few degrees higher than the closing air outlet temperature. 
> If the air runs a bit warm, the thermostat opens a bit.  If the air
> cools off a bit then the thermostat closes a bit.
