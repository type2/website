Right Isaiah!

Also - does the 16 year old kid know when he's being setup or taken 
advantage of?

I'm 30 years old and worked as Military Police for 3 years, was enlisted 
for 6 years and am now a Police dept. radio dispatcher. Maybe it's the jobs 
I've had but there are some BAD people out there. Some of these BAD people 
will do what they do for no reason at all. I know at age 16 I was in no way 
prepared to deal with this type of person.

My advice - do what you think is right. You know your kid. Is he going 
alone? It's alot more fun with a pal along for the ride (or you, his 
father) and alot safer too. Somebody to share the experience with.

At about 16 or 17 a friend and I went to the Smokey Mountains for 4 days 
with our bikes (we were really into touring then) on the back of his Eagle 
(remember those?) Great fun and only 3 hours from home. We ate good, slept 
good, and most of all had more fun than I thought possible at the time.

Yes we could have met up with the wrong people there like anywhere else but 
we were close to home and could get family help if we had mechanical 
troubles (in that Eagle - SURELY not... <grin>). All done in the age before 
cell phones...

Just work up to it - for the bus and for your son.
