At 08:04 PM 11/22/98 -0700, Matthew Libby wrote:
>I want to install an oil filter on my 1968 bus with a 1600cc sp.  Whats
>the best way to do this?

In the past few weeks, you have had no bus, a '73 bus you wanted to put a 
1776 upright engine in, and now you have a 1968 with a 1600sp...

I am sure there are many people who would like to help you, but it looks 
as though you are "kicking tires".

If you have not bought the vehicle yet, it would be better if you said 
something like, "I am considering buying a 1968 bus with a 1600sp.  If I 
bought this bus, I would want to install an oil filter.  What is the best 
way to do this?"

Then someone (like me) might be willing to say that the best way would be 
to have the case machined for full-flow.  The engine should be disassembled 
to do this.  A threaded plug is put in the outlet of the oil pump.  Use a 
full flow oil pump cover, Gene Berg sells a nice one that has a 45 degree 
outlet that will clear the rear hanger for the '68-71 buses for about $35.  
The main oil galley on the case will have to be drilled & tapped to accept 
a fitting to return oil to the case from the oil filter.

The fittings are about $8 a piece, SS covered hose is about $9 a foot, 
the filter mount is about $15, and Fram HP1 filters are about $9.

If you buy the bus and decide to add an oil filter, I would be happy to 
explain how to do the work step-by-step.
