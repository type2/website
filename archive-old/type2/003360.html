Had this problem with every VW I ever owned at one time or another.  The
villain is time.  Over time all those connections corrode and there are a
few.  Also, the ignition switch gets real hot when you are in the cranking
mode, especially when the cranking time is longer than a few seconds.  So
let's look at the picture from a few feet back and I will paint you my
solution.  Engine in rear of vehicle, battery in rear of vehicle, switch in
front of vehicle, 37 feet of wire running from back to front and back again,
numerous connections along the way and a steel chassis that has to carry the
ground (return path).  I ran a new ground cable directly to the starter
stud, lower, and the pig tail coming off the new ground terminal at the
battery got connected to the chassis.  I then installed a heavy duty push
button switch in the hole meant for the timer of an auxiliary heater and
by-passed the ignition switch.  If you run you hand behind the dash where
the light switch and rear window defroster switch are, you will feel these
holes in the steel so you just have to poke a hole through the plastic and
ream to fit the switch shaft.  Works great and when you stall the engine for
whatever reason, you simply push the button and away you go!
