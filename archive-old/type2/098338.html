I can only speak for the wasserboxer.  17mpg,
city, highway, uphill, downhill, loaded,
unloaded, 17 mpg.  US gallons so you'll have to
do the math.  

> and reliability of the vehicles. 

It depends on whether you can afford to replace
the 275 feet of hoses (the whole set is $500-700)
and, of course, the head gaskets every few years.
 Also the fuel injection is finicky. 

Other high failure items, the metal or plastic
coolant lines that run for and aft, the gas tank
will be rusted out on top, the rubber components
and filler neck will need replaced, the front and
rear heater blowers will need replaced, the rear
heater will leak.  Price this stuff prior to
buying a vanagon so you will know the total cost.
 

If I were buying a wasserboxer, and mind you I
cannot think of a reason I would, it would be a
2.1.  The hoses and fuel injection components for
these are somewhat cheaper.  Some of the 1.9
components are NLA.  Also, list member DArrell
Boehler makes a "digitool" to help you diagnosis
the various known fuel injection issues.
