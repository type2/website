Subject: Re: [T2] The Physics of Cooling

On Sat, 25 Oct 2003, librarian wrote:

> I have driven a bus with a cylinder head temp (CHT) gauge, an oil
> temperature (OT) gauge and an oil pressure (OP) gauge.  The CHT rises
> quickly with load and falls quickly when the load is removed.  OP follows
> it with significant damping and OT lags OP very significantly.  The lag
> between OP and OT may be a function of sensor placement.  The OP sender is
> located right after the pump and reacts almost instantly to changes in
> pump efficiency whereas the OT sender is located in the sump and has to
> wait until the cooler oil reaches and mixes with the reserve oil in the
> sump.

This is interesting.  Since nasty effects like stud pulling are a function
of CHT, it looks like driving by the oil temperature might yield a
situation where you could have the heads at dangerous temperatures before
the temp rise shows up on the oil temp gauge.  Looks like a CHT gauge is a
pretty important thing to have if you push the limits.
