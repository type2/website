     When you start hopping up a type 1 you have to consider all the other
increased stress loads. This is where balancing helps. I used to run an
1835 in my 66 in the 80's. I didn't have any guages on that bus but I put
75k on the motor before a caddilac  t-boned me at an intersection. I ran an
external oil cooler to keep the temps low, but I often wondered about head
temps. Perhaps others have had more experience with the type 1. I generally
stay as close to stock as is reasonable, which is why I went with the type
IV.
     How much $$ are you looking to spend on a motor? That will sort of
determine how far you can go with modifying a type 1. To go big in type 1
may be more costly then a stock type IV. But you will have to get the
mother in the bus which I have never done.
     If you build a bigger motor you should think of where you want the
power. I sometimes get this one mixed up but generally if you increase the
stroke you will get more tourque which equals better acceleration. If you
go bigger bore you will gain more top end. It sounds like you are looking
for highway cruising so I would look at bigger bore and maybe slightly
larger crank. Think about cams. If you go with big valve heads you may want
a higher lift cam to open those puppies up. Perhaps a higher ratio rocker
set up too. Get case savers and have the motor drilled out for full flow
oiling and  an external cooler. I am not a big fan of extra sumps because
it just gives you more oil to get cool. If you look in the archives I think
there are a number of worksheets for calculting displacement and then
determining the right cam. I would start with displacement then decide on
heads, a cam, carbs, exhaust. Hydraulic lifters may be the way to go but I
have heard people complain they can float at high rpms. I have never had
hydraulics so I couldnt say for sure. You may also be limited in the types
of cams available for hydraulic lifters.
     Maybe you should ask the vintage list guys. But surely there are some
of you guys out there running sooped up type 1's in your 68-71 Bays? Lets
hear about it!!
