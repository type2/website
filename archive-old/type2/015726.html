Hi,

When temperature starts going under 0 degrees Celsius I use to bypass the
external oil cooler. This is a type1 1776 engine. I belive the sandwich
type oil outlet can be taken away during the winter. It should be attached
with a bolt/tube. With this removed you just put the oil filter in place.
You can´T just pull the lines and plug them. The oil have to circulate from
the outlet to the inlet of the sandwich adapter if you leave it in place. 

The thermostat system works like the water cooled system and bypasses the
cooler while the oil is cold. This would save you  the messy
disconnecting/connecting in fall and spring.

I don´t know anything about what climate you live in but if I have my oil
cooler connected during the winter season the oil would never go up to
operating temperature.
