>Last year, a few weeks after I first got my '70 on the road, I was unable to
>turn it over one day after work.  I initially thought the starter was the
>problem but a new starter didn't help.  Like you, I noted that the lights
>worked and the battery was fully charged.  The problem, after much
>frustration, turned out to be a bad ground on the tranny.
>
>If you aren't getting gas, I would suspect that you have the lines hooked up
>to the pump backwards, or the pump is defective, or you have a pump rod from
>an alternator style pump (the base of the pump is shorter so the rod has to
>be shorter).
>
>As for the tranny being stuck in gear, are you the one who talked about
>leaving the shift lever in gear when attaching the linkage?  If so, I
>remember thinking at the time, that you should hook it up while everything
>is in neutral.  I'm thinking that your tranny is on one side of the H
>pattern and your shift lever is on the other and there isn't enough free
>play to allow it to get into neutral.  Other possibilities are that maybe
>your clutch cable isn't adjusted right or you have the wrong throwout
>bearing for the style of clutch plate you are using.
>
>I'm just trying to think of all the stupid things I've done over the years
>and maybe I can help you get one step closer to getting it on the road.
