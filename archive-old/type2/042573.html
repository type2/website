Hey Everyone,
This weekend we were camping in the local San Diego mountains and 
experienced a new problem with the '70 Westy.  When coming down the mountain 
,or on fast deceleration, the engine lets out a real loud backfire... POP!  
It only happened a few times, but it can't be a good thing.  Sounds like a 
hole in the muffler or loose exhaust connection. I took a look under the 
motor, but did not see anything obviously wrong.  No holes or loose clamps.  
I recently adjusted the valves and points.  It's been running just fine.  
This engine is a 1600 DP single solex carb with a header exhaust and a 
hide-out, tuck away muffler. I've had this setup for over 2 years with no 
problem.  Any suggestions on tracking this problem down would be 
appreciated.
