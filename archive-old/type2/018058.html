Paul,

It's not the voltage that is a concern (just 13.8 v max, BTW), it's the
amperage.  That red wire CAN carry the full output of the alternator at times
and consequently is sized appropriately.  I'm not sure, but it's probably at
least 10 awg.  Automotive wire is preferable in that it lives in an
environment of oil, fuel and heat.  The others are probably not more than 14
awg.  And lastly, the engine case SHOULD never get hot enough to melt the
insulation off the wires.  Abrade it?  Maybe.  But never melt it.
