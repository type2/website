David Schwarze wrote:
 
> I see the same thing with head temp, but I don't pay very much attention to
> CHT.  I am more concerned with oil temperature, since that's where I seem
> to get close to dangerous temperatures.  CHT just tells me when I need to
> drop down a gear, or in the extreme case it will let me know if the fan
> stops turning, as you are well aware.  But for staying "in touch" with the
> engine, I don't think CHT is that important.  Oil pressure, oil temp, and
> vacuum are much more useful.

  Yeah, I just got the oil temp gauge installed recently, haven't had an
engine that was in decent enough shape to really get familiar with it.
But I know oil temp is critical -- somewhere a long time ago I read
somewhere that anytime the oil temp goes over 200, the oil starts
breaking down. That's another reason why I have no comprehension of what
people are talking about when they say the flap missing would cause the
oil to be "over-cooled" --- that might be possible in frigid winter
temps, but any other time -- in a air-cooled engine, at least -- it
ain't possible. VW's are *always" going to have an oil temp over that
200 degree break down point, unless maybe with a very large external oil
cooler. I used to run an external cooler on the type I engines, but I
had thought that the type 4's were better that way -- after looking at
it, tho, and seeing the actual temps, I think I'm going to have to
invest in a external one.
