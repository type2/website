--- Mrten <mrten_at_dds.nl> wrote:
> George Lyle wrote:
> > 
> > The voltage (and therefore the power) delivered by the spark
> depends
> > upon the gap across which the spark jumps.  Normally, this gap is 
> > the one on the end of the spark plug.  When you create a larger
> gap, 
> > you force the system to provide a higher voltage, therefore the 
> > spark energy becomes greater, therefore your mixture ignites
> better.
> 
> That would imply that one could have bigger spark plug gaps than
> recommended for better ignition. It would burn up the distributor
> faster, though.
> 
> Would it be a sign that the spark plug needs adjustment? Or maybe a 
> thorough cleaning?

Bigger spark gaps do indeed give better ignition, but the gaps have to
be narrow enough to ensure that a spark will jump the gap in the
worst-case situation (low voltage, points at limit of adjustment, full
moon, etc).  Put in an ignition capable of more voltage reliably, and
you can increase your spark gap.

I doubt that the plugs need adjustment, but they might be worn or
dirty.  Pull one out for a look-see!
