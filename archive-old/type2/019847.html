[...]Both of my ACVWs exhibit the behavior described, and I usually (but not
always) find that the procedure given by George Lyle gives repeatable
results.  I think there may be something going on with surface tension  of the
oil.  If, after checking, I find the level to be low, when I expect it to be
higher, if possible I go away for a half hour and come back and check it
again.  If it's still low, I add a little.

Not sure I agree with not keeping the level at the full mark at all times.
While it could be (and often is) true that blowby is increased on a higher
mileage engine, that same engine probably needs all the cooling it can get,
and the proper oil level is bound to help.
