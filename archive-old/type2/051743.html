I taught my youngest brother to drive my 71 Westy while I had it.  The low
gearing was very forgiving to him and he drove it like a pro and actually
enjoyed it while I had it.  The best place I've learned to take a new
drivr out is in the local cemetery with its winding roads, simulated
intersections and on occasional car or traffic to deal with.  There is
also a reminder all around you what irresponsible driving can cause.

I tried all manner of methods with my sister to try and explain and teach
her how to drive stick shift.  It never took - she can do it but doesn't
enjoy it because it takes too much thinking. <SIGH>  The best thing that
worked for her though was my advice that said, "Listen to the engine.  It
talks to you and tells you what it needs."  Ever since then, she's managed
to never kill it ("IT" being the 74 Super.)
