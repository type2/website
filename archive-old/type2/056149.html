Gee Michael, sounds like a lot of stuff to use to drop one little VW engine.  I have been getting by for years with a hydralic bottle and floor jack.  The floor jack is an el cheapo 1 ton, pretty small in fact the both jacks take up very little space under the rear seat of my camper.  Since moving to Oregon I have had two outings where the engine had to come out.  The first was a burnt <hell, toasted> clutch on Elmo my 59'.  No sweat.  Albert my 69' Riveria ate a throw out bearing arm last summer as I was pulling into a campground.  Needless to say the camp host sorta freaked when he came by and saw the engine sticking out the back of the bus.  But I had explained to him that I had made all fluid lines longer than they needed to be.  Hence I didn't have to disconnet any fluid lines!!  That set his mind at ease and a new throw out bearing fork was soon installed.  In fact, it took longer to do the fork than remove and reinstall the engine!!
