Well boys and girls, it's not rocket science to do the math yourself
(it's more like hot-rodder science).
What we're dealing with here is finding the volume of 4 cylinders.
V= pi x radius(half the bore) squared x h(the stroke)x 4
A simple trick that works with 4 cylinder motors is to just use the bore
dimension  squared (which will be 4 times the radius squared) and then
don't multiply by 4; so for a 4 cylinder the displacement formula is:
v= pi x d (the bore) squared x h (the stroke)

The other handy thing to remember is to start with the kinda units you
want to finish with, i.e., want the result in cc's(centimeters cubed)
use dimensions in cm. going in, want the result in cubic inches, use
inches going in, want the  results in cubic miles, use miles going in
(warning-this will be an extremely small number).
On second thought I guess it IS rocket science- oops, lost another $100
million satellite! (I digress.)
The numbers for a stock 1600 are bore= 85.5mm (8.55cm)and stroke= 69mm
(6.9cm.)
displacement for a stock 1600 then is:
v= 3.14 x 8.55cm. x 8.55cm. x 6.9cm = 1583.8 cm cubed (cc.)
common type 4 motors: 1700 bore 9.0 cm. stroke 6.6 cm.
		      1800 bore 9.3 cm. stroke 6.6 cm.
		      2000 bore 9.4 cm. stroke 7.1 cm.
You can do the math yourself now! (I'm sure most could before!)
