Definately not.  If one head is having problems, I would bet the other

>Engine replacement?

>Rebuild?

I think you will probably be alright with replacement cylinder heads.  It's
a lot harder to mess up the bottom end of the motor, IMO.

>At the minumum it will probably be around 900.00 CDN for this job (assuming
>a cylinder head  at 400.00 and 9-10 hours labour).

>In retrospect the 1000.00 not spent on buying  a VW factory rebuild at
>installation two years ago  was clearly a false savings.

I've heard some bad things about VW of Canada's rebuilt engines.  When I
rebuilt my 2-liter it had generic, unmarked pistons in it which were
oversized.  I was told that VW of Canada often used such oddball parts in
their rebuilds.  Maybe it's no big deal, but I couldn't find new rings for
those pistons ANYWHERE!  Had to buy new stock-sized ones.

>I am going to go after GEX to help cover some of the expenses here. I am
>thinking poor quality workmanship is likely.

Likely?  Oh boy, I'd better not respond to that...

>It is possible that the valves
>are not seating properly due to  the seats having worked their way out but
>at 20k?

Don't think there is any way to know until you have it apart, but if I were
you I would order a pair of heads from Mark Stephens and have them put on.
Then you will likely have a reliable engine again.  Again, sorry to hear
about this...
