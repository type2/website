I thought it was 10W oil with a bunch longchain
polymer VII's, which, as have been noted earlier,
do not lubricate and shear easily.  I've read the
same thing in print a few places, but I was
confused by two points:  

1. Why do the owners manual, even owner's manuals
for cars built after the olden dayes, say my
Previa or late model Camry, show that 10W30 is
not recommended for temps as high as the temps
recommended for SAE30?  In fact, 20W50 is not
rated for temps as high as SAE30.  

2.  Also, why does my pressure gauge read higher
with SAE30 than 20W50?  (I wouldn't put 10W30 in
my VW, but I'm sure someone else can tell us if
their pressure drops as I expect.)  

I once read a few pages from a petroleum engineer
who explained the reasons in terms that I didn't
fully understand.  The gist of the topic was that
the way engine oils are weight rated is how fast
the oil flows at certain temperatures.  For this
purpose VII's can make thin base oil appear to be
thicker.  But that does not equate to the same
lubrication properties (as noted VI's do not
lubricate) or the same pressures.

guy.  :)
