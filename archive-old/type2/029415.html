If the cooling system is working properly, then there shouldn't be any
"forbidden" speeds.  If the system is deficient or if the engine is
modified, then this may be true.

VW engineered its engines to be usable in all operating modes likely to be
encounted by its customers.  An engine of low power output is expected to
be operated at or near full power for considerable periods of time, so it
must be designed to do so.

More powerful engines are not expected to operate at full power for long
periods of time so they can and do have limitations on how long you can
pull full power out of them.  This is one reason that you see so many
"modern" vehicles overheating when they are called upon to pull heavy
loads such as big travel trailers.  The engine can produce more heat than
their cooling systems can exhaust continuously, so the excess heat goes
into heating and boiling the liquid coolent. 

Our aircooled engines
draw upon a huge reservoir of fresh coolent (the atmosphere) so it's
unlikely that a properly-mantained stock VW engine will overheat.
