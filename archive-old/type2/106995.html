Interesting - I've seen that 1540 number quoted as gospel so many times on the list that I just assumed it was in print somewhere.  I am not one to believe in interpolation here as I've seen plenty of examples just in the last 2 hours of tires with the same load rating and different max pressure values.  In fact it looks to me as if max pressure is related to the style of tire rather than the load rating.  For instance, the Bridgestone Turanza LS-T lists max inflation pressure as 44psi across every size tire, from an 82-rated 175/70-13 to a 98-rated 215/65-16.

Looking at the information in the type2 Library under tires, I see the Michelin MX Reinforced in 185R14 listed as an appropriate choice for a bus tire.  It has a max load of 1540lbs and max psi of 40.  But elsewhere 50psi is listed as minimum spec for a bus (I assume this is from Bentley).  So what exactly does max inflation pressure tell us about how the tire behaves?  My guess is that it is only an indicator and not an absolute.  Non-reinforced tires with 35psi max would suggest a problem to me, but a 35 max psi alone would not.

As for load ratings, I think they are more straightforward than most people believe.  *I* believe they are the maximum static load that should be applied to the tire and nothing more.  Other factors such as the profile of the vehicle are compensated for by the reinforcement of the tire, not the load rating.

Note that in general as the aspect ratio of a tire increases, so does the load rating with all else being equal.  That means given a load range C tire in 195 width, a 70-series tire will have a higher load rating than a 65-series which will have a higher load rating than a 60-series.  This is in black and white on tirerack_dot_com (look at the link for "Specs" for a given tire).

My conclusions - a reinforced tire is key.  That means a load rating of C or D and/or the word "Reinforced" on the tire.  Max inflation pressure not critical.  Maximum load should never be exceeded and have some safety margin left over.

My current tires are load range C, 50psi max, 1520 max load at 50psi.  I keep them inflated to 40psi which means my actual load capacity is approximately 10% less or 1370lbs.  Do I lose sleep over this?  Hell no - why should I?  It's well above the weight of my heaviest wheel.
