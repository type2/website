Subject: Re: [T2] Engine vacuum

More importantly - is the needle on the vacuum gauge
steady or does it rock or have a blip? Those are
indications that one or more cylinders are not equal
to the others. That's the real problem.

Unlikely that any damage occurred from too-loose
preload. The ends of the valves and the adjusters take
a beating, but very unlikely a problem.

Pinch off any lines you don't want to plug - to test.
Do you still have a decel valve? It was a problem for
me, plus I liked the snappier reponse with it gone.
EGR valve? Still on? Hard to check without removing it
and blocking off the port. They usually end up
decaying at the bottom end and suck road grime into
the engine.

The hoses you clamped - Be sure to leave at least one
big one un-clamped so it can blow off in event of a
backfire. If it can't blow off relatively easily, real
damage can and will occur elsewhere! -BaH
