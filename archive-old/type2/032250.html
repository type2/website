> Those innocuous looking little pieces of tin are important.  I don't think
> you'll melt down your engine without them (at least not immediately), but
> it's worth the time and effort to put them back on.  What they do is close
> off the air flow from the heat exchangers BACK into the fan housing when the
> motor shut off.  That prevents all that hot air from rising back up into the
> engine compartment.

I don't really know the reason for these little bits, but I figured they
were there so that when the electric blower fan was on there would not be
a backwards flow of air into the fan housing.  The idea above also sounds
plausible, but I wounder how much of a probilem that heat flow would
be relative to the huge amount of heat which I would assume must rise
through the tin and etcetera.
