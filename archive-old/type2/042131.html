Subject: Re: Joining wires / soldering / crimping ?

At 15:41 7/2/00, George Lyle wrote:
> > Wires in a vibrating object like a car are generally of the 
> stranded type,
> > presumably because solid wires are prone to breaking. However when 
> you start
> > soldering them you actually turn them into a solid wire. The 
> soldered end
> > will become brittle and is a probable source of breakage. Actually the
> > crimp-on connectors are not so bad.
============================>
On vibrating objects (cars) use stranded wire.
Tin the very end of wire.
Cut the tinned portion to a desired length of just enough to make the 
connection.
Tin the connector.
Insert wire into connector and solder to connector.
Try not to "wick" the molten solder any further up the wire's length.
Come STRAIGHT out of the connector before bending the wire to the 
desired route.
