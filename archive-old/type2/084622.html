Have you ever seen one this nice for sale??? How much did they want??? If 
it's not worth $16,000 to someone, it won't sell. If it is, it will. Any 
original rubber on a 24 year old car can fail as soon as put back on the 
road. If the brake fluid is black, you know issues will be there. If it's 
clean, pay the price for such a rarity. You are likely never to see another 
24 year old vehicle with evidence of adequate continuous maintenance since 
new.

Condition of the CV's can tell a tale. They are all date stamped. Are they 
stamped a few months prior to the chassis date?? They're originals. Are the 
boots originals, and do they appear serviceable, no cracks, still supple, 
not swollen??? BIG points for rarity again. The environment has been good 
for the rubber.

Regardless, when considering paying a premium for a premium vehicle, a 
knowledgeable inspection is required. It wouldn't suprize me at all if some 
cash flush Yuppie didn't think 1/3 the price of a new Euro Westy would suit 
him just fine and buy it.
