> > RED DOESN'T GO TO NEGATIVE, RED GOES TO PLUS!!!!!!!  Beat that in your
> > head over and over and over!  And then one more time!
> >
> > I think it's probably safe to say that red is always positive, except in
> > some special wiring situations, but more often than not, it is positive!
> >
> > Don't hook it up the other way unless you want to FRY everything.  If
> > you have any radio's or other devices, those are prone to FRY too!!  My
> > friend fried his amp by hooking it up backwards.. that was a costly
> > mistake.
> >
> > The negative terminal should be hooked up to the ground wire.  That is
> > the wire that goes straight to the chassis.
> >
> > And when you hook up an extra jumper battery, you hook + to + and - to
> > -, not the other way.  By doing this, you put the batteries and parallel
> > and increase the amps available to the starter.
> >
> > I'd first hook your battery up the correct way.  Hook up the wiring
> > harness with all the red cables on it (and a big fat (probably black)
> > one that goes to the starter) to the positive(+) terminal and the other
> > braided one that goes to the chassis to negative (-).
> >
> > put in the new one, and test out the voltage at different points in the
> > car.  Fuse box is probably a good place.  You should have 12v on your
> > meter.  This is with the key off.
> >
> > Then try and see if the car will start.  If it does, go and test the
> > voltage again and it should be running at around 13.7 volts.  If it is,
> > your voltage regulator is still working correctly.  If it's higher, then
> > it's not working.  I don't know the reprocussions of having a bad
> > regulator so be careful..
> >
> > I don't how else you'd check your regulator, but I'm sure if I missed
> > something that someone else will chime in.
> >
> > good luck!
