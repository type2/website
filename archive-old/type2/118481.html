Any chance all that turning over, getting fuel but no spark, filled up one
or more cylinders with gasoline?  Pull all the plugs and try turning it over
again.  Absent that, it could be a separated valve head.  They can break off
without making all kinds of expen$ive noise.

The history of folks using easy-outs is a mixed bag, at best.  You don't
want to screw up on the crank.  The best way to do this is to drill *dead
center* with a succession of larger drills, preferably left-hand drills,
until you've just about got all the bolt gone, then reach for the easy-out.
But, unless you've got all the equipment to do exactly that, you'll have to
spend big bucks to buy left-hand drills and the proper sized easy-out;
probably better to take it to a machinist who will remove it for you.

The bolts on a TypeIV engine are M10.
