I use LPS #1. It drys grease-free and non-sticky and
it also displaces water. I just stick the spray tube
in the key slot and spray away - in the fall, when
we're liable to get rain followed by a freeze. It
amazes me ho much water gets in those door locks. I
also spray LPS1 on my door gaskets - hopefully before
any big freeze!

I don't like the banana smell of Tri-Flow and they
upset me years ago when the name of the company was
Tri-Flon. I sold it at my bicycle shop and I bought it
from my bicycle parts and components distributor who
was a licensed TriFlon distributor. I stopped selling
the product when I started receiving regular
phonecalls from the factory wanting to sell direct to
me for substantially less than the distributor could.
I told them that because they did not support their
distributor I would no longer sell their products. I
didn't like the banana smell anyway - I guess I
mentioned that...

When racing motcross (motorcycles now, not bicycles) I
used to demonstrate LPS 1 in this manner: I removed
the mag cover off one of the bikes and started it up.
I ran a garden hose into the mag as it was running.
The engine died instantly and would not restart. I
sprayed WD40 in there and on the points surfaces and
restarted the engine. Again I sprayed water in the mag
and the engine died instantly. I sprayed LPS1 in the
mag, restarted the engine and then sprayed the water
in the mag and the engine kept running!

Typical water-resistant treatment on the MX bike with
WD40 was every moto and sometimes that was not
adequate (MX in western Washington in winter!) LPS1
treatment lasted all winter.

I used it as chain lube for the BMX bikes also - when
we weren't using that banana smelling stuff. It's
great stuff but not readily available except at
industrial-oriented stores like the fleet oriented
FLAPS. -BaH
