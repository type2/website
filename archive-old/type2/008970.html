----SNIP---I used commercial-strength toilet bowl cleaner to eat
away the rust.  Don't let the toilet business fool you.
The basic chemical is phosphoric acid which comes under
a bewildering number of names.  It took about five
gallons to fill the tub and even then, some of the parts
had to be etched one end at a time.---SNIP----

I recommend sand blasting - much more friendly to mother earth - what do
you do with 5 gallons of a phosphoric acid liquid after cleaning parts? 
I don't even use that stuff on my toilet.  My vanagon tin came out great
after sand blasting - holds paint real well. Many welders or fabricators
offer sand blasting services.
