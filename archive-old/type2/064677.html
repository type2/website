>Ron, Nat, et al:
>
>What you have described is a fairly classic "bad coil" symptom.  Performs
>fine when cold, but once it warms up, the magic goes away.  Another
>possible cause is bad wires, but I would try another coil first.
>Seriously.  Other things to check include (but, as the warantee book says,
>are not limited to):
>
>Is there a bunch of side to side slop in the rotor shaft inside
>the distributor?  If so, get a new one.
>
>Are there carbon tracks inside the cap?  If so. a new cap and rotor are in
>order.
>
>The 1-3 and 2-4 wires are about the same length.  if you switch the 1 to 3
>and the 2 to 4, do the symptoms now manifest on different cylinders?  New
>Wires, mein freund.
>
>Given that you have installed a new (rebuilt) engine, I would have advised
>new spark plug wires, new plugs, new cap and rotor, and a new set of
>points as part of the job.  If you are still running the old ones, I would
>now advise adding a new coil to the list as pennance for your sins ;-)
>
>                           H Steven Dolan
>                           Alexandria, VA
>
>PS: (stop reading here if you like your "009" distributor)
>
>Sermonette (rant) on "009" Distributors:
>
>I HATE 009 distributors!  At their very NOS, OEM, Bosch stock, they suck.
>There is a flat spot a mile wide built right into those things from the
>factory.  And the SOTB (brazilian/mexican) repros have an advance curve
>straight from hell.
>
>They were never meant to be run on the street, only the race track.  They
>have a bit more advance over 4K RPM, which gives racers a tiny advantage,
>at the cost of rebuilding after every race.  To get that maximum advance,
>however, you need to advance the idle timing to a ridiculous degree.  You
>don't run over 4K RPM on a regular basis, you probably don't want to
>rebuild after every drive, and you probably spend way more time at idle in
>a week than a Formula V racer does in a year.  The stock vacuum controlled
>distributor will give you much better performance in the range you usually
>drive in and better acceleration too. Honest, believe me, Please!
