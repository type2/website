Needless to say, this is risky business. So I just want to make a few
points:

*Even a propane stove that is clean burning one minute, can be spewing out
CO the next - example: when the fuel gets low and/or the fuel canister gets
cold.

*Catalytic heaters are notorious for CO output when not running right.

*Ventilation may not be enough - if the wind is from the right direction if
may cause emissions to build up inside the vehicle.

With the advent of Carbon Monoxide alarms (I'm sure 12 volt models are
available at RV supplier - although I'll bet they're expensive), using one
if you are heating with gas would be a great idea.

Please note that I am not some tight-ass crumudgen that worries about every
little thing - I may use a propane heat in the future as well - but I do get
concerned that Rick's post will be used as a testimonial to the safety of
heating your bus this way by some impressionable folks.

For a couple of sad stories about families dying from CO poisoning *while
camping* visit:
http://www.cdc_dot_gov/epo/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/mm4832a1.htm
