Try using a really strong penetrant.  Something like PB Blaster (no
affiliation ;-)  WD40 probably won't work.
spray some on and tap the nut w/ a hand sledge then spray some more -
wait a few minutes - repeat.
You didn't say what you were using to remove the nut.  If you are using
a socket, get the recentl;y discussed axle nut striking tool.  If you
can't get one, then put a pipe on the end of the breaker bar to get more
leverage, while putting force on the bar, pop the back of the socket w/
the hand sledge. apply "bouncing"force on the breaker bar (not constant)
and pop the socket when you apply the force.  If this doesn't work, get
the striking tool.
If you have the striking tool - do the penetrant thing, then pop the
striking tool.  You can put a braker bar on the striking tool as well.

Also, look at the threads on the axle stub.  If I remeber right, on one
side the threads are reversed.

good luck
