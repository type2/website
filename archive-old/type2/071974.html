I don't have the factory specs, but I can tell you that the Vanagon engine
I took apart had about the same deck height - I measured it at .030".  I
was also concerned that this was not enough after reading Wilson's book,
and ended up putting .060" spacers under the jugs to bring my deck height
up to .090".  With my roughly 51cc heads, this gave me 6.8 to 1
compression.  After a while I removed the .060" spacers and replaced them
with .030" (deck height .060") for about 7.0 to 1 compression and felt a
slight increase in power.  I was going to remove the .030" spacers and run
with just the .030" deck height but I got some new heads with smaller
chambers so I think I will end up leaving the spacers there.  I guess what
I'm trying to say is that I think there is adequate clearance to run with
.030" deck height as long as you are running the stock cam, rockers, and
valves.  There, now you have two opinions.
