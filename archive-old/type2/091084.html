On Sun, 11 Aug 2002, Jim Kirk wrote:

> I was speaking with a guy at the Columbus Ohio VW show. He and I both have
> 1971 busses. Mine a stock 1600. His an 1835. He was telling me his bus had
> been running a bit hot until he added a 2 quart oil sump. Something about
> it hanging a bit lower down in the air stream. Now his oil never gets hot,
> plus there are 2 extra quarts which help distribute heat better.
>
> Mine runs a bit hot on warmer days.

A deep sump might run a bit cooler due to greater surface area, but I
cannot see how it could have a major effect on temperature.  More oil
doesn't mean it runs cooler, only that changes in temperature take a bit
longer.

Disadvantages:

Loss of ground clearance
More joints to leak
More waste oil to deal with.

IF you're looking to lower oil temperatures, and your tin and seals are in
good order it would be wiser to work on the cooler.  Either go to (in
order of difficulty) a close-fin type III cooler (no tin mod), a type IV
cooler (some tin mods) or a full flow cooler plumbed into the oil
gallery.
