Think about it.  You expect this stuff to go on smooth and glossy and to
look perfect for 10 years in the sun without oxidizing.  That's a somewhat
higher expectation than you would have of $20/gallon house paint.
Designing a coating that will do that is not a trivial exercise, and they
don't sell nearly as much car paint as they do house paint.

Personally, I think that the manufacturers are holding us hostage by
feeding the desire for a mirror surface on sheet metal.  If cars came with
matte or semi-matte finishes they would be a lot easier to keep looking
new.  I'm thinking about adopting a "Afrika Korps" look on our '69 bug.
Heck, I want body panels molded out of Little Tykes plastic with
color all the way through and "pop it back out" bodywork capability!

The last printer I bought is a HP Laserjet 4L.  I bought it 12 years ago,
new, for $600, but in the time I've been using it I have replaced the

perfectly, and will probably keep working as long as I want it too.
Unfortunately, it has only a parallel interface, so compatibility will
rear its ugly head soon.  I think that it was the last decent consumer
level printer HP made.
