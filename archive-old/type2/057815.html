> I was told that the Federal version WAS the California version NOT the
> "other 48 states/canada" version!!!???

No.  Generally, VW manufactured a "California" version for the stricter 
California emissions requirements and a "Federal" model for the other 49 
states and Canada.  However, Canada was generally less strict than the 
US, so there are also "Canada-only" variations of the "Federal" model.

So, California stuff was the first to have FI instead of carbs, 
hall-effect distributors instead of points, O2 sensors in the FI system, 
catalytic converters, etc.

Looking at my diagram for air cooled Vanagons, this is what is identified 
as the "49 states and Canada" version, though Canadian versions had no 
catalytic converter nor EGR.
 
> I was told that the federal model required more stringant emission controls
> (from inspector).  The tech at RMMW stated that the fed version was made for
> the strict CA emission controls, hence the cat converter.  They both stated
> that the other model "other 48 states/canada" had looser restrictions.

No.  Speaking only of the air-cooled Vanagons, the California model had 
the most stringent emission controls.  The "Federal" model also had a 
catalytic converter, in a differnt configuration than the California 
model.  The Federal model had no O2 sensor, while the California model 
had one.  It was the Canadian model the didn't have the catalytic converter.
