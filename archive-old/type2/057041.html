Volks,

Well as I said in my previous post, I have a vacuum reading of 10" which
is way below where I like it.  I think I have an explanation now, tho, so
hold off on the cards and letters for a bit.  This AM I had a '73 bus with
a '78 engine, new heads and valves that sounded like the tango finals in
Barcelona :-)  First thing I took a reading and noticed a distinct lack of
balance (reading #1 on the chart below).  So I adjusted them "by the
book".  Rotate the engine counterclockwise through TDC on each cylinder,
adjust valve to contact +2 turns for each cylinder.  I then took a second
round of compressions and was much happier (reading #2), but I also took
that 10" vac reading and was none too happy at that.  Both of these
readings, tho, were done "cold" (a slight misnomer in DC in August, but we
let that slide :-) and I wanted a hot reading, so I drove the car around
the block twice (~1/2 mile) and popped the plugs out and took a
compression reading (reading #3).  Well that explains the low vacuum :-) 
Tomorrow AM, I am gonna go back to contact +1 turn, at least for cylinders
1 & 3.  I will report further knowledge as I gain it :-)
