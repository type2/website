Steven,

	Sorry for being late on this thread. But your mail made me think, how am I
doing it? I'm so used to manual transmission that I don't think about it.
I'm automatic, not my tranny! Studying my own behaviour on my way home from
work today I came to the following conclusion: I sometimes coast to a stop
only using my brakes but mostly I downshift. To make this even more
confusing I sometimes shift down trough the gears without lifting the
clutch pedal! 
Don't ask me why, this is just old habit. It could have something  to do
with the traffic situation and my speed but I have not analyzed this any
furter. I'm not good at "self psychoanalyzing" :-)

	Automatic vs. maual is also a cultural thing. Here in Finland you have to
learn to drive with manual. No drivers school have autoshift cars.
Handicapped people naturally get exeptions. Naturally you are allowed to
drive automatic with any drivers license!

	  In the seventies cars with automatics were very rare here, owned mostly
by handicapped or increadibly rich people. I have never seen a Breadloaf
Bus with automatic here. In the eighties it started to become more common
with automatic but still about 90% of the new cars sold here today are
manual. Could it be the other way around in the USA?

	Can't stop myself from telling about the first time I drove a car with
automatic transmission! At the time I have had my drivers license for
twelve years. Driving a truck in work for six of theese. At first
everything went fine but when the cars (BMW 320i) speed increased and the
engine was rewing a bit high my left foot by habit pushed the wide brake
pedal hard like it was a clutch pedal............well, we stopped:-) Later
I have learned to keep my left foot like glued to the floor whatever happens!
