The question is, how many miles/hours has this motor been run? 

If, however, this is a high milage motor that has developed wear beyond
spec, it's long block time. 

Excess end play means every time you use the clutch, the crankshaft slips
back and forth. This misaligns the rods too much if the end play spec is
too much. The rod bearings wear excessively at their edges. Excessive end
play in new assemblies is the root of many 20k- life motors. 

Gee, if I spend twice as much for a 100k motor than I do for a 20k motor,
am I fool for value or what???
