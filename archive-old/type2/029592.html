On Tue, 26 Sep 2000, Isaiah wrote:

> Hello Ethan, and others.
> Why did you choose the 30PICT-1 for a 1600 dual port?

Our '69 bug has a 30PICT-1 carb (original) on a dual-port manifold.  It
works great!  Easy to tune, holds its settings well.  I have a 30/31 carb
on the bus, also a dual port, and it works OK when clean, but tends to get
gunked up much easier than the -1.  I wish I could find another 30mm
center section for the manifold, as I'm not all that happy with the
adapter on the bus.  I found the 30mm center section for the bug by just
pure dumb luck while junkyard cruising for the DP parts.  At the time, I
didn't realize that the carb bases were different!

I think that the 30-pict-1 carb was the last of the "no compromises for
smog" designs.  This is probably why they are so easy to work with.  They
lack the complex idle passages that are so sensitive to particles of dirt
and debris.
