I had exactly this problem in my dual port 73, it which came & went,
 but more often came.......... 

after lots of this & that with all the normal things mechanical & electrical

(infact in the mean time I had totally re-built the engine, reground crank,
new pistons
 & sleeves, new cam, new heads & valves etc..... new coil, overhauled the
carb.. 
I decided to pull the dizzy out & give it a thorough overhaul (yep that was
untouched 
& original!!)

once it was apart, I confirmed that it all worked correctly, but I noticed
that the vertical
play in the shaft was around 1.5mm & the lobes on the cam were visibly worn,
so 
much so that when I pushed the shaft up, the points gap would open to about
1.4mm!! 
and when I pushed it back down (to the wear marks on the lobes) it was down
to 0.3mm!!
..... so if the shaft is moving vertically during use, you have a lovely
variable points gap 
- not good!!!!

Ringing around I have been unable to get spare parts for the original dizzy,
so since I 
had my dads 009 in the garage (he didn't like it on his 69 single port) I
set that up with 
new points & condenser & now my bus runs sweeter than ever!!!!

in-fact it is so sweet, smooth & powerful through the rev range, I wonder if
the 009 is 
only suited to certain set-ups (e.g.. 34 pict 3 dual port) and maybe the bad
stories are 
when used in combination with other - anyone out there looked into this?

so check that dizzy for wear!
