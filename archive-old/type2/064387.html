1. I've done the following:
    a. Bought a gallon container of carburator cleaner. It has a parts
basket inside. Just put the bearings, etc in the basket and place in
solution until clean.

   b. Take parts out and rinse with water.

   c. Let dry. Use compressed air to speed up process.

2. If you do not know the age of the non-ripped boots, just replace
them. For the amount of hassle to do two, replacing all will give you
peace of mind. Well worth it!

3. Never encountered the problem.
