> so I've mannaged to get the engine out and the fly wheel and all to replace

there are needle bearing pullers available that expand behind the bearing
and pull put with a slide hammer.  You can also make one yourself with a
washer and some creative grinding and filing.  It should tilt to go
through the bearing and then straighten out behind it.

You can also use a small chisel to cut the old bearing down one side.
then it will pull out easily.

I wouldn't go anywhere near the bearing with a drill!  A dremel mototool
might be used with a small carbide cutter to cut through the side of the
bearing.

If we're talking about a type 1 engine, then you could simply replace the
gland nut.  Not an option on type 4 though!

> Does anyone know what could cause this to happen to the needle vearing.  Am 
> i in need of a new tranny soon???

usually the culprit is lack of lubrication.  Be sure to lube your new
bearing with the thickest grease you can find.  I use Bosch distributor
point grease.
