> As far as your carburetors, I would be inclined to keep the original dual
> carbs.  You might have to rebuild them, but its not terribly expensive or
> difficult.  New carbs usually mean a weber or some other setup that wasn't
> necessarily designed vor a bus application.  They will work, but also will
> require a lot of tuning, setup and sometimes different timing adjustments
> to get them to run right.  These new carb setups usually don't come with a
> hot air induction system which is necessary to prevent carb icing and
> crappy running in cold weather.  If you buy new, non-stock carbs, you will
> have a non-stock bus that a lot of mechanics will not touch because there
> are no manuals or other specs which discuss the particular setup you have.
> In the long run, getting your stock carbs to work properly will be the
> least expensive and more reliable way to go.
> 
> Just my opinion for what its worth.
