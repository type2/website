Volks,

There are a million and one tricks for removing a broken stud, and if you
are a machinist/pro or have a spare head waiting to be popped on, go ahead
and try them.  BUT... if you are just starting out, or it's your daily
driver then the answer to "How do extract a broken stud?" is that you
take it to a competent machinist.  Right away, without trying anything.

If you bring it to him now, with a couple threads still on it and 3/4" of
stud sticking out of the head, he will pat you on the head, talk down to
you a bit, take it in the back room, remove the stud in 5 minutes, and
charge you $5-50.

If you bring it to him after you have broken the stud off below the
surface of the head, then drilled into the stud and broken off a easy-out
in the remains, he will look at you, look at the head, and start into his
"I'm busy" routine.  If you are very lucky he will give you a long lecture
about "do-it-yourselfers", tell you how important the other work he has is
and how long the lead time is, tell you how many hours it is going to take
(at $50/hr), accept the job, and give you your bus/head back in a week
along with a $100-150 bill.

Even if you have to pay $50 to have your bus towed up to the machinist,
not doing it yourself turns out to be *much* cheaper, honest!
