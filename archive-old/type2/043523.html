 If you're going to this extent then get stronger tyres. See if you can
find some Michelin Agilis', which are their current light van range and
will have proper commercial vehicle ratings: look for a "C" suffix,
185R14C etc.

 Check out:
http://www.michelin_dot_com/us/eng/news/agilis/3.htm

 Getting full on, commercial rated tyres will make a big difference to
the handling of the bus. Either the 61 or the 81 will do: the 81 is
stronger and designed for heavier vans.

 I have Agilis 81s (though on 15" rims), KYBs and sway-a-ways on my 
camper and it handles very well. On the minus side the ride is
pretty damn stiff (tyres & shocks do this). The Agilis 81s are Mud
& Snow rated, which might suit you or might not. I'll probably go
for Agilis 51s next time (205/65R15C) these are more road
orientated but they arn't available for a 14" rim.

 Hope this helps,
