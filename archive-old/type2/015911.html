At 05:29 AM 6/7/99 -0700, Mark Hineline wrote:
>Suppose you took a 2.0 L case, had it align bored, new bearings all the
>way around, dropped in a counterweighted crank, balanced everything else,
>ran synthetic oil, changed the oil at the regular interval. Could you
>expect to get over 100,000 miles out of such a case? 150,000? More?

First of all, why have the case align bored?  According to what I've read,
type IV cases almost never need it.  If you did everything else listed
above *except* for the counterweighted crank I would expect 100k+ miles
>from the engine (except for possibly the cylinder heads).  

>I am trying to decide whether the cost of a CW crank is justified by 
>extended engine life.

It seems to be the general concensus that a counterweighted crank is not
really 
necessary (for engine longevity or anything else) unless you spin the
engine above 5,000 rpm.  However, I'm planning on getting one eventually
just because I like a nice, smooth running engine.
