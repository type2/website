Subject: Asbestos in Heat Exchangers, etc. was: [T2] Heat Exchanger
  Quality

Back in 1996-1997 or so, VW of America was replacing heat exchangers, etc. 
in old Bay Window buses, at no charge. They sent a VWoA representative from 
Dallas to my local garage, who, with my help, replaced the heat exchangers, 
accordian tubes and flapper boxes on my '79 bus, all at no cost. They used 
Danish heater parts. The old heat exchangers and flapper boxes had, indeed, 
been wrapped in asbestos; the old accorian tubes incorporated an internal 
layer of asbestos in their design. The new parts contained no asbestos; the 
heat exchangers and flapper boxes were not wrapped with anything.

VW wouldn't officially use the "A-word" in any communications with me, but 
the VW rep who installed these parts told me confidentially that yes, their 
legal department had ordered this program to limit their liability somehow 
by demonstrating that they cared and were proactive about the asbestos 
issue, etc. A VW rep asked me on the phone when I was thinking about buying 
a new VW. I told her maybe in another 17 years, as the one I was driving was 
not even halfway worn out yet. I'm sure that she was shaking her head.

Roy
'79 Transporter
