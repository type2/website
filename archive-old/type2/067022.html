When driving in Baja CA, I have seen truckers and others 
commonly signal a left turn indicator when the lead vehicle wants
to tell the following vehicle that it is safe to pass. Oncoming
vehicles will commonly flash their lights at you (high/low beam, or on/off)
to indicate, "Pay attention! There is a hazard ahead!"

Karl Franz (The People's Guide to Mexico) says these signals are 
understood throughout Mexico.

I'm not aware of any signal used there for, "Don't pass! It's not safe!"
It's very clear and convenient to know these signals when motoring
south of the border.

Anyone seen these signals in use in our country?
