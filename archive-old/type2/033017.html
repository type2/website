I haven't tried it.  But I teach electrochemistry in a coupla courses.

Removing corrosion from Al and/or Mg by electrolysis may (should) not work,
as they are extremely active metals.  In any event, the cathode should be a
fairly inert metal.  Lead or tin (solder) should do nicely.  Platinum would
be best :-).

Essentially the process is electrolysis of water, so O2 is generated at the
cathode, hence the need for an inert electrode.  Hydrogen gas generated at
the rusty electrode is what does at least some of the work.  This is best
done outside, as hydrogen explosions are considered undesirable by most.

The process should be limited only by the size of the tank.  A 55 gal
plastic drum might do a passable job for big pieces.  Worth trying.
