Your vintage alternator will not like this. Look at the electrical load 
rating in the tech sheets of the new systems. Your bus may only have 65 
amps to run the whole vehicle.

May also be too heavy to lift if you mount it to the pop-top.

It can not be run in an inclined position.

Might work if installed in the cab roof. That said I would be hesitant to 
cut a hole that big in my cab roof. Your opinion may differ and I support 
that. Would be nice once parked though.

Don't think that your only options are dealer installed vintage VW units or 
the full size RV systems.

Many of the hot rod magazines offer nice kits for $$$.

We have put together several air conditioning systems from junkyard sources 
for our family hot rods. Our sources have traditionally been Japanese cars 
with hang-on systems - dealer installed a/c systems. We only buy systems 
that are complete and unopened. That means when you loosen that first line 
Freon will escape. That means the system is dry inside - a line wasn't cut 
or removed allowing moisture inside the system to create rust and havoc and 
dirt. We've used systems from old Subarus and old Yugos with great results. 
These systems have been US built and dealer installed. Cost has been $150 
or less because the systems come off of cars that nobody wants b/c they are 
ugly and orphans. They often go to the crusher will little removed.

We generally do not use the interior case but create a simple one from 
sheet metal and create a nice 1/4" wood face to match the interior decor of 
the cars they are mounted in. The systems are nice because they come with 
small Japanese compressors too. We do use the fan, fan controls, and 
thermostat with new knobs.

They may not be sized correctly for a van so take it all with a grain of 
salt and make up your own mind. Once installed we've never been able to run 
them but on the hottest days on anything but low because we get too cold.

There are some good shadetree mechanic books on installing kit based a/c 
systems. Check the larger book sellers. I think "Motorbooks" may be the 
publisher.

I'm building my system from a '78 Westy dealer installed system. The system 
will be more carefully installed than the dealer did and will use one of 
the smaller rotary compressors. I'm going to be running it on a Corvair 110 
HP so my mounting will be different than a VW. The outside coils 
(evaporator I think) will be from a '73 Westy because it mounts forward of 
the front axle and seems to be a bit less vulnerable than the '78 Westy 
version. I'll have to sandwich some electric fans on top of it for a/c in 
traffic.
