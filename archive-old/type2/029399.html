Unfortunately, I'm 400 miles from my old box (if I could find it), so I
can't verify the number.

The only oddities with the boots is that, being a modern design, they are
a bit shorter in length than the accordian-style boots.  The axle has a
pair of "humps" forged into it to retain the small end of the boot, but
the Rockford boot should be positioned just outboard of the outboard
"hump" so as not to stretch the rubber at full extension.

They also use the old style removable CV flange instead of the crimped-on
flange.  If you use a screw-type narrow hose clamp instead of the
supplied one-use clamp here it makes it much easier to relube the CV.
Just loosen the clamp, pull the boot back, and you have a nice size
opening into which to squirt grease.
