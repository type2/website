At 12:52 PM 01/10/2003, you wrote:


>What you need to do is tight the nut down. I use a heavy hammer to pound the
>crap out of the wheel nut tool.

You risk destroying the bearings with hammering. Get a 3/4" drive crossbar 
and socket and a pipe cheater. No, I'm sorry, not cheap Chinese, but high 
spec. Calculate pounds/distance to set the torque at spec, then continue to 
the next hole. Same with the cotter key. Use the right size and quality 
pin. Stay away from the $9.95/1000 pin junk kits, for cotter pins or 
anything else. It can be hundreds of dollars cheaper to pay a $1 at the 
dealer for the proper spec cotter pin.


>  The cotter pin holes are slightly offset so

>any further.

>into the hole.

>If the metal is too soft and the pin is loose, the nut will work loose and
