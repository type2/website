>Dave,

>It can be done!  and peacfully too!  it is a solid 4-5 hr job, but I
>flopped a '78 rear main harness into my '73 when I installed the FI in it
>(some of the FI wiring is included in the main harness)  I removed the
>fuse box from the front and the instrument cluster as well.  Then I
>unplugged *everything* that went to the bundle of wires that headed for
>the rear and fed it back thru the frame members.  On the FI, there are a
>number of large plugs that will not feed the other way, but on your '72 it
>might be easier to unplug it at the rear of the bus and feed it forward.
>Your '72 has only the connections for the various lamps and ignition
>(watch the reverse circuit!).  You will need a wiring diagram whichever
>way you go, the diagram is available at:
>http://www.vintagebus_dot_com/wiring/index.html
