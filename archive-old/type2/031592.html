bus to the dipstick.

into the boiling water, don't let it touch the sides or bottom and read
your gauge... at this point you will have a rough idea of the gauge error.

high sustained speeds.  personally I wouldn't take it over about 55mph for
the first 3,000 miles or so. and vary your speeds to help the break in.

TAKE IT EASY, I know it is hard not to push it across the prairie but
resist!  not over 55, take lots and lots of breaks, enjoy the scenery take
some back roads but don't wreck your new engine before you even get "home"

don't drive with the adjusted temp over 240-250 if you slow down to about
50MPH and the temp doesn't come down to at least 230 pull over in the
shade, leave the engine run (some controversy about this) but when
standing still (not driving) open the rear engine door, wait for engine to
get below 200 before driving again.

change the oil ever 500 miles or so on this trip, drive in the early
morning and early evening to make use of the cooler air, stop often. take
your time and take some back roads (did I say that before?, can't say it
enough) Don't push the engine and what you don't know can hurt you and
your engine.

overheating a new engine is one of the prime causes of premature failure.
