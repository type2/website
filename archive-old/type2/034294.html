I don't think that warming the fuel is a problem even in the winter.  I
stopped our VW on a chilly day, opened the engine bay and noticed that
the gasoline was vaporizing and bubbling through the transparent
filter.  The fuel line runs along the intake manifold and into a fuel
pump that is attached to the crankcase, both of which warm the fuel.

The main fuel atomization problem occurs when the fuel is drawn into
the venturi of carburator.  The fast-moving air there experiences a
pressure drop and a corresponding temperature drop  of several tens of
degrees.  This can be partially remedied by a properly-operating hot
air tube between the cylinder and the air cleaner (not to be confused
with the heat riser).
