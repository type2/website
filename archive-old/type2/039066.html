Re putting a non-walkthrough seat in a bay bus-
  I did ths way back in 1973 in our 71 Westy, but remember the early
busses didn't have sliders for the passenger seat- I think I used a
"double" seat from a split window bus, and managed to hinge it so it
slipped into one rear "catch" when flipped down.  The other side
required a notch to be sawn into the seat pedestal so it could flip down
all the way.  In the front I put hinges that kept the seat in place &
allowed the seat to be flipped up.  The seat cantilevered over the
walkthrough area.  Made for great "intimacy" with the future "SO" and
allowed snoozing, but we eventually preferred the walkthrough to
climbing over the seat!
   BTW, the space between the seats (where the stool sat) held a
portapotti with the stool top as a seat matching the rearfacing seat; a
padded seatback was made from the spare tire vinyl; it hinged from the
side of the fridge/sink unit and allowed walking through when the front
bench seat was removed!
