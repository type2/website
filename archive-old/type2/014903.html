A used vacuum advance distributor is no worse than a used centrifugal 
advance distribitor.  With either of them you want to disassemble them, 
clean them, re-shim the dog gear, and grease the advance plate.  With 
The vacuum advance you have an extra step of testing the vacuum cannister.  
All the "failed" vacuum advance distributors I have ever owned did not 
advance were due to a gummed up advance plate, not a bad vacuum diaphram.

I do use 009 distributors on dual carb engines with no vacuum lines.  
But I DON'T use new Brazilian ones.  I find used German ones at swap meets 
& rebuild them.
