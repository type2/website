Hi Candra,

Reverse will be better for hill climbing simply because it is a lower 
gear ratio.

What are the dwell and timing set to? These numbers are critical for 
getting usable power.

If you have between 48-52 degrees of dwell and timing at 7.5 degrees 
BTDC and it's still not running right, set the valves and test 
compression. It's not impossible that you might have a bad ignition 
component... even the brand new stuff these days can be bad right out 
of the box sometimes.

If it was working when you left your friend's place, then something has 
changed. Either something has broken or it's out of adjustment. If the 
distributor is loose the timing will have changed, or it might be that 
the points might have slipped, affecting dwell and timing. Either of 
these is a simple fix. If we're not including the carbs in the analysis 
then it has to be ignition, timing or a mechanical problem.

A bit of extra advice: Even if you KNOW something is good, check it 
anyway, just to be sure. It only takes a minute.
