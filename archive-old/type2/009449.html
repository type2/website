No sense in that!  All you have to do is plumb the manifold for the
booster.  If you get the manifold center section out of a '71 bus the
connection is there.  I think that late model bugs also had a similar
port.

Even if you have to put the connection in yourself it isn't all that
hard.  Just get a short length of 1/2 OD tubing, fishmouth the end to
conform with the vertical part of the center section (just below the
carb flange), and braze it into place.  Then use a drill a bit smaller
than the ID of the tube to open the hole into the manifold.  This
method yields a good sturdy vacuum port with nothing extending into
the manifold to interfere with flow.
