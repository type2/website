Hey Mat,

You will never get the amount of juice you are looking for using a 110V
heater in a bus.  You alternator is pretty lacking in the output department
and the inverter will not be able to keep up with the demands of the heater.
A dead battery, a fried space heater and possible electrical fires are the
usual results.  Getting your stock system sealed up and functional is your
best bet by far.

You are in Hampton, right?  If you want to make a voyage out to Newmarket, I
will help you get everything all hooked up and blowing some hot air for you.
If your heat exchangers are solid you can probably get things going for
under $100.00.  Otherwise you are looking at $400-$500 regardless of what
approach you take.

A gas heater is your best bet if you want to really be warm in your bus, but
if you don't mind wearing a sweatshirt on cool days, your stock system
hooked up with a recirculating air return and a bilge blower in the main
heat tube will probably be all that you will ever need.
