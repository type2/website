Hi Phil, I have a 40 quart Igloo Koolmate, which is similar, I think.  It is
cooled by a peltier chip, which is a solid state thermoelectric device, for
those unfamiliar with these things, that moves heat from one side to
another.  They're often found under the main processor in a computer, but
somebody figured out that you can use them to cool a refrigerator.

Anyway, I love the thing.  I bought it 6 months ago for about $60 off ebay,
and had doubts about it, but I thought I'd take a flyer.  Much to my delight
the thing works, and works well.  I run it off my solar panels, and unless
I'm doing some entertaining, I keep my kitchen fridge shut off.  It draws
about 5 amps, so it would not be suitable for running continuously off a
starting battery.

It will also not keep things ice cold when the temp is over 100 degrees, and
it is better at keeping things cold than at cooling them.  I think you could
help it out with bags of ice for faster cooling as long as the ice stayed in
the bags and you didn't use it as a regular ice chest.
