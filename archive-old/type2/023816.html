> Actually, those pumps put out over 60 lbs, which is controlled by the
> regulator to a running pressure of 28-32 lbs. Your looking for nothing but
> problems to use other than Bosch parts in a Bosch system. Why are those
> pumps so expensive??? That's what it takes to make a pump that will last
> 15-20 years. I have seen generic pumps used a few times. In every case
> they
> were VERY noisy and the fuel and lines carry that noise throughout the
> system. Any noise other than a very subdued hum from a Bosch pump means it
> will probably fail soon. Sometimes, it's just a clogged filter that make
> the pump work very hard and brings on their demise.
> 
> I would try a used Bosch pump from the salvage yard before trying
> something
> else if I couldn't afford to fix it right with a new pump. Odds are it
> will
> work long enough to sell the Bus to the unsuspecting, or just might
> outlast
> a new wannabe pump.
> 
> Know also that your conversion to FI on an original carbed chassis will
> require plumbing a return fuel line to the tank, or replacement of the
> tank
> with a FI tank. The time/money to do that right could well exceed the cost
> of a new Bosch pump.
> 
> An FI 2.0 liter is a very good way to go and probably worth the effort.
> Probably the easiest way to pick up some extra horse power without
> diminishing longevity. However, just putting FI on the 1.7, 1.8 carbed
> engine could get a bit problematic. No provision for a temp sensor mount,
> ECU mount, pressure regulator mount, air filter mount, yadayadayada. Be
> prepared to spend time/money getting all this fit up.
