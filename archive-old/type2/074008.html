crank screws into and then slips into a bench mounted engine holder. Not

> > What do you guys use to hold the crank while you install the rods?
>
> My legs. I spread an old army blanket on the floor, put a well-washed
> bedsheet on top of that (it keeps blanket lint out of the crank), then lay
> the crank on top of that. Assemble the rods onto the crank with the nuts
> finger tight, then sit down. Stick one rod up and put one leg on the crank
> on either side of the rod. Then torque the nuts to 10 lb-ft, make sure it
> falls freely, then to 18, check for free motion, then finally to 24 and
> check once more for free motion. If the rod moves nicely, do the next one.
