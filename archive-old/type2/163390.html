Subject: Re: [T2] windshield replacement

VW didn't introduce those "alligator teeth" and change the inner lip
appropriately until 1970.  An enhanced "safety" feature introduced by VW,
but not required by the USDOT, they were more of a pain than an ehancement.
When I was in full swing manufacturing OE Style and Cal Look Window Rubber
in the late 70s thru the 90s, I only made my rubber cross-section
(profile) for the non-alligator teeth style flanges, which worked just
fine.  Go ahead and rip-'em out, clean the flange surface, coat with some
rust inhibiting paint or tar, and install the seal BD shipped to you.

How they got on your buddy's 64 is a mystery, maybe they were retrofitted.
Having changed windshield seals on a 56, 59, 62, 64, 67, 68 and a 69 Bug, I
never encountered them.  Now my buddy's 72 Super Beetle, he had 'em, but we
ripped 'em out to install the OE style (Brazil-made) seal.  Didn't encounter
any problems with the installation.
