Subject: Re: [T2] Synthetic Oil: done to death?

Several years ago I decided synthetics were superior due to less
friction yielding cooler running temps and stronger films meaning less
likely to allow metal to metal contact under stress.

One of our local club members has worked in some of the local FLAPS
systems and spent some time explaining to me that oil is basically oil
+ additive package. But I really didn't appreciate what that exactly
meant until Chris posted the following in October:

http://volkswagen.msk.ru/vw_doc/the_motor_oil_bible

I don't think anybody responed to Chris' post at the time. I myself
got so caught up in reading it that I didn't get back and say, "Thanks
Chris", so now I'll say it: "Thanks, Chris", ;-)

Anyway, after reading this and seeing the facts behind the
conventional wisdom we pick up from each other verbally,  I was truly
impressed with how important it is to run synthetic, so I am repenting
of my evil ways I have been guilty of in putting regular oil into my
VW's lately, even though it has been higher quality Valvoline VR-1
racing oil.

You will want to read some of the precautions such as increasing oil
leaks in older seals.

John
