I too had problems w/ the fuel gage to Bullwinkle.  The solution was to replace
the 'voltage stabilizer' .  This is a 2 terminal device that is bolted to the back
of the speedometer. It's function is to provide a constant voltage source for the
fuel gage. The easiest way to gain access to it is to remove the 4 screws that
hold the instrument cluster to the dashboard and also remove the speedometer
cable.  You can now tilt the inst. cluster forward and voila - straight shot to
the voltage stabilizer.  BTW a new voltage stabilizer unit is fairly expensive
(~$40 I think),  however if you have a local junk yard, you should be able to get
one cheep (my yard had a perfectly good unit for only $3.00 - the ultimate in
recycling!).
