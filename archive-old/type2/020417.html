Not sure what  engine code was original to your bus, but AD sounds 
correct.  I can check my Bently manual tonight if you like.  Anyway, 
what really matters, as has been said by another poster, is what 
distributor you have.  The original distributor in 1970 was a 
combination vacuum/mechanical advance type, with ONE vacuum line (i.e., 
vacuum advance only, not vacuum advance and retard as on 1971 and later 
years).  On my 1971 (with original distributor with two vacuum lines), I 
set my timing to 5 deg ATDC, at idle, with all vacuum lines connected. 
The reason I would need to check my Bently manual to be sure, but I 
think your '70 would be something like 5 deg BTDC, at idle with vacuum 
line connected.  Be cautious with my statement though, as I'm not sure 
what the timing mark on your pulley means (again, assuming you have the 
original pully).  I seem to recall that on some of the early years of 
the Bays, you disconnected the vacuum line and timed at 0 degrees, which 
is where the mark on the pulley is positioned on those engines.  To be 
sure about what you are doing, you need the right data, you need to know 
what distributor you have and what the position is of the timing mark on 
your pulley. Let me know if you'd like me to check, and I could pmail 
you from home tonight.
