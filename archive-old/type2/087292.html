Subject: Re: Re: [T2] '78 Deluxe Westy-$16000- Worth it?

I would probably have to agree with Glenn on this one. Even if the 14.5K miles on the odometer is original and accurate (a BIG if), the bus is still nearly 25 years old, and as soon as it begins to get driven regularly, things WILL start to go wrong.  A machine is like a vintage musical instrument, it needs to get regular attention and playing to keep its tone and "balance" for lack of a better word.  Plus, who knows what kind of attention it has gotten during those miles? Was the oil changed regularly and tuned regularly, even though the usage miles are low, these would still have needed to be done in timely intervals. I think that a lot of people think "Hey, I'll spend an assload of money on a nearly new, low miles bus and then I won't have to worry about messing with all the maintenance and repair issues" My bro-in-law wants a bus and he actually said "I want to spend enough money on a nice enough bus so that I won't have to do anything to it except drive it around." But we!
 all know this is not reality.
My advice is: If you've got the $16K to spend, find a well-cared for bay with a nice, straight, rust free body. Don't worry about the engine. Spend up to about $4000 on such bus. Then have a VW professional build the engine of your choice, there are plenty of knowledgeable engine builders who will give you a warranty on the engine (which you WON"T get buying the $16K bus). Dunno the cost of such a new engine but it shouldn't cost anymore than $2K-$3K. Spend another couple of Grand on body work and paint if needed.  Spend $1,000 on new rubber seals, tires, interior, and misc stuff, etc. Then drive your nice "NEW" bus with a warranty on the engine down to the bank and deposit the $6,000 that you still have left over. ;^)
FWIW, John Baker
