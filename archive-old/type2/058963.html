>34 PICT 3 carbs someone would have tried to transplant a FI system from

This is an idea.  The FI system used on the '75 and later Beetles is the 
same as used on our '75 and later Buses, Bosch L-Jetronic.  The system 
used on the '67 and later Type 3s, though, is Bosch D-Jetronic, which 
seems to have a reputation for being finicky.  

I am highly tempted to take a fuel-injected Beetle engine and put it in 
my '74 Karmann Ghia.  I would, however, need an FI gas tank (has an 
outlet and a return inlet), I would need to run a fuel return line from 
the engine to the gas tank, I would need to figure out how to rig up an 
air cleaner.  I'd need to see what needs to be done with exhaust -- 
could I use the stock exhaust and heat exchangers or would I have to go 
with those from the FI model years?  If the latter, will they work with 
my heat ducts/cables (they probably should since the pans of the Beetle 
and Ghia are similar, but you never know).  I think the engine would 
physically fit in the engine compartment just fine, but it's the details 
that will get you.

It would be a lot of work, I think.  And probably much more of a pain in 
the butt than just buying a new Solex 34 PICT-3 which is probably what I 
really need to do :-)
