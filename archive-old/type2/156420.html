Hey, Chai.  Mine tended to diesel and even backfire when it got  hot.  I found out that the throttle wasn't closing all the way at  idle.  (Thanks again, Mark!)  What I did was turn the mixture  screw 1/4 turn CCW to idle it up.  Then the idle screw 1/4 turn  CCW to idle it back down.  I did that a few times and it got  progressively better until it completely stopped.  Problem was  that I was running too lean and had to have the throttle open too much  to maintain idle.  That allowed fuel to flow for a second or so  after shut-off.  Give it a try.
  
  As far as overheating, the finger on the dipstick test is great.   The main thing that I found was sealing the engine compartment.   If that is all done, you may have a problem with your oil cooler.   Partially clogged?  I don't know much about that as mine is fine  now.  I put a new engine seal in and did some patchwork with  aluminum foil and metal-ducting tape.  You know you can't trust  any guage made by VDO.  Good luck and keep us posted.  
  
  Cheers...KR
