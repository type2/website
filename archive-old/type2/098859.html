On Fri, 3 Jan 2003, Rik wrote:

> Be sure to have anti-seize for the adjusting stars, brake grease
> for the shoe rub points, and new cotter pins to lock the axle nuts in place.

Lucky folks with '71 and up buses don't need the cotter pins as they have
composite drums.  There is a handy divot in the hub that allows a socket
wrench to have a straight shot at the cylinder bolt.

> This would also be a good opportunity to clean, inspect, and re-grease your
> wheel bearings and CV joints (be sure to have new CV boots handy, in case
> one/some are ripped, or get ripped while you work on the joints).  Me, I'd just
> replace 'em all--it's a lot of work to *just* replace a torn CV boot, later on.
> .....AND BUY GERMAN ONES.

Having done "precautionary" CV boot replacements with German Lobro brand
boots and then having them tear a few months later, I have concluded that
if the boots aren't cracked or leaking it is better to leave them alone.
I carry replacements and the tools to change them with on trips, just in
case.
