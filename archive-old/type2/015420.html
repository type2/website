At 04:12 AM 10/31/2000 GMT, you wrote:
>cold). My question, how can I either fill the depression, or otherwise apply 
>some sealing compound (which?) to the O-ring and block surface that would 
>effectively complete the seal.  What compound could I press into the
depression 
>and then file flat, or perhaps a form-a-gasket compund I could apply to the 
>joint at time of reassembly that would be compatible with rubber and iron 
>and withstand coolant? Any suggestions would be welcomed.

J-B Weld works very good on cast iron. I would first hold that plastic
flange on a flat surface and check around the port with a light to veryify
it is flat. They do warp with age.

Also, if the pitting in the cast iron is quite thin, I would grind it a bit
deeper. The J-B weld will hold better if it is not ultra thin.
