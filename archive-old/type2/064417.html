Well, I'm suprised there have not been more postings on this.  Maybe
because it's kind of an FAQ.  I would think that you'd first want
to know what the cause was of the over heating of your engine.  I
suspect as with most rebuilds, it was probably the valve getting
out of adjustment.  I've heard this story over and over many times.
You must check the valves after very short miles after a rebuild.
I even recommend to check them after 50 miles of driving.  I've 
found that I could not slide the feeler gauge through several valves
after 100 miles after a rebuild on a friends '68 bus.  You've
really got to watch them.  

Other possible causes for overheating are:

           insufficient engine seals
           time/dwell not adjusted correctly
           air/fuel mixture too lean
           fan belt too loose
           Missing engine tin
           Cooling fan/shroud obstructed

Make sure that all of the above conditions are fixed if they exist.

Once you've got the engine cooling system completely in order, then
you might consider an AC system.  Many VW people won't even consider
an AC system in an air-cooled VW.  I believe you can use one, but you
have to be judicious about how you use it.  For instance, when climbing
any uphill grade, I would turn it off so as not to over-tax the engine.
You're depending on a small 1600 engine to push a great big box and
that is enough of a load already.  When you're on the flat or downhill,
turn it back on again.  Don't drive when it's over 110 out.  That is
just too hot for an air-cooled VW.  Drive in the morning or evenings and
your engine will be happy.
