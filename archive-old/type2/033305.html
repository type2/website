Hey Alex,

this could give a long long story, but I'll keep it short.

Some years ago (I think it was just only 22 years ago) I bought a 58 split
for about 200 US$. I was in my early twenties then, repaired it, used it,
repaired it again - and I love it up to now.

I learned to accept the infernal noise, the bad back, the freezing on cold
days, the little room inside, the hours to come from A to B.

Back in '92 I've got a '88 vanagon, suprised how warm, fast and comfortable
a bus can be.

In '95 I changed to a '77 westy, thats in my opinion the best bus. Not too
slow, not too cold, but comfortable enough to use it in modern traffic (we
have no speed-limits here on our Autobahns). And - the aircooleds are easier
to repair and cheaper and easier to keep them roadworthy.

My opinion: Buy the best bus you can get for your budget, it's easier to
keep it alive than to repair a heap of scrap. My '77 was in the 2,5k region
and much much better and more original than the '58 was after all repairs.
Be patient to find a really good one. I didn't want one in '95 and it jumped
directly into my garage.
