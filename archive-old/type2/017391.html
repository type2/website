Kevin:
While in theory you can adjust the intake valve while the exhaust of the
same cylinder is open (as your plan says) it works much better on a
chevy than a VW boxer motor. The reason for this is that the rocker
shafts deflect and show more apparent lash, so you end up making a few
valves too tight. (Not a problem where each rocker is independently
supported.) At least that's what I noticed in my 78 (W/ early type 4 cam
and solids). I'd set em to 6 and 8 and then re-check and they'd be too
tight!
