There's been a lot of posts recently about
dual-battery systems.  This always seems to come up
and normally the correct info gets posted.  I can't
remember reading this anywhere in the recent threads
but if it was posted I apologize.

Anyway, for proper charging with a dual-battery
isolator you need to have an isolator with an 'R'
terminal.  Normally you have a voltage drop across the
isolator around .5-1V.  This doesn't allow for
complete charging of the aux battery.  The R on the
proper isolator stands for remote regulator sensing. 
You connect the R terminal to the.  The alternator
senses the voltage drop and increases its output so
you get proper charging.  See this PDF for an
installation diagram.  Look under group 3 which is
page 4 of the PDF.  --->
http://www.surepower_dot_com/pdfs/180012n.pdf
<---

I know that VW used a relay for dual-battery charging
but I'm going to take a guess and assume it was for
cost reasons, not performance reasons.  Relays are
cheap, isolators aren't.  I'm not an EE so I won't
pretend that I know all the pros and cons.
