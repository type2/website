Just a few observations...

First of all, that's 4-6% at the driven wheels, as in, what you might
observe on a chassis dyno.  The HP gain at the crankshaft (where that 60hp
is measured) is probably more...  possibly not much more, though.

Secondly, the "e-ram" as they call it, also provides a corresponding
increase in torque, which is arguably more important to the bus owner.

Of course, nomater how you look at it, it's only a small gain.  One would
probably be better off just getting a real (crankshaft-driven) supercharger,
which would produce more like 5-15lbs of boost.  Then again, increasing
manifold pressure (and therefore compression) in a gas engine shortens its
life.  Maybe it's just better to drive slow.
