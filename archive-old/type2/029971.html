Keep in mind the purpose of the return line.  It's supposed to keep a flow
of cool fuel from the tank to prevent vapor lock in the pump.  The fuel
tank has a large enough surface area to transfer the heat picked up by the
fuel to the outside (even though it pretty much lives in an enclosed area)

A small sump contains a small
volume of fuel will be picking up the heat, which cannot escape easily 
through the small surface area of the tank.  You've increased complexity
without reaching the desired result.

I see two ways around this.  One way is to install a small heat exchanger
between the return line and the tee.  This will transfer the excess heat
to the air much better than a sump tank.  An oil cooler or even a power
steering cooler would work well for this.

In my '71, the fuel fitting is essentially a washer with a tube brazed
through it.  You might be able to make a custom version with two tubes
that will put the return gas right where it belongs, in the tank.  There
will be complications with the fuel screen and you might want to dispense
with it and run a good in-line filter in the outlet line.  That way you
could put some separation between the inlet and outlet lines.
