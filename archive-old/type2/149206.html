>One is the big ground strap.

That is how the starter gets power. The case is the ground.

>One is a
>double spade connection (term 50?) with the solenoid trigger from the
>ignition switch (via a WR-1 relay, in this case).  The other spade terminal
>was empty. Voltage here was ~8-10V during cranking (normal?).

The solenoid needs at least 7V to kick over. There should be a voltage drop at the battery while the starter is operating and since everything is tied to terminal 30 I would expect to see the cranking voltages you saw if there was high resistance in the wiring. It's a big wire so I would check your battery and starter terminals for cleanliness.

>But, the bus still starts!  I don't get it.  Where's the fuel pump
>getting power from (I confirmed that it gets power during cranking)?  Could
>the AFM flap deflect enough during cranking to trip the fuel pump switch in
>the AFM?

During cranking, the flap in the AFM oscillates as the pistons pump and this deflects the needle. I'm not sure if it's enough to provide constant voltage to the fuel pump for startup all vacuum levels (engine condition) but I suspect it might for most.
