Mike S wrote:
>
> Oops. I worked that backwards - you're set to about 42 degrees of 
> dwell, so your point gap is slightly too big. That should not cause 
> the points to burn, though.
>
> To figure dwell with a voltmeter, measure as you did, then divide 
> (7.3/13.6 = .54 percent), for a 4 cylinder engine multiply by 90 
> degrees (.54*90 = 48 degrees), that gives you the "anti-dwell" (i.e. 
> how long the points are open). Subtract from 90 to get the dwell angle 
> (90-48 = 42 degrees).
>
> This works pretty well as long as the meter shows a stable reading 
> when the engine is running (which indicates the meter is averaging the 
> voltage well). If the numbers bounce around, it will be less accurate.
