Good point.

Years ago, I worked in a boxcar repair facility as a painter. The 
standard charcoal filter type mask works fine to keep paint particles 
out of the lungs... privided the filters are clean and the mask well 
fitted (if you can smell fumes, the mask isn't 100%).

We insulated boxcars, after repairs, by putting up plywood, drilling a 
2" hole at the top, and pumping it full of a 2 part liquid that expanded 
into insulating foam. These fumes were so poisonous that we had to use a 
forced air mask.

A belt was stung around the waste, with a regulator valve, plugged into 
an aircompressor line. A 1 1/2" hose came from the belt to the mask, 
which looked like a gas mask. The air passed through a filter to keep 
compressor oil out of the mask.
