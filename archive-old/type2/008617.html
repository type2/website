If the basic metal of the boxes is in good shape, then you might want
to try this repair:

From 16ga or so metal strip 3/4 inch wide, make four semicircles
molded to the OD of the exhaust pipe.  Slide these into place where
the pipe goes into the heater box.  Braze the pieces to the heater
box.  Add whatever pieces of metal you need to keep the semicircles
secure.

The wider bearing area of the strips make this repair last longer than
the original metal.

For basic holes in the box, make a patch from 16ga sheet steel that is
larger than the hole.  Mold the patch to the shape of the exchanger,
then braze it into place.

Unless you can score some OEM heat exchangers, patching your old ones
is the best way to go.  Aftermarket exchangers are usually ligher and
less durable.
