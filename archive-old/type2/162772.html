> On 1/4/07, Lee Pedrazas <lapedrazas_at_gmail_dot_com> wrote:
>> I have about a 1/4" by 2" crack in my air intake boot where it curves at
>> the bottom.  Can I just repair this with something like silicone?
>> Could this be one of the reasons that my bus isn't starting?
>> 
>
>
> Purchase some silicone tape. It has no adhesive, but instead bonds to
> itself when wrapped VERY tightly.
>
  Clean the whole thing with degreaser then wipe with alcohol. Apply
  just enough silicone to fill the crack(s) and a wee bit more. Let dry
  overnight at least. Then apply a skim coat of the silicone all the way
  around the s-boot. Even where there are no cracks. Make sure it's
  about 1/8" thick. Let dry overnight. Wrap with 3 layers of the
  silicone tape. I'd coat everything with a good rubber protectant (NOT
  Armor-All) to reduce future cracks.

  The skim coat gives a huge sealing surface so if for whatever reason
  the silicone in the crack itself comes loose the skim coat keeps it
sealed. The silicone tape backs THAT up and also provides a good solid
> structure to minimize flex.
