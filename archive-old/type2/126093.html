Don,
If the metal surface is prepped properly--and scuffed up--either the
Rino Hyde--or the spray can urethane bedliner will adhere to almost
anything.
Spray some of it on your finger---let it set up for 10 minutes---try and
get it off--

This product is really sticky and doesn't come off very easy--

I have used this product for udercoating on both my Vans and Bus--
Pressure wash the bottom clean--and spray it on--

It's been on the bottom of my 85 for at least 5 years--hasen't moved an
inch.--
And ths one I drove in the winter months.
Salt doesn't touch it--

My front and rear control arms on the Vanagons still look like they were
painted black at the factory--

Pretty tough stuff-

Morton is the outfit that makes the rattle Can Bed Liner--

I know of a few guys that not only did their beds of their pick
up's--but aslo had the underside done in Rino Hyde--

I'm impressed.
