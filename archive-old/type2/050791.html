I followed a pretty extensive thread on the another elist recently and the
best advise I got was to look at 220v units.  Here an explanation why:

Subject: Re: <VV> Re: Volts/Watts/Amps 

An excellent example of the difference between current (amp) and power 
(watts). 
For a given power load, 220 v will need only 1/2 the current of the same 
wattage load on 110 v. Furthermore, since the voltage is double, and the 
current is half, you can tolerate 4 times the voltage DROP at 220 v before 
you see an equivalent reduction in performance, dimming, etc., that you
would 
see at 110 v. 
In other words, high power loads, if you have a choice, really want to be
fed 
at 220 v. My air compressor, welder, heat pump, etc., in my garage all are 
wired as such, and are a LOT happier for it!
