that doesnt sound right to me. pop your distributor
cap off and look at the points, see if there's any
pitting or metal transfer from one side to the other,
make sure the rubbing block is still there, grease the
shaft, and set the gap to .016" when the rubbing block
is on one of the "lobes".

the bentley manual (CH 5 p72) says the wear limit for
the dwell is 42-58 degrees, and new points should be
set to 44-50 degrees.

when you readjust the points you should also set the
timing with a strobe type dynamic timing light to 5
degrees ATDC with vacuum hoses connected at idle.

the bentley also says that if the dwell reading varies
more than 1 degree at 2000rpm from the idle reading, a
worn distributor shaft is indicated.

>Help before i give up and Weberize this Bus

you'll never get your bus running as good with that
weber as with the original FI. the weber has bad
problems with carb icing because there is no provision
for a manifold preheater. since there's no heater, the
fuel vapor condenses in the manifold and doesnt get
enough fuel to your cylinders, another lean condition.

I bet you dont even know how powerful your bus can be.
