I have towed several Buses and I prefer to hook to the front beam. I built a
tow bar that hooks to the bumper bolts. I towed a 74 Bus from Tacoma Wa. To
Salem Or. And then the new owner towed it to the Coast. It worked fine for
both of us, but I never did like the fact that it only used 4 small bolts.(I
added several safety chains)
I have also towed them with a Bug tow bar attached to the sway bar for very
short tows, this is a bad way to go. Way too much play and the Bus is about
2 feet behind the tow vehicle. I am now building a tow bar that will attach
to the beam with U-Bolts from a rear wheel drive cars axle(the spring to
axle U-bolts). These U-bolts are very strong and at least grade 5 not like
the grade 3 All-thread. If you get one that attaches to the beam, you can
tow any year. If it attaches to the bumper only, you are limited to up to
72, or 73+. I have never towed a Vanagon.
