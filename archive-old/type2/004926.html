Hey Shawn,

When I had to get some studs for my Vanagon, I ran into the same problem
with the local FLAPS supply. Instead of drilling my hubs, I took the studs
and used the bench grinder to take off most of the knurling on the shank of
the stud. It was not as hard as I thought it would be to take it off pretty
evenly (I did have to use a file to take off the radius left at the
shank/head intersection). Then I pulled them through the hole with a spare
lug nut, a socket for a spacer and a lot of loctite blue for lubricant. It
trashed the lug nut, but it pulled them through fine and snug. Loctite green
or red would be more appropriate. (I did have a caliper to measure with,
maybe not a good idea without one.)

I was paranoid that one would loosen when I tightened the lug nuts (that
would have been bad news!) but it went fine. I too thought about drilling,
but I could not be sure of the exact size bit to use, and those flanges are
really thick too. I think that the grinding procedure qualifies as closest
to the DM under a casual interpretation.
