Subject: Re: [T2] "bucking" engine on 77 bus (long)

On Tue, Sep 02, 2003 at 09:56:02PM -0400, hejira wrote:
> After driving on the highway for a number of hours at about 60
> mph(i.e, over two hours so the engine gets nice and warm) the engine
> will sporadically "buck" or surge to the point where the whole bus
> jerks forward as a result. 

I have had 2 things cause this on my 79. 1 is the temp sensor II. the
other is an afm that had a worn area on the contact and caused a
certain range to get a bad connection and "lurch". The sensor is a
small device on the drivers side of the engine that screws into the
head. It has a single wire connection. It is tested by measuring the
resistance (in ohms) at particular temperatures. You might want to try
and see if the little bugger is loose (especially when hot) which
would cause a bad connection. 

> I haven't done a lot of investigation but my preliminary guesses are
> spark plug wires or dist cap. The plugs are pretty new as mem.
> serves. I *hope* it is not a FI problem as I have heard some horror
> stories trying to diagnose this.

anyone who has horror stories about FI has simply not spent the time
to read the FI section of the bentley. It's a very simple system that
can be tested completely with just miminal tools. if you don't have
the bentley, get it.
