The cold start system can be messing you up in a couple of ways: not
providing the "squirt" of gas you need to start when it's cold, or,
providing that "squirt" when you *don't* need it, flooding the engine.
If you suspect the latter (strong smell of gas back at the engine) try
starting it with the electrical connection to the cold start valve
disconnected; if you've been trying to start it and the engine's
flooded, holding the gas pedal to the floor will give the engine the
air it needs or wait several hours, disconnect hoses to the air
distribution box, and let the gas evaporate.
