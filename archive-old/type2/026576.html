The rear shifter coupling seems to be the weak point in the linkage.  It is
found right where the tranny shifter shaft attaches to the rear shift rod
and is a metal bracket with 2 rubber/plastic inserts held together by a long
setscrew.  These are pretty easy to replace and don't cost much (of course,
you'll end up with a face full of dirt and crap so you might put on your
safety goggles for this one.  When I did mine, I didn't replace the metal
bracket portion (couldn't get to the set screw to remove it) and just
replace the inserts with the new ones.  I used a "high performance"
polyurethane one first and hated it.  It transmitted a lot more tranny noise
up the shiftrod into the bus.  Replaced it with and even cheaper
plastic/rubber one and am much happier.  YMMV
