Volks,

An addition on this subject...  Howard Magen sent me an e-mail suggesting
that SPS Flexloc nuts were very appropriate for the purpose of keeping
the parking brake lever afixed to the brake shoe.  I did a little looking
around and their web site is:

http://192.215.160.123/sps/admin.html

The flexloc nuts look very appropriate for this application and should be
"available from most any industrial fastener distributor or jobber" as
Howard suggested to me.

In addition, in the past I bought a box of "copper exhaust nuts" that
turned out to be wretched as exhaust nuts but would probably be wonderful
in this application.  They had a slot cut into the side of the nut and the
crown of the nut was then bent down to provide a locking force.  The
problem is that the copper turned out to be coating on plain steel nuts,
which rusted badly inside a year.  I gave up on them after that and have
no idea of the manufacturer.  Indeed, I would give thes pups a hearty "not
recommended" on their advertised application... in exhaust applications,
go for stainless!!  But I would say that any all-metal locking nut would
work on the parking brake lever.  If you can't find one, at least deform
the threads on (peen) the bolt. You do *not* want that nut coming adrift
inside the brake area!
