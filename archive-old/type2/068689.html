There's obviously a difference between the '78 bus and '81 van I'm not
aware of.  In my van the trans is secured to the chassis _only_ by the
transverse bolt.  The rear of the trans bolts to the front of the engine,
and the engine is secured to the chassis by a rear carrier bar.  Since
the rear sheet metal of the car body can't be removed, there is no way
to remove the engine without lowering the rear of the trans.

I'm in over my head, since I've never worked on a '78.  I can't imagine,

> 
> By the way the fuel tank was very easy so I don't know why you say its

In the van, the fuel tank is positioned under the chassis in such a way
that you're forced to work "blind" up over the top of the tank (in about
2" of space) trying to make various connections.  I'm sure during manufacture
the tank was installed before all the running gear so that assemblers 
could work behind the tank, reaching up over the top to make these
connections.

> 
> How do you tighten up the gearbox bolts, is the design different in that
> there is no tinware in the way?
> 

I'm not sure what bolts we're talking about since it sounds like differences
between '78 and '81 make the comparison difficult.  On the van there are 2
bolts which insert through the trans and through the bell housing of the
engine, and 2 studs which are affixed to the trans and go through 2 other
holes in the bell housing.  The tinware is not involved at all.  (Although
getting nuts attached and tightened down on the top 2 bolts is a struggle;
if you can stand on you head and have fingers as thin as spatulas, it sure
helps!)
