Coating the inside of the hub would be fine for rust prevention, but packing
the inside is a waste of time and money, you are going to have to clean all
that back out to clean all the small particles that could re-enter the

I should have been more specific on the over packing statement.  Being a
teacher, in the summers I used to work as a mechanic for industry.  I don't
think on a VW the seals are tight enough, but in industry, many high speed
shaft bearings can be overpacked which can cause excessive heat build up
that can ruin the bearing.  Call down to your local industrial bearing
outlet and ask them about it.

The front disk brakes on a bus do get very hot under heavy braking
conditions and that heat can cause the grease to heat to a point it expands
the grease and pushes it past the seal and dust cap.

All this talk about front wheel bearings, does everyone know that VW
suggested packing the REAR wheel bearings every 30,000 miles?  Those rear
wheel bearings take more of a beating than the front ones do!  They carry
more weight and have the torque of the drive train added to them.
