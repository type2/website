Hi Tom,

Todd Hill, when he was active here in the past, recommend the Redline
synthetic... and he was also big on using GL4 (and not using GL5).  This was
with the two transaxles I had him build for me (1967 and earlier style).

Anyway, from what I understand, it is sulfur that is of concern... GL5 has
it... sulfur and brass (the synchro sliders).

I also have found other VW Transaxle rebuilders to endorse Redline... here
is a message from the Archives that might be of interest:
http://vw.i405_dot_com/sm.asp?path=\vintagebus_at_type2\Y1999\September\9448

I say go for it on the Redline... especially if you can afford it :)  Me, I
went with the Sta-Lube GL4 stuff... cheaper... I like more frequent changes
to check condition of transaxle (shavings, gear teeth, and so forth on
magnet).  Barb&Eric made this point very well in this Archive message:
http://vw.i405_dot_com/sm.asp?path=\vintagebus_at_type2\Y1999\March\1360

Hope this helps.

  Stephen Gutknecht
