Otoh, if you do it with the suspension somewhat compressed it makes it a
lot easier to start all those bolts into their threaded holes in the
flanges.  I use a small floor jack and carefully jack beneath the shock
mount.  Since the shock mount is behind the axle, this gives a greater
torque on the torsion bar and compresses the suspension a bit more.  I
just barely lift the tire off of the ground so I can rotate the axle to
get at all the bolts and then hold the tire with my knee to torque the
bolts.  That way I can do them all at one time.

Needless to say, I block all three remaining wheels securely before
working under the bus supported in this manner.  With the wheel on, if the
jack fails the bus won't drop, since the wheel will simply drop a quarter
inch or so to the ground

My Rockford boots have the old-style clamp on the flange side of the boot.
I replaced the crimp clamp with a narrow worm drive hose clamp.  Adding
lube is just a matter of loosening the clamp and prying the boot from the
flange.  Not all that hard to do, but be sure to clean everything first so
that dirt won't find its way into the lube.
