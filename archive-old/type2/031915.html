On Thu, 25 Feb 1999, Gary Basey wrote:

>      I recently purchased an '80 Westy that had a great interior but 
> wouldn't run (paid $200).  I'm currently rebuilding the 2.0 but have a 
> couple of questions.
> 
> 1. In an effort to fix the problem the PO removed the fuel injection 
> wires (I don't know why!). The problem is he didn't mark electrical 
> plugs so I can't tell which injector to plug them into.  I have a 50% 
> chance of getting them right.  How can I find out which plug goes where?  

If you lay the wires in place, the "bend" in them will probabaly tell you 
where they went.  However, it doesn't matter since the ECU fires all the 
injectors at the same time.  That is, you can't get it "wrong."

> 2. Is it necessary to always replace the oil cooler with rebuilding the 
> engine?  Is it possible to flush the oil cooler out and replace it 
> later?  I'm on a limited budget with this project, but would like to 
> start camping locally ASAP.

No.  Unless the engine self-destructed and there's a real risk of metal 
particles contaminatting the cooler, you can clean it out and use it 
again.  Slosh a solvent through it several times until it's perfectly 
clean, let the solvent dry, and you can use it again.
