Maybe your vacuum gauge is lying to you... can you borrow another one to
compare with?  Is the connection between gauge and manifold impeccable?

How about killing one cylinder at a time?  Hook up the gauge and pull
one spark plug wire while watching the gauge and listening.  Put that
plug wire back and pull the next one, etc.  This may not have a huge
effect on the gauge as the piston will still be sucking air, but if one
cylinder makes the gauge do something that the other three don't, you
have narrowed it down a little.

I'd call it an isolator, maybe.  Not quite the same thing as, say, a
fuel pressure gauge isolator, but close.  Many cars with vacuum operated
accessories (heater doors and cruise controls on many older cars) have
some kind of vacuum reservoir under the hood that could be used.  Many
GM cars had a black plastic sphere about 5" diameter, and many Fords had
one that looked exactly like a tin can, about the size of a one-pound
coffee can.  Don mentioned the Wabbit one that was a series of small
spheres and was sometimes, literally, on the underside of the hood.

If you didn't have one handy, you could probably make one out of a
section of pipe, plus threaded end caps on each end to step down to a
pipe thread-hose barb adapter for the hose.  You need a decent amount of
volume; that 5" sphere would be about 66 cubic inches.
