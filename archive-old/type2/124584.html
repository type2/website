Markus,
The load in a moving vehicle affects many of its systems. Suspension is the
most obvious, but engine and braking ability are also important. I am
assuming you have the proper tires installed; most often the load rating and
inflation values are overlooked (ask me how I know, a really big load of
glossy paper brochures in a '50 Chevy P.U. with passenger car tires. Live
and learn). Given that you are within spec, how you drive is most important.
Lots of stopping distance, low speeds, and focus on the job at hand will get
you to your destination.
Guesses at safe limits as time passes? As long as everything has been
properly maintained and there are no structural defects, spring compression
(can I call it that on a torsion spring?) is your only real factor. This can
be compensated for by resetting the spring plate on the torsion spring. Some
believe that the springs may work harden and  possibly break. Given that
VW's have multi leaf (again, I have a problem describing a torsion spring in
such terms) springs, catastrophic failure is unlikely, but a capacity load
increases those odds.
I would not hesitate to load a VW bus, properly maintained, to its rated
capacity regardless of age.
