Tommy, list:
I have no doubt you could do a nice job without a spray gun.
I have, in my vast collection of collectables, an old Sherwin- Williams product
guide that details painting auto (Circa 1925!). It starts out " the key to a
good finish is a high quality brush"!
I'm sure you could still do such a thing with SOLID color paints (no metallics
or micas).
Slower (higher evaporating point) thinners are what makes paint flow out and
have gloss, however, they also allow the paint to run!
To get the runs and brushmarks out you'd need to block sand it, maybe 400 grit.
And then progressively finer grits and then rubbing compound. To get it hard
enough to sand, you'd need lacquer or to catylize acrylic enamel or possibly
force dry it with heat lamps.

some pics. (Couldn't possibly look worse than it does now!)
