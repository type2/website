but it could also be a problem with the distributor
itself, as well as a vacuum leak... I had a similar
problem that was cured with a new distributor.

And yes, I use a "crappy" .009 ;-) Not to start
another Holy-War here, but in an upright motor that is
properly tuned, a .009 works just fine. I have *zero*
problems with the .009 in my '69 Westy (1600sp),
including the notorious "lag" that some people
experience from a dead stop. 

Also, compare the price of a new .009 at $50.00 to a
new vac-advance at (Gee, I dunno....I'd have to *find*
one first;-)... I think you see my point! *If* you can
find the proper vac-advance distributor (new), you're

worth it? Not in my book...
