Actually, the fourth digit means something but since for the type 2 it
is always a "2" anyway, I didn't want to explain. In the late 1960ties
VW used a 9 digit VIN:

	239 012 345

The first three numbers stand for "model" (23-kombi) and "modelyear"
(9=1969). The rest is a serial number. Since VW made about 250.000 T2's
a year this could be anything from 000000 until 250000 or so.

>From 1970 until 1979 VW used a 10 digit VIN:

	2302 012 345

The first three numbers are the same as in the sixties: "model"
(23-kombi) and "modelyear" (0=1970). The fourth digit is a "2" to
distinghuish it from the 1960ties numbers but in case the production
would reach 1.000.000 vehicles the "2" would turn into a "3". This never
happened with the Transporter production but with the beetle it did.
