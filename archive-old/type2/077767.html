Subject: Re: [T2] running engine with no seal, what happens?

Yes, the engine will overheat by pulling air from the lower parts of the
engine and "superheating" the air over and over.  A aircooled engine needs
cool air going through it, not preheated air.  Of course in the winter, this
is not such a problem, but I would get a seal in there as soon as possible,
they are cheap.  Fixing problems when you find them, or better yet BEFORE
the problems arise will give you many miles of trouble free driving, it's
called preventive maintainence.
