>- At higher engine speeds (idle RPM is not constant) however,
>cyl. 2 and cyl. 1 disconnection does seem to have the correct effect.

At higher engine speeds, other things are going on.  At higher engine
speeds, you have both idle and main fuel circuits contributing, and the
effects of low compression are not as pronounced because there is less time
for compression to escape from the combustion chamber when the engine is
spinning faster.

>I guess that means cyls. 1&2 need overhaul. And that means engine
>removal... Hmm. I've never liked the idea of doing that.

Don't conclude that just yet.  We were using the idle-wire-pull test to try
and get an estimate of compression.  While a drop in rpm is a sure
indication of at least *some* compression, no drop doesn't necessarily mean
no compression.  This gets back to the assumption that your fuel/spark
systems are working 100%.  I think your idle circuit on the right carb may
not be working properly.  Have you fiddled with the set screw in the
throttle body yet?  Have you checked for vacuum leaks at the carb/manifold
and manifold/head junctions?  Search the archives at http://vw.i405_dot_com for
info on how to check both of these things.  Also I would recommend getting
a copy of the Bentley manual for 68-79 busses.  It has a good procedure for
adjusting the dual carbs.
