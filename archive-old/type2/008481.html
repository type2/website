> talking about rockers *and* lifters.  In other words, the lifters have
> wheels on the cam end which "ride" over the lobes.  Roller rockers are nice
> but they don't do much for cam wear - the roller lifters are what saves the

This is done in the watercooled world all of the time, but
they dont use one lobe to work two lifters.

What I always thought would make a nice design would
be a spherical ball stamped into the end of the lifter, but
free to move around. Drill an oil passage through to the 
center, same as the pushrod lube path. A ball about the same
size as a CV ball should be about right. Being asymmetrical,
it could rotate any way it wanted, but could still rotate to
lessen friction. (Not as much as a roller would, though.)
