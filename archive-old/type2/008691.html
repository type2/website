Replacing the coldstart injector is not a big thing.  Yes, you
have to pull the dizzy, but what I do is get an awl and lightly
scribe a mark on the dizzy and the clamp.  Replace the cold start
valve. When you put the dizzy back in, you just realign the scribe
marks and you sould be almost dead on.  

One thing to do to test it though, is to get the engine good and 
hot and then, with a piece of metal tubing, just plumb around the 
cold start valve.  If you run fine after, then your valve is shot.
If it runs the same, something else is it.  Make sure you don't 
ignore the head temp sensor too.  If it reads too high a resistence,
then you'll run to rich also.  When they're BOTH bad, well, I had
it happen and it WASN'T fun!!

Good luck.
Mark
