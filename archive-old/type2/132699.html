Subject: RE: [T2] Dual Port - What does that mean?

Some argue that the single port induces more turbulence at low RPM, thus
giving a more mixed fuel air mixture. The result supposedly is more
power at low rpm when compared to a dual port. Since we need all the low
end power we can get in our buses, you'll see some people prefer to
single port to the dual port head. I think the single port has more
metal in the head and is less susceptible to cracking. Somebody correct
me if I'm wrong.



Steve
