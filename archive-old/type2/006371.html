No matter what distributor you've been running, the maximum advance of 52*
would cause serious problems.  You're very lucky the pistons haven't all
melted by now.

There is some difference of opinion about the max. advance that's acceptable
[it varies from 28 to 32 degrees], but most will agree a max. advance of 29*
is a safe and conservative setting.  And this applies to _any_ distributor
you might install.

If there's no obvious problem with your present vacuum advance distributor,
try setting it with a max. advance of 29* and see how it runs.  That should
give you an idle speed setting between 0* BTDC to 7.5* BTDC depending on
your engine and the model/type of vac. advance distributor you're using.

A good auto electric shop can check, analyze, and calibrate your distributor
using a distributor testing machine.  Overall, you'll be better off with the
stock distributor if it is repairable.
