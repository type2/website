Again I agree. I was amazed at how much better my engine ran in the winter 
when I plumbed hot air to the air cleaner, as VW did. But while VW may have 
had a reason for installing what they did, I don't think everything is 
necessary. I wouldn't remove the thermostat, for example, but I have no 
qualms with ditching the throttle positioner and tuning the engine to run 
without it. VW engineering is bone-simple. It can, and has been, improved 
upon many times by many people. It took VW years to bring out the doghouse 
cooler and by that time, some owners had begun auxilliary cooling on their 
own. So there's no reason to believe VW was perfect.

Philosophy does account for much of it. I don't expect to break a piston 
skirt before I rebuild this engine, or run it out of oil and gall the 
bearings ... but I *could,* and if I did I might have to replace the oil 
pump because I had no screen ... but then I would most likely replace the 
oil pump anyway, so it doesn't matter to me.

I might give that oil-pressure relief sender a try on the next engine, 
though. This mounts in one of the plunger holes under the engine? Sounds 
better than the sump plate model, which inevitable picks up a lot of oil.
