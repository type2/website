To the contrary, new cars do have de-humidifiers in them. The air 
conditioning works quite effectively as a de-humidifier, and is 
usually engaged automatically when you select defrost/demist. Also, 
new cars have better insulation, better door seals, and they've got 
body exhausters, which promote better ventilation. One of the 
problems with the heat system in a bus is that even when you fix all 
the leaks, the incoming heat has nowhere to go. Opening a window can 
help, but the real fix would be to locate exhausters in a negative 
pressure area. Exhausters are essentially one way valves. All new 
cars have them, and they are usually located under the rear bumper 
cover surround, in or around the tail lights, or, on some cars, under 
a grille (that seems to be decorative) along the rear sides of the 
car. They are crucial to proper climate control.

Installing a modern climate control system in a bus would be pretty 
difficult, but not impossible. You'd need to do some experimentation 
to find the right place for body exhausters, and re-work the heating 
and ventilation system considerably.
