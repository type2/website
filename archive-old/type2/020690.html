HMF STOERMER wrote:

> I'd suggest starting the inspection by making sure the carb is firmly
> mounted on the manifold, and that there's actually a gasket in there.  The
> mounting bolts don't have to be very loose for air to be drawn in through
> that interface.
>
> The carburetor body casting material is 'relatively' soft.  It's not unusual
> for an improperly mounted carb to have a slight arc across the base.  This
> is the result of overtightening of the mounting bolts when a too thick, too
> soft, or incomplete gasket was used.  Less common is carb base distortion
> because of being mounted on a manifold with an arced mounting flange.
>
> If base distortion is bad enough the lower part of the carb throat can be
> ovaled and the throttle butterfly may be sticking or unable to move/function
> properly.  A carb with an ovaled throat is beyond repair.
>
> FWIW.....
