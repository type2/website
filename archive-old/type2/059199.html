  You might find that using 1/4 inch plywood a more viable solution.
Treated and covered or painted it will last a lot longer, the cost is
not that great and will probably look a fair amount better then
cardboard. It also lends it self to a number of coverings form vinyl
adhesive,  carpet, paint , tile, just about any thing you can think of
and will stand up to wear and tear much better. Properly fitted and
secured, it should probably last almost the life of the bus, granted a
slightly more involved install, although in the long run probably
cheaper and easier to maintain. And would probably give more over all
satisfaction, and considering that a 4x8 foot sheep of finished  1/4
plywood generally runs about $10-15  the total over all cost should be
well under $75 to do all of the interior, and being as thin as it is it
will add it self to a small amount of curving with out any special
effort, and can be forced in to more dramatic curves with the use of a
couple of block and a weight. Might be some thing to think about before
you go and duct tape up cardboard.. but that's just my $0.02 worth..
