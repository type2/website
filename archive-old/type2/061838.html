I have the diagrams for most of the eberspacher heaters.. including the BN-2
However, the BN-2 is the heater most often found in beetles.
The original system in a 1980 Vanagon should be a BA-6
So..is it the little gray, round unit with the red fan in the end (BN-2)
or the big, hulking monster installed under the floor (BA-6)?
you may all know this but, For the record :
B= Bezine (gasoline)
D= Diesel
N= self contained, independent heater
A= heat "booster" that works with the cars duct work etc.
2,4,6= number of Kilocalories per hour produced by the heater alone

So..the type 181 has a BN-4 as does the bus with the heater in the engine
compartment
Bugs etc? BN-2 in the trunk(no temperature regulation on these,BTW..off or
HOT!)
the 411-412 has a BA-4 above the transaxle,
the later buses with the heater between the frame rails? BA-6
and the lowly diesel Vanagon...DA-6....
Simple, non?
