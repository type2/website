Subject: Re: [T2] Electric Fuel Pump on T1 engine?

On Wed, 12 Mar 2003, John Baker wrote:

> I have recently seen a couple of vendors selling
> electric fuel pumps that can mount in the same spot as
> my stock mechanical fuel pump on my 1600 DP engine.

If your stock pump is working, leave it be!

> Anyone have experience with doing this? Do they work
> well? Are they really just a scam? Where does it have
> to be wired to get power, coil? Run wire to fuse box?

They work OK.  You may have to add a pressure regulator if the pump puts
out too much pressure.  Usually power is drawn from the hot wire on the
coil, but make sure that you're not overloading that circuit.

> I have noticed that my fuel filter seems to run empty
> a lot. Even after installing a new mechanical fuel
> pump.

Filters seldom run full as they trap bubbles in the fuel.  Nothing to
worry about unless you have fuel starvation problems.  I don't think that
my fuel filter has ever run full.

> I don't know if this has caused any issues with
> my engine getting enough fuel or not, but it seems
> like it could. Could the electric replacement help
> this out?

I think that it's a non-problem.  The stock setup is reliable and doesn't
tax the electrical system.  Unless you have a really good reason to change
it (hot engine that needs more fuel flow, perhaps), stick with the stock
pump.
