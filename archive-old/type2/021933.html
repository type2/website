Don't rely on opinions.  Tackle the problem scientifically.  Replace all the
ignition stuff with tune up parts: wires, plugs, cap, rotor, (no points for
you) and a new fuel filter.  Adjust and check the timing.  Start by
adjusting the valves, then warm up the motor and adjust the timing with a
light (for your model it should be 5 deg ATDC).  After that, set your carb
in accordance with the instructions (I only know how to adjust stock).  Your
motor WILL run poorly until warm, especially if you are running a 009.  The
advance distributors really help a cold motor.  Mine still runs bad until it
is warm.  I don't know about what your friends opinion.  It would run REALLY
bad if two cylinders were not firing.  Do the steps I mentioned and get back
to the list with you finding or p-email me.

Justin
