So, I was playing around with the engine stand mount and finally decided
(with the help of list member's suggestions) that I would have to take
the clutch off to safely attach the stand mount.  So, I got the clutch
off pretty easily, but MAN are those flywheel nuts tight!!!  I used the
idea in the Muir book but used a pair of plyers around a bolt through
the engine mount and into the teeth of the flywheell while I used my
wrench to undo the bolts.  I was going to leave the flywheel off, but
I'm glad I didn't because I wouldn't want to inadvertantly scratch the
innards or get any oil inside there.  That'd prolly be bad ;)

off.  If I have time I'll try and get the exhaust off too.  I'm very
anxious to get to that cylinder #4 to find out why I lost compression!
