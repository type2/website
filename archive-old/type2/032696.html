Greetings -

And welcome to bus-wheel hell...

I guess I didn't quite get the year of the breadloaf.

The 68-70's (drum brakes all around) use the wide-5 bolt pattern, and I've
heard that the pre-68 15" wheel are a direct fit. Anybody heard different?

The disk brake models have 112mm diameter lugs. A wheel book I saw showed
only Audi Turbo and Mercedes wheels as a identical fit, though I don't have
the data on the wheel offset.

Be careful of the American 4-1/2" wheel patterns!! That translates to
114.3mm, and may "look" right if you try to physically mount a wheel on the
hub, but would certainly cause some sort of bad things while under load.

If you're looking to create taller gearing, there's a "baja bus" article in
TYPE2 that says 215*75/14 should fit front and 225*75 in back. Those rears
would yield a calculation of 678mm diameter tire, as opposed to 633mm for a
more popular 185*75 - a 7% (speed increase / rpm decrease) difference. I
would be more inclined to play it safe, and put 205*75's all around, for
663mm and a 5% difference.

Good luck -
