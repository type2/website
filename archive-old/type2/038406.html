Also consider the gear ratios of the various model years. The 68-71 had
these 5.375 R&P ratios that make the engine turn around 3700 rpm _at_ 60 mph.
>From 72-75 (me thimks) the R&P went to 4.86 (3300 rpm), and the 76-? 4.37
R&P (.88 4th) lowered it slightly to about 3250 rpm.

Up to 68-70 used 1600 single port upright motors. 71 used the 1600 dual
port. 72 on up used 1700-2000cc type IV motors.

Thru 70 used drum brakes up front. 71-72 used the first generation disks.
73-? had bigger, beefier disks.

Of the upright motored busses, the 71 was probably the "best", seeing as it
had the disks up front. Of the pancakes motored bussessess, I'd probably go
with a 2-liter (76 and up?), due to the higher final gearing. Then I'd go
looking for a Carerra motor... but that's a Friday thing.
