Subject: Spraying starting fluids into airbox (was " Runs and Runs")

NOT a good idea to spray into the air cleaner with a starting fluid- we 
did this on a 75 and it backfired, ruining the flow flapper.  Starting 
fluid should be introduced AFTER the AFM, as into one of the tubes 
feeding the airbox.

Chris M. wrote:

See if you can get it to briefly fire with a generous squirt of WD-40 or 
other flammable spray. My guess is that all is well except the fuel supply.

  If it will fire with a spray then you can say you've confirmed 
ignition and the mechanical condition.

  Usually takes a long spray into the air cleaner which the engine is 
cranking. Never a problem for us but keep a fire extinguisher handy and 
the bus not in the garage.
