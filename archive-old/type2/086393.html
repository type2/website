Paul,

The '68-'71 wiper switch.

A hot wire from the third fuse runs to the brake light switch. The brake
switch on the master cylinder makes a connection when the brake is pressed,
this brings 12 v to the 54 terminal on the emergency flasher switch, then
back out on 54f to the turn signal switch terminal 54st. When the turn
signal switch is in the neutral position the now hot 54st is mechanically
connected to both HR and HL these brighten the brighter filament in the rear
tail lights. When the turn signal switch is put into the left or right
position. The 54bl terminal is contributing and now the emergency flasher
relay interrupts the power intermittently to turn on then off the left or
right bright filament. The other brake light (none directional side) is
still on solid.

1. No
2. Yes
3. No

The tail lights are the dim filament and the brake and the turn signal are
both the bright filament.
