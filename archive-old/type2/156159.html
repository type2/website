Subject: Walmart awning - "Dining Canopy"

Ok guys and gals, I finally got around to making pics of the Walmart  awning.  I had a lot of requests.  I was going to make a web  page of them but my HTML is rusty and I got frustrated trying.  So  I'll just do it here.  If you just want to see the assembled  product, scroll to the bottom pic.  It's cheap and easy to  assemble and it's the perfect height for the bus.  
  
  The carrying case:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/Case.jpg
  
  The tarp clips for cheap, cheap at Harbor Freight:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/HF-AwningClips.jpg
    
  Buy two of these connectors in the PVC pipe section of your hardware  store.  IIt takes a 7/8" wrench.   I don't know what they're  called and neither did the kid in the store.  So here are 3 Pics:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/Connector1.jpg
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/Connector2.jpg
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/Connector3.jpg
  
  The first time, you'll need the wrench to thread it up into the leg  holders.  There are fine plastic strips up in side and it will cut  threads into these.  After that, they go in and out pretty easily  by hand.  Self-tapping, har.   When screwed in it looks  like this:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/InLegHolder.jpg
  
  The poles are spring-loaded so they just pop right into place:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/PoleSprings.jpg
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/AssembledPole.jpg
  
  Three sizes of poles:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/AllPoles.jpg
  
  Assemble the frame as per pretty good factory instructions:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/Frame.jpg
  
  Not easy with one person, you'll let it fall apart a couple of times at first, but assemble the whole thing first like this:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/FrameAssembled.jpg
  
  
  Now remove a rear corner leg, screw that fitting into it, and carefully  rotate the frame around until one end and the fitting will set in the  gutter.  Now do the other side.  It helps to make sure that  all the fittings are all the way in.   Go around to the other  corners and the center and move everything a little at a time checking  the fittings as you go:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/FrameOnBus.jpg
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/ConnectorInGutter.jpg
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/InRearGutter.jpg
  
  Now, pull the tarp over and snap the velcro tabs over the sides.   In the gutters, pull tarp up underneath the leg holder/connector.   Take a tarp clip and clip it over the tarp and into the gutter.   Roll up the excess and tuck it under the frame:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/TarpInGutter.jpg
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/InFrontGutter.jpg
  
  Now take the other two clips.  One in the gutter and one on the  tarp and a piece of string to pull them together and secure the  center.  I didn't get it as tight as normal in this pic.  If  you monkey with it, you can get it down below top level:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/CenterClips.jpg 
  
  Here's it fully assembled:
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/FullyAssembled.jpg
  
  But this one's the best,har.  Cya at EveryBus!
  
  Http://www.bopsax_dot_com/Bopmobile/Awning/CyaAtEverybus.jpg
  
  I spent all afternoon messing with my computer to get these pics  up.  USB cable to camera must be bad.  Wolf Camera put them  on a disc for me.   
  For a price!  I'm just praying that there's not a link limit or  something that will kick this back.  Then I'll have to brush up on  my HTML to do it.  Hope this was helpful.  Cheers...KR
