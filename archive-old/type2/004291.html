Since people have been building (and rebuilding) the basic internal
combustion engine for a hundred years or so, I wouldn't think getting
good hiop in an engine would be a "trade secret" by now, unless the
rebuilder was doing a radical redesign of the basic engine or its
components.  Isn't the "secret" to good hiop to look at the engine's
spec's, particularly bearings and oil pump, and to work these
components (i.e, "blueprint" the parts) down to "minimum clearance"
specs?  It would seem to me the "secret" to good hiop is to have
perfectionist tendencies, and the equipment, and the skill, and the
time, to get the critical tolerances "right."
