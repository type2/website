Chris n all,

I think you'll find that another common name for this tar sheeting is
"Malfoid", at least that's what it's known as in the old boating set ( I've
got friends with old boats, and this WAS the "proper" was to seal wooden
decks on the early 20th Century work boats ).

Probably the easiest way to confirm whether we're talking about the same
thing is, does it come with a sand layer to prevent the sheets sticking
together, and does is split easily if you try and bend it without heating
it? If so, we're probably talking about the same thing. If so, older "work"
boat Chandlers would probably stock it as well, and the price isn't too high
there, either.

NB. The traditional way of fixing it down is with tins of thinned tar, think
yourself lucky you used spray adhesive, you were as much tar as the surface
you're fixing the sheeting to when you spend a day on your knees with a 4"
brush!
