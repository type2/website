Mike/All,


I don't know the source of the fogging, but I'm
willing to bet some cold, hard cash that it's not
related to whether they are mounted tightly or not.

I have the typical VDO trio (oil-temp, oil-press,
cyl-head temp)<as well as the large VDO tach in place
of the factory clock> under my stereo, above the
ashtray. Mine are held in place
by...(ahem!)...duct-tape! And I have *no* fogging
problem. Not in dry-arsed Arizona, nor in soggy-arsed
Oregon.

Now, before anyone chews me a new one, the guages are
mounted 'solidly' in their holes...I simply used the
duct-tape to wrap around the individual guages and
fill the gaps. I actually threw the VDO screw-on
mounts away, and rely on the tight mount to hold them
in place. It's worked fine so far (2+ years, 18,000+
miles).. and I can remove the guage-trio quite easily
for access behind the dash.

Is it the best way to mount? Probably not. But I had
my reasons;-)

Still, I don't have the fogging problem, so I think
it's safe to say that this is not the culprit.

Contrary to another listees advice, I would suggest
sealing the guage itself, but make sure that *all*
moisture has been removed, first. Maybe place them in
a *very* low-temp oven for a few hours, then seal
them???


I dunno. Good luck at any rate!


Jaime
