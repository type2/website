> Dear Volks,

> The '74-'79 VW bus shop manual
> (http://www.simplecom_dot_net/dakhlia/images/VW-90.4.jpg) shows how you can
cut
> into the rear deck for direct access to the fuel tank sender. (Yes, it's a
> trade-off, but I wasn't going to pull the engine to pull the firewall to
> pull the tank, which, knowing myself, would have lead to a complete engine
> rebuild. :)  The key, I think, is in doing a *neat*, clean job.)
>
> For the record, I just wanted to point out three things:
>
> (1) The "lid" part of the '73-'79 sender has a 3-5/8" diameter, hence will
> not fit through the hole if it is cut to the smaller dimensions given in
the
> manual. (This also means that the non-availability of the VW cover plate
> shown in the manual is irrelevant to '73-'79 busses.) I went ahead and cut
a
> 3" x 4" rectangle.
>
> (2) There is a fair amount of room between the rear deck and the sender,
> thus allowing the use of an electric saber saw (set to medium speed) to
cut
> the hole without cutting into the fuel tank, too. Obviously you must use a
> short blade for metal. For easy gliding of the saber saw, I oiled the deck
> surface. The oil will also help cool the blade, prolong its life, as well
as
> bind the small metal particles.
>
> (3) Finally use a vacuum cleaner to remove shavings and most of 20+ years
> worth of dirt sitting on the tank and sender. Wipe everything clean with a
> wet rag. Now you can remove the old sender and install the new one.
>
