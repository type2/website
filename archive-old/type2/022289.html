I drove part of the east coast of Italy around Bari. I lived in Naples for 
3 years.

The place seems to be the wilds of Italy. Pretty sparse but at least you 
are driving a VW and there should be good repair service everywhere if you 
need it.

Right south of Brindisi is a region full of chestnut groves. Gorgeous 
travelling if you stay on the small highways. Try the Via Aurelia. Very 
flat and much drier than the western parts I travelled more commonly. There 
used to be a US base there - a communications base. I don't remember the 
name but I can look it up if it is important.

Are you US military? (I want to move back over someday --- civilian DOD 
contractor or something...)

Be sure to keep the tanks full as - in my opinion - the Agip gas stations 
(the only stations our GI issue gas coupons were good at) were not as 
common as in the denser populated areas.

Beware - my friend in Naples tells me that gas is right around $5.15 a 
gallon right now.
