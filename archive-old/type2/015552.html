I can only guess that the commutator is worn and rough. What else could eat
the brushes?? Poor quality brushes perhaps, but truing and polishing the
commutator might put things right. If the commutator and brushes are not
mated properly, it can account for the low and varying output. Old and
porous solder at the armature wires will pass fewer electrons as well as
generate excess heat that will increase brush and bearing wear. I would go
for a genuine Bosch reman and be done with generator problems for a long
time. 

I don't see the need or advantage of an alternator with a stock system. It
won't solve your problems any better than a new/reman generator. Compared
to a generator that has served a full life, either will be a vast
improvement.
