Excuse the boring units talk, but (from Marks Handbook, 7th ed):

kW-hr to BTU (ENERGY) [or kW to BTU/hr(POWER)] :  multiply by
3412 BTU/kW-hr   The number in Harry's table is correct, the
units are off.

kcal to BTU:  multiply by 3.968 BTU/kcal  The number in the table
is off.  The 4000 kcal/hr heater would be a 16 000 BTU/hr heater.
Sound more real?

I didn't check the other units.
