For the brake vacuum line, there's a hard hose that comes through the
firewall on the driver's side of the engine compartment and makes a
90degree turn towards the intake manifold and then hooks up to the
manifold.  This piping is hard, not soft like rubber tubing.  Make sure
it isn't leaking anywhere because then you'll get incorrect FI readings,
not to mention dirty air.

I'd make sure that "S" tube is AIRTIGHT!  Any leakage from that tube
would again cause incorrect readings for the AFM (bad).  Next, make sure
all vacuum lines are hooked up and not leaking.  Spend the 6 bux and buy
all new vacuum lines to replace the ones that run to the distributor,
air filter (if you have that one), decel valve, fuel pressure regulator,
retard line (if you have it) and any others in case I forgot.  Make sure
that the vacuum advance that's hooked up to the throttle body is hooked
up to the advance side of the distributor vacuum can.

also make sure that the big hoses that go to the decel valve, aux air
valve, and the other one( can remember where it goes) are all snug and
airtight.

Next, make sure your timing is correct and steady and not hopping all
over the place.  Make sure it's also advancing correctly.

check the fuel pressure, both with the vacuum line on and off.

Check you compression... check your valves (they do need adjusting every
now and then -- couldn't hurt and very easy to do)

that's about all I can think of now... just make sure those air leaks
are fixed!  and also make sure you're not sucking duct tape into the
engine!!!
