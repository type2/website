> Now... the real reason for the post...  the one thing that no one tells
> you is that to successfully bleed brakes, at least by the pumping the
> pedal method, you need to adjust the rear shoes hard against the drums!
> This is true whether you are bleeding the rears or the fronts.  The MC
> piston moves a grand total of maybe 1 cc in a stroke.  If the rear shoes
> have motion, then 90% of that cc will go to moving the rear shoes against
> the drums, then the remaining pittance will push air out.  It goes 10x as
> fast if you use the whole stroke to push air out!  (and, face it, rear
> brake shoes *always* need adjusting :-)
