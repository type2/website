> You don't really need the retard function of the distributor. Retard

I think I want to disagree with that statement for the '71.

Unlike the '76-'78 the '71 bus stock distributor, retard range is
11-13 degrees, while advance range is only 2-5 degrees (i measured 2
degrees on mine). Hence if the retard breaks, the benefits of a stock
vacuum dizzy is mostly gone. At idle, retard is typically ~10 degrees.

The 76-78 does not seem to be a push-pull advance-retard, but advance
only vacuum.
