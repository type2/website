Sorry, my procedure is for a Vanagon, not a Bus.  The Bus and Vanagon
use very different engine and transmission mounting methods.

The transmission on a Bus has a front mount but there are also two bolts
above where the tranny meets the engine that hold the tranny to the
frame.  These must come out in a Bus for the tranny to come out.  The
Vanagon does not have these bolts.  The Vanagon has a hydraulic clutch
(hence the slave cylinder) while the Bus has a cable-operated clutch (no
slave cylinder).  The Bus also has a different shift rod attachment
arrangement than the Vanagon, which has a side shifter attachment to the
tranny.

Other than that, they are fairly similar.  If you were just dropping the
engine and comparing between a Bus and a Vanagon you'd find them quite
similar but if you include the tranny it gets different fast.

I thought I had a procedure for pulling engine/tranny from a Bus on the
site as well as one for a Vanagon.  I may have to double check that.
