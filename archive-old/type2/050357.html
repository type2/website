NQ, don't listen to the nay-sayers! If you pay retail for everything, and I
never do, you will possibly have 2 grand or better in an engine, T-4. BUT the
alternative is like you said, less power. Also, you paid almost 1500 for that
Bug motor, done right. I am now in the process of rebuilding several sets of
1700 heads, and fly-cutting them for 2.0L. With Manley 1 pc. stainless valves,
silicon/aluminum/bronze valve guides, properly tentioned valve springs, a
3-angle valve job and glass-beading, they will cost me about $110.00 per head,
until I get my own Souix grinder! Then, $78.00/head! I refuse to pay the really
exhorbitant rates some machine shops charge for essentially scut work, as there
aren't mant machinists out here who sprechen VW, anymore.I will re-use a good
std crank, micced out, and a cam and lifters that shows little wear, as the
three I now have from scrap engines. The oil pump I usually re4place with a
Mellinng cast-iron unit, as they last longer and can be sealed to the
duraluminum case easily. Other parts are replaced as needed. I built a stock
1700 motor for a lady who immediately drove it to Chicago from S.C. and had no
problems with it(single center-mount DFev weber!), and drove it for seven years
after that, only replacing the front/rear seals and thr push-rod seals at 35k
miles. No new cam, only lifters, rebuilt heads, new p/c's, all new bearings and
rebuilt rods. My cost:$487.00, hers was $800.00,
less than half of youur avg. shops charge(1987 prices).double that today!
