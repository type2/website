Richard,

It isn't hard, just wearing. someone else posted that you should plan on a
full day and I strongly agree.  Your steps are OK, but I have three
points of difference with the manuals....

First, again I say that getting the stub axle out of the bearings is the
hardest part.  With the big Snap-On CJ pullers, it goes fine, but back at
the beginning of my VW career.... it was bad.  I finally resorted to a
no-no that a jack-leg machinist friend taught me and got away with it.  I
reversed the castellated nut and turned it on flush with the end of the
stub axle and drove it out with a BFH.  This is *NOT* recommended by
anyone, but sometimes you do what you have to do.  the brass drift idea
sounds like a winner, but I have not tried it.

Second, something that Bentley and Muir imply is that the spacer between
the the inner and outer bearings will come out and in my experience it
*won't*!  you have to punch around it to get the outer bearing out.  And
it is full of grease, and you have to remember that there is *not* a
shoulder there! which means you have to be careful not to scar the housing
too badly as you drive it.

The third incredible <euphemism>bear</euphemism> is the circlip.  I have
found no common tool that will remove it peacably.  First, you get *ONE*
shot at pulling it with a pair of needle-nose pliers, after which you have
rounded it and it *won't* come out.  After that, I wound up customizing a
pair of chinese needle nose pliers with my dremel tool.  No internal
C-clip tool I have found is strong enough for this pup, I am sorry to say
and I have broken more than a few on it.
