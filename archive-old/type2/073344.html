I'd like to jump into the oil discussion with some unconventional wisdom
from a friend of mine who is an aircraft mechanic and a VW enthusiast. He
runs diesel formulated oils - Shell Rotella, Chevron Delo - in his 80
Vanagon, and has convinced me to do the same with my 78 Bus. His theory is
that the operating conditions of a diesel are more similar to an aircooled
engine than an aircooled is to a watercooled. Therefore the diesel oils are
designed to cope with the high heat and extra combustion by-products better
than oils designed for waterpumpers. These oils are readily available,not
too expensive, and come in a multi-grade 15w-40, which is ideal for winter
here in Florda. During the summer I switch to a straight 40W. Results have
been good.
