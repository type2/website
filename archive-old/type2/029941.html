Probably, since it's a good bet that WM bought the oil from one of the
majors and it's coded with the correct rating.  However, if WM sold a bad
batch of oil the bad press wouldn't make much of a dent in their business.
If Texaco (for example) did the same and it fried a few engines it
definitely would.  Bought any Firestone tires lately?  

My rule of thumb is
that I buy the least expensive brand of oil that has an oil company's name
on the bottle.  that way I can be pretty sure that it is what the specs
say it is.  I change every 2000 miles.  For the transmission, I use
Redline MT-90, since it doesn't get contaminated or changed as often.  If
I had a filter on the engine I might consider synthetic for the engine as
well.
