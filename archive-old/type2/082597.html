On Mon, 25 Feb 2002, RUTHERFORD EDWARD wrote:

> I'm looking at an advertisment from Pertronix about their
>"Flame-Thrower" coil.  It says it "maximizes energy and reliability over
>the full RPM range"

When looking at claims, the first question you should ask is if the claim
is measureable.  This one isn't.

> and allows for larger plug gaps, which produces
>greater fuel efficiency and power.

This might be measurable, but the difference won't be huge.

>  I'm running a basically stock set-up
>in my '71.  Upgraded to a Bosch 009 Distributor and Pertronix Ignitor.

This isn't really much of an upgrade.  The 009 distributor isn't ideal for
a bus application (it needs revs to advance, so it tends to hesitate while
starting off)  The pertronix is just a replacement for the points.  The
advantage is that it doesn't wear like the points, the disadvantage is
that when a pertronix dies you are stranded.

>Have always felt under-engined.  Can I get a few more hp out of a hot
>coil?

Sure!

> How much, as a percent?

About 1%.

> Is there a down side?  Harder on the
>engine?  Thx.

Any time you stress one conponent more than it was designed for, there is
a danger.  In this case, the primary wire feeding the coil from the
ignition switch will carry more current.  Consider your ignition switch
before making the change
