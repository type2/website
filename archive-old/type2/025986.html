Our tests were just static but did show how oils react under heat.  Most of
the oils turn into a thick sludge, very much like where they came from
before the refining proccess. And you are correct, the multi vis oils break
down more than the single weight oils.  Of course the best in our tests are
always the synthetics.  And unless your engine is leaking or burning the
oil, the synthetics would be the best way to go.

I do agree with you on the single weight oils, we always use 40W in the race
cars and it is a racing formula oil.  But in my westy which sometimes runs
in colder temps, I do prefer the 20W50 and the Lucas additive.
