Shawn,

I did this job last month.  Here's what I found....

1. No way to tighten them.

2. Removing & replacing ball joints on Type 2 torsion arms is very 
difficult; I recommend that you take it to a machine shop w/a press.  Buy 
good, German ball joints.  You might want to go ahead and replace all the 
ball joints as I don't know how having one new ball joint out of four will 
affect your steering/suspension.  Do all four esp. if your bus has more than 
175,000 miles.  If you have any extra $, do the shocks too, and you'll have 
a sweet ride.  Be aware, this is a burly job, and use a big-ass hammer to 
bang the torsion arms back into place.
