Subject: Re: [T2] Burnt Exhaust valve?

On Feb 4, 2007, at 1:47 PM, sunbugg16_at_aol_dot_com wrote:

> Hello All. I have a 77 auto westy with a 1.8 dual carbed that is  
> having trouble. I have a dead miss on cylinder 2. The plug is a  
> nice brown color, the ign system is up to par. I pulled the ign  
> wires off the cap until I came to the one that had no effect. I  
> pulled the plug out and grounded it and it fired nicely.

Sounds like the wiring is ok...

> I adjusted the valves and the exhaist and intake valve on the  
> number 2 cylinder were tight. The other 3 were good to go. I've  
> never had any expierence with burnt valves so I don't know what  
> their symptoms are. I'm asking if this sounds like a possibilty  
> before I go to pulling the head off. I have not done a compression  
> test as of yet but that is next on the list as well. Any ideas?  
> Thanks in Advance.

The valves are leaking so much the cylinder can't build compression.

The compression numbers on that cylinder should be low. If not  
conclusive, also perform a leakdown test and see exactly how much air  
going out the ports.

Perform those tests then pull the head.

Resist the urge to re-adjust the valves and run the engine before the  
valve breaks and ruins the pistons, cylinder and head.
