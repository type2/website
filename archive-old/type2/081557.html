Are you talking with the engine in or out? If its in, its easy, just leave
it in gear. The pully dosent really want to walk very much anyway, it just

investment too, I've read and done the 'walk' it off procedure and it can
take forever.

> The second bus I looked at was also an early (68-70) bay, owned by a

> generally worried.
>
> The history as I got it was that the bus had been sitting for quite a
> while and he had just pulled it out of the weeds and gotten it running.
> Good job, Jose!!!!!
>
> Checked his dwell, which was within limits (~46 as I remember) and
> timing, which was also OK.  He had a stock pulley, which had two notches
> and it lined up with the one I remember as being the 7.5 VOR notch, so
> if he has hte right puley, his timing was fine.  I recommended, as I
> usually do, that he get degree wheel pulley.
>
