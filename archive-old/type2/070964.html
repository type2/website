Ok..upright fuel pumps..
The "alternator" ones lean towards the distributor to allow clearence with the
larger diameter alternator...this provides clearance allowing you to remove
the pump
The "alternator" pumps use a different push rod too..
*HOWEVER*..Normally, one can use a "standard" pump with the
alternator...strong language may be required to wrestle the thing off the
motor..
So._dot_use the correct push rod for the pump, or the correct pump for the push
rod...
and if the pump studs are a *wee* bit long, you may not get the standard pump
on and off the "alternator" engine....
