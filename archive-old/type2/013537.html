Hello,

I personally have not had the guts to examine my front
beam but I have heard another solution that the list
has offered. It was to drill a hole in one end of the
beam and install a grease nipple fitting. Drill a hole
at the other end and then use a grease gun to pump the
beam full of grease until you start to see it comming
out the other end. then you can plug th other hole. Of
course this will not correct the thick rust and rust
dammage, but it sounds to be a good preventative
measure. It would take alot of grease but grease is
cheaper than a new front axle beam.

Using the por-15 that is formulated to coat gas tanks
in the beam and then pumping the axle beam full of
grease sounds to be the most full proof way of
stopping the growth of rust. The Por-15 for gas tanks
would not detoriate from the grease.

That is my $0.02 worth.
