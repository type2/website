Actually, the easiest way is to, , , , , ,
FIRST, make sure there is no flames or sparks ANYWHERE NEAR where you are working.
NOT in a garage where there is a hot water heater nearby.
THEN, turn on the gas at the tank, WITH ALL OTHER VALVES TIGHTLY CLOSED.
Then take a solution of dish washing soap and water, and liberally paint it on all the connections
and hoses.
Be sure to cover ALL of the rubber hoses. Look for bubbles. If there are ANY bubbles, you have a
leak.
This is what the professional gas people do.
BTW, remember, Propane is heavier than air, and will lay in low spots.

I'm an Electrical Engineer, and gas just scares the hell out of me. That's why I always have a
second
person there with me when I do it. Mainly for safety, but also to watch for my mistakes.
