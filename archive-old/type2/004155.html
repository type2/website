Jonathan-
No need to pull the engine to remove the heater boxes, but you will
need a "blue book " for the proper oem cuss words. ( personally,
i don't know a cuss word from Shinola :).
Forget the liquids penetrating oils, i use a torch ( not a flashlight )
and heat the nuts cherry red to remove and not break the studs
or pull them with the nuts rusted on, galling the aluminum heads
in the process.
Take a  long straight file across the top of the mounting flanges to true them.
Remove the old copper gaskets with a screwdriver and replace them.
I prefer to use new stainless nuts and anti-seize compound to install.
Any missing  or broken studs will need to be replaced, ( this can
be difficult if you are not careful ) Accuracy is the key to this job.
The technique depends on the situation.
Dave
