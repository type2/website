My 78 Westy was bought from the PO at 60k mi and ran without the
thermostat cable hooked up until 150,000 mi when I pulled the engine for
the first time to replace the clutch.  I replaced the split thermostat
and cable then (1995).  Now it has 238,000 mi on that same engine.  Near
as I can tell, not having the flaps shut for warmup affects only the
time it takes to warm up the engine.  However, for proper air flow, the
flaps must be present.
