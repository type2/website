Michael Benthin (MUTS) sends:

> 2. Any reason paper made shims can't be inserted between any 2 metal
> ones to increase stack thickness?  (I remember, in the 71 I had, a paper
> shim is used to bring out the flywheel, but it's fixed and doesn't
> spin)-since crushed lazer paper is about 2 thou I could also bring out
> the flywheel a hair and use the 4 lowest shims?)

Paper continues to get thinner the more you crush it. Printing presses use a
special paper called packing under the plates and blankets, and we did a
test once with our Akiyama Hi-Ace 428--we put a sheet of .010" packing and a
sheet of .002" packing on one blanket cylinder, ran it for a week, miked the
two sheets, changed the .002" to thicker and thicker packing as needed to
keep the cylinder packed to .012" (retaining the .010" packing sheet, as it
was the test sheet), then miked the .010" after two months. It was at .004."

Packing on a press is easy to change when it crushes too much. Packing on a
flywheel isn't.
