Hi Robert,

Just go to the library and check out a textbook on automotive engineering
and design...something I guarantee your "pretty competent VW mechanics" have
never done, or they would not be telling you such hogwash.  I'd bet VW
engineers also have slightly more education than your average "specialist".

Ford Motor Co. published a textbook in the '50's that concluded; 90% of all
engine wear occurs in the first 10 minutes of operation.  Extensive testing
with wear sensors in every conceivable part of the engine showed near
immeasurable wear at 180 degree F oil temp with full flow filtering.

I've never heard of any modern factory installed automotive engine that
ships without a thermostat.  Parts left out cost the manufacturer no money
and cause no maintenance worry, so they must be putting them in there for a
reason and it's not because they like throwing away millions of dollars.

Common sense tells you why you need a thermostat if you just investigate
from reliable sources.  If nothing else and without informed study, it's
reasonable to conclude that a thermostat on an A/C VW allows the dissimilar
metals of cast iron and aluminum to achieve relatively uniform expansion
rates during warm-up so case to cylinder/cylinder to head leaks are
minimized.

It's all about that first ten minutes if you want your engine to laaaaaast.

Steve Trembley
