On Thu, 29 Nov 2001, Richard Atwell wrote:

> Another thing I forgot to mention.  One of the hydraulic lifters is stuck
> from sitting so long.

Richard,

Waht do you mean "stuck"?  If the bus has been sitting while (1), then one
or more of the lifters have lost the oil that keeps them expanded.  As a
result, they are clatter like a flamenco dancer on speed.  They are not
"stuck", they are dry.  They will usually pump up and be fine in 30
seconds to 30  minutes.

> This happened once before and what we did before was pour some Rislone (pine
> tree smelling stuff) into the crankcase and it freed it up after running
> (idling) a while.

Yup, they do.  The rislone is not necessary.

> Is this still recommended?  If see that the lifters are only about $10.  Are
> these hard to replace?

They are not hard to replace, but I sincerely doubt that it is necessary.
Start the engine, let it idle for ~1 minute.  Drive it around (*gently*)
for about 1/2 hour.  If any lifter is still clatering, replace it.
