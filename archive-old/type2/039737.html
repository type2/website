> I could see 90 amps being used up easily.  One thing most people forget to 
> factor in is that the engine's ignition system takes a significant amount 
> of power.  four spark plugs firing 2,000 times per minute (at 
> 4000rpm).  That's at least 5-10 amps right there.  Then you add radio, 
> lights, fans, wipers, A/C (if applicable), CB radio, gauges... it all adds

Well, I have instrumented to measure actual current draw (not
charge/discharge) and never saw more than 25 amps, and that was with all
systems on full blast.  You might be able to pull more current with big
audio amps, but if the draw is continous the sound level will be such
that you won't be able to occupy the bus unless you are between the ages
of 14 and 18!

Of course, extra capacity doesn't hurt, but the stock bus generator is
more than enough for my needs, even with several amateur radio rigs and
a stereo adding to the load.
