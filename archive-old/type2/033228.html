It should be, according to Hooke's law, but unfortunately the real
world sometimes works differently than theory.

I tried to calculate a "correction factor" in a similar fashion and
ended up way too high.

Considering your heavier engine, you might want to add a couple of
degrees to the stock spring plate setting, then measure the deck angle
to see the result.  Unfortunately, this might involve a couple of
disassemblies to get it right.  When you change things, you become the
engineer! :)

I measure deck angle on a level surface with a level on the sill of the
sliding door.  I put a friend in the driver's seat to make the
measurement, as that seat has to be occupied when the vehicle is in
motion.  With stock parts, the deck comes out within a half-degree of
level.
