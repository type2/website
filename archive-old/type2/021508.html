Your mechanic is exactly right- it lets in more air. Even on a cold engine that
is flooded, there is a device that opens the choke blade a little to let in more
air and unflood it.
On FI engines, this is less relevant, because the injectors on inject relevant
to the position of the blade in the AFM anyway. Some OLD FI might still need
open throttle when hot starting.
