Your mechanic is wrong, period.  Refer to Gene Berg's technical writings
about the flaps.  He ran them himself.....few people have as much knowledge
about the ACVW motor then the late Gene Berg..............I doubt it very
seriously that your mechanic is more knowledgeable than gene.

Actually, those flaps have different angles to them that actually help aim
the air over the cylinders when open....taking them out assures uneven
cooling.  Also, taking them out assures you that for most short runs in your
car, your engine will not be warmed up, meaning decreased engine life.
Also, after you run it a while, take off your oil filler cap and look at the
grey sludge in it...no flaps encourages this to occur for the motor rarely
gets hot enough to get rid of the little moisture that gets in the
system....

My .02......I think that someone out there should really read some of Gene
Berg's technical writings in regards to this issue, and they will be sold.
