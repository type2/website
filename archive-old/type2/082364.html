You can locate all your refinishing needs at any local automotive body
shop supply house---everything except the foam roller and brushes---

In days of yore, the painter's id use a product by the name of "Japan"
paint--and it was brushed on, wet sanded, and repeated again and
again---too time consuming, too labor intense.

I think this stuff was a lacquer based product, and not very durable for
everyday use.

Forget the POR primer CA-CA----
Get a gallon of Dupont's Vari-Prime, acid etch, zinc chromate primer.
This stuff will lock down any corrosion, and prevent any from re
occuring----good stuff.

Rent a Booth?

Two chances---slim and none---

Set up a fan in your garage, or shed, in a window, this will clear out
any overspray---

If you use PPG's urethane paints --(Concept) the set up time is
short--so the material will be dust free in a matter of minutes----more
good stuff, and will give you a long lasting durable finish---

I use a Binks #7 gun--the best fora lay down flat finish.
HLVP guns lay down a concrete block type finish----High volume low
pressure guns spit paint---just as the name implies lots of wet sanding
to get a nice finish.

One more thing---hold on to your shorts, when the tab appears for the
equipment and materials---

Them red's your looking at ain't cheap---

your talking about $238.00 per gallon, plus catalyst, plus reducer, plus
the Vari prime, plus the fill primer, plus the DA,and paper, plus the
tape, masking paper, plus the compressor and the gun, plus the finishing
stuff---( wet sand paper, rubbing compound, polishing material, buffer,
pads, etc )

The time you will have wrapped up in this project won't be nothing to be
laughing at either----mucho timo-----

Pay the piper, and have a shop do it----

Have fun----

Later,


Terry
