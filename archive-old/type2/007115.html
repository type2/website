OK Stiff engine, eh?...
Did you, perchance, use a connecting rod from an engine that had dropped a 
valve?
A twisted rod will behave this way...
I had a dear friend (I was dating his daughter at the time) who had grabbed a 
short block to drop in the daughters car..a fastback, it were....It got to 
the point where the starter would not turn the engine over..Luke was a 
plumber, not a mechanic...out came the 5 HP pipe threading machine and a hole 
drilled through the back of the car, a socket welded to a pipe, with the pipe 
locked in the machine and the socket on the crank 
pulley.....................Yes, it started...once........
It was later found the short block was from a car that had swallowed a 
valve...the rod was twisted about 4 degrees....the piston has to move in 
plane just a *wee* bit different from that of the cylinder....it binds and 
scuffs and distorts.....
I could be bearings and all that...but the clue is that it started when you 
got "above" the crankcase...
I've also seen the clip left off the crank gear and the timing gears work 
toward the pulley and *really* screw things up..maybe this explains your 
timing problem??????
My college engine professor, Oscar Tinettti, said it best.."Engines don''t 
wear *in* they wear *out*!".... Once you reach a point where it stops turning 
freely..stop..it ain't gunna get better!
It's gotta come out and get checked...sorry...
