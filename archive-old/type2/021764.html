Thomas,
Make sure that you have the rotor pointing the correct direction when
you're adjusting the valves.  When it's in firing position one, the
rotor should be pointing out of the back of the engine compartment, to
the right.  If you're unsure if it's actually at TDC, just stick a long
screwdriver in cylinder 1 and feel the piston as it moves up and stops. 
Then go adjust 1.

Make sure your adjusting the correct cylinders at the correct rotor
angles.  Looking at the rotor from above the engine: 1 will be at around
5:00, 2 at around 2:00, 3 at 11:00 and 4 at 8:00.  Spin the engine to
make the rotor spin counterclockwise and adjust the valves 1,2,3,4. 
Bentley has a good description of this..

Question.. what year is your car?  If it's hydraulic, it's a different
method of adjusting the valves!

Mike
