On Tue, 18 Aug 1998, Boleyn Rodney wrote:

> Since it had been sitting two weeks, and the starter isn't in the
> greatest condition, I didn't want to crank a long time till the fuel 
> pressure got up, so I ran the fuel pump for 30 seconds before I tried 
> to start it.  This is accomplished by shorting pins 36 and 39 on the 
> AFM connector while the ignition is ON.  Okay, so, battery's good, 
> I've run the fuel pump, I go hit the starter, and the bus fires right 
> up immediately.  And immediately dies.
> 
> Crank, crank, crank, won't restart.
> 
> Go run the fuel pump again, and it fires right up.  And immediately
> dies, and won't restart until I run the fuel pump again.
> 
> Anybody have any idea what's going on here?  It's not the fuel pump,
> because that clearly runs.  I think (but correct me if I'm wrong), 
> it's not the double relay, because running the fuel pump from the AFM 
> proves out the relay also (I think).

That sounds right to me, but you're not running the fuel pump from the 
AFM, you're shorting out the line *as if* the AFM was in the system.  So 
the problem could be either a bad connection at the wiring harness/AFM 
connection or within the AFM itself.  

> And it's apparently not the fuel pressure regulator, because it holds 
> the fuel pressure from the time I run the pump until I get the AFM 
> reconnected, walk up front, and hit the starter.

Not necessarily.  The "startup" could be coming from the cold start 
valve's initial squirt (from minor residual pressure) without enough 
pressure to keep it running.  Unlikely, but spend $10-$15 to make 
yourself a pressure guage and check it out.

> Eh, it's probably not the fuel system at all.  I'm probably just leading
> myself on a wild goose chase, but if anyone has any suggestions, I'd 
> be glad to hear 'em.

No, sounds like fuel.  Start with the pressure check.  Run the fuel pump 
with the gauge attached, look for proper pressure, turn off the pump and 
watch for proper pressure maintenance.  If that's all OK, then fire her 
up and check the gauge again.  Could be harness, AFM, maybe even filter.
