On Mon, 12 Oct 1998, Ian Cook wrote:

>  How would I tell, from my VIN number, what type of BUS I have?

The general theory on the breadloaf busses is as follows:

The first three digits tell you what you have and when it was built.  The
first digit is a 1, 2, 3, or 4 and means the following:

1 - you own a beetle
2 - you own a van (and you wondered why we called it the type 2 list :-)
3 - you own a squareback, fastback or notchback
4 - you are always in the market for separate headlights :-) err... really
    you own a type4.. they resemble type3's but have separate headlamps
 
the second digit tells you what body style...  For busses it goes:

1 - Panel Truck
2 - Station Wagon
3 - Kombi or Campmobile (Westfalia)
6 - Pickup Truck

The third digit tells you the year of manufacture.  

1 - 1971
2 - 1972
3 - 1973
4 - 1974
5 - 1975
6 - 1976
7 - 1977
8 - 1968 *or* 1978
9 - 1969 *or* 1979
0 - 1970

The 1968/78 and 1969/79 can be distinguished by the bumpers.  if they are
square, it's a '70s model.

Thus if the VIN starts 222 then it is a bus, station wagon, 1972.  A 237
is a bus, Westfalia, 1977.
