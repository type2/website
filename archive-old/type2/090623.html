At the risk of starting it up again, I visited the Motorsports Hall of Fame in Talladega, AL this past weekend.  Lots of interesting old American iron there.  I noticed that in many of the cars, they had turned the gauges so that the needles would be pointing straight up at "normal" readings.  In every car I looked in, a vertical needle on the oil temp gauge corresponded to 250-280 degrees.  I have pictures of this.  Also, nearly every car had an external cooler for the differential fluid (in case you were thinking that these cars were too old/unsophisticated to have used external oil coolers).  They could have easily done the same for the oil to keep it at whatever temperature they wanted.  Apparently, in NASCAR 250-280 is (or at least was) the norm.
