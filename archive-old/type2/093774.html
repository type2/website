Hi Jay, 

I've been off list for a couple of weeks on holiday so I've missed the start of this thread and may be repeating things already said. 

Your signature line doesn't say where you live - if you live in the USA or Canada then a perusal of the list archives will save you leg work as others have already found out out that they do not meet the import requirements for those countries. If you live elsewhere then start with your local vehicle licensing authority to find out what the requirements are for your country. 

Most parts are the same as for a '79. Major body exceptions are the roof, and removable rear valance, everything else could be replaced with 70's parts. 

The engine also is the same as a late '70s 1600cc engine, but the fuel management system is NOT. It is a multipoint fuel injection system giving 28mpg (imperial) at 60mph. Combined with the catalytic convertor this gives a very clean exhaust (0.00% CO and 35ppm HC, on mine; that's with the engine cold by the way) I'm told this will meet even current California emissions standards. 

I believe that you are correct in saying that the conversions being offered are the same as the flat-pack Just Kamper conversions, with obvious modifications to allow for the different roof line. 

You should also note (for you continued existence on the list) that the charter specifically excludes discussion of post '83 aircooled VW buses - a change made just after I purchased my Brazilian Camper in '98. 

As for the cost being high, here in the UK a T4 based camper would cost 50 to 60% more so you make up your own mind as to the value... 

If you have any specific questions regarding the Brazilian T2s (which have now ceased production) please feel free to ask off list. 

Good luck
