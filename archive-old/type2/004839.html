Hi all,
    If you want cheap, I recommend going to the bone yard.  You could get
lucky and find yourself a beatle, bus or karman ghia that was a top runner,
well taken care of and still has the row of VDO gauges intact.  You might
even find a great engine underneath a smashed car that an insurance company
bought, then sold for salvage.  I found a 1600 DP for my second bug this
way, ran great by the way, but I also found a compliment of gauges in the
dash and the yard only charged 20 bucks for the set.  CHT, Oil Temp, Oil
Pressure, Ammeter, Clock, and Tach.  I also grabbed all the sensors that I
knew about (not many at the time).  Its cheap, maybe a little better than
using an oven thermometer.  BTW, when you add lengths of wire to a device
that utilizes current for the read out, doesn't that add resistance to the
system making the display read out incorrectly? YMMV
