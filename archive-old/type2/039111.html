Subject: Jump Starting with a Deep Cycle Batt

George, hate to disagree with you, but jump starting from a marine or 
standard deep cycle will not damage the batt. All boats, with multi-batt 
systems, usually have a switch( generically called a Perko or Guest switch, 
after the popular makers of the same) that allows you to cycle between all 
batts(including deep cycle, or "house" batts, as they are known in the 
industry) depending on circumstance. I've owned all sorts of watercraft over 
the years, and am also a factory certified master marine tech., and can tell 
you from experience that I've never destroyed or even shortened the life span 
of a deep cycle batt. by  occasionally requesting it to act as a cranker 
batt.Every batt. maker that I've talked to(and I've talked to them all, 
nothing is as short lived as a batt used for marine application, with the 
exception of a customers temper, who just shelled out mucho bucks for a new 
batt last season, only to find it kaput at the begining of this one) have 
stated that using a deep cycle as an occasional cranker will not harm the 
batt, and is in fact, par for the course. Now pounding the hell out of a wet 
cell deep cycle batt. while running through high seas is a completly 
different can 'O worms...
