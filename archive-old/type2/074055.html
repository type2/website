H Steven Dolan wrote:

> Paul,
>
> The horn should not ground to the frame!!!!  It is insulated internally
> and there is not supposed to be any isolation between the mount point and
> the frame.
>
> Do this as a diagnostic:
>
> Pull both wires off the horn,            No horn, check??
> then turn the bus on;
>
> Put each wire on the horn,               If the horn began sounding when
> one at a time;                           either of the wires was attached,
>                                          label that wire 12V+ and replace
>                                          your horn, it's dead.
>
> If neither wire alone causes the horn to blow, then put one wire back on
> and connect a jumper to ground.  If the horn blows, then (a) that's the
> 12V+ wire and (b) your horn is fine.  If the horn will not blow, then
> switch wires and try it with the other wire.  There is no insulation
> between the horn and the body.  The electrical isolation of the horn is
> internal to the horn and if it has broken down (rare), the horn is dead
> and must be replaced.  Ask away with more questions if you need to!
