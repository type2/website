Apparently, this is a problem regardless of how new your starter and
solenoid are.  I had a similar problem on my '77 Westy, and rather than
replace the starter and solenoid, I was advised to install an ignition
relay.  There's been alot of talk about it recently on the type2 list;
Bosch makes a kit to do this.  

I used a replacement Chrysler ignition relay.  Rather than send juice
up the thin wire from the battery to the ignition switch and back down
to the solenoid, the thin wire runs back down to the relay (much less
power to operate than the solenoid), and the relay sends the full fury
of the battery into the solenoid.  Hasn't stuck again since!
