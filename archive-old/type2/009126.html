It doesn't make any difference to you, but it makes a lot of
difference to those who depend upon the supply of used parts to keep
their own vehicles running.  The used parts industry is one example of
recycling that has been working well for decades and the smog people
want to eliminate it.

Also, "voluntary" programs have a habit of becoming "mandatory" once
the door is opened.  How "voluntary" will the program be once the
standards for the roadside sensors start being tightened up? The
choice will be "sell it to the crusher or just let the city confiscate
it for non-registration."
