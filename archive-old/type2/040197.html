Subject: Re: Oil pressure light flickering (similar problem on my '69)

Yeah, I'm having a similar problem on my '69....

I started a new job recently, and have to drive some
highway miles to and from. There are some pretty
decent hills, to boot. After driving the hills at
highway speed (or as close as I can get;-) and coming
to an idle, my oil light comes on, and stays on until
I rev the motor a bit, even though the dipstick checks
out fine. And to further confuse the issue, it only
happens every few days!

I haven't logged/measured my oil consumption lately,
but the last time I did, it was in spec (per owners
manual), of 1-2 quarts every 1,000 miles. I will do
that again, as soon as I need oil. I have a good idea
on this one..._dot_carry 2 qts. of oil, along with a black
felt-tip pen, and when you add the *first* quart of
oil, write your odometer reading on the outside of the
*second* quart. Then replace the *first* quart with a
new one, and repeat the proceedure. That way, you
check your consumption (accurately) every time you add
oil. Each time you need oil, you will see the writing,
and you'll never forget to write it down again (I
tried the notebook method, but I kept forgetting to
write everything down).

The bus is a '69 Westy, 1600sp (120+/-5lbs all
around), with a doghouse cooler installed. I also have
the little 'T' fitting on my oil pressure sending unit
for my VDO oil pressure guage.

The only recent changes (in the last 2 years, anyway)
have been a switch from 10w-30, to 20w-50, and the
fact that I'm driving on the highway (lots of short,
stop and go for the last 2 years....very little
highway).

Any ideas??


Jaime
