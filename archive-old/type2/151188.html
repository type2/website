My only thought is that you could test it and perhaps use the $ on needed
parts.

Servo initial test:

Take off the master cylinder, insert a piece of cardboard or something into
the hole that can reach the bottom of the inside of the servo.  If there is
more than 1/2&quot; of fluid coming up the dipstick, then it is likely that your MC
has been leaking brake fluid enough to compromise the servo's diaphram.  If
not, why not change the MC (_at_ $20 for Brazilian) and see what happens?

&gt; will I need to bleed the brakes after installing

If you disconnect any of the brake fluid lines during the process, then yes.

&gt; Are there seals which I will need to replace when re-attaching the
&gt; master cylinder?

No reason not to put a new O-ring on the MC.
