Richard Childress in Welcome, NC (http://www.rcrracing_dot_com, but his site is
under reconstruction), builds an engine that will make a Bus go 90 MPH
without pushing it, but the price is a bit steep--he charges $35,000 for one
of his engines--and you'll need to call Kennedy Engineered Products for a
flywheel housing adapter. (Ask for one for a Chevrolet 350CID V-8.) Then
you'll need to reengineer the suspension to go that fast, and run hoses for
a radiator.

In case you haven't noticed, the front of a Bus is shaped like a brick wall.
In order to get a Bus to go 90MPH without straining the engine, you'll need
many hundreds of horsepower...so yeah, a Winston Cup race engine will
probably do it. Maybe.
