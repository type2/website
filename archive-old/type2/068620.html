The nacelle is fitted with heat sensors connected to warning lights in the

And yes, you can do the same thing.  In fact, it's a lot easier to do with a
car because you don't need to use certified aircraft materials.  

Here's one trick that works:

   Buy some new-surplus coffee-pot thermostats from Ass Company, otherwise
known as American Scientific Surplus.  

Screw a couple of them on the overhead of the engine bay.  Wire them to 12v
thru a suitable fuse and wire their outlet to an audible alarm in the
cockpit.  

The ones I used to control my oil-cooler fan turn on at about 170 degrees F.
 Cost was about a buck per switch.   One-seventy might be a tad low for a
fire-warning circuit but since the only thing it's going to set-off is you...

As to opening the hatch to put out a fire, bad idea.  A better bet would be
to make your fire extinguishing system semi-permanent, with the bottle in the
cargo bay but the nozzle(s) in the engine compartment.  Fire horn blows?
 Grab the D-ring and pull.

Of course, the best kind of fire safety is prevention.  Fires don't happen
unless we allow them to.
