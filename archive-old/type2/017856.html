Karl von Salzen wrote:

> >
> >I pulled a brake servo out of a '78 bus in a junk yard and have a
> >question about one of the hoses that attached to it.  One hose attaches
> >to the metal servo unit and that is for the vacuum.  The other attaches
> >in the front where the brake lever push rod attaches to the unit.  The
> >hose winds up and into the body and disappears into the door pillar.  I
> >have looked through my bentley and cannot find any reference to this
> >tube other than showing it.
> >
> >What is it for?
> >Where does it go?
> >
>
> The front hose is clean air for the diaphragm to move. In a passenger car
> that end of the booster is inside the car. In order for the booster to work,
> that side must be vented to the atmosphere or it wouldn't move. Since that
> end is pointed forward and all the road dirt and water would go into it, a
> line is installed and it goes into the body at the bottom rear corner of the
> channel below the drivers door.
>
