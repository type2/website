Hi Parker,

I  used a propane radiant heater in my bus last winter, and it does work.
But there is still a downside, even with the shutoff for carbon monoxide ---
water vapour

When you burn propane, you create heat, carbon monoxide, Carbon dioxide, and
LOTS of water molecules. I found I usually had to keep the vent windows open
and the fresh air ducts up front open unless I wanted the windsheild
completely frosted over.

For early sping / late summer camping, they're terrific. Just give the
interior of the bus some time to dry out afterrunning the heater for
prolonged periods.

That's my $.02, YMMV.
