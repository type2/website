Greetings ,

I had a similar problem with a 77 bay window I picked up in Eugene Oregon.

Not long after purchasing my first bus, driving it up to Portland and then
running a few tanks of gas through it I started to notice sort of
droning/buzz. I had an appointment at the "No Name Garage" in Eugene a day
or two after I noticed the noise, but I still felt uneasy about driving 90+
miles with a mystery noise.

Before hitting the road to Eugene I decided it would be best to have the
issue addressed prior to departure , so I looked for a shop in Portland. I
got real lucky and found a great VW/Mercedes shop in SE Portland, these guys
took a few minutes out of there already scheduled jobs to have a listen to
the mystery noise. After a quick look around he was able to identify the
culprit as the fuel pump, stating that it possibly was struggling from
restricted fuel flow caused by a clogged fuel filter.

They put that sucker up on the lift, changed the fuel filter and awhala...
problem solved. After talking with the mechanic for a few minutes we deduced
that the filter had become clogged because ...

1. the bus had been sitting for a while and the tank had some rust/sediments
in it(as indicated by fuel filter).

2. I had been cruising around and sloshing a fuel which loosened up a good
bit of it.

He recommended changing out the fuel filter every 2nd or 3rd tank for a few
1000 miles and if the filters don't come out clean after a few months then I
might want to consider a tank swap depending on how bad off it was.

That was about 10K miles and 4 fuel filters ago, without a moan from the
pump, just to be safe I keep a spare fuel pump and few filters in the bus.

Hope this helps.
