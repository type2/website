> A colleague has told me of a tool consisting of two halves which are fitted
> to front and back of the metal via a pilot hole, and then a blade of some
> kind which is turned by an allen key to cut the hole.  It's intended for
> just such a job as this.   
> Anyone know what thy're called, or where to find one ???

It's called a chassis punch.  They work well on thin sheet metal, but I
don't know what the maximum thickness is.  They are pretty expensive as I
recall.  They used to be used a lot for punching holes in electronic
chassis for tubes and other components.

Check suppliers of electronic tools to see if they still carry them.
