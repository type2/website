Well, Muir was being a bit poetic about it!  Basically, the rule on dwell
is that the points have to be closed long enough to build up a full
magnetic field in the coil.  The duration of the spark is determined by
how quickly the magnetic field collapses, not by the time that the points
are open.  Of course, if the time the points are open is less than the
duration of the spark (not likely) this would limit duration.

The time consumer here is the building up of the field.  If you have
insufficient dwell, the field will never reach full strength so the spark
will be weak.  There's really a wide range of dwell that will work and the
dwell spec is to ensure that the setting is in the middle of the useable
range.
