 I've tried this for short stretches and thought it was pretty close. My
wheels+tyres are nominally almost the same diameter as stock (659mm vs 
654mm) but the rolling radius is more different than this suggests because
of the stiffer sidewalls on the new tyres.

 I suppose the main point is that if this isn't taken into account you
could see some substantial economy variations that were artificially 
produced by a small tyre (I suspect that this is the case for some of the
numbers that have been posted to the list). If you want to improve your mpg
you can just let your nearside front tyre down (or fit Lo-Pros) and 
magically acquire another 5mpg :-).
