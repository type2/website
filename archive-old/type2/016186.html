Chad,

It took a while for my 2.0L rebuild to idle smoothly.  It's one of those
things you may need to adjust several times over the next couple of weeks or
few hundred miles.  It will take a while for the rings to seat properly and
increase and stabilize the compression and vacuum.  You may also need to
adjust your hyraulic lifters for one last time after a week or so.  My
rebuild now has about 2,000 miles on it and it runs better and better
everyday. In the meantime you should check your hoses for any vacuum leaks,
as you mentioned.  Vacuum leaks are sometimes hard to find, but cause many
many problems if ignored.  I had a vacuum leak at the rubber gasket under
the throttle body.  This was tough to locate but worth the time to find and
fix.  Since your EGR valve is still in place it may be causing a vacuum
leak.  I cut a solid oval shaped gasket from gasket material and installed
it under my EGR valve.  This keeps the EGR valve in place, but seals it from
leaking.  Later on you can simply remove the gasket if you decide to hook up
the EGR valve. Good luck.
