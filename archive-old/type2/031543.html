I know about those screws on the underside of the fuel tank fire wall, but
the rest of the job really isn't that hard...the sender will fit in
without removeing the engine or fuel tank...

.  take off air cleaner
.  remove rear panel covering gas tank
.  lay on rear deck over engine compartment
.  reach in and pull the wires off
.  unscrew the sender
.  wiggle, jiggle and twist it off to one side, it will come
.  put O ring on new sender.
.  drop O ring into tank.
     repeat
.  finally get sender back in tank.
.  replace wires, usually wrong first time (check at this point)
.  switch wires to right terminals
.  put firewall back in,
.  put air cleaner back on

total time with beer break about 2 hours, faster if you've done it once or
twice before.

my 78 is on it's 4th sender and I have never taken the fuel tank out, and
only once did I have the engine out (for other reasons)

should have picked the collective wisdom of this group first.

they have saved me many hours of trial and error
