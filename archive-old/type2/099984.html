On Tue, 22 Oct 2002, Sharpe, Steven (Facilities Planning) wrote:
Its really worth it to install relays and run power directly from the
generator or battery. You lose a lot of power when its routed through
the light switch. It makes a difference even if you're running
conventional lights. Daniel Stern Lighting http://lighting.mbz_dot_org/
sells relay kits and lights if you don't want to assemble the few parts
yourself.
I agree on this one. Running relays will also save your headlight
switch, since it will no longer have to carry the full headlight current
Make sure that the new current-carrying wires are fused, preferably with
self-resetting circuit breakers. It's a very good idea to run separate
left and right feeds to the headlights, so the failure of one will not
leave you completely in the dark.
