I'd be inclined to look at the ignition and then the FI before thinking 
about the tank..

My Reasons:

If you were suffering from fuel starvation due to gunk on an FI bus, 
you'd probably have the following symptoms:

noisy fuel pump = sucking gas out of the tank. (mine was at all rpm's)

stalling at high rpms's - you wouldn't be able to 'gas through' it 
because the fuel pump was already delivering all it could when it started 
stalling.

when you change the fuel filter you get a minor dribble out the fuel 
lines..

with the engine running, the larger diameter fuel line (filter to fuel 
pump) gets compressed under suction.

my bus had all of these symptoms + stalling on left turns at speed.

being able to 'gas through' a fuel starvation problem to me rules out 
delivery from the tank.

just my .02
