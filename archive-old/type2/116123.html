Hi Bill,

This is a great Friday post, I'll be watching the responses on this 
one, since I recently bought my wife a bus of her own.

Here's my list for you:

#1. FUEL LINES. Inspect and replace if required ASAP.
#2. Structural integrity - Esp. frame and the front beam, but also 
inspect floors and sliding door parts.
#3. Brakes.
#4. Steering.
#5. Tires. Look for proper load rating and 2-ply sidewalls.
#6. Suspension.
#7. Exhaust leaks - Probable cause of backfires on decel.
#8. Vacuum Leaks- Possible cause of other carburation issues, and 
oft-neglected by P.O's.
#9. Compression test. Get the values recorded now and you'll have a 
benchmark to refer to in the future if performance changes. Make sure 
to adjust the valves beforehand.
#10. Steering coupler. It's a rubber coupling at the bottom of the 
steering column, under the cover plate. It could possibly be 29 years 
old, and may need attention.
