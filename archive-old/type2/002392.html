This sounds like you don't have the rear shoes adjusted correctly. The
travel of the master cylinder stops as soon as the front disc brakes are
completely engaged. If the rear shoes have too much clearance, they will not
come out far enough to stop the bus. When you pump the pedal, you are taking
up the clearance. This will make the rear shoes reach the drums and give you
four wheel braking.

Pumping the pedal uses up your vacuum and decreases assist. Normally, the
vacuum does not come back quick enough, so this means you don't have a check
valve or vacuum booster hose problem.
