At 9:28 PM -0700 7/17/98, pitcher_at_shore.intercom_dot_net wrote:
>Hello!, I am rebuilding a 1978 type IV 1700.  When I pulled the push rod
>tubes out I noticed that two of them displayed a shinny worn area about 3/8
>of an inch long.  The two rods wear on cylinders that oppose each other.  Is
>this normal for this engine?  Does this indicate a problem with the lifters
>or the camshaft?  Can someone please advise me on this?

That is probably where the wire spring retainer came loose and was rubbing
on the pushrod.  I've seen it so bad that it wore through the aluminum
pushrod and it broke in two.  You should have steel pushrods so you
probably won't have that problem.  Make sure the wire spring retainers are
in properly.  They should be nice and tight.
