I suggest testing it out in boiling water.  If it's coming on at 212 degrees
it's gonna be on a lot and you'll never really know if you are overheating.
The VDO gauge setup is really the way to go.

Improve your cooling capacity. T-stat and flaps in place?  Cool tin or
standard "forget me nots" under the cylinders? No vacuum leaks...test with a
water bottle that has a 2 ft fuel hose on it and an old main jet in the end.
All the other basics in order?  Then if you are using decent oil (I like SAE
40 above 68 degrees), 240 is acceptable on the highway at 65.   55?  I think
you have something amiss unless it's real hot outside.  Remember, 10 small
air leaks around the shroud can equal one big one.  Triple check and *seal
all air leaks*.  Metal tape is great on the shrouds.  Might be worth
checking to make sure you have the correct fan as well.  I've seen many
shops put in the narrower one.

To your q's.

1. Possible but very unlikely.  It's fairly idiot proof with a stock oil
pump and can't open at the wrong time
2. The stock spring and "grooved" plunger always works best for me. Leave
the front bypass alone.
