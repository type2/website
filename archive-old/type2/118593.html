Sorry, I think you're wrong, at least regarding the original question.  The
reason Phillips screws are commonly used in fast-paced assembly is that they
*don't* cam out as readily as regular slotted screws.  Sure, the square
drive is even better yet, but that wasn't the question.  Maybe, just maybe,
the regular slotted screw and screwdriver is superior for *removal* if
you've got a straight shot at the screw and a properly-fitting screwdriver -
I'm not sure of the physics of the situation - but if you're forced to come
in at anything other than the perfect straight alignment with your
screwdriver the Phillips is the better bet.
