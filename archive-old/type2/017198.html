Pedro:
I'd look for a plugged fuel filter first- it seems like the 72-74's have
one someplace between the tank and pump, or maybe built into the pump
like a type 1. If your pump is bad, I'd just rebuild it. It seems like
kits are available for around $50 US. The stock pump delivers the right
pressure and is safe and trouble free, though it doesn't last as long as
the rest of the vehicle.
Another source of trouble might be debris in the tank. Just 
disconnecting the fuel pump inlet hose and draining some fuel into a
container should assure you if this is the case.
ALWAYS be real careful of fire danger when working around gasoline. One
cupful of gasoline on fire is enough to engulf your van (and your shop)
in flames. It's a good idea to do this outside and still have a fire
extinguisher handy.
It seems bus owners aren't the only ones w/ fire problems. At the local
John Deere factory on Monday they were servicing a new tractor, draining
the fuel, some fuel splashed onto a trouble light and engulfed the whole
thing on fire. And this was diesel with a much higher kindling
temperature! $175,000 damage, they said- would be a very bad day for me.
SO THE MAIN POINT AGAIN- BE EXTRA CAREFUL AROUND COMBUSTIBLES!
This goes for cleaning agents and paint thinners as well!
