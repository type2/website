Heck I towed a Hyundai from VA to TN in 1995 and the Hyundai was not 
insured, registered, nor mine (beyond a handwritten transfer of ownership). 
The cops did check me over at a DUI check site. My Beetle (tow vehicle) had 
current Italian registration which they could not read, doubtful insurance 
(also Italian), and the towee Hyundai, was on a tow bar (Tomken Machine in 
Colorado, nice collapsible towbar). Hyundai had no lighting beyond the 
parking lights whose battery was sustained by the Beetle through a single 
hot wire from the one of the Beetle's taillight. Ground wire was 
accomplished by the towbar connected to the Beetle.

It worked, I wouldn't recommend it, wouldn't do it again. Oh and I'd get 
the hell out of the Soviet Republic of CA today... <grin> Good lighting is 
a must - ones that include brake lights, turn signals and running lights.

Seriously, try a good towbar by Tomken (for example) or a tow dolly. I have 
both and both have done me well. Flat towing is better but I have neither a 
trailer like that nor a suitable tow vehicle.
