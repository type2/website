It most likely would be the sender. Exact same thing happened to me little
over a year ago. Just recently (last month) there were many posts about this
very situation. and ways to do it WITHOUT dropping the motor. If you can
turn a wrench, read, and above all else have patience you can do this
yourself. You might even find a fellow VW owner willing to help and show you
the ropes. The list is very forgiving and quite willing to help. I'm new to
the list but not the spirit of owning a van. It's a part of the mentality of
owning a type2 - help each other!

If you don't already own them there are two books you should have. A must is
'How To Keep Your Volkswagen Alive For The Complete Idiot' - Muir. The
author finds some degree of humor in even the most aggravating situations.
Even if you decide to have a shop do the work on your van I would highly
recommend this book. The other is the 'Volkswagen Service Manual' - Robert
Bentley, you'll see this referred to a "Bentley's" in the list. Good luck.
