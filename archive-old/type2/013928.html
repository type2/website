> If this is true, then if the distributor drive is 180 degrees off, as 
> someone posted earlier today, will it then be retarding #1 instead?

Maybe, maybe not.  If the rotor is pointing at #1 (notch on distributor body) when #1 is in firing position, then #3 will still be retarded (if the 
distributor is designed that way)

It's relatively easy to check this.  If the points are static timed to open at, say, 7.5 degrees BTDC, and you rotate the engine 360 degrees 
(firing position for #3) and note that the points open at 5 degrees BTDC, then the distributor is oriented correctly to retard #3, regardless of 
the distributor drive gear orientation.  If, however, the points open at 10 degrees BTDC after rotating the engine 360 degrees then the 
distributor is installed wrong.  Rotate the distributor body 180 degrees and criss-cross the wires on the cap to set things right.
