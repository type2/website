It means that the person is asking you to give them $xxxx and that
they won't sell the bus for less than that amount.  Seems pretty clear
to me!

However (and this is a big however!) I used to participate in the
buying and selling of quite a few vehicles.  My experience is that no
price is really firm unless the seller really doesn't want to sell the
vehicle or unless someone comes along with more money than brains (not
a rare type!).

The true worth of a vehicle is the price that you're willing to part
with to possess it.  If that sum is more than the price the seller
must have to part with the vehicle, then you both will be happy with
the deal.  If it isn't, the deal won't happen.  No reason to fret!

If your price is turned down, then wait a week or so and make the
offer again.  Often the passage of time will change the seller's idea
of the worth of his vehicle.

If the seller mentions that other buyers have already made offers on
the car, take his statement with a grain of salt.  I've seldom seen
these other bidders in the flesh, and never had one actually buy a
vehicle.

Above all, don't fall in love with a car before you buy it.  It's a
machine, after all!
