I forget which one it is and I'm not near where I can look.  As I recall,
you can modify a more commonly available relay.  Here's what I wrote,
courtesy of the archive:

-------------------
The 4-pin 411-941-583C relay is no longer available (at
least locally)  Web searches revealed only one source, a Porsche supplier.

Luckily, I have a fairly savvy parts guy and he pulled out a 111-941-583
5-pin relay to compare.  The extra pin (#30) is supposed to be attached to
a hot wire to energize the relay coil.  In the old relay the coil  was
attached internally to pin 56 which
is the voltage source supplied from the headlight switch.

Apparently, the idea of the 5-pin relay was to allow the beams to be
switched with the
headlights off by supplying the relay with power from the ignition
circuit.

I soldered a jumper between the #30 and #56 pins and plugged the relay in.
There was a hole in the fuse block to accept the #30 pin, but no connector
inside the hole.  The modified relay worked perfectly.
-----------------

Before installing the new relay, drill a small hole through the top of its
case.  If water gets in, this hole will let it drain out, saving the
relay.

The complete post is at:

http://208.61.227.213/archive/type2/Pine.BSF.4.05.10101131705580.56843-100000_at_lenti.type2_dot_com.html

Hope this helps you out.

George Lyle
