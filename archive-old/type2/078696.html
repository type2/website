> When you know what you are doing, and when the bolts involved are clean
> and easy to remove, the whole pull-job can take less than 30 minutes.
> We used to pull the engines in our Beetles in under 20 minutes. One
> friend removing wires and cables, me removing the bolts and nuts.
> Then we'd pull the engine - allowing it to fall about 1/2" onto wooden
> blocks and then 3 of us would push the car up on two wheels and roll it
> forward.
> Installation wasn't as slick - requiring jacks and such.
> The bus pull is easy, can be done alone, but I would suggest the help of
> a friend.
> Earlier models are easier than late models.
> Which model is yours?
> The short version is: jack under the engine, disconnect the wiring,
> disconnect the heater cables, disconnect the throttle, disconnect the
> fuel line, remove 4 bolts/nuts on the transmission to engine joint.
> If yours is an early model you can remove the bumper and apron and
> simply roll the van forward leaving the engine behind sitting on blocks.
> If yours is a late, it will require more maneuvering to get the engine
> out as you cannot get the bumper and apron off of these models.
