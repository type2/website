Subject: Re: [T2] 55 mph speed limit(longish)

Part of equipment operation is knowing the design limits and capabilities. 

Responsible operation includes remaining within those parameters
unless you are an emergency responder or in combat.

The only way I can see to accurately know whether or not we are
operating within design parameters is with full instrumentation:

Tach
Oil pressure gauge
Oil Temp gauge
Cylinder head temp gauge
Air / Fuel ratio gauge

This instrumentation is crucial in aircraft mechanics because people's
lives are at stake more so than in a car. In a car, your engine blows,
you pull to the side of the road unhurt in most cases. But in aircraft
this is so vital that at least in some applications I have seen each
cylinder is monitored for A/F ratio. I'm sure we have aircraft pilots
or mechanics on the list that could elaborate on this more, but from
my limited experience, all I can say is that this is the only way for
us to really monitor empirically the things we have been discussing
here. I don't have the money to equip all my vehicles this way yet,
but I am working on it!
