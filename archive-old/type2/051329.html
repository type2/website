> Try getting a Dremyl tool with a cutting disk and creating little slots
> all around the inside edge of the hole in the plug.  You can also drill
> very short holes all around.  Cut a 1/2" bolt short so the cut part fits
> within the hole and lets the hex head stick out. Creat flats on the 1/2"
> threaded part using a grinding stone.  Use JB weld (the 5 minute stuff
> to glue the bolt within the hole.  If it wants to run out, put tape over
> it to hold it there until set.  Since this has to resist torque, the
> holes and the flats are to keep it from spinning in the hole and
> gripping well. Use an ordinary socket to twist the plug out!  MB  78
> Westy 221,000 mi on unrebuilt engine!
