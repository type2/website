the aux battery while driving. My opinion is that it큦 ok if the current

My cooler box uses 4 Ampere (54 Watts) My stereo a bit more, depending on
cicumstances as speed and wind :-) This has not been any problem for my
system, five years old by now.  If the generator charges with it's maximum
current the main battery will eat part of that along with driving lights
and so on. What큦 left over will find it큦 way to the aux battery trough
the relay, few generators will give a charging current large enough to burn
the relay, that큦 why you have a fuse anyway.........

For those with big power consumers adding a second relay might be a
solution. This relay connects your accessories to the aux battery when
ignition is off and to the main battery when ignition is on. Use a standard
switching relay, theese are found rated up to 40 Amperes at your FLAPS. If
