I don't know the whole history (let see if I get it right) but long ago a Canadian by the name of Robertson invented a fabulous screw with a square drive. Henry Ford's assembly was suffering under the agony of the slotted head screw and wanted to use the Robertson to save time on the assembly line in the order of a couple of hours. Robertson was a fool and refused to let Ford manufacture the screw under license so Ford made a deal with Phillips who invented his screw 28 year after Robertson and the rest is history, sadly.
