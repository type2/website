On Wed, 13 Oct 1999, Michael Geurink wrote:

You'll never know the answer to your questions until you have the engine 
opened up.  If the mechanic who did the compression test is competent (an 
unknown) then he can tell the difference between low compression due to 
head problems vs. ring seating problems.

As far as just doing rings vs. a complete P&C replacement, it's been 
reported that the VW engine doesn't take well to simply replacing the 
rings, unlike most American iron.  Has to do with the way these engines 
wear; the cylinders end up somewhat egg-shaped, so new rings don't seat 
well and soon wear out themselves.

The idiot lights should light up when the engine is first started, then 
go out with a little blip of the throttle (battery and oxs/egr) or when 
pressure builds (oil.)  If the lights come on at startup, then they are 
probably working.
