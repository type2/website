I wouldn't use lock-tite here.  These bolts are usually difficult to remove and lock-tite wouldn't help things at all.

One thing to check is that the serrated lock-washers are in place beneath the bolt heads, as well as the three 2-hole plates.  Good boot 
kits come with new bolts and sometimes the link plates, but I've never seen them come with the washers.  I've always cleaned and 
re-used the lock washers and link plates with the new bolts.

These lock washers are rather unique and I cannot recall seeing them in any other application in the bus.  They are neither a spring 
washer or a wave washer.  They have a serrated pattern on their bearing faces that seems to lock the bolts rather well.

If I couldn't prevent loosening of these bolts, I'd use safety wire before lock-tite.  At least then I'd be able to get the bolts out when I wanted 
to!  :)
