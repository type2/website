Tim (and the list):

There's a line in all the manuals to the effect of "Check that all
electrical connections are clean and tight."  After having random starting
problems for several months, replacing various components in the system,
getting more and more frustrated, I (for some unknown reason) grabbed and
pulled the battery cable several weeks ago, and it popped out of the
terminal that connects to the battery!  It had LOOKED fine, and the "clamp"
was clean and tight, but the connection inside was intermittant and nasty
with corrosion...

I now have a back-up starting system to haul around with me (alt, v.r.,
extra cables...) or assist others on the road.

So, please start with the simple stuff so that my expensive lesson benefits
someone besides me.  8^)
