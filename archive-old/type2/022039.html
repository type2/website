This sort of odd light behaviour is usualy down to bad 
grounding of the light cluster. The current illuminating 
one bulb cannot earth so goes the 'wrong way' through 
another bulb and then earths through another bulb on 
the same circuit.
I've seen front indicators come on when people press 
the brake pedal, and they are realy impressed when a) 
you tell them and b) you know whats wrong.
The rear lights holders are mounted in a metal plate 
which acts as a common earth for all the lights in the 
cluster but requires a short length of wire to connect the 
plate to the chassis. This wire may have fallen off, 
otherwise a quick rub with some emry paper on the 
conacts both at the plate and where the wire connects to 
the chassis should solve the problem. 
Do the other side at the same time and you should not 
have a recurrance for a good few years.
