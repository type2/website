Always verify that the shoes are adjusted properly first. That alone will
account for a low pedal in most cases, all other things being proper. Only
then can the e-brake cables be properly adjusted.

If that doesn't resolve things, flush out the old fluid and bleed all
cylinders. A sticking master cylinder can give this symptom as well. Fresh
fluid can disolve the gummy residues and free things up, if not right away,
within a few days. If the fluid is filthy black, follow up with another
flush and bleed in a few days. Then monitor the fluid level for several
weeks. On a contaminated system, this can start things leaking. 

The fresh fluid acts like a solvent and dissolves the varnish and crud that
builds up if you don't flush it periodically. Yearly ist beste to prevent
contamination from getting started. Virtually all brake system leaks are
founded in inadequate maintenance which creates the gunk that makes seals
stick and/or leak. Otherwise, it takes 20 years + for the seals to get
hard, then wear to the point of leaking.
