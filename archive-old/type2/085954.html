I had similar intermittent problems. The tip off was with the lights on I 
could measure a small voltage between the negative battery strap terminal 
(at the battery) and the chassis ground. The voltage would vary as I 
manipulated the strap.
I finally pulled the woven strand battery to ground strap. I measured the 
resistance as I twisted it and noted a changes in reading. It seems in 
certain positions only a few strands were conducting. Maybe some would fuse 
under an increased (lights or starter)load and cause problems. I installed a 
big fat Napa strap and have had no further problems.

I will suddenly lose all power to the motor,

If you lose all (I mean all) power and it comes back on later and acts 
normal it is the battery cables or terminals opening. Nothing else will 
cause this condition.

A positive battery cable short would cause insulation to melt. Opens do no 
such damage.

A total open (many ohms)shuts every thing down if the open is in either 
positive or ground cables. A few ohms causes relays not to work and other 
strange symptoms.
