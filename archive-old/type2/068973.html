Hi gang:

This Saturday I'm heading out to Dog Island, Fla. to work on a late 
70's bus belonging to some friends who own a house there.

Dog Island is a real throwback and I hope it stays that way.  One of 
the few inhabited-but-not-bridged islands left in this overpopulated 
state.  There's a ferry -- a forty-foot boat for foot traffic only.  
The ferryman also own an old WWII LST that can carry your car out.

But no one uses that, except when their current "island car" 
succumbs to rust.  When you arrive at the dock on the island, you see 
what looks like a junkyard -- but it's where the island's few hundred 
residents leave their cars parked until they return from off-island.  
We're talking post-apocalyptic car park here, with a predominance of 
VWs and many vehicles sans windshields, doors, etc.  There are no 
paved roads on the island, no stores, one 8-room hotel, and a garbage 
dump.  It's freakin' heaven, man.

Anyway, their bus is an automatic with FI.  The gang says it starts 
fine but cuts out after a few minutes.  I'm taking tools and basic 
tune-up parts (plugs, rotor, condenser, points) to give it a 
look-see.  I don't want to touch the FI because it scares me :).  Any 
pointers would be greatly appreciated.

BTW a very kind fellow by the name of Helmut in Dusseldorf just sent 
me a raft of cool images of various tents attached to some very 
late-model campers.  Maybe before Christmas I'll have them up on the 
tent pages.
