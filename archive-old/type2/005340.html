In my experience the CDI "black boxes" are intolerant of the heat in a Type
2's engine compartment.  When they get overly warm the ignition system
starts to fail, missing and backfiring or simply ceasing to function.

It's a real bear to troubleshoot.  Function usually returns to normal when
the black box cools down.  Of course, this generates a great deal of
frustration because when you stop to fiddle with the ignition system the CDI
box cools down and the fault disappears.

Been there, done that, sticking with a stock ignition system for its ease of
repair in the field.....
