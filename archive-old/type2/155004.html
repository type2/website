Hi PM,

DON'T PANIC!

The engine stops spinning after 90 degrees when you turn the fanbelt  
because you're running into a compression stroke. If you tighten the  
fanbelt by pushing it inwards you will probably find that the engine  
will spin past the compression stroke and will do the same thing  
another 90 degrees later. If it still won't turn then pull all 4  
spark plugs and try again.

Testing the 1972 starter requires a support for the armature, unless  
you have converted to the 73-79 Auto-tranny starter. The most  
commonly available support for this is a 1968-75 bell housing.  
Without that support there is no way to properly test the starter.
