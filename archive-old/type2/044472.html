> I walked to a nearby phone and called Merle, my trusty VW mechanic at the

His advice was sound in this case.  The fuel pump sits in a relatively
dead-air region of the engine bay, so some turbulence from the open lid
would actually cool it better than the stagnent air normally around it.
Your engine itself probably ran a bit hotter, but the "weak link" was a
bit cooler.

Of course, the obvious question is why didn't you install your spare pump?
