Matt,

Think of the two relays as the power latch and the pump. pin 85 is the
common(ground) for both relays.

12 V applied to 86 or 86a or 88a should energize the pump relay ( 88y is
closed to 88d and 88c). 88d and 88c are tied together.

12V applied to 86c should energize the latch relay (close 88z to 88b and
88a) 88b and 88a are tied together.

In the run state both relay are energized. the latch is held on by the
ignition on switch. The pump is held on by the combination of the latch
relay and the closed switch in the air flow box (pins 36 and 39).
