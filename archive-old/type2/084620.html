Heat exchangers can be removed with the engine in the bus.  Remove rear=20
tin, then muffler, then heat exchangers.  Be careful with the exhaust=20
studs, they may pull out or come out with the nut.  There should be copper=
=20
washers between the exchangers and the heads - they often stick to the=20
heads.  It's easy to have them move on installation and get squished in odd=
=20
directions, causing exhaust leaks.  Don't overtighten the nuts when you put=
=20
them back.  A few lb/ft is all it should take to seal to the head, a few=20
more to keep them tight.  Maybe 10 lb/ft max.  Use anti-seize on the=20
threads and use lock washers, preferably the stock flippy washers but use=20
the cut ones if you have to.
