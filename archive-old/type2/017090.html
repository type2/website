Dave, Frank:
In real life I'm an electrical contractor, so whenever I'm at a loss in
a makeshift situation I use what comes naturally to hand- wire.
What I've used is #12 THHN which is regular commercial- industrial wire
that goes into conduit. THHN is a nylon thermoplastic insulating jacket
that is real shiny and slippery (slides into those pipes really nicely).
It has also worked better than anything else I've ever used to put in
rubber mounted glass. It's thick enough to not cut the lip of the rubber
and tough enough not to get cut by the pinchweld. I like solid rather
than stranded because it'll hold the shape it's bent to.
You still need at least one helper to push while you pull the wire out
and the rubber lip over the metal. I start in the lower center and work
out and up evenly. Remember, GLASS DOESN'T LIKE TO BEND so go easy and
evenly. I also have a Somaca glass installing tool which is nice. It's a
nylon heavy duty tongue depressor that's tapered on the ends.
