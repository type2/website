>..."will do compression test when we change the oil"...

I'd do the compression test now (especially since you have nice weather) and
then do it again after the tune-up--particularly if you plan on adjusting
valve clearance, etc. The pre-tune-up test will give you additional data to
help you understand the condition of your engine. Also, don't forget that
you must warm up the engine to get accurate results from your compression
test. Many people (including myself) also prefer to change oil on a warm
engine to theoretically get more of the suspended junk out of the engine.
