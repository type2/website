I've seen a few threads lately regarding these, and I thought someone 
might like to know of a source for them. Here's the poop from Jameco:

Thermoelectric coolers (TECs) are solid state heat pumps that utilize the 
Peltier effect. During operation, heat is transferred from one side of 
the pump to the other resulting in a hot and cold side with differences 
up to 60 deg. C. Cascade two for up to 131 deg. C.

- - Heat or cool by changing direction of current
- - Rated lifetime over 200,000 hours
- - For stationary use recommend 13.8VDC _at_ 10A supply. Operates on 15.4VDC 
max.
- - Can be operated from a 12V car battery
- - Pumps 50 watts continuous _at_ 6 amps
- - Size:
        - Heat Sink: 5.0"L x 5.0"W x 1.3"H
        - Pump:      2.7"L x 1.8"W x 1.2"H

- - Weight: 1.9lbs

Part no. 131975 Price: $29.95 each
