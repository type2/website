In the following:
"What I would do:
  1) inspect the ball joints -- this is, alas, a job that you cannot do at
     home :-(  Torn boots are a bad sign."

I do *not* mean that you can't inspect them for torn boots at home... you
can and should!  Just that if the boots are torn that they need further
inspection by a pro and that if replacement is necessary, that
replacement is a *very* arduous process.

To replace ball joints you need to remove the trailing arms from the bus,
press out the old ball joints and press in new ones.  Alternatively, you
can buy replacement trailing arms with new joints pressed in for $200 for
?RMMW? or take your old arms and some new ball joints to a local machine
shop to have them pressed out and in or, if you happen to own a large
press, you can do it yourself (we have people on the list who have this
capability, I know).  In any event, replacing ball joints is a *BIG* job,
only the terminally cheap or the true masochist will attempt it at home
for fun (see my previous post... *I* will be swapping out my trailing arms
in a week or two :-) (and I leave it up to y'all to determine which
category I fit in :-)

The whole front end.  Sorry :-(  possible sources are bad tire rods or
drag link, worn idler arm bushings, worn ball joints, misadjusted wheel
bearings.  Usually at 100,000 miles, the front end is just getting a bit
"tired".  

 2) jack up the front end fo the bus, put it on jack stands and have
    someone lay under the bus while a second person turns the wheel.  Look
    for: vertical movement in the idler arm
         play in the steering box (wheel moves, pitman arm does not)
         play in the tie rods/drag link.
 3) check the wheel bearings.  While they are off the ground shake the
    wheels.  if there is any play, that's bad.

> Does the accessory battery charge while driving or does it need to be

should recharge while you are driving (assuming your factory wiring is

> What is the small silver receptacle under the glove box?

Search the archive at www.type2_dot_com for "silver socket"  this will give
you a lifetime of entertaining reading :-)  It is an accesory socket for
european 12V stuff.  You can buy a plug that fits it at BMW motorcycle
dealers and use it to power a dash fan, or electric socks :-)

> Is there a cigarette lighter receptacle?  Where?

Cigarette lighters are rare from the factory.  if you look behind the
dash, you will see punchouts in the metal that you can use to install an
aftermarket lighter (or a junkyard lighter if you can find one)

BTW, at least one of the "silver socket" posts mentioned a cigarette
lighter that worked in the silver socket.  never seen one, tho.
