The brain is different, but I believe the other parts will be the same.
Many people choose the 1.8 over the 2.0 because of head and valve features
that tend to make it run a little cooler. The valves are bigger on the 1.8.

You'd want the brain from the 1.8 on the 1.8 anyway; if you upgrade to a
2.0, you'll want the brain from that. I havn't been following your thread,
so I don't know if you're just planning to rebuild this to 2.0 when you get
it. If you are, holler at me. I'm currently running a 1.8 on a 2.0 brain
and we might be able to work out a swap.

If I were in your shoes, though, I'd probably opt for the 1.8 since it
runs. I have a 2.0 as well as a 1.8 and although I *can* tell a difference
in the amount of power between the two, it's of little consequence. Either
engine is quite adequate for my '72 Riviera. The only time I'd rather have
the power of the 2.0 is when I want to accelerate on a fairly steep
incline. I can hold my own with a 1.8, but I can build speed with a 2.0.
Top end on either engine is at least 85 or 90 and that's too fast in a
Riviera, IMHO.

Thanks! Joe
