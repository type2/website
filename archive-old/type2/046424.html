All of the Coleman disposable propane bottle adapters I've seen are for a
Coleman brand gasoline stove being retrofitted to use propane.  And they
place the bottle right next to the stove, on the right hand side.

You might try an outdoor or RV store for a flexible hose, with an adapter
added to allow it to connect to the disposable bottles.  Bear in mind that
this a very expensive way to buy propane.

Your old tank can be pressure-tested and re-certified [if it passes
inspection and pressure test], and repainted.  Don't be deterred by surface
rust and a scuzzy tank appearance.

Replacement bulk propane tanks are available in many sizes, shapes, and
vertical or horizontal orientation.  there ought to be one that would work
for your stove.

For many years we've used a 2-burner Coleman camp stove burning propane from
a small bulk tank [our '70 Campmobile didn't come with a Westy-installed
stove].  Works very well, and it all stores quickly and compactly.
