I disagree. I have a '69 Westy 1600sp, 30/31 and .009 and it works
fine. Yes, there is a SLIGHT hesitation of the start which is cured by
SLIGHT slipping of the clutch.

This bus was purchased with this set-up (though I did have to replace
the .009 when it wore out), so I don't know how it would perform with
a vacuum advance. HOWEVER, my previous '69 station wagon had vacuum
advance and other than the slight hesitation in the .009, performed
the same. Yes, it got better mileage, but I attribute that more to the
fact the Westy is less aerodynamic and several hundred pounds heavier.

If I could get a vacuum advance for the same price as a .009, I'd
probably try it. But not 'til this one wore out.

As far as I'm concerned, this is a FART
(Frequently-Annoyingly-Recurring-Thread). There are ardent supporters
in both the pro-.009 and anti-.009 camps. Personally, I'm neither. It
was a matter of cash-flow in my case.
