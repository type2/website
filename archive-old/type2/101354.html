Subject: Question for Type1 experts on Valves

For those that a familiar with Type 1 engines.

I'm scrambling to get a 1600 long block into my van to replace a 1500
that is going south quick.

The engine came from my son's '72 Super Beetle that got totaled.  The
only physical damage to the engine from outward appearances was a dent

couple of exhaust studs were broken due to the header and mufflers

least stressed.  This engine ran very well previous to the SB being

very happy, this engine is going to be great!  Cylinder 2 was 50% - hmmm

strong runner.  It looks like it was a rebuild because there is a metal

over the regular serial number.

I'm going to pull the heads anyway to replace the one damaged push rod
tube and all of the push rod seals so I'll be able to inspect them.  My
question is:  Do you think that just doing a quick lapping job on the
offending intake valves will likely fix my problem?  It's been a long
time since I've done this stuff and never before on VW parts.  Just
looking for more experienced opinions on whether I seem to be on the
right track or not.
