The further you advance your timing (the more BTDC) the hotter it will run.
I'd make sure your total advance when you rev the engine up is no more than
28d BTDC (for an upright).  Also, I think I remember you saying that oil
temp light comes on at 220F?  If so, I think that's still pretty cool.  And
you don't know if the oil temp was going to stabilize, if it was going to
stabilize (equilibrium) around 240F then I wouldn't worry about it
especially if the engine is new (they say new engines run hotter).  I'm
still dealing with a new pancake engine I installed in February.  My oil
temp gauge stops rising at 255/260F, that's a little hot right, but it goes
to 280F if you gotta push it (shut it down, shut it down).  The bottom of my
oil filler tube melted off somehow.  Ugh..  I'm going to try thicker oil,
and make sure none of my wheels are sticking, and if I have to remove my
catalytic converter to unload the motor.
