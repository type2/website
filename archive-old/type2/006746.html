Kevin:  I'll second this.  There are no adjustments and such to make on the
exhaust, and it really is something that you can do yourself.  It's more or
less remove-old-attach-new.

I pulled the entire system at one shot, using a floor jack and a piece of
plywood to support muffler & exchangers.  The jack goes at the center of
mass, which is farther back than you might think.  Took a lot of wiggling to
get it off.  Time consuming but not really hard.  If the muffler will come
off by itself, do it that way; prolly easier.  I tried but succeeded only in
turnin rusty hex nuts into rusty round nuts.

Use copper nuts on the muffler.  Liberal application of anti-sieze on
studs/bolts before installing the new.  My muffler was from Bus Depot.  It's
German, tailpipe is Brazilian.  Eight months later the tailpipe is covered
with surface rust while the muffler is clean as the proverbial whistle.

When replacing the system, I installed exchangers, then tried to install
muffler.  No go; bent pipes and such meant that only one side of the muffler
would bolt up.  I had to loosen the nuts at the head, then install muffler
loosely, then go round and tighten everything in turn.
