You might want to talk about the symptoms further.  When you go for that
"first start" I'm assuming the engine turns over OK, it just doesn't start
quickly?  When it does start, does it "run" fairly slowly, going "chug,
chug, chug", threatening to die all the time, or is it more an issue that
it'll start up, idle OK (very briefly) and then just quit?

The fact that you floored the accelerator pedal (I'm assuming this is
sitting in the driveway) and got the engine running would suggest to me you
might have a "too rich" situation on startup; holding the pedal to the floor
gives the engine lots more air which will allow it to burn off the excess
gas, typically accompanied by quite a bit of smoke out the exhaust pipe.

If it's fairly warm now where you're at a faulty thermo-time switch might be
triggering the cold start valve even though it's not needed.
