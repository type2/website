The problem is your bottom-feeder engine builder
has a history of purchasing rejected parts in large
quantities. Parts suppliers offer these deals to
reduce overstocks of junk and Gex scoops them up.
Then these mismatched pistons and cylinders,
connecting rods, rebuilt heads, etc. are combined
into sets for your engine. Specifications will be found
beyond the acceptable limits, and despite the fact
your engine produces some power, it won't have the
expected service life you would like.
You've already experience one failure.
