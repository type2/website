The problem with using weld thu primer for an undercoating, first off is
cost-$20 for a rattle can of this stuff and second-you still have to seal it.
This primer is zinc rich and is activated by heat...ie..spot welds.  And as
you know 99% of joined panels have sealer around the edges, hence this stuff
is great for reconstruction, but I wouldn't use it for an all around primer.
But due to most joined panels having sealer the old school of thinking is to
screw weld thu primer and just weld away.  My thoughts are sealers can be
broken and moisture can get to the welds.  As for the outside, I'll stick to
Vari-Prime, followed by a primer surfacer, and then primer sealer.
 
Where to score Weld Thu?  Most body shop suppliers carry it.
