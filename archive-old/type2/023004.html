No experience (yet) with aftermarket cams but new stock cams will go a long 
way with hydraulics if taken care  of.  My stock hydraulic cam has nearly

longevity of this VW of Canada cam.

>This is starting to seem like way too many questions for one email, and I
>still haven't touched on any of the stuff about
>porting/polishing/unshrouding/CCing heads, CW vs. standard cranks, shot
>peened rods etc... (ie what the heck is all of this stuff and do I need
>it/want it?), so I'll sign off for now.

Porting - probably not necessary and has the potential to screw up your 
matched intake system.

Polishing - probably not necessary - only minor gains possible 
anyway.  Possible issues with fuel condensation on polished port 
walls.  Only exception might be polishing the combustion chamber - I had 
this done and it didn't hurt anything.  Supposed to decrease detonation by 
eliminating sharp edges which become hot and can spark preignition.

Unshrouding - I had this done and I'm happy with the results.  Supposed to 
allow the air to enter the cylinder more easily.  It's a low-risk modification.

CCing heads - definitely.  Helps balance the forces in your engine.

Counterweighted cranks help balance the engine which will make it smoother 
and quieter.  Definite win.

Shotpeening rods - this makes them slightly stronger but is unnecessary in 
non-race engines, IMO.

Anything else?  :)
