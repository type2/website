Howard,

I don't want to ruffle any feathers here, but it
sounds like a few people are saying to use the
space-shuttle to fly your kite;-)

I used a metal-cutting hole-saw that I got for $14.95
from my local hardware store. They start at around 2"
dia and progress in 1/8" increments and I went 1/8"
over because I was using the busboys triple-guage
mount (it's just a 'beauty' plate that sits between
the dash and the guage...it's not really a mount) and
needed a little play (there is no play in the mounting
plate, so I needed a little room in the holes to
accomodate for possible miss-measuring). I attached
the hole saw to a *good* drill (i.e., heavy duty) and
it cut all 3 holes *PERFECTLY* in less than a
minute...no oil, no nothin'.

I did pre-cut the vinyl, though. Even though I went
1/8" over what I needed, the guages sat in there
perfectly (even without the Busboys 'beauty' plate)
and looked stock. I almost didn't use the mounting
plate;-)


Just my .02 (trying to bring the mountain back down to
a mole-hill;-)
