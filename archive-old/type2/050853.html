Platinum plugs have a smaller electrode than standard plugs.  This allows
the spark to jump at a lower voltage than the larger electrode of a
standard plug, which means that less energy is delivered per spark.

This is OK if the system is designed to use these plugs, but probably
isn't as good if the system isn't designed to do so.
