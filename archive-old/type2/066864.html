Actually, sodium *is* also extremely reactive (i.e., flammable).
It's fun to carve off a chunk of sodium (which you can do with a
butter knife--it's very soft) and toss it on a puddle.  It
skitters around and burns.  Like phosphorus, once it starts
burning, it's pretty much going to finish no matter what you do.

Storing it in oil (or inside a valve stem) keeps the oxygen away
so it doesn't oxidize (i.e., burn).
