Hi Ed,
Kinda hard to explain it but......it depends on the year of the bus/
location of the cannister as to whether you are supposed to have the tube on
the left end of the fan housing. [This is pertains to USA buses with the
type IV engine] In the early years, 74 to 76? the charcoal cannister was
mounted in the right corner above the battery. 77-78 it was mounted on the
left side of the gas tank panel. 79 it went back up in the right. 77-78 had
the tube on the left end to go to the left end of the cannister. The early
ones and 79's got the hose tube connection from the 1/2 upper cylinder head
tin. I put a couple of pics of it here:
http://photos.yahoo_dot_com/bc/aufevervw?d&.flabel=fld15&.intl=us&.src=ph&start=
1
This is a 79 Cal version I had laying around. You will see the tube sticking
out forward of #2 spark plug hole. There are 2 versions of this tin. 77-78
does not have the tube.
Yes, people mis-match the fan housing and tin all the time....
Where is your cannister mounted?
