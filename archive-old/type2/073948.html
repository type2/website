Subject: RE: [T2] Syn oil to regular oil- Dangerous?

I am a distributor for Amsoil.  Amsoil developed synthetic oil for the Air
Force long before Mobil ever thought they could market it.  There are two
things that Synthetic oil offers engines. 1) Viscosity is stable from
extremely cold temperatures to extremely hot ones. This means easy starting
and quick oil pressure in sub zero conditions and no boiling off, leaving
waxes & sludge at high temps. Synthetic gear oils make your trnsmission
shift as easily in the winter as it does in the summer. 2) less friction is
afforded by highre wear protection,  this gives you better mileage.  These 2
thing combined mean the oil last longer like 10,000 miles, and only needs
changing to remove wear partices not trapped in filters.  In fact many
truckers and fleet owners run dual remote filters and only change oil when
oil analysis indicates high levels of contaminates 20-30,000 miles. If you
are aiming to get extended drain intrvals from the use of synthetic you must
use really good oil filters like hasting who makes them for Amsoil which
filter down to 1-5 microns and of course have anti-drain back valves.

Now saying all that I will also say it is not for every engine.  If your
engine is already worn, 60,000-100,000 miles it is usually not worht using
synthetic since you will be burning it.  You also do not want to break in a
newly rebuilt engine with it as it is too slippery and rings won't seat
properly.  Run natural oil for the first 3,000- 10,000 miles and then switch
to synthetic.
