Hey volks, I'm back after being off the list for a couple months.  I never
unsubscribed, anyone know what happened?

To try and make a long story short, I am now going on month 18 of not having
a bus under my backside.

This weekend, I embarked on a trip 2 hours north to the Lake Mille Lacs area
of Minnesota to hang out with some friends.  This is a perfect trip to take
in a bus.  However, I was forced to take my winter beater which, at the
moment, is a 1985 Buick Electra Estate Wagon.  Just the name by itself makes
me cringe.

The weather was raw and wet when I left Friday morning.  45 miles up Hwy 169
the Buick started running very rough and I lost power brakes at low speed.
Great I thought to myself.. just what I needed.  I got to the hotel and
missed the first entrance because I could not make the Buick slow down fast
enough without power breaks.  The brake pedal was rock hard.  Let me tell
you, driving a full-size American station wagon without brakes is scary.  I
finally slowed down enough to make it into the parking lot and park the car.
At idle the car sounded like it was only running on 4 of the 8 cylinders.
It was pouring rain so instead of looking at it then I pouted and checked
into the hotel and went to bed very depressed.  There is no feeling worse
than being stuck 120 miles from home especially when you are an anti-social
geek with limited friends like me.

The next day I popped the hood and started drying everything off and checked
for vacuum leaks and was getting frustrated.  After an hour I gave up and
called AAA for a tow to the closest garage.  Expecting the worse I waited
for the tow truck.  About an hour later this older gentleman shows up with
his wife in the passenger seat.  "Garrison Service Center with 36 years
experience" proudly written on the side of the truck.  He asks what is
wrong, tells me to start it and pop the hood, and starts playing with the
carburetor.  2 minutes later the engine is running nice and smooth and power
brakes have returned.  Somehow, the secondaries on the 4-barrel were staying
open causing more fuel to enter the engine than it could burn.  Time for an
oil change!

So, Sunday morning comes and I start heading back home.  Miserable weather
again and I am the only one on the road at 3am.  As I make my way down the
wet highway, I start to thinking about how cool this trip could have been if
I only still had a bus.  My bus never let me down and if something broke I
could fix it!  I thought back to the spring of 1996 when I took that 4300
mile/10 day road trip out to the North East and back in my 1972 Bus.
Whenever I have drove any of my VWs in the past it was as if I always had a
close friend with me.  Now, I am scared to drive and almost hate cars
altogether.

But, in the end I made it home with no problems.. only a headache from
thinking of so many things in such a short time period.

So there we have it, I am back.. I am still wannabuser trying to save up
cash for a '71 or earlier (split-screen Sundial or early deluxe preferred)
Bus or Single Cab with a solid body.  However, it looks like the prices for
decent busi keep rising each year.  Searching the classifieds I came across
TWO busi for over $19K.  That is obscene.  But, that is a different rant.

Thanks for allowing me to vent.
