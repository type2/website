> Also, what "ages" brake fluid and makes it less effective/problematic?

Brake fluid is hygroscopic. This means it absorbs moisture. It pulls it right
out of the air! The water settles into the lowest point: the wheel cylinders.
This causes 2 problems. One is rust pits in the cylinder and the seals start
leaking. The other is worse, water boils at 212F. Dot 4+ [MB brake fluid]
boils at 500F. Using your brakes going down a mountain with water in the fluid
will cause NO brakes!
