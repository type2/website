Hi Bob,

I think it has to do with a number of factors, but loose head bolts  
were not among them.

1. The engine sat for a LONG time. I think that explains the pitting  
on the pistons, and perhaps it could have affected the rings as well.

2. It wasn't broken in properly.

3. Timing. I had timed the bus to run at 7.5 BTDC _at_ 900RPM with the  
single-vac ditributor from my previous engine. It's a 205P with  
pertronix from a 2L engine.

Subsequent research has shown me that the timing spec for this (high- 
compression) engine using the original distributor is 27 degrees at  
3500 rpm.
http://www.oldvolkshome_dot_com/ignition.htm#D7172FA

I will have to wait until I can compare the timing specs on the  
engine... I don't yet know if there is a significant difference  
between 7.5_at_900 and 27_at_3500.
