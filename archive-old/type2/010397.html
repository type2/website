NAMAIXELSYD_at_aol_dot_com wrote:

> 1.  What size engine originally came in the 71 Type 2?  From what I
> can tell, 
> I guess it was a 1500 or 1600cc.  Does anyone know what it's
> horsepower 
> rating is?  Max speed, etc.

The engine is a 1600 dual port, the last refinement of the venerable
type 1 engine.  It's a good engine, puts out 50 horsepower or so, and
will push the bus along at a comfortable 60 mph.  I've had my '71 up to
75, but it is much more happy and comfortable to drive at 60 or 62. 
(these are true MPH figures, BTW.)
> 
> 2.  If I wanted a more powerful engine, what would be the most
> logical 
> choice?  Would all the bus parts still interchange with it?  (I know,
> I 
> know...it's a bus, not a race car...but I'd still like it to have
> some punch 
> to it.  Nothin wrong with that, is there?)

No, but if you go for an engine swap be aware that you will increase
the complexity of the installation by several times.  You can easily
get an adapter to bolt just about anything to the VW transmission, but
then you have to mess with cooling, electrical, fuel, gearing, other
linkages, etc.  Once you get another engine in that engine bay, don't
expect any mechanic to want to touch it.  You'll be on your own!  If
you want more power you might be better off to get a later-model bus
with a type IV engine as standard.  They are more powerful than the
type 1 and will last a long time.

If you decide to go this route, let me know!  I'd _love_ to find a
rust-free '71!  :)
