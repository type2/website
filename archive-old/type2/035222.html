Subject: I told 'em so.....

I worked at a VW-Audi dealership back in the late 60's, and one summer day
the owner handed me a car to test as 'maybe the replacement for the
beetle.' As I recall, it was black and small, about the size of a beetle,
boxy, conventional-looking, with a hood and trunk, like a Fiat only rear
engined. Maybe like a squared-off Renault Dauphine of that era. All I was
asked to do was drive the car for a day and report my impressions. At the
time, I was driving a 62 beetle.

The mule was an OK car and I said so, noting that it was just another
square econo-box, and adding the remark that I didn't know why VW was
considering changing the most successful and popular design in history.

Never heard anything more about it. The test mule was only at the
dealership for about a week, other people drove it and reported their
opinions, then it went back to limbo in VW of America land.

And there you have it, for whatever it is worth. Doesn't seem to illuminate
much at all, in my opinion.   But I was right, and they didn't listen to me!

Then, in the 70's the Rabbit appeared, followed by the Jetta and the Golf,
and finally the Passat and Corrodo.  Now, retro-enter the New Beetle, 30
years later. I told 'em so. Maybe somebody found my report.
