In preparation of my bus (76 2.0L FI) for trip thru southwest, I had
changed points, plugs, dist cap, air cleaner, fuel filter; all the
usual maintenance items that I feel comfortable working on. I had not
changed the condenser in the last decade because I do not own a timing
light and did not want to remove the dist. A few days later the bus
began cutting out on slight inclines at highway speeds. I immediately
suspected the FI system, the expensive stuff that I know nothing
about. A friend of mine that works on VWs suggested I change the
condenser. My experience with condensers is that they are either good
or bad, no in-between. If they are bad the points will pit really bad
and this had not been the case with my points. I changed the condenser
anyway and borrowed a timing light to reset dist. The bus runs better
than ever now. My lesson learned: buy a timing light and change the
condenser when I replace the points. Much better to learn this now
than when in middle of nowhere in south texas.
