Subject: Re: [T2] Understanding AFC fuel injection--question

The auxiliary air regulator doesn't have anything to do with the fuel
mixture.  Like you said, this air is measured by the AFM so it is taken into
account when the fuel mixture is computed.

The purpose of the auxiliary air regulator is to let ADDITIONAL air bypass
the throttle body.  Some air can always bypass at the throttle body itself,
this is how the engine idles.  The air regulator allows more air by, so the
effect is kind of like holding your foot down on the gas pedal to raise the
idle speed.  The whole idea is for the air regulator to allow a little more
air past the throttle plate to allow the engine to idle a little faster so
it can overcome the extra drag of cold oil, etc.  That's all it does.

The mixture is adjusted for temperature depending on information from the
intake air temperature sensor and the cylinder head temperature sensor.
