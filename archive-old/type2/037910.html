Matt -

The service manuals I have shows that stock motors put out maximum torque at
about 3200-ish RPM, which should be the motor's most efficient speed. See
Scott & Maggie's RPM/MPH calculator at:

http://www.scottandmaggie_dot_com/gearcalc.asp

to find out what your corresponding MPH would be. Just because that's the
motors most efficient RPM doesn't mean you get the best MPG at that speed.
Pushing a breadbox down the road at 65 takes more throttle than pushing it
down the road at 55, what, with the drag co-efficient and all. I don't know
of a MPH/MPG calculator yet, but one thing is clear - the slower you go, the
better your gas mileage. How many MPG do you want?
