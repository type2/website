There is a product from Victor (gaskets) called
Muffler Seal. It's at a lot of FLAPS - a white and
orange tube. It is the consistency of toothpaste and
you smear it on to the uneven surfaces, bolt 'em
together and it sets up like concrete! Down the road
when you get everything out where you can work on it
in the open, you can chip the stuff back off and do a
proper job. I use it on all header joints. Unless the
gap is extreme, I use just the muffler seal. It will
handle 1/4 inch gaps! 

Before you settle on a leak source, pop the little
rubber boot out of the hole for the oil pressure light
sending unit (behind *forward of* the distributor) and
just watch that sucker while the engine is running.
Many of them seep oil. Quite a few ooze oil and a few
gush oil. It's right above the oil cooler and in a
high wind area so oil travels from there quite a bit.
Buy two different brands - it's a lottery to find one
that seals! -BaH
