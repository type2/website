Subject: New starter?

Well Volks,  While taking a carload of Boy Scouts camping this weekend, my
'80 Westy decided to give me a hard time.  On the way down we stopped for
food and then the sliding door wouldn't close. Got is shut and it started
slow, like the starter was straining to turn.  Got to camp and after a short
stop, tried to move the van - no start.  Solinoid would click, but starter
wouldn't go.  

BTW, I had the typical no-start after a long ride type of problem.  Then it
would start if I rapped on the solenoid.  I fixed it then with the well
known additional relay.  This is different, it sounds like the starter is
straining to turn, almost like a weak battery.  Then later, or the next day,
it will eventually start.  

Would a failing starter refuse to go when hot, and then tend to work when
cool?  How do you know when the starter is really shot? 

BTW, I took time to open up the door and fix my latch on Saturday.  While I
did the some of the scouts got in trouble messin' with a nearby farm
tractor.  But at least it started on Sunday and we made it home!
