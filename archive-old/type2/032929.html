At 9:55 AM -0800 11/21/99, Dale Goodvin wrote:

>1.  Should I stay with the braided hoses (when I went to parts store, they
>said "no")

It would be preferable, but most "emission hose" that you will find in the
FLAPS will stand up just fine to the 20-25 inches of max vacuum that will
be contained by the hoses to your distributor.  After all, it's carrying
the same kind of vacuum as in all the American cars for which it is
designed.  There is nothing special or different about the vacuum in German
air-cooled engines.

>2.  Is there a "spec" size for both hoses? What are the correct sizes for
>the 2
>hoses?

Don't know offhand.  You could check one of the service manuals (if you
don't have Bentley yet, I suggest you buy it - it's not expensive) or buy a
vernier caliper (an essential tool IMO) and measure the OD of the nipples
yourself, then buy hose with a slightly smaller ID.  The quick and dirty
method is to just go to your FLAPS and buy a few feet of each size from
1/8" to 1/4".  It's cheap, and having extra hunks of hose under your seat
for emergencies can never hurt.

>(Parts stores i went to do not have the exact same size as the hoses
>presently
>connected to my distributor)

>4.  Is it critical that one stay with the spec sizes?

The hose should fit snugly.  If it is a loose fit, don't use it.  If it is
so tight that you can barely force it on, don't use it.

>5.  Which hose goes where?  (as I cannot be certain that the present set-up is
>correct)

The port on the outer half of the canister is vacuum advance, and goes to
the port in the middle of the left or right carb.  The port on the inner,
flat half of the canister is vacuum retard, and should be connected to the
port at the base of the left carb (inside, throttle-body level).

>6.  Finally, why is one hose bigger than the other?

I don't know, but I'm sure it has something to do with "DM".
