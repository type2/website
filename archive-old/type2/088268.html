I have also heard that the fuel is in the process of being reformulated and
reblended right now. My industry knowledgeable friend heard from a
technician that this same thing happened before.. That it was rotting out
the insides of fuel metering systems, since it would settle out in the
pumps, and was also very corrosive to some metals. Result, frequent fuel
metering system failures.
This material can be seen very easily by pouring some of the fuel on
concrete.. It will appear as globules, looking as water would if mixed with
fuel. If the concrete is dry, the fuel with soak right in, and the globules
will stand up on the surface.
Alcohol may be OK in fuel.. If you can keep it emulsified and blended. But
it's no good if it settles out.
So, that's the deal then.
If you have any problems with your idle, consider your fuel. I would drain
some directly from the tank, raising the driver's side of the vehicle so
that it settles into the drain end of the tank, and test it by getting some
into a clear glass or plastic jug, then allowing it to settle for a bit. You
have to look very, very close for the line of demarkation, an almost ghostly
line that will move as you slowly tip the container. If you find this, then
switch fuels or fuel sources.. at least go for the lower octane grade, but a
change of brand might be better. If you really needed a higher octane, then
you might consider buying the lowest, unblended grade and adding some octane
booster yourself from the auto specialty shelves.
