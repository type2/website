Subject: Re: [T2] Backfiring and not start - suggestions sought

The backfiring could be from the lean condition of yet
another massive vacuum leak or it could be
cross-firing in the distributor cap from moisture or
cross-firing between sparkplug wires. It could have
even been caused by a drop of water making its' way
through the fuel system but I find that unlikely.

You are accustomed to having one or another vacuum
hose blow off, so I'm sure you know that you need to
actually grab hold of each end of each one and check
that it really is firmly attached because the
preformed hoses appear to be attached when they are
not.

One that usually gets overlooked is the air bypass
valve hoses which are buried and take on so much heat
over the years that the entire undersides blow out. I
have seen several such cases. Check every hose for
decay / cracking / splitting.

If you still have the decel valve, it and the hoses
connecting are also likely. -BaH
