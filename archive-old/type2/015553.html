Suggest if you don't have the tools and skills to confirm running
clearances, you should hire the machinist to assemble the bottom end
including mounting the flywheel with spec end play. Make them responsible
for the assembly complete. You can check if it turns freely and has proper
end play BEFORE you pay the invoice.

Find a machinist skilled with euro engines. If they do mostly Fords and
Chevys or Asian, do your self a favor and find better. In the NW US, you
can't beat NW ConRod for spec or better VW assemblies, day in and day out.

Don't waste your time with guesses. Pull the drain plate and check for
chunks and slivers. Then pull it out, tear it down and inspect the parts.
The cause of the failure may well be obvious, but sometimes the root of the
problem may require calibrated micrometers and skill to determine. 

Lots of things can cause a motor to seize. Lots of things can cause
overheating: clearance problems (ring end gaps or bearings too close) poor
lubrication (oil pump setup, crud in oil galleys, sunken carb float or
loose jet), the wrong lubricants for breakin, heavy foot or loads during
breakin, sporty spark advance, wizbang plugs or auxillary oil coolers. Just
a few guesses which have little meaning compared to teardown and inspection.
