Subject: Re: [T2] clutch problems

I had a guru tell me that a flywheel can be worn out. They need to be
reground and there is a critical dimension, the distance between where the
pressure plate bolts on and the friction surface. If really worn and/or
burnt, this dimension opens up and there will be insufficient spring
pressure on the clutch disk, causing short life and the burnout you already
experienced.  I don't know the specs- Maybe someone on the list does. I
believe the flywheel has to be reground on 2 surfaces, maintaining this
specification.
Also, as someone else mentioned today, you may need a new pilot bearing in
the end of your crankshaft.
Have a good look at the clutch fork assembly and throwout bearing and clips
when you get in there. Look for cracks etc.
