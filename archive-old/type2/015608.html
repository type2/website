Not grease, Patrick, light oil. Sewing machine oil, like 5 weight. Most
hardware store have what's called a Zoom Spout Oiler. That's what I use.
ATF is also acceptable. 2 or 3 drops. Get a spark plug thread chaser and
clean up the head thread. Use the grease on the thread chaser to capture
any crud so it won't fall into the head.

If you check the archives you will see I warned of this problem with
anti-seize several times in the past. Even if you use a small amount of
anti-seize, after about the third time it builds up into a problem unless
you chase the threads every time. Why add time/money to a simple repetitive
maintenance procedure?
