I have not installed this in any of my VW's but I did years ago install one
in a non-VW.  It worked OK, but there was an "issue" that would, I think,
apply to almost any combination of car and aftermarket delay unit.  The
issue exists due to the fact that the delay control is not in any way
interlocked with the wiper speed control.  The way the delay works is that
you cut and splice you factory wiper wiring to insert relay contacts from
the delay unit into the low speed and park circuits for your wipers.  To
operate in delay mode, you turn the wipers on low speed and then switch on
the delay.  The delay intermittenly closes the contact in the low speed
circuit, and in between, closes the contact in the park circuit so the
wipers continue moving until they get to the park position after each
intermittent wipe cycle.  You MUST make sure that you never turn the wipers
on high speed with the delay turned on.  When you do this, the wipers
intermittently get energized on both high and low speed at the same time -
which I can't imagine is very good for the wiper motor.  I usually
remembered this, but I could not count on the SO to do the same when driving
my car.  MY solution was that I did not tell her (the SO) about the delay
unit.
