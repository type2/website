	Nathan,
	
	There are a few problems that can make your tach go banas. The last thing
i would suggest is the tach itself or the diode. Of course the ones you
have already checked: points, condensator and the ignition coil. 

	BUT, the problem can also be in the high tension part of the ignition
system. The ignition coil is basicly a transformer, the points give a 12
volt high current power peak. The ignition coil transforms this to a high
voltage low current power peak suitable to generate a spark in the ignition
plugs gap. If you fed the HT side of the ignition coil with the same
tension (AC) it gives to the plugs you would get 12 volts (AC) at the LT
side. 

	If you have a bad plug lead the small shortcut causes an interference
going "back" trough the ignition coil to the low voltage side. Your tach
think it was a pulse from the points and counts it, that's what tachs do,
counts pulses. Therfore you have to check the high tension side of the
ignition system too, leads, rotor, cap and plugs.

Hope this was of any help.
