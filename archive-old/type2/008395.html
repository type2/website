before you do this, consider the following:  (bad Bill Nye impression!)

The oil passages in your engine are all different sizes,  Sometimes a
large oil passage leads to several small passages.  What happens if
your engine flush knocks loose a chunk of grunge that then gets lodged
in a narrow part of the system?  In a human, this is called a
"stroke".  In your engine it's called a "clogged oil cooler" or a
"fried bearing"

Also, consider that any grunge you knock loose is conducted to your
bearings, where it probably won't have any beneficial effects!

The only right way to clean oil passages is with a complete teardown.
