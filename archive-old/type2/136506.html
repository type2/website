Have you used an O2 sensor to check the mixture at operating temperature,
both with and without the stand-off? My concern is that even a small
amount of heat radiation off the stand-off will lower the sensed
temperature during operation and lean the mix.  Not a lot, just a little,
but I would worry until I knew.  Of course insering a low-value resister
would probably make up for the temperature loss but then you are back to
square one on the starting.  The whole subject of heat transmission,
transfer, and radiation within and between different metals and alloys
gets very complicated very fast.
