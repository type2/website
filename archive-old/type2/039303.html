FWIW -

I do all of my highway/freeway cruising in the lane to the left of the "slow
lane" for various reasons:

1)    The semi's are usually in the "slow lane", which causes that lane to
undergo more wear and tear, thus usually a much rougher ride.

2)    The semi's can pass without having to change lanes.

3)    The merging/exiting traffic becomes of little concern to me, as far as
merging or getting in the way of the off-rampers. Just hold 60 mph, Mr.
Checkov.

4)    If two semi's are bearing down on me abreast, I can always move left
one more lane to get out of both of their ways, a maneuver which has
prompted the friendly "blinking of the lights" from the gratuitous
professionals in their mobile offices.

5)    It affords the cars in the #1 and #2 (and #3) lanes just a little
closer look of Itch. I'm sure they appreciate that on their hurried ways to
work or play.

The freeways I drive are usually 4 or more traffic lanes, so I still leave
at least 2 unhindered lanes to my left. YH(ighway)MV, but the extra
smoothness of the "lane to the left" is very noticeable to Me & Itch.
