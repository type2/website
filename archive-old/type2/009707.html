Yes, I remember the discussion last year.  In that discussion, I wrote
about the experiment that I did by attaching a pointer to one of the
flap pivots so that I could visually check the position of the flap. 
I discovered that at low outside air temperatures the flaps do indeed
modulate the flow of air.  On a freezing day, I stopped after running
at 55mph for a few miles.  The flaps were about midway in their travel
and were actually opening up due to the reduced airflow at idle.

The thermostat regulates the flap position to maintain a 70C outlet
air temperature.  Simple, and it works well.
