Hey All,

I am still new to doing my own maintenance, and I need some help with a 1976 
FI Westy,aka Yellie.

After having followed the Muir guide to find out why it stalls out after it is 
warmed up.  I am left with the following dilemna.

It starts up after about two attempts and runs fine for about 15 miles.  After 
that if I slow down to 2nd gear or stop it will stall unless I continually 
pump her with some gas.  If it does stall out, I usually need to wait about 
30-45 minutes to rest/cool off/decide she's ready.  Then she'll start up and 
run me to final destination.  Where I almost made to....

Everything in the Muir book checked out except that when checking the spark 
from the coil the spark was orange ("OK for now but needs to be 
replaced"--Muir).  
The other recommendation had to do with the EGR filter.  Since Muir didn't 
have much on that, I went to Bentley.  According Bentley my chassis number 
indicates that I have the later EGR system.  However, when looking at my 
engine, it appears that I have the earlier version EGR system installed.  The 
kicker is that either way, I cannot find the EGR filter anywhere.

My question(s)are: Would having a weak coil cause the stalling and not 
starting problem?  Is that fact that I don't appear to have an EGR filter 
causing it? Is it some combination of the two? Or am I following a red herring 
here?
