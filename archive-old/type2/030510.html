My indicated 70 is probably closer to an actual 65-67 i have checked my
speedo with a gps.

If you do the math with the stock ratios and stock tires, at 4100 rpm one
will be going approximately 70 MPH.  I consider that a safe cruising rpm
for a well built motor. (25.5 diameter tires, 5.375 ring and pinion.  I
couldnt remeber the exact size of the tires, so i calculated that they
were shorter than i remeber, so one is probably turning 4,000 at 70 rather
than 4100..)

Also, i go 70 if i can maintian that with partial throttle, i rarely mash
the pedal to the floor to maintain speed.

It is my humble estimation that 4100 rpm will kill a motor much slower
than heavy loads.  Push it hard, and you will kill it i agree.  But i dont
believe speed to be a good indicator of how hard a motor is being pushed
neccecarily. One can go 70 in some situations with little stress, in other
situations trying ot maintain 55 will heat your motor up right quick.

I dont neccecarily expect this motor to go a real long time as it was my
first t1 rebuild and i have made a few maintenance mistakes.  But i dont
think my tendency to go 65-70 has accelerated things too terribly much.
