> 1. how is the best way to remove the pilot bearing outer casing?  That is
all
> that is left.  I read the archives and it talks about a. pilot bearing
puller,
> b, paper towel soaked in oil, and c) grease and a smack of the hammer with

> socket properly sized for the opening...  So any recommendations?

Well, the *easiest* way to remove it is to use the tool for the job, which
is an inside puller.  I believe many FLAPS will lend you one for free, or
you can rent one at the local tool rental place.  It's about a 1 minute jog.
I guess those other ways might also work, depending on cicumstances, but I
haven't tried them.

> I have the
> flywheel off.  Is there a bearing in the flywheel?  I see a small case
where
> the pilot bearing passes through....

No, the pilot bearing lives in the end of the crankshaft.  No bearing in the
flywheel itself.

> 4. how tough is it to replace the pilot shaft seal in the tyranny?  while
I
> can see it....

The hardest part for me was getting it out since it's kind of hard to get
to.  I used a seal puller and basically destroyed the seal out.  Getting the
new one back in place is easy.

>  3. Anything else I should do while IO have the engine out?  clutch looks
> good, I will be replacing all fuel lines, cleaning and painting the tin,
> replacing air/intake main\fold hoses, front and rear main seals, por 15
the
> battery box areas, sealing up everything I can to provide a good cooling
> system.  What else?

FI seals, large O ring and felt washer for the flywheel, check out the
thermostat and flaps function.
