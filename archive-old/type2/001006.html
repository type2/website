I would look at it this way:

Never have to replace points again.
Coil always puts out maximum voltage. As the points get dirty, they lower
primary voltage. When primary voltage drops, so does secondary output.

I have been using a Cal Vanagon electronic ignition in my 77 Westy for the
past 55,000 miles and it has been 100% troublefree. This system does not use
any friction or a rubbing block. Nothing to wear out in the distributor. I
have been putting together the pieces to make this setup [all original VW
parts available just about anywhere] and they run around $60-$70 when I can
find them at the wrecking yards. Thats a distributor, coil, ign. module, and
a harness connecting the 3.

I dunno.. you do the math....
how many times do you want to replace points in the middle of a road on a
cold, rainy night?
