> No, 79 Cal buses and 80-83.5 Cal vanagons have electronic ignition. It is
> not Capacitive Discharge.
> 
> > Increased milage, is this true?  If so, it's another thing that I'd like
> > to add to my list of items to purchase!
> 
> The Cal electronic ignition will give you better efficiency of the ignition
> system. The coil output will always be at maximum. With new, clean points
> coil output will be high but degrades as the points get dirty. Secondary
> coil output is relative to primary voltage. The Cal system has no parts to
> wear because nothing touches. Dwell and timing never changes. As the rubbing
> block on points wear, dwell increase and timing retards.
> 
> >
> > Anyone have recommendations as to what kind of system would work well on
> > my 76 2000FI?  Does bosch make a system that is better or do the
> > aftermarket ones work as well?
> 
> The Cal system is a drop-in replacement if you get all the parts :
> distributor, coil, module w/heatsink, and harness.
> I may have enough pieces around to make 2 sets... :)
> 
> I am not familiar with the Tiger unit but if it is like the early 70's
> Mercedes system that used points.....we had lots of problems until MB came
> out with totally electronic distributors. The CD unit used very low voltage
> across the points. It was not enough to burn off residue and keep the points
> clean. We had to drill a vent hole in the side of the dist. cap to make the
> points last. The oil fumes in the crankcase would go up the dist. shaft and
> coat the points, since the cap was the highest point. Drilling a hole
> allowed the fumes to vent, instead of lingering.
