Hey Stevo, Merry Christmas.
Top of the season to the list as well.  It's great to have such a resource at
my fingertips.

    I just fixed the leak on my '76 and no water yet.  We live in Vancouver,
BC,  so i figure it's sealed with all the rain we get here on the "Wet
Coast".  My fix was a simple tube of black silicone sealant, the kind of
sealant used on GM and Ford for gaskets.  I used a caulking gun and snipped
off the optional plastic nozzle that comes with the sealant tube at the
almost smallest opening possible. Then I manuvered the nozzle under the
windshield seal and started "injecting" the sealant all around the inside of
the seal.  The rubber seal is very rigid and popped the nozzle out so I had
to restart the nozzle a few times.  I started from the top center and worked
my way around to the bottom center and started again around the other side.
Then I performed the same procedure on the seal where it makes contact to the
body and went around.
    The hardest part was the clean-up...
    Once I had the silicone in both sides of the outer windshield seal, I
used my thumb to push on the seal to distribute the excess silicone around
and push out the excess as well.  Then I used disposable rags to wipe up the
mess.  You need  a fair amount of rag material as silicone can and will get
everywhere if you're not careful. Once that was completed, I used my finger
with a liberal amount of saliva, yes saliva, to make a nice even surface.
Saliva won't allow the silicone to stick to the fingers.  Ask any plumber who
has done this on the perimiter of a bathtub.
    This was two weeks ago and we're still dry.  I know that it's probably
frowned upon by some list members but if you aren't making a show vehicle and
want a good fix, it's great.

Good luck'
