You have a dual vacuum advance, correct?
You have vacuum at idle to the inboard nipple, correct?
If you disconnect that vacuum your timing goes to 7.5 BTDC, correct?
If all 3 answers are correct, your total amount is correct.
You are neglecting to realize that when you drive down the road at a steady speed, your vacuum retard comes back and cancels vacuum advance. Both vacuums are equal and the spring in the dual unit centers the advance. As soon as you give it throttle, manifold vacuum drops and you get advance. Ported vacuum overcomes the spring. At a steady speed above around 3600 rpm's you would only have about 25 degrees of centrificul advance.
