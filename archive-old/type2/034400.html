Hi Chris

I bought a broken bus earlier this year. The number 1
cylinder was shot (looked like the head hadn't been torqued
properly). Did a poor mans rebuild (found a used
P&C, honed and rerung all four cylinders), put it back together
and headed for the Yomega YoYo competition in Rhode Island
my son had entered in.

The 73 engine has a weber DFEV conversion and the breather
hose was simply sticking up like yours. Being smart (or so I thought)
I connected the breather hose to the air cleaner when I repaired
the engine.

On the way to R.I. I started loosing/using oil like crazy !
Remembering that the P.O. had the breather hose sticking up,
I disconnected it from the air cleaner and continued with the
trip. I did not use/lose another drop of oil !

Once in R.I. I stopped at a local FLAPS and bought a length
of american heater hose and connected one end to the breather,
and routed the other end through the foam engine tin gasket pointing
to the ground (figured it was better to vent it outside of the vehicle).

That was 7,000 miles ago and the bus still runs fine,

Good Luck
