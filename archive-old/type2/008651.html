Ok, let's do the math....

Let's say you drive 12,000 miles per year, your normal
fuel-consumption is 15mpg (which is true of my '69 Westy) and gas is
at a constant price of $1.00/gallon. Oil changes at every 3,000 miles.
Also, figure in that "normal oil consumption for a VW", per my owners
manual, is 1.7-4.8 pints per 1,000 miles; we'll use a quart per 1,000
miles for comparison. Again, for the sake of comparison, we'll use
$1.85 per quart of regular oil and $4.75 per quart of synthetic. We'll
also say the fuel savings with synthetic is 1.5 mpg.

Here's how it works out for one yar on regular oil:

Gas: $800.00
Oil changes and consumption: 2.5 qts x 4 oil changes + 12 qts of
consumption = 22 qts total at a cost of $40.70
 Total cost of oil and fuel $840.70

Now for synthetic:

Gas: $720.00
Oil changes/consumption: $104.50
Total cost:$824.50 

Total savings of $16.20, but this is only THEORETICAL. The MPG savings
has yet to be proven and none of this is scientific.

Still, I agree that synthetic oils ARE superior to regular oils, but
that using "facts" that are not followed up with un-biased statistical
data to sell them is the same as "Slick-50"s marketing scam; the facts
simply DO NOT bear out the claims.

I think the better way to market ANY oil or additive is to show
UN-BIASED statistical <empirical> data that proves longer engine life.
I have yet to see that.

One last thing to ponder: The military, contrary to popular belief,
does NOT willingly waste money; they have to fight for every dollar
they get. They have spent literally millions of dollars on testing
oils and additives <if they get even a few 100 miles more out of EVERY
motor, they save many millions of dollars on their budget>. Because of
this testing, they use SOME synthetics, but mostly regular oils. They
also found that when properly filtered, oil can last as long as 50,000
miles with regular filter changes!!!
