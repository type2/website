>try to crank it again, they go back out. & no turn of the starter. Could it

Verify good contact at both ends of the posative and negative battery
cables, then verify good contact at the ground cable from the front of the
tranny to the chassis. If the battery is old and filthy, replace it, or at
least clean it and load test it to confirm function.

Make sure the starter motor bolts are tight as well. Sounds like main
voltage path resistance due to loose and/or coorroded connections. Could as
well be the ignition switch contacts for the same reason, coorroded/pitted
contacts. Verify voltage drop from Batt + to starter solinoid feed from the
switch. If less than 9 volts at the solinoid, it's a switch path problem.
If 9.5v or more, it's a burned/pitted starter. Jump the ignition feed
terminal at the starter to the large Batt + stud with a 10 guage wire to
activate the solinoid. If that don't work, run the jumpwire from the Batt +
post at the battery. If now it works, it's a main cables problem. If that
don't work either, the starter motor ist toast.

responsible for getting the vehicle out of there. No rational person would

to an alternate site, like the new owner's driveway. Then get your $600,

come with old starters and switches and aftermarket relays and such and
