Just brought home from Montana a '75 2.0L RSI pop-top camper.  A trip 
report will soon follow.

Today I got my first whiff of gas blowing through the vents thinking it 
was the "old rotting hose" syndrome passing through the vent lines into 
the overflow traps - wrong - it was a mis-seated FI jet or the 1.5" 
fabic hose on the ring main.  It appears that the metal flange that the 
injector passes through that is bolted to the induction housing is 
rubbing the engine tin - pushing the jet slightly up and out of the 
induction housing.  

In the 400 plus miles from Libby, Montana to Bellevue, Washington I 
didn't smell a thing.  We had the heat blowing through the defroster the 
whole time.  Yet yesterday - take a whiff of Jiff - gas was puddling 
under the #2 jet on the tinware.

I tried lifting the bottom of the flange while tightning the nut to the 
induction housing - no change - it dropped down on to the tinware.  

Would there be any detriment to the flange if I filed it a tad to take 
the miniscule amount off the bottom that is rubbing?  Or, give the 
tinware a tap with the rounded end of a ballpeen hammer?  Is the 
injector flange supposed to rest on the tinware?

The one thing I did do to attempt a fix was to remove the 1.5" of fabric 
hose from the ring main to the injector.  I also removed the jet from 
the flange and pushed the o-ring back into the flange then placed the 
jet back through and into the induction housing.  I clamped the new 
fabric hose on the jet and on the ring main and started it up.  It 
didn't leak but I'm still gun-shy seeing the flange scrating a small 
grove into the tinware.

Any thoughts?
