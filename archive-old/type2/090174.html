> Playing with propane has me concerned.  Is this safe or do I need
> special tools?

Kyle, playing with Propane *should* have you concerned, but that is about
the right level... concerned.  Not frightened, not paniced.  This is dirt
simple if you have ever worked with your brakes, or with a toilet or sink.
The connections are very standard compression fitting and all you need to
do is:

close off the propane (that valve is separate and you will not be
  touching it),
drop the shield and tank onto a low bench/footstool,
disconnect the feeds to the appliance(s) (stove if you have a bay or
  fridge and stove if you have a vanagon),
remove the old regulator from the main valve,
attach the new regulator to the valve,
attach the appliance(s) to the new regulator,
test for leaks with soapy water,
tighten leaky fittings,
test for leaks with soapy water,
reattach tank and shield to bus,
test for leaks with soapy water,
done.
(BTW, you might want to test for leaks with soapy water after a week or
so, just in case :-)

As i remember from when John and I replaced mine, the Vanagon one was a
hair longer than the original and we had to carefully and creatively benfd
the appliance lines to get the tank back on, but it was a simpe
replacement.
