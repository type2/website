Hi Dave,

The experiment was terminated when the engine died. The water  
injection wasn't the culprit, it seems to have been a piston ring  
failure on pistons 1+2. I  will know more about that in a week or so  
when I have time to pull the engine apart.

This particular engine was a VW Canada reman with VERY low miles on  
it that sat for about 18 years before I got it. It was from a 412  
that had been sideswiped and the vehicle was subsequently stored  
indoors until the owners mother passed away 18 years later at which  
time the car was parted out. I have seen documentation that the  
engine was only a week old when the wreck occurred.

I ran it about 500 miles around town and on a weekend trip before  
blessing it as acceptable for the 800-mile excursion to High Country  
Bus Festival this weekend. Unfortunately, it failed about 90 miles  
into the trip; happily enough on my own side of the border and in a  
location where it could be recovered at a reasonable expense. If the  
failure had happened an hour later on the elevated highway through  
Buffalo it would have been dangerous and expensive.

Once I have the engine repaired and back in the bus I plan to  
document 1000 miles of driving without water injection before I  
reinstall the system. This will give a better idea of what is really  
going on with it, both before and after WI.
