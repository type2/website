On Wed, 27 Jun 2007, Robt Mann wrote:

> 	Yes, evaporating some of the water on the surface of the
> suitably porous canvas will cool the water; and that is the primary
> purpose.
> 	However, it is also true that the air flowing past the
> KoolBag. will be cooled.  The latent heat of evaporation of water is
> taken some from the bag but also some from the passing air into which
> the water evaporates.
> 	If that air then goes thru a radiator, all else being equal
> that motor will run cooler.

The amount of water evaporated is the problem.  The systems that spray
water on the RV radiators use the RVs fresh water tank for a supply, and
use several gallons of water per hour when in operation.  The water bag
evaporates its contents much slower so the effect on the temperature of
the air entering the radiator would be tiny, on the order of a fraction
of a degree.
