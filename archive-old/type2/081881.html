> >     With the key "ON",  check the wire to the sending unit with a volt
> > meter, (wire disconnected) you should have 10.0 to 10.5 volts with the
key
> > on. If the voltage is correct, ground the wire and the gauge should read
> > "full".  This will check the wiring, voltage stabilizer, and gauge, if
it
> > shows "full", then only the sending unit is left to be the trouble!
> >
> > VOLTAGE INCORRECT:
> >
> >     If the voltage is "In-Correct", check the voltage Stabilizer behind
> the
> > speedometer, going into the gauge. You should have battery voltage
> available
> > (12.4 volts plus or minus) going in, and 10.5 coming out going into the
> > gauge. If not replace the stabilizer and recheck.
> >
> > VOLTAGE CORRECT:
> >
> >     If the voltage going into the gauge is correct, and the gauge
doesn't
> > read "full", with the wire grounded, replace gauge, and recheck.
> >
> >     Sounds complicated but it isn't. The idea behind all this is, that
if
> > there is not a fixed voltage going into the gauge, it would "swing" as
the
> > available voltage changed. Such as at idle, high RPM's, or when you
turned
> > on a high voltage consumer, headlamps for instance.
