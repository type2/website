Using my fav...Valvoline Moly grease, I would,

A. check the fit of the seals preferably before installing...you did grease
the hub and seal lips right?
B. check the fit of the end cap...they get boogered up by carelessness...a
little brake clean and a bead of silicone upon assembly can accommodate a
bad fit.
C. adjust the bearings correctly...don't cinch down on the nut more than
finger tight...after making sure the bearings are seated, back off and
retighten by hand and you'll be about right. The thrust washer is supposed
to move under gentle nudging. A lot of guys just reef on the adjustment
nut...those poor bearings!
D. Check for brake drag (calipers sticking?) or driver riding the
brakes...slow down and take it easy
