Hi Y'all,
I was on the list briefly back in the early spring & made an inquiry about
a massive oil leak in my orange '73  Westy w/ penthouse pop top, (the
"pumpkin"). I got several responses about the oil gallery seal plugs behind
the flywheel.  I pulled the motor & sho enuff, the sucker was lying there
in the bottom of the bell housing. I drilled, tapped, & installed a plug in
the one that was out, and repeated the process next to the one that was
still in, screwing in a cap screw so that the head overlapped the plug.  It
seemed like a better idea than pulling the remaining plug , drilling &
tapping. (less chance of swarf getting into the motor).
So,  I've been happily driving the 'ole girl all summer.  THANK YOU to all
who responded !!
Now to the next project, rust!, the pumpkin has a softball sized hole in
front of the RR wheel, small (dime sized) holes under the sliding door and
in the same panel on the drivers side. The battery area needs repair, & the
panel under the motor hatch, behind the rear bumper is almost completely
gone. There are also patches of surface rust in various places (rain
gutters, nose panel behind the spare, etc.) 
The structure underneath & windshield area are fine so I think welding in
some body panels & a paint job would give her a new lease on life.
