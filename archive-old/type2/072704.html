79 & up California do have the Lambda closed loop (O2 sensor ) Control
built in to the ECU.  For a good background on this and the O2 sensor I
recommend the following URL's :

http://www.conservatory_dot_com/vw/manuals_nyx/L-JetronicWithLambda.html
http://www.pelicanparts_dot_com/techarticles/mult_air_fuel_monitor/mult_air_fuel
_monitor.htm
http://www.students.tut.fi/~k124775/L.html
http://www.conservatory_dot_com/vw/manuals_nyx/L-Jetronic.html

I welded a 18 mm spark plug anti foul plug onto the elbow of one exhaust
port.  You can buy a Bosche O 2 sensor for under $20 at Auto zone.  I use
mine just to check richness of mixture and tune up.  You will see in the
above references that the O 2 sensor is set to measure what they call
stoich or perfect ( for emmissions) air fuel mixture of 14.7:1.  A chart in
the above also will show you that maximum performance is a little richer
than this _at_ 12.6:1 and best fuel economy at 15.4:1.  In checking the O 2
sensor readings after a emmissions check with idle CO _at_ 1% the multi meter
reading off the O 2 sensor averaged a little higher than stoiche (450 mv)
at approx 550-600mv.  Therefore, I think the spec for the bus is a little
richer than perfect.  I have a altitude switch and circuit on my bus.  This
is to lower the fuel mixture at altitude over what it would be at sea level
(actually it raises richness at sea level to correspond to denser air
volume).  Anyway, I plan to put a parrallel circuit up on the dash to raise
richness and give me extra power for climbing hills at the flip of a
switch.
