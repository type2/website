No, shouldn't be a problem.  The clutch mechanicals
can travel that far.

Frequent clutch cable failures at the clutch actuating
arm could be caused by electrolytic-induced
corrosion...if your tranny-frame grounding strap is
missing, frayed, or corroded the clutch cable might
end up as the next-best conduction path for the ground
to the engine! The resulting current can cause rapid
corrosion.
