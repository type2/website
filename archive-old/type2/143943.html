Subject: Re: [T2] Cooling a Type 1 (1641cc) Engine in a '73 Westy

On Mon, Jun 13, 2005 at 04:43:01PM -0400, kevin wrote:
> I have '73 Westy with a Dual Port 1641cc engine with header exhaust
> without heater boxes and no main engine bay cover tin. I noticed last
> weekend that after 1 hour the engine would not start unless I let the
> engine cool for 10 minutes. I any looking for cheap or free ways to
> correct the overheating problem. Thanks.

does this engine have the late bay tin? or is it simply a std 1641
that has been bolted up to a 73? If you don't have the correct tin,
I'm surprised you can really drive it for an hour without any other
major problems other than hard start. You can try to get some sheet
metal ducting from a local how-to place, and screw them to the engine
bay and tin to try and make up the space. Getting the engine bay area
and outside area seperated goes a long way towards cooling things
down.
