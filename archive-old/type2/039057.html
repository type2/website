Muir needed time to roll his cigarette--it's no longer PC to smoke.

Aside from that, my philosophy is the engine will warm up faster if you
follow the instructions VW gives you--remember, Muir lived in the desert...
An engine will have less carbon build-up if run hotter (but not too hot),
less carbon build up is a good thing.  Carbon build up can lead to sticking
valves, blocked pre-heat tubes, and more crap in your motor oil.

Don't worry about babying your Bus, when you try to drive on the highway
after the breaking in of the motor, you'll find when you're around any hill
steeper than a curb cut that you never have enough power.  There's a
fine line between use and abuse.

Love it, care for it, but drive it.
