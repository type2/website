Another option done around here is to tap a grease nipple into the ball
joint to allow grease that's dried up or is leaking out to be topped up or
replaced.  I think it should be possible to do this on a bus.  I did this
recently on my Hy*ndai P*ny [sorry, sorry...] recently, and while the
problem causing the leaking grease (a small hole in a boot) is still there,
it bought me some time before I have to replace the joints, which is a job
I'd like to put off for as long as possible.

If I can keep good clean grease in the joints, I expect to get several more
years of use out of my existing joints.  And that's good enough for my
"daily" winter driver.

(I did only the lower joints BTW, but it's usually the lowers that wear the
fastest anyway.)
