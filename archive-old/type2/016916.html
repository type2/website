George and David:
Two ways more that seem more likely to me are:
1. Choke stuck on.
2. Failed fuel pump diaphragm.
Having said that, however, I tend to agree with George. To affect lube
quality of the oil, you'd have to be thinned with 1.5-2 Quarts of
gasoline! If, however, your oil level is going UP instead of down, this
is something you should suspect. Oil dilution is a good reason to change
oil more often in winter- the choke's on more and the oil isn't as hot.
Even at 125-150F, though, gasoline evaporates rather quickly.
I'd rate the odds of this causing bearing failure at about 10-15%
likelihood.
