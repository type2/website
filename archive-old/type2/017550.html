Clark:
Sorry, diesel fuel will NOT dissolve varnished gasoline. Lacquer thinner
will, as will a host of other aromatic solvents: probably Methanol, MEK,
Toluol, etc.
Disposal of the dregs is an evironmental nightmare. I'd be tempted to
pour in a couple of gallons of new gas (gasohol would be MUCH better),
maybe a gallon of methanol, and just drive it away! Next best idea is:
remove tank, slosh it around with solvent in it, pour it out on sand or
gravel drive, get the tank a long way away, light it with a match. 
WHOOMP!! (if it's volatile) At least this keeps it out of streams and
rivers. Be real careful around this mix, but at least you'll be
expecting a fire, so it's easier to control its time and location!
By the way, it looks like 75 was the end of the removeable filter. 76
and later are just pickup tubes sweated in.
