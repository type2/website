> system' for type 4 engines
> as unlike the type 1 engine they've been out of
> production for many years.
> 
> How ever I'm sure it would be possible to take a
> system off of a modern 2
> litre light truck and use that with suitable
> adaptors and adjustment.
> 
> The system used by VW on the 1600 could be retro
> fitted to any type 1 1600
> dual port.
> 
> There is a different input manifold with the
> injectors mounted near the
> spark plug area. The throttle body is mounted
> exactly where you would expect
> to see a single carb. In side the throttle body
> there is a very fine wire
> which is heated and the control unit measures the
> change in resistance of
> this wire due to the cooling effect of the air being
> sucked into the engine.
> The air box system used on 70s type 4 attempts to
> set the mixture by volume
> of air - the hot wire system set the mixture by mass
> so makes a much better
> correction for ambient pressure / altitude.
> Final correction of the mixture by a lamda sensor.
> No fifth injector for starting.
> 
> Apart from that the system is pretty much black box
> as the control unit does
> all of the work, firing each injector twice per full
> turn of the cam shaft.
> 
> One of the interesting features is the location of
> the fuel pump - inside of
> the petrol tank! which I understand is not uncommon
> on modern cars. Also the
> tank outlet and return are on the top of the tank
> not the bottom. I think
> this is a good idea as there is no chance of road
> damage to fuel lines. Easy
> to inspect too.
> 
> Due to the lambda sensor and catalytic convertor the
> exhaust is very
> different to an 'old' 1600. J tubes lead to a
> collector (no provision for
> engine supplied heat to the interior - vents not
> even cut out on top of dash
> board) and the lambda sensor is mounted where the 4
> tubes meet. The
> Catalytic convertor is mounted across the van with
> outlet to the left. The
> pipe goes from the cat to an exhaust box outboard of
> the left J tube. Then
> on forward to cross the van in front of the gear box
> returning up the right
> hand side to a second box outboard of the right J
> tube. Then a straight
> tailpipe to the rear.
> 
> I used to have a '73 with a 1600 twin port and used
> to get about 25mpg at 55
> and at first was a little disappointed to 'only' get
> 27mpg as the engine is
> both new and more powerfull. But then I realised
> that one of the prime
> objectives of the FI is emissions control which sets
> a mixture which is
> richer than maximum economy so with 0.00% CO and
> 33ppm HC I suppose I'm not
> doing to badly at 27mpg at 60mph.
>
