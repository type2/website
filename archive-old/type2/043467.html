Subject: Re: Brake servo question

Engine vacuum is the power source for the brake servo.  When the engine is not
running, there is no vacuum, therefore, no power to the servo.  When you apply
the brakes when the engine is not running, you have to overcome the residual
pressure in the servo, as well as the hydraulic pressure in the master
cylinder, so the pedal effort is now considerably higher than when the engine
is running.  It's normal - nothing wrong with your servo.  If the brakes get
hard to apply when the engine is running, that's a different story.
