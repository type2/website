The helpful Mr. Stoermer is on the trail of your problem, Nick. My post sat 
unsent, but expands a bit. Here it is, a bit late but sincere nonetheless:

My 71 won't unlock with the exterior key if the *inside lever* is thrown to 
lock. Likewise, you cannot unlike my door from the inside if the key has 
locked the outside.

Also, if the door is not closed completely (sliding door handle *raised* to 
the click), all bets are also off. I feel sure it is one or a combination of 
these things that has you befuddled.

FWIW: I like a locked sliding door when sleeping, but one through which I can 
escape should need be (visiting hornet or Brown Recluse perhaps). I therefore 
always *unlock* the key, and *lock* the lever from inside. Sometimes I forget 
and get up to corect the situation--which I feel important.

Fortunately, no such problem exists with the rear hatch, which can be unlocked 
from inside regardless of the keyed status. Good for me, bad for transporting 
curious kiddos in my 71 (which has yet to happen).
