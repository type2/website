I have done a lot of sandblasting, it is messy to say the least. No matter
what you do sand will get every where. That being said it is the best way
to remove the nasty scaly stuff. I have used a lot of different mediums for
blasting and have found that for the money you cant beat simple play sand
from Home Depot or whoever. You must make certain the sand is totally dry,
not the least bit damp, and you have to sift it through a fine screen to
get rid of any impurities or big stones. You can use sandbox sand, or
cement mix sand, but you must observe the first 2 rules.
