When I welded in some new metal in place of the rusty battery tray in my
1971 Kombi I managed to squeeze in two 60 Ah batteries on the new battery
tray. They are turned 90 degrees and behind each other, held in place with
a "cargo-tie-down" belt. They are positioned as far up front as possible to
not interfere with the rear lights. Don´t think this is possible with any
bigger batteries.
This leaves free space for my winter and summer equipment: A BN-4  to keep
me warm during the winter and a external oil cooler to keep the engine cool
during the summer :-)
