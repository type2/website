Subject: Freeing up a stuck engine

Yes, the key is time.  A friend who restores old farm tractors has a large vat 
with maybe 10-15 gallons of Diesel fuel in it.  He removes oil pan, valve 
cover, plugs, etc.... then soaks the block with crank, pistons, head for a good 
long while.  He lifts the engine, allows it to drain, then blows it out, then 
tries to turn it a bit..... and repeats the soaking.  His method has the 
advantage of cleaning out oil passages. YMMV.  But basically the important 
thing is not to rush it.  The engine took months/years to get stuck.  Take it 
easy.  Glenn
