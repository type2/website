I've converted two engines to Carbs from FI. Have been very (very) pleased
both times. Neither engine ran when I received the vehicle(s), so I can't
comment on how the FI systems worked pre-conversion. My current 79 Westy has
dual DellOrto's and it runs very strong. I'm a little frightened that I
can't remember what size those carbs are, but I've changed venturi sizes
several times anyway to get power characteristics that I like (strong low
and mid range at the cost of top end). 

I really encourage anyone considering this route to go for it. My dual carbs
are a breeze to set-up and maintain (get the good UniSyn tool), the look
cool, and I just like Italian pieces. Don't be frightened by the
synchronization prospect. It is very straightforward and once set, doesn't
get out of whack easily. There is an _excellent_ article on (I think)
aircooled_dot_net describing the procees. A link to it was posted here several
days ago. I've been synching my carbs for probably 10 years, and I learned
from the article.

As always, there are certainly many widely varying opinions and experiences
relative to a carb conversion, mine is just one.
