Back in the early 70's, as part of the Calif smog program, we had to either disconnect the vacuum advance or
install a more expensive device on that would regulate the vacuum advance.*

This was on 66 to 70 cars. Most 1970 GM cars were exempt because they already had TCS. TCS means Transmission
Controlled Spark. Vacuum advance was shutoff until the trans got into the highest gear, 3rd or 4th depending
on the trans. The cheap kit that got you by because of the $40 max you legally pay consisted of 2 rubber caps
and a sticker to put on the speedo! The high dollar unit was made by Pure Power and it was rpm controlled to
cut off vacuum.
If you find a 66-70 Calif car with its original windshield, it may still have the blue [for Passed] or red
[for Exempt] NOx sticker on the bottom right corner.
The * was for VW's. Since they ran vacuum only distributors, you cannot cancel vacuum advance.
[Although I did see alot that were done by incompetent smog techs!]
For VW's, you had to install an EGR valve. You had to drill a hole in the heat risor, screw in a pipe, screw
on a valve, r&r the carb, put in 2 longer studs, install a spacer plate, and then a hose between the plate and
the valve. The valve was tee'd into the vac advance hose. This was called an STP device...... Made by Grancor.
[for 10 bonus points, What name is associated with STP? If you guessed Andy Granatelli...... ding, you win!
Does the company name now make sense? Granatelli Corporation]  [I have person to person experience with that
short fat greaseball, but that is for another day....]
