For a late bay the easier way if removing engine only is to support the
engine on one jack and the tranny on another. Unbolt the rear engine
carrier then reach up over the top of the tranny and remove the two bolts
that hold it to the rear tranny support.

Lower the engine and tranny together and you get a few extra inches that
will keep the oil filler from snagging when you unbolt the engine from the
tranny and slide it back. 

If you need to pull BOTH the engine and tranny then do them at the same
time. Make sure you have EVERYTHING disconnected as Jason stated, including
ground strap, shift coupler, clutch cable, and the CV's. You only need to
disconnect the CV's from the tranny side. Use wire to hang them up out of
your way. With engine and tranny supported level undo the rear engine
carrier, the tranny support bolts above where it mates to the engine, and
the front tranny support holder bolts. Then you can lower the whole unit
straight down. If you have both jacks lined up and the bus high enough you
can roll them out together. If not you can unbolt them now that they're
down and drag them each out on their separate jack.

I plan to install the engine/tranny as a single unit in my Rivi some time
this week.
