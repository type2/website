Subject: Re: [T2] Engine rise on breaking...

You might have a failing brake booster and/or master cylinder.  
The booster works by "storing" vacuum inside the canister.  When 
you press the brake pedal DOWN the stored vacuum helps you apply 
the brakes.  When the pedal comes up, the vacuum is replenished 
via the intake manifold.  That is a simplification, but basically 
how it works.

So, you should really notice a drop in rpm only when you release 
the brakes and allow the pedal to rise, not when you press it down 
although if the vacuum in the canister is depleted you may notice 
it when pushing the pedal down also.  Hope that makes sense.
