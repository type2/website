The only catch is that gas can still flow through a dormant fuel pump thanks 
to gravity. Thankfully I found this out without a fire. 
Shortly after I joined this list I was inspired to change the fuel lines that 
had been in my bus for at least the 5 years I had owned it at the time. And 
yes they were ready for replacement! At the time I was running one of those 
noisy square fuel pumps installed by PO. Since the ignition and engine were 
off I didn't think I needed to worry about gas leaking from the line 
supplying the carbs. To my surprise, gas flowed as if I'd opened a drain 
valve, forcing me to scramble for something to plug the line. 
With a broken fuel line or a loose carb fitting, fuel would still feed the 
fire after the engine and fuel pump had shut down. 
A solenoid such as Richard Beckers mentioned could be wired with a relay. 
Perhaps both solenoid and and electric fuel pump (where applicable) could be 
powered by the same relay.

With FI, I wonder if you would be able to start the bus without the fuel pump 
running.

As far as near-catastrophes wrt to leaking carbs. BTDT twice!! Each time it 
was the little brass nipple on the left hand baby Dell. Yes, I plan to use a 
couple feet of safety wire on that junction when the engine goes into the new 
bus.
