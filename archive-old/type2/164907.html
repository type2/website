Subject: RE: [T2] Flux Core Welding Problems

All I know is that when you weld galvanized, it feels like you are a 70
year-old chain smoker with a hang-over trying to run down a cab. Besides, it
welds like crap. The intense zinc burn-off contaminates your "inert" gas
area thus rendering the weld poor at best. It also has a tendancy to kick up
so much spatter that it can clog your nozzle and pit your welding surface.
Flux-core welding on regular steel is crappy at best; welding galvanized
with a flux-core is almost impossible.

 

Zinc burn-off may be "inert", but if it costs you a weekend in bed away from
your family, then that is as toxic as a getting the flu.
