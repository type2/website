My 71 Westy sometimes stumbles on acceleration, and I sometimes get small
backfires on deceleration.  After 16 or so years of ownership, I attribute this
to the quirks of the 34mm carburetor.  I.e., I think that the accelerator pump
is not calibrated quite right, so, under hard accleration in too low a gear, the
engine stumbles a bit until the vacuum builds up at higher revs.  The
deceleration backfiring I attribute to too rich of a mixture under that
condition.  With a muffler in good condition, you hardly hear it anyway, so I
fix it by turning up the volume on the radio.

I have run a two outlet bug muffler for all of the time I've owned the bus -
works fine - easier to get the part, cheaper, don't have to fool with tailpipe
to body mounts when pulling the engine, etc.
