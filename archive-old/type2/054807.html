Parker,
Yes, '78 and '79 Buses were fitted with heat exchanger exhaust pipes (good
name).  They're really flapper box exhaust pipes.  They were simply pipes
that screwed to the frame outboard of the heat exchangers.  Flexible tubing
ran up to the heater flapper boxes, and hot air from the flapper boxes,
which in previous years had been vented right from the flapper boxes, now is
sent through these pipes and exits at the rear of the Bus.

I would doubt that you need them as you'll notice '78 and '79 are the only
years to have them.  With the start of production of the Vanagon, these
pipes were eliminated even though the same engine was used.  The Vanagon
goes back to dumping that hot air right out at the flapper box.

My '79 still has the pipes and I'm not going to remove them but I think
you'll be OK without them.
