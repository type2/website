> >measure the end play without a dial gauge?
>
> Yes, there is. You can use any flat metal stock, a bolt, 3 nuts, a few
> washers and a feeler gauge to make an endplay measuring tool.
>
> What you do is attach the flat metal stock to the bolt securely; then
> attach the bolt to one of the engine-to-tranny mounting points; position
> the metal over the flywheel (close but not touching); then measure the "in
> distance" and the "out distance" of the flywheel to the metal bar. Use
> basic math (subtraction) to calculate the difference and you have end
play.
>
> The trick is to get the right size metal stock (length-wise and wide
enough
> for your bolt), make sure it's straight, and make it mountable to the bolt
> you use (e.g., drill a straight hole in the metal bar stock if you have
to).
>
> You're only talking about a few thousanths of an inch when you're looking
> at end play. It's likely not going to be perceptable if you're *looking*
> for movement. You can *feel* the movement of end play, but it's not enough
> to really *see* it. You really should measure it, though. Someone may have
> a drawing of the end play tool. I want to say I think there might be one
in
> Muir...but I might be wrong about that too.
>
> Good luck! Joe
