It could be the automatic choke. Happened to me, I was getting gas in my oil
big time. Turns out my auto choke element wasn't working and gas was
bypassing the rings into the bottom end. Needless to say, this is pretty bad
for an engine.

Warm up the engine. Then, being very carefull of the spinning
fanbelt/pulleys, feel the auto choke element. Is it hot? If not, make sure
12 volts of power is being delivered to it from the + side of the coil.
After 3 minutes or so, the choke must be fully open-verify this.
I also agree with your idea about the fuel pump- good idea to replace or
rebuild these things regularly.

btw- change that oil soonest.
