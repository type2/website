At 09:03 AM 1/24/01 -0800, George Lyle wrote:

>I'm glad that you saw an improvement.  The question is, what caused the
>improvement?  Few people do this sort of conversion start with a fresh,
>well-adjusted stock ignition.  I'd venture that a fresh plugs would result
>in pretty much the gains you cite over worn plugs.

George, based on what I've seen at the dyno, I would respectfully disagree with you.  I have run old, worn plugs and seen no increase in hp on the dyno by changing to new ones.  I'll admit that I had changed some other things at the same time, but I think it is highly unlikely that the other changes negated any increased hp from the new plugs.

>Also, was it the Pertronics, the CDI, or the plugs that resulted in the
>gains cited?  It's hard to figure out what is going on when you change
>more than one thing at a time.

My guess is that the CDI produced the increase in torque and hp.  I had a friend dyno his Mustang before and after a CDI system and he picked up 3-4hp also.  No, it's not a bus but is a related piece of hard evidence.

>> Was it worth it? To me, hell yes. No tune ups. Change the 
>> plugs every 60k miles or so. Never check the advance again. No hassle starts 
>> at -10f. More Torque, no points bounce, mis-fires or weirdness associated 
>> with a Kettering Ignition System.
>
>I must be charmed.  I've never experienced those symptoms with points.

My plugs do not wear quickly with points.  In fact, I would expect them to wear faster with a CDI system since there is more energy going through the plug.  I have however had minor issues with points - mainly slowly changing dwell which must be adjusted out periodically.

>The electronic systems are clearly superior in performance, but the points
