You wrote:

One popular cause of thin watery oil is that fuel pump bottom diaphragm.
Leaks there cause fuel to dribble into the crankcase, thinning the oil.
Not good.  This sort of problem is more common than many people think.
Obviously, if the oil level mysteriously rises, the pump is suspect.   Such
thinned oil will usualy smell like gasoline, and seriously thinned oil will
stink up the car.  Been there done that.   

Of course, sometimes overheated multigrade oil will thin out, depending on
brand... some of the older multigrades didn't like high temps at all.
Word has it that some of the cheaper oils today (house brand stuff seen on
the shelves of "Cost-Cutter" etc) aren't quite up to the standards of name
brand oil.   But I'm not really an authority on oils... I just watch.
