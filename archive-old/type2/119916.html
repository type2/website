Hi Mick,

Sounds like your discovering why the stud broke...  If the stud/bolt is
siezed, getting it out is going to be a real pain.  In my experience,
the easiest approach is to apply heat to help break whatever corrosion
bond is holding the bolt into the casting.  If the situation is
"unheatable" then the next best thing is to drill out almost all the
stud/bolt and hope that by reducing it to a shadow of it's former self I
can work it out.  When that fails, I just drill the whole thing and use
one of the various rethreading techniques (tap, helicoil, threadsert,
etc.)  As far as the use of the screw extractor is concerned, I tap it
gently into the hole to help the teeth bite with a small hammer and then
use the appropriately sized wrench/visegrip to turn it with liberal
applications and waiting times of PB Blaster.  If you've been chucking
the actual screw extractor in your drill and spinning it (I assume in
reverse) I would think the teeth on the extractor are pretty much gone.

Hope that helps,
Bill
