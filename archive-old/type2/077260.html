On Sun, 11 Nov 2001, Isaiah Smith wrote:

> In the air tube from the AFM to the air plenum I
> found lots of oil in this tube.  I'm assuming it got there from the
> old bus engine swirl(you know the one that leaves oil on the back
> hatch) and sucked through the AFM.

Nope.  Probably not.  The crankcase ventilation hose feeds directly into
that tube.  It's the hose that runs to the box in the rear center of the
engine.  If you are getting substantial blow-by thru the rings/valves,
that feeds a lot of oil up into the intake stream.  I see that tube really
oily all the time, so I would not be *really* concerned, but it may be
time to take the engine to a mechanic who can do a "leak down" test on it
once you have it back together.  This will tell you the condition of your
rings.

> I haven't gotten into a dissection of the AFM yet, but was wondering
> if it could be cleaned of oily deposits if any are found and what
> solvent would be best for this application.  Also, the plenium will
> probably have its fair share of oil.  Would this affect opperation??
> Any thoughts or suggestions would be welcome.

Well, the AFM is probably fine, unless you have been doing a lot of back
firing.  The AFM is upstream of the injection point for that oily gunk.
If you do have a lot of oil in the AFM, that's a separate problem.  If it
were me (and I've never done it, but what I would do...) I would use WD-40
as a cleaner.  It's kerosene by and large, and should not hurt the
electrical parts while loosening the oil.
