>Motor starts and runs fine cold, putting a whole lot of moisture out the

>moves the fast idle cam progressively lower as it warms up in proper
>fashion. Idle is maintained properly until the last one or two notches in

What is the ambient air temperature?  Is the manifold preheat system
working properly?  My bus exhibits these symptoms occasionally in the
winter (below 40 degrees F ambient).  My thought is that the high vacuum in
the manifold at fast idle may cause some icing.  One clue would be the
behavior after the vehicle is warmed up say, another 5 minutes - after the
engine heat has had time to soak up through the manifold.  Does the problem
go away?  Or does it die forever thereafter?
