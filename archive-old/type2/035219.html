I can't comment on Stephens, whom I don't deal with, having been scared off
by their core policies (see the archives), an issue that I have _never_ run
into with AVP.  If there has been a failure of an AVP head, I have not heard
of it (in that I sell them and have never had a single warranty claim on
one, nor have I had anyone who purchased one from me later complain about an
out-of-warranty failure).  Not that I'm saying they're perfect, but I have
been sufficiently impressed that theirs is the only '72-83 head I'll sell.
If their track record is that good (at least with my customers), then I
wonder how much additional benefit could be obtained from buying a new,
possibly-flawed head (at high cost) and then sending it out for new valve
seats (at even higher cost)?  Maybe it's worth it if price is no object. But
I'll say this, when I needed a new engine for my wasserboxer Vanagon, I
weighed the options carefully (including VW factory engines, still available
for that year) but bought mine from AVP.
