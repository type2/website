Don, I set them full-soft.

By way of background, I have a few years' experience with Koni red
adjustables.  I run them on my Audi 100CSq and my 5000Sq before it.  In my
100, they were full-hard but I backed off to 1/2 way and, while that is a
fun setting on those rare times I can open it up on a twisty road, it is
still too harsh to drink coffee without spilling it during bumps.  Coffee
everywhere! On my 5000, I ran them full-hard (with Nuespeed lowering
springs) and liked that setting at the time.  But with the disintegrating
roads in the mid-Atlantic corridor, and the subsequent construction, those
harsher settings just aren't worth it.

I wondered about the Hankooks, too.  I think it can be explained by their
stiffer sidewalls.  The Firestones, while supportive of the Bus's weight,
were much taller and softer than the Hankooks.  I imagine the combination of
tall, soft sidewall and firm shocks roughly equals less-tall, stiffer
sidewalls and softer shocks.  I had no fear of zesty cornering in the
KYB/Firestone setup.  With the KYB/Hankook mix, it was frightening.  (I
should note that I have 7/8" front and rear sway bars installed.)  The
Koni/Hankook combination seems stable enough but I need to put a few
thousand miles on them to tell for sure.

Joe
