Sorry, Ron...

But...


I feel the change to a good gas-shock (like the KYB
Gas-adjust) is a *very* wise choice.

My '69 Westy had O.E.M. (I.E., dealer installed)
shocks when I bought it. They were 5 years old, but
still working properly at the time. I had some extra
cash at the time and chose to follow other listee's
advice and try the KYB Gas-adjusts.

I'm glad I did.

I realize that shocks alone *should* not account for
too much of a difference in handling; but they did.
EVERY aspect of my bus's handling has improved;
cornering, wind (ok, not much, but with a bus...every
little bit counts), nose-dive when under hard-braking
(BIGGGGG PLUS, here!).... 

It's ALL better....*much* better and it only cost me
$24.00 per shock, with a lifetime (pro-rated, of
course) warranty, to boot. WELL worth the extra $28.00
(but no shipping;-) to do the whole bus! 

Now, if these were the ol' green Bilsteins at this
price.... 8-)


Sorry, Ron....
