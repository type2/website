A very important point to remember when diagnosing a bad cam:  If you have a 
very flat lobe on the cam, TWO valves will be affected!  Think about it.  There 
are 8 valves in your engine, right?  4 intakes and 4 exhausts.  But there are 
only FOUR lobes on the cam.  Each lobe drives a pair of valves - one on each 
side of the engine.  So if you have a bad cam, 1 valve on each opposing side of 
the engine will be affected.

I thought I had a bad cam on a type IV motor for weeks.  Turned out to be a 
severe leak at the intake manifold on one side.
