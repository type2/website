On Thu, 19 Sep 2002, Ken Hooper wrote:
> The mugs do seem quite benign because they are so cartoonish and
> homemade-looking, and because they are one-offs. And IANAL*, however, I
> don't think the guy has a prayer. There are VW marks all over these
> mugs, the Federal Trademark Dilution Act works against him, and there
> just isn't any fair use provision or First Amendment protection for this
> kind of stuff.

Well, since the art is a custom depiction of an actual object (bus) that
contains the logos involved, it should be considered a work of art.  After
all, if a newspaper prints a picture of a VW that happens to contain
their circle trademark they don't threaten to sue the newspaper.

OTOH, the web page itself has many references to VWs.  It would be better
to get rid of those references and expand the concept to custom depictions
of any vehicle on a mug.

Since VW is trying to market a new microbus, I wonder if they have
trademarked the "double-curve" front of the splitty bus.  Jeep trademarked
their 7-slot grille in a similar way, though I notice that the Hummer H2
pushes the limit on that account.  It might well become legally dangerous
to take a photograph of any bus.

> If the artwork on the mugs were obviously a parody or satire of some

Problem is, the cost of defense would break any ordinary individual.
