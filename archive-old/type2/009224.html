Magnets are efficient collectors of ferrous particles, so they do
provide a filtering function of sorts.  They are commonly used in
drain plugs of transmissions, which have a lot more steel-on-steel
running parts than engines.

Cow magnets do exactly that, collecting ingested metallic particles
>from the digestive system of cows.

As far as VW engines go, I really don't know where you would put a cow
magnet.  You'd be better off with a "donut" shaped magnet from a old
speaker, which you could stick onto the sump plate.  I don't know if
this would help, since most metallic debris in an engine is
non-ferrous, but it probably wouldn't hurt anything.

For heaven's sake, though, don't remove a magnet after you have
applied it unless you are draining the oil, as you might release a
gout of ferrous particles into the oil system all at once!
