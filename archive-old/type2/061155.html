  You want to save the sandblasting for the panels that are all ready warped
--dents and dings.  Sandblasting is okay as long as your going to do repair
work in that area.  But for non-dented areas that are nice and straight
plastic media blasting is the way to go.  The plastic doesn't remove metal and
create heat like sandblasting.  
   I question everybody's desire to have there buses down to bare metal.  At
work (I'm a bodyman) we do everything we can to save as much of the factory
corrosion protection as possible.  Even in the 1990's we still can't duplicate
the factory corrosion protection of a 30 year old VW.  We can come close, but
nothing beats the factory stuff.  I myself prefer to sandblast damaged areas,
then Variprime them and hit the rest of the sound paint with a DA (Dual Action
Sander).   Prime the bus with a good sandable epoxy primer, guide coat it and
then countless hours of blocking it out (this is where you and your bus really
start to bond--think happy thoughts)
