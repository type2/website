TSP is just a set of initials that can represent many different
combinations of words.  It is commonly used as a short way of saying
Tri-Sodium Phosphate, which used to be a very commonly-used cleaner.

Years ago, some sharpie figured out that he could simply use "TSP" as
a trademark, much as "STP" (hmm, same letters) "WD-40" and "VW" are
trademarks.  There's no need for him to put any specific ingredient
into his product, as the "TSP" doesn't really specify anything.  When
the ecologists restricted the use of phosphate-containing cleaners,
(phosphates are fertilizers that supposedly caused excessive growth of
water plants if the phosphate found its way into a
river/lake/whatever) the brand name "TSP" went to the phosphate-free
formula.  

The irony is that the product prominently labeled "TSP" isn't really
what you want.  You will probably find real tri-sodium phosphate on a
nearby shelf, usually in a plainer white bag or box.  It'll be quite a
bit cheaper, too!
