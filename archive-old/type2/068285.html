> The blue one controls the two 'fresh-air flaps' in the front.  Up is

Emily is right about the blue lever.  For the red levers, the right one
controls how much heat gets into the car by pulling a cable attached to
the heater flapper boxes at the rear of the car.  Down is heat on and
when the lever is all the way down, the blower fan in the engine
compartment is supposed to come on.  The left lever controls which vents
the heat comes out of.  If the lever is all the way up, all the floor
vents get heat, that's the two up front, the one at the back of the
walk-through, and the one under the middle seat.  If the lever is in the
middle, only the two footwell vents in the cab get heat.  If the lever
is all the way down, none of the footwell outlets get heat and all hot
air is routed to the defroster vents on the dash.
