>I have never tried POR-15, but anything that seals the metal from air will
>prevent rust. By definition, rust is oxide of iron. No oxygen, no rust. I
>have gotten excellent results from carefull preparation and good old
>Rustoleum or Krylon. The tin on my Porsche looks magnificient with the wipe
>of a rag after ten years. People walk up and look at the motor and
>typically go, "WOW, you really keep it clean!!!" Yeah, five minutes with a
>rag every year or 2000 miles. Simply maintained, perpetually, indefinatly,
>ad infinitum.
>
>I have not heard of Fertan, but what is typically available at any marine
>store around here is the similar rust conversion chemical called Ospho.
>Stuff works wonders, and it will seep into seams, too. Should at least slow
>down deterioration in places you can't get to. Be sure to let it dry, bone
>dry, before final prep and overcoating. Use a good hot air gun to speed up
>the process but be very carefull not to get things too hot. Just a big fan
>strategically located to move the air around will dry things in a coupla
>hours as good as overnight without.
>
>And as always, several medium-light coats properly spaced will prevent any
>runs or wrinkling. When all else fails, read the instructions. Or, read the
>instructions before you buy, and reduce the risk of failure (Murphy) to nada.
