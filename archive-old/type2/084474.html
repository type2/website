> On Thu, 4 Apr 2002, Chuck Harvey wrote:

>>  Need some insight on how to wire the lights of a 79 loaf to be towed.
>
>>   Where do I hook into the running lights, brake lights and turn
>>   signals?

> Chuck,
>
> First, the rental set from whatever company rents you the dolly is just
> *easier*, as it will plug right in and clamp on.   The two times I have
> towed cars for any distance, that was the way I went.
>
> But, second, if you want to use the vehicle lights you can either run a
> harness along the side of the bus and plug them in at the tailight
> buckets or do as follows for direct connections to the systems:
>
> driving lights - just turn them on;
>
> brake lights   - there is no fuse between the switches on the master
>                 cylinder and the lights.  You would need to unplug the
>                 wires from the switches and plug in your feed from the
>                 towing vehicle.  The wiring diagram indicates an eight-
>                 connector multi-plug between the switch and the lights
>                 in the rear, but I don't remember where that connector
>                 is located.
>
> turn signals   - the easiest place to feed power to the turn
>                 signals is at the emergency flasher switch.  Look for a
>                 black wire with a green trace (right) and a black wire
>                 with a white trace (left).
