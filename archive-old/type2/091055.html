Jaques,

What you are contemplating is certainly possible, but when the rear
torsion
tube is bent, you are at a major decision point. In my book, your
insurance company is correct... your bus died.  With enough money,
however, resurrection is possible for a car.  Certainly the "restorations"
of some early Rolls Royces prove this!  Start with an original gas pedal
and build a car around it, basically :-)

I would really prefer if Kelly would weigh in here (and I actually called
to ensure that :-), but slicing out the rear housing and installing a new
one is a *MAJOR* project.  First, it is a big sub-assembly, second,
alignment is *critical* and third, your life depends on the welds not
cracking.  All three of these militate toward getting a real pro to tackle
the job.  If i had a bus that I was so sentimetally attached to or if I
had a bus that was so valuable that I would attempt this, I would look for
someone like Kelly, who has *years* of experience and the tools to attempt
it.
