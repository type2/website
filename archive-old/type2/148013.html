On Aug 18, 2005, at 8:59 AM, Darren Evans-Young wrote:

> Cold engine. Takes 5-10 seconds for oil light to go out
> and gauge to register pressure.

Usually it goes out right away: 1-2 seconds. I've found that with  
hydraulic lifters the light will stay on longer (5 sec) if the  
lifters don't pump up immediately.

> Oil pressure goes to 60psi at idle.
> Pressure stays 55-60 psi at speed and at idle as long as oil
> temp is < 180F.

Seems normal.

> As the temp approaches 180F, the idle pressure starts
> to come down. Once engine is warm, oil pressure at idle is 15-20psi.

OK.

> Does the 2.0L engine have 2 oil pressure valves?
> If it has two, I know one is to prevent excess pressure from going
> to the cooler and the other is to bypass the cooler when the oil
> is not hot.

The second valve limits overall oil pressure. The solid lifter  
engines have two, the hydraulic lifters engines have one.
