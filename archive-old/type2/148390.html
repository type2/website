Subject: Re: [T2] '72 Vacuum readings

On Sep 8, 2005, at 4:42 AM, J Barry Burneskis wrote:

> This is a '72 dual PDSITs. It does not have the carburetor  
> 'crossover pipe' that the '73 & '74 has, so I cannot take the  
> manifold vacuum reading from the middle of the pipe that run  
> between the carbs.

Take the reading from the left carb where the distributor picks up  
it's vacuum retard signal.

> The left and right carb manifolds supply the brake booster with  
> vacuum [so to speak]. At idle, what manifold vacuum should I be  
> reading off the balance pipe at bus-boy's    item  F. 021-129-637D  
> Elbow-Manifold Balance Pipe Bus/PU 1700 Right Side 1972 Only
>
> in this diagram http://www.bus-boys_dot_com/carb1.html

I'm not sure how the reading is going to differ at that location as  
I've never measured it there.

> And a second question, again at idle, what vacuum readings should I  
> expect to see at the left carb for advance [throttle vacuum] and  
> retard [manifold vacuum].

This depends on your distributor. If it's stock at you have vacuum  
retard hooked up you should see 9-10" of manifold vacuum at 5 ATDC  
and no ported vacuum signal. If you have the engine timed to 7.5 BTDC  
with another distributor you should see 15" of manifold vacuum or  
more. That would indicate an engine that is breathing correctly.
