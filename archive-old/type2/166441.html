This is where it goes:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/MoreParts3/071- 
FrontBushing.jpg

First, disconnect the front and rear rod at the coupler by the master  
cylinder:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/Engine3/41-ShiftRodCoupler.jpg

Then lube it up, fit it in the hole and slide the front shift rod  
through:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/MoreParts3/072-MuchBetter.jpg

> I know that the two bushings that look like shuttlecocks are for

> rod, but there are also like 3 or 4 more pieces that I am not sure of.

The kits includes a plastic part #19 for 68-73 models seen in this  
diagram:

http://www.ratwell_dot_com/technical/Microfiche/t205700.gif

The reproduction plastic bushings do not fit as well as the  
originals. I found the metal ring they give you for the front bushing  
is the wrong size.

Front style (w/ metal ring):

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/Engine3/49- 
FrontBushingBoot.jpg

Rear style:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/Engine3/48-MiddleBushing.jpg

> If there is a good illustration that someone could turn me on to  
> for this,
> that would be wonderful as the Bentley diagram sucks.

This is what it looks like assembled:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/Engine3/44-RearShiftRod.jpg

A 3rd middle bushing was added to 77 models at VIN 217 2 053 839.  
There is a tech bulletin that shows how and where to modify the  
earlier rods to improve shifting/support if you want to do this (not  
a big deal).

I don't know of an easy way to get the rear bushing on without having  
the engine out to pull the rear shift rod out.
