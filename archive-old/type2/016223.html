Sigh... my previous post (T does not mean Type) and its use of 
"flat-four" will further confuse everyone in this thread I fear.  So 
allow me to clarify:

A Type 1 engine comes either as an "upright", which means that its 
blower housing is located above the engine, or a "flat", with its 
blower behind the engine block (RIR).  The former was used in the 
Type 1 (Bug et.al.), Type 2/T1 (Splitty) and Type 2/T2 (Bay).  The 
latter was used in the Type 3, and something similar in the Type 2/T3 
(Vanagon) in Europe, which I believe is what Andrew might have if 
it's in a Vanagon.

Type 4 engines, or "pancakes", always are "flat" in this sense, i.e. 
with their blower behind the engine block (unless custom-converted to 
upright blowers for use in a Bug or such).

But confusion arises from the English (as a language) use of "flat" 
to describe a boxer engine, where the cylinders indeed lie "flat" 
instead of "standing (more or less) upright" like they do in other 
engine types.  This is the sense in which I used "flat-four" in my 
previous posting, and which Andrew might have meant if he has a Bay 
or a Splitty.

Sorry for the confusion, and about the bandwidth.
