I am not a chemist.  Neither have I been involved in the conversion of any
engine to any alternative fuel.  I have been curious about it but it never
seemed that there would be much ultimate benefit.

The heat content of propane is about 92K btu/gal.  The heat content of
gasoline is 124K btu/gal.  As a result you either have to provide ~26%
more air/fuel mix or lose ~26% of your power.  If displacement remains the
same, then power loss is what happens.  Add a blower and you can come back
up but that is an even larger can of worms. It could be worse.  Convert to
methanol with a heat content of 65K btu/lb and you lose about 50% of your
power.

You make the cost of energy loss back on cost though.  Propane is nearly
1/3rd cheaper than gasoline so your cost per mile would be lower.  You do
get into a rats-nest of paperwork on road taxes which eats into the
savings.

Source: http://www.ext.nodak_dot_edu/extpubs/ageng/structu/ae1015a.pdf
