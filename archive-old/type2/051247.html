To me it sounds like the cylinder head temp sensor.  Once it dies after
starting to warm up simply connect a wire from the sensor connector
to ground, this will simulate a warm head signal, and try starting it up
again.  If it works, go get a new cylinder head temp sensor.  It's about
the only thing that changes as the engine warms up - the signal from the
temp sensor in the air flow meter should not change as the engine
warms up.

Good luck
Christopher

> It's probably the cylinder head temperature sensor.  It is located on the
> drivers side head (#3 cylinder) and has a white wire that runs to it from
> the main fuel injection wiring harness.  Wire may be loose, but if the bus
> runs fine until it warms up the sensor is probably bad and needs replaced.
> Fairly common preventive maintenance part.  It screws in and out much like
a
> small spark plug with a wire attached to the top of it. About $15 at the
> local import parts store or online retailer.  If this doesn't work the air
> flow meter also has a temp sensor built into it.  Try the cylinder head
temp
> sensor and let the list know if that fixes it.  Good luck.
