> Jamie,

> I have to admit that I did not look at your prototype pics. I have one
> concern with the way your hitch is mounted. The OEM hitches used the
> threads in the bottom of the frame rails (the bumper support mounts) and
> the mounts in the sides of the frame rails (where your hitch is mounted)
> were used in addition to the bottom mounts for high capacity hitches (75
> instead of 50 kg tongue weight and more than 1200 kg towing capacity).
> It may be worth contemplating to weld an additional angle iron to your
> hitch that would allow you to utilizes the bottom mounts. In addition to
> the extra mounting points the angle pieces would prevent the hitch from
> pivoting around the side bolts. With your current design the bolts in
> the sides of the frame rails have to carry all the weight and keep the
> hitch from pivoting - lots of stress there. I had a couple of these
> welded nuts brake loose on my hitch, but the design of the stock hitch
> helped to prevent a major disaster. Had this happened with your design,
> I would have screwed up one framerail end, my muffler, and the trailer
> at the least ...
