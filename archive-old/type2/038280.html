>>Just an idea:  unscrew the idle jet (it's midway down the carb throat on
>the
>>right hand side, has a nice big brass head on it pointing directly to the
>>right (toward the air cleaner),
>
>This is an Idle air bypass screw, it allows air to pass for Idle, if its
>clogged, no air will pass.
>>
>>  Unscrew it, hand onto it as it leaves the carb casting,
>>take it out and vigorously blow it clean.  Once it looks spotless, put it
>>back in nice and snug.
>
>Don't put it in snug! That will close off the passage completely, it needs
>to be backed out a turn or two. These were set at the factory and  is said
>to never need adjustment, but on a 20-30 year old car these have been
played
>with 100 times. There is lots of info on these carbs in the library. Look
it
>up and fix it right. Get the Bentley out, may be time for a carb rebuild.
>
>You need to check for vacuum leaks. Some people like to use WD-40 for this.
>Other tings work, just watch out what you use as it will be flamable. Spray
>it around all intake track brakes(carb base, intake boots, gaskets, hoses,
>carb body) If the RPMs rise, you found the leak. Check the carb to see if
>its tight, they like to work loose. If the carb dirty and been a long time
>from last rebuild it may be time. Could have dirt in the Idle jet or idle
>passages.
