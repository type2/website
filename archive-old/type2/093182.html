question was "worth" which implies at least considering mainly the money 
involved.... and lots of other things like the value of a smile, 
etc.   This may need some tighter boundries.

Then there were variables like "how safe do you want it to be?"  When it 
comes to brakes.......I'd agree that $800 is high.....representing a Midas 
lifetime brake job.   I'll back that down to $500.  There.....shaved $300 
right off the bat. Or, do it yourself and save about $250 more. How about 
using $250? Better? I'll take a 'yes' on that one.

And, maybe they could get away with not replacing the rotten front axle and 
steering box. Maybe that one doesn't need another front axle from Texas. 
But mine did. Personally, I'd rather make that choice on the side of 
safety. In fact, I did. And it cost about a grand before it was all over. I 
hope they look at that axle and steering box.

I guess they could rebuild the engine/engines  themselves. That might shave 
another thousand.
Or, they could ask RAT to put in something even more expensive.....up 
around $5000. There is considerable latitude there.  I did offer an option 
of $2,000.

In defense of the $10,000 total, I've seen at least 3 buses in the past few 
years stickered at $10,000. They looked _better_ than mine looked when it 
was new, in 1976. And my figures didn't include new pop-top canvas, or 
redoing all the fabrics and rubber and inside panels. Now the total 
estimate starts moving a tad toward the higher figure.

But I'm allowed to have those opinions, high, low, right, wrong, debatable. 
But am I allowed to express those opinions on this list? Probably should 
exercise a little judgement there.
