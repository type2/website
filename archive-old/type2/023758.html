Shawn,
	You shouldn't need to redrill the hubs. The old studs will tap
out and new ones tap in. It doesn't even take that long.

 You can do this yourself, with a little care. Put a large socket 
(or similar) under the hub around the head of the old stud to
distribute the load and avoid warping, thread a wheelnut (preferably
an old one!) onto the end of the stud to protect the threads and then
tap it out with a big hammer.
 The new studs should tap back in in the same sort of way. I had to
do a little file work on a few that were a smidgeon oversize.

 Once you've got everything back together make sure you check 
and tighten your wheelnuts very frequently in the first few hundred
miles, as the new studs may take a little time to settle on their
final home.

 One final detail- the studs I bought have a longer unthreaded part
than the stock ones, so on the rear wheels I need to use a thin spacer
(2-3mm) with the stock wheels (ie my spare!!) or the nuts won't seat
onto the rim properly.
