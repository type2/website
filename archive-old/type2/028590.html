Rick,

I read a day or two ago about how a worn starter bushing will mess up your starting ability. First it starts just dragging; then it gets worse and worse. Of course, the bushing is tough to remove without the engine out, but I actually read about this in a VW Trends article describing how to use a starter bushing removal tool. You can get the tool, the article says, at:

Bugpack (714) 979-4990
or
SoCal Imports (562) 633-9731

The article was in the November, 1998 VW Trends, page 60.

As I re-read your note, though, you said it was all of a sudden. Got a prescription for that too. My old '71 used to have a bum ignition switch. It was worn just right to mess with me from time to time. As it got worse, it started killing the battery too. The PO had a heavy-duty toggle switch in there to run extra juice to the starter. It powered on the coil too, so I had to be sure it was off whenever I turned the bus off.

If you use a little electrical tester thing, you can check to see if you got power coming off the appropriate lead from the ignition switch. You may need a color wiring diagram to figure it out. From looking at mine, I think the wire you're interested in is red and black striped, connecting to terminal 50 on the ignition switch and the starter.
