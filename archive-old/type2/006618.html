Volks -

I'm just had to come out of "digest hiding" mode to share a great discovery
with y'all!!

I found a 1969 Westfalia owner's manual in the farthest recesses of Itch's
spare tire well. In the back portion of the manual, I found driving safety
instructions on a page labeled "appendix F". To wit:

"Refrain from popping wheelies, as this inhibits the ability to steer the
vehicle".

"Keep your speed under 160kph (100mph). This will aid in fuel economy and
keep insurance costs down".

"Do not attempt to take corners past the rated .92G's. Though the vehicle
will remain upright, the tires may peel away from the rims, causing a
potential hazard".

"The maximum recommended speed with the top up is 100kph (62mph), with the
wind". (my note here - remember that the '69's hinge from the front, so I
think they're saying you can use it as a sail, but only to a safe speed of
100kph)

"Check local ordinances before walking to the cabin icebox for a beer while
the vehicle is in motion. It is not legal to drink and drive in some areas".

"Do not fill the fresh water tank with more than 3 gallons of Mai Tai. This
will allow for expansion as it warms".

"Do not admire yourself in the closet mirror while the vehicle in is
motion".

"When using the cable TV receptacle, ensure you have enough cable to reach
your destination". (my note here - has anyone every found said receptacle? I
didn't know they had cable in '69).

"Do not use the rear window defogger voltage terminals to run a large string
of holiday lights. This will surely cause your fuse to blow". (wish I'd read
that sooner!)

and finally...

"Do not pass Corvettes when going uphill. This will totally blow the
impression everyone has about busses being slow".

Wow!!

So, you can understand my elation when I stumbled upon this page. From now
on, I'm going to abide by these recommendations, and I should suggest that
y'all do the same.

I'll go back into slink mode now. Happy holidays!!
