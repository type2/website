'Morning Folks,
     Well I'm about to head out to the garage to continue on my axle beam
replacement. I have the new torsion arms/ball joints in place, I hope to
get a lot more done today. I'm hoping I can get the clips that hold the
rubber stops in place off the dead beam, the ones on the donor beam fell
apart when I tried to remove them. I'd like to put in new stops, but it
just isn't in the budget right now. Anyway, the "new" beam has been painted
on the outside with POR-15, but as Steve Dolan pointed out to me a couple
of months ago, they rust from the inside out. And, in fact, looking at the
beam I pulled off, which is pretty much rusted away on the upright where
the lower tube passes through, there is obviously a cavity here where
moisture accumulates and starts the rusting process. How, then can this be
stopped? I have an idea: drill two small holes, one at the top of the
upright, one down at the bottom. Pump POR-15 into the top hole, while the
bottom hole is plugged. Remove the plug and let the POR 15 drain into a
container and repeat on the other side. This, I'm thinking would flood the
area where the lower tube passes through with the POR-15, hopefully
stopping any further rust. Crazy? Desperate? Maybe, but by God, if I live
to 100 I don't ever want to replace another beam! Please feel free to blow
any holes in this idea, that's what I'm looking for.     Jeff S.
