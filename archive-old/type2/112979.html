> Hi to all:
> Our club had a very big event this past Saturday:  we were
> interviewed, and our cars filmed, for a segment on World News Tonight
> about the end of Beetle manufacturing.  The clip will air at the end
> of Peter Jennings' news program  THIS WEDNESDAY night, July 30th, at
> 5:30pm.
>
> We had probably 14-16 cars there, and maybe 30 people.  The filming
> took over 4 hours, and was held at Rick Spohn's house/acreage in San
> Martin.  It was hotter than blazes that day, over 100 degrees, and we
> stood out in the sun in front of our cars.  The oldest models were
> parked in a semicircle in front, the next-oldest behind them, and the
> newer versions in back.  Both of my Beetles, my '69 Sedan Bogart and
> my '58 Convertible Oscar participated.
>
> Rick started the interview with a history of the club, Jan Peters said
> a few things, then the rest of us were able to chime in any time we
> had something to say.  The interviewer was a guy named Bill, who I
> recognized from newscasts on KGO news in years past.  He would ask
> pointed questions, and then we'd answer.  It was more like a
> conversation than an interview and went very well.
>
> It was terribly exciting to be able to be part of this great event and
> also to be part of something that will note "the passing" of our air
> cooled cars.  Be sure to tune into World News Tonight this Wednesday
> at 5:30pm, and watch Peter Jennings' report!
