I would guess a vacuum hose cracked or loose or off.
If you can get it started have someone feather
the gas to keep it running and listen at the
engine for a hiss.
  If it is a stuck flap on the air meter box
you can tap it with a screwdriver handle which may
unstick it. Had a Jetta that I had to do that to on
occasion <it's gone now>... :-)
If the flap is busted off in there.. ure hosed. Had
that happen on a fuel infected bug and it
sucked the flap right into the stack after a
backfire/hiccup (no damage done but was hard to find
the damn thing). 
  On my 74 dual carbed there is a metal crossover
pipe (vac line) which has rubber elbows at each end.
when they come off or crack it does pretty much what
you are describing.. donno if your 79 has that
"feature" or not. Pipe runs across the back along the
firewall
then down on both sides almost to the tin.. way in the
back.
