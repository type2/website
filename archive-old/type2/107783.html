> How can I check to see if my cold start valve is leaking??

You can, in theory, remove the cold start valve from the air distribution
box - with gas lines still in place - and then pressurize the system (I
jumper terminals 36 and 39 on the AFM plug) and watch the cold start valve
to see if it's leaking.  Kind of a PITA since the distributor gets in the
way.  You could also ground out the wire from the coil to the distributor
and then have someone crank the engine for a couple of seconds - thereby
engaging the thermo-time switch - and see that it's spraying at "startup"
and not leaking after the engine is no longer being cranked over.

> position.  But Sean says the CSV gets it's electrical impulse from the
> double relay, and if my termo time switch (ground for CSV) is also bad,
> couldn't this cause CSV to dump fuel into engine??

Shouldn't.  Remember, although the CSV gets its power from the double relay
that power is "stopped" if the thermo-time switch isn't grounded.  The heat
from the engine opens the thermo-time switch, giving the current no ground
to flow to.

> It could be that one went bad, and nothing reulted, but if the other went

>

as

>
> Now, I am balking when I snap my footoff teh gas pedal at 60MPH, and
> stalling just before I come to a stop.  Only when warm.

You might be suffering from a too-rich mixture, causing you to stall when
the engine is warm, but lots of things can cause this so I wouldn't focus
just on the CSV.
