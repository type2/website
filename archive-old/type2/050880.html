The compressor at the engine compresses the freon which then goes to the
unit under your dash through a component called an evaporator.  Fans blow
through the evaporator which delivers cooled air by letting the freon
expand which absords heat as it expands.  The warm freon then goes to the
underside of your bus to the condenser which removes some of the heat from
the freon before the freon passes back to the compressor to start the
process over.

The old style of compressor used on VW's was the York unit which took about
10HP to turn.  The newer rotary style compressors use about 3HP to turn.
Your system was also an R12 system which has been outlawed and the freon
costs about $40 per pound.  The bus system if I remember correctly used
about 6 pounds.

In order to convert to the newer styles of freon, you would need to change
the compressor, dryer and some O rings.
