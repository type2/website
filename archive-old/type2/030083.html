I've been using Rockford  boots since I got disgusted with the rapid
failure of Lobro and Qualitat "OEM" boots.  I have 3 years on a set and
they look very good with no cracking.  The Rockford boots are a modern
design made for much more twisting (as in FWD cars).  They do have a
quirk in that they are a bit shorter than the stock boots, so you have to
locate the axle end closer to the CV, not in the axle groove like the
stock boots.

their website is http://www.rockfordcv_dot_com

I buy mine at a local brake and axle outlet.

If the old boots are not letting dirt into the joint, then you are better
off not giving the CV a full solvent cleaning.  Just wipe the grease off
with a clean paper towel and add new grease.  Full cleaning causes the DV
to start up without full lubrication and may introduce grit into the
joint.
