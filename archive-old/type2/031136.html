I just did (mostly) the whole Magilla and it was just a bit over $2,000

had a machine shop with VW experience do the long block then I finished
I mostly watched and even helped while the shop worked on it.  had the
crank turned and balanced, balanced the connecting rods, new Mahle P&C,
new rings, 1gram balanced the whole bottom end, assembled.
new oil pump, honed in
head cleaned and checked, new exhaust valves, new springs, honed but not
polished the intake ports.  valves were 3 angle seated, and lapped in. new
valve seats, peened in place
all new bearings throughout
all new seals, new torque converter (not included in price)

old stuff.
fuel injection system (it was working well)
intake manifolds
exhaust
tin (cleaned and repainted)

runs very smooth, oil presure idles at 25-35 never falls below 55psi at
any temp or speed, even breaking it in on 100 degree days it never got
above 240 degrees, usually runs at about 200 or so.

new engine comp seal too!
