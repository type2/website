Alistair Cullum sends:

> Remember that the forward "thrust" of an automatic with your foot off the
> gas is only as much as is provided by the idling engine, minus the loss in
> the torque converter.  On a sufficiently steep hill, an automatic will drift
> backwards when you take your foot off the brake, until you give it enough
> throttle to counter gravity's pull.  No danger of stalling out, of course,
> but you do need to make the brake-to-throttle transition fairly quickly or
> use the handbrake to avoid rolling.

Brake to throttle transition? Apparently I didn't communicate this technique
properly.

Put your left foot on the brake pedal and right on the gas. At the same
time. 

We use the handbrake in manual-transmission cars because they have more
pedals than we have feet; if you have two pedals and two feet like auto-
transmission cars do, you don't need the handbrake.
