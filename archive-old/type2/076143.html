In the past I have salvaged old 40hp blocks with all sorts of stripped
thread problems. The method I use is the same as the head studs, I insert a
6mm case saver. I will have to go out to my tool box and check the drill
size and tap size for the external threads. I insert them with a little JB
Weld on the outside threads into a thoroughly cleaned case and after 24
hours of setting, I insert the 6mm studs with a little locktight, I use 524
red. The case savers I get come from a local bolt and nut dealer called
Tacoma Screw. They can get anything metric and it has been a life saver for
me. The only items I can't get are the fancy half round head screws for the
hinges and striker plates and such.
