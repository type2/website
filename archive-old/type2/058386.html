The true resistance of the earth strap, while low, is not zero.  When
current passes through the earth strap, there is a voltage drop across
it.

The meter I saw appeared to be a standard panel voltmeter of perhaps
100mV (a guess) input for maximum needle indication  It could
accurately measure the small voltage drop across the ground strap.

Thinking of it further, I have no idea how they handled the starting
current, which should have fried the meter if it was designed to
indicate mid-scale with normal charging current.  Perhaps they put a
diode in the circuit so that only charging would result in current
flow through the meter.

If I were to do this, it might be better to use the hot wire that goes
from the isolation relay to the aux battery.  If you measure the
voltage drop across this wire, you will still get a good indication of
the amps going into the battery.  Since this wire isn't used for
starting, your meter would be protected.  You would need two wires
instead of one to the meter in the front, but two-conducter wire is
easy to find and use.
