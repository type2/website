Does the term 'polar moment of inertia' mean anything to you?  By moving the
wheel in relation to the bearings you are changing it's moment.  ie: the
lever that is acting on the bearings.  Assume that the stock wheel is
centered between the inner and outer bearings.  The load is carried equally
by each bearing.  Move the wheel either way (but lets assume out for the
sake of discussion.)  You now have a greater portion of the load being
carried by the outer bearing.  Using a spacer to move it moves its
'moment'.  The point about which it axially rotates.  Translated through the
longer studs.  Might be that there's a physics expert on the list that can
better explain it. (Or correct me if I'm in error...)
