These are called the "condensers" since this is where the hot, high
pressure gaseous refrigerant loses heat and condenses to liquid (a
phase change can liberate a lot of energy).

The heat exchanger inside the car is called the "evaporator", where
low pressure liquid is boiled into gas, absorbing heat from the air
inside of the vehicle.

ACs are funny beasts, boiling at low temperature and condensing at
high temperatures.  Still, that's how they work!
