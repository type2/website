> I thought that maybe that connecting the (-) and (+) to the coil would give 
> me the proper operation....but I checked tonight and while there is no power 
> between the poles of the coil when the ignition key is OFF, as soon as it is 
> turned on, there is power (~11V), even when the engine isn't running....so 
> that knocks down my idea of connecting to the coil.

I really don't think this avenue of thought will get you where you want
to be. the only thing that happens only when the engine is running and
not any other times is the ignition system (i.e. spark/coil/dist). The
idea of a centrifugal switch comes to mind, but I see that only being a
real problem. (bus won't start because pump won't turn on, because
engine isn't running, because pump won't turn on). 

I would just use the signal from the coil as the control voltage. How
often will you have the ignition switch to the run position and not have
the engine running.

another point is that you are relying on previous pressure to make sure

only runs when the engine runs, I see a problem here also. Go with the
coil.
