On Mon, 17 Sep 2007 rayvwbug_at_aol_dot_com wrote:

>  This has a master
> cylinder with (3) 2 terminal brake switches.? Looks like the master
> cylinder may be ported in such a way as to detect pressure differences
> between the front and rear portion of the master cylinder, thus turning
> on the warning light by the third switch mounted in the center????
> Could poorly adjusted brakes cause this?

The two outside switches are pressure switches.  The middle switch is a
mechanical switch that responds to movement of a separate piston in the
MC.  It works unless the separate piston is gunked up, which it probably
is!  VW switched to the 2-three terminal switch MCs because they are
simpler and more reliable.

Maladjusted brakes should not cause the brake warning light to illuminate
unless they are way out of adjustment.
