> Hey Karl,
> of interest to me (and possibly others) is what exact components you need
and
> from what vans/golfs/etc. will work in the pancake bus??

The harness from a 79 CAL bus is a perfect fit with no mods necessary. The
harness from a 80-83.5 CAL Vanagon will work with a couple of mods: cut the
coil connecter in half [it's part of the EFI harness] and toss the idle
stabilizer [you plug the 2 connecters together just like you were timing a
80-83.5 Cal Vanagon]. There are 2 different styles of the 3 pin connecter
for the distributor, one has 2 guide lugs, the other has 4. Likewise, the
socket on the distributor has to match the connecter you have. The harness
has a plug for the module and a plug for the distributor and 3 loose wires,
+ to coil, - to coil, and ground.

>  Ign. module?

Same as early Rabbit w/electronic ign. or Audi 4 cyl. Make sure it has the
heatsink attached.

> coils?

Any early Rabbit or Audi electronic ign. coil will work. Bentley says "under
no circumstances should a coil other than VW part no. 211 905 115C be used
with the breakerless transisterized system". At the time Bentley was written
this was the only coil out. It has been subbed repeatedly.

> stuff like that.  and then also where can we find the info on the advance
> curves or is that done from your own research??

The curves are in the 68-79 Bentley. See section 5, pages 77 thru 80 on the
79 up Cal. ignition.

The only thing that stops this from being a simple drop-in swap is the dist.
drive gear. It will be around 3 teeth off and will have to be moved if you
want the rotor to point to the index mark at the top of the base edge.
