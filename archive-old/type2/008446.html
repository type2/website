Thanks for the reply - you're right, the deck height, is just a number
and it is the compression ration that matters except as an indicator that
you have enough clearance for ensuring the the valves do not hit the
pistons (although if the heads have been surfaced, then this can be a
false indicator)

I still have a few questions - head volume - how do you measure? -Do use
use water? Also when the heads are rebuilt, I would envision that the
valves and guides are replaced, maybe some carbon deposits cleaned up,
but is really anthing done that would change the head volume? Only
resurfacing the face where the cylinders lie would change the
compression. A slight resurfacing would have little effect on the C.R.  
It seems that any work would increase the head volume, effectively
decreasing the compression ratio.  One final note -my replacement Mahle
stock pistons,  as well as the old ones,  have a dish in them. 

(I did a calculation and found that a 0.020" reduction in deck height
would change the C.R. from 7.4: 1 to 7.7 :1)
