I'm in the midst of this same process on my '70 and having recently replaced ball joints on a Type 3 I thought I would have no problem.  Well, the first thing I found is that the upper control arm setup is different and I can't figure out how to get it
out.  On the Type 3 all I had to do was take out a stop plate and the whole thing rotated up in the torsion tube and the drop arms slipped right out.  The Type 2 has tension against the rubber stops and I can't figure how the drop arms come off.

The second thing I found is that the same shop I took the Type 3 drop arms to claimed they could only replace the ball joints while the arms were still on the vehicle (by heating it up).  Having already been told by several people that is not the case I
took my business elsewhere.  I turns out my local VW FLAPS has welded up a little jig specifically for the Type 2 ball joints.  As some of you have found out, the Type 2 ball joint has a flange that goes all the way to the edge of the control arm with the
exception of 2 notched areas so its hard to set it safely in the press.  The VW shop's jig lets the control arm sit in those 2 notches while the press exerts pressure on the ball joint.  So today I will pick up my 2 lower control arms with new ball joints
and only pay $30 labor for the pair instead of the $125 each that the tire store wanted to charge me for doing it on the vehicle.
