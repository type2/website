> is noted as opposed to model year production. Therefore if a euro

> been refering to the 1972 model year (begins August 1971) for which the
> type1 motor was installed as standard.

A little while ago I scanned a few microfiche prints from VW. I got them
from Ron van Ness. here's the link to them:

	http://www.type2_dot_com/molenari/microfiche/
	http://www.type2_dot_com/molenari/microfiche/microfiche_p25_26.jpg

It states that the type 4 engine was available since august 1971. I am
quite sure it was never an option before. Simply because it doesn't fit
in the engine bay. I have been registering M-plates of T2 for a couple
of years already, I never crossed a bus that had the type4-engine
option.

By the way, in Europe the type 4 engine was never a standard. It was
available as an option from august 1971 on.
 
> As long as we are dicussing "urban bus legends" I have read and been told
> that front disk brakes were optional for the 1970 model year. Any buses out
> there to support this?

The parts catalog I have says discbrakes are fitted since august 1970. I
don't think it was available before that date and that this story is
indeed a "legend".
	before august 1970: Not Available
	since august 1970: Standard equipment.
