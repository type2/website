Well not to get into it, but cable ends are just good ole crimped.
What you want to find is an industrial type hand held crimper, looks
like a set of cable cutters with 2.5' handles but has interchangeable
dies.  Used to make up aircraft cable style assemblies and commonly
for crimping large electrical connectors.  A good set can cost about
$300+ with only one type of die though.  Now there are also cheaper
things at Ace Hardware for crimping the connectors and stops onto
aircraft cable for garage doors and the like.  They are split half things
that you polt together.  They are harder to use, slow, and don't work
nearly as well.  But they might work for this.  I think you'll be OK with
either, I've used both many times to make new cables for VW power
window assemblied and both have proven sufficient over the long haul.
There are also hydraulic versions and versions that you bang on the
die with a big hammer.  As I no longer have acess to the nice crimper
I've always thought a $20 set of Taiwan cable cutters that you welded
up the blades a bit then ground or filed a permanent 1 size die into
would probably work jim dandy.
