"Sage Green" <honey_bus_at_hotmail_dot_com> wrote:

>Opinions sought: could I remove some of the oil through my oil filler tube 
>by slipping a tube down there and siphoning out some of the oil??

I've done it before.  It's nasty, but gets the job done.
Use tubing and catch can which will NOT MELT w/hot motor oil.

Try a Topsider MVP.  These are used on boats, because with a
boat motor everything has to come in and out through the top.
You can't put a boat up on the lift and remove the oil drain
plug to drain the motor oil.   ;-)

Shop around, this is only an example.
<http://www.westmarine_dot_com/webapp/wcs/stores/servlet/ProductDisplay?storeId=10001&langId=-1&catalogId=10001&productId=27074>

It removes the 8 quarts of oil from my MB turbodiesel crankcase
without pulling the oil drain plug.  In my bus I pull the
drain plug each time, and clean the screen every other year.
