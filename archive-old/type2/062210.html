>I am soon to embark on installing a new (stock) muffler on my 1600Dp bus
>engine (for 65' westy) are there any tricks or thingsthat I should watch
>out for? Or is the install pretty starightforward and self evident?

A couple things:

* get the 12mm copper exhaust nuts: makes it a lot easier to put it on -
more wrench room
* get some anti-sieze stuff and use it liberally
* make sure you have the clamps for between the heater box air intake and
the muffler.  Not the exhaust clamp, but the large, wide band-clamp.  Put it
over the heater box before you mate the muffler to the bus - you'll never
get it on otherwise.
* get all new gaskets (*duh*) but some people forget...
* get new fresh-air tubes
* good time to replace the engince compartment seal in the apron

>Also has anyone had any luck painting a muffler w/ grey paint that actually
>stays on for any length of time. I suppose I good get the muffler powder
>coated or something but that would cost more than a new muffler costs. Do
>most people just give up and let the muffler rust?
