The preheat that I am using is from the '72-'74 dual carb, courtesy of
Ken at theBusCo in Seattle (my tin was not stock '72). This routes the
warm air from the RHS right above the fuel pump through the front tin.
On top of the carb is a snorkel air filter (its called a "Sprint") I
got online. There is no way to control flapper that routes warm or cold
into the top of the carb, so that gets to be another adventure, but
I've found it works pretty well. I've tried to come up with a
resolution for that, but nothing really good has come to mind. My last
thought was to tie it into the thermostat cabling in some way, but that
felt a little cludgey. So, on cold mornings I just run it with a lot of
warm air until it warms up (its running nice or I smell heat in the
vent), and then pull over, and kill the warm to a trickle.

Let me know if you want to see how it all fits together and we can find
some time. If a picture is worth a 1000 words, being there is worth
1000 pictures.
