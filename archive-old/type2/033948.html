Bob is quite a guy.  He is currently writing a book on the various
aspects of rebuilding the AC Type 1 and Type IV motors in conjunction
with Gene Berg's son (whose name I can't remember right now).  He did
a bunch of dyno-based work last year and will do some more when
the winter work-load drops off a little.  To unjustly oversimplify his
work and findings it seems he's ending up on the side of signficantly
reduced compression (7:1 and lower) with well massaged heads for both
usable power, lowered heat and longevitiy/reliability.  This all reminds
me of a conversation I had with Leonard at AVP last year.  I think their
Type IVs come out with a CR of 6.5:1 in some cases.  

Just passing on what I've heard...
