Subject: Re: [T2] Weber manifold problem?

This works well enough that when GM built the Corvair they used one carb on 
each side feeding 3 cylinders.

They placed the single carb between two cylinders so one cylinder wasn't 
getting most of mixture and the others running lean.

As a result the engine tuning is a BIG compromise.

You are tuning to make the leanest cylinders rich enough and simply wasting 
fuel in the other cylinders.

This is advantage of those fancy, 'spensive carbs. You can tune for each 
cylinder.

I THINK the idea here is that the fuel/air mix gets distributed based on 
vacuum - that is since each cylinder produces the same vacuum, each 
cylinder gets the same charge.

Also the last single carb conversion I saw had a hollow base that both dual 
runners ran to - - - that is to say that despite the visual offset of the 
carb, no harm is done.

You're carb is a progressive so that one throat is closed until you floor 
the gas pedal and this helps keep the fuel mileage reasonable.

I'd love to hear more from the list experts.
