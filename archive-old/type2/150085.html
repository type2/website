Hi Eddie,

My secret weapon for the exhaust-stud skirmish is a custom modified  
set of middle-sized vice grips. Not "Mastercraft Locking pliers", but  
honest to goodness $14.99 Vice-Grips from Irwin Corp. The Real McCoy,  
so to speak. I bought them specifically with this task in mind,  
though it doesn't stop me from using them for other things.

In order to grip the stud from a perpendicular angle, I used my angle- 
grinder to put a V-shaped groove into the jaws of the vice-grips.  
This allows the pliers to contact the stud in four places instead of  
only two, and the pliers can also close slightly tighter, which  
allows a bit better clearance to spin the vice grips.

http://www.pottsfamily_dot_ca/westfakia/V-Jaw_Vicegrip1.jpg
http://www.pottsfamily_dot_ca/westfakia/V-Jaw_Vicegrip2.jpg

But in the end it was still too tight a working space, so i wound up  
removing the push rod tubes for the #1 cylinder. I heated up the stud  
cherry red, then applied some candle-wax to the base of the stud.  
Capillary action wicks it into the tight spaces between aluminum and  
steel molecules. Then I heated it again and quenched it with an ice  
cube, to shock it with thermal stress. After that I the vice grips  
yielded the satisfying CREAAAK!!! of the stud breaking free. It would  
only turn about 4 degrees at a time, but at least it was moving. Soon  
it was out, and a new stud in its place.
