> By the way, The stuff in the bags labeled with the brand name "TSP" isn't
> really tri-sodium-phosphate!  It's some substitute cleaning agent that
> doesn't contain phosphates.  Phosphates are an ecological bogeyman because
> they are a terrific fertilizer and tend to promote plant growth in
> waterways that have sewage dumped into them, so don't do that.
>
> _Real_ TSP is available and is the preferable material.  It is usually
> found in white bags right next to the brand name ersatz "TSP".
>
> Dispose of the cleaning solution by allowing it to sit and cool, then skim
> the oil from the surface.  The remaining liquid can be poured on your
> lawn, which will love it.  Be sure to spread it around over a large areas
> so the grass won't OD on the fertilizer!
>
> If you have bare metal parts, don't forget to primer them before you paint
> them!  Unless it's special stuff, the finish paint will not stick well to
> the bare metal
