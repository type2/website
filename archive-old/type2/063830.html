At 01:53 AM 8/20/96 -0400, you wrote:
>After seeing some of the older model type IIs at the VW Jamboree in Costa
>Mesa this weekend I had a question concerning license plates I hope someone
>could answer.  I saw quite a few vehicles with the original (black on yellow)
>California license plates.  Did these people just get increadibly lucky and
>purchase their resto projects with the original plates?  Or is there a way to
>go through the DMV to get the original plate re-issued?  It seems like
>re-issuing original plates would be next to impossible but states do seem to
>be printing different variety of plates these days and maybe that's a new
>option I'm not aware of.

You can register your car with "year of manufacture" plates.  You get the
plates at a swap-meet or whatever, and take them to DMV to register your car
with them.  The plates have to be "clear," meaning no car is registered to
them, you have to have a matching set, and they have to be from close to the
same year as your car.  The hard part is getting plates that are clear.
I've bought 2 sets of '51 plates, neither of which was clear.  I got a
refund on one set, the other was at a discounted price, so I took my
chances...  Even tho he swore they were clear and had come off of his
split-window bug.  Yeah, right - they were registered to a '78 Ford pickup!
It would do you well to get some type of enforceable guarantee...

Plate colors: (CA only)

'72 -> 8? blue with yellow letters
'64-'72? black with yellow letters, narrow, registration sticker on upper
right corner of plate
'56-'63 yellow with black letters, narrow (normal) registration sticker 'til
'62, sticker holder above right corner of plate after that.
'51-'56 black with yellow letters, wide, registration on metal chip on lower
right corner, new chip every year
'47-'51 yellow with black letters, wide, registration tag on metal chip that
fits onto upper right corner of plate, new chip every year
'3?-'46 - I _think_ yellow with black, different font.  I think you got new
plates every year, at least in the early 30's
