On Thu, Jul 03, 2003 at 10:39:03AM -0400, Acoustic Canada wrote:
> If a cylinder is not firing should/would you hear a miss in the eng ? 

depends on how good you can hear stuff like that. My ears aren't very
good at hearing stuff like that myself, so I use a different technique
to help identify a cyl that is not firing.

Hook a tach up to the engine. then with the engine running pull a plug
wire from the distributor. If that cyl was firing (this includes
building compression as well as firing) then pulling the spark from
that cyl will result in a lower rpm. Replace the wire & the rpm should
return to normal. If you pull one & the engine doesn't respond then
something is wrong with that cyl.
