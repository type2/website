>How do corvairs heat?

The Corvair engine thermostats close the cooling air exit point under the 
engine where the VW regulates the flow of cooling air to the engine. The 
whole thing is much like the early "stale air" VW heating systems.

When it is cold the thermostats keep the doors closed most of the time and 
the heated air is collected under the heads and cylinders and off the 
exposed exhaust manifold and sends it into the passenger cabin. I am told 
that the Corvair has a much more effective heater than the VW... Probably 
just more effective because there are few places to leak and fewer parts to 
lose.

BUT any oil leaks in the engine heads, valve covers, pushrod tubes or 
exhaust leaks will pollute the heated air. That is the main reason I'm told 
that so many Corvairs were parked with low mileage (my donor car had only 
65K miles on it). The OEM pushrod tube seals were made out of the wrong 
rubber and they failed quickly under normal use. The average car owner may 
not wanted to pay someone to do this replacement annually. The current 
seals are made out of Viton just like our VW's and make it right long term.

There is one Corvair fellow attending college in the north east this winter 
and he reports his Corvair makes GOOD heat at any temps.
