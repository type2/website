One thing-  I started up an engine that had sat for 5-9 years, it ran fine
until I put it under load.  At which point I dropped a valve (old, brittle).
I cracked the case to find a crankcase filled with 1" of solidified sludge.
Yow!

I did change the oil, but did not pay close attention.  When you change the
oil, run a finger or small screwdriver in around the lip of the oil screen
location.  Search for solids.  If you find ANY, I would tear the engine down
and rebiuld before even one start.  I learned my lesson very quickly.  (like
6 blocks)

I would also get a friend with a large truck (tow vehicle) to follow you on
a nice country drive if you decide to start and drive it.  You never know
what awaits after decades of neglect...
