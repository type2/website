Hmmm, well I have been driving Westfalia vans off and on since 1970, most of
them stock, with vacuum advance distributors.  I have never gotten over 20
mpg in any of them.  I am certain my driving habits have a lot to do with
it, but Westfalia's weigh a whole bunch, too.  Though my current Westy does
not have a pop-top, that was also a consideration in my earlier vans.
    I think the real answer though, is that the mechanic who tuned the carbs
believes that a little rich is far better than a little lean in an
air-cooled engine, an opinion shared by Gene Berg in his catalog.  I think a
little loss in gas mileage is a small price to pay for an engine that runs
cool and will last a long time.
    However, it may well be that a leaner running engine would save enough
fuel to offset the possible shortening of life of the engine.  This is a
matter of personal preferance, I suppose.
