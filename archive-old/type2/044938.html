Hi Garp,

If your conversion to a 1641 did not include a semi-hemi cut to the heads to
increase the combustion chamber volume, dished pistons, or spacers between
the cylinders and case, the compression has been raised to a point you can
not deal with it by retarding the spark.  Another avenue to explore is to
richen up your carb's mixture by putting in a larger main jet in the area of
a #135 to a #140.  (Stock should be a #125.)  A step or two colder heat range
spark plug should also help, as cold as you can use without fouling.    Are
you running premium fuel?  In a heavy vehicle such as a bus, I prefer an
earlier spec vacuum advance distributor, set to 3 - 5 degrees initial
advance.  It is load dependent and the bus is usually under load just pushing
all that air out of the way.
