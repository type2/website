It doesn't sound like the cat to me.  More than likely its the O2 sensor.
This can be checked easily with a $6 VOM meter from Harbor Freight.
Disconnect the 02 sensor lead after the engine has warmed up and attach a
VOM set for milli volts.  It should bounce back and forth between say 350
to 750 mv.  If it stays on the high side only, look for something else that
is making it rich, i.e., cold start valve, plugged aircleaner, bad temp
sensors, etc.  If it stays on the low side (with the O2 sensor disconnected
from the ECU) you probably need to replace the o2 sensor as it is the
culprit that is causing the ECU to make your mixture rich.
