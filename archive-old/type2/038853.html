A rebuilt is the best quality engine if it is a _really_ good rebuild on a
good case.  The only rebuilts of this quality level that I know of are
owner-rebuilt engines.  I doubt that a garage mechanic will approach this
level of quality.  The flat-rate hours just don't allow meticulous work.

Crate rebuilts are the worst.  They are built to last just a bit longer
than the warranty.  This is the "long block" that the mechanic is
referring to.

New engines are imported from Mexico, but according to some there is a
difference in sourceing.  I don't know the particulars, but I've had good
luck with the new engines I've received from Bus Depot.

In VW terms, a short block is a case with crank, cam and rods installed.
A long block is an engine complete with heads.  A "turnkey" is an engine
complete with manifold, carb, tin, distrubutor and coil.

In your position, I think that the best chance of a good outcome is to
install a new mexican engine.  keep in mind that these engines come
without a core charge, which means that you keep your old engine.  If the
mechanic keeps it, the core charge (100-150) should be deducted from the
bill.
