This weekend I pulled the engine to do a lot of little things I've been
meaning to do when it wasn't so hot outside.

Pulled it yesterday, did about a hundred little chores, put it back in this
afternoon. Redid all gas lines, fine. Hooked up all the electrical bits on
the engine, fine. Everything back like it was.

Put the negative cable back on the battery, about ready to cruise around
the block and make one last check for the smell of gasoline--and the hot
wire to the alternator immediately LIT. I don't mean the insulation melted,

marked the wire, and anyway I know where that wire goes. That's the only
post-style electrical connection on the whole engine.

Nothing else melted or even got hot but that one wire.

Now I'm scared to death to hook it up with a new wire and try it again.
