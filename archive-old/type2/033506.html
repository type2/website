Curt:
I'd have to agree that having FOUR studded snow tires is a real
eye-opener! I've been using such a setup on 2 water-leaker Vanagons for
3 seasons now and have been really pleased. The difference between
having 4 studded tires and 2 studded tires is right up there  with the
difference between having 40 lbs of air and 10lbs! (It's a BIG
difference). Here in Iowa we tend to get only a few deep snows each
year, but because of the humidity it does not subliminate (okay physics
people, is that the right word?), but rather turns into packed, frozen,
ice-mud-salt gunk that stays on the road for 2 weeks or so.
If you decide to go with such a plan and buy 4 rather obscure tires, I'd
recommend you buy 5. If you ever ruin one, you may have a lot of trouble
finding another, and besides, it's always nice to have a spare with the
same characteristics.
