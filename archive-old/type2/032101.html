> I emailed Bus Depot about this, but have not gotten a response yet.

Let's see. You have the canvas now, yes? Seems to me you should be able to
answer your own question by direct observation. Just assemble your tools
and give it a go. Using a few staples to hold the canvas temporarily in
place before you tack/staple/whatever down the plastic strip will
definitely let you see what you've got and what it will take. Mind the
height difference with support hinges bolted and unbolted, as noted in my
archived post, and you should be fine. I'd be surprised if Bus Depot has
accurate information about the factory tolerances of the length of those
difference is going to be plus or minus fractions of an inch. And then
there's the factory (and real world) tolerances of the height of an
elevated Westfalia poptop to consider. Bus Depot certainly can't tell you
exactly how high your own poptop happens to be.

My canvas was big enough to fold. Yours probably is too. And if it's not,
you'll find out when you're installing and then you can do it the unfolded
way. The fabric can also stretch a quarter inch or so too, so you're
working with a sort of forgiving medium here.
