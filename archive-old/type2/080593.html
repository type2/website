It works because the operation of a dual-vacuum distributor with the retard 
disconnect is very close to that of a single-vacuum distributor.  The 
vacuum retard function on the dual distributor pulls the timing back about 
12 degrees at idle.  Therefore, if you disconnect the vacuum retard on the 
dual dist., you need to advance your base timing by about 12 degrees (from 
~-5 to ~+7) to compensate.  I'm actually running the same way at the moment 
- did it as a test last year and never put it back to stock.  It increases 
emissions at idle (which I think is why they brought out the dual-advance 
dist. in the first place) but the advance curve under full throttle will be 
identical, which is why your bus still runs beautifully.  At part throttle 
though, you will be getting somewhat more total advance, which may or may 
not be a problem.  With an engine that is prone to detonate, it might make 
the difference between pinging and not pinging - it would go something like 
this:  With a dual-vacuum dist. with both hoses connected, it would never 
ping.  With the retard unhooked and the timing bumped up 12 degrees, it 
might ping under part throttle but the pinging would go away upon 
transision to full throttle (this is because at part throttle, the 
vacuum-retard is also pulling out some timing - not the full 12 degrees, 
but varying between 0-12 with throttle position - more throttle equals less 
retard, not linear but sloped more steeply at higher vacuum).

Another aside, because of what I just mentioned above, switching from stock 
dual carbs to Dellorto DRLAs changed my timing curve drastically!  I have 
less total manifold vacuum and as I depress the accelerator, the vacuum 
drops off much more sharply than it did with the stock carbs.  This is 
because of a) bigger throttle plates, and b) vacuum is pulled from only one 
cylinder instead of four.  The net result of the carb swap was a 
significant increase in advance at part throttle!  Therefore I've set my 
base timing back a bit to compensate.  I think I am running around 4 
degrees BTDC instead of 7.5, to be safe.
