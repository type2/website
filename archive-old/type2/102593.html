Subject: Re:propane heaters 

Propane freezes into a solid at MINUS 187~ degrees Celsius, and boils into 
the gaseous state
at MINUS 42~ degrees Celsius. So, it should be a gas  at the temps you 
describe.

Butane, on the other hand, boils at zero degrees Celsius, and would be a 
liquid at the temps you describe. That would be 32 degrees Fahrenheit. 
Below 32 it would be a liquid and would behave as you describe.

I suggest that you were not being supplied with pure propane for your BBQ 
tests in Montana.
