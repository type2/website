The Vanagon is probably heavier than the baywindows, but I doubt that is why it is so slow.  If I were you I would take it to a chassis dyno and have the hp and torque measured.  Make sure they have a wideband O2 sensor so you can also check your A/F ratio.  This will cost under $100 and should help answer your questions.  I bet you are running way rich which would kill your power and mileage as you describe.  I also have my suspicions that the cam in the Vanagon 2-liter is a conservative grind although I have nothing to back that up.
