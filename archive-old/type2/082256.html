Subject: Re: [T2] PSI vs. Weight Rating

Firstly look up in your hand book / workshop manual the tyre pressures for a standard tyre, then measure the width of the tread on a standard tyre.
When you get the new tyres measure the width of tread.
Then ... pressure for the new tyres = pressure for standard tyre * tread width of new tyre / tread width of standard tyre.
This will give you a starting point from which you could adjust up or down by a few psi to fine tune the ride quality.
Keep an eye on the wear pattern too - wear in the centre of the tread indicates over inflation, wear at both outer edges of the tread indicates under inflation.
