Subject: Re: discovered rocker rust (was cold weather painting)

I have never checked on a Bus but I know that Karmann Ghias are
constructed with a series of drain holes at the bottom of the rockers so
that any water that does get in there will have a chance to escape. In the
case of the KG those holes are essentially small gaps at regular intervals
in the seam between the inner and outer rocker skins. The problem is that
over time dirt, sand, and salt get into the cavity as well and clog up the
holes so that the mixture sits in there and stews. So, what I'm getting at
is that if Busses also have these holes make sure you clean them out
before shooting POR 15 into the rocker cavity and make sure that the POR
15 doesn't clog them up in the process.

And, regarding cold weather painting, I don't recall anyone suggesting
that 'fast' reducer be used when painting during the winter. But even fast
reducer won't do you any good at 32 degrees. I think the paint can would
probably have directions warning not to use it at temperatures under about
50 degrees.
