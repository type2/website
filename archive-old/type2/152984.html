Subject: Buses by the Beach Fall Camp Sept 16-18

Hey, Volks:

It's almost time for everyone to be together again at "BUS BOO!" Buses
By the Beach annual fall campout an Halloween before Halloween party
in Grand Rapids, MI Sept. 16th, 17th & 18th. It looks like we are in
for another great turn out of Bus Pilots and VW lovers from all over
the country. So bring your musical instruments for some campfire
jamin'. Remember, it's not about sounding good, just feeling good!

 

If you have not made reservations please call the campground so we can
plan for camping space. Call Grand Rogue at 1-616-361-1053 located at
6400 West River Drive, Belmont, MI 49306. Make sure you tell them that
you are with Buses By the Beach group so they can group us all
together. http://www.grandrogue_dot_com/

 

Back by popular demand, we are camping during the West Michigan Living
History and Educational Association presentation of Early America
Encampment at Grand Rogue Campground. Visitors can view first hand
what life in early America was like. There will be period craftsmen
demonstrating their skills, including a blacksmith and a woodworker.
Working with hand tools, they show how everyday implements were made
in early America. At the Encampment, history buffs wearing authentic
period clothing and living in period shelters, present programs
throughout the weekend. Activities will include musket and cannon
firing, men's and women's period fashion shows and tactical
demonstrations. For more information go to:

http://www.westmichiganlivinghistory_dot_com/

 

*Helpful tips for those coming to "Bus Boo!:*

 
Plan for all of your mealsexcept for Sunday morning. We are having
BBBBBB! (Buses By the BeachBreakfast Burrito Buffet) Please bring an
item or two to donate. Suggesteditems...tortillas, sausage, eggs,
cheese, etc.
 

2.      Alcohol is permitted, obnoxious drunks are not. Let's have a
good time but please be responsible.

 

3.      Saturday night is our annual Halloween before Halloween
costume and site decoration contest. Bring a costume, decorate your
site and let's have some fun!

 

4.      Park where ever it makes your VW happy. We do not have to park
on designated camping sites. Try to avoid wet or soggy spots. We
should be fine because everything has been dry but we want to respect
the campground so we will be invited back again.

 

5.      Electricity and water will be very limited. You might get
lucky and get a spot next to a hook up. Try to plan for not having
hookups so we can save those spots for those that absolutely need it.

 

6.      Please feel free to bring an instrument play. Don't worry
about your ability level. We are not concerned about sounding good,
just feeling good!

 

7.      *Please do not bring firewood!* We do not want to contribute
to the spreading the Emerald Ash Borer.

 

8.      Kids are welcome. Grand Rogue Campground is a very kid
friendly campground so feel free to bring bikes or toys for them to
play with. We will be located right next to a playground and
restrooms.

 

9.      AJ's Family Fun Center is a few miles away. They have
go-carts, mini-golf, large video arcade, and other games to keep the
family entertained. More info at http://www.ajsfamilyfun_dot_com/

 

10. There are grocery stores, restaurants and many other retail shops
close to the campground in case you need supplies.

 

11. Campground Quiet Hours is at 10:30PM. Please be respectful of other campers.

 

*Schedule of Events:*

 

Friday:

 

5:00pm to 10:30pm - Check in and set up. Then join everyone around the
community campfire for conversations. Say hi to old friends and meet

Pine State Trail. It is a paved bike trail that winds around the Rogue
