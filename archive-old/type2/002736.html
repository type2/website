> A few years ago I ran an oil temp sender in the oil pressure relief valve
> location that you suggest.  At the same time I had a thermocouple attached

> identical.  Both in temperature and the time it took to get to that
> temperature.
> If you want to know what your oil temp is the "plug for the oil pressure
> relief valve" location will not tell you.
> If you want to know your oil temp on a type 1 engine use a dip stick
sender.
> I'm not sure what senders are available for the type 4 engine.  If I were
> doing it I'd use the round cover plate at the rear of the engine as my
access
> point and install a sender that extends at least 2 inches into the oil.
The
> issue is that the case runs at least 20 degrees cooler than the oil and
takes
> much longer to heat up.
> If you screw a metal sender into the case and the sender isn't really well
> thermally isolated from the case you will read the case temperature.
Metal
> conducts heat much better than oil.
> The best way to know how hot your engine is getting is a CHT gauge.  On
the
> type 4 engine you can cut off the big circle on the sender and wedge
what's
> left between two fins near the spark plug.  It's not difficult and will
give
> you useful information instead of vague indications.  Vague indications,
> however are better than nothing.  But if your going to the trouble of
> installing a temp gauge, why not install one that gives good information?
