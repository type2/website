Hello David,
              I fixed this kind of problem on a 69 Type 3 squareback ~20 years 
ago... so the value of my recollection is limited.  The bushings in the arms 
were pretty severely worn, ditto the clevis pins.  I was able to buy new arms 
and made the pins on a buddy's lathe.  The _key_ to reinstalling everything was 
a tool made from a large screwdriver (or maybe tyre spoon) -- I used an 
oxy/acet torch to re-shape into a J shape.  With this tool you can then 
reinstall the torsion bars.  I still have the tool someplace and may have the 
write-up from an old Bentley... so if you are interested in pursuing, let me 
know.  Where are you located, in a rough geographic sense?
