Subject: Re: [T2] replacing lifters/ pushrods

On 7/23/03 8:56 PM, "Jasan C." <type2-78_at_comcast_dot_net> wrote:

> In the next couple weeks I am planning on replacing my pushrod tube seals.
> While I'm in there, I want to pull my lifters and look for any wear. I can
> just use a magnet probe to remove them right?

Yes, telescoping magnet.

> When I put them back in do they
> need to be lined up in a specific to the cam or can I just carefuly drop them
> back down the bore?

You can orient them with the oiling hole facing up. I can't remember where I
heard that.

Just be sure to keep track of the lifters and put them back into the same
bores they came from.

> Also I seem to remember that the pushrods need to be
> rotated when I put them back untill they drop into the lifter?

The lifter has a cup in the center where the push rod makes contact. No
rotating is required you just need to make sure it's seated properly before
you do your valve adjustment. If you haven't done it right, as you rotate
the engine to being another cylinder to TDC you'll hear a loud click and the
adjusting screw you previously tightened will loosen up.
