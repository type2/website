I second Tom's recommendation..... after fuel stands for a year, it loses some 
of its volatility.  You began to address condensation effects by adding what is 
generally an alcohol product, but this would have the side-effect of lowering 
octane rating.  I don't recall how difficult it is to flush a Vanagon tank, but 
if there is any way to get a sample from your tank, I would check that 
immediately.  It might be smart to rig a temporary, known-to-be-clean (small) 
tank filled with fresh fuel, and see how the engine runs on that.  Best of luck!
