Subject: Re: Fuel injectors made by GP-Sorensen?

Thanks for the reply Alex,
I only got two other replies, I rather suspect that not many have tried 
them. I typically say Bosch is the way to go but sometimes the wallet 
says otherwise. You are quite correct about the seals that come in the 
box with the injectors. I had problems with the large O-ring pulling 
through the hole in the bracket that holds the injector. Fortunately I 
remembered I had a new set of seals I got about a year ago. On 
comparison of the large seals I found the outside diameter of the German 
seals to be larger to the point of perfect fit in the bracket and the 
rubber much denser than the Sorensen seal. I also found that the German 
small seal is slightly longer than Sorensens small seal and the slightly 
tapered shape of the German seal seams to seat on the injector hole much 
easier.

On testing the Sorensen injectors, They appeared to have a healthy spray 
pattern and ZERO leakage. After doing a fuel pressure test, I took a 
lunch break. When I returned, maybe 45 minutes later, the gauge still 
read about 25 psi. I suspect most of the pressure that was lost went 
through the pressure regulator or the fuel pump. Don't get me wrong, I 
would bet a new set of Bosch injectors would hold pressure at least as well.

On completion of the install, I found my bus to run a little smoother 
and a little cooler. This is what I wanted out of a new injector so I'm 
satisfied. Now if they work as well as the Bosch units I just removed, 
27 years from now, I'll be impressed.

P. S.
I'm keeping the old Bosch units, just in case.

Thanks again for your observations.
Tom
