What this means is,

If you remove the plugs from an engine while it is warm and thenput in 
new ones, When the engine cools and shrinks, it will strain the threads 
and maybe cause the spark plugs to seize up. I am pretty sure that the 
case material expands more than the steel for a given amount of heat. I 
would not worry about doing the compression check.IT IS SUPPOSE TO BE 
DONE on  A WARM OR HOT ENGINE. Also , all the spark plugs are supposed to 
be removed from the case when checking compression. I f you have a 
problem with the above, warm the engine, remove all spark plugs, check 
the compression on all four cylinders, wait for the engine to cool down, 
and replace all the spark plugs - there, your done.
