So I'm out driving my 70 Bus (less than 70,000 miles on the clock).  Just 
toodling along.  Not going more than 10 or 15 mph on a curvy little road 
through some beautiful parkland.  As I negotiate right hand turns at this 
speed I OCCASIONALLY hear a clear knock-knock-knock coming from what seems to 
be the left rear of the Bus.  Once I hear this same sound (same speed) on a 
straightaway.
In general daily use I hear nothing like this at all.  Freeway speeds, 
surface streets, turning, backing - nothing.  Since this little incident I 
have heard nothing from the Bus.  But, then again, I have not driven it in 
the same manner.
Which brings me about to the dreaded question......is this knock-knock-knock 
the likely telltale sign of a CV joint going out?  Everything looks solid 
underneath.  Boots are looking good - no tears.  Or could it signify 
something else entirely?  Or, for that matter, is this sort of thing to be 
expected at this uncommon speed?
