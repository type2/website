I said jump the circuit fuse. It's not a reach to consider the problem could be
a bad fuse fit or loose connection at the fuse wires. And it is one of the
easiest places to start looking. If the owner puts the jumper on prior to
starting the car the result, or lack of one, will be one step closer to
understanding the problem.

Put an inline fuse in the jumper wire if you like BUT  I can guarantee you that
if you short the jumper to ground you WILL see sparks .....well should anyway.

Don't do that.
