Well, I did some checking yesterday, and found that #4 sparkplug (which is
where I have my CHT sender installed) was loose.

When I first tried to tighten it, it kept turning and did not seem to want
to lighten up (as in, stripped cyl. head threads).  I took it out and saw
evidence of combustion gas leakage on the plug threads (black stuff), put it
back in and then it seemed to tighten up OK, although not as tight as the
other plugs.  I'm guessing that a). I never fully "crushed" the CHT sensor
or b). the threads are really stripped and I need to do something about
that.  I'm gonna try installing the plug without the CHT sensor to see if
the crush is the issue, then go for a test drive.

Anyway, it makes sense to me that the observed CHT gauge behaviour (i.e.,
higher temperatures and more rapid temperature fluctuations) would result
from the plug being loose as 1) it came on rather suddenly, 2) the loose
plug would loose its thermal contact with the cyl. head and thus loose the
cyl. head as it's heat sink, 3) combustion gas leakage past the plug threads
could easily impinge on the CHT sensor.

So, right now I'm thinking that I have a short term solution at best, and
that I need to either replace the head(s) or, given the mileage (about
55,000) on my engine, start looking for a new engine.  Any opinions on
cylinder head vs engine replacement?  This bus is not a daily driver, but I
do go on long trips so reliability is important.

Any recommendations for sources of cylinder heads?  If I decide on engine
replacement, I was thinking of buying a long block from Boston Bob or Jake
Raby - any other suggestions?  I am in the Philly, PA area.
