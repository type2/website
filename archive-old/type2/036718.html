Rick,
Is the smoke coming out of the end of the exhaust pipe?
If so, my theory is a broken ring or piston. Does it lack power?  The smoke is
blue/white right?  If so, oil (lots of it) is getting into a cumbustion
chamber and its probably NOT coming in through the valvetrain.

I don't think this is head or valve related because if it were, the cylinder
in question would probably cease to fire - no smoke at all or very little
coupled with a significant loss of power and rough running - but all the time
- not just after it warmed up.

If the smoke is not coming from the exhaust pipe, you've sprung a serious leak
somewhere - a hole in a pushrod tube (or case-side seal) leaking oil onto a
hot exhaust?  The fact that it happens only after she warms up points more to
an oil leak, hopefully.

Hope not (i could be wrong and quite frequently am ;-), but my gut feeling is
you're looking at at least a partial top-end rebuild. Depends on your
mechanic...$150-500+?

good luck and keep us posted if u can - you are not the only one who doesn't
know what happened or (most importantly) why - your findings will be important
to the list.

thanks and good luck,
