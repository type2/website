I usually hate worn out threads, but this is a vastly important issue.
Assuming my oil temp gauge is correct, my bus runs like crap at 170-190.  It
runs significantly better between 200-220, but I start taking measures to
cool down when I reach 240.  I can't imagine driving all the time at
170-190.  I hear a lot of people say that you should be able to comfortably
pinch the end of the dipstick after driving a bus that is cooling properly.
To them I say try pinching a dipstick that has been boiling in water at only
212 for a few minutes and tell me how comfortable that is.  I have even
tried pinching the dipsticks in my water cooled Jeep Cherokee, Nissan
Frontier, and Pontiac Grand Prix and this old wives tale is simply garbage.
If you can pinch the dipstick you are running waaaay too cool.  Take care.
