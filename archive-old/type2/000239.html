I have an automatic bus which has been converted to a weber

Phil,
this is an 'easy' one, as I just went through this on my 73 with a 1700
stock engine...I installed a new single Weber 'kit' and found the 'stalling'
you refered too...apparently the Weber set-up gives you too much vacuum for
the stock vacuum dist...my cure was to install a '009' centfrigual advance
dist and keep the vacuum dist for a spare...with the 009 dist, my guru told
me to time it by hooking up the timing light and rev up the engine to about
2500 rpm or so and look at the full advance on your degree scale on the
engine...the 009 dist has about 28-32 deg's full advance...I set mine to 30
deg's and it runs great...this combination (single weber/009 dist) hasn't
worked for everyone if you look back at the archives, but mine runs OK,
however I think I'm getting fewer MPG than the dual stock carb's - but
without the carb synch headaches...good luck.
