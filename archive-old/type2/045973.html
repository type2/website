On Sun, 17 Sep 2000, Mike Soultanian wrote:

> I beg to differ on this.  The higher(or lower) the pressure, the more(or
> less) fuel that will be sprayed into the manifold. 

Mike is absolutely right on this.  There is an additional parameter, tho.  
The three factors affecting the final amount of fuel delivered are:

Fuel pressure,
Injector timing, *and* 
Orifice size.

If you have a clogged injector or if your injectors have many miles, then
the diameter of the orifice is almost certainly not "stock" and the mix
will change based on that.
