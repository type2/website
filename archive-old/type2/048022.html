Start with the body...

Paint inside and then outside and the bottom (KILL THE RUST! - <grin!>)

Remove the windows before all the painting and then replace them when your 
done.

You are basically replaying the factory assembly process -- build the body 
and then attach the mechanicals and then the drive train and then the 
interior...

If you plan to drive this bus at the same time - you will have to design 
your own version of this process.

This is what I have done in the past.

My version while I drive the bus is fix everything as you go along in order 
of budget and importance. Brakes first -- hehe. Engine next. Electrics as 
the problems come up. This will give you good exposure to what parts cost 
and the true quanity of rust the old car REALLY has (always 2.817 times 
more than I thought it had before I got started...)

Eventually if I feel like the bus is getting fixed right and staying fixed 
and if I STILL like the vehicle then I'll test my love with bodywork and 
the high cost of paint jobs.

Lastly still comes the inside.

Usually this takes years.... <grin>

Keep us updated...
