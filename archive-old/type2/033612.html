>I found the source of the noise. It was the fuel pump.
>1. should it run with the key in ACC?

It's wired to do that apparently. carburated pancake motors from 72-74,
when people got rid of the twin carbs and switched to center carbs, sometimes
junked their stock mechanical fuel pump and switched to an electric aftermarket
one, which is mounted on the body above the transmission and is usually called
'facet' and is usually wired to start pumping when the key is turned to the
first notch, when the idiot lights come on. I don't know why people went with
this pump, probably because it could deliver more fuel for a dual barrel center-mount carb, and probably because the
hose connection diameters are similar.

On the 75 FI motor, it might have replaced a bad stock fuel pump, which are much
more expensive to replace?   I remember that the 75 computer sometimes wouldn't
cut off or cut on the fuel pump - maybe someone else will know.....
