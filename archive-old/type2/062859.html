Yes sir, you make some excellent points. There are two things that make 
up a good collector's car. The first is its rarity, the second is its 
desirability. If, for some reason, there were only 100 pre-68 VW vans in 
the world, their price would jump dramatically. On the other hand, they 
have a reputation that attracts "radicals", which might make them less 
desirable.

My parents were middle class, mom worked for IRS, dad in a wood mill. 
They bought our '65 deluxe as a family wagon, because of price, 
functionality, and supposed reliability. 30 years later, I'm looking for 
the same van. I've received comments like "oh, you want a drug van" and, 
"why not buy one of the new ones... they are so much better", etc.

As you say, as long as it's cheaper to restore these vans than to buy a 
restored one (or reasonably close), they will be restored. Personally, 
I've considered buying a couple, but they had moderate cancer rust... 
but I pass on paying $7500 for a fully restored van. I guess I'm guilty 
on both counts... it's not worth enough to fully restore a rusted VW, 
and that's the majority of what's left out there.

Oh well, maybe I'll take my vacation in CA this year. Who knows, I might 
drive back a souvenier.
