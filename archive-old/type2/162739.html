On Sat, 21 Apr 2007, Gundesens wrote:

> Hello list, my '82 2.0 liter AC Vanagon w/ megasquirt went kaput last
> fall, making horrible noises and with zero compression now in #4.  I
> suspect a valve seat let loose or even a broken valve from the noise
> made while it ran for the minute or so in this condition.  It was
>
> Question:  Is it OK to rebuild by replacing one piston and cylinder
> only?  If so, does anyone have one or where can I buy just one?

the answer is a qualified "Maybe!"  I have done it and the engine ran fine
for years, but officially it is an absolute no-no.  once when desperate I
pulled one cyl and piston without even taking the engine out of the 78
bus.  was a pain and was probably dumb luck that I found one that was
close to balanced.  you can probably find a decent piston and cyl (get
match piston and cyl from same cyl)from an engine that blew one cyl itself
and has 3 servicable ones.  some considerations, when you lost a valve,,
did you do any other damage??  unless you are very lucky I would not
consider this type of repair a long term permanent fix, although I ran
mine for 4-5 years and over 75,000 miles like that.  check your head very
carefully

>
> When I rebuilt it, I put in the Webcam 107i and their lifters. I could
> never put any pre-set negative clearance on those lifters without
> obvious trouble with some valves staying open. I ran zero clearance or
> even .006 like a mech lifter, but then was not getting the benefit of
> hydraulics.  I am thinking of pulling it and going back to stock (new)
> cam and lifters.  The rebuild was totally with stock except for the cam.
>
> Comments on this?  Better breathing with this cam should have meant
> cooler running, no?

changing a cam changes so many other things that you probably were doing a
"test" just by running it, and the problem could have been from something
totally unconnected.

> Obviously before it goes back in the van there will be a thorough
> reckoning of all factors leading to the overheating problem.

yes, I would do that first before the evidence is "tampered" with.
