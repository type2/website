>Do you think switching to hydraulic valves will give me more life in this

I love having a hydraulic valvetrain (the valves are the same, but you knew 
that!).  Many purists do not, so you won't get a definitive answer from 
this group.  You will not experience valve float until well over 5000rpm 
with hydraulics so don't worry about that.

>I've heard people say bad things about an 1835 in a bus...  that it will
>overheat more, true?   I figured building a decent T1 over the stock bore
>would help me out a lot, but I don't want one that overheats a lot.
>I don't really want to "go fast" I just want my bus to cruise on the freeway
>pretty well.

I don't think a large-displacement engine is what you need.  You simply 
need to spin a stock-ish displacement motor a little faster.  I agree with 
Theodore who suggested a small bump in bore with a stock-stroke 
counterweighted crankshaft and a cam that will allow the motor to have good 
volumetric efficiency at the higher rpms  You may need slightly larger 
valves too, but don't overdo it.  Large valves are way overrated.  If you 
were to go with the bigger motor (1835 or similar) then you would probably 
want to change your gear ratios to lower engine speed on the highway, and 
that seems to me like it would be a lot more trouble and expense.
