CAFE [corporate average fuel economy] figures into each model brought in. Asian cars have never had a problem
with it. Euro cars have a big problem with it.

"The current penalty for failing to meet CAFE standards is $5.50 per tenth of a MPG under the target value
times the total volume of those vehicles manufactured for a given model year.

Since 1983, manufacturers have paid more than $590 million in CAFE civil penalties. Most European
manufacturers regularly pay CAFE civil penalties ranging from less than $1 million to more than $20 million
annually. Asian and most of the big domestic manufacturers have never paid a civil penalty.

For MY 2002, five imported passenger car fleets, including BMW, Daimler Chrysler, Fiat, Lotus and Porsche are
projected to fail to meet the 27.5 mpg passenger car CAFE standard. In addition, two light truck fleets,
including BMW and Volkswagen are projected to fail to meet the light truck CAFE standard of 20.7 mpg. "
