Subject: Re: [T2] Plugging oil galley


I did the 2 behind the flywheel. I used 3/8" pipe thread plugs. They were
brass and used an allen/hex key to install. You will need a 3/8" pipe
thread tap, and the correct drill bit. Its a something 64ths. Ask when you
buy the tap, I also used some locktite. I chose pipe thread because it is
less likely to come undone. I also chose brass because it expands and
contracts at about the same rate as aluminum, or so I have been told. The
flaw could be that brass and aluminum don't exactly like each other too
much and I guess there could be a problem with electrolosis but this is not
a marine application so I don't think it will be a problem.
     In retrospect I don't know if I would do this on my next engine. I
have personally never heard of these plugs coming loose.  (ANYONE HERE?)
Also they were a lot thicker than I expected them to be. Also there are a
bunch more of these plugs all over the case and I dont see why they would
not be just as likely to fail. I would love to hear more about this subject
from experience not just theoretical speculation...
