Not really....though I'd recommend against the 356 if you plan on driving
in the rain.  Everyone I know of who's used these have had problems with
low traction in the wet.

The Bridgestone, which has a similar tread, is great in the rain. (I'm on
my first set with a dozen+ thousand miles on'm, plus a set on my moms
vanagon.)

If you plan to spend any amount of time OFF pavement, the 603v (and I
suspect the 356) is NOT the way to go.  They have virtually zero traction
in sand, grass, mud, gravel.  (Compared to my prior sets of Yoko 370s, as
well as some generic mixed passenger tires before those. (PO installed)
