I'll bet he means the differential. That is supposed to be full of
differential gear oil, not ATF. It may be that the previous owner filled
it with the wrong stuff, or maybe the ATF is somehow leaking in there
through a crack? Either way, this is bad news.

I'll bet he means the bell housing for the torque converter, which
attaches to the engine with 4 big bolts. This is a problem too. Sounds
like the bell housing got clobbered hard by something, maybe hard enough
to also cause the leak discussed above.

I generally agree with your comments. But I paid $300 for a (barely) used
auto tranny with a (used) torque converter a few years back. Mine came
from an '81 Vanagon and it still fit my '78 bus perfectly, except for one
weird bracket near the shifter that I threw away. Maybe I was overcharged
(I probably could have haggled more), but it was way cheaper than
rebuilding my old dead one. It generally costs $700-$1400 to rebuild a VW
auto tranny, depending on how much inside needs replacing.  They generally
can't tell you what the bill will be until they tear it down and see
what's in there. They should be good for 250K miles on a rebuild, I am
told. They often outlive several engines.

Your actual mileage may vary,
