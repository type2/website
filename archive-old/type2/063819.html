> WestonI'm not sure which model you have... but as I recall, my '73 had an 
accessory position that came on when the key was pushed in and turned 
"backwards" (in the same direction as you turn off the ignition). I seem 
to recall these busses had the ignition marked accordingly.

>From wiring numerous cars, you can go to the fuse box, and it's often 
marked with a radio prong. Also, if unmarked, you can find the proper 
place to splice by turning the ignition to the ACC position, & seeing 
which terminals/wires are hot, turn it off & see which go dead... and 
attach to one of those wires.  Most radios don't draw much power, so 
overloading a fuse is usually not a problem.

When you can't find the "proper" wire, this method will work.
