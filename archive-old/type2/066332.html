> The term "Deadhead" has been used by cross-country truckers since the
> 1930s to denote driving their rigs from one point to another with no cargo
> to pay the way.  A "Deadhead" sticker in modern etymological parlance
> signifies that the driver of the VW bus has made at least one such
> cargoless trip from one point to another with conscious intent.  The
> subcultural clue here is "conscious intent".  It speaks of a way of life
> where the "process" of driving is more important than its "content", and a
> "Deadhead sticker" is a way of signalling this consciousness.  The VW bus
> is chosen vehicle for those who are conscious, realized by only a few,
> understood by even fewer, and the "Deadhead" Sticker their overt
> acknowledgement of their status...
