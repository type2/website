Hi all,

Spent a deep and meaningful afternoon today underneath Sally, changing the
clutch cable.  Not easy when you've got a bad back (cue violins).  It all
started a couple of weeks ago when I was taking my daughter to a concert.
Pushed the clutch in, there was a faint 'ping' and the pedal felt looser
than before.  The following day I had a job interview and just as I started
off, there was another 'ping', now it was getting hard to change gear.  I
drove the 60 miles to the interview thinking "why me?", "today of all days"
etc, tightened the clutch in the car park of the hotel where the interview
was (in my best clothes of course).  It was right at the end of the
adjustment and I knew I was on borrowed time.  Anyway the interview went
fairly well and I got back OK.

The cable had broken inside the flexible conduit.  I presume this is a
common place for it to go, it's a straight run otherwise.  It was down to
the last 4 strands.

The second interview is on Friday about 150 miles away.  At leaset I will
feel more confident of getting there this time.
