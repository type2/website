>The motor was stripped of it tin, fan housing, intake, exhaust and

>bolt it to. We can score an intake manifold, carb and exhaust. We are 
>considering trying to run it for a short period without the tin in 
>place while on the stand. Can this be done safely and if so for how 
>long? Seconds or minutes?

If you set up a reasonably sized fan to play on the engine, you can run it
for as long as two minutes.  Outdoors, in winter, you can probably run it
longer than that.  If you live in a fair-sized town, or have a high school
with a good art program, or can find a potter, get a couple of pyro cones,
the things used to check temperatures in kilns.  350 F won't hurt an
aircooled engine, so put a 350 cone on the head before you start it.  When
the cone slumps, it's time to shut down.
