If you think you got a $100.00 bus then paint it with a brush.
If you think a little more of your bus, save your money untiul you can do
a job you'll be proud of.
My 83 Westfalia has some minor dents in the left quarter panel and a few
hail dents, I feel that it is better leaving these non-rusting dents
alone rather than using body filler or any other fix. I just have never
had any luck matching 18 year old paint and I don't care to nickname my
van pinto.
Restoring a vehicle isn't an overnight project, be patient and you can
enjoy your van as you work through the process of getting everything just
the way you want it.
I've seen a lot of vans at VW Camping and Swap meets with considerable
rust and most owners are more concerned about the safety and nechanical
reliability than the repairing the rust.
Many vans rust out from the inside from window leaks and other various
sources and all that must be addressed before you fill, paint or peform
any cosmetic bodywork. A bad paint job is a lot harder to get off than it
is to put on.
