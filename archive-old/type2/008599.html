You don't have to make them, stepped studs are a fairly standard part.
 You still have the problem of drilling and tapping the holes that you
would have with the heli-coil.
 
Another route is to find someone with an EDM (Electrical Discharge
Machining) machine.  This gadget eats the metal out electrically and
can even cut through a stuck tap or EZout, leaving the original
threads intact.

Problems like this are the reason that I advocate using copious
quantities of penetrating oil for several days before you try to
remove exhaust nuts.  Use the correct exhaust nuts when reassembling
and put a dollop of anti-sieze on the threads.
