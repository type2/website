Dave Wolfanger says about Flux Core Arc Welders:

> Adequate for tinkering- if that's all you ever want to do....
> However, once you start welding, the projects escalate very rapidly,
> and a toy welder is not going to do it. the gasless mig makes big splatter,
> nasty unprofessional welds, and requires the flux-core wire in expensive small
> spools. Go to a local welding supply where they sell equipment and ask for
> a demonstration ( they will let you try the machine) . you will soon see the
> difference.
> You don't need 220v. equipment for bus projects, and it isn't very portable.
> Try 110 v. welders, with and without inert gas.Thank the man, and then go buy
> it
> at Harbor Freight .

Then remember where the welding supply place is. You will need Argon, wire,
gloves...lots of consumables...and the best place to get it (sometimes the
only place--they don't have argon at Home Depot) is the welding shop.
