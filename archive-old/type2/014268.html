Subject: Cylinder Head Temp Sender Wire

I had the exact same problem when I recently installed my VDO CHT gauge.  I
was on a schedule, so I didn't stop to ask any questions.

This gauge/sender is a thermocouple.  The gauge is driven directly by the
voltage produced by the thermocouple - no external power required.  The 12V
connection at the gauge is for lighting only - the gauge will read the
temperature with or without the 12V connected.

Since the the signal from sender to gauge is a voltage at very low current,
I reason that a small increase in wire length/wire resistance has very
little (read, no significant) impact on the gauge reading.  To make things
as accurate and reliable as possible, I soldered all of the connections,
including the replacement push-on spade terminals I installed at the gauge
connection, and I insulated everything with heat shrink tubing.  I used 16
AWG hook wire from the local FLAPS.

My gauge starts to come up within about a minute after starting the engine.
This makes sense to me, since the low end of the scale is 100 deg F.  I
expect that as the outside temps get cooler, this will take slightly longer.
