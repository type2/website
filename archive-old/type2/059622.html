Are you kidding?  You're making a 5"x5" hole, just enough room to get the 
sender in and out.  Zero effect on "structural integrity".

After you replace the sender, make a cover from sheet steel and secure it 
down with self-tapping screws.  The cabin would be protected from gas 
entering in the event of a tank rupture, a very rare occurance.

By the way, this kind of repair/modification is approved by VW in Germany.
