The heat in a VW thread, with associated warnings, goes around again!

I am convinced that the Carver Propex heater is the ideal solution to the 
problem of heat in the bus. But these things are pricey.

In the archives, I noticed that someone had planned to install a Force 10 
boat cabin heater in a bus. These are $299 (about 1/2 the price of a 
Carver). They burn propane, and have a safety cutoff that kicks in if 
oxygen level "drops too low." (I wonder how low is too low.) West Marine 
claims that the furnace is "Built for use on the move," and is 
"unaffected by motion."

The downside is that you have to cut a 1" diameter hole in the roof for 
the flue. I can see it attached to a sheet of plywood bolted to the 
backside of the bulkhead on the passenger side. They're kinda perty -- 
brass and stainless steel. 16-1/2"X7-3/4"X7".

Seems like the way to go, to me. Anyone?
