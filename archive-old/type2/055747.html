Briefly as we can::The most common measure of gasoline quality guidelines are established by the ASTM. Currently, ASTM D 4814 is the standard for automotive spark ignition fuel. Gasolines are most commonly rated based on their antinock index (AKI)., a measure of octane quality. The AKI is a measure of a fuel's ability to resist knock (ping). The AKI of a motor fuel is the average of the research octane (RO) and motor octane (MO). R+M / 2. This is also the number posted on the yellow and black decal on the gas pump. The RO of a fuel is typically 8 to 10 numbers higher than the MO. For instance, 87 octane fuel has an RO of of 92 and an !
