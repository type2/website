Sounds like your fuel pump.  Its attached to the case with two socket or 
"allen" head bolts.  You can remove the pump pretty easily and it can be 
disassembled and rebuilt with not much more than a flat screwdriver.  If you 
can't find a rebuild kit, you might get it working again with a good 
cleaning.  

Its held together by about 10 screws.  It does have an internal filter that 
is removed by by unscrewing a "plug" screw on the side of the pump above the 
diaphragm. (not sure what size wrench, a small adjustable should do it.)

Be VERY careful about replacing it with an aftermarket electric pump.  You 
not only need the pump, but you may also need a pressure regulator to get the 
correct fuel pressure to your carbs.

You will need to run wiring, fuse it and you should also install a relay of 
some kind so that the pump will only run when the engine is running.  
Otherwise, if you were ever involved in a collision that ruptured a fuel 
line, the electric pump would continue to run and pump gas out the broken 
line until the key was turned off.  (Not a safe situation)

Some electric pumps are also very noisy.

A PO had done this to my 74.  I yanked the whole works and installed a used 
stock fuel pump that I cleaned up a bit.  IMHO, MUCH simpler, more dependable 
and "glitch" resistant.
