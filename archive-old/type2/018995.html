> >does it all mix and make 88, or will the gas have a natural tendencie to stay
> >seperate?
> 
> They mix.  In fact, when you get mid-grade 89 at the pump (at least in
> Texas, not sure about other places), what you are actually getting is a
> mixture of 87 and 93 octane gas.  The pump just pulls from both tanks, at a
> specific ratio (two to one in this case).  The gas stations don't want to
> have to produce, ship, and store three kinds of gas when they only really
> have to handle two, you see.
> 
> I didn't know this until one day when I was filling up with 89 and the gas
> was coming out real slow.  Reason being they were out of 87, which meant
> that I was getting straight 93 octane gas but only paying for 89.  Needless
> to say, I didn't mind waiting a few extra minutes for the tank to fill.  :)
