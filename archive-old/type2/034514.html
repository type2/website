I strongly disagree with the recommendation below for stock applications.
Perhaps it's appropriate for high-performance engines as Zac stated, but
aren't we then talking about increasing engine life from 2 races to 3?

As your engine heats up, the torgue you've applied is multiplied. I can't
remember where I read about over-torquing (Hoover, I *think*), but 10 lbs.
translates into A LOT more when the engine heats up. In aluminum cases,
such as the Type IVs, maybe a little more torque wouldn't hurt, but in the
magnesium cased, I wouldn't overtorque case or head nuts.

I'm not a VW mechanic, but I've read often that the factory recommended
retorque at 300 miles. In fact that is what the 300 mile new car warranty
service was for!
