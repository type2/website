Jaime:
In my town and I think in all of Iowa we have rather stringent laws
regarding floor drains in garages. When built they must have both sand
traps and grease-oil traps. This applies to residential garages as well-
if there is a floor drain, you must have this structure. This adds
probably $1000-$2000 to the building cost of a new structure, so most
hobbiest's garages don't have grease traps. What this also means is that
the majority of the grease-oil that is washed in a regular car wash is
disposed of at the landfill or recyclers and not loaded into the sewage
disposal system. I suspect it is so in most states, but if it makes you
rest easier, come to Iowa to wash your engine!(We also have good
pheasant hunting). :-)
