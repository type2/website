I would speculate that it might be a slow leak (most likely internal) in the 
fuel system.  When the engine's cold, the cold start valve will give a 
shot of gas (even with very low residual pressure) that get's things 
going.  When the engine's still very warm, that means only a short time 
has passed since it was running, and you still have enough residual 
pressure in the system.  It's that "in between" stage that's the problem; 
cold start valve is too warm to kick in, but you've lost most of the 
internal pressure in the system because the engine's sat for awhile.

You might want to test the system's fuel pressure.
