	Folks,
	Short form: 
	I backed our bus out of the drive way and it died. Plenty
of gas smell, points open and shut, wires on tight as far as I can
tell. What's wrong? Don't know, can't fix it, have AAA tow it to
the local VW dealer (Broadway VW, Oakland) since a) it can't be
very hard, it was working fine b) they say they still work on
aircooled c) I don't know any independant shops up here.

	IN THE END IT WAS ACTUALLY FIXED by (and I recomend) 
Bauer German Repair on University Avenue in Berkeley.

	TURNED OUT it was an ignition wiring problem- condensor
wire or some other minor wire that wasn't in my prior-problem
list and therefore I didn't check. Shame on me. I've got to have
a brief conversation with the mechanic (who's first language is NOT
English) tomorrow to find out exactly what was wrong.

	Long form:
	William Of Occam 1, William of Oakland -$400.

	Broadway VW couldn't figure it out, thought the carb was
flooding, suggested I needed a new carb since they don't recognize
31PICT as a stock item. $600 estimate to fix it. :(
	I said no way, check over the electricals and if your
theory is right, confirm it by pinching-off or disconnecting
and plugging the pump-to-carb hose. They said, "Fouled plugs, bad timing,"
and in a moment of weakness I let them do plugs/points/condensor/cap/rotor,
well heck, it never hurts to get that out of the way. They did 'em,
and said that they leaned it way out and got it to run. Come pick it up.
Nearly $300, bill, $40 for parts, the rest labor.

the completely trouble-free 8K miles we've had since it first came
to life, with the exception of that points problem... So I got 1.8 miles
or so from the dealership, about half-way home, on a steep hill, and it
lets loose with a couple of REALLY loud ka-bangs and dies utterly. Can't
be started. This is Wednesday, day before Thanksgiving, about 4:40pm.

	Well now I'm believing their story, I figure the carb float
or float valve has failed, so I stew all through Thanksgiving because
I don't have time to work on it... I feel SOooooooo sorry for myself,
family life and raising a 22 month old just don't seem to leave any
time for diddling around with the truck. I sure wish I could take time
to rebuild the carb, then it would run fine...
