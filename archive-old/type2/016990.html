The headlight relay in my '70 Westy failed before I purchased the
vehicle. Water entered the relay and rusted the pice of wire at the
bottom of the relay (on the inside) that is maybe a 3 to 4 inch piece of
wire bent into a hook-like shape on either end... it rusted it in two,
disintegrating the middle section of wire and most of one end.
	 One of the hook-like shapes was for a tension spring, and the other
was to do the switching of the actual contacts, back & forth in a rather
ingenious way...
	So, after the sacrifice of one thick paperclip and an hour of tedious
bending and trial/error later, the relay was functioning perfectly again
with a new wire piece. The water had fortunately damaged only the piece
of wire, and not the electrical components of the relay itself. 
	Once it was all back together, I Permatex-3H'ed the top of the relay so
that I could continue to use it without worry until the windshield leak
is fixed... 
	It's a temporary fix, but may come in handy sometime to get someone out
of a jam.
