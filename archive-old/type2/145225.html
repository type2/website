> My feeling: bad practice!  I don't know about latter-day VW wiring, but the
> earlier buses and bugs used un-tinned, stranded copper wire.  It corroded
> quickly inside the crimp lugs, resulting in voltage losses, especially if
> moisture was present.
>
> Dim headlights made it easy to identify on-coming VWs :-((
>
> It's not difficult to obtain #14 tinned, stranded wire; it's just a bit more
> expensive than solid, untinned.  FWIW older cars, even British cars, were wired
> with stranded wire -- soldered where we now see crimp-connexions.  Surely a
> matter of "value engineering" since the problems do not surface until a couple
> of years have elapsed and the warranty has timed-out.

Properly done crimp connections are airtight and are as robust as a
soldered connection. good crimpers are expensive though and usually handle
only one or few specific connector types. the typical crimper does a
mediocre job of it.
I dont think the degradation of old vw harnesses is usually the crimp
itself but the connectors corroding.
