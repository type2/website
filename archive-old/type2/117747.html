Seann,

This is one of those times when I actually use rtv on my bus.

Had one on a convertible that was the same way.  I found that
the valve cover gasket behind the mirror around the edges
would make it even with the chrome surface all around(yours may be 
different).

I glued the mirror to the cork and back all at the same time making sure 
that it stayed cetered in the back and even all around.

The next day I either used rtv or regular houshold "lifetime" silicone 
caulk in white.

Wasn't worried about it being a perfect application cause I just waited 
a coule of days for it to dry and then used a razor knife to cut the 
exceess off even with the surface of the mirror.

I doubt that anyone could tell how I had done it and unless they were
specifically examining my mirrors, no one would *ever* notice.

You might try www.mcmastercarr_dot_com

They have some of the most amazing things that you can not find anyplace 
else...including several kinds of those "good" hose clamps that we were 
discussing the other day.
