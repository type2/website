> > The easiest way to fit the sliding door seal is to undo the two bolts
> > that hold the rear sliding hinge mechanism onto the door (these are to
> > be seen from INSIDE of the bus just below the window). Then with the
> > door ajar, WHILE SUPPORTING THE DOOR (bloks, axle stand, jack,

> > gap, slide the hinge back into the door. In my experience this is much
> > easier than removing the track cover and takeing the slider off of the
> > track.

> Err... Bob....
> 
> On US spec buses there are four bolts.  Two are accesible from inside the
> bus, at the rear of the door about half way up. If your bus is a passenger
> model and very original they may be covered with black plastic caps.  The
> other two are inside the rear lip of the door.  Pop the door open and look
> in the gap at the rear.  All four are 10mm heads.  After they are out,
> removal of the hinge goes as you say.
> 
> On replacement, it is important to center the door in its opening at the
> rear for the seal to do its job.  A two-person job, alas, with someone
> holding the door in position while the second snugs the bolts.
