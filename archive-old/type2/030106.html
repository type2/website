Volks,

Last week I asked for suggestions of books to bring when I went across the
United States next month.  I recieved a number of excellent suggestions,
and will definitely be bringing my copy of the LOTR trilogy, all four of
them[1] and Blue Highways!

Here are the responses I received:

John (Baggins111_at_aol_dot_com) wrote:

> I recommend The Lord of the Rings trilogy. Other than the fantasy part
> about hobbits and wizards and elves and such, it's a good "travelling"
> book. Ones to curl up with when you're off the road and need to wind
> down.

Tony (ARoman4047_at_aol_dot_com) wrote:

>          "Blue Highways" - William Least Heat Moon -
>          "Manifold Delights" - ? - The Joys of Cooking on a vehicle's
>                                    engine...
>          "Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance" - William Pirsig
>          "The Crying of Lot 49" - Thomas Pynchon

Michael Benthin (MUTS) wrote:

>    I'll root amongst the books; there a series out there called Free
> Campgrounds USA, broken up into regions.  Found it useful biking across
> the country back in 1984, but removed sections from it as I was
> traveling light.

>      Having a Woodall's or Good Sam Camping guide helped me lots of
> times- Woodall's has a state map at the beginning of each state section;
> Good Sam's has color maps in one section at the beginning.  They do list
> State Parks and Forest campgrounds.  

Jack Stafford (sunbus) wrote:

> I've had good luck with the "Let's Go - USA" book.
> It is written with the hitchhiking, backpacking and
> on-a-budget type traveller.
> AAA road atlas, because I get them free as a member.

F. W. Stoermer wrote:

> Especially if you plan to do much camping, the DeLorme state atlases,
> based on USGS topographical maps, are very useful.  Most of the
> warehouse stores [Sam's Club, COSTCO] sell them for ~$10.00 - half the
> cover price.  I've only used their Washington and Oregon atlases, but
> with their exhaustive gazetteers and large format maps they are
> definitely superior to the more typical Rand McNally or AAA
> publications.

bren wrote:

> My personal favorite (which, by the way, does live in the book rack
> behind my head) is Karl Franz's "People's Guide to Mexico".
> Informative, a fun read, and it's published by John Muir Press.
