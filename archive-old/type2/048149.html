The sender is too big and would require cutting a new hole in the tin to get
it in. I bought the extension adapter. You take the end that threads into
the hole where the current sensore is and use some plumbers tape (teflon) to
get a nice seal and no oil leakage. The other end requires an adapter (comes
with extension) that then connects to the sensor. Do not use the tape on
this end because the sensor must be grounded to work. There is also a
mounting strap for the sensor and a sheet metal screw. You can mount to the
engine tin or I mounted mine to the fan housing with an existing screw.
