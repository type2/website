Chet,

First you have to ground the plug or else no sparks will fly. Hold the
threads to a ground then crank.  This should be a problem if your getting
spark off the wire. First loosen the Philips head hose clamp that mounts the
elbow from the air flow meter and just pull it to the side and crank it
over. If then it starts right up check out your air flow meter, refer to
Bentley. If not, start with the basics tune up stuff. Remember go Bosch or
go home. New points cap rotor condenser. A valve job is easy and a good
thing to know how to do, get new gaskets if you do this. Later good luck I
just spend a few days on a fellow listies 77 and we got almost every thing
except the head temp sensor was loose. Nothing beats a good mechanic well
except maybe $200 bucks still in your pocket. Have fun and oh a few beers
also helps keep your head in the game when wrenching on stuff for the first
time.
