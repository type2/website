Arnaud,

I don't currently have gauges installed, so my observations are even less
scientific.  But my experience is very similar: I definitely agree that uphill
grades and outside air temperatures make a *big* difference in top speeds when
keeping the engine cool is a priority.  My Bus will run cool at 65 ad
infinitum if it's cold outside.  If it's not crisp outside, I'm not at all
comfortable cruising at 65.  More like 55-60, or 55 period if it's warm
outside.   So far, I've never driven my Bus in temperatures above 80.  I drove
to L.A. last winter, kept it at 55 through the San Joaquin valley, and the
engine stayed satisfyingly cool.

- Miles
