John:

I would guess (and it's only a guess) that "deck height" is not something 
that you'd ordinarily find in a manual.  After all, you take new P&C's, 
new rods, new heads, and then bolt them all together, and what could 
possibly go wrong, right?

I mean, it's not like a crankshaft, where the "engineer approved" way of 
rehabing it is to grind the journals; since there are "standard" 
undersizes for reground journals, these might reasonably be listed in a 
comprehesive repair manual.  (BTW, the Haynes has the journal sizes 
wrong and they're not even mentioned in the Bentely.  So much for manuals.)

But there's no factory-approved "shorter" or "longer" rods, pistons, etc. 
so I would think "deck height" would be one of those obscure measurements 
that might be part of factory blueprints, and not show up anywhere else.

But the truth is that cases get reconditioned, replacement rods, pistons, 
etc. get made more-or-less in spec, and at some point the concept of deck 
height becomes important to a rebuilder.  (And, maybe, it becomes 
important sooner to a hot-rodder who has to build to tighter tolerances.)

Looking through the archives I see .035" mentioned as the actual deck 
height of Mark Stephens "zero deck height" engines, but it looks like 
other modifications get made to the head in order to keep compression 
ratios reasonable.

If you're down at the .035" range then something's going on to get it 
there - like a machined case or too-long rods - and I would probably opt 
to bump it back out to the middle of the range Wilson specifies.

If you do manage to find an honest-to-god factory spec for deck height 
please let me know as I'm currently rebuilding a couple of 2.0l engines.
