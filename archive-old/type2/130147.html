On Monday, August 30, 2004, at 11:23AM, Sharpe, Steven
<sharpes_at_uncw_dot_edu> wrote:

>Lets think about this. These cold air intake kits are, by and large,
>for water pumpers. With a water pumper, air comes through the radiator,

>gets hot, and then gets sucked into the carb (or throttle body). A cold

>air intake for this engine makes sense.

If you look at the shape of those intakes, they are in a position where
the filter is down low and out of the way of the radiator with an
opening to the side. I don't know why they are called cold air intakes
because the OEM units are often in the exact same spot. Cool air intakes
would be a more appropritate name: the aftermarket versions have a tuned
tube and it makes a "cool" sound.
