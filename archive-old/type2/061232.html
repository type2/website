Chris Binder writes:
>  The engine runs very well.  It has an electrical problem that could be
> solved by rewiring the van.  This sounds horrific, but with a little time
> it would be rather simple.  The problem is that it does not charge, and it
> blows regulators, and it does have a good generator.
  
> Can such matters be sucessfully dealt with or is this a perpetual headache
> in the making? Past threads (archives) deal with specific problems but how
> about the general sort?  

If this is all that is wrong with the electrical system (ie. lights 
and gauges, turn signals, etc. work okay) then rewiring the whole bus 
sounds pretty drastic.  The generator circuit is fairly straight 
forward and mostly confined to the engine compartment.  If the 
generator and regulator are charging okay then there is probably a 
short somewhere else, possibly a wire intermittently rubbing against 
the body.  There are techniques to see if there is something drawing 
current when the ignition and all is off.  If this is the case, you 
can further narrow it down by removing fuses until you determine 
which circuit is giving you problems, then track it down from there.
