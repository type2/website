On Thu, 25 Oct 2001, jeremy norris wrote:

>
> On Vacuum Distributors:
> I have seen two different kinds, one has the single hose fitting and the
> other has 2 (front and back). My 77 2.0L has the two hose kind, but the
> front hose was disconnected for 4 years and it ran great, i finally got it
> hooked up right, and it runs about the same. I have always wondered what the
> main difference is on the two hose vs. one hose dizzy. Is there an
> advantage? I assume the one hose vac advance pulls more air from the diz
> shaft area.. anyways, food for thought.

The single vacuum and dual vacuum distributors have the same advance under
power, so they should yield the same performance.

The difference is that the dual-vacuum distributors had an additional
vacuum retard that futher retarded the spark when the engine was
decelerating.  This slightly improved burning and reduced emissions.

Either vacuum distributor is better suited for a bus application, as they
allow the engine to pull smoothly from a lower RPM than the centrifugal
distributor.
