On the way south-bound to college back in `74 we had a peace of cap
smack our vehicle. We heard the cap pop and almost immediately heard and
felt the impact.
The interesting thing was that the tractor trailer that lost the cap was
on the other side of the divided interstate traveling north-bound, and
that the piece of cap traveled from the far north-bound lane, across the
other two north-bound lanes, across the grass median, across the two
south-bound lanes on our side of the interstate, and THEN hit our
vehicle traveling in the remaining south-bound lane, the piece of cap
remaining airborne during it's entire trajectory.
Very impressive. I'd hate to think what that piece of cap would have
done had we been along side of the tractor trailer. (Makes one
appreciate the US ban on hitchhiking/bicycling on interstates.) After
that experience, I usually choose to avoid driving close behind or right
along side of tractor trailers.
