Subject: RE: [T2] Voltmeter and wiring and sticky readings.

A voltmeter would not need a particularly large wire, but maybe that's what
the installer (or PO) happened to use. If the needle sticks, that's almost
certainly due to mechanical reasons in the gauge. The postion of the needle
will not have any real effect on the actual bus voltage (pun intended) or
current. No current should flow through a normal voltmeter. I don't think
you have anything to worry about.

An ammeter measures current and would have the current flowing through or
near it, but your gauge is a voltmeter if it has a 9 volt mark.
