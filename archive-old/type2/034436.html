Here are a couple of sites that talk about gear oil:

http://www.lubrizol_dot_com/referencelibrary/readyreference/15-gears/gearclass.h
tm
If you look at the 1st thing listed for GL 4, it says 'manual
transmissions'.
This tells me that this is the 1st choice for a tranmission.
If you look at the last thing listed for GL 5, it says 'may also be used in
manual transmissions'.
Hmmm, sounds like last resort.....

http://www.vtr_dot_org/maintain/lubricants-redline.html#gear-oil
The second paragraph is very informative:
"Gear oil is acidic, motor oil is alkaline. Gear oil needs very high wear
protection- Extreme Pressure
(marked as EP). Therefor, it has a very high sulfur and phospor content.
Sulpur and phosphate reactions
start at a lower temperature, and gear oil has much more additives than
motor oil. This additive is corrosive
to copper bearings and bronze synchro rings."
Scroll down, GL 5 is " high sulfur content".

Is GL 5 a modern day replacement for GL 4??  I don't think so!
Read the back label of a bottle of GL 5. It says "can be used in manual
transmissions IF the transmission
requires GL 5". VW transaxles require GL 4. Only the final drive of the auto
trans requires GL 5.
