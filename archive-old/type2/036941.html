This doesn't make sense.  Pushrods seldom fail.  It would only be about
$20 to replace them all, and it could be done in an hour of shop time.

Since this is such a simple repair, it would be to the seller's advantage
to get this fixed himself and have a good-running bus to sell.  Since he
wants to sell the problem to you, I would be suspicious of his 
information.

Considering that '79s have one of the more complex engines, I would insist
that the bus be in good running order before I plunked down my money.  If
it isn't in good running order, consider it to be the same as a bus
without an engine at all.
