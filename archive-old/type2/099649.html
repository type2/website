The little propane heater I mentioned has means for attaching it to a wall 
- presumably 5 feet off the floor.

It won't heat a cold garage understand. It's total output is less than 10K 
BTU. However you can do what I do with it now and put it on the work bench 
near you.

I have a big kerosene heater I use when I want to heat the whole garage but 
it stinks a bit and I would not want it in my Westy or in my house.

I do not know of any electric and gas heaters combos. I have two space 
heaters I will brag about. The first is a tiny Duracraft space heater about 
10 x 10 inches. It has a built in blower, 1500w and good for small spaces. 
It is a ceramic heater, several years old (8 years?) from K-mart or 
Wal-Mart or some place. Less than $30. In the house in my son's room we 
have a large oil filled heater with thermostat (Italian made) that we use 
on it's lowest power and thermostat setting because that room is on a 
corner of the house and cools off quite a bit after midnight. We have to 
keep his door closed because the cats will try to sleep with him and he 
doesn't like that (wakes him up) - he's a typical 2 year old I guess.

We used these oil filled heaters alot in Italy when I lived there and they
