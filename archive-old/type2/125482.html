I bought the bus w/tranny, the OE engine, a spare and some other misc. parts
a while back.
The guy I bought it  from did the dissas'y and I bought it to re-assemble.
My thought is that the PO dropped the engine and then thought to remove the
throttle linkage.
I found a strange bent-up shaft with a flange and bolt in one end and a
broken stud in the other when unpacking the parts.
I just set it in the not-needed parts pile till I got the engine in and
couldn't see how the throttle cable was going to reach.
That is when I went back to find that weird bent-rod-looking thing.
I had to mill the rod end a bit and weld a cap screw to the end of it to get
it back to near OEM.
That all seems to be going well but now I am trying to replicate the factory
bends in the linkage.
After the bends the PO put in it I am not sure which ones are intentional.

As to the try another question...I tried everything that I could find from
my "nuts can" and nothing worked.
I will try the BD guys next I guess.
It is, as you said, the linkage on the side, same spot as the shift linkage
arm.
Seems to be on some sort of concentric shaft.
It sticks up while the shifter lever hangs down.
