>From the data (5.5", 39mm offset) and (6.0", 30mm offset), it would seem
that most of the additional rim width goes outboard. More precisely, if my
math is correct, of the additional 13mm, 15 are added outboards and 2 are
substracted from the inside.

I also vaguely remember reading about 7.0" rims for the vanagon and I
*believe* the offset was 23mm. Compared to the 5.5" rims, this implies an
additional rim width of 38mm, 35 of which are added outboards and 3 of which
are added "inboards".

Hence it would seem to me that the "correct offset" is simply more of
function of what _fits_ in the wheel wells, rather than being a function of
what is "right" for the bearings. Comments?
