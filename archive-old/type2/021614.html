Probably one of the best things to do is clean out your fuel injectors
if you haven't already.  There are shops that will service your
injectors and get them spraying nicely.  Another thing is to run Chevron
through your system (if they sell that gas near you).  I have heard many
people swear by the Techron that's used in the gasoline.  Otherwise, get
yourself some quality fuel injector additive and give that a try.  Best
bet is the shop option, though, cuz they clean the screens and test
them.

Personally, I'd be weary of getting another FI system unless it was
designed for a particular engine and tested thoroughly to get the
correct settings.  Or, if it had some kind of system allowing you to
dynamically change the mixture settings for various rpm, air pressure,
manifold vacuum, etc (whatever design it uses) settings.. 

Then, even to set that up, you'd need to either purchase a quality
air/fuel mixture meter (expensive) or go get it dyno tested.. a bunch of
times....

I'd just clean up what you have real nice, and make sure all your
connections are good.  One thing that I did was coat all my contacts
(including inside the AFM) with ProGold.  I have no affiliation with
them (caig products) but I know LOTS of audio engineers and electricians
that use it all the time.  It cleans contacts and also lubricates them
as well; something that can't be said for most electronic cleaners. 
Plus, it also seals up corroded connections and fun stuff like that...
check 'em out:

http://www_dot_caig_dot_com/

Hope that helps!
