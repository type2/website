What year bus do you have?  Maybe we are talking apples and oranges here.
Steve and I have '73 busses, which have different trans and final drive
ratios than earlier and later busses.  If your bus is earlier, your shift
points are probably normal.  If it's '72 or later, you are definately
babying it.

I agree completely with Steve - aircooleds need RPM to stay cool.  The
heavier your right foot is in it, the more RPM you need.  I wouldn't feel
comfortable giving it even 3/4 throttle without at least 3500RPM on the
tach, OTOH I do putter around town at 35-40 in 4th just barely breathing on
the gas (and keeping an eye on the temp gauges) because it saves fuel.
