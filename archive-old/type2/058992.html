Hi folks,

With the few miles I have done so far in the new bus the mexican 1600 FI
aircooled engine seems to be good and strong - Beetles UK from whom I bought
my camper towed a Beetle at 70mph behind a bus without any problems. I caught
my self at 80mph whilst overtaking without having to push the engine hard.

A major consideration in retro-fitting one of these to a bus is heating. 
If your country / state requires you to have everything present for the year
of the engine then there is no room for heat exchangers an auxiliary heater is
a must.

the standard exhaust on these engines is as follows....J tubes leading to a
collector, lambda sensor positioned just after merge of pipes, catalytic
converter to left of center in front of rear valence, pipe to left turning
forward, exhaust box to left of engine, pipe turns right underneath gearbox,
pipe turns rearward, exhaust box to right of engine, short tailpipe ending
under the battery tray..

I am told that this will meet all USA emmisions standards.

But if your local rules will let you dispense with the catalytic converter
then a standard exhaust with the lambda sensor in the tail pipe could be used
alowing the retention of the heat exchangers.

The tinware under the engine is of a much heavier gauge of metal giving strong
protection to the pushrod tubes. 

Also there is NO thermostat - I suppose thats because the FI system will be
measuring the temps in a more sophisticated way and can comensate for a cold
engine much better then a choke and carb
