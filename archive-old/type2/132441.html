Hmmm.. The middle of the coil is actually the positive side of a separate
coil of wire in the coil. It is really a transformer, with the primary side
having fewer wraps of wire than the secondary. Here's a link that helps:

http://www.physlink_dot_com/Education/AskExperts/ae427.cfm

In our coil, the secondary winding is grounded to the case.

> Pulling the center wire off the dizzy with the motor
> turning, by starter or by hand,and ignition on will yield a spark
> when the points are open.

But not constantly open. The high voltage is created when the magnetic field
in the primary winding builds then collapses, inducing current in the
secondary; the magnet has to be moving. The points have to be closed then
open.
>
> Pulling the center wire out of the dizzy and grounding it should
> yield a spark if the points are opened manually of by action of the
> motor turning the dizzy. Yellow spark=dying coil, no spark=deal coil.
>

> the coil should bring the light on every time the points open which
