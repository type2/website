I have not used Hammerite on a Bus yet, but do have experience with it in a
hostile environment.

We have a heat register in our bathroom that's right next to the tub/shower.
It gets a bit wet every time someone showers, and of course cycles between
cool and warm as the furnace cycles.  I fought rust and peeling paint on
that register for over 25 years, and no finish lasted more than ~a year
without rusting.

Six years ago I hauled that register out to the shop yet again and cleaned
it down to bare steel with wire brushing and steel wool.  A few spots of
oxidation remained deep in grooves of the stamped louvers, but it wasn't
worth the trouble or expense to have it sand-blasted or acid dipped.

I painted it with 2 coats of bronze Hammerite [spray can], following their
instructions closely.  The register still looks as if it's freshly painted,
with no sign of rust or any other defect in the finish.
