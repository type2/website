This month's VW Trends had an interesting, although limited, article on 
early delivery VWs (bus & PU). Of interest to me was the 50s model van, 
painted red/orange, w/ the VW emblem replaced by the CocaCola "cap" 
(round sign, says Drink CocoCola)... used in England to deliver product.

Another interesting accessory for early single cabs was the delivery 
cap. From the rear, it looked like a windowless van (the rear panel 
was removed, and sometimes the side panels, making the PU into a 
flatbed, w/ this unit bolted to the top). On the sides, a canvas cover, 
perhaps w/ wooden stiffeners, could be lowered to cover the produce. To 
tie the rear panel to the front, a "banner board" is attached, which 
usually carried the name of a softdrink company, or such.

The vans/PUs were delivered in white primer.

They also showed one of the VW van ads, which ran frequently in the late 
60s. VW had a terrific ad campaign going back then... nothing like the 
Farfromthinkin ads of the later campaigns. This particular ad showed a 
65 or 66 Deluxe, w/ a bunch of nuns climbing onboard. The banner read 
"Mass Transportation".

The marketing people of VW were very savvy to the needs of Europeans, 
but I don't think they ever penetrated the US market properly. With few 
exceptions (such as the recently posted ambulance) the US did not see 
the commercial value of VWs. I suppose this is partially due to the 
reliability of US manufactured PUs, but perhaps more because of the 
mindset against VWs in general.
