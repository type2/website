> Why install a whole alarm, though?  Just put in the flashing red light!

The procedure is described in the 17th edition (and perhaps others) of
Muir.  Look under the electrical chapter at the end of the book.  I
think it can be done for under $10.  

Another option is to make your stereo easy to take out.  Most new
stereos come with a bracket that you bend prongs out of to fix it into
the dash.  Don't do that, and if a thief comes along and wants your
radio, then at least he doesn't have to destroy your dash in the
process.  Of course you do have to find some way to "fix" the stereo so
that it doesn't shake or vibrate too much when you're driving (maybe
some foam?).

The other option of course is to get a theft deterent radio, such as one
with a removable faceplate.  Not a guarantee, but more likely to be left
unmolested, especially if you don't do something stupid like leave the
face on it (or in the glovebox).  A bit of a pain, but less expensive
than replacing a radio.

Just a few thoughts.
