On Sat, 25 Jan 2003, Jamie wrote:

> Water injection makes an interesting thought,, you are putting oxygen into the
> combustion chamber, [H2O] effectively raising the compression ratio as water
> does not compress,  removing HP from the steam that is created and at basically
> the same time,, cooling the gasoline. Too bad the water doesn't burn!

Water is hydrogen that has already been burned.  Neither the hydrogen or
oxygen will do anything to the combustion process as they are already
strongly combined with each other.

The purpose of water injection is to absorb the energy spikes that cause
detonation.  This allows the use of much greater compression ratios or
boost in racing engines.  Unfortunately, the energy lost by flashing the
water to vapor is not recovered as power.  For racing, you don't care
about the extra fuel used, but this is not acceptable for street use.
