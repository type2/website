She's running again.  Thanks for the advice and ideas.  Let me say 
that, as with so many things in life, I'm not quite sure what happened.

degrees.  I decided to switch back to the 009 and see what happened, 
and when I did, the engine ran as expected, and the static light went 
on and off as usual.  The new SVDA came with points and a condenser, 
and I was going to be rational and hook up the SVDA stock before 
switching to the CompuFire, but given the problem I had at first, I 
decided to go for broke and install the CompuFire.  Then I put the SVDA 
back in the engine.  And it worked just fine.  I set the initial timing 
with the static light, as per the instructions that came with the 
distributor.  Then I started the engine, warmed it up, and fine-tuned 
the timing with the gun.

distributor seated all the way down (and thus the rotor wouldn't turn)
