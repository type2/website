On Tue, 4 Mar 2003, Greg Potts wrote:

> My understanding of the term "doghouse" is that it refers to the large 
> cooling shroud fitted on top of the engine to house the fan at the back 
> of the generator/alternator on type 1 engines. This is a common usage 
> with the many type1 engine users I know, most of whom have buses 
> without the 71's oil cooler. If this practice varies by region, it's 
> news to me.

pre-71 upright engines have a cooling shroud which, if viewed from the 
top, is rectangular, with the "XXXX" being the oil cooler:

	FIF
==========================
XXXX
==========================
	RIR

"doghouse" refers to the 1971 fanhouse, which removes the oil cooler 
to an isolated outcropping attached to the back of the fan housing:

=====
XXXX|	FIF
==========================

==========================
	RIR


The oil cooler being "in the doghouse".  This results in separate cooling 
air going to the oil-cooler than the 3/4 head.  Unfortunately, the 
oil-cooler outlet still blows over the #3 head, but this still ends up 
being better for cooling.
