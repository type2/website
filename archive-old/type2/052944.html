Dear Jon,

Oh boy. I had a big mystery like this a year or so after I bought Gus (2L.
AFC fuel injected pancake). Nobody could figure it out. Gus would backfire
and buck and finally lost power to a point where he would barely go 30 mph
in 2nd, 3rd or 4th gear Out of frustration I started poking around in the
engine compartment and found a vacuum hose that was barely touching a
connector it was supposed to be snuggly fastened to. I smooshed the hose (a
smallish 14mm OD fabric covered hose) back on to the plastic "T" connector
which goes to the air cleaner (I'm pretty sure) and Gus fired right up and
zipped around the parking lot.  The mechanic (NOT the redoubtable Dr.
Jimmy-way before Dr. Jimmy) freaked until I showed him the hose I had
reconnected. He said that it was a vacuum hose. I don't know if he was right
but I *always* check that hose when Gus has a loss of power problem and yep,
sure every time that hose is loose (finally put a radiator clamp on it-see
chapter IX entitled  "Huff's Bobby Pin and Duct Tape Mechanical Repair" in
that totally forgettable tome  "Condom Plumbing and other Quick Fixes")
Check *all* your vacuum connections. I'm keeping my fingers crossed that
that's all that's wrong.
