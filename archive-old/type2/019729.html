Hey Randall,
I don't know who wrote that article that you quoted, but it sounds kinda
hokey to me.  I also don't know what they mean by the #3 retard cam and
meltdown and all that jazz, but I could just be spacey :)

I had the same problem as you.  When I got my engine, the wires were
reversed on my dizzy cap and it confused me at first.  Later I found out
that whoever rebuilt the dizzy put the drive dog on 180 degrees off. 
Steve Dolan was able to get the pin out easily (under the retaining
spring), but I had to use a hydraulic press to get mine out.  If you
aren't very mechanically savvy, I wouldn't mess with ANYTHING! including
the gear.  According the list, if you mess up, you can drop  parts into
your crank and then you'll really be hating life.

I would either pull out your dizzy and take off the drive dog and spin
it 180 degrees and put it back on or just deal with the crossed wires. 
Here's the reversed order that you should have:

1 4 
2 3 

instead of the normal:

3 2
4 1

double check my numbers here making sure you have the correct firing
order.  I believe it's 1-4-3-2.. I don't have my book in front of me. 
(SOMEONE CORRECT ME IF i'M WRONG HERE!)

It's no big deal.. Just cosmetic.  Just put the dizzy back in just like
it was normal, except for the wierd wires.  The diaphram shouldn't have
any clearance problems either.  I wouldn't wrack your brain over this
one!
