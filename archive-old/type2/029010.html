Go with the gas heater.  Can't get enough heat electrically, the van only
has about 75 amps from the alternator,a hair dryer, for instance, draw's
1500 watts.

The heater you want is the BA6.  If the late model bus you are going to pull
it from is stock, this iw what it will have.  I have no idea its value, make
your best deal.  Its underneath vans from 74 through 84, as they were in
vanogons as well.  Make sure you know how to pull it, and that you get all
the bits.  There is an article on this at www.type2_dot_com  Look in the
Library, under "Heating", and you will find an article on installing a BA6
out of a wrecker.

Biggest drawback with BA6 or BN4 for that matter, is that you can't get ANY
new parts, so if you put one in, start hording spare components.

As for safety, it will be safe if you put in the proper fuses and the safety
relay.  In otherwords, examine the  manual and put it in stock set up.  the
safety system has a number of redundancies.  From there, its a matter of
maintaining it properly, particularly keeping it from rusting out, keeping
the combustrion chamber and glow plug clean, and making sure there is a
working exhaust pipe as the air from the exhaust is very hot and can cause
damage if not properly ported away.  You may need to fabricate something.
It is important that the body of the heater is intact without rust, dents or
cracks.  If so, it should be fine, but be your own judge.

There is a manual for the BA6 online at:
http://www.vintagebus_dot_com/eber/index.html

Test each component before you buy the thing.  The manual will be of
assistance in doing this.

Also, search the archives, as this is a common subject in the posting.
