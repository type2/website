Yes, the stock fuel pump will be more than enough to supply your dual
carbs.  Unless you're building a drag racing engine the stock setup is
fine.

When you get ready to install your alternator pump, be sure to measure
the pushrod protrusion to ensure that the pushrod length and spacer
length are correct.  If this protrusion is too short the pump flow
will be less.  If too long, the pump will be damaged.

I normally install alternator pumps anytime I change pumps,  They work
fine with generators and you never know when you might want to change
to an alternator.  As I understand it, you _can_ fit an alternator
with a generator pump, but then the pump cannot be removed without
removing the alternator, kind of like painting yourself into a corner!
