Ten years?  I can't see keeping an engine on the shelf for that long.  If
the crank, cam, or cylinders began to rust from simple condensation it could
cause pitting which would obviously be a bad thing.  Even if you oiled the
engine down to prevent this and turned it over by hand every few months it
would not be worth the hassle over 10 years.  I would be even more concerned
about the seals drying out and shrinking.  This just sounds like a bad idea.
The VW of Canada engines may has sat for a while, but I seriously doubt that
it was by choice.  Take the $1,000-$1,500 you would spending building the
spare engine yourself and put it in the bank.  Ten years from now, and
anytime between now and then, you'll have the spare grand to build an
engine.  Take care.
