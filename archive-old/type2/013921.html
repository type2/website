> I missed out on the earlier thread, and I've never heard of this super
> stuff you're talking about, but here's my opinion on regular (Permatex, for

> I use it on everything:  spark plugs, body hardware, exhaust nuts, lug
> bolts and nuts, etc, etc.  I just hate breaking off rusted bolts, and
> anything which might prevent that is OK w/ me.  And when all threads are
> lubed w/ anti-seize, I have a better chance of getting meaningful torque
> readings.  I just put a brand new set of factory 16" alloys on my T100
> truck, and I sure wanted these megabuck wheels to be properly torqued.

I tend to agree, although I never use it on lug nuts/bolts.  I've never had one of these sieze on me and I really don't want to make them 
easier to get off!

On spark plugs, I make _very_ sure that the anti-sieze never gets close to the insulators or electrodes on either end.  I use a small dab 
located at least one thread in from the end.  Some have reported  misfires while using antisieze on spark plugs, but I've never experienced 
it.  Plug removal is always a fingers-only exercise once the plugs are broken loose.
