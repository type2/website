If you're trying to get exact measurements, relying on an automatic
shutoff isn't the way to go.  They are notoriously inconsistant.

It is better to set a definite level in the filler and then fill the
tank to this level each time.  The level need not be the very top, but
can be a few inches down inside the filler.

Personally, I love the VW's big filler.  After fighting with the
"standard" US pre-restrictor fillers and even a couple of those
horrible  restricted fillers I'm happy to have that huge easy-to-fill
hole on the bus.  My father's new Mercury has a long narrow filler
tube that shuts off _3_ gallons before the tank is full!  Sheesh!
