> not get thin under the heat and pressures that are generated.  Force the
> grease all the way throught the CV by hand."
> 
> I haven't done quite as many CV joints as Rick, but I do have a nifty little 
> trick for doing the actual greasing of CV joints.  Place your new (or 
> absolutely clean old) CV on a piece of plastic wrap.  Just the usual kitchen 
> variety plastic wrap (like Saran wrap).  You should have enough plastic wrap 
> (about a foot of it) to completly bundle up the CV joint.  Then add a big 
> dollop of moly (CV) grease and close up the plastic wrap around the CV joint. 
>  Secure with a rubber band.  THEN work the grease through the joint.  You'll 
> find that you can actually get "pressure " on the grease and force it into 
> all the little cavities between the balls.  It makes for a much better job 
> than doing it "freehand".  What's more you keep your hands clean in the 
> process.
