Subject: Re: [T2] Latest heaters for Westy use?

On Fri, 28 Oct 2005, courtney hook wrote:

> So the latest rage I had read about was the Black Cat by Coleman, but that was a few years ago. What is easy and safe to use these days for my 77 Westy? I usually carry a ceramic plug heater, but what is there for propane/butane/ etc?

The coleman catalytic heaters are weak.  I have a Procat which is
advertised as being 3000BTU.  This translates to 900 watts or about the
output of a mild blow-drier.  At BBB one of these would warm the interior
of my bus after quite a while when it was 50 degrees outside, but I
wouldn't depend upon it for colder climates.  You also have to deal with
the condensation and venting issues.  I wouldn't run one of these while
sleeping.

You'd actually be better off to get a Coleman 2-mantle propane lantern.
It will provide both light and heat, and also makes more heat than the
heater!  Same caution about sleeping, though.

There are stronger propane heater solutions out there such as the Buddy
heater.

Cadillac solution is the Espar Airtronic 2:

http://www.espar_dot_com/htm/Specs/airtronic2.htm

which is the modern version of the Eberspacher gas heater used for
heating truck sleepers and boats.  Bring your Visa card, though!  About
$1300!.
