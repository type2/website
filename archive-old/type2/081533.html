Subject: [T2] Downshifting and RPMs (was: Acceptable speeds)

There is only so much air you can blow through a straw.  Maybe at speeds
over 40 in third there is not enough air going through and things overheat.

If my '73 1700 at 3/4 throttle will not pull a hill at 45 in fourth, I
change to third and do 35.  If it wont pull the hill at 30 in third I change
to second and do 23.  If it wont pull the hill at 17 in second I change to
first and do 12.  If it wont pull the hill at 8 in first I change to reverse
and back down or turn around.  This way the engine never overheats.  230,000
miles on original engine and still going strong.

Ian
