I had the same question about two weeks ago and was having the same problem
that you were with the location that Gene Berg recommends.  I mounted mine on
the left side of the frame where the rear engine mount is.  I drilled three
holes in the frame which I am not to sure is a good idea but thought it would
be the best place to mount it away from the muffler and to have enough ground
clearance under it.  It is also in a good location to change it.  Most of the
filter is also protected from the rear wheel by the fender well.  I was going
to mount it further (front) up the frame but I felt that the tire might get in
the way.  I made sure to paint the holes that I drilled so that they will not
rust.  I do not think that I made the frame any weaker.  I did have to rent an
angle drill to get the job done ,as there was not enough room for a regular
drill.  I shold have drilled and tapped the holes for the bolts which would
have been a cleaner install.
