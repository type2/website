This past week I remedied a similar problem in my '72.  I am not sure if
your gas tank is the same as mine.  I was smelling gas when I filled and
made turns, especially left turns.

I removed my carbs, air filter, all the junk between the engine and the
cargo area.  I then removed the panel between the gas tank and engine
compartment.  I found that the ventilation hose was shot. The filler hose
was old and I could not find a suitable replacement.  So I replaced the
ventilation hose and that seems to have fixed the problem.  The panel was
hard to get out.  I loosened one of the motor mounting braces and lowered
the engine just a bit.

I have had three gas leaks, two in addition to this one.  The first was the
fuel pump diaphragm had failed.  I replaced the fuel pump.  The second was
the accelerator pump diaphragm on the right hand carb.

Fuel injection will have some differences.

I have seen pictures of busses on fire and I do not want that to happen to
mine.  That will surely be then end for the bus if not me too.  I endeavor
to fix these kind of problems.

These busses are old and rubber does not last forever.

Good luck, and do not catch fire.
