Subject: RE: Oil Sensor Wiring Help

The piece of plastic is not important.  In my '78 2.0 L it is just a cover
for a connector.  If you yank on one end or another one of the connectors
will pop free.  If the two metal connectors weren't covered, they could
inadvertently ground against something in the engine compartment and cause
the light to come on when in fact there's nothing wrong.  Cutting it out of
the circuit is no big deal, it just means that next time you replace your
oil pressure sender you'll have to cut the wire.  And also, to ensure it
doesn't accidentaly ground, put some shrink tubing over the connection.
