I have noted different readings depending upon how the dipstick is
handled.  I've found that putting the stick in rapidly and removing it
rapidly will result in a low reading.  I usually push it in, pull it up
slightly, seat it firmly, wait a couple of seconds, then pull it for a
reading, with much more consistent results.

Of course, it's important to let the engine sit for at least a few minutes
before checking, so the oil can drain back to the sump.  Otherwise the
reading will be low.

Note that it isn't such a good idea to keep the oil to the full mark at
all times.  I've found that oil blowby will increase above the midway
point.
