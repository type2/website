> > My motor has 6.9:1 compression, will I get by okay at high altitudes or
> > will we have problems?
>
> Higher altitude effectively lowers compression ratio, due to the thinner
> air. I don't know the equation to figure out the exact ratio. So at
> altitude your 6.9:1 compression ratio will go down. IMHO 6.9 is a little
> high for a stock type bus motor so it should do great at higher altitude,
> and run cooler. Once you get much over 5,000 feet you'll probably notice
> some power loss but you'll just have to go slower over those big mountain
> passes. Be careful not to overrev the motor in a low gear. Thats how the
> PO of my bus grenaded the motor driving over the continental divide.
