I don't believe these lines exist on the 30/31. At least, this is the
first I've heard of it;-)

>Although, on my 28 pict 1, the electric choke works quite well, and
> the settings are very similar....  Go figure...

Here's what I did: 

1-Put the choke solenoid in place, but not closing the butterfly at
all. 
2-Tightened the 3 screws that hold the solenoid until I could just
barely turn the solenoid.
3-Turn the solenoid until it begins closing the butterfly. Now here's
the fun part; you have to try and guess just how far the choke would
be closed at the current temperature outside. I started at about 1/4
closed and started the motor. Not enough. Reset it at about 1/3 and it
seemed fine, so I tightened the screws up *not too tight*.

I let it sit for a couple of hours and then went out to dinner. It ran
better than it has in a LONG time. It sat outside in the cold for
another couple of hours while we ate, came out, fired it up and pulled
away in less than a minute!!!! PLENTY of power and NO SPUTTERING!!!!

I still need to check it on a truly cold morning and also check that
it shuts off completely when fully warm, but I feel pretty good about
it.

Other things to check...

~make sure the little lever-arm that connects the choke-solenoid coil
to the butterfly-valve is, in fact, connected to the solenoid-coil
properly;-) and that it moves freely.
~make sure the throttle-valve stop screw is resting on the highest
"step" of the throttle valve plate before you adjust the choke.
Otherwise, the butterfly valve will only close so far and you won't be
able to adjust the choke properly. Don't mess with the screw, though!
If this makes no sense to you, look at diagram 5-10, (page 14, section
3) of the Bentley manual. 

If you still have no idea what I'm saying, write me back and I'll try
again. If you don't have the Bentley, maybe I can scan the photo so
you can see it.

A BIG THANK YOU!!! To everyone who wrote me on this and offered
advice! I kind of took all the advice and combined it and it worked.
