>This is common. The distributor drive pinion was installed 180 degrees out
>of phase ("backwards"). Find a distributor drive pinion puller, pull out the
>pinion (if you remove your distributor and look down the hole it was in,
>you'll see a piece of metal with a big slot in the top and a spring sticking
>out of it. That's the pinion.), turn it around and put it back in.

This is risky.  There will likely be a washer under the pinion, and if you remove the pinion the washer may fall into the crankcase.  Just rotate your plug wires 180 degrees and drive it, IMO.
