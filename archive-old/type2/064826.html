The rear disk rotors on my car are shaped somewhat like one of those "straw"
hats that seem to reappear every 4 years for political campaigns or on "old
time" stage shows (w/cane).  Flipped on it's side, the flat "brim" is the
usual surface for the caliper/pads to grip. The inside of the raised portion
forms a small drum, and a conventionally shaped set of drum brakes (smaller
than in a standard applicaiton) fits inside to form a manually (cable)
actuated parking/emergency brake mechanism.
