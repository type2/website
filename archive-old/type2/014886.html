The 050 can present a problem in that the total advance can be as much as
10 degrees higher at total advance at 3000-3500rpm, causing detontation
(pinging) and overheat the engine under sustained operation.  050's a great
for "ricky racing" on the street or drag strip, which is what CB has the
distributor designed for in the first place.  It's essentially a "poor
man's" Porsche 912 (0231 129 031) distributor.

My advice is to install a Late 76-78 distributor with a single
vacuum/mechanical advance combination.  Originally, this distributor is
designed with fuel injection specs in mind, but personal experience
installing this distributor on dual carbed engines have yielded very
satisfactory results - the advance curves between the original Dual
Diaphragm (Push-Pull) Distributor and the FI Distributor are close enough
to be very useable.  It's nice to have that vacuum assisted advance in stop
and go traffic and the mechanical advance for highway use.  Recommended
timing is 7.5 degrees BTDC with the Vacuum Hose Connected.

Why did VW use so many distributors between 72-78 (and Federal 79-83) when
their advance characteristics were only a degree or two apart?  Passing the
EPA and CARB specs at the time each vehicle year was introduced is the
primary reason.  Nowadays, you'll find VW and Bosch superseding a multitude
of original distributor numbers to just a few for replacement.
