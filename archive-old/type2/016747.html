Ian:
Yes, you just totally screwed up. 
Indeed, you COULD just set cylinders 1 and 3, and cylinders 2 and 4
while the engine was in the same position, except that cylinder 3 has
its timimg event (and fires) 360 crankshaft degrees after that of
cylinder 1, which is half of a cycle (180 CAMSHAFT degrees) away from
the heel of the camshaft lobes (which is where you want to adjust for
clearance). As it is, you adjusted this number 1 cylinder for .006 and
.008 clearance when the valves are in the process of lifting and
returning closed, so they have about .020-.040 too much clearance on
that cylinder. It'll run there okay, but eventually all that hammering
will take its toll in damaged/worn valve train parts.
Actually, in addition to setting the engine to where the rotor is
pointing to #1 at the notch, make sure that #1 wire is really at that
position on the cap. A non-VW-savvy mechanic may have timed the engine
incorrectly.
