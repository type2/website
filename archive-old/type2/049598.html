However, that being said, I'd like to add that they have performed
adequately for myself and other WetWesties members.  I had the
'correct' Kumho six ply 'rated' 185-14s on my Vanagon GL.  They lasted
about 30k miles.  They were not 'squirmy' but they were abmysal in any
kind of percipitation.  I had LT27/8.50-14 house brand tyres on my '77
ASI.  They were wonderful tyres.  For 20k miles.  I replaced the
Vanagon tyres with Uniroyal Larados.  I replaced the ASI tyres with
Kirklands.  The handling is similar with both. (It remains to be seen
how they perform in the snow.)  The wear has yet to be determined, but
after 10k, it appears that I'll see the warranted 50k out of them. At
about a 30% savings over the Larados.

If it's a choice between a lesser price tyre and walking, the
Kirklands work for me.  If money is no object, by all means, purchase
the Michelins. 

Another alternative is Continentals available from the Tire Rack. 
However, a couple of years ago there was a fair amount of discussion
about tread seperation with them.
