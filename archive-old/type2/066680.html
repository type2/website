Subject: Mikey's '59 Single Progress

Well, my little '59 Singlecab is in green sealer.  All the body work 
is done, and it looks great!

Here I sit eating humble pie, as I was warned by Rusty van Bondo, et 
al., that my cost estimates were way too low (See archives "Mikey 
gets schooled").  I had estimated, I believe it was like $600 for 
some quick body work, etc.  Well, about $1200 later the body work is 
done.  As I got into it, I got more anal about making sure it was 
done correctly and that meant more detail here and there.  I managed 
to cut off about $300 in doing some of the work myself (like wet-
sanding the whole thing.  YUCK.  It was no fun and I was so sore!)

I had planed to paint it pearl-white and green right away, but the guy 
who is doing the work doesn't have a booth, and its now too cold to 
paint.  

The local "good" body shop, after I approached them, were like "Yuck, 
the INSIDE and outside of and old VW _AND_ you want two-tone... You'd 
have to give me $1000 plus paint just to spray it to get me to do 
it."  Is this guy MOTIVATED or what?  Where's Richard Simmons when 
you need him?

So my body guy put it in a green sealer for the winter.  

I'm still getting the gates all finished up, and then it's time to do 
the mechanical--The new engine/tranny and brakes and the rest of the 
hardware installed.  

My goal has been Halloween, and if the engine is forthcoming in the 
next two or three days, I should make it!  Woo-hoo!
