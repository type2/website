On Thu, 26 Aug 2004, Will Anderson wrote:

> there is a circlip (inboard left side of bellhousing) that holds the cross
> shaft in place against the force of the return spring which has the tendency
> to pull the shaft toward the driver's side.

One thing that I always do is to safety-wire this circlip together with
thin wire.  This keeps it in its groove and prevents the engine pull that
seems to follow work on the clutch.

On old-style throwout bearings, another loop of safety wire goes through
the two spring clips and around the perimter of the bearing.  Keeps things
together.
