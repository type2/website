He's full of it!  Sure, a small amount of gas can get into the
crankcase via a float valve leak, particularly if the vehicle was left
in a nose-up position.  It's happened to our bug.  _HOWEVER_ it only
happened when the bug was left on an uphill incline with a near-full
fuel tank and it sat for more than a month!  The crankcase pretty much
filled with oil in this time and promptly blew out the front seal with
no damage to bearings.

Normally, a small amount of gas gets into the oil via blowby, and it
is evaporated by the heat of the oil and then burned via the crankcase
breather tube.  If you drive the bus regularly then there is no way
for the gasoline to build up to a level that reduces the lubricating
properties of the oil appreciably.  If you did screw up the oil, the
likely result would be a siezed or spun main bearing, not a broken rod.

If you get a _big_ float valve leak, then a cylinder will fill up with
gas.  The engine won't turn over, since the gas locks the piston
hydraulically.
