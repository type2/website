Conditions inside the crankcase of a running engine can be described
as a maelstrom of oil droplets suspended in air.  Oil is atomized by
the rapidly moving parts.  If any of this air moves out of the engine,
the oil vapor will go with it.  Some of this oil will condense in the
plumbing, causing the mess you describe.  The oil filler serves as a
crude oil separator, but obviously some oil vapor still escapes.  In
some engines, the oil is delivered directly to the intake manifold, so
you never see it, but the VW runs it to the air cleaner where you do.
