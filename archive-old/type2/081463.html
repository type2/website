> The cam gear does more than power the mechanical pump.  It lifts and
> lowers your valves and rotates the rotor on the distributor. If the cam
> gear has loosened on the cam shaft, then the valves are opening and
> closing at the wrong time and the dist is firing at the wrong time.  I
> have seen a cam shaft with the holes in the gear egged out 1/2" and the
> gear free to rotate 10 degrees, but have never had it happen to me.  the
> only way I can think of to test for this is to do the static time
> procedure turning the engine clockwise, then do it turning the engine
> counterclockwise.  If the results are significantly different, then you
> have a big problem.
