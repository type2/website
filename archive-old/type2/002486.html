I am currently starting the sound proofing of our 71, 7 pass. bus , that
once was a hard top camper.  We did the engine compartment with canned
rubber and foil bubble pack insulation, topped of the ceiling of the engine
area with flashing pop rivited and sheet metal screwed. (Do the screws, they
were easier to install ).

We did the canned 'Stuff' in as many holes as we could fine that didn't look
like they were doing something. A little one sided tacky foam insulation on
the sheet metal apron, cleaned my battery terminals, etc. and removed all
the interior panels . Which was fairly sound proofed on the floor already.
The walls hadn't been touched. I think the canned 'Stuff' in all the hollow
spots will be a major fix.

I am looking at different sound deadeners for the surfaces,  Q pads (or
similar products) are pricey, and  the apparant favorite, but I found they
are available in larger sheets for cutting. Some one reccomended spray on
bed liner ( like in the back of a truck). Now there's and idea. It would be
quick and good, and it makes the Qpads look affordable.

More later. How are others sound proofings going?
