> Melissa,
>
> Each of these lights is on a separate circuit and each will have to be
> checked separately, but the general  rule is to get a VOM, turn the dial
> to 12VDC, attach the black probe to the body (ground), then start probing
> with the red, start at the battery and go 'til you stop seeing 12VDC.
>
> For the reverse lights:
>
> The reverse lights take power from the coil, run it thru an inline fuse in
> the engine compartment down to a switch on the left forward side of the
> transmission and then back to the bulbs in their holders.   From the bulbs
> it then goes to a ground on the body.  OK?
>
> First, take the black wire of your VOM and attach it firmly to some shiny
> piece of metal in your engine compartment.
>
> Take the red probe and touch it to the positive terminal of the battery.
> Got juice?  If it does not read ~12V, then the black wire is not attached
> correctly.  wiggle it a bit until you get a positive 12V when you touch
> the + terminal of the battery.
>
> Turn the bus on, but don't start it.
>
> Touch the red wire of the VOM to terminal 15 of the coil, the one with the
> black wires (*not* the one with the green wire to the dist).  It should
> read ~12V.  Yes?  If it does not, then the bus won't start/run.  If it
> does read 12V, fine.
>
> Now look at the black wires attached to the coil.  One of them runs toward
> the front of the car and has an inline fuse holder built in.  See it?
> Take the fuse holder appart, inspect the fuse and test for 12V on  the
> coil side of the holder.
>
> Insert the fuse in the holder and test for 12V on the exposed end of the
> fuse.
>
> Close the holder and crawl under the bus.  On the forward end of the
> transmission, left side, near the nose cone is a switch with two wires
> coming off it.  Pull these wires off and test both for 12V.  One will and
> one will not have 12V.
>
> put the wire that had 12V back on the switch and check that it still has
> 12V.  Check the other terminal and see if it has 12V.  crawl out from
> under, go up frront and shift the bus into reverse.  Back under and check
> the switch.  at this point *both* terminals of the switch should have 12V.
>
> Go back to the tail lamp housings and check for 12V on the lowest bulb
> socket.
>
> Somewhere in there, you are going to find a break, when you do, fix the
> break and your lights will work, honest!  The same thing has to be done
> for each circuit, headlights, turn signals, brake lights, etc.  It all
> works the sme way, power flows from the battery, thru a fuse, thru a
> switch, to the device, and on to ground.  Get the wiring diagram from
> vintagebus_dot_com or in your Bentley's and have at it!
