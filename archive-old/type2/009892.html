Subject: $10 digital dipstick thermometer?
Message-Id: <199906181124.HAA12874_at_mime3.prodigy_dot_com>

While at the local Shopright, I noticed they had $15 digital "meat"
thermometers on sale for $9.99 - with a range to 300 degrees and a battery
life of one year continuous.....so I got one.
The wheels started spinning....realizing these things have a thermister in
the tube......Popped the case and see 2 nice free wires 1.5" long going
into the tube end!   The tube has a slot in the end facing the electronics,
and I slid it right out using a fine screwdriver.  Hmm- the wheels started
spinning- the tube is more than narrow enough for dipstick tube- less than
1/8" wide.   A digital dipstick sender?   What if you clip the wires and
get the Dreml out and carefully grind  the 5" tube circumferentially near
the top to remove the thickend flange.  Finely solder an extension double
wire  between it and the readout top.  I have extra phone wire!  Gob
non-conductive JB weld or epoxy over the exposed wire connections   that
will reach into the dipstick tube and let set.  Slide a brass tube over the
tip+wires so about 3" of tip is exposed (hobby shop- they come 12" long in
1/32" steps-I measure 15/64 inner diamter to dipstick tube, so 7/32" tube
should work) .  Fill this with epoxy from upper end and when tip shows it's
embedded, tape it so epoxy stays in to set.  Solder a brass washer to upper
end so it can't fall into case.  Now you have a dipstick sender!  Any long
time set epoxy will withstand the 400 degrees of my woodstove- used it to
glue a handle to my flue gas diverter on the stove side.
    Radio Shack sells indoor outdoor thermometers with about 10 feet of
wire- the sender end looks small enough to fit down a dipstick tube if you
tweak it a bit with grinding/sanding.   I don't know if their max range
goes to 300, though and you have to protect all wires that might melt their
insulation at 270 degrees.
