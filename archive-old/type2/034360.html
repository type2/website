Ken,

I was unsubscribed at the time you asked about the tranny.

I have a '76 (091) in my '69, and had KRC (in the back of Hot VW's) build
the tranny "correctly" for use in the '69. No cutting, etc.. need be done,
and he can tell you exactly what needs to be done to the nosecone, bell
housing, or whatever. I did not need a new starter.

One thing worth mentioning: The "guide cylinder" (I don't know off-hand what
it's called - it's that tubular thing supported by three bolts they started
putting on, I believe, '71 and later tranny bell housings) must match the
clutch TO bearing opening. I don't know if you can simply pull the guide
thing off if you have an earlier clutch setup. I put a new KEP in mine when
swapping the tranny.

I hope this kinda made sense, since I haven't poked my nose inside the
Clutch/Tranny/Motor myself, I had the local VW guru do it.
