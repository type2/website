This comes up sometimes but I don't recall that anybody has ever dealt with
it (I shall not address its relation, the "how do I ship a car
cross-country" question, here):

Common freight fares are similar to air passenger fares in that there are
"published rates" that nobody ever pays because they are ludicrous, and
then there are various levels of discount. Judging from your questions you
have no access to a loading dock or somebody who has an account with a
trucking company. This should not be such a huge obstacle but in the real
world I'm afraid no carrier is going to fool with you.

It's probably best to break it up into UPS-sized chunks. I don't remember
their limit, something like 85 lbs. Make five of them, it's really the
cheapest most reliable way to do these things.
