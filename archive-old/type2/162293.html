Don,

Comprehensive is all about damage to your vehicle.

It is my understanding that if you don't have comprehensive
from *your* insurance company, that the other insurance company is not 
going to pay anything either....or at minimal, blue book.

On the other hand, whether it is your fault or the other guy's fault, 
*your* insurance company will pony up the bucks and then *they* fight with
the other insurance company about the amount.

I'm betting that for items like agreed value, they have a "gentlemen's"
agreement on the settlement.  Anyone who has a vehicle with an agreed 
value policy is generally a gold mine....*and* someone who cares 
passionately about the car that has a policy like that.
