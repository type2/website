When you first turn the ignition on and without turning over the engine, your
Oil light and alternator light should be ON.  After cranking and developing a
few lbs of oil pressure, the oil lamp should turn off.  Once the engine
starts, the rpms will be such that the alternator/gen will be able to start
start providing a voltage that is greater than the battery voltage.  At that
point, the alternator light will turn off.

This sequence insures that your warning lamps are functioning and not burnt
out.  If you cannot get past the first step with the lights on without
cranking the engine, fix that first before going on.
