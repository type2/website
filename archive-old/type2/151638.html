Faster,

Maybe I mis-spoke.  The engine needs a nice fat spark, *especially* under 
load.  Can't find the info on the net, but I have a vo-tech engine class 
book at home that uses a whole chapter to cover the kettering system and 
talks about how strong a spark is needed at varying engine conditions....hi load, no load, hi rpm, 
low rpm.

You can probably find a book likethis at the local "used" book store...it 
is invaluable!


If the coil is weak, you are going to have problems, if it makes a 
"weaker" spark after it gets warmed up, then when the engine is under a 
heavy load, it may not have a strong enough spark to fire off the mixture.
It's a reach, but possible.  Ask anyone who has tried to drive on a 
failing coil.


Which reminds me of something else....the guys in the bus need to check 
not only the coil, but that the connections to the coil are nice and 
tight!
