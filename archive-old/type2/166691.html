Hi Gerry,

What year is your son's bus? If it's a 68-71 you don't need a jack,  
you can rest the engine on a solid object and roll the bus away from it.

If it's a 72-and-later, you will need a jack. I usually balance the  
T4 engine with the jack positioned just slightly rearward of the  
circular cover plate.  It helps a LOT if you use a dolly jack with a  
large (6" or better) diameter lift plate on it.  It's certainly do- 
able with the 2" diameter surface found on the typical $30 walmart  
dolly jack, but it's much, much easier when you have a larger surface  
to work with.

Another support option is to use a motorcycle/ATV lift like this one:
http://www.discountramps_dot_com/mcImages/BW0101-motorcycle-jack-1.jpg

Harbor freight also sells a similar item at a good price

Safety shoes might also be a good idea, just in case....
