This is what my HS electronics teacher referred to as "Turning a meter
into a motor"!

I keep a small digital meter in the parts box, along with my Heathkit
volt/dwell/tacho-meter.  The digital meter is just about
indestructable (as long as it is kept on the voltage settings) and can
take hard knocks.  The Heathkit has a huge analog meter that is very
handy for interpreting varying readings.  Since it is designed for
automotive use, it only has one voltage range (0-20v)

I also have a nice (almost antique) Simpson 270 analog VOM, with the
big analog meter, lotsa ranges, and even a nice mirror on the dial
that helps you correct for parallax error while reading the meter
(whoo whoo!)  I take it out of its leather case once in a while,
caress its knobs, and admire a product made by craftsmen!
