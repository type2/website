Yep.

In Wilson's book he lists the correction facors as follows:

Sea Level: 1:1
1000ft: .9711
2000ft: .9428
3000ft: .9151
4000ft: .8881
5000ft: .8617
6000ft: .8359
7000ft: .8106
8000ft: .7860

So, if at 8000 feet you get 78lbs on a compression test, it will equal
100lbs at sea level.
