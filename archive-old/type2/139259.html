From what I have heard it is a REAL BEAR to fit a Type 4 into an early bay
(read: major mods) and I have still never seen one.  And we all know how
long it takes to get a trans-vair on the road ;)  IMHO you are best sticking
with Type1 power in a '68 if you want an easy path.

From my punched-out T1 experience, I recommend going with a 1776cc Type1
with 041 heads (port work optional), a counterweighted crank, engle 100 cam
or equivalent, fueled by Dellorto DRLA carbs, and driving an 091 trans from
a 76-79 bus.  This is still a mild engine, C/R at 7.3:1.  Runs cool for
longevity.  Enough power to move a Westy with dignity and alacrity.  Can be
assembled by a pro for ~$1600 with all new parts (carbs and trans extra).
Smokes buses with stock T4's.  Supposedly the stroker 1914cc is even better
but costs a bit more and is a little more modified (custom pushrods, etc.).
