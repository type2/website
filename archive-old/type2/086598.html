All very good advice. I personally have been there multiple times with
multiple vehicles. When I buy an older vehicle like the bus I always assume
the motor is about to blow up because I have no idea just how well built it
was to start with and maintained thereafter. I typically run all of my cars
hard.(70-75mph in the bus) I build my own motors and generally push them
hard. I monitor their health and do the proper maintenance. I have only had
one engine I put together myself fail me and that was my first 1600 DP
because I put the baffle plate under the generator stand in backwards and
had bad crankcase vetilation problems. Hey I was 15 and it was my first
car...
     My point here is the same as was made in the previous post. If you
want to sing the blues you gotta pay your dues. Never put to sea without a
life-jacket, dont take any wooden nickles, etc. I like to drive my cars
hard and when they break I have fun taking my time and fixing them right. I
also have the luxury of a 5 mile commute to an office I can ride my bicycle
to and shower in the gym. And by having  several cars if one is dead I
drive another. If you don't have tools, time, and $$ old cars are not the
thing to be driving.
