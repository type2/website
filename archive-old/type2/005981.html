I'll gladly pop up for the airsucking vanagons. <step up on soapbox>

As far as the best year, I don't think just 1 stands out. I've had a 72 
bus, 73 westy and now  I have a 81 vanagon (Converted to camper). My 
favorite engine of all ....74 1800 cc carb.
 My 81 vanagon now has a 1800 cc type 4 engine (ala 74) w/ no smog stuff- 
Best year for heads (round port) big exhaust valves(sodium)- best year for 
exhaust. Big production year-exhaust from 72-74 fits. 1 piece muffler- no 
crossover pipes. Good for used parts such as heat exchangers (NO triangle 
ports here)&(cost)- alternator w/ external voltage regulator. Also 
mechanical fuel pump and carb setup (mine is now a webber progressive)- 
cuts down on high pressure fuel fires and I get to giggle at fuel injection 
troubleshooting messages. Also, solid lifters ! (gotta love that sound) You 
can even use an .009 dizzy.

<turn on hose to avoid potential flames>

My favorite body style, vanagon westy setup.
 Add all the above to the aero- design of the wedge. Mine was a sunroof- 
now with a ragtop- but has full deluxe west interior- stove, fridge, plenty 
of storage -cabinet layout is great. Got a set of GL seats w/ swivels. 
Vanagon has a fan for cool air circulation- Good suspension system- ala-I 
don't have "butt sag". Brake parts swap w/ earlier 73-79 type 2's.  It's 
still aircooled, big windows, lots o visibility, steering is better than 
"loaf", hydraulic clutch, I like gear ratio's of tranny. Deck lid is lower, 
van is roomier. Spare tire tucked up under the van- saves space. Got westy 
springs w/out poptop weight. Retract seatbelts. Engine hatch !!- Hinge 
front tranny mount- The easiest to remove an engine from on side of the 
road.(2 jacks not 3). Fuel tank is not gravity fed like early type 2's- 
easier to extingush. It even fits in my garage ! Sits level too !!

As far as the affection goes, I was born in 1970. I grew up with punk rock 
and new wave, swatch watches, buttonfly jeans, the walkman, atari home 
computers , and the list goes on. My first bus was assembled when I was 2. 
I didn't love is less, but I love my wedge. The aircooled wedge is like a 
middle child in the family, not alot of attention. Doesn't get the 
"affection" that a splittie or bay gets because they are older. Or seems 
outdated by having "real heat" and creature comforts such as power butt 
massager or whatever. It also doesn't have the stereotype associated with 
the bus. No beads on the windows, rainbow paintjobs or dead 8 tracks in the 
glovebox.

 "I do yearn to have the street cred of a type2"

Only 3 years of airsucking wedge- a limited breed. There are more "loaf" 
type 2's than vanagon's in my city. I also do not have my wife asking me 
about the fear of death, as in my bay, when semi's would blow by as the 
wheel would turn side to side. I might as well have put a rudder on the 
back of that bus.

IMHO, the best bus to get is the cheapest, with the most spare parts in the 
back !! :]  How many of you have a "bus allowance" ? Your spare parts 
corner in the garage ? Repair manuals on the back of the toilet seat ? Can 
you stop at just 1 ?

Nobody can say that one year is better that the other, but mine is my 
favorite !!
