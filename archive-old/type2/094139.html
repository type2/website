Subject: Re: [T2] Hydraulic clutch info for you

Very funny that I noticed a slot in my axle beam boss in the same location.
I wondered for about 15 seconds if this wasn't where the clutch cable was
supposed to go.  How did it ever get there, I wonder.  I wondered if this
was a lazy way to adjust the clutch cable after it had really stretched.
 
> A couple of years back I noticed that the cable routing went through a
> narrow slot on the backside of one of the big threaded inserts for the
> front axle beam attach on the frame.  Closer inspection revealed that the
> cable was _cutting_ through that boss after being misrouted on the wrong
> side of the insert.  The clutch action had caused the cable to cut halfway
> through the approximately 1 inch diameter solid steel insert!  It must
> have been that way for decades, but the cable showed no wear at all.  I
> rerouted the cable and the clutch action became much easier (it wasn't
> hard to begin with) and "slicker".  Before playing with hydraulics, take a
> look at the clutch cable routing to see if the same error might have been
> made on yours.
