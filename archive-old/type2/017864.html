> > i have read many mails lately about putting in aftermarket oil press.

> > are determined to use teflon, on a piece of npt that small do not over-wrap

> 
> Am I missing something here?  The body of oil pressure senders need to be
> grounded and tape and pipe dope will insulate the sender. The path to ground for
> the senders is through the pipe threads.   Tape has to potential to insulate
> more than dope as the dope wipes off.  Use tape and you run the risk of the
> sender not working.

Scott:
It will cut through enough to conduct, trust me! Pipe threads are
tapered and one tightens them till there's an interference fit, i.e. the
inside part is stretching the outside part by several thousandths. The
pipe dope/thread simply ends up in the irregular voids. Take a joint
apart and you'll find the tape shredded!
