Hey folks, just thought I'd pipe in here.

Up here in the great white north (Canada), there are quite a few hills as well.
We live in Vancouver, BC and I've been driving for 25 years and in all that
time, I, nor many of the people who I've been a passenger with, have used the
handbrake method of driving with a standard. on a hill.  I actually tried it
when we first got our busses and frankly, it's more hassle than it's worth.
It takes more effort to do all that is necessary to do a handbrake start  than
to simply release the clutch until it noticeably starts grabbing ,
then quickly slide the foot off the brake pedal and on to the accelerator while
letting the clutch out and pressing on the gas pedal like a normal take off.
Done corectly, you will maby roll back 2 feet and even less as you get more
experienced.  The fear of rolling backwards into the other car and of stalling
at an intersection and hearing 20 horns beeping at "some hippie in a VW Bus" is
why most people have a fear of hill starts.  Do as the other listees have
suggested, practice where it's not going to affect traffic.  Like any motor
skill / coordination skill, this takes practice, and with time, is second
nature.  This has always worked for me and the ones I know who started with the
park brake take off did exactly that;  started, and then they switched to the
standard take off I just described.  You can do this with a properly operating
bus on any hill.  We've got some good hills here in Vancouver.
