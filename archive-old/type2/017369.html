Harmon:
That almost mirrors my experience exactly! I bought a 70 from a
junkyard- had to get an Alabama title, got the key and the owners manual
from the PO. It had 115k miles on it then. One day #1 owner stopped me
and told me he had bought the thing new, put white pinstripes on ONE
side only, so he knew it was his old bus. Seems this bus had been from
Iowa to Alaska 4 times, now on its 4th engine. Failures had been a
variety of things, but a couple of broken crankcases. The then current
engine was a German case, 1641 cc, single port,and it was a good one, I
finally took it out at about 80k miles only for excessive endplay. I
think the owner in between us had probably not changed the oil very
often. We used this motor hard. My brother took it to a Porsche swapmeet
in Pennsylvania, said he had the speedometer needle pointing STRAIGHT
DOWN going down some of those mountains! (It did read high, but that was
still pretty fast.)
I would be really interested in stories about long lived motors,
including:
1. How long they lived
2. How hard they were loaded, driven, city, highway , desert,etc
3. How they were built- size, oilcoolers, intake, exhaust, cams,
rockers, valves, etc
4. How they performed- I know that's pretty subjective, but isn't it
all?
5. What kind of maintenance you gave them
