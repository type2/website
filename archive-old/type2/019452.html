> Two things:
> 
> Dwell is inversly proportional to width of point gap.  Since dwell is the
> degrees of distributor rotation while the points are closed, increasing the
> point gap decreases the dwell.  If your dwell is at 44, decrease the point gap
> to attain a dwell of 50.  Changing the gap also changes the timing and any point
> gap change requires a timing adjustment.  Over time, the gap closes up due to
> the rubbing block wearing.  If greased properly, the rubbing block should wear
> very little.
> 
> Timing:  If you run a mechanical advance only distributor, total advance should
> not exceed 31 - 32 degrees at 3500 rpm.  The 009 has a total of 10 degrees of
> distributor advance which eguals 20 degrees at the crank.  The 050 has 14
> degrees at the distributor and 28 at the crank.  Using the 050, you can time to
> 3 or 4 degrees initial advance.  Easier starting and low speed operation.  In
> the heavy bus, a lower initial setting gives better bottom end while the vacuum
> advance gives a cleaner burn and increases milage while cruising.  If you have
> the double vacuum distributor, pull and plug the retard hose (On the side of the
> vacuum pot towards the dist. cap.)  Set the timing to 7.5 degrees.  The
> mechanical distributors react only to engine RPM; the combination distributors
> react to both speed and load.
