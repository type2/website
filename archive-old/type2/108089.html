Top ten? Hmmm. Let's see...

1) Brakes: especially the rubber brake lines, but check the whole system
over. Profesionally if necessary.
2) E-Brake: Working? Adjusted for good action? Is the cable in good shape?
3) Suspension, front & rear. Does it handle well? Shocks OK? Steering not
too loose?
4) Fuel lines: Check for chaffing, replace any worn or old lines. This has
killed many VW's.
5) Lights: All working? Brake Lights?
6) Tune-up: Get the VW idiot book by Muir and the official manual from
Bentley. Oil & Tranny fluid.
7) Exhaust System: Intact, not leaking?
8) Seat Belts in good shape? Make sure the panel they attach to on the floor
isn't rusted out.
9) CV Joints: Rubber boots intact? If not, do the full
teardown/inspection/lube.
10) Engine cooling: Are all the tin pieces present? Is there a foam seal
around the engine? The engine can cook itself on long highway runs or hills
if the cooling parts are missing. Does it have the thermostat & control
flaps (more important in cold climates)
