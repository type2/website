Hartmut made the good point WRT why the headlight switch would have
those unusual terminals on it...  And furthermore - the data that Karl

headlight switch still used the 1/4" push-ons is getting' a new bodily
orifice :-/  And for those ROW applications, that would be a 3mm

terminal.  I don't know WTF those of you using inch decimals are going
to do ;-)  Since it's St. Patrick's Day, you should probably have a
drinky-poo or 5 and hopefully you'll be struck by inspiration!

Some models of Volkswagen products (I flog a 914 when I'm not honking
around in a Bay Window so I know this), will light the parking/tail
light circuit when the turn signal lever is left in the activated
position and the motor switched off.  It sort of freaks you out the
first time you park the car and the turn signal hasn't canceled, the
front parking light, side markers, and rear tail light will be
illuminated...  and you start tearing around thinking "OMFG, I've got
ANOTHER hellishly complicated electrical problem to sort out on my
'teener..."  Relax, 'salright!

For those of you who prefer to snake old but non-boogered rheostat
"plain label" headlight switches when you find them at the 'yards, a tip
from the boneyard toolbox.  Pull out the headlight knob first, then
using a pair of needle-nose pliers, clamp the shaft in place so you are
not turning the knob against the extremes of the rheostat as you unscrew
the knob.  I've found that sometimes the extra pressure of unscrewing
the knob against the rheostat stops is enough to make a decent/passable
switch give up the ghost the first time it's remounted and the rheostat
is fiddled with...
