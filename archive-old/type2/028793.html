Yes, but if I understand correctly that he is going to keep his existing '79
heat exchangers (and just replace the exhaust system behind it), then that
is a moot point. The '72-74 exhaust is a direct bolt-on to the '79 heat
exchangers, no modification needed. In fact, in most of the rest of the
world it is what came stock on a '79; only we lucky U.S. folk got the funky
exhaust setup with a CAT.  As to whether it can be done with regular tools,
no torch, etc., in a perfect world, yes.  That is, if you don't come across
any frozen/rusted bolts that need to be heated, if your heat exchangers are
not slightly tweaked or bent (making reinstallation more difficult once you
remove the old exhaust and what's left "springs open or closed" slightly),
etc. The '72-74 setup will actually bolt up to a '79 more easily than a U.S.
'79 setup does, because it is a much simpler configuration.  But one frozen
bolt can ruin your whole day, so allow some extra time.
