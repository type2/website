	While it is true the synthetic properties of Mobil 1 or any synthetic for
that matter do not break down like conventional oils and can go 5,10 and
even 25,000 miles per manufacturers claims, you need to take two things into
account.  One, the additive package in the oil does wear out and needs to
changed. To a certain degree this will be a prolonged period in a VW motor
because of you stated fact of consumption of 1 qt. every 1,000 miles.
Adding a fresh quart of new synth that often will delay the change to a
point.  However, the second thing to consider is the large percentage of
contaminents built up in a VW motor need to be removed.  This is a problem
since the vast majority of these motors do not have oil filters.  If yours
does have an oil filter, then changing the filter every 3,000 miles and
topping it off with fresh oil will prolong it to a certain degree.  This by
no means is the best answer since before then the filter will have probobly
been clogged enough to trip the bypass valve on it. meaning no filtration at
all, hence dirty oil.
	Do not get me wrong, I personally think syntetic oil is the totally
superior to conventional oil but just because the oil will last does not
mean you should leave it in for 10,000 or more miles.  If you want to run
synthetic do it but use it just like conventional oils.  Your bus will be
happier.

	Something else to consider, VW engines do not have water pumps and
radiators and run hotter in general to their water cooled/temp regulated
counterparts.  They also use the oil to disapate heat and help cool the
motor.  Plus some Synthetic manufacters stte not to use the in vehicles made
before 1980. Seals and gaskets maybe?  Don't no why, will try to find an
answer.
