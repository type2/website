Peter:

You'll need to get a hold of a small enough puller to get that bearing
out.  I did the same thing you did several years ago whild doing a
clutch job (i.e., bending the outer lip of the bearing) but I was lucky
and my neighbor had a puller small enough for the job.  (That said, I
do remember reading about another way of removing the bearing:  Pack the
bearing and the hole it resides in full of grease, then fit a dowel which
_just_ fits inside the bearing into the hole.  Smack the end of the dowel
with a hammer and hydrolic pressure pops out the bearing!  No guarantees,
but it just might work!)

As I recall, the felt washer goes on the _other_ side of the flywheel.
