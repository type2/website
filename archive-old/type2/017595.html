I have never gotten a good answer on the trailer tire question. LT's do
have a stiffer sidewall, but not necessarily a heavier load rating. LT
loadrange C's are not rated for the load of a LT loadrange D. 

Are we talking about 15" or 14" rims?

In my experience, good 14" 6- or 8- ply tires are available in loadrange
c and d ( and remember, the number refers to EACH tire, not the total 4
or total axle...)On my '65 splitty I run a tire called "trailbuster"
27X8.5, available through Beggs tire factory all through the western
part of the US.  It is about the size of a 205/75, and is 26.75 " in
total height, nice for a reduction box rear end. It is loadrange c,
6-ply rated, with about 1750 lbs per tire at 50  psi. 
I just ran them 8000 miles through mexico, loaded to the gills (4600 lbs
in a bus weighing 2600 empty) and was delighted.

I have gone looking for 15" tires for earlier buses, and have absolutely
no lock finding a 205/75R15 with LT 6- or 8-ply sidewalls. *EXCEPT* in a
trailer Tire.

So what the heck is the difference between trailer tires and others?????

The sidewall stiffness ( not necessarily strength) is very nice for
handling in a taller tire like this, as it lessens the "tire wag"
experienced by many buses. I refuse to run a passenger rated tire
because of the feel during cornering. 

BTW this thread has been gone over and over in the vintagebus list,
since the taller tires needed with reduction gears and potential of 15"
stock rims makes it an even bigger issue for splitty owners than bubble. 
But still no good answer......maybe the late model people can solve it
once and for all.......:)
