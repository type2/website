While slightly off topic, it is applicable to the people on the lists
I'm on (for musicians and VW bus owners).   I'm both a musician (oboe)
and tinker a lot with VW busses.   Tinkerers around engines can get
burns, and musicians need the use of their fingers.  I won't bore you
with the details, but I got a severe burn (blistering within seconds due
to hot surface contact)) on a finger that ran from the tip to the palm;
some other fingertips were involved.  My spouse is a pathologist for
J&J, and while I was running cold water over it (hurt like hell when
taken out of water), she gave me 3 ibuprofin tablets orally and then
made a paste of ground up ibuprofin tablets and a solution of
lidocaine/benzocaine ("No More Ouchies" pump spray by J&J).  You need a
Morter&Pestle for this; any pharmacy has them.  Keep applying this to
the burn and let it sit there- one can also pack this on a gauze pad and
tape over the area.  I reapplied it with a q-tip as it dried and caked. 
In a few hours, it showed the blistering, but no redness and very little
pain; most of the discomfort was due to the blister.  Left to air dry,
the blister (not broken) receded to a more comfortable level.  I applied
more before bed.    
  Though I have a nasty blister running all the way from my palm to the
fingertip, there is no redness or inflammation at all, and no pain- 
just some resistance to bending due to the blister.  I've tried this
before with aspirin, but the ibuprofin worked much better- according to
the pathologist spouse, the anti-inflammatory/anit-prostaglandin effects
of the ibuprofin prevents the migration of white cells to the area and
stops the release of inflammation inducing substances, which actually
causes the "collateral
damage" of pain, swelling, redness, and even cell death.  MB  78 VW
camper    MUTS oboe/EH support
