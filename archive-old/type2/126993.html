Subject: Re: [T2] don't bead blast pistons

Can't agree with you on this one Gary, bead-blasting the pistons is routinely done in high performance engines, i.e. my 2276CC Type1. This is to say Glass Bead blasting, NOT sandblasting which is corrosive/damaging to metal. Glass beading or walnut beading are great ways to clean things up. As to getting glass beads in your piston oiler holes, just make sure you check with the approprate pipe cleaners or other methods to assure everything is clean. It is the best way I know to clean piston ring grooves, and some even say it leaves minute "dents" which retain oil and increase lubrication.
Just my 2 cents of course.
Courtney
