> Volks,
> Had a couple of hours this afternoon to really get into the nitty 
> gritty of my no start problem. Found problem, but will share the 
> steps for those that have similar issues.
> 
> decided to run 12v wire direct from battery to terminal 50 of the 
> starter solenoid............turned key on, and touched the lead to 
> the battery positive side, started right up.
> 
> Next decided to trace wire from connector at base of ignition switch 
> (used Bentley for info) found a 4 wire coupler under dash.    I 
> tried to jumper wire from 12v in connector at base of ignition 
> switch to terminal 30 in the same connector, and no start.  Then i 
> disconnected the 4 wire terminal, and jumpered 12v to the terminal 
> 30 wire and it started right up.  This told me I had a bad lead from 
> the 4 wire connector to the connector on the ignition switch.  I 
> removed the wire from the ignition switch, and discovered the PO had 
> rigged another wire in there to get a key on + lead for 
> something.........  removed wire connector, crimped on new one, and 
> the car starts per stock design now.
>
