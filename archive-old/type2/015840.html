At 11:34 27/04/99 -0400, A. Blake Sartin wrote:

>The only reason that
>the type 4 engine was ever employed was to meet the insatiable power
>demands of an American buying public.  

I like to think it was a natural evolution of a good design.  I don't see
anyone arguing that a gland nut is better than 5 smaller (and lower torque)
bolts.  There is a reason that all modern engines (that I know of) attach
their flywheels similar to the way the type IV engine does and not with a
gland nut.

There is some truth to the "insatiable power demands" argument, but it all
comes down to how much is enough.  For me, 50hp is not enough.  Not enough
to pull hills at a reasonable speed, not enough to keep up with traffic on
the interstate, not enough to beat that train at the crossing.  Just
kidding about that last one, but hey, if I can't stop because I greased my
brake drums, I have to speed up, right?  :)
