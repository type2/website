Often materials which have a great ability to absorb heat also have a great
ability to give up heat.  Aluminum is a case in point, as is anything black.
Great ability to absorb and trasmit heat would tend to make oil seem cooler
rather than hotter (it seems to me).  Whenever there is a transfer heat the rate
of transfer will depend on the difference in temperature between the two
material.  In the two cases (A) engine to oil and (B)oil to oil cooler, both are
oil to metal / metal to oil transfer.  Since the surface area in the oil cooler
is small relative to the surface area of heat transfer in the rest of the
engine.  The rate of transfer is improved by keeping the temperature difference
great by cooling the oil cooler with air.

engine is running hot, though.
