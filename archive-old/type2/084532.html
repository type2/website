At 01:09 PM 2/22/2002, Rob Henley wrote:
>I met a guy here in town and when I told him I had bad rings he helped me 
>to check it out. He removed the oil filler cap and felt over it and then 
>reved the engine. He said there was no pressure, that it was fine.

Not a conclusive test.  The engine needs to be under a load (like full 
throttle) to be sure there is really no crankcase pressure.  Simply goosing 
the throttle isn't going to tell you squat.  If you *really* want to know 
the condition of your rings, a leakdown test is THE way to do it.

>I did have the oil breather capped off and he said it should be open and 
>preferably routed back to the carb or something, but there isn't a place 
>for it on my single Weber Progressive.

He is right, it definately has to be routed somewhere, or at least 
vented.  You can't cap it off and expect not to blow oil past your 
seals.  You should route it to the air cleaner housing - if there isn't a 
port, you can install one.

>Both the front cylinder readings jumped up a bit. My spark plugs were very

Yeah, the one cylinder went up a bit with the oil, but they were fairly 
even when dry which is a good thing.  Sooty plugs mean rich mixture, as 
long as they weren't wet.  My guess - there is nothing major wrong with 
that engine and the oil leaks would slow down or stop if the breather box 
were plumbed to the air cleaner or at least vented to the atmosphere.
