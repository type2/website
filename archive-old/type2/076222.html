Pete,

I know you have the problem solved, but let me add one more data point for
the archives...  I use a 6" stiff putty knife and 1 kilo sledge. insert
the knife in the underside of the cover, up against the junction between
the cover and body clip, then giive the end of the knife a couple whacks
with the sledge.  Move the knife along a bit and do it again.  After 2-3
trips up and down the cover, it is popped along its length.

On Sun, 3 Feb 2002, Greg Potts wrote:

> Hi Volks,
>
> I've removed this part from 4 different rusty Ontario buses, and the way
> that has worked for me is with a 12" length of 1x4, slipped UNDER the cover
> so it will apply force as closely as possible to the slot where the cover is
> binding.
>
> Whack the board, move forward 6", give it another whack. Keep moving along
> the cover from end to end working back and forth, and eventually it will let
> loose.
>
> With the buses I've dismantled the screws almost always break as they're
> coming apart. One trick I've found is to TIGHTEN the screws very slightly
> before trying to loosen them, this will break the corrosion without
> stripping the threads you need to back the screw out. It's a technique that
> also helps with rounded out engine tin screws.
