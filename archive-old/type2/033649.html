I have not seen the commercial with the Caddy, but I have seen the one 
where the girlfriend empties out the U-haul truck and another truck runs 
over the guy's motorcycle.

The commercial is so over-the-top it makes me cringe and laugh at the 
same time.

I don't think that these commercials are about man-hating at all. I 
think, on the contrary, that the ad agency has done some crafty 
psychology by suggesting that women in relationships should "do the math" 
and match their SO's spending on toys with spending on shoes.

Nevertheless, this is one in a whole strain of advertisements that I have 
noticed where it is considered "cute" or "funny" when people are very, 
very mean-spirited. I do not want to bore anyone to tears, but: this is a 
reflection of where pop culture seems to want us to be. It is also 
probably a byproduct of a booming economy. The middle to late 1920s were 
similar in some ways. And that's good news. It won't last forever.

So on to bus content, sort of. A VW bus is a symbol of the polar opposite 
of the attitude discussed above. It's about waving, and signing on to the 
AIRS page, and a reminder of a time not so long ago when it was not 
considered cute to be mean. 

Somewhere, sometime today, someone in a commercial or a TV show or on a 
radio talk show is going to make fun of giving people hugs. So hug 
somebody. Do a good deed. Go the second mile. Turn the other cheek.  

Be the bus.
