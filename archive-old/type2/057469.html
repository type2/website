     Recycling an older vehicle is preferable to buying a new machine,
with all the attendant pollution involved in creating that new vehicle,
waste of resources, etc. Plus it's a camper. In fact, I never intended
it to be a daily driver -- when I lived in WI, I rode my bike just about
everywhere, winter and summer, only used the bus when I needed to carry
big stuff. But Mobile -- well, you'd be stupid to try to ride a bike
around Mobile much. Believe me, I've tried. There are no bike lanes
anywhere. Most people are very hostile to bike riders, blowing their
horn at you, giving you the finger, etc. They also drive faster than
anywhere I've ever been before. And I see more accidents and have more
close calls than I've ever experienced anywhere -- riding a bike is
pretty scary. Bums me out, because I assumed I'd be able to ride to work
-- I've tried it a few times, but it's not worth the risk. It's a nice 1
hour bike ride to work. Instead, I spend an hour a day in the car, then
ride an hour on a back road after work for exercise. Even there I get
people blowing their horns at me. And I almost never see anyone else on
a bike, except right around the University. Nor is there any real public
transportation -- a few token buses, but far and few between. And since
vehicle insurance isn't required, and there are one heck of a lot of
poor people driving $15 cars -- if you get hit on a bike, you're
probably SOL even if you survive.
