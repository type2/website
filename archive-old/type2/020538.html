[...]I carry a portable inverter that I got at some discount 
superstore.  It's rated 300W continuous, has a lighter plug with an 
adapter to battery clips on the input cord and two 120VAC outlets.  
Since my bus did not have a lighter socket, I rigged a 12V power 
outlet.  So, while under way, I can run 120VAC stuff in the bus, like my 
son's laptop, and a CD player.

While camping, I use a separate battery and the battery clip to lighter 
plug adapter to power the inverter.  Usually, we run strings of 
miniature lights put up around the edges of our canopy.  We sometimes 
bring a blender, to make those late afternoon cocktails - and impress 
the neighbors.

I suppose I could rig it up so the inverter could power the 120VAC 
outlet in the bus, but that would require some sort of transfer switch, 
i.e., please heed the advice given in a previous post about not creating 
a situation where you backfeed and thus cause the exposed pins on your 
Westy's 120VAC input to be exposed and live.
