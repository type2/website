STOP!  Dont go slapping aftermarket parts on a problem!!  you have 4
(methinks) bushings as well as a coupler in the shift linkage.

the two that cause the most problems are the coupler (at the nose of the
tranny.  10 bucks, easy to replace), and the front-most bushing.  this one
is a bear, as the tranny pretty much has to be out to replace it. (the
other 2 or 3 along the shift rod require the shift rod to come out 100%
which DOES require total engine/trans removal)

You MAY, with the coupler and shifter removed, be able to get the front
bushing replaced...

anyway.  this bushing sits in the front end of the shift rod, in a hole.
there is a pin that the hole and bushing ride on...crawl underneath and
take a look, it is just forward of the shifter.  wiggle on the shift rod
at this place.  gotta lot of slop?  that would cause some problems..

the coupler in the back again will cause problems.  have someone wiggle
and work the shifter while you watch that coupler.  see all that wasted
movement?  are the big side bushings worn out? missing totally?  Yup.
replacement time..

after those 2 parts dont slop around, you are allowed to consider
disfunctional aftermarket parts...:)
