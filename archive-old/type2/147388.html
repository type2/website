Yeh, but it's a good thing to look there on a newly acquired bus and 
perhaps periodically- I found pieces of piston ring and compression ring 
in mine - no cause for alarm then because it was junk from damage 
already repaired (I did a compression check just to make sure) - but I 
do check occasionally, any new junk and I know I've got a problem if I 
hadn't any other indicators.  Same reason I strain my oil - you want to 
know sooner rather than later.  If I found any bits in my oil I would 
immediately pull the screen and check carefully.  I use my fingers or a 
small screwdriver or dental pick to very gently feel around the edges in 
there - that's how I discovered the pieces - had to tease 'em out.

Just proceed with gentleness and caution and get the right tool to 
ensure that your torque is to spec.  Though I agree that if you can't do 
it right, don't do it at all.
