ZR72:
In addition to Joe Fournier's nice list of things to do to put a 1600 in
a late loaf, I'll add a couple more:
1. The input shaft on the trans has to be changed to a shorter one (the
type 4 engine uses a pilot bearing that's not in the gland nut (they
don't hod the flywheel on with a gland nut).
2. Air cleaner- if you're gonna drive this thing and enjoy it you'll
need preheated air 3/4 of the year in the US while starting and warming
up.
As anyone can see, unless you have access to many of these parts for
free,or if you are a master engineer/ craftsman, then it's probably NOT
economical to put a Type 1 engine in a late bay bus just to save a few
hundred dollars on a rebuild.
