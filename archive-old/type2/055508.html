Tom:

Your "black box" (ECU?) and #2 temp sensor have NOTHING to do with the 
Cold Start Valve.  

The cold start valve is wired to be energized (i.e., let fuel into the 
air distribution box) ONLY WHEN THE IGNITION SWITCH IS IN THE "START" 
position.  The ECU and the #2 temp sensor don't interact at all with the 
cold start valve, don't even know it's there.

If the engine is warm, then the cold start valve "senses" that itself and 
won't energize and inject fuel.  If the engine is cold, then the cold 
start valve will "sense" that and will energize and inject fuel (with the 
key in the "Start" position) for a short period of time.

I'd suspect either a leaky cold start valve, that is a purely mechanical 
problem, or some sort of wiring problem.
