> Karl wrote:

> >New throwout
> >forks are available, including "heavy duty" versions that last longer.
> >
> Isn't the big difference between the OEM version and the H-D versions
> the welds? Seems like I bought one 15 yrs ago and the original version
> had spot welds and the H-D version was fulled welded. If so, weld the
> OEM version and you've got a H-D version.

The good ones also have thicker material in the forks. Instead of a
little divot on the front (FIF) side of the "hook" there is a hole with
material ahead of it. This stops the hooks from breaking off at the
"divot" when they wear.

You're right about the welding, though. If I pull a throwout fork out and
it isn't completely welded I will weld it up. However, if the hooks are
worn the fork is trash unless you can weld up the fork a bit and reshape
the ends.
