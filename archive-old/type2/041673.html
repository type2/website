I don't know if this helps, but I had my boxes apart to switch the covers
over from an earlier model. This required a load of messing about and I
won't do it again. The later heater boxes have a thread in them with a hole
through the outer tin. Some earlier ones don't have the support bracket, but
there is the same lump of metal inside into which you can tap a hole. You'd
just drill through the outer case and into the inner pipework. There's a
flat on the outer tin where to drill, though I've no idea how deep you can
go.

I don't think you would want to rely on a threaded insert into the outer
tin.

I decided in the end not to fit a bracket as I reasoned that the earlier
ones managed to get away without them, and so far in 1 1/2 years it's not
given any trouble.
