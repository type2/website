Subject: Re: [T2] Does the grim reaper drive a bus?

I have a friend who is an accident investigator for the insurance 
companies.  This friend doesn't own a bus but is the one who helped Nikki 
Elko out in San Angelo after the deer hit her bus (so he's okay).  I asked 
him if he would comment on his thread - here is his reply:

========================================================================
You might be able to find stats at the National Highway Transportation and
Safety Administration (NHTSA) website.  I will see if there are any other
sources.

I will comment on relative safety of older vehicles, and I don't think that
this will be any revelation to anyone.  The modern car is FAR superior to an
older car as far as safety is concerned.  Safety technology has come a LONG
way in just the past ten years.  Airbags, side impact beams, anti-lock
brakes, deformable/energy absorbing structure, seat belt improvements, fuel
cutoff switches and fuel tank rollover cutoff valves all work together to
provide a safer vehicle than what was even imaginable in the not too far
past.  Don't get me wrong, I thoroughly enjoy older cars, I have a 1970
Chevelle and a 1969 Torino.  But, I know that my 1991 Explorer and my 1995
Civic are safer overall.  Also, don't forget vehicle handling/dynamics as a
safety feature with regards to accident avoidance.

I'm sorry to sound grim, and I am not discouraging anyone from driving their
bus.  But, at least for me, I know that when I drive my classic cars that I
am accepting lower safety/protection compared to more modern cars.   Please
also remember that no matter how safely or carefully you drive, you cannot
predict every possible danger.
