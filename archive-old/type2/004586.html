For some unknown-to-me reason, 'run-on' has been associated with
timing.....not sure why.
Run-on is actually 'dieseling'. You have fuel, you have air, you are short
on spark except for the heat from the hot cylinders. If you look at a diesel
engine...they have fuel, they have are, but they do NOT have a distributor.
They fire from compression.
Look at newer carbs like 34pic...they have an idle solenoid on the side. It
stops dieseling by shutting off idle fuel.
The correct fix for run-on when you do not have an idle solenoid is = lower
your idle speed!
If your timing is set to specs....leave it alone....it is not your problem.
900 is too high.
