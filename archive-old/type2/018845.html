Brian:
You're right, it's a bear. I usually can get it with a stubby phillips, 
but sometimes have to grab the screwdriver with a channellocks. Removing
the distributor will give you more room, and if you leave the clamp
attached to the distributor, the timing will remain the same. Just stuff
a rag in the distributor hole immediately to prevent the screw from
falling into the hole. Also, don't forget about the copper sealing
washer on the screw for the cold start injector.
Another thing to bear in mind is that ANY injector could be leaking,
they should all hold pressure.
If you find more than one leaking, I'd recommend some of the
pour-in-the-tank injector cleaner. I've had very little problems with
bad injectors and attribute this to my religious use of GASOHOL.
I think that the slightly solvent effect of the alcohol keeps the
varnish-like molecules in suspension and keeps the tank and injectors
clean. I've used it in all my injected VW's (6 or7 to date) and also my
69 double cab which is completely stock and probably would run on third
world fuel.
