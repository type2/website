> Hi Travis, and everyone else too,
>   The hot start problem certainly is a common one.  To avoid it I took to
a
> yearly maintenance routine for the starter.  Now, once a year I deal with
> the starter when and where I decide to.  You know, at home in the driveway
> on a warm dry day.
>    I completely disassemble it and clean, inspect and repair it as
required.
>   I lubricte all the mechanical parts at reassembly.  Also at the same
time
> I clean all of the wire connections for the starter and solenoid as well
as
> the posts on the starter/solenoid.  Since you have to disconnect the
battery
> to do this safely, I remove the battery, clean it(posts as well as the
> outside of the battery case) and service the water as needed.  This is an
> opportune time to clean the terminals on the battery wires and the chassis
> to engine/transmission ground strap.
>    Since I've started doing this I've never had the dreaded hot start
> problem.  It may be the answer to your problem, or it might not.
