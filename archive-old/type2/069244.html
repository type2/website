>Allen,
>
>I just spoke to my mechanic tonight, and can describe his oil
>cooler bus heating system to you. He prefers not to further
>overload his limited home-life with extra calls, so I will
>describe his system. 
>
>First of all he uses a 'passive' external oil cooling system
>rather than a full-flow system, as I have on my van (and he
>constantly chides me about). I'll describe that first, then add
>in the internal/external heat bit.
>His passive system uses a "T" fitting at the oil pressure sender
>switch position to run both the VDO dual sender (switch and gauge
>sender unit) and the line going out to his passive coolers. 
>He uses steel or copper 3/8 ID tubing to accomplish the local 
>sections, and power steering or aircraft Stainless steel webbed
>hose with appropriate screw-on fittings as desired or necessary.
>The line from the T goes to two external coolers mounted either
>under the car up in front of the trannie, or in the usual ugly
>position on the upper rear corner on the outside of the bus. A
>bypass manual valve enables bypassing them. The output from the 
>coolers goes back into the engine compartment, and goes to a fitting 
>screwed into the fuel pump block-off plate, as he has since 
>gone to electronic fuel pumping, as have I too.
>
>The interior oil coolers with fan are installed when he goes to 
>Alaska, and are described as follows-
>
>  He runs the copper or steel 3/8 ID lines strapped to the hot air
>heating duct which goes the length of the car underneath to just 
>in back of the front seat area (he has a bench front seat).Then they
>run up into the vehicle, where he fits them to flexible power steering
>hoses connected to the oil cooler. The flexible hoses allow him to 
>place the heater/cooler in the front cab or in the back as needed.
>He has the hot air heat duct under the car wrapped in an insulating tube 
>wh ich covers both the duct and the oil lines for the interior unit.
>This keeps the oil lines warmer. The lines for this interior unit are
>placed in parallel with the existing external oil cooler system,
>he has a valve to close down the lines when the interior unit is not 
>in the car. He can thus regulate how much hot oil goes to both cooling
>units external and internal. He also said he blocks off the doghouse fan 
>housing "with a rag" when using the interior unit. Obviously he is in
>very cold weather when doing this. I dont know how he blocks off the
>air intake of the fan housing with a rag. Probably a removeable
>intake cover would be better.
>He says he has to drive in a teeshirt with the window open in subfreezing
>weather when using this interior unit.
>I believe him. He is good at what he does.
