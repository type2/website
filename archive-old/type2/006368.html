I fought this battle with the Turtle.  It had been sitting at least six
years and no gas was coming out.  I started pulling fuel lines and yellow
gas and targoo drizzled out, sloooowly.  The answer was to remove all the
fuel lines and tossem. Then remove the hexnut plug where the tank feeds.  A
screen type filter lives underneath, and is plugged with goo.  Make sure you
have a big funnel and gas cans ready.  The tank will drain along with the
goo.  Then with the bottom still open I sprayed a big can of gumout into the
gas filler.  A couple of flushes with the old dirty gas, with the goo
strained out, and the tank is probably pretty clean.  As for the dirty gas,
I mixed about a gal. at a time into a nearly full 20 gal tank in my pickup.
Diluted like that with good gas, it just burned away.  That's the
environmently correct method IMO.
