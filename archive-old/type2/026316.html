I read an article that claimed in 1997 only 17% of cars sold in NA were
standard transmission.
It predicted 13% for 1998, and asteep decline after that.
It  was a chicken and egg problem. 
Less standards are being sold, so less models offer it. 
( A lot of cars don't come in standards anymore)
Becasue of this, demand drops further, making it not worth while
to spend the $1million required to emmisions certify the extra 
drivetrain configuration. It pointed out that if you only sold 
a few thousand manual transmissions, then the testing cost would be 
$500-1000 PER CAR!. It even raised the possiblity that 
an automatic would become standard equipment,
and a "Standard transmission" could become an extra cost option
on a few models.
