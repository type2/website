Subject: Re: [T2] Pushrod tube removal

The tubes can stick when you try to pull them out, especially if somone
used some sort of sealer in a past install.  There is not much gripping
room, and resist the urge to use a plier if you want to reuse them. 
Clean oily tubes real well (Gumout works well) in the center 3".  See if
you can turn any by hand, and if so, twist them out; you'll have more
room to grip adjacent sticky tubes. I've had good luck wrapping
reinforced strapping tape around the tube center and then yanking on the
tape to get it going.  I suppose one could try a small strap wrench also
and yank sideways on it.  If you can find a perfectly fitting "core
tube" that fits in the tube, push that in and then use a grip plier.  
   You can use a puller that expands internally, much like the bicycle
handlebar stem that has a threaded wedge pulled up into the puller as
you tighten the long bolt at the end.  I envision using a piece of pipe
that just fits into the pushrod tube.  Cut on a steep diagonal to
produce a wedge end.  Use a piece of allthread or very long bolt to hold
the 2 pieces together.  Figure a way to prevent the nut at the wedge end
from spinning, like weld/epoxy it to the end.  Insert, and tighten the
exposed nut or hex head.
