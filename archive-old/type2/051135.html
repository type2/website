Marcus, the first thing you should do is go to Home Depot and buy a 50-lb
bag of Sterilized Cow Manure from the garden center. That way you will have
something that's less full of crap than your co-worker. People on this very
list run self-built motors all the time. Most of us are not professional VW
mechanics.

Here's the deal: old, old VW engines needed leaded fuel to lubricate the
valve seats; without it the seats would get little pits in them. VW started
installing hardened valve seats (IIRC they are made from Stellite, a very
hard metal) in their engines before the Type 4 engine was released. So
you're fine.

Compression ratio (CR), the ratio of cylinder volume with the piston all the
way down to volume with it all the way up, is a different matter. The
Volkswagen engine was designed for regular gas, research octane 91. Current
regular is (research + motor / 2) 87; midgrade is 89, premium is 93. You can
go two ways: leave the CR alone and burn high-octane gas, or lower the CR
and burn regular. You lower the CR by putting metal spacers on the cylinder
barrels (they go between the bottom of the cylinder insert and the case) to
increase the "deck height." Instructions for doing this are in John Muir's
Idiot Book, and so is the CR formula. (Someone who's dropped the CR in a VW
engine would know how far to go--I just leave it alone and run premium.) If
you do your rebuild correctly you will measure all four cylinders anyway, so
setting the CR is not difficult.
