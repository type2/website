In a message dated 5/13/00, John in Flint writes:

> A while back a listee said something like  "...Armour All is terrible for
>  your dashboard."  Why?  What's the long term downside and do you know of a

What it does is to pull the "plasticisers" out of the plastic - makes it 
shiny, but it no longer has the "moisture" to make it supple (like old 
pavement - the oil evaporates over years and cracks and weeds grow through - 
you see this a lot in Nevada!!) - and the now stiff nougahyde breaks and 
cracks **JUST* like old, dried out leather.

Does the same to tires, vynil tops, all that stuff...  anything thats a 
petroleum-based substance.  Even does in plastic after a while.

How I know?  They did a big-dollar test where I work (Caltrans) to find out 
why these "new" trucks were replacing dashboards and tires and upholstery 
every few years... and after the ban, the problem (with the newer trucks) 
went away 100%.  Once they've been squirted, tho, the process starts.  

Stay away from that stuff!  Serious.  (And sorry, Armor - all!)

I saw this silicone sealer that might show some promise, but after having the 
proof, as it were, put in my face, I don't put anything on it, but to wash it 
once in a while!  And guess what?  It works!  And cost?  <grin> Works for a 
single mom!
