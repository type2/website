I have a double-burner Coleman that is dual-fuel
(i.e., it can run on unleaded gasoline as well as
camping fuel), but I've only used camping fuel in it.
Camping fuel has no odor when it burns and I smell
burnt gas(oline) (petrol for you non-Americans!)
enough just *driving* my bus.

I've had no problems using mine, but I only use it
outside for obvious safety reasons (that and the bus
is never parked level...kinda hard to keep the pans
from sliding off the stove ;-), but if it were
raining, I would have no qualms about using it inside
the bus or my tent.

I also have a single-burner "Olympus" backpacking
stove that I use for making coffee. It lights faster,
takes no time to set-up/break-down and it actually
boils water *faster* than my Coleman....go figure.

I'd never really thought about it before, but I guess
that *is* a bit confusing! We pretty much call
everything gas: butane, methane, propane, gas(oline),
etc. 

Larger stoves (generally) use either propane (LPG), or
camp-fuel...which is also called "white" gas, further
confusing the issue.

Smaller stoves (like my backpacking stove, which is a
self-contained cannister roughly 6"Hx4"Dia. when
closed), usually run on camp-fuel (like mine), or
butane cannisters. My stove came with instructions
saying that in Europe, camp-fuel is pretty much
non-existant and to use unleaded gasoline....it also
said to NEVER use unleaded outside of Europe....what
do you guys do to your unleaded, anyway???

Also, while my "Olympus" stove says *not* to use
American unleaded, my "Coleman" says it's *okay* to
use it, but it's not preferred. I find this confusing,
as they are essentially the same type of stove, just
that one is (much) smaller.
