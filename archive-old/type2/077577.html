> A relay can be bought to solve this.
> It works as follows.
> The wire that would normally activate your solenoid on starter goes to the
> relay.
> A wire from your + side of battery goes to the relay.
> A wire goes from the relay to the terminal on the starter solenoid.
> The last wire goes from the relay to ground.
> When you turn your key the juice from your ignionswitch goes through the
> relay to ground and closes the circuit in the relay allowing battery juice
> to flow 4 feet directly from battery to the starter through the relay
rather
> than 24 feet from back of bus to front and back again through 30 year old
> wires.
> Mine is a chrysler part I bought for $7, it came with a schematic to make
it
> simple.
> This fix is assuming you have checked all other possibillities.(starter
> loose, dirty connections, bus is mad at you:-)
