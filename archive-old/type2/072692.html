No, the pins are in sequence, Starting from the furthest bottom (wire
harness side) it starts up the longest side  of the plug #1 (coil) thru 18
(not used unless you have a external throttle valve switch, '75 & 76 I
think).  The other side starts at 19 (not used) through 36 (not used).  The
numbers  in between are pretty much as I gave you earlier depending on what
options you have and what vehicle your driving: BMW, Toyota, or VW Bus.

Look at the diagram:
(http://www.conservatory_dot_com/vw/manuals_nyx/Images/FeulInjection/L-Jetronic/
LJET-27.GIF)

The grounds wires that lead to the block terminal are: #17; # 16 and #5.
#15 leads to the Aux Air Valve.

Maybe, but check your pump switch in your AFM also.  Is the cover off your
AFM box?  Check to see if the poteniometer arm is triggering the pump
switch correctly.  (Careful if you haven't removed the cover from the AFM

or contact arm which is not allowing the proper readings. Also check the
throttle valve for obstructions or stickiness.  And check to see if the
accelerator cable is adjusted correctly.
