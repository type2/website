Alan,, you really don't need the "wheel" in place,, if fact i prefer to have
the wheel off.. it allows easier access to the bleeder without having to be
completely under the vehicle.

you MUST have the "drum" in place,, or ----the caliper on the rotor in the
case of disc brakes.

The piston in the wheel cylinder,, or the piston in the caliper is going to
move and MUST not be able to come right out ...

so the drum and the brake shoes must be in place to bleed brakes while pumping
on the brake pedal
