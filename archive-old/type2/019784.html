Hey there,
Usually this is vintagebus material...but there seems to be some
misunderstanding here between the old and "new" buses...

1949/50-1952? -- 25hp
1953-1958 -- 36hp
1959-1963 -- 40hp with optional 1500 engine in '63
1964-1967 had 1500 engines I believe
1968-1971 -- 1600 engine - 71 had a 1600 dual port.  all engines before this
were single port.
then in 72, all he!! broke loose....

I take it you've never driven around an old bus?  If it has the 36hp engine...it
is SLOW.  Although 36hp was not stock for 59 buses. I just put a 40 horse engine
in my 62 and its SLOW...won't top 57mph on straight aways....50 up little
hills... 60 down them. SLOW.  These early buses have transmissions that are
geared really low.  I run the stock/original "small nut" tranny in my 62 (59-63)
and before with my 1500 engine...it would push the bus 75mph if I wanted to go
that fast.  Its like driving a whole different bus.  If you're used to driving
late model buses (72 and on) you're in for a BIG suprise when you hop into this
59.  Don't get me wrong...these things are a hoot to drive... just warning you
;-)

Sounds like a good deal really...

Keep in mind that even if you build a bigger engine....you could go faster...
BUT... you're still turning the same RPMs.  55mph is around 4000 RPMs in my 62.
in my 71... 75mph is around 4000 RPMs I believe....

Just a little informative bit....
