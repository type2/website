Warburton, William wrote:

*****
 Out of interest, is there anyone here who can say they were able to
prevent
damage to a stock engine, through the use of gauges, which would have
happened
otherwise? ie not "I heard it pinking and noticed the oil pressure was
low"
or "I smelt hot oil and saw the CHT was rising" but "everything was going
perfectly and then the oil pressure started to drop. I pulled over and 
found that my engine was about to explode with no other indication!".

*****

That would be me!!!  I have a full suite of gauges in the '80 and I firmly
believe that without them I'd have blown the engine right out of the '80.
The current engine really does not need them in normal circumstances, but
driving down I-5 in California where the air temp is 105+, I have to slow
down and the oil temp and pressure gauges tell me when and how much.
Back when I had the MSHP engine, they were *vital*!  after 20k+ miles, I
turned the engine over to John and there no dropped valves, in large part
because I never overheated the heads... per the CHT gauge.

On a stock engine in a normal environment, gauges are not necessary...
with a modified engine or in extreme environments, they can be very very
useful.
