Klahd13_at_aol_dot_com says about pulling dents with dry ice:

> Really?  Sounds cool...How do you do that?

It's a variation on the old "heat shrink" trick bodymen have used for ages,
and it probably only works with very small dents. Just get some dry ice and
rub the dent with it; if it's small enough it should come out.

The classic way to heatshrink requires a rosebud tip on your oxyacetylene
torch, a bucket of water and a sponge. Heat the dent to a dull red then
quickly wipe across it with the wet sponge. Recommended first step: sand the
paint off.
