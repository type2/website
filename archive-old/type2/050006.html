Assuming you have what I think is the correct distributor for the year 
of your bus, this would be a dual (vac & centrifugal) advance 
distributor, with a dual diaphragm vacuum can (advance and retard).  
It's possible that your vacuum can is bad and/or the breaker points 
mounting plate in the dizzy is sticking.  Also, a bad vacuum retard hose 
could be at fault.  My 71 has the same (or similar) dizzy  (on a 1600 dp 
engine), and a bad vacuum can was the cause for a similar problem I was 
having.  I replaced the can, and the engine behaved nicely after that.
