Mark, are you talking about some pistons being lean and others rich?
Or are
you talking about rich and lean areas on the face of the same piston?

I don't know VW FI that well, but in aircraft, this kind of pattern
can result
>from incorrect engine installation. If the engine isn't level (fore
and aft
and side to side) you can see rich pistons on the "low" side and lean
on the
"high" side. This could be the case in VW's? Anybody else comment on
if this is
a problem?

Also, rich and lean areas on the face of the same piston are a pretty
good
indicator of clogged/screwed-up/miscalibrated fuel injector nozzles.
Time to
send 'em in to an expert -- it's worth it to get 'em right.

Again, I don't know VW FI that well, I'm a wannabusser that used to
own a '68
Bug...but I have been around FI aircraft a fair amount of time. Most
of the
principles are the same.
