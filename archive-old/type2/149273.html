Hi Todd,

It's not quite  so simple as just ordering in and bolting up some
Brazilian parts.

The brazilian baywindow only looks identical; it uses a splitwindow
front suspension. Because of this I suspect that the nose from a
watercooled brazilian bay would not be a straight bolt-up to a german-
made bay.  And we have no idea what that brazilian bay uses for a
transmission or shift linkage. Could be the same, could be different.
If it's different, then you're back to Kennedy for something home-grown.

Also... We're not talking about surplus inventory or OEM's here; the
only source for the parts you're talking about is through the VW food
chain. Have you priced new parts from a dealer lately? Purchasing
body panels and structural components from a dealership is going to
cost serious coin. Then you have to export them to the U.S. You need
to speak fluent Portuguese if you're planning on going this route.


Finally, Here are the brazilian engine specs as reported at
www.limbobus_dot_org:

Longitudinal, 4 Cylinders, Inline
2 Valves/cylinder
1390 cm3 (1.4L) Displacement
Compression Ratio 11:1
HP at 4800rpm, 78 (gas)
HP at 4800rpm, 80 (alc)
Multi point sequential fuel injection

78 HP is the same output as the 2L aircooled engine. And I'd expect
that the 2L probably has better torque at low rpms.

It IS cheaper to buy something with higher performance that bolts
right in. Call Boston Bob or Jake Raby and build a high-performance
aircooled engine.
