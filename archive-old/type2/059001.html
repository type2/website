The answer is yes and no, of course (bureaucrat that I am)....

First, the dwell angle, if absolutely correct is 50 degrees, which means,
in theory, that the odds are ((360 - 50*4)/3.6)% or 44.44%, but...

Second, I think the odds of the points landing closed are near 100%.  The
reason is that the top of the lobe on the distributor cam is also the top
of the compression stroke for one of the cylinders.  Since that is an
uncomfortable place for the engine to stop, I believe it is much more
likely to either slide forward or back from there, leaving the points in
the closed, overheat-the-coil, burn-the-points position.  In other words,
*don't* leave the ignition on with the engine not running!  or at least,
unplug the coil if you do!
