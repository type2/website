One way of characterizing the working or not of a FI system
is the width, frequency, and voltage of the injector pulses.

the duty cycle of the ignition pulses.
Is there anything that could be learned from using a tach/dwell meter 
hooked across the injector plug. Obviously the existing scales would 
not be useful, if one measured the readings from a good,
system, and one with various problems: Temp sensors, 
bad grounds, ballast resistors etc
would it be possible to diagnose a system using this.
Maybe tests with, and without the injector hooked up would tell something
about
injector health and/or balast resistor/ground health?
