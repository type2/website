Replacing the rear wheel cylinders is an afternoon's work--  not more 
than that unless you run into hidden problems while you're at it. Take 
them apart  one wheel at a time so you can have something to compare to 
if you have trouble remembering what went where.

When the rear brakes are adjusted properly they  are completely 
adequate to stop a fully loaded bus. Since the front disks provide 70% 
of the stopping power, I'd recommend you spend your money on better 
tires and/or rims instead, you'll get better bang for your buck.
