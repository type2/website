I assume your "hot start" wire is connected to the starter solenoid 
along with the activation wire from the ignition switch.

If so then what you are experiencing is a temperature dependant bad
connection, This causes sufficient voltage loss to be unable to activate
the starter solenoid.

This was particularly (so I'm told) a problem with 6 volters where you
have less juice to start with.

To permanently circumvent this problem use the wire that comes from the
ignition to the starter solenoid to activate a relay. This relay will
switch in a supply straight from the battery to the starter solenoid, 
thus circumventing the long route and many connections the electricity
would other wise flow through.

I think Muir describes this.
