Personally I find the best trick is to use the correct tool. If you have a
hex recess in the bolt get an Allen key socket for your socket set; more
likely you will have a tri-square recess in the bolt (looks like a 12
pointed star) so you should get a M8 tri square socket.
Fit the appropriate socket onto a 4 inch wobble socket extension bar which
will allow for the slope of the drive shaft.
The put on the biggest breaker bar you have on the other end of the wobble
bar and unless you are very unlucky that sucker will come out.

I wouldn't mess with vice grips until I had tried the correct tools first,
then you may well get a better success rate with long nosed parallel jawed
vice grips.

into a 10.9 grade metric bolt, others are not.  Heck maybe not even Vice
Grips my newest set is 10 years old.  Heating is also good though if you can
heat down at the threads.  Again my best tip always, put the correct hex or
triple square socket in (about even likelihood of either being used or still
in a '78 I'd say) then HIT IT, repeatedly with a hammer, 50 times, good
solid
whacks, cound all 50.  Cleaning out the socket first, then hitting it, I've
not
lost one (even stripped out by PO ones) in about 10 years.  And the same
applies to brake bleeder screws, 50 little taps square on the screw before I
ever touch a wrench to it.  And throw any slightly stripped CV screw right
into
the trash and find new ones.
