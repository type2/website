	Shortly after posting, I decided that eight hours in a parking lot
at 50 deg ambient was cool enough for me to go poking about and see if I
really had screwed up. Admittedly I did not check the wiring, but with the
rotor itself on the #1 firing mark and the timing mark set to 0 deg, Al's
postulation was entirely correct - both intake and exhaust for #1 were
PLENTY loose, somewhere in the neighborhood of .02" (as compared to
.006"). To answer Ken, I can say that provided the bus' previous owner
hadn't set the valves too tight, valve clearance for #1 when the rotor is
set at #3 is abysmally small - tighter than .0015" (the smallest I checked
when resetting them incorrectly the first time and thinking, "Gosh, that's
tight, but I guess everyone says these things are never done right
anyway."). 
	When starting up after making them real loose the first time, the
bus roared to life and ran a lot louder. Not just loud valve clicking, but
louder in general. Start-up times in the morning were cut to nothing;  I
could feel the initial squirt of fuel from the automatic choke Muir hates
so much. It was so energetic I figured I'd done something right.  After
resetting again last night, it's back to its mellow self. So: how should
we expect a type IV to run with real loose valves? More air in the mix?
