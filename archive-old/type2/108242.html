*AHA! Yeeee-ESSSS! That number, 0 280 160 303 is a "vacuum limiter" for a
Porsche 928. I thought that it might work, because the technology had to be
roughly the same for the same year Porsche and VW type 4. These are still
available and it looks as if they will continue to be, since Porsche has a
very active (and somewhat more affluent) collector community and Bosch seems
to be very responsive to and supportive of classic car (German and European
make) collectors.

Now, will the valve I got from the '75 replace the one in my '78 without any

* I believe that it works somewhat like a carburetor dashpot on earlier
American cars to prevent stalling from sudden deceleration.
But I don't know how well each of these particular parts (300 and 303) will
cross over. They will probably both work, with some minor differences in
performance. My concern with a used one is that since this is something with

(especially when they are allowed to dry out after having once been used),
