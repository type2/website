That box with the five wires is the resistor it is required to reduce the
voltage for the injectors.

The voltage regualtor is in the back of his alternator, it can be round
or rectangle shaped. They are about $35.00 new if you need one.

To repair the resistor unit you can take a small copper wire and wrap it
around a coat-hanger wire till you wind up with what looks like springs
out of ballpoint pens and then put it over the wires where that sloder to
the resistor unit. Resolder the wires with this reinfoecement and they'll
stay in place. The unit really doesn't build up that much heat so solder
holds even though the original electrical connection is a spot weld.

I'd look at the starter and make sure that the alternator is plugged onto
the push on terminal and be sure that the auxillary wire is connected to
the wire loom. (it has a little square connector).

Clean all the connectors that connect to the alternator.
