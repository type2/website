> Having said that, check your sources VERY carefully.
> 
> Unfortunately there are also cads & one particular Australian firm

> Anyone on the list I'm sure could point you to a decent engine builder
> in the California area(Yes, I know it's big) & if you are going the
> after market FI/supercharged route, you will need to discuss carefully
> with anyone who is building the engine what specs will give you the
> most power to engine life ratio.
> 
> The supercharger & FI may seem like a big outlay to begin with, but
> once you have them you can go on using the system practically for ever
> & if you use a late model Japanese s'charger from a Toyota, for instance,
> they are clutched & can be programmed into the FI management computer.
> 
> As I say, what may seem a little crazy to begin with, may with some

> better than the later model engine.
> 
> I must stress at this point that while Judson's are cute, they are
> little better than a gentle breeze compared to modern superchargers
> & were made to placate the 1950's US market, not make VW's into fire
> breathing monsters.
> 
>  
> 
> I run a 2.0l engine that cost me AUD$4500 to have rebuilt at the end

> longevity & reliability of the engine.
