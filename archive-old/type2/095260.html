For that same price, you can get the Crane CDI.  It comes with the optical 
trigger(replaces the points) and the CDI 'brain'.  The brain is an epoxy 
encapsulated unit with a beefy heatsink on it that mounts remotely(in my 
case on the firewall), triggering the coil, and adjusting the dwell.  The 
unit DOES require a coil without internal ballast resistance(Bosch blue), so 
I bought the 40KV Crane for $29.95.  It hasn't failed me yet, and its been 
on the vehicle for 6 years.  Check what every 15 thousand miles?  I haven't 
even had to replace the cap or rotor yet.  No tracking, and no pitting of 
the contact surfaces.  I recommend them to everybody.  In case the 
Muscle-Car name scares you, Crane aquired Allison Ignition and revamped the 
line a little.  Same technology.

I think the Igniter II goes for about $100-$120. If you're stepping up to
CDI then the Igniter I is all you need since dwell issues go away and all
you need is a simple trigger mechanism, which for all intensive purposes is
what the Igniter I really is. It just happens to give you the correct
"fixed" dwell if its hooked up to a coil. As a matter of fact, I wonder if
the Igniter II would act screwy since its expecting to "see" and control a
coil, not a CDI.
