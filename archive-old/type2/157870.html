My advice is to never mess around with the AFM. Look
at things that could change suddenly like the CHT
sensor at #3. At about 70 degrees it should have 2400
ohms resistance - warmer = less, colder = more. At
around 4000+ ohms it should be laying down a thick
smoke screen and chugging. Unhook the electrical
connection on the coldstart injector. Check fuel rail
pressure at the port between #3&#4 cylinders with a
gauge that will handle gasoline. Prior to that you
might just look the fuel hoses over to see if there
are any kinks or crushed sections on the return side.
Make sure the vacuum line to the regulator is
attached. When there is high vacuum, pressure
maintained in the fuel rail should be about 28psi. At
low vacuum or with the hose disconnected, pressure
should be around 35psi. If you have readings higher
than this, the regulator might be bad but more likely
there is a blockage in one of the flexible lines,
usually deterioration of the hose itself, creating an
internal blockage.

Bentley lists all the test procedures for the AFM.

Is the air cleaner clean - has it ingested anything
foreign or had a bad drink of water? Try without the
filter element if in doubt. -BaH
