If I'm not mistaken, a car will warm up faster and with less wear and tear
to the engine if you drive it rather than let it idle in the driveway. I
can't remember exactly why but I think it has something to do with the raw
fuel washin away the oil from the cylinders when the engine is cold. Lack of
lubrication is bad. Keep in mind that Muir is great for step by stepping
through some things but just flat out wrong on others (read automatic
choke). If you don't already have a Bentley manual, go get one. Use it to
cross reference with Muir and IMHO always go with Bentley's recommendations
if there is a conflict between the two.
