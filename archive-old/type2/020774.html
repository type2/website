Working with formica is not too difficult. You'll need something to rough cut it
with: table or circular saw, contact cement, spacers, and a router with a flush
cut bit and maybe a file for touchup AND appropriate safety gear.  There's a
special small router available for laminate trimming.  I've seen them in pawn
shops for as little as $15, sometimes with the bit.

You'll probably need to remove the plastic T moulding and other trim stuff
first.  Start by rough cutting the formica about 1 inch larger than the surface
to be covered on all sides.  Coat both surfaces with contact cement and allow to
dry to the touch.  Use small strips of wood as spacers between the formica and
surface to be covered.  This allows you to line them up.  Remove the spacers one
at a time and press the formica onto the new surface as you go.  Rub it all down
good and hard using a roller or just a piece of wood as a rubbing block.  Then,
fire up the router to trim it flush. The flush cut router bit has a roller
bearing to ride on the surface below the edge so set the depth of the bit
accordingly (this will be clear when you see the bit). Make sure that the
bearing isn't riding on the edge of the plywood with the dado for the T
moulding.  Use the file to clean up edges and corners.  You may want to slightly
chamfer the bottom edges of the cabinets with the file to assure they don't hang
when installing/removing.

Good Luck.
