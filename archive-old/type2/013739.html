At 11:37 PM 7/14/01 -0400, you wrote:
>Anybody out there painted their Westy top with a Gloss White product and 
>proud of it?  What did you use?

Painting the top is a PITA to get it glossy.  I've done one, on a '76
that was pretty crazed and degelled.  Cleaned it, and again, and
pressure washed, and TSP'd, and pressure washed.  Sprayed it
all with a sprayable polyester filler to restore the gel-coat.  Topcoated
with white acryllic enamel.  Looked real good till I sold it and I've seen
it around and it still does.  Royal PITA, $200 in paint easy, many
days work, had to wax it from then out.

A friend bought a gallon of white reflective RV roof paint ($20).  Cleaned
pretty well (the TSP and scrub brush then the pressure wash) and
rolled it on.  Looked every bit nearly as nice as mine and it muffled
heavy rain sound a bit.  Might have reflected heat better I don't know.
I think he got the stuff from (aghast) JC Whitless, but most RV places
sell the stuff.  It wasn't "glossy" per say but it was, and stayed
real darn white as the stuff has some sort of antifungal antimildew
crap in it.  If I were to do it again and I might on the '82 it is the
route I will take.
