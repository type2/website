I've had really good experience with ceramic coating. But.. it really has to be done right, and it really depends on who you have do it for you. I had one friend ceramic coat his exhaust manifolds and Y-pipe in a Pontiac Fiero. Within a week's time, his engine bay was covered in what looked like shiny metal flake (it was EVERYWHERE!!!). 

But in my own experiences, I have a ceramic header in my Pontiac Solstice.... it was done VERY well... it grealy reduced the underhood temperatures. I beat that car silly every day to work.. and the ceramic coating has held up. 

I also have ceramic coated inside and out ported exhuast manifolds and Y-pipe on my 87 Fiero 5-Speed, and they've held up for about 4 years now (with LOTS of abuse).

I think it really has to do with preperation and the proper formula. There is a guy in Fort Lauderdale that only charges like $180 bucks for a set of V8 manifolds... and the quality is SUPERIOR to anything I've seen.
