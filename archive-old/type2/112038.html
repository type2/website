On Fri, 24 Oct 2003, Mike Benthin wrote:

> Watching my temp gauge, it seems it runs coolest at the lowest speed you
> can do in a given gear- ie, lowest rpms and lowest throttle.  In 4th on
> our 78 high milage engine, that's about 50-55 on the flats! I've gotten
> it to a scary 270 on long hill pulls even with shifting down, because of
> the tendency for one to use the top of the rpm range in the lower gear-
> best to settle in at the lowest rpms you can use, which is hardly moving
> the throttle very far.

For those with fully instrumented engines, I wonder if there are different
peaks for oil and cylinder head temperatures?  It's the cylinder head temp
that causes mechanical problems for the engine, such as broken and/or
pulled studs.  The oil temp is just a symptom and may not follow along
precisely with the cylinder head temp.
