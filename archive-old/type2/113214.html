Jason,

This symptom sounds very much like a little bit of "fun" I had Labor Day 
Weekend.  I pulled the negative lead off the battery and it looked 
clean, but I cleaned it anyway.  Tried to start it again and it cranked 
for a second and then everything went dark again.  Pulled the positive 
side and it had a lot of corrosion on the inside, outside looked clean.  
Cleaned up that lead and the van started up and ran just fine... until 
the next stop.  I came out from the store and the same thing happened.  
I tweaked on both connections again and hooked them up. 

This time as I was connecting up the negative lead I got the usual 
little tiny spark, but not at the terminal.  It was about three inches 
down in the woven cable.  Aha! Bad cable! 

That's when I discovered the kludge job of a cable installation that a 
evil previous owner did. He had run a webbed cable from the negative 
terminal on the battery down to a bolt on the frame rail, then back up 
to the ground point on the bulkhead 6 inches from the negative terminal 
on the battery.

Anyway, I replaced the negative cable and all is well.  In fact, the 
starter seems to run faster (to my ears).

Anyway, there's another vote towards checking those battery cables.
