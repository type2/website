> How can I chase this down (I promised her I would consult with the experts

They're blowing smoke.  "grinding knuckles" has no meaning on a VW.
"Knuckle" is a vague term relating to old-fashioned universal joints.
> 
> I'll probably go ahead and replace the cv joints (about due according to
> maintenance schedule).  Does the price quoted for CV Joints usually include
> the joint next to the tire and the one at the transaxle per side?  Or am I
> looking at buying 4 joints?

If the joints aren't bad, then don't replace them.  I have one joint on my
bus that has over 200K miles on it and still works well.  There's no way
that you can predict the life of a CV
 
Bus CV joints are priced per joint, with four joints installed.  All four
are identical.  You don't get a new shaft with the joints.

> While I have the CV Joints apart, would this be an opportune time to go
> ahead and replace the wheel bearings?

It's a good time to spin the wheel and feel for rough bearings.  If they
are rough, replace them.  If not, add some grease (via a needle) and leave
them alone.
