    Well this is the solution I'm going for and because some of you are 
in the same boat I thought I'd share my results.

    I went to the Evco, known as the "house of hose" basically a store 
that has nothing but hose, lots of air compresssor stuff too.  I showed 
them my broken hose and told them it was an air hose and I needed 
something of the same length and the same interior diameter.  They 
showed me something close and for only $2.50 it was worth a shot.  This 
is what I got  http://users.sisna_dot_com/baca1/new_hose.jpg

    Despite all my effort I could not make it fit and it was close... 
very close.  So then I go thinking... 
http://users.sisna_dot_com/baca1/dremel_tool.jpg  that's right.  I used the 
cylindrical sanding bit on the dremel tool to make the interior diameter 
wider.  There's basically three levels of the hose, the inside rubber, a 
nylon web, and that blue stuff you can see.  To make it fit I only 
needed to sand away a little of the inside rubber, not even touching the 
nylon.  Of course the dremel tool isn't neccasary, a drill would suffice 
or even a round file.

    You can see that the hose even has a natural curve to it which made 
it a nice fit.  Check out the result 
http://users.sisna_dot_com/baca1/hose_in%20_engine.jpg

    The obvious disadvantages are that it isn't stock and doesn't look 
anything close but if your next step is to consider duct tape it's 
probably a step up.  Also despite modifyiing the diameter of the ends 
its still a fight to take them on and off.  I liked how my old hose 
would cooperate after  I loosened the clamps.  I'll probably be using 
this until I find a like-new replacement.
