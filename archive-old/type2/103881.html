At 01:07 PM 3/2/2003, David Kelly wrote:
>I found that the bus had 2 condensers!! Is
>this normal? One was mounted under the front cab and
>the other was mounted under the middle.

It's not uncommon.  Many times people add a second condenser when they convert to R134a.

Here is what I was thinking. I wanted to up grade the
>existing condensers with one big condenser from a
>Tahoe. (36" by around 24"). Can a usually vertically
>mounted condenser be mounted horizontally?

Yes.  But you will need to protect it somehow, and of course ensure that there is adequate airflow through it.

> I was
>planning on using 2 high out put condenser fans on
>this condenser. From what I have read, this should
>make the evaporator cooler right?

You need adequate cooling through the condenser in order for the system to work efficiently.  Too much will not help, but it shouldn't hurt anything either.  It probably won't be too much though, especially if you are using R134a.

>Is it big enough to have
>another evaporator mounted in the back?

Difficult to predict.  If you installed it with a single evaporator first, and got about four digital thermometers (inlet and outlet side temps for both condenser and evaporator), you could probably tell whether or not you could support a second evaporator.
