The FI pancake engines don't have a "pre-heater" set-up of any sort.
There's essentially 3 "cold start/cold run" components on these
things:

1) Cold-start/thermo-time switch.  This supplies extra gas while
cranking the engine when the engine's cold.  It helps on start-up but
has no function once the engine's running.

2) Temp sensor II in the left head.  This sends a signal to the ECU
telling the ECU's "the engine's cold" which results in the ECU
increasing the pulse width of the firing of the injectors.

3) Auxiliary Air Valve.  When the engine's cold this valve is open,
sending extra (metered) air to the engine over and above what's
supplied by the idle air circuit.  Kind of like keeping your foot on
the gas peddle a little bit.  As the engine warms the Aux Air Valve
closes.

Sounds like the problem could be in the Aux Air Valve.
