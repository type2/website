What I do is tighten the shoes as far as I can (same
number of clicks on each starwheel - they turn
opposite directions, remember), get in the bus and
exert as much pressure as I can on the brake pedal,
then go back out and adjust as tight as it will go -
repeat - then before you start backing off, hit the
pedal one more time as hard as you can. What this does
is center the shoe to the drum. Now start backing off
like 3 clicks back, 3 clicks front and so on until the
wheel will turn with some resistance. Now get back in
and do the pedal again before checking for "no drag."

My bus has always had some little bit of drag, so to
be sure, especially if I am driving a long distance
shortly after an adjustment, I stop after around 50
miles and feel the wheels near center to see if
they're  warm - they shouldn't be.

The method of stomping on the pedal to center the
shoes has worked well for me for many years. You can
start the engine and use the vacuum booster if you
want, but just remember the longer you run that
engine, the hotter the exhaust pipes are going to be
when you crawl back under there.

While you're down there, check all your CV joint
bolts! -BaH
