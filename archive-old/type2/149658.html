Hi Todd,

I live in The Netherlands myself and must say that the splitties over here
are a minority. And the ones that are admitted to the roads mostly come out
for meetings or fundriving. In my neighbourhood is however a daily driver,
but it is indeed a seldom sight.

For the baywindow buses I can say that more and more they are used in a
similar way as i described for the splitties above. I see them drive along
although. Normally about once a week I see a bay driving or parked on the
roadside.

The road tax in Holland does not apply for vehicles over the age of 25
years. So that is not the reason. Most people have a modern daily driver and
just take the bus out on sunny days and for family outings.

The eurovans are very popular as deliveryvans over here. It is one of the
best selling vans on the market. The campers you have seen are most probably
Westfalia Campers. It is just not written in big lettering as they used to
do.
