Hello Brian,

Just happen to have a (slightly battered) thermostat to flaps rod for a
1600cc engine in the cupboard...

If you take the rod and look straight on at the top end then you will see
something like this (if you are not viewing this mail in 'plain text mode'
then copy it into your word processor and select a non-proportional type eg
Courier New in Windows)

	o
	 \
	  \
	   |
	   |
	   |
	   |
	   |
	   |
	   |
	   |
	   |
	   |
	    \
	     |
	     |

The "o" in the picture is the end of the rod

The length of the section coming straight is 22mm
The upper cranked section is 20mm long and is at an angle of about 45
degrees to the long section and at right angles to the part of the rod
pointing towards you.
The long straight section is 160mm long.
The lower cranked section is at an angle of about 30 degrees and is 25mm
long.
the lower section including the treaded section is 15 mm.
In the view show above it is 215mm from top to bottom.

Now looking straight on at the lower end of the rod it looks like this
	  _____
	 /     |
	o      |
	       |

This shows that the lower crank is angled at about 45 degrees to the section
which fits into the flaps the other end.

All of this suggests that there will be a fair amount of trial and error in
achieving good fit and operation.

Good luck
