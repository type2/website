> Ok, ok -- what's all this math that you guys are doing? I understand
> that in 185R14, the R stands for the width of the tire and the 14 is
> the
> size of the rim so the 185 is the diameter of the tire, I presume,
> but
> where are you getting these decimal numbers and what does it all
> mean???!!!

14 is the size of the rim in inches.
185 is the width of the tread in millimeters.

The decimal number (.82, .75, .70, etc.) is the "aspect ratio" of the
tire.  This is the ratio of the tread width to the section height
(distance from rim to tread) of the tire.  The smaller the aspect
ratio, the "squatter" the tire.  If no aspect ratio is used, as in
"185R14", then an aspect ratio of .82 is assumed.  If it's different,
then it's included in the tire size: "195/75R14"

Now, calculating the numbers to a gnat's eyelash is an interesting
intellectual exercise, but in reality tires need only fall into a range
of dimensions to get a particular "nominal" size.
