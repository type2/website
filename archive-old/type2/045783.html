Burning it in effigy can be done in several ways.

Drive it carefully up to a big tree and rest the front bumper against it.
Rev the motor and dump the clutch in first. See that big cloud of smoke???
That's the oil burning out of the friction fabric from the intense heat. It
will seem to work better for a few days or even weeks, but you also burn up
the adhesive matrix and fresh oil comes around too soon. Replace the seal,
have the flywheel ground, put in a new disk, clutch, t.o. and pilot
bearing. And don't forget to check for wear of the cross shaft fingers and
bearings. If anyone heated the clutch up enough to heat stress the surface,
those springs all got very hot and some of them will be weak. Makes for
very poor, short life function.
