Good idea!  I have the sockets and not a torch, so I go for the hub nut

> I did the front next and that was an amazingly easy

I know!  Ain't it tho!  99% of the time, I can do new pads on good
calipers in <20 minutes!

> After getting the old pins out, I had to push the
> pistons in, alot.  I must have used all 200 lbs of me
> behind a 12" bar, for 5 minutes each piston to get
> them in enough to have space for the new pads.

Mark, this is a sure sign that the flex lines in the front are swelled
shut on the inside.  the pistons should go back in with only moderate
pressure.  If they take excess pressure, it is because there is a blockage
in the line.  The MC will apply tons of pressure and will blow fluid past
them to appply the brakes, but the return is slower and those blocked
lines will eat your new pads!

BTW, a Vice-Grip 12LC pliers will make pushing the piston back in an
easier task.  Forgot to mention that in the original post.

> Got it all together and it brakes very well now.  One
> thing I did find out, this morning, is that the
> reservoir likes to be FULL.  Otherwise, it acts like a
> bad master cylinder.  Push the brake, stops, a sec
> later goes down a little more.  After filling the
> reservoir FULL, no more of this.

Mark, again I believe this is a symptom of blocked flex lines, tho your
attribution to a topped-up resevoir is interesting.  That should not make
a difference, but if it did, it did.  I have had this symptom and
replacing flex lines cured it.  Next time I experience it, I will check
the resevoir as well
