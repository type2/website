.....................Hello Joe...If you can still put a "hold" on that 
GALLON of Sherwin Williams special order paint, maybe you should do it and 
run some small-area tests first, to be sure it is compatible with the 
Rustoleum.


..................I have tried using Rustoleum brown rust-inhibitor primer 
on my bus, and when I went to paint over it with automotive primer, I got 
serious crinkling/incompatibility that had to be allowed to dry and then 
had to be sanded off. The fish oil in the Rustoleum didn't work well with 
the automotive primer solvents, I guess.  So I say, run some preliminary 
tests in places that aren't critical. Be sure to let the Rustoleum cure for 
a few days, before trying your Sherwin Williams color coat product. Try a 
few ounces before you buy a gallon, and do some  trials.

.....................If I were doing it, I'd try not to mix 
systems.......I'd stay with the Rustoleum system, and use a color coat from 
the stock Rustoleum collection. Or, use a Sherwin Williams metal primer and 
the Sherwin Williams color coat. I'd probably pick battleship grey.  Saw a 
bus done in that once and liked it.


..................In the Rustoleum system, I think you could color coat a 
bus with about a dozen spray cans of Rustoleum, maybe 50 bucks worth, if 
you bought the commercial size cans rather than those little  ones that 
most hardware stores carry. The commercial units look about twice as big. 
The Por-15 has been good for me, over bare metal and even on bare metal 
with small rust pits. But whatever you do, run small trials first.
