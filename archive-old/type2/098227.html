If you look at the second number, it is the same as what you see on your
distributor except for one number, and given the age of the dist, I call
that close enough :-)  If you remove the distributor and clean it with
steel wool, you will almost certainly get to that number and will find the
113-905-205AJ or 043-905-205C number imprinted below it covered in 30
years of road mung.

This is not the distributor that came in your bus when it was new, but it
is a perfectly valid distributor to use in your bus, so let's ride with
it.  This distributor has three different things happening with regard to
the spark advance, as you can see in the last entry (Advance/Retard Range)
First it has a mechanical advance, second it has a vacuum advance, and
third it has a vacuum retard.  In order to have decent performance two of
the three functions have to be working correctly (you can get around a
dead vacuum retard)

> My comment on vaccum was related to the hose condition and sucking on
> the advance. But no I have not used a guage.

Paul, I am sorry to say you *have* to go out and buy a gauge.  I am
incredibly solid on this, especially if you have a dual port engine.  The
boots between the center section and the ends are famous for springing
leaks and that loss of vacuum can cause exactly the symptoms you are
experiencing.  An Actron vacuum gauge at any FLAPS (friendly local auto
parts store) or a Craftsman gauge at Sears will cost you ~20 and is worth
every penny!  You are very lucky if you actually still have the original
intake manifold, BTW.  There is a manifold vacuum port in the left end
casting that is very useful for checking vacuum (and shuld be where your
vacuum retard line is plugged in, BTW)

I am 90% certain after this that your problem is going to be vacuum
related, tho it could turn out to be a wonky distributor.

> Timing is set to the notch in the pulley, with the vaccum hooked up.

OK. from Jim Thompson's page, with the 205AJ dist, timing should be set at
5deg ATDC at 800-950 RPM.  *IF* you have the original pulley (not certain,
but a pretty safe bet) then you should have two notches.  the one on the
left is for TDC (and setting valves); the timing to the notch on the right
is at 5 BTDC and will be fine for timing to.  The problem is that VW made
half a dozen different pulleys wiht half a dozen different notches.  If
you pulley was replaced at any point, then the notches mean nothing and
you are up a creek.  I like degree wheel pullies on T1 engines, even tho
the quality of them is truly horrendous :-(  I usually have to buy one,
install it, discover it is flawed (either rubs on the tin or has bad
run-out (wobble)), pull it, exchange it, install second, third, etc. until
i get a good one.  I could save time by buying Berg, but I am cheap and
Berg is not.
