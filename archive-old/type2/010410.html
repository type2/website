Yep, this is true. HOWEVER....I bought/installed a
Pertronix unit on my '69 Westy and the first unit
failed out of the box. I returned it for another and
it worked, but the timing was no longer correct. I had
to set the timing *way* advanced for it to idle/run
properly.

I ran it for about 2 months and finally pulled it back
out and switched back to points....I really didn't
like running it that far advanced.

I'll stick with what the factory recommends from now
on.


Also, I've never had to "change my points on the side
of the road on a dark, rainy night", either. In fact,
I've never had them go out at all. Maybe it's because
I replace them before they get to that point.
