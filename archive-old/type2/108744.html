Well this is just a list of some things to check but:

Is the gas old? Or is this happening on one tank of gas? Maybe you filled
her up with some crappy gas.

Also, is she idling rough in your driveway? You can just easily hook up
another gas tank with a fuel line to the fuel pump and see if that fixes the
problem. That would allow you to rule out the gas tank without having to
pull it out. If you find that new high octane fuel from a gas can doesn't
solve the problem, then the problem isn't in your tank... it's somewhere
else in the engine.
