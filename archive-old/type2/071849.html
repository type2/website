  Thank you all for your terrific advice and support recently, as I tried to
get Ramona to ignore the light and return to the land of the living.
  After replacing many things and testing almost everything, we not only found
the problem, but also learned *a lot* about 'Mona's guts and their workings.
  You may remember my "Crank, Spit, Nada" post. After all the tests and
replacements, we finally deduced there was simply no spark. We'd looked at the
points and sanded them and cleaned them all up and they looked good from what
you can see in that position, and after cleaning them we tested them w/ the
timing light and got the light to go off once (!), but then she stayed on
again.
  So we just yanked the points anyway and there was the rub! Pocked little
pits on either side of the moveable point!! So the points were staying closed
all that time!
  Yay!! Replaced the points and ole Ramona fired up with a happy vengeance!!!!
  Only problem was we did not replace the condenser, but only because we
didn't have a small enough stubby to get behind the dizzy at the screw. I
know, I know, bread and butter, but that I'll try on Monday. I'm just so
thrilled she fired!
