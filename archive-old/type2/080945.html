I've had it happen on a 4wheel disc brake GM following the 'correct'
procedure. Went out and bought a big ass clamp instead. But, unless I'm in a
hurry, or its a car I've worked on before, I pull the piston and clean and

little crud can gum things up.

> Both Chuck and vwbus make excellent points I had not thought of.
>
> You may have the piston canted in the bore.  If you do, and the caliper is
> off, just drop the caliper and old pads back into position on the wheel
> and stomp the brakes once or twice.  That will straighten it back out!
>
> If this is true, tho, I would worry about the surface of the bore being
> marred by the piston edge digging into it.  This has never  happened to
> me, which is why I didn't think about it, but if it did, I would seriously
> consider replacing the calipers, as that one is probably going to leak
> pretty bad around the piston/bore.
>
> Chuck, has this happened to you?  Was it OK after you straightened the
> piston out?
>
> What vwbus says is an excellent check for swelled brake lines.  If you
> have real problems getting the piston back in with the bleeder closed, but
> it goes in easily with the bleeder open, then the flex line is
> clogged/dead.
