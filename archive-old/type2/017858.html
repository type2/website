After 3 and 1/2 years of waiting, my 78 Westy (elmer fudd) is alive and
running well.
This is the bus that was blowing oil out of the dipstick hole.  Turns
out that the previous rebuild went awry and that the cylinder walls were
badly scored, allowing the blow by to push the oil out the dipstick
hole.  After another rebuild, this one by the great folks at Johnson's
Enterprises,  Statesville, N.C. (who are GREAT with type IV engines) it
is running again.
