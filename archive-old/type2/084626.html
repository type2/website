Please, don't ask any mechanic to look at your car until you find 
references for his character. If he's a thief, it won't be that hard to 
find out. Likewise, if he is known to be honest in his dealings, that isn't 
hard to find out either. Ask at the BBB, or town hall or the court house.

Proper procedure from Bentley by memory. Rule out all possible vacuum leaks 
before doing anything about the shafts. Now you can do a very quick leak 
test for the shafts. Get it idling. Reach in and wiggle the throttle shaft. 
If idle changes, you need bushings or new carbs or unworn used carbs (fat 
chance).

If the mechanic doesn't conduct diagnosis in the proper order by procedure, 
the door to a waste of time/money gets kicked open. If you have vacuum 
leaks, you can fiddle with the carbs until your a fossil and get no 
results. So you rule that out first, by procedure. It's in the opening 
paragraphs of the fuel system section. Skip a step, waste the weekend 
fiddling.
