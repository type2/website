OUTGOING INFO:

SAGGY BUTT seems to be a common malady for busses. Over the years there have
been occasional discussions on the list about saggy butt solutions, and in
the archives a number of old listers suggested coil-over shocks, air shocks,
or torsion bar adjustment (the last being a time consuming and potentially
dangerous task). The problem was that no one had much information about
where to get coil-over or air shocks.

Anyway, about three months ago I discovered that there ARE AIR SHOCKS that
will fit VW vans. After much searching, frustration, and a little creative
research, I found out that air shocks for '85+ Chevy Astro Vans will fit the
VW van (cost: about $80 beans). This is a good quick cure for the saggy-butt
problem, and I have had good results in my bus so far. But a warning is in
order: The shock towers on the VW are not designed as heavy load-carrying
devices. If you have moderate sagging problems, air shocks can help; or if
you are carrying heavy loads for short distances, they can help prevent
excessive bottoming-out. But if you have serious sag, a torsion bar
adjustment is your best bet. I don't know this for sure, but I will also bet
that if someone makes coil-overs for the Chevy Astro, they'd fit the VW too.
