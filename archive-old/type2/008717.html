Kevin-All,

I use a Craftsman (Sears) torque wrench that measure in
both foot-pounds and Newton-meters. It is a 1/2" drive
and measures from 10-79.5 foot pounds (or 16.9-111.2
Newton meters). It also has a little dial on the handle
for setting the desired torque that allows you to
dial-in *half* pounds of torque. Very nice and not very
expensive. Also rugged as hell and guaranteed for life
to boot!

This is the kind of wrench that "clicks" when the
desired torque range is reached; not the kind with the
little pointer. This style CANNOT over/under-torque
like the pointer style (I said style, not sisters;-)
can and often do.


I believe it was in the $50.00 range, but don't quote
me. If you think that's too expensive, check the price
of damage done from over/under torque-ing your heads,
etc. 


Jaime
