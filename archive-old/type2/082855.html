The first signs I had that the seat was dropping
was puffs of smoke on start up. As it got worse
it smoked all the time and tailpipe was black with
oil. Then it got real bad! Killing all of the flying
insects in the neighborhood.
   Soooo valve job time. If you have a carbed
fuel system you can keep the old brass alloy seats.
Bore them out to fit the diameter of the throttle
shaft. shave the OD of the seat down untill you have
a thin walled bushing. Drill out the worn hole through
which the throttle shaft fits to the OD of your new
bushing and press fit it in. No more slop in the
throttle shaft which makes it easier to sync those
original carbs. I think the seat material will last
longer then the carb material does.  

  Black tape over the VW emblem at the VoA show is

you have the side tent with a covered access to it.
Need to go for supplies? Roll in the awning an drive
away.
