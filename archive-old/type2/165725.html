Are these AVP heads you recently had rebuilt or AVP rebuilt heads  
from 3K miles ago?

The budget rebuilders aren't in the habit of replacing the seats:  
they just replace the exhaust valves, regrind the seats with stones  
and ship them out.

> There could have been issues before I got it, the seats may have  
> been pressed with inadequate interference fit.

The factory interference fit was probably a compromise between fit  
and mass production. You can get a better fit these days if the  
rebuilder takes the time and charges for it.

With regard to peening: you can't know if this process has induced  
cracks or if the stress from the compacted metal will create one later.
