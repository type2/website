Sounds like you need to start with a visual inspection of all the rest of
your intake and spark devices. Any component with a small defect can have
some effects on the engine performance,, and a few items can pile up to
make it hard to find one solution....

After that,, inspect the valves clearances... 6 thou for solid lifters,,
turn the hyd lifters adjusters in till they touch, then 1 turn more to
start with.

install your compression gauge,, should be in the 130+ psi area for a new
engine, if the rings have seated

install a leak down tester if you can ,, especially if the compression is low

install your vac gauge,  it should have a steady needle, and should be
about 15hg at idle

install your dwell meter, ensure the point dwell is about 50 and steady as
you rev it

double check your timing with a timing light, and make sure it is steady

As you have a bit of a Hungarian gooolosh engine, you should report back
with more information..

dist # ?
cylinder head temps
fuel pressure, should be about 5psi  if it is the same as the stock solex
fuel volume delivery , should be about 400ml in 30 seconds if it is the
same as the stock solex
what type of air cleaner is on the carb... if it has a flat top it may be
causing fuel vapour condensation to drip into the venturi at idle

can't think of anything else ,,
