Mark,that sounds like a winner....  But since I don't have a tap, I would 
drill the holes right through the metal and slip two long, skinny bolts 
through the two holes from the bottom side of the steering wheel, with nuts

save any decent pre-75 steering wheels I found.  At $10 a pop, that is a

successful.  Since I didn't have to worry about buggering up the threads on 
the steering shaft with junk yard buses, I could have a passerby hammer 
away.  And yes, sometimes they do come off quite easily, but usually by 
someone who is stronger and more determined than I am...
