Hi Aaron,
There have been a couple of answers to your post that I have seen, but 
maybe this will be a bit more accurate of an answer to the query.

The item is a tuneable L-C filter assembly consisting of an inductor (L, 
coil) and a Capacitor (C, Condenser).

The filter is then tuned to deaden the noise (a whining sound) in the form 
of RF energy that is created by the generator brush contacts against the 
generator rotor.

Essentially, the unit does little for the ordinary guy, except to possibly 
filter out RF hash that might be created by the brushes on the generator, 
and keep the "hash" from being heard on a standard AM radio. With FM, 
because of frequency and reception processes, the benefits of installing 
this filter are almost nil.

The unit is tuned, by varying the electrical values of the components, so 
as to resonate the circuit to the frequency (or frequency multiple thereof 
- "harmonic") of the generator hash, and once properly tuned, the generator 
created radio frequency (RF) noise is then shunted to ground. The tuning of 
the circuit is optimized by listening on the AM radio, and as the filter is 
resonated, the AM radio whine will slowly drop in value.

I would like to get my hands on one of yours, if this might be possible. It 
does offer some advantage to a guy like me that operates ham radio from 
their bus. The same advantages might be obtained for CB radio installations 
in busses.

I still had one of the circuits stashed back in my junk box, as well, and 
it is currently mounted on Casper.

Ignition noise.........
That is another matter, and this tuneable circuit does not aid in dampening 
the noise created by the ignition firing. That requires other attacks.

The filter will not aid in the dampening of ignition "popping". That 
requires shielding of the spark plug wires, distributor shielding, high 
resistance spark plug wires, etc., etc.
