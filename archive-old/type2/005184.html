I sent this the other day to hopefully answer a dist. question.
On this subject, you can never really believe the number on the side of a
distributor unless you know for sure that it was the original one that was
installed at the factory. If you go buy a cheapo rebuilt, the number on the
housing may or may not mean anything! The only number that may mean anything
at all is the rebuilders part # on the box, but that went by-by when you
turned in the core in that box.
The reason I mention this: I was at one of the local self serve yards today
and they were just putting in a fairly complete 71 bug. It had a 'brand new'
rebuilt distributor in it. Single vacuum advance, full centrifigul... not
what a 71 bug should have. Should have been a dual vacuum unit. So I grabbed
it thinking this will work in my 66 bus.  I am not sure what the vacuum
advance values are on this thing! I really am not sure what the centrifigul
values are either. The number on the housing is 021 905 205E which means it
originally came off a 72 1700 bus with a dual vacuum unit. Without an old
Sun distributor machine who knows what this thing is set up for..... [I wish
I still had one, sold it]. Oh well, nothing like the 'seat of the pants'
test.
Bottom line: beware of the crap out there.
