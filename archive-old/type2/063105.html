However, my wife and I were driving thru Arkansas a few years ago to see her relatives in Centerville/Dardanelle/Russellville area, and we waved.  And waved.  And waved some more.  We got few if any returns.  I was not a happy camper, as I was used to the incessant waving encountered near my grandparent's farm in West TN.  I had heard central AR was a friendly place.  Maybe it was me.  Maybe it was our car (her parents' new Grand Am (a.k.a. Grand Mistake). I don't know. 

 Anywho, I enjoy the simple art of waving, but I'd rather wave at farmers and hillbillies than at urbanites.  I guess I'm just a country boy at heart.  I do no-tice that the younger generation of country folks are less willing to acknowledge my presence, except for the occasional glare.....("It's one of them "fern" cars, Lester! Must be one of them hippie-types!")

My grandparents will never understand my passion for german engineering.
