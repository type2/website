Yep. That's why many of the early 12V cars ran a resistor inline w/ the 
coil. In normal operation, the coil would work off 6 to 9 volts... but 
during cranking, it got the full 12V. Chryslers started right away, or 
not at all. They used a variable resistor. 12V when it started cranking, 
but gradually increasing resistance. (If it didn't start in 2 or 3 
cranks, shut of the ignition, wait 15 seconds, try again.)

I'm glad to see someone else has had the same problem.
