Ken listed ten good ones, but there is an eleventh:

(11) Wire. I carry a roll of stainless wire, about .032", if
I recall correctly. You can use it to (a) make a hose clamp,
(b) keep that errant part from falling off the vehicle,
(c) serve as a locknut, (d) secure the door with the broken
latch, etc. and (e) serve as a second set of hands to hold things in
place while you assemble them properly (with duct tape, no doubt).

Bailing wire rusts, and so if done in stainless, your "temporary"
repair will last much longer!


(12) Now that I think about it, there is also a neat tool used with this
stuff that takes both ends of a strand and twists them together
perfectly, tightening as it goes. Perfect for clamps. $20.00 from Harbor
Freight.
