Sounds to me like one of a couple of easy things to check:

1) Check that the vacuum can on the distributor is OK and not leaking.
(What, no vacuum can?!? Get the original distributor on there!)
2) Check that the two vacuum lines are hooked to the right places.
The one on the dist. side of the can should be connected below the butterfly
and on the away side of the can should connect above the butterfly at the bast
of the carb.
3) Mabye it is too rich. Check that the main jet in the base of the fuel bowl
is still screwed in tight and that the idle volume and mixture screws (big and
small on left side of carb) are tuned according to spec.
4) Check advance timing with a timing light to make sure that it moves
about 30 degrees advanced, or notch about 2.5 inches to the left at 3000 RPM.
5) Check the timing on all four wires. 1 and 3 fire near notch, 2 and 4 fire
opposite.

Overall, it sounds like it is not happy with the advance curve. Like when I
had
the dreaded 009 in mine. Idled OK, but you had to 'pump' the accel to get it
to advance. Check the vacuum lines to the advance vacuum can and that the
weights inside are free to move. A timing light is the best way to check 
the advance.

Good luck!
