No, endplay is checked externally.  Just attach a dial indicator to the
case with the stem touching the flywheel and pointing parallel to the
crankshaft.  Push the crank into the engine ,, note the indicator reading,
pull the crank out of the engine, note the reading again, tne subtract to
get the end play.  A properly rebuilt engine should have an endplay in the
"new" range (.003-.005 inch)  The wear limit is .006 inch.  Do not accept
an engine with an end play greater than .005.  Even if you have to walk
for a few days, it isn't worth it to install a junk engine.
