The last engine we put into our old '68 Campmobile was set up with an
"external" oil cooler mounted over the fan housing air intake, and an oil
filter in the circuit.  The oil filter used a "Ford" cartridge and was
mounted to the engine compartment overhead just inside the upper left corner
of the engine lid opening.  The cooler/filter circuit tapped off the usual
aftermarket adapter that replaces the stock cooler.

This engine was a dead stock 1600 SP, very carefully balanced and
blueprinted.  When we sold the bus the engine had 143,000 miles on it, ran
perfectly, and had ~110 psi compression on all 4 holes.  The engine was out
of the bus once during that period, to deal with a defective #3 exhaust
valve seat.

The cooler, hoses, and filter added a quart of oil capacity to the engine.
We changed the oil [Castrol 30W] and filter every 3,000 miles, and 95% of
the miles were spent cruising I-5 at 60-65 MPH.

I don't know if the engine's good health and longevity were because of the
cooler, the filter, the combined effects of both, or just dumb luck, but I
still wish I'd kept that engine.
