DOT 3 and 4 are compatible.  The only advantage, that I know of, in using
the DOT 4 is a higher boiling point.  This might make a difference if you
drive really hard in the mountains and get your brakes really hot.  I drove
my '71 to the top of Pike's Peak once and they had signs everywhere saying
"HOT BRAKES FAIL".  Does anyone know of any other differences in the two
fluids?  I'm not talking about DOT 5.  It's evidently lots different but I'm
not sure it's worth the hassle.
