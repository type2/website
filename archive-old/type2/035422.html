I did a quick calculation to see what electrical power would be required to
replace the engine driven fan.

Assuming for a moment that a 3 HP electric motor per side would provide the
required cooling.  Converting mechanical HP to electrical watts:  

Power = 3HP(746 watts/1HP) = 2238 watts

 and

Power = (Voltage)(Current)

 so

Current = (2238 watts)/12 V = 186.5 amps

The actual current draw for a 3 hp motor would be somewhat larger to account
for friction and copper losses.  To make it work you would need to be able
to obtain sufficient cooling with a fairly small motor(s), like 1/4HP total
(about 20 amps). Good Luck.
