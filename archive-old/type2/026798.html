Well I finally got up the courage to do the equivalent of tearing off the 
"DO NOT REMOVE UNDER PENALTY OF LAW" mattress tag from my '75 van. I 
disabled the idiotic egr warning lamp actuator positioned on the speedometer 
cable. This gear driven contraption turns on the EGR warning light every 
15,000 miles to "remind" you to go to the dealership to have the EGR valve 
checked. Because the EGR is still being checked every two years at the state 
smog test, I don't think my action will result in any unnecessary pollution 
of the Golden State.
Rather than just pull the electric plug or the whole box, however, I pulled 
two gears out of the inside mechanism. The box is still needed because it is 
an integral part of the speedometer cable; you can't remove the whole box 
unless you want to put in a new cable. The reason for pulling out the gears 
was that by so-doing I got rid of an annoying source of "subliminable" (with 
acknowledgements to "Shrub" Bush) noise.
Like the rest of us, owning a Van is a very aural experience, and the 
grinding of those gears was a real distraction when I am trying to enjoy the 
tappet rhythms wafting satisfyingly from the engine compartment. So if you 
want to greatly improve the 'fidelity' of the sound in your mid-70's type 2, 
and remove a disturbing grinding sound that sub-consciously causes anxiety, 
just get in there and pull the gears from that idiot box.
