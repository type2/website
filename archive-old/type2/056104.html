Hello Friday people,

I realized my dreams had come true a few weeks ago when I found myself
greasing the CV joints with my wife lying by my side.  She's a keeper.  Had
grease up to her elbows, in her hair, and most other places.

Good woman has come to develop kind regards for utility of VW Westy.  She has
a little trouble understanding why there are so many things that can go wrong,
but realizes there is a finite number of mechancial, electrical devices
stacked on top of four wheels.  So why do I have to screw around with it all
the time?

Junkyards:  I've owned the same VW bus for the last ten years (got it from my
mother who bought it new).  I don't know of a single VW specific junkyard in
the state of Utah.  If there are any, I would like to know.

Of course, there is Tom Tom's VW "Museum" in Moab.  It changed from being a
junkyard to a museum when the city fathers outlawed junkyards in the city
limits.  He's got some spits, a single cab, some loaves, bugs,and a whole
bunch of  other "museum" pieces.  Trouble is, he won't sell parts.  A lot of
buses pass through his lot, so he must sell them complete to other "curators".

For those who ride mountain bikes, Moab is the place to be.  If you do go
there, by all means visit Tom Tom.

Don't work in the summer, but I did drive my VW to play today.

Fred Brittain
