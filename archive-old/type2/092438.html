Yes, that is "sort of" right, but the pump STILL does not suck. 
The pump uses 14.7 psi atmospheric pressure " 1 BAR" as an initial inlet
pressure number 
(minus)
altitude correction
(minus)
pumpage vapor pressure
(minus)
elevation lift
(minus)
inlet side fuel line friction losses
(minus) 
inlet side fuel filter losses .................. so as to yield a number
equated in linear dimension value or psi equivalent that is calculated
and called NPSH. 
This NPSH Net Positive Suction Head is a positive number. If there is
not sufficient NPSH as a function of the pump or pump impeller design,
there will be no pumping. 
In many cases, the pump will prime and it will still pump with inlet
pressure less than 1 BAR, 
but this may not always be presumed.
