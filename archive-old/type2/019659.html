Yep, that was me. I used the POR-15 Marine Clean degreaser on my engine
tin before I painted it. The crud just hosed off with the garden
sprayer. It was pretty impressive. I understand that cleaner is
water-based. (So's Simple Green, but it's stinky.) That's what I'd do if
I were in your position. It's just my idea -- someone else may have
other ideas for safely and effectively cleaning out your heat
exchangers. You could also just let 'em sit in the sun for a few days to
neutralize the gunk by baking and airing out, if you're not in a hurry.
