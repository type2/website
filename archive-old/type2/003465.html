> - How can you tell if a transaxle CV joint boot (not sure if this
> is VW lingo ... my father said this is what they were called when he
> worked on them) is bad and/or needs to be replaced?  The boot on the right
> side of the bus was cracked and we fear that it will lose grease soon.  Is
> it a major problem to replace these?  If labor is the main thing then I'm
> not incredibly worried ... money is the main issue.


If it's cracked fix it. It is much cheaper to get a new boot now (about $25
for all 4 with clamps) than a new CV later (about $175 (or more) for all 4).
To test the other ones you can squeeze them and look for cracks.... It
wouldn't hurt to replace all 4 boots as well as check your CV's (especially
the balls and cage) for pitting and re-grease them.

> - We found that the cooling system had been removed from the
> engine - there aren't any flaps, linkages, cables or a thermostat.  How
> much difficulty is it to replace all of this, and are the parts available?
> We just glanced in "The Bus Depot" catalog and didn't see the necessary
> parts ... are there resources for this type of thing?  And would I have
> problems this winter if I don't fix it?

Actually it is more of a heating system than cooling, however the left flap
does direct some air through the oil cooler... you can find the thermostat
but you are probably going to have to go junk yard digging for the flaps (if
anyone knows where to get them I am needing a set as well). You may have
problems getting the engine to warm up during the winter (depending on where
you live).  Now that I think about it your engine is different than mine so
the flaps may not be as necessary on yours as on my 2 Lire. But they were
put there for a reason so I'd find some.
