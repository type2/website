Subject: Double Cab Snorkel?

Hi All.

I had the great fortune of meeting list member Bill Abbott this weekend. 
He brought his single cab over, and we poked around each other's busses 
and chatted for quite a while.

During the course of our conversation, Bill pointed out a unique feature 
on my '76 double cab. In the right rear corner of the passenger cab (on 
the exterior) is a small vent that goes down into the interior of the 
cab. It then makes a couple of right hand turns through a plastic part, 
and then the passageway goes through one of the frame rails all the way 
back to the engine compartment.

Now, given that this is allegedly a former West German army vehicle, Bill 
and I surmised that it must be a snorkel for the engine while fording 
through a stream.

Anyone have any ideas on this? Ever seen it on other late model double 
cabs? I'd love to find out more information on this.

Curiosly yours,

Bren
