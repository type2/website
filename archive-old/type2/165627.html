> Through a farm magazine, I got a flyer for

Skeptical is good.  Let's look at their claims:

    * 5% or More Fuel Mileage Increase
    * 8 to 14-times Less Engine Wear
    * Extended Oil and Filter Service Life
    * Up to 18% Less Toxic Exhaust Emissions

"8 to 14-times Less Engine Wear"

What does that mean?  You can reduce wear by some percentage or to a
fraction of its previous rate, but you can't have "14 times less" wear.
It's a claim meant to impress the ignorant.

"Up to 18% Less Toxic Exhaust Emissions"

Yeah, right!

Definitely snake oil.  Magnets can collect ferrous metal particles, but
most of the particles in your engine are non-ferrous.  The steel is the
hardest metal in the engine so it wears the least.

If you really want to play with magnets, go to this outfit:

http://www.wondermagnets_dot_com

and get a hefty neodymium magnet or two.  Stick them to the outside of
your filter and you'll be in business.
