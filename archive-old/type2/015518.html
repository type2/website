This could be just the rear shoes needing adjustment, or it could be the MC
full of crud and varnish, or just air in the lines.

Flush and bleed the old fluid/air out of the brakes before driving further.
If that doesn't bring the pedal up, inspect all seals for leaks. If black
crud comes out of the bleeders, rebuild or replace all cylinders and
calipers. Look over those flex hoses as well. Then flush/bleed the fluid
every year as standard maintenance. Don't let the varnish and crud get
started.
