Speaking of books, I've found that libraries are wonderful havens for
travellers, especially when in a busy city where withdrawing for some
peace is difficult. In addition to recreational reading, a library
is an excellent resource for non-biased local information. Just about 
any other information source for travellers will be biased towards
places that part you from your money as quickly as possible.
Even better, many libraries now have internet terminals. Only 
problem is that figuring out how to rlogin to get and send mail
can be tricky and an activity viewed with suspicion bu the locals.

One thing that irritates me is that when staying for a while in a
place it would be nice to get a library card and be able to check out
books. That priviledge usually requires a residence street address, which 
may not be so easy to come up with. (Although people who are regulars
in a particular park can usually use that as a residence address.) 

   OTOH, I have had a life-long wish to live the simple
