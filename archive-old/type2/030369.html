Volk, 

Sean wrote me privately about a shifter rattle that was driving him
bonkers.  I am posting my response, because I I have the same problem and
would love to hear a solution.  My take on the problem is that it is a
combination of factors, worn bushings, wear in the trans, and the ball and
cup getting worn over time.  Replacing the "shuttlecock" guide bushings
on the shifter rod would help, if anyone made them.  Replacing the shift
coupler does help, but is only one step in the process, and it seems a bit
extreme to rebuild the trans for an annoyance.

Sean,

The shifter in the '73 rattles as well, and I don't know what the solution
is.  filling the cup with grease lasts about a week.  I think the real
problem is just age and wear.  The cure is to dig up a NOS shifter and
"peace pipe" (my name for the forward section of the shift rod).  Since
that isn't really possible, I have wondered if getting a roll of teflon
tape and wrapping the bejeesus out of the ball might not help a bit.  The
other (non-classy) solutions are that I have found that pushing the shift
a *hair* out of gear will quiet things for a couple of minutes so I can
get my sanity back or that it may just be bungie time :-)
