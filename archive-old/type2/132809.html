DELETE THE MESSAGES!! They are viruses. Someone on the list is infected.
I've been getting a handful also and this email address is used *ONLY* for
the type2 list so only someone on this list would have my email address on
their computer for the virus to pick up.

I am the mail administrator for an ISP. I started using a separate email
address for each list specifically for spam/virus tracking. I'm not getting
any spam at this address but I am getting 4 or 5 viruses/day. With that low
of a number it's probably only one or two people infected.

So, -- everyone get an UP TO DATE virus scanner and check your system.

And, I know that all bus geeks are not computer geeks but everyone needs to
follow a bit of common sense advice (that Chris appears to be following).
If you receive an attachment in email that you did not have PRIOR KNOWLEDGE
OF then just DELETE IT! It doesn't matter if it from someone you don't
know, from your boss, or from your Mom -- if they didn't send a PLAIN
message (no attachment) or call you on the phone saying they were about to
send you a "cool attached file" then just DELETE the message. If they ask
if you got an attachment from them later then TELL them you deleted it
because you weren't expecting it and it could have been a virus.

The bagle virus slammed our support lines with "Hey, you sent me this file
and nothing happened when I clicked on it" calls. Bagle sends itself from
"support_at_", "admin_at_", "administration_at_" addresses using the domian it is
sending TO as the part after the "_at_".
