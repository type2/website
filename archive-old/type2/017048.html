Just my 2 cents here....

I gotta agree.

I've been wanting to get rid of my pop-top (not the bus, just the top) for
quite a while now. I've been looking for the past year for "something
better"... like a high-top or a riviera pop-top. something that would be
functional AND made for that model.

First of all, you need to know that my pop-top is in horrible shape. The
canvas is excellent, but the shell has a huge crack going down the
centerline and is only getting bigger. the hinge supports up front can only
be described as "not good"

Anyway, getting to the point, yesterday I was perusing my favorite salvage
yard and found a '74 Riviera pop-top conversion. The engine had been
stripped out, the body was in ok condition, pilot area was gone, but all of
the camper stuff was there and in very good shape.

I pulled off the pop-top. everything was there, the wood was in great shape
not rotted, the vinyl was not torn, the zippers worked perfect, only one
screen was punched out.

I'll be able to do this conversion easily as no structural changes need to
be made. I'd recommend this type of change as no cutting/welding needs to be
done, it's an original accessory, and it fairly easily done (except for it
being dog-gone heavy)

Oh, if anyone in the San Francisco bay area is interested in the
cabinet/sink (water tank & AC hook-ups) or the rear bench (i think it's a
"z" bed) it's at "AC Auto Wreckers" at 220 Rankin Street, at Evans in San
Francisco.
