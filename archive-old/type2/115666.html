> I just replaced the wheel bearings on both my front wheels, and I think I
> may need new spindles.  I noticed a wear pattern on the outer end where the
> outer bearing's inner race appears to have spun on the end of the spindle.

That's not good. Bluing or discoloration means there was a lack of
lubrication and things heated up and you should pay closer attention to them
in future.
  
> First, are these races supposed to stay put or are they supposed to move?

The races are supposed to lock into the hub with a press fit. You usually
need a drift or bearing driver to get both in. Getting them out it should be
tough also. Only the bearings should freely rotate in their grease. You can
understand the design: spindle fixed, races in hub fixed, bearings
contacting two surface allowing rotation in one plane of motion.

> The original races pulled out easily, and the new races went in pretty
