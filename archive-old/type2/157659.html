At 10:00 AM 1/30/2007, Jon Stiles wrote...
>Just to clarify:
>
>TDC = TOP DEAD CENTER (Mark corresponding to Zero on the pulley)
>BTDC = BEFORE TOP DEAD CENTER (Marks corresponding to numbers located
>counter-clockwise on the pulley)
>ATDC = AFTER TOP DEAD CENTER (Marks corresponding to numbers located
>clockwise on the pulley)

That is exactly backwards. The pulley rotates clockwise.

If you're using a pulley with degree marks (which is what you're 
describing - "on the pulley"), and comparing that to a fixed pointer on 
the engine, BTDC is clockwise (on the pulley) from 0.

At least on a stock AC 2.0 engine, there's a single mark on the pulley, 
and a scale fixed to the engine - with that setup, BTDC is 
counterclockwise (on the fixed scale).


>As for my motor, the idle timing is 7.5 degrees After-Top-Dead-Center 
>at 800
>RPM (Revolutions per minute). The centrifugal advance works like it 
>should
>and progresses the timing to 28 degrees After-Top-Dead-Center at 3000 
>RPM.

Timing _advance_ is when ignition occurs earlier (more BTDC). What 
you're describing is timing _retard_. You're obviously confusing the 
two - a VW engine won't run 28 degrees ATDC at 3000 RPM.
