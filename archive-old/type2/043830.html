 You'll get a variety of opinions on this, probably about as many as you
get responses...

 IMHO, The big brands are the ones with a reputation to defend and they 
charge a premium to finance that defence. You may not necessarily get a
better tyre from them but you are unlikely to get a worse one. Any new
technology or design improvements are likely to be introduced on these
labels as well, since the price premium justifies the research.
 There probably isn't a "best" brand, though from a bus-driving point 
of view it'd be more sensible to shortlist the ones who compete in the
commercial vehicle sector, not those who focus on "sporty" products.
 Even that is questionable, though, given the list recommendations for
Yokohama & Bridgestone tyres.

 Michelin are the biggest, I believe, and their "Kleber" brand is the OEM
fit for the T4/Eurovan. I think (not sure about this) that many baywindow 
busses were originally fitted with Michelins, too, though I assume that
there was a second source that would have been used concurrently.
 There have been posts to the list on delamination problems with 
Michelins- I don't know what would cause this but it doesn't really
dent my faith in them, to be honest. I expect that there are factories in
various places so perhaps the problem was specific to one of those? FWIW
my Agilis 81s are made in Germany, and if I was looking for a stock tyre
for a stock bus I would buy the Michelin Agilis 61 (185R14C, 99R) which is
a load range "C" tyre, the Agilis 81 is the load range "D" version (102R)
which I use in a 195/70R15C.

 Hope this is useful.
