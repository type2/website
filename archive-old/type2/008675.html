All,

Thanks for the info-I have one of these POS in my '69 and didn't know
about *this* particular problem....

One other thing to watch for: the brass (ok, it LOOKS like brass!)
tube fuel-inlet is apparently known to fall out as well!!!!!! YIKES!!! 

My former mechanic showed me a beetle that was in for this very
reason. Apparently, the driver was in 5:00 rush hour traffic when he
smelled a strong fuel odor. He did what he thought sensible; DROVE IT
TO THE MECHANIC!!!! He didn't pull over....didn't bother to check
anything, he just pushed on! 

I might add that it was about 90 degrees outside at the time and
sitting in traffic, on HOT pavement, the fuel could have easily hit
flash-point and....well, you get the picture!!

In the process of driving to the mechanic, this *very* lucky fellow
had to cross a draw-bridge (this was in Portland, Or.)...if the car
had caught on fire at that critical moment..............!

Anyway, I used to keep bailing wire twined around my carb and inlet
AFTER I pounded it in with a 5# sledgehammer (and yes, it moved a good
1/8" when hammered!), but this carb just <loves> to be rebuilt, so the
wire went away.

I guess next rebuild (carb, that is) time, I'll do a more permanent
repair.
