Sounds like my experience a few years ago on the 1.7 in my 73.  I
replace the rear seal and was surprised at how easily it went in.  Made
the test drive and discovered much to my horror that it was still
leaking.  So I replaced it again.  This time I though, "I'll use a
little sealant - that'll fix the bugger".  Wrong!  Still leaked like a
sieve.  

I was suspicious because each of the seals I put in seemed to go in
easier than I thought they were supposed to.  So back to my VW FLAPS.  I
told them of my problem and the guy behind the counter laughed and said,
"What you need is the GERMAN seal, not those Brazilian pieces of junk"
(that he had sold me the previous day no less)!  So I paid the extra $1
and got the "good" seal.  Had to install it with a homemade tool
comprised of a stud, a nut, and a really big washer.  This baby was
TIGHT.  Fixed the leak!
