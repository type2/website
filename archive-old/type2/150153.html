Subject: Re: [T2] Vanagon rotor differences

On Mar 1, 2006, at 9:06 PM, Gundesens wrote:

> I have a California model 1982 Vanagon Westy. I am confused about  
> distributor rotor nos.  Some online vendors list rotors that fit  
> "any stock VW from '59 onward" ,

It's because most of the rotors fit most of the distributors.

> usually the Bosch number 1 234 332 215/04 033.  Others list the 1  
> 234 332 300/04 038 rotor for all vanagons regardless of federal or  
> Calif.   Bentley clearly shows a Calif. rotor is supposed to be  
> 1000 ohms resistance, while federal is 4000 - 6000 ohms, so there  
> would have to be 2 different Vanagon rotors.

Right.

04033 has the 5kohm resistor under the black epoxy.
04038 has the 1kohm resistor AND the tip is much wider.

> All this is probably mute for me as I am installing a CDI system  
> and must remove the resistance from the rotor anyway.  But, is  
> there a PHYSICAL interchangeability issue between these rotors?
> The distributor is part no.039 905 205C.

Either will fit.

You'll find that the 1kohm rotor will suffice when running CDI (at  
least it won't burn out quickly like the 5 kohm rotor will).

Removing all of the resistance will help but I can't say how much  
because it depends on the rest of your wiring, etc.

I was thinking of doing a group buy for 0 kohm rotors from Germany.  
Prices probably have changed since I last looked at them but $11 (inc  
ship) was the ballpark figure for a factory rotor vs. $30 + ship for  
one cut open to remove the original resistor.
