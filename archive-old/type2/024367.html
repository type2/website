 No, No, and almost certainly, yes...  :-)

 All of the documentation on petrol (white gas etc.) fired stoves that
I've come across specifically warns against using them in any sort of
enclosed space. They are prone to "flaring" because they use the heat
of the flame to vapourise the fuel. If you have a spillage and cool the 
vapourisation tube the flame will flare up.
 I have been know to carry an MSR backpacking stove for emergencies,
but if
I had to use it I'd cook outdoors.
