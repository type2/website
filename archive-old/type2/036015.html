> My experiments with vacuum bleeders is, you must typically remove the
> bleeder and pack the threads with grease or sealant or you can draw air
> bubbles through the threads. Note when you pressure bleed a system you
> almost always get some weepage out the threads. The treads don't seal.

I second that - it took me a while to figure out that this was why I 
kept getting bubbles my first time out with the little plastic (Mity-
Mite?) vac pump from the generic FLAPS (under $30).  I wrapped a 
piece of teflon tape around the bleeder screws and it helped.

Jury's still out on the effectiveness of this thing, but it may be 
inexperience.  I think a willing helper with a patient leg is still my 
favorite method.  (Oh!  But we're talking about brake bleeding!)  The 
"submerged hose end" method just used too much fluid for me, 
although if you're sucking air past the threads with a vacuum pump 
you'll go through almost as much :-).

I broke one of the tangs off on the reservoir almost immediately & 
now have to hold it down to keep the vacuum from escaping and 
poisoning my neighbor's dog.  Need to fab a replacement from a 
pickle jar and a couple of bulkhead fittings.
