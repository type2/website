Chris, you said the caliper was hot when you removed it, but you didn't say 
that you had to do anything to retract the piston (maybe squeezing it back with 
a large C clamp).  In my experience, when a caliper seizes-up, usually it is 
the left front, which gets the worst of US/Canada road salt exposure; usually 
the symptom is that the piston cannot retract.  AYMK, getting the caliper off 
requires retracting the piston so that the pad assembly can be lifted over the 
unworn ridge at the outer edge of the disk.  I didn't hear you describing these 
conditions.  My guess at this point would be that something (moisture, rust, 
cup/Oring detritus) in the brake fluid has caused the master cylinder to 
malfunction, to the extent that the front pistons do not retract sufficiently.

You didn't describe the fluid coming out of the m/cyl or the caliper.  Maybe a 
little more narrative (rather than less) would help at this point.
