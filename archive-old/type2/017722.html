>>It contains Methyl Propyl Ketone (107-97-9), Xylene (133-20-7), N-Butyl
>>Acetate (123-86-4), Napta (6472-95-6), Magnesium Silicate

Each number is a CAS Registry number, sort of an ID number for the chemical.
The first four are solvents.  Magnesium silicate is a powder similar in
nature to sodium silicate (water glass).  Water glass with the appropriate
additives makes a good glue for many things, so I assume MgSiO3 does much
the same thing.

Magnesium silicate is very different from magnesium metal.  And the solvents
listed actually are classified as "pleasant-smelling" by most chemists.
Unpleasant = methylamine (dead fish), butyl mercaptan (skunk), skatole
(human feces)...
