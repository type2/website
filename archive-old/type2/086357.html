Having removed many drums, just a word of caution. The hub does as you have
stated slid off of the hub flange when the very special coveted, 7mm nuts
are removed. but only if it is not seized onto the flange. Then if it is, it
is almost impossible to get off. There is no good place to pull or punch it
off from. Then leaving no choose but to remove the stub axle nut 46mm. This
socket is hard to find and expensive but can be substituted with a 1-3/16"
socket. Maybe easier to find and cheaper. Also If you have to remove this
nut it is best to do it when the vehicle is on the ground and the ebrake is
applied. If you do have to remove the hub flange this way, do not be tempted
to pound the stub axle back instead pull the hub off. If the axle is pushed
out of the wheel bearing housing then bearings can fall out and cause damage
if to carefully placed back . My two cents.
