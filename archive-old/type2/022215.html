Comment,
Mary Beth and I have had several occasions to take in road warriors as
they come through Houston on their bus journeys or have road problems.

Evening chit-chat, a few beers, morning breakfast, handing over a few
bucks to those that we could see were on the poor side of town, sometimes
hiding a few dollars on the bus for them to find later so as to not
embarrass. It has all been great fun and warmth.

One thing that often is a source of discomfort as a result of these
meetings is that on several occasions we have failed to receive a card or
note letting us know how the trip went after the bus left our house.

I know it is not a mandate to send post cards or stay in touch, but we
always want to know how the person(s) we helped made out. 

Next time you spend some rest time or "down" time with a fellow busser,
remember to drop that person a line later on down the pike. 
You just might find that it will make everyone feel a lot better about
helping and being helped.

Bob in Houston
