Tommy,

Right on!!!  Great post!  I vary from your procedure in that I
treat bleeding and adjustment as different procedures.  I don't know that
my way is better or worse, just different.  Let me know what you think...

Procedure 1: Bleed the brakes:

1a adjust the shoes hard against the drums (until the wheel turns with
some  difficulty) on all shoes;

1b bleed brakes, start with cylinder furthest from the M.C.

Procedure 2: Adjust the brakes:

2a back the the adjusters to take the shoes off *all the way*; 

2b turn each adjuster on a wheel, 5 clicks at a time (OK, 10 or 15 at
the beginning) until you get a whisper;

2c at that point, I do something I was taught that I have never heard from
anyone else...  I do something called "centering the brakes".  Get in and
pump the brakes 5-6 times. then go out and check the wheel again.  Adjust
one click at a time on both adjusters 'til you get a whisper again. Repeat
this step 'til the pumping does not relieve the whisper, and back the
adjuster off one notch. I usually go thru the centering procedure about
3-4 times before I am happy.
