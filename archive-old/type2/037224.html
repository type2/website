I'm leery of any method that can cut metal.  I've had good luck with
removing debris from tubes with a length of gas welding rod.  Hammer the
end flat and give it a slight bend.  chuck it  into a drill and slowly
feed it into the offending tube, lubing with light oil.  As the tube
clears, bend the flattened end more to clear a bigger hole.  the idea is
that the flattened end scrapes the inside of the tube as it rotates.

Key point is not to force it.  Having a bit of steel lodged in the tube is
worse than what you have now.
