Hi Blake,
           Before you lay out big bucks for gen/alt replacement, how old is 
your battery?  You can get a new one for less than $50, maybe less than $40, at 
the mass merchandisers.  If your battery is 4+ years old, a new one is cheap 
preventive maintenance.

You may be able with a flashlight and mirror to look in the opening in your 
generator to see the top brush.  If the brush is grossly worn, all you may need 
is a new set of brushes [though to install them it's best to pull the 
generator].  

I agree with the other List member who said you probably ran the battery down, 
and did not give it sufficient recharging.  You could think of removing it, 
checking the electrolyte, putting it on a charger..... etc.  Then cleaning the 
terminals and posts for a nice re-install.

Good luck...... Glenn
