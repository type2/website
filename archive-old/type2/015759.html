Forget about the water cooled conversion if you are on a budget.  As for
the carb bushings, replacing the carb is ridiculous... why?  Because the
freakin throttle plates unbolt from the carbs!!  Any competent machine shop
should be able to rebush them.  BUT, your bus can run okay with worn
throttle shafts.  I ran that way for years.  You CAN get it to idle
smoothly.  You can compensate for the slight vacuum leak by richening your
idle mixture.  Now eventually you will get to a point to where the shaft
holes are way oval-shaped and then you will have to replace them, but don't
let the carb-nazis scare you into buying new carbs or converting to a Weber
until you've taken a close look at what you've got.  Try fixing the
stockers first and save yourself a bundle.  Rebuild kits are available -
try Bus Depot or RMMW, if they don't have them call Vee Parts in San Diego
at 619-477-4787.  They are under $15 each.  Clean them out well and see how
your bus runs.  If it still won't run right and you're POSITIVE that the
carbs are the problem, then consider the Weber conversion, but like any
aftermarket conversion you can expect to have problems with tuning,
installation, etc.  Or maybe you'll get lucky.  Good luck with it!
