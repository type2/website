Subject: Re: [T2] Why VW buses die

here's my take on your questions. Plus take a look at the library
section at type2_dot_com I believe there are some things to know about
when thinking about a bus.

1. I drive my 79 80-90 miles every day. This is w/ a way worn out
engine, but important things like lights/brakes/tires in good shape.
When in decent shape they make great daily vehicles. Your price range
will dictate how nice the bus is, but except for a steal of a deal,
expect to pay to get one either up front or in repair later. In my
case I prob have about $1400 in my bus. Not bad for a reliable piece
of transportation.

2. I live in TN, & no we don't get crap for a winter weather, but one
thing we do get which other more artic areas of the world don't get
are ice storms. My bus performed quite well the few times I have had
it on questionable roads. But that's around here. Oh the high
clearance is nice when you have to take it off a paved road. :)

3. see #1 above :) A type 2 in good shape should not leave you
stranded, but it's nice to know that many of the repairs that would
leave you on the shoulder of a road are usually easily fixed on
location w/ minimal tools. Plus if you bought a bus in good mechanical
shape this should rarely happen w/ a good maintenance habit. Plus how
many other types of vehicles do you know that have a list of people
around the US (& world) who can help if broken down (airs).
