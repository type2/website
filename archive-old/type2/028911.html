Hi Ric,

Well before you burn your bus I would get your tyre pressures way down.
The max pressure marked on the tyre would be for a vehicle of the max
permissible weight for the tyre - and for a D rate tyre your van is way
below that.

A 69 bus would originally have had cross ply tyres, radials being introduced
in August 71
So as you have bought radials (better than cross ply) you do as for a 71
bus; Bently manual says
front 30psi rear 34psi up to 3/4 payload
front 30psi rear 40psi above 3/4 payload

Inflation over this will cause bad tyre wear - the centre part of the tyre
width will wear out first.

This alone may be the cause of the bad braking.

Next as someone else has suggested check the torque of the wheel nuts  - it
should be 94 foot pounds.

If that doesn't cure your problem then take it back to the tyre shop as the
most likely thing is poor balancing. If they are not capable of doing the
balancing correctly (and with a 69's large hole in the middle of the wheel
they will need a special adaptor on the machine) then take it somewhere
else.

Take it somewhere else if they refuse to do the wheel nuts up with a torque
wrench too.

Good luck
