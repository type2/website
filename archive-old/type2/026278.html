> If I'm going to spend most of my time on the Motorway, what would be

> best replacement engine ?

Your best bet would be to rebuild it yourself (unless you have piles
of cash).  Not really difficult, just time consuming.  Then you will
*know* it's been built properly and is not going to seize on you.
There are lots of books around on the subject.
For optimum reliability, go with a stock engine.

> I thought to go for the biggest i.e 2.0l but the garage has said that
> anything with twin carbs is going to require a lot of tuning.

Much more expensive too and harder to get parts.  The stock carbs
aren't a problem once they're set up properly, provided they're not
worn out.

> Is cruising at 75-80 mph an unrealistic goal for my Van ? If so what
> speed
> does every one else go at ? Is this foot to the floor ? :->

This is more like the *maximum* speed.  You will not do your van any
favours by going at this speed all the time.  Running at a good 10 mph
less will put a lot less strain on your engine and you will use a lot
less fuel (and stay below the speed limit).  If you really want to go
fast, get a newer vehicle.  Sorry if this sounds "preachy", just use a
bit of common sense.  These type 2's are getting old and need to be
preserved.
