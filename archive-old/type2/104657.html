At 08:28 PM 3/11/2003, Greg Potts wrote:

>To rephrase: With the crank end of the rod held in one place the piston end of the rod can be moved fore and aft about 2-3mm. 

I don't remember mine doing this.  If I recall, the spec for bearing clearance is only a couple of thousandths of an inch.  With the surfaces oiled (I assume that area is not "dry", even though you've started to disassemble it) there should be no "pivoting" of the rod at all.  If there was no oil on the bearings at all, it might pivot a tiny bit, but even then I think 2-3mm is way too much.  Just for the sake of knowledge, go buy a plastigage kit and measure the clearance:

http://www.dana_dot_com/perfectcircle/Plastigage.htm

My opinion is that the bottom end of your engine will need to be rebuilt.  :-(
