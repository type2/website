Subject: Re: [T2] cross drilled drums

Normal bus highway speeds combined with our inherently high drag have never
caused me any brake heating or fade problems even on some of the longest,
steepest grades in the US (assuming you're not running your bus in a grand
prix). My experience with bus rear brakes is that they have more than enough
capacity to lock the rear wheels, after which their performance is
irrelevant.  Remember, a large percentage of the bus's weight gets shifted
to the front wheels, so the front disks really have to do most of the work
since that's where the traction is.

The one time my front brakes didn't work, it was really nice that the rear
ones did, though they didn't do much before locking up.

I'd agree with the other posters that cutting/drilling/welding brake
structural components would be a bad, bad idea.
