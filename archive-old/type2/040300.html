> How would you adapt the VW half-shafts to the Subaru 5-speed?  I'm
> embarrassed to admit I've never seen a Subaru transaxle out of the vehicle,
> so can't visualize the connection/CV joint arrangement.  Are the Subaru

Frankly, I don't know.  My subaru experience is rather dated.  If the ring
gear can't be flipped, then the point is moot anyway.  I would expect to
either use adapters between the subaru flanges and the VW cvs or perhaps
have special axles made with the Subaru CV on one end and the VW spline on
the other.  I'm pretty sure that special axles will be required anyway, as
the chance of their having the exact length required would be small.
 
> How much re-engineering would it take to mount the Subaru transaxle in a
> baywindow bus?

It's a question of size.  Subaru borrowed a lot of its design parameters
from the aircooled VWs.  Brackets can be fabricated fairly easily as long
as the transmission isn't larger than the VW unit.  the shift linkage
would be an adventure!

> Where would the radiator live?

I've seen very clean conversions where the radiator lived in the left side
of the engine bay and was fed from the left air inlet only.  If
well-sealed, this arrangement seems to work well.  I've also seen
variations with the radiator in the rear hatch and under the bus between
the frame rails.

I've toyed (and done nothing else) with this idea for years.  If the
