Actually it was sort of right the first time, a solid lifter pusrod is
indeed 2 piece, 3 actually, 5/16" steel ends pushed into a roughly
1/2" diameter aluminum rod.  Hydraulic lifter pushrods the ends
are welded on or swaged down and appear essentially 1 piece.
But as Karl mentions this isn't reliable as custom pushrods might
appear either way.  The original "give" tip is a pretty good clue, even
with well pumped lifters you can usually lean on the pushrod end
with a mongo big screwdriver there where it is knurled and feel
give on a couple.  If it gives it sure ain't solid.  You got to do it
on vaguely non opened valves as loaded the "give" is loaded but
not really at TDC, just pop a cover and lean on a few of them.
Looking down the pushrod for the spring like he says is definitive,
unless they are crappy non OEM non dissasembleable lifters
(they do exist.)
