I agree, David, but think we now have to make a distinction between
types of EGR valves.  The '78 2.0 has a strictly mechanical EGR
activation via direct linkage to the throttle bellcrank, and I think
other late loafs used an electrically actuated EGR valve.

I think vacuum would be a potential factor only if

  a) the valve was vacuum operated,

or

  b) if the EGR valve was simply rendered inactive but left in place
     and not blocked off, and the EGR valve itself leaked.
