 My experience with the bus heating system is that a well sealed system
with good heater boxes is ok down to -15 celsius (32 farenheit) on highway
speeds. Cruising at the same speed for long distances seems to cool the
heaterboxes down. Traffic and varying speed gives more heat, overtake now
and then ;-) 

	If the door sealings are in good shape you are free from a lot of draft.
Also check where the cluth and brake pedals are going trough the floor.
Originally there is a seal there. If this seal is gone a lot of cold air is
flowing up around your feet. 

	Fur clothing is good but it won't keep the windows from iceing. The most
important in your clothing is warm boots. The lower left front corner of
the bus seems to be the hardest section to heat. If we could drive our
busses sitting back there over the engine compartment there would be no
problem. That's were my cat use to reside on cold trips. No creature finds
warm places like a cat...........

	A boat bilge blower on the  tube under the bus is very helpfull in city
driving when you don't use high engine speeds for long or waiting for
traffic lights. At least it helps keeping the windows free from mist.

	Here in Finland we can buy 12V electric seat heaters that fit in under the
seat upholstery. They draw about 80 watts and are very nice, at least your
butt and back gets heating right from the start. They are effective enough
to make you want to switch it off after a few minutes of driving. Most cars
sold here have these seat heaters as an original option.

	The ultimate system is a gas fired heater such as the Ebenspracher, Carver
or Webasto. With a good working Ebenspracher BN4 you can drive till your
gas freezes over. 

Stay warm out there!
