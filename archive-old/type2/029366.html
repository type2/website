My personal oppion from simialr experiences is that its not worth the
damage to use the tar based sound proofing techniques,  any modifications
that are easily undone , imo, are potential disasters,  whats going to
happen if we ( i made the mistake of using the "cool patch" garbage in my
bus) need to do body wpork in the future. are we going to somewhow scrape
all of this stuff off? thats not a very appealing thought, i have t4ried
to remove small amounts of it to no avail.   Essentially it is linig your
bus with a sticky goey runny mess that will forever get stuck on anything
that touches it and forever be a hinderance to repairs.  I kick myself
whenever i think of what i  did to my bus, i must have devalued it by 2
grand.  Dynamat, which i have used on many cars, is somewhat less
destructive BUT after a few years can become a pita to remove as well.  i
do like the use of carpet padding as it can gbe easily removed for repairs
and is effective. i guess my point is just that there are serious trade
offs to consider when trying to "sound proof " a bus, and that they should
all be weighed,  is it nice to have a quiet bus? definetly, am i glad i
did what i did, absolutley not. 
good luck whatever path you choose
