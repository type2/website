I am aware that what a welding professional calls a "wire-feed welder" is
probably a MIG. What is today commonly (by nonprofessionals) called a
wire-feed welder uses only flux-core wire, no gas. This is not a MIG, there
is no Inert Gas. I didn't invent the use of the term. Whatever "wally world"
is, I don't know, but yes they are the budget machines. This is what the
original poster was asking about. Call it crummy, whatever, they work fine
for most home users who can't justify the kind of machine you are using.
