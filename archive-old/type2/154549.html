============Karl wrote==============

It takes a little work but it does come out.

Gently remove the filler neck [a claw hammer works if you are careful]
Remove the little plastic nailed in stops holding the wood wall in at the
bottom. [wire cutters work if you are careful] Gently remove the wall.
Loosen the 2 clamps holding the pump and the drain hose. Unscrew the drain
fitting. Then unscrew the pump.
Now remove the phillips screws holding on the black rubber things.
Tank slides out by lifting the back end so the two holes clear the shelf.
