If your bus was assembled in Germany I can decipher a
lot of facts on your car by looking at the so called
M-plate. This plate is fixed behind the drivers seat
and has production information on it. 

> manufacturing year for example: In the registration
> card says it's a 1969
> model. I've been surfing in the web and I can't find
> exact data about buses built in that year.

It sure looks like a 1969 model because:

- it has a dashboard painted in the body colour (this
was only done in modelyears 1968 and 1969, since 1970
they are black).
- there is a metal wall in between the gas tank and
the engine. This was done since the spring/summer of
1968.
- the 3rd digit of the chassisnr is a '9' meaning it
is from modelyear 1969.
- The inner rear-view mirror has a long standard and
has a vinyl cover instead of being chromed. (chromed
is 1968 only and a few cars in 1969, in 1970 they came
with mirrors that had a short standard)
- the sun vizors cover the entire lenght from the
window side to the standard of the inner rear view
mirror. in 1968 this wasn;t the case and in 1970 the
shorter standard of the mirror required different
shaped sun vizors.

...and there is more. But I will not exhaust you with
all those details ;-)
