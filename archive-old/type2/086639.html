I replaced my faucet a couple years back, back when the replacements were
twice the $$ they are now. I had the same problem, which I solved by
unscrewing the nipple from the old faucet and using it on the new faucet,
now the old hose fit perfectly :-)

I have since replaced my dead water pump with an RV style on-demand pump
(pump controlled by integrated pressure switch) and pressure tank which is
connected to the city water faucet nipple and I blocked off the old nipple
we are speaking of here. It's kind of nice to have total control of the
flow rate, no more turning on the switch and having the stream of water get
deflected by a spoon in the sink all over the place. If your pump is not
responding to the switch, double check your pumps wire connections, it's
kind of a weird connector, with screws holding the bare wires. Also check
the ground which is attached to the body at the waste water vent area on
your 79.
