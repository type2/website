-->  I had the same problem, except at .3.  Your odometer gear is
cracked, wedging against the gear it meshes with, and slipping on the
shaft.

I solved this by taking out the speedometer and removing the damaged gear
-- it'll slip right off the shaft.  I put some epoxy in it, then slid it
onto the shaft, and wound copper wire tightly around the gear's hub to
pull the cracked portion together.  Added a little epoxy to the wire, so
it'd stay put forever.  That fixed the slipping.  Now, because the crack
had spread a couple of the teeth a little, the gear was still wedging
against the one it meshes with.  So, I filed the teeth on either side of
the crack.  Little bit at a time, with an emery board, where it was
interfering. Every so often I'd run the thing up a mile by hand, turning
the speedo input shaft, to check my progress.  Eventually I got it to the
point where it meshed okay, and put it back in the van.  It's worked great
since. 

Unexpected benefit:  My speedometer bobbles less.  The wedged gear was
putting a lot of tension on the cable.
