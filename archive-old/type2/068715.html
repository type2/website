Jon,
  To address this problem methodically, I would check one component after
another. Start with the battery: The fact that the lights are still on
doesn't mean squat, the starter is MUCH more demanding than the
lights...Take out the battery, clean the terminals, then trickle charge it
over night. Take it to Les Schwab, have them do a load test. It's free. Then
either buy a new battery or reinstall the charged old one. Before
reinstallation, clean the terminals, both at the battery and on the cables.
If the problem persists, I'd investigate the condition of the brushes in the
alternator. Heck, while you have the alternator out, replace the brushes
anyway.
They're, like, 1$ a pair...Check it's bearings while you're at it. Have the
alternator tested at your local FLAPS,
also free ususally. Reinstall. If problem persists: Get back to us!

hours now...
