Rob,
 The voltage regulator controls the field current applied
to the alternator. When there is no current applied to the
field, there is no voltage produced from the alternator.
Sooo, when voltage drops below 13.5 the regulator will
apply current to the field and the alternator will start
charging. When the voltage exceeds 14.5 the regulator will
stop supplying voltage to the field and the alternator will
stop charging. In other words I dont think it would be a
problem with your plan...
