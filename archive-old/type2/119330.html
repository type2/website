Subject: Tempeh and Bus Apathy in SFBA

On Mar 4, 2004, at 8:59 AM, Seann Stafford wrote:

> Non bus content:
> what the heck is a tempeh?

Tempeh is a Japanese fermented soy bean cake (not to be confused with 
natto, with is a Japanese fermented soy bean stinky-goo-pile).  It can 
be used as a meat substitute (or not; it's pretty good in its own right 
:-)

When I was in university, there was a Chinese restaurant in town that 
would do General's Chicken with tempeh instead of chicken; it was 
absolutely delicious.

You should be able to find tempeh at any asian market or natural food 
store.  It's also easy to find recipes for it.  Deborah Madison has a 
great cookbook called _Vegetarian Cooking for Everyone_ with several 
terrific tempeh recipes in it.  Don't be afraid of this cookbook if 
you're not a vegetarian (I'm a dedicated meat eater, but I use recipes 
from this cookbook 3 or 4 times a week).
