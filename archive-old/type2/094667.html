Jamie,

	If the brake booster is filling with brake fluid you have seals
that are shot in the master cylinder. Each time you are pressing down
the brakes fluid is leaking around the seals and making its way into the
booster itself. I've seen this happen on my parent's volvo and my
mercedes every time the MC failed except once, so I would say it is
fairly common way of failure. Replacing the master cylinder should solve
this problem but, since the person said he never had to add fluid, this
is probably not the case. The only way I could see water getting into
the booster is through the vent line, which might be caused by going
through a big puddle. I'd say check the location of the vent line and
see if it is possible for water to be entering it. These are the only
two ways I could see fluid getting into the booster.

Jonathan
