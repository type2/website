Hi Folks,

This thread is getting old, but I thought I'd toss in my 2 cents about the 
fuel tank reserve.

1. The reserve really is just a line on the gauge.
2. My 1979 owner's manual says that when the needle is in the reserve area, 
approximately 1 gallon of fuel is left in the tank.
3. The reserve appears to hold more than 1 gallon, since this weekend I 
ended up driving from Moab, UT to Fruita, CO on less than a quarter-tank of 
gas.  Due to excessive smoke inside my Bus, I neglected to stop at one of 
the gas stations near Moab, and ended up driving nearly 100 miles to Fruita. 
  We came into the gas station "dead stick", since the engine quit at the 
offramp and we coasted from there.  Anyway, I'm either getting better gas 
mileage than I thought, or my reserve is larger than the owner's manual 
states.

So, here's my current hypothesis on the fuel reserve:
1. The reserve on my Bus indicates about 2 gallons.
2. The space between 1/4 and R indicates about 3 gallons.
3. To avoid excessive worry, fill the tank when the fuel gauge reads less 
than 1/4.

Your mileage may vary....
