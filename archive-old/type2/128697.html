On Tuesday, July 13, 2004, at 12:15PM, Paul Manning <paulmanning97_at_yahoo_dot_com> wrote:

>In looking through the archives, it seems that (a) no one has had a
>good experience with high temperature paints and (b) no one's posted
>about it in a while. Has anyone that's had a more current experience
>with POR20, BBQ paint, Eastwoods, VHT or any other manufacturer's high
>temperature paint? 

I've tried two high temp paints and both were a disappointment. The last one burned off:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/Painting/74-Rear.jpg

I thought I'd give it one more try and this time I used VHT. The finish is certainly matte and rough and not that appealing at first but so far it's held up better than the other two after the initial curing I gave it (The muffler temps aren't hot enough to adhere to the curing instructions which claim the paint won't be solvent resitant. So far water hasn't been a problem). It also smelled very bad for several days but the smell has now abated. I would park the car and then get a strong whiff from it.

I've put 2k miles on it so far with no deterioration and I'll report again in 6 months.

>Would it be a good idea to replace the rusted bolts (from heater boxes
>to 4->1 and 4->1 to muffler) with stainless steel ones?

Definitely. There is no benefit to using anything else.
