Let's make this easy, convert it to an upright, use the type I conversion
info from Joe Cali. The rear type IV engine mount is easily adapted to the
narrow width of the early type II frame. Just drill the three mounting holes
after narrowing the mount from the outside ends. Transmission should be
swapped at the same time. Be sure to utilize the hangers on the later
transmission by cutting the old hanger supports from the later bus and
welding them onto yours. (Again not much of an issue.) Tire size may be
slightly different, but hey. Bigger is better. I would not even consider
"adapting" a flat style engine to an upright engine compartment. Especially
given the advantages (cooling and otherwise) afforded by the upright
conversion. If you are worried about resale. Plop the old engine and
transmission on a pallet and store in your garage. The only item you welded
on was the hangar support from the old van, which can easily be undone.
