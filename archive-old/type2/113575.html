>>Could you drill a small hole in the side of the bellhousing and squirt 
>>liquid grease through it onto the shaft?
>
>No grease, anti-seize is the ticket here. The hole in the bellhousing deal 
>sounds like a good way to end up with grease on the disk. Very bad news. 
>Clean and dry will work if the spines are unworn. Biggest problem would be 
>if it sits in high humidity sometime and rust gets started. It could then 
>get sticky. The anti-seize seals the metal from the air so prevents this 
>issue, as well as providing the type of non-migrating lube for free 
>movement.  I lube the splines on the shaft and disc then slip them 
>together then remove the excess before I assemble the clutch. Thin film on 
>everything and nothing left to foul the disk.
