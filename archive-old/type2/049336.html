Sounds remarkably like a cylinder full of fuel.  Pull the two plugs on
that side of the engine, remove the little wire from the coil to the
distributor, get your fire extingusher out and crank the engine.  I'll
bet that it'll now turn over and pump a quart of fuel out.  After the
fuel has evaporated, put it all back together and it'll probably
start.  

It sounds like the float needle is stuck on that carb... Might want to
rap it smartly on top of the float chamber with a screwdriver handle.

Good luck, and BE CAREFUL!!!
