I bought a brand new steering box for my 72 Bus in Mexico last year.
Interestingly enough it also fits the Busses up to 79.
It is made by "WAT" in Spain (I think) and cost approx. $230.

At the time it was a kind of panic buy on my way back from Belize and Guatemala. In Guatemala I had fallen into a huge pothole/mountainslide, bent the bumper, flattened the 72-only towhook, flattend the front pan and mostly landed on the steering box. The steering lever was pushed backwards with force, which means I turned left. Luckily I stopped before going downhill. Don't drive at night in Guatemala.
Afterwards the steering wheel was offset 45 degrees and I was worried. Imagine my surprise when a dealer in Mexico handed me a new steering gear on request. I never installed it as the problem turned out to be a bent tie rod. The steering gear is now my security blanket.
In 2 weeks I will take my 67 Westy to Panama. Now that steering gear is a problem! They don't even remember the splitty busses down there.
