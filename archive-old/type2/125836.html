Barry ---
           Generally speaking, you run the risk of transients when connecting a 
charger or jumping.  I'm not talking about people who move from place to place 
frequently, but short-lived voltages well above the nominal voltage of your 
system.  In the days before computers, the system could tolerate higher 
voltages for a short time; but if you have a fuel injection system -- and 
remember the Type III circa 1969 was fuel injected -- you can damage the 
semiconductors inside the control system.  These are very efficient at what 
they do, but they are not generally tolerant of transient high voltages.

Play it safe: Pull the ground off the battery before you connect a charger to 
it.  Or, if you frequently charge the battery, go to your FLAPS and get the 
kind of disconnect switch that fits on a battery terminal.

Regarding your question about the second battery, the most likely case is that 
the connection limits the current.  The second battery is probably not 
installed with large cables, or perhaps the terminals are corroded.... anything 
that offers resistance to current flow would account for what you describe.
