	[Crick, Bill [KAN:0W40-M:EXCH]]  Diameter, lift, duration, timing,
and amount of restriction/pressure  in the intake tract,  all affect
aspiration. You could have small diameter valves that lift higher, for
longer in a freer breathing intake tract, and get more air/fuel in to the
cylinder. Note also resonance effects can also have significant effects at
certain RPMs, although I suspect these are minor in most four stroke
engines.
