> older story about my 1960 23window deluxe.

> I was a scout leader at the time and our troop was at the annual Camporee

> get hit with a tornado.  (yes one boy was killed) most of the scouts did
> the right thing and formed up on a nearby beach.....
> 
>    Since the campsites were destroyed and everything bar none was soaked
> the kids were loaded into cars, parents were called etc.  but no scout was
> allowed back into the campsite.
> 
>   I stayed behind to guard and help clear the campsite up, also I was the
> only one left who was medic trained...My van was parked between two other
> larger vans (fords I think) in any case a tree crashed down and clobbered
> both other vans, mine was untouched and just needed a little brush
> clearing and driving it into the clear.
> 
>   In anycase the next day the leaders who remained went in to recover as
> much gear as possible.   drove my bus into the site after some trees were
> removed.   looked around at the destruction and tried to figure out what
> to do next????
> 
>    I opened the sunroof on the van and started throwing gear into the van
> over the top.   when finished I lashed my canoe onto the roof and drove
> back to the church.  I arrived with all of the gear and tents for 23 boys
> and  3 other leaders, and my stuff all in this one van.  water (everything
> was soaked) was dripping out of the side door and I was down on the shock
> stops, probably could get it up to 35-40mph.  well the kids were waiting
> and one runs up to the bus and opens the side door, and gets hit by a
> tidal wave of water that had drained from all of the wet stuff!  
> 
>   That probably was the heaviest I ever packed any of my vans, soaking wet

> van, three days later we used it to haul a half dozen of my scouts down to
> the funeral of the scout that was killed....his scoutmaster was a friend
