     Since I'm sure there's a relatively new audience out there (we get a 
fair amount of turnover around these parts), thought I'd repost this old 
favorite of mine. Original text is by Bob Hoover, and it explains how to 
make your very own degree wheel. Once you've got that, you can time your 
engine using a static timing light, rather than the more expensive 
advancing timing light. It's in the archives at 

<http://vw.i405_dot_com/sm.asp?path=\type2_at_primenet\11\0721.txt>.

     Basic gist of it is using simple high school algebra and precision 
measuring instruments to make your own degree wheel. Pretty cool. Note 
that the link in my original post doesn't work anymore, since the Vanagon 
archives have moved, but the original article is included in the post.
