You're referring to 'Stick's' method for determining torque - for low torque
applications such as the 6 acorn nuts on oil drain plate of the upright motors,
or the center 13mm on the oil drain plate of the late motors. You turn until the
tendon in your wrist pops out.
You have to be careful not to overtighten because you could strip the nuts,
and/or 'damage
the cam' in the case of the later motors. Undertightening of course would lead
to loss of
oil and main bearing failure - so be sure you get it right.

Exhaust manifold nuts should be tighter than that though- I would say about 14
foot pounds.
