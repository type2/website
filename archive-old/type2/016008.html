Ted,

	Your radio is probably powered from the same fuse as the brake lights, or
from a fuse powered only when ignition is on. The voltage drop caused from
using the brake lights is enough to temporarily shut the radio off. This is
normal on such an old car where the contact surfaces on all connections
between the battery and the radio have corroded a little. 

There are a few that are easy to check:

-First the battery connectors. Clean the battery poles and the connectors
with a wire brush. Also check the connection between the minus cable and
the bus body. 

- The plus cable from the battery ends up at the starter, from there it
continues to the ignition switch. Clean the connections at the starter.
Remember to disconnect the battery first!

- Old fuses can also cause bad contact, try to change the fuses and clean
the contact sufaces on the fuse holders. Remember to disconnect the battery
first!

-The ignition switch can also be bad but then you would have other syptoms
too.

-Check the ground connection to the radio. 

And before you do anything, remember to disconnect the battery!
