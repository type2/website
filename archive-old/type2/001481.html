Mark::

Make sure that you have 12v at the coil when the ignition switch is turned
to "start" (cranking).  Requires a helper to turn the key or a push button
"jumper switch"  from the engine bay.   A defective ignition switch  (among
other causes) will prevent 12 v from getting to the coil when turned to the
start position, and no spark.  When you release it and it return to the "on"
position, it allows the 12v to the coil, and the engine tries to start in
that last few seconds it is still moving as it finishes turning over.
