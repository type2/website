What you said is correct and reflects my understanding based on the book I 
have for performance Corvairs.

>No, What the Ignitor II does is have active variable dwell. In simple
>english, dwell controls the electrical saturation of the coil. You want the
>coil to be a full saturation so that, when it discharges, it gives you the
>hottest and longest lasting spark. With points ignition and the Ignitor I
>ignition dwell is a set number; therefore your ignition is only optimized at
>a particular RPM. The Ignitor II has electronics in it that varies the dwell
>so that you achieve coil saturation at a wide range of engine RPMs. If your
>planning to run a capacitive discharge the Ignitor II probably isn't better
>than an Ignitor I since coil saturation is controlled by a different device.
