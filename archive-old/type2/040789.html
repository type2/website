	Beth,
	It bucks in low gears, not in high gears (thinks, hmm, more inertia
in high gear..). Does it backfire when it bucks? If so I'd have mechanic-san
check the ignition again. Points have been replaced, how 'bout condensor
and wires? Cap and Rotor?  What color are the spark plugs? Sooty? Clean?
light brown indicating too-lean a burn?

	Try a swap with another engine's parts, just to troubleshoot, or
with the last-working-set you saved from last tune-up. (that's why
you save them)

	Bucking could be bad sparks from any number of sources- coil,
the condensor and wires I just mentioned. I've had two major points
failures in my life and replacing them is a good start, but they
aren't the only suspects in that area.

	The wire from the points to the coil might be intermitantly grounding-
which would give bad sparks and backfires, but not just while accelerating.

	Bucking could also be jumping distributor- which an ignition
strobe timing light or dwell meter should show easily. Distributor
drive OR vaccuum hose problems could cause this. Will it buck if
running without a load? If you just step on the accelerator while
the bus is idling?

	Low fuel pressure from a bad pressure regulator or worn-out
resistance tracks in the air-flow sensor (If applicable) strike
me as possible causes too- the later more likely, but harder
to check. Other '78 owners?
