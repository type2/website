On Sun, 9 Oct 2005, craig wrote:
>this g/w/blk wire comes ->from<- terminal 49a on the emergency flasher
>switch ->to<- term 54 on the switch. this wire also connects to blue
>wire 49a on the flsher relay box.

Right.  Electrically, this wire "starts" at 49a on the flasher relay.
It is the pulsing output from the relay - if you hooked a tail light
bulb between 49a on the flasher relay and ground, it would flash as
long as it was hooked up.  The blue wire brings the juice from the
flasher relay to the three-way connector near the turn signal switch.
Here, it splits into two pieces: one black-white-green wire goes to
54 on the turn signal switch to provide pulsing voltage to run the
turn signals in normal driving, and another black-white-green wire
goes to 49a on the emergency flasher switch to provide pulsing voltage
to run the emergency flashers in an emergency.

>anyways, i got the thing to stop blazing when the ignition key is on,

>sometimes the right rear light won't flash when i activate the t/s

>also, if i tilt the lever assembly just right, i still get the
>problem of hearing the flasher relay clicking as if i have signalled
>for a turn, or have the hazards on.  when this happens, the turn
>signal arrows on the dash do NOT light up, nor do the outside lights,
>or the e-flasher knob.

This sounds like the black-white-green wire at the turn signal switch
(54) is being kinda-sorta shorted to ground.  If it was a dead short,
the arrows in the speedo would light up once and then the fuse would
probably blow.  If the short has a few ohms of resistance, it will draw
enough current to start the flasher relay working and clicking, but not
enough current to light up the arrows on the dash.

>in fact, i might need to replace it anyways: for a long time, whenever
>i hit the brakes, a faint yet distinct -tink- would come from the fuse
>box area; i figured out it was the relay can making the noise.  this
>makes no sense, as the brake lights and turn signals are totally
>seperate - except for where the brake switch wires enter the e-flasher
>switch!

The turn signals and brake lights aren't separate; they live in sin.
The brake lights go THROUGH the turn signal switch, so it can do the
trick of having the left brake light on steady and the right brake
light blinking when you're stopping and signaling for a right turn.
Trouble in the turn signal switch could conceivably result in the
turn signal flasher going 'tink' when you hit the brakes.
