Dave,
Everyone suggests using one shim per cylinder to prevent oil leaks.

My CR adjustment called for a .070" shim under the cylinder.  No one sells
them in this size (at reasonable cost), so ordered a set of .090" shims and am
having them ground to .070".  Interesting find:  the shims i bought were as
much as .018" out with respect to each other, so having them ground (both
sides) trued them up, which will reassure me that all of my torque specs will
be true when i tourque the heads down.

I wonder how many engines just get slapped together with shims that are all
out of whack and what sort of 'mystery problems' are a result of poorly
machined shims?

something to think about?
