> Hey there --
> 
> I recently put some sound into my 76 westy and am *very* happy with the
> results.
> 
> CD: blaupunkt las vegas in dash (this are flying out the door for $199
> from http:www.crutchfield_dot_com) I didn't get mine quite that cheap but imho
> the blaupunkt is a nice way to go in the VW. The styling fits in well and
> I love the auxiliary input for a walkman in case I want to listen to a
> book on tape or whatever). I also put blaupunkt 6.5" speakers in the
> doors. I wired through some existing channels where the door meets the
> body (below the hinge). I wired 6x9s in the back by burying a channel
> beneath the carpet along the sill near the sliding door. The actual rear
> speaker installation was some work: I removed the small door beneath the
> bench seat and took some MDF (medium density fiberboard) and fashioned a
> new front panel slightly larger than the opening. I made cut outs for the
> 6x9s, covered the board with some scrap black speaker box material from
> the local car audio place, and front mounted the speakers. The board
> screws on to the bench seat panel with four *tiny* woodscrews.. sniff..
> 
> So far I've kept everything pretty close to stock. cutting the front doors
> was the worst (most painful) part but it was worth it for the better sound
> possible from the big front speakers. I do not recommend mounting in the
> kickpanels because the weight of the speaker could pull the kickpanel out
> of its guide (not to mention some clearance problems).
> 
> That resulted in some *very* good sound up front with the right deck
> tuning -- the blaupunkt is a nice enough deck that you can make this
> happen.
> 
> Here's where things got excessive though:
> 
> I got a *great* deal on a someone's traded-in amplifier and 10" Kicker
> subwoofer w/bandpass enclosure. I mounted the amp (200W bridged) beneath
> the bench seat on top of the box containing the 110V outlets/fuse/switch
> and just put the small sub box in the "way back" area for now. Eventually
> I am thinking of transforming half of the hanging cabinet into a sealed
> sub enclosure -- I would need to add an "internal" box on the left side
> (front is front) of the cabinet to dampen vibration and I do not relish
> cutting any more holes in my bus but I have a decent plan for doing so if
> and when the somewhat bulky sub box gets on my nerves. For now I can pull
> the box out if necessary and the rest of the time this system is pretty
> damn impressive -- all for ~$500 parts. 
> 
> Admittedly that is an outrageous amount of money for an audio system in a
> bus but an order of magnitude less than many people spend in their
> vehicles and it's been a long time ambition to have a such a kickin'
> system. I mostly don't play it loud but I have to admit cranking up (list
> ___ your favorite artist here i.e. Ben Harper in my case) can give one
> cool goosebumps. My bus=fun for me and I got a good enough deal on it
> including post-buy parts and labor to just barely afford the indulgent
> additional expenditure on sound.  I probably should have bought a propane
> heater instead but I wouldn't have a bought a T2 if I was looking for the
> most practical vehicle, eh? In other words, I am only marginally
> conflicted ;) I really enjoyed figuring out how to put everything in and
> getting the cost/value quotient to a place I could live with. More than
> anything I guess I appreciate the humor? dichotomy? // whatever of a
> clearly 70s styled rig with such a 90s style sound track <g>
> 
> I put a lot of time into thinking about how to design the layout and am
> very happy with the results. Again, I highly recommend the Blaupunkt deck
> and the close out price is amazing! (I've never bought anything from
> crutchfield and their service may be poor but the price is right ymmv) If
