As part of getting the BA6 running I had to splice into the fuel line by the
tank out-let. I have a 3" line then the fuel filter, this being a '76 1600
up-right, the out let is on the bottom an 'L' shape & non-removable.

I crawled under to change the fuel filter & fit a 'T' piece, I noticed a
dark spot on the line near the outlet. I wiggled it to see if it was a leak
& the pipe started to drip, by the time I had got a big fuel container the
pipe had poped off! Fuel was being pumped _at_ pressure over the solenoid
WAAAAAAAAA!! I switched off the inspection lamp, compressor etc. Got a 2'
length of pipe & had to lay in the stream of fuel to get the new pipe on.
Drained the tank & got the new lines in, when I last checked the lines were
perfect.

I found several things about fuel that day.

1/
Although I had a wet blanket & a fire extinguisher under there with me,
ALWAYS have some one near. Even if it's a just a neibour.

2/
Petrol stings when it gets in your eyes.

3/
Petrol makes you sick when you drink it

4/
Petrol make other list member go "poo you stink" when they turn up 5mins
later (hello Stuart, Monday looking good for the parts bus)

5/
The smell comes from your skin for several days even when you bathe & change
your clothes.

6/
You get an uncontrollable fear of any-one with a cigarette!

yours in flames
