	1.  Propane is not much different in any way from CNG, once 
you get it in the vehicle.  (It is much more dangerous in bulk 
distribution.)

	2.  Methanol is considerably different  -  octane rating also 
well above 100, tho' as I say you don't need that if you use WI. 
Our Kiwi hero Munro used methanol  -  do see 'The World's Fastest 
Indian', tho' it omits most of the evidence that he was a genius.

	3.  Methanol is a good fuel, but can be difficult.  It 
dissolves the 'terne metail ' lining of most petrol tanks  -  and 
dissolves quite a few types of flexible hose used in petrol systems 
within vehicles. We have the world's biggest methanol factory, and 
can't export to the previous main user (conversion to the petrol 
additive MTBE in Calif), but are not contemplating creation of a MeOH 
distribution system within NZ.
