John:
The two oe manufacturers of VW valves were ATE (Arthur TEves)(aka
ah-tey-ay, auf Deutsch) and Eaton. I think TRW's are ATE's.
72 and early 73(pre sodium exhausts) would routinely break the heads off
their exhaust valves. The Eaton's would do so at around 60,000 miles,
the ATE's would last longer, to 100-110,000 miles. Usually such failure
occurred while on family vacation, and precipitated severe mechanical
trauma to the engine, always ruining 1 head, the piston, of course, and
sometimes if you were lucky, the crank and case. I am serious about the
vacation part, (it seemed to be long trips that precipitated it).
Virtually ALL 1700 bus motors (without sodium valves) experienced such
failure and I'm sure that fact alone cost VW tens of thousands of repeat
customers. Figure how many type 2's were imported into the US in 72 and
3/4 of 73 and you have an idea of what happened to the guys that
invented the mini-van (sorry, Lee)- sales plummeted.
I'm sure that's a lot cheaper than they have been, but there is probably
a greater supply than will ever be needed. They are only listed for
1700's actually, 2.0's didn't need them so bad because the FI allowed
the engine to run cooler and still reduced  its NOx. They will FIT 2.0's
however and couldn't hurt valve life.
