Hi JC,
The Bolts and bushings that hold the tops and bottoms of the shocks in place.
There are a couple of rubber bushings and a cupped washer that sandwich the
top of the shock tower to the  shock stud on the top of the shock and a rubber
bushing with a metal bushing inside that on the bottom on the front of the
bus. On the rear of the bus, as I recall, you have the same setup as on the
bottom of the front shocks on both the top and bottom of the rear. Everything
should be snug tight. If the rattling continues to happen look for evidence of
cracks around these anchor points.

To adjust, carry an approptiate screw driver along and while on a straight and
smooth road observe how the high and low beams project. Adjust the low beams
to as high as possible without blinding the oncoming traffic about 100 or so
feet and keep the main part of the left beam just to the right of the center
line and the right beam just to the right of straight ahead. The high beams
take care of themselves.  To learn which direction to turn the screws for up
and down, and right to left, drive up to about 10 ft from a wall and turn the
screws in and out and see what the beams do. This way when you are out on the
road and the oncoming flashes their lights at you, you can quickly pull over
and make a quick adjustment and be back on the road in seconds. This is
similar consumer feedback or testing as a means of quality control in new
manufactured products.
