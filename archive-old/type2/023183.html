When it stalls check to see if the coil is getting juice from the key. I 
would also check to see if the grounded side of the coil is getting a good 
ground when the motor is rolled over with the key - a test light or meter 
will show 12V-0V-12V etc while the engine is being turned - by hand (pull 
that coil cable to dist. first) or by the starter.

Could be a few possibilities - I would be absolutely sure that this is an 
ignition problem and not a failing fuel pump. Also are the connections on 
the coil or in the dist. (points) still tight if you try to pull them off? 
Is the double relay for the fuel pump working correctly?

Might rig up a test light and pressure gauge for the fuel ring (test nipple 
is on the fuel line on the driver's side of the engine) and have somebody 
ride in the back seat with the engine cover off. Either the ignition system 
will cause the test light or timing light or dwell meter or plain test 
meter (whatever you use) to quit or the fuel pressure gauge will go to 
zero. If the computer is having problems then it could quit firing the fuel 
injectors with the same results you described.

Hang in there - these engines and their injection aren't that complicated.

Also plan on replacing the fuel injector seals - mine were baked and 
causing big vacuum leaks (idle problems, low power).
