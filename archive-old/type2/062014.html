        Or, if you have a standard timing light, you can measure where your
full advance should be. Take the 7" pulley, multiply by pi, divide by 360,
then you have the value of 1 degree. Multiply that by however many degrees
you want as your full advance (I run 30-32 depending on the local climate,
less for hotter climates), then measure that on a piece of paper or
something. Now, wrap that paper around the pulley, and mark the spot. I
used a file to scratch in 30 and 32, so I have both permanantly marked.
Easy. Just remember to say a word of thanks to your high school math
teacher-and Bob Hoover, who pointed this method out to me...
