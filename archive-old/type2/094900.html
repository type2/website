Set the 009 timing at 30 degrees BTDC _at_ 3,500 RPM. The 009 does not have =
the ideal advance curve for your engine; it has a bad flat spot when the =
car is taking off. You'll have better performance at all RPMs with a =
rebuilt stock vacuum/centrifugal advance distributor.

Used Bosch dizzies usually develop excessive axial (up & down) play long =
before they develop side-to-side play. This can often be fixed by =
replacing the insulative washer(s) above the distributor shaft dog gear =
on the bottom end of the dizzy shaft. Bosch makes a distributor rebuild =
kit which contains insulative washers.

Place the distributor shaft in a vice, using a nail punch and hammer, =
drive the pin out of the side of the dog gear/dizzy shaft, remove the =
dog gear, replace the washer(s) and dog gear, and drive the pin back =
through the hole in dog gear and shaft. You can replace the dog gear two =
ways: don't get it 180 degrees off or it'll back fire like a .38 =
Special.

While you have the dizzy out is a good time to scrub it in solvent and =
lube it thoroughly with heavy oil, including the swinging centrifugal =
advance weights in the bottom half of the dizzy. On the side of the =
dizzy is a little metal cap that you can pry off the get at the swinging =
weights.
