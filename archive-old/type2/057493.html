T. Howze wrote:

> Ok... Provided my flaps are in working order and my timing is in place,
> how do I know if I've got my fuel mixture just right?  And what is an
> acceptible margin of error?

    A nice, rich brown color on the insulator of your spark plugs, for
one. Or a exhaust meter reading, but the spark plug reading is pretty
good especially if you take it after a 15 minute highspeed freeway
cruise, then just shut off the engine and roll to a stop, no idling,
etc.


> I've set my mixture screw per Bentley, i.e. turned all the way in, then
> out 2.5-3 turns, and then out more based on idle speed.  Is there anything
> else?  Or any way to know (backfiring, etc) that the mixture is correct?
>
    That's just the idle mixture. The main jets and airjets control the
real mixture.
