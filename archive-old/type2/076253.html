Subject: Re: [T2] Carburetor Icing (was: FI vs. Progressive carb)

Carburetor Icing has been addressed by GM for years by preheating the
intake manifolds, I recall heated intakes on Mercedes 190SL intake
manifold and my MG Midget and although I cannot be specific I believe
that I've seen aluminum spacer blocks for a variety of SU, AMAL and
Weber. These spacer blocks can be heated by water flow, exhaust gasses or
just a copper tube wrapped around something hot that can cause convection
air flow. You'd be surprised how effective a set of double gaskets can be
in changing the temperature of your throttle body. In days of old 40
Fords we simply taped the heater hose to the fuel line and it preheated
the fuel enough to solve the problem. Your fix may be that simple.

Stan Wilder
