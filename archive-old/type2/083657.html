I got the years wrong on the SVDA.  It is 76-79.

Let me see if I can compare these and make it
make sense to you.  (It is really easy to
understand once you have the timing chart, a
strobe, and a vacuum guage.)  

Your stock dizzy was set at 5 ATDC.  When you hit
the throttle, you instantly lose the retard
(11-15 degrees).  This puts you at 6-10 BTDC. 
Look familiar?  If you capped the retard on a
dual vac dual advance dizzy, 6-10 BTDC should be
your timing at idle.  This should look alot like
a single vac dual advance dizzy, right?  See that
the 76 is timed at around 8 BTDC.  

As you rev your engine and hold vacuum on it,
either of the dual action dizzies (a 72 DVDA or a
76 SVDA) will give you 8-12 vacuum advance and
around 25 centrifugal (the 72 DVDA a little more
than the 76 SVDA), for a total of around 43
degrees advance (8 BTDC + 10 vac advance + 25
cent advance).  

I hope this makes sense.
