>Sure, it's easy.  You may first have to change the position of the
>distributor in order to remove the hold-down nut.  You do this by
>locating the long 10mm nut toward the FRONT (front is front!) of the
>distributor.  It sticks out to the right side of the distributor.  Put
>your 10mm open end wrench on this and loosen it.  Now you can turn the
>distributor body by hand to make room to get a wrench on the hold-down
>nut.  Once you remove the hold-down nut, then the distributor simply
>lifts off straight up.
>Sean Bartnik
>Fairfax, Virginia


An alternative method, not as neat as Sean's, but it saves having to
readjust your timing, is to gently tap a corner of the nut with a small
hammer via a screwdriver.  After I got mine off the first time, I filed some
neat little notches into the nut so I can tap it on and off quite easily.
BTW, if you lose the nut, don't ever start the engine with the dist just
sitting "firmly" in there.  Its a sure way to learn more about timing, dist
shaft washers etc.  And that's another story.  I learned the hard way ;-)
