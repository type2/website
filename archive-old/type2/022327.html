Assuming you don't want to remove the dist. due to correct timing already 
done - do this...

Remove the nut that attaches the dist. clamp to the case. This way you can 
remove the dist. and then reinstall it without affecting the timing.

While the dist. is out, you can remove the rear fuel pump mounting nut with 
a short 13mm wrench. I use a combo wrench - open at one end and closed at 
the other end because it is the wrench I've got that is a reasonable length.

There should be an 'O' ring on the dist. shaft below the dist. clamp. If 
you replace it, cut the old 'O' ring and walk the new one on with a 
screwdriver (you can stretch it quite a bit without breaking the new 'O' ring.

Use WD-40 as much as you like to walk the ring up the bottom of the dist. 
shaft. While it is out, why not use some carb cleaner to clean the dist. up 
well (outside) if it is an old one and a mess. I like working on clean 
engines. My engine is sterile. (put your dirty minds in park... <grin>)

Reinstall the dist. and tighten the 13mm nut with a socket on a long enough 
extension and ratchet handle.

Done.

You may wonder why you engine is leaking in these places in the first 
place, and I'll bet that you have excessive blow by - your piston rings are 
wearing out.

Question - is the generator pedestal the original style and is the breather 
clogged up or venting correctly? It should bleed off any pressure built up 
in the crankcase.

Recommendation - buy Muir's 'Idiot Book' and Bentley's factory shop manual. 
They have been the most valuable books on my shelf. Muir isn't always right 
in every detail but her is just about the best writer of descriptions for 
home mechanic there is (to me...) and Bentley is good to verify his 
procedures and shows very good pictures. To me they are to be used together.

With these books I have learned to do everything on my bus. It has never
