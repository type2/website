>The question I have is this: 
>Is there some type of material like Plasti-Gauge that can be used on the
>top of the piston or outer raised portion to set up cylinder head spacing
>and achieve balanced (or very close) compression?

modeling clay or play dough

Don't laugh folks, it's a good cheap trick, not all that accurate but it
will keep you safe.  Accuracy will depend on your measurment
technique, and I doubt you'll do better than 0.015-0.020 or so which is
about 3-4 cc a pretty hefty amount.

However a set of cheapo chinese depth mics, and a strap of  1" x 1/4"
bar stock with 2 holes drilled for caddy corner head studs, then a couple
of sockets and washers as spacers will set you back less than $30 and
provide results (by my rather extensive trials) nearly as good as my 1"
thick deck plate provides with the dial gage.  The depth mic technique
setting it on the barrel beside the strap holding down the barrel will be
withing 0.005" or so of what it is actually torqued home under a head.

As to hardness, that is interesting, a few years ago I did take a few heads
to the hardness tester and the AMCs at the time were as their rep quite
a bit softer than the VW heads, all square port heads I was testing.
