Last fall I decided I would take care of all of the little rusty dings on

with Rustoleum.  Now a few months later I am seeing rust pop out in many of
the spots I "fixed."

So I'm going to take him into a real body shop and have them do it the
right way (I was going to have them paint anyways, so I'm not too bothered
by contracting out a little extra work.)

like to take care of the interior myself.  After reading the archives, I
find that rust converters are not a good long term solution, and that I
should have used a epoxy based primer that would etch and bond with the
metal.  But, after searching the archives and the web, the only epoxy based
primers I can find require a special spray gun.

it that doesn't require special equipment to apply?  In the end I'm not
looking for a beautiful finish; just something that won't rust and won't
chip when I walk on it or throw my sailing gear in.
