*Extreme Caution - a boring physics lecture follows*

Different materials require different amounts of heat per unit mass to
increase their temperature.  ie adding the same amount of energy ("heat")
to two different substances will cause them to heat at different rates,
due to their specific heat.  The equation: Q = mc*(delta T), where Q is
the heat input, m is the mass, delta T is the time interval and c is the
specific heat, a higher specific heat means that more energy (Q) is
required for a similar gain in temperature.  

Basically what I'm getting at is the if this Kendall has a higher specific
heat, it will remain cooler while keeping the motor cool as well.  It can
absorb heat (energy) from the cylinders and heads without raising its own
temperature as much as conventional oils.
