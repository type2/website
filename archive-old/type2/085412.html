Well done Westfakia's should sell for very close to a "real" Westfalia of
similar quality. There is a lot of variation in the quality of both, but if
all things are equal the price should be close to equal as well.  If you're
uptight about "Original Factory Configuration" then this isn't

Putting the hole in the roof is easier than you might think, particularly
with a 68-73 westy. I built "Bob the Tomato" with a 79 body, 73 westy engine
and interior and a 74 pop-top.  I wanted the double bed upstairs to sleep my
family of 5 inside the van, but I like the 68-73 layout for general utility
around town. 

I cut the roof from the 74 with about 6" of metal framing the hole all the
way around and then fit it over a new hole cut into the 79 roof with a 2"
overlap. The roof sections were welded together and the seam isn't very
noticeable since its under the fibreglass when the roof is down and no-one
looks above the driprail when the roof is up anyway. The whole job took
about 100 man-hours... doing a 68-73 roof would take half that time since
the roof structure is simpler. If you have welding or autobody experience
you could cut that figure in half again.

There are lots of pics of the process online at:
http://www.pottsfamily_dot_ca/westfakia

Let me know how it goes... I'd love to get more before and after pics for my
website.
