When I camp for a weekend, I carry about 4 gallons of distilled water and 
keep it in the plastic gallon jugs in which it is sold at our supermarkets. 
I mainly use it for drinking and cooking. Most campsites supply local water 
and I use that for washing.

I use the empty jugs to carry the local water. The downside risk is that a 
jug will develop a leak in the bottom, as it jiggles around on the floor 
during travel. The positive point of using disposable plastic jugs is that 
they are clean inside as long as you need them, and they are conveniently 
available almost everywhere. And your water supply is totally flexible.

If I were putting in a permanent tank, it would be about 3 gallons.

But I wouldn't put in a permanent tank.  My Westy was equipped with one 
from the factory, and I think my disposables are a better idea. I never 
have to clean my tank or worry about contamination.        Doyt Echelberger
