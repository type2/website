At 6:38 PM 9/30/96, dakhlia_at_wuecona.wustl_dot_edu wrote:
>I, too, tried the propane (actually Sears Mapp gas (sp?)) trick but didn't
>notice any noticeable change in rpm (over and beyond the usual slight
>hunting at idle), even after I pointed the gas directly into the air
>cleaner intake. Is the acceleration supposed to be significant?

Try carb cleaner, and spray it at the base of the manifolds.  I wouldn't
aim the gas into the air cleaner if there is any kind of mass airflow meter
present (maybe yours doesn't have one - I don't know squat about FI).  May
damage it.  Could damage a throttle position sensor too, if your bus has
that.

The acceleration should be significant.  Negative acceleration, in my case.
When I tried this with vacuum leaks, it darned near killed the motor.
