>You may already know this or have been told this, but in a worn engine, 
>there are two sources of endplay.  The first is movement of the crankshaft 
>w/in the bearings.  This is present in a new engine and  is adjusted w/ the 
>three shims.  The second is movement of the bearings w/in the case--i.e. 
>worn thrust surfaces on the case.  This is the product of wear and cannot 
>be corrected w/ shims.  On a worn engine, you can't tell how much of the 
>endplay is due to which source--thus it is impossible to adjust endplay on 
>a worn motor.  Just use the original shims till it self destructs or, 
>better yet,  repair the motor.  Good luck,
