I agree.  While I have done my share of bailing out folks, I tend to
feel that if you are going to travel about the country you should
expect to pay for your own repairs.  The list should be concerned with
providing access to services, not paying for the services.  In certain
cases, we may pass the hat for a deserving person, but this should not
be expected in every case.  

If a listee wishes to assist me, I will be most grateful and will
thank them profusely, but I should not think of their assistance as
being my right or of providing assistance as their obligation.  

On the other side of the coin, I do my best, but sometimes I just have
to say "Sorry, but I cannot provide further help to you."  I'll help
someone chase down an ignition coil, but a complete rebuild is beyond
my ability to assist.

If we keep money out of the program, I see no reason why this program
wouldn't work worldwide, as long as there are enough listees to cover
a given region.
