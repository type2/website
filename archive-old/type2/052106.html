Darrin:
Yes; and yes (it's not very well mounted in there).
Cheesey might be a good word. Inches downwind from 600 degree heat exchangers
and handling the lifeblood of your engine!
The dipstick bellows on my 78 once gave out when I didn't have a spare and I
replaced it with a piece of fuel hose, probably 3/8" or 5/16" US size, and a
couple of small worm gear drive clamps. Much more substantial and never gave any
trouble again.
Good to know that VW eventually corrected their own mistake with a redesign on
the 80 Vanagon, fully 11 years after the type 4 came out.
This is why I don't have a whole lot of reverence for the caliber of German
engineering. (Maybe it's management's call- makes little difference when your
oil is on the ground and you're walking!)
