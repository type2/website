Ooh! Scary Halloween story!

Makes you wonder, don't it? Our '71 Westy was advertised the same way -
mint, rebuild, etc. Got there and it was rusty in the rockers and
corners, windshield cracked, thick coat of primer, engine with
compression of about 50 in #3, rust holes in the floor, front axle
problems...

We ended up buying it because the seller was able and willing to
recognize reality and come *way* down on the price - but why do people
even do this?! Sure, I have had to sell cars of mine before, and you try
and make it sound as good as you can, but you can't change reality!
