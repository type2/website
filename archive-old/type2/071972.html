>Hi all,
>    I'm getting ready to put together a 79, 2liter, engine.  While
>inspecting the connecting rods I didn't see the raised marks on the
>shanks,that should be on the top side.  Tom Wilson, in a photo caption on
>page 44 of his book, says that type 4 rods do not have these bumps.  I'm
>confused because the Bentley manual doesn't say anything about the type 4
>rods being exempt from having these marks.
>    I've searched the archives but can't find the info I need, so I'm asking
>all of you.  Must there be a raised mark on my connecting rods to indicate
>the top, or can they go in either way ?

There will be no mark on 1700, 1800 or 2-liter rods, and If you look on
page 118 of Wilson, it mentions that you should "orient the rods so the
bearing tangs are at the bottom of the journals when the crank is
horizontal".  This implies (and I'm pretty sure it's true) that the type IV
rods can go in either way.  Back on pg. 44 Wilson mentions "offset small
ends" on type 1 rods but not type IV rods, which seems to back this up.
