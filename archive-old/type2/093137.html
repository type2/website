Subject: Re: [T2] CV problem

I had a '87 Accord hatchback with 180K miles and could not find the source 
for a vibration in the car. I replaced the tires when the old ones wore out 
and checked the bearings which were tight and then checked the wheels for 
an out of round condition.

Had an old tire guy look at it and he put it up on the rack and showed me 
that the CV joints - though not making noise were worn (axial play).

I replaced them myself a month or two later with local FLAPS axles and the 
vibration went away. The vibration was not noticeable at any speed if the 
car was coasting but did exhibit the problem when there was power going 
through the drivetrain. He old tire guy explained that this would cause the 
CV to twist and lightly "lock" off center.

So.. if your half shaft propelled vehicle is demonstrating a weird 
vibration, check your CV joints.
