The stamped number at the base gives it away.  It is a Holley 5200.  Actually
a Holley manufactured Weber 32/36 DGAV.  The R7243 number can be interpreted
by any "real" parts house.  Called the "List Number."  With a Holley "Master
Catalog" (mine's dated mid seventies...) one gets the jetting, the original
application, the gasket p/n's etc.

You might have to try the local speed shop to find the Master Catalog.  Or
call Holley.  They are probably on the web.
