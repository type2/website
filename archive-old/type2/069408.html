Agreed, but isn't proper timing just as important. I think I have damaged
more engines by not timing them properly than I have by not adjusting
valves. (I know how to do that right, but I'm not sure I've yet mastered
the art of timing.) In some ways I'm the "idiot" Muir was writing to. I've
only recently come to understand that over-advancing the timing causes
your engine to run HOT, which can cause your oil to break down, your heads
to warp, and your valves to fry. I dropped a valve seat on #1 most
recently (should 260 degrees cause you to drop a valve seat?). 

My engine's now on the garage floor in pieces. Unfortunately, I also
discovered that this engine suffers from the same thrust facing damage my
last engine died from, so now I'm looking at rebuilding an engine I just
picked up for $100...which has good DP heads and seems to have good
end-play. One of these days I'll have to visit a machine shop and get one
of these cases machined.

Next time I'm going to pay way more attention to my timing...and
temps...and I'll carry a spare head with me too.
