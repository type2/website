Jamie: IMHO, it is a way to make your bus a much better camper. It is really
nice to be able to stand up, it adds a lot to the camping experience.
Especially after crawling out of bed, etc. You'll be adding a lot of storage
space, I have a ton of junk stowed in the roof. Another bed for your family.

Will it look better? Aieee, it took me a while to be able to stand the sight
of my bus with that upside down boat on top of it. I even took the top off
once for about a week, and contemplated filling the hole in with a tonneau
cover. However, my bus looked so crappy with the roof off of it that I
finally realized I had something rather special and unique(especially East
of the Rockies), and taking the roof off would have diminished it. Your bus
isn't an original Safare though, and wouldn't impress any Safare purists,
assuming that such exist.

Wherever I go, I see tons of pop top Westies. It's kind of nice to have
something different, it's grown on me. Plus which, you have the quality
Westphalia interior goodies, not the clapped out sun and water damaged old
high roof interior junk.

I met a man in Des Moines Iowa who had a Campmobile hardtop that he'd done
the same thing to. He was quite happy with his decision. He said he couldn't
stand crouching in the hardtop.

As I told you before, there's been times where I've wished I had a hardtop
camper. Less wind resistance, sealed to wind and rain, actually fit into
garages, etc. Tough choice all around, not sure what I'd do either.

You do have the option of putting the high top on your bus as a trial fit,
and having a good look at it before cutting or drilling anything.
