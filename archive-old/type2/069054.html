In my experience, the Type II transaxle/reduction box assemblies are nearly
indestructable.
I drove my '58 camper home from the Sierra's with a nearly dry transaxle
(clunking terribly intermittently). Filled it with 90wt when I got home and
forgot about it. Never had any trouble.
Suggest you try this first. Replace the axle seal and brake shoes (both
sides). Examine the outboard bearing while you are in there. If it looks
ok, refill the reduction box, adjust the brakes and drive it. If the
thumping persists, look to the bearings in the reduction box first. They
are the first to be affected when the boxes lose oil. As I think about this
more, since you heard the noise before there was any leakage, a failing
bearing may have caused the seal to fail.
Good luck, and keep it running.
