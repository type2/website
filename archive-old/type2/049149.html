> 69, but the chassis # and the door handles say it's a '68.  "Cool," I

> Westfalia plate on the bed is stamped 1969...and it has no pop-top."  Does

If the third number in the chassis number of your bus is an "8", then
the bus is a model 1968. For example: 238 ... ...

The stamp on the Westfalia-interior tells you something about the date
the interior was made. I suspect it was swapped or was fitted on an old
microbus or delivery van. Send me the codes on the M-plate in your bus
and I'll tell you what it looked like originally.

Westfalia-campers were indeed made without pop-up as well! I parted out
one like that two years ago, it was a 1969 imported from the USA into
the Netherlands. It had Mcode 517 but not Mcode 518 which stands for
"pop-top".
