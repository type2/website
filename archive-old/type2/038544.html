RS does sell a "high intensity" LED.  I use one as an indicator for my
alarm system.  It in on the dashboard in an existing hole just behind the
windshield.  It is bright enough to be visible in direct sunlight!

The bad news: Despite the clear housing, the LED glows red.  the color is
the nature of the beast, and a white LED is a rare animal.

This LED needed a dropping resistor to run on 12V.  Just divide 12 by the
LED's specified current in amps and you'll get the value of resistance you
need.  Get something close in 1/2 watt and it'll work fine.

If you want way kool red-illuminated instruments, that is!

I've been thinking of making some taillights out of these LEDs.  Some
truck lights and 3rd brakelights use LEDs very successfully.  After
chasing corroded sockets and burnt out bulbs, it would be nice to have
something that doesn't burn out.  I could even arrange the LEDs in a VW
emblem that gets filled in when I hit the brakes!  :)
