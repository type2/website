Hi Adam,
I converted my '69 camper before I knew there used to be a cot up there.  
I started w/ a 4'x8' sheet of 3/4" plywood (you might be able to get away w/
thinner) and cut a rectangle just as wide as the tracks (supposed to be for
sunroof) and maybe 6" longer than the hole.  Beveled the edges and cut and
hinged two doors at the front, maybe 1/4 of total length.  Then covered the
whole thing w/  vinyl w/ some padding underneath.
When not in use the board slides back where a sunroof would, and extended
the hinged doors rest on the front of the opening.  Flip one door up and use
the backwards facing seat behind the driver to climb up.
I usually use a thermarest, and keep in mind the space is shorter than the
newer ones, so if you're over about 5'8" you'll be touching on both ends.  

Enjoy,
