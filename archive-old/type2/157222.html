I used "exterior" grade double-sided foam tape to stick the glass to the
housing and then ran a bead of hot-melt glue around the exterior edge to
fill the gap.  Leave a little drain hole on whatever edge you consider to
be the bottom.  I considered using black RTV sealer, but was worried that
it might attack the silvering on the back side of the mirror.  Others
might have more data.

This was on a set of Hagus replacement mirrors.  The glass and metal are
excellent, no corrosion at all.  The plastic bead that holds the glass
into the mirror deteriorated over the years.  I wish I could just find a
replacement bead, but quite a bit of searching yielded nothing usable.
