Did you check to make sure that your distributor's vacuum cannister was 
actually operational?  Is the vacuum line from the dizzy to carb connected 
to the correct port on the carb?  Is the carburaetor new or recently 
rebuilt?  You may want to change or replace the diaphragms(esp. the 
accelerator-pump's) because they can develop tears and holes.  In my 
experience, exhaust leaks don't become problems until you're actually 
driving awhile.  These manifest as pops and increased cylinder head  
temperatures.  Hesitation upon acceleration could also be caused by improper 
air-fuel mixtures.  Check/replace the carb's o-rings on the idle air bypass 
and mixture screws as well.
