On Thu, 14 Sep 2000, HMF STOERMER wrote:

> If you want reliable starting in Winter weather, and some reserve to handle
> the camper's added electrical demands, buy a good quality Group 24 battery.
> You can even install a Group 27 battery if you want.
> 
> Installing a non-standard battery sometimes requires a little ingenuity to
> hold it in place, as often the stock
> hold-down clamp won't work.  A very common arrangement is to install a Grp.
> 24 in a plastic marine/RV battery box for corrosion protection, then
> stabilize it with blocks of wood cut to fit snugly around the base of the
> box.  You can also use the hold-down brackets and strap that often comes
> with the battery boxes, but that requires drilling some holes to bolt down
> the brackets.

Another approach is to make a base plate that is held down by the stock
hold-down bolt.  Attach new hold-downs to this base plate to secure your
battery.  The plastic battery box is a very good idea!
