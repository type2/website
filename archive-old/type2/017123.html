I too installed an oil temp gage and had great difficulty getting the sensor
to seal properly to the sump plate.  I finally ended up fabricating a new
sump plate out of alum and mounted the sensor to this plate - and no more
leaks!  I have found the oil temp to run a max. of 180F in the "winter"
months here in the SF bay area (ambient temps 40F coldest).  It is possible
that the oil temp is really ~130F if the outside temp is in the 30"s and you
have only driven for ~15 min (see what happens in 30 min).  I also installed
a cylinder head temp gage (from a wrecked VW GTI) and get readings of
anything from 80C to 120C depending upon the engine loading (i.e. up or down
hill) while the oil temp gage stays constant _at_ ~180F.  This shows that the
oil cooler is doing it's job.  Good luck with the oil leak (the sump plate
may be warped from the welding process- if so try and get a replacement from
the junk yard).
