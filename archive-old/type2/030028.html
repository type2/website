> And while I am asking questions, exactly what part does the Bowden 
> Tube play in the clutch system?

It provides the "push" to counter the cable's "pull".  That way, the trans
can flex around without affecting the disengagement of the clutch.  If you
just tried to use a straight pull without the bowden tube, the clutch
would start to engage, which would push the rear of the tranny downward,
which would slightly disegage the clutch, which would cause the rear to
rise engaging the clutch and so on.  In short, it would chatter.
