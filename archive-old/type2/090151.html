On Sun, 9 Jun 2002, Brian RICHARDSON wrote:

> One of the things I need to know is if there is a way to repair rusted
> fuel outlets and returns on the gas tank.  They are rusted down to
> little stubs coming out of the tank.  I felt around to see if there is
> a section that is removable but I couldnt tell.  I have a feeling it
> is not replaceable.

If your fuel outlet and return stubs have rusted off, they are not easily
replaceable.  I am sorry to say I have seen many FI tanks with this
problem and I have a bad feeling about it getting worse as time goes on.

The stubs are welded to the tank, no nut on the bottom like on the '68-71
tanks. It is possible, but difficult, to find somone who will weld on a
gas tank.  Usually it is cheaper to buy a junkyard replacement.

> ordered the Bentley for the bus but i was curious in the meantime.
> Also I was wondering if anyone knew of a good place to get a full set
> of replacement fuel lines for this engine (metal and rubber lines).

You can buy and use metal brake line as a replacement for the netal in the
straight sections.  The fule ring is a junkyard item, tho you might check
wiut VW.  I bought an NOS Fuel ring there not too long ago.  the hose is
available from any VW supplier.

> I need to replace some exhaust parts too.  I have seen these
> aftermarket mufflers that supposedly "replace six pieces with one"
> they eliminate the crossover tube and elbows and such.  Has anyone
> used one of these mufflers?

Yes.  The quality is so widely variant that it is tough to say anything.
My general rule is that the "Monza" style rusts faster than the "stock
look" but I have seen solid old Monza style and have seen the stock look
rust out very quickly. One important point on the 76-78 exhausts is that
all four corners of the heater boxes are supposed to have support bars
that bolt them to the engine case.  I rarely find them in place and they
are important!
