The cyl head temp sensor is a temperature dependent variable resistor
whose role governs startup to warmup mixture-Based on Bentley, the
resistance across the sensor at 68 degrees is 2500 ohms.  Don't forget
that in the 77, you have another temp semsor in the AFM that should be
checked a la Bentley.  Take your sensor and ohmeter and probe between
the disconnected lead and ground and it should read near this.  I can't
remember, but know that as temp rises or lowers from 68 deg, the
resistance will change proportionally -not sure if it's inverse- you can
get readings of a hot engine from a friend or take your sensor and apply
heat or stick it in the freezer to see what it does.  Yes you could get
a variable resistor at Radio Shack and duplicate the readings if you had
a meter across the leads, or you could solder in a rotary switch that
selects resistors.  Makes more sense to see if the head can be retapped
for an insert like a helicoil.  I suppose you could try JB weld to
reinsert the sensor, but a wiser person on the list could say if you
need a good ground for the base of the sensor that the JB weld epoxy may
compromise.
