Hey Todd.  If your cylinder in the caliper doesn't move, it will need to be
repaired/replaced.  You should be able to push the piston in with a pair of
channel lock pliers.  I'd check the master cylinder as well.  Squeaking is
caused by vibration of the pads on the disc.  Kinda sounds like microphone
feedback at a rock concert.  That is where the goop you find on the back of the
old pads comes in.  It's designed to absorb the vibrations and thus quiet it.
There's a multitude of products that are sold as Disc Brake Quiet.  It's a sort
of glue that doesn't harden.  (except for over long periods)   I just put new
pads on the westy and did a real quick 20 min job, ( I love the simplicity and
design of these vehicles), replacing the pads only and didn/t put the stuff on
and guess what,  they're noisier than ever before.  When the weather warms up,
I'll do the compleat job.  With a properly operating brake system,  these
vehicles stop nicely.
