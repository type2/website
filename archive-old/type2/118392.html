I don't think so.  The timing light will flash when the plug fires; what the
timing scale then shows you is where that piston is as it travels up and
down, usually right near TDC.  If the distributor drive gear is off by much
you might have to twist the distributor way away from its "normal" position
to get the ignition timing right, but what you read on the scale with the
light is still correct.  Now, what really *will* screw you up in a fairly
"non-recoverable" way is if the cam/crank gears are off a tooth.  Any chance
of that?
