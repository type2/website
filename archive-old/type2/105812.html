On 3/30/03 3:22 PM, "Steve Pribyl" <steve_at_unliketea_dot_com> wrote:

> I was ajusting the rear breaks today and found the adjusters still and
> almost frozen.  A quick spirt of screw loosener set them free.  Took em
> out and cleaned them.   However, they were still a bit stiff when I went
> to adjust them again.
> 
> What is the proper grease,if any, to use in this location?

Anti-size lubricant (sometimes called copper grease). Like this:

http://homepage.mac_dot_com/ratwell/.Pictures/Maintenance2/198-3M08945.jpg

Permatex makes a copper and Aluminum one as well that may be easier to find.
Coat it everywhere there's a surface: adjuster threads, screw threads, in
the cups where the adjusters fit in the mount on the backing plate (term
escapes me).
