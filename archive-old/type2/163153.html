I rarely bust a move like this, but your story is so gripping and I
have so "been there" that I have pulled out the Bentley which it seems
you have not yet acquired, despite the struggles.

Page 5 of the brake section (appended):

"...it is very important that the correct clearance exist between the
pushrod and the master cylinder piston...."

1. Remove cover

2. Check to see that the pedal lever is being held against the brake
pedal stop by the brake pedal return spring

3. If the pedal is against the stop, check the clearance between the
connecting rod and the brake servo.  The connecting rod should not
exert pressure on the servo valve housing when the pedal is all the
way up

4. If necessary, remove the cotter pin from the clevis pin, and
disconnect the connecting rod clevis from the pedal lever

5. Adjust the connecting rod by screwing it in or out of the threaded
part of the servo.

6. If necessary, loosen the locknut and make fine adjustments by
turing the connecting rod connection until the clevis pin can easily
be inserted  as you install the connecting rod on the pedal lever

7. Install a new cotter pin on the clevis pin.  Tighten the locknut
for the connection on the servo

Good luck,
