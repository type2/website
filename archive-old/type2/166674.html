Mark,

I'd recommend starting out going straight north. Once you hit the NY
state line I'd go I-86/US17 across the southern tier to the finger
lakes. This terrain is generally flat to easy rolling hills. From
there you could follow 86/17 all the way to Binghamton, the route is
generally bus friendly, generally no grades too steep to handle. From
Binghaton I-88 to Albany, some long steep grades here but good truck
lanes and you won't be the only one using them as this is a popular
trucker route. Alternately, if you end up on the north end of the
finger lakes, US5 through Syracuse and Utica is a very viable bus
route. It follows the Mohawk river and canals and the rail lines much
of the way and is a good, mostly flat route. From Albany east is a bit
trickier as I wouldn't take my bus up I-90 across the Taconics on the
NYS Thruway or through the Berkshires on the Mass Pike, just too much
fast moving traffic on long uphill grades, you might not meet the
45mph minimum speed limit. I'd checkout US20 or US2 as good, slower
moving alternates. US 2 from Troy NY West (the Mohawk trail) to I-91
is (in my opinion) one of the more beautiful drives in the north east.
If you're up to checking out the hills of southern Vermont and a more
northern route, NY7 to VT9 east is also a great drive across the green
mountains to southern New Hampshire. Though from there though you'd
need to drive all the way down and through Boston to the Cape.
