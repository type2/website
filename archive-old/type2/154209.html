I'll second Bob's comments.  You'll need to yank the trans (not an easy job
I'm sorry to say) and replace the seal after flushing her out.  I imagine
you got water in the valve body and who knows what happened when it finally
vaporized.  Vapor lock?  Also, I imagine your clutches/bands should be
inspected as water is not a good lubricator.

Finally, there is an internal seal between the gear box and the final drive.
It's easy to blow that seal too if internal pressures go too high.

While it's out you might as well rebuild it if you are not funding
constrained.

Ask me how I know all this.  I drive a 1978 auto, too.  (It's normally a
real workhorse but you can't abuse it as you would a manual tranny that can
be punished until it explodes!)
