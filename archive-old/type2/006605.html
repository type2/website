I did this job last summer on my '69.  Here's what I did:
Take out windshield, exposing rust.  It was all along where the bottom of
the seal sits, punctuated by a few ~3/16inch  holes completely through the
metal.
I scraped, then sandblasted the rusty areas, then swept and vacuumed the
sand from a 150 foot radius around my bus.  When all was nice and clean I
applied bondo from my local auto body supply place to wherever there were
holes, then sanded until it looked smooth. After letting the bondo cure
overnight I primered, then painted the seal area to around an inch past
where the where the edge of the seal is.  It helped that the PO saved a bit
of paint from the last paint job.  It doesn't match exactly, but you really
only notice the 'racoon' effect on the paint job when you are standing right
next to it.  I did the job outside, over a few days in the summer.  I also
hade someone else put the windshield in, since I cracked mine while taking
it out, and couldn't afford to buy too many of them.  It sealed up tight and
the rust hasn't come back yet.
