 Bear in mind that highway fuel usage in a bus depends a LOT on your 
cruising speed. When I had a '71 I used to get 20mpg out of it on
open roads because I used to drive at 50-55mph to preserve the engine.
 Now that I have a '77, with a nice chunky 2.0l type-4 I drive at
70-75mph (or more) when conditions allow: This saves me a lot of time
but costs a lot of fuel. I'm sure I could get 20mpg out of it if I
wanted to, but it's not worth the time.

 That said, the efficiency of the aircooled engines isn't going to 
vary much. At a steady 55mph you're going to be using pretty much the
same amount of power in a '77 as in a '71 (a little more uphill or
accelerating because of the extra weight). Either should get about
18mpg or more for this sort of driving, and if it doesn't then it
would be worth finding out why not.
