I can't explain that. It's supposed to go up. From the Valvoline website:

> SAE 30 is SAE 30 no matter what the "W" prefix number is: 0W, 5W or 10W. This
> viscosity in centistokes (cSt) _at_ 100 degrees C is with the minimum of 9.3 cSt
> and a maximum of 12.5 cSt.
> 
> SAE 50 is SAE 50 no matter what the "W" prefix number is: 5W, 10W, 15W or 25W.
> The viscosity _at_ 100 degrees C is within the minimum of 16.3 cSt and a maximum
> of 21.9 cSt.

Most of the 20w50's out there rate ~18 cSt _at_ 100C. Hence the 20w50 is much
thicker than the SAE30 at the same temperature the oil pressure should be
much higher.
