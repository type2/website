In one of the "make your vehicle last forever" books (I forget which one) 
there is an interesting rule of thumb: determine what payment you could 
comfortably make each month on a car, and spend that amount each month to 
maintain your "forever" vehicle. My guess is that the "comfortable" 
payment may be something less than what you are paying for your Honda.

This rule of thumb has a logic similar to the "a bus is worth whatever
someone would pay for it" (i.e.: market) logic. If you spend a comfortable
amount each month to maintain your bus, and it lets you down on your
commute repeatedly, you cannot afford to maintain it. 

But I will bet that at an average monthly expense to maintain the bus of 
$150-$200 a month ($1800-2400 a year) your bus will be reliable, fun, and 
look darn nice. And maybe you will run out of things to fix!

But what do others think? Is $2400 a year enough to maintain a bus that 
is driven every day?
