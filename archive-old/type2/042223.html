Sue:

You're pretty much between a rock and a hard spot here unless  you can work
on the car yourself; you've left yourself at the mercy of others, which is
OK if they're honest, not OK if they're not.

In order for an engine to run it needs spark (at the right time), fuel (in
the correct amount) and compression.  What part of the equation is lacking
here?

You say it "turned over fine" but the shop says it "won't turn all the way"?
There's a conflict here.  Either the starter motor will spin the engine just
fine, and it won't start, or the starter motor won't turn the engine over.
Which is it?  If the starter motor won't turn the engine over, why not?  A
competent shop should be able to distinguish the difference between a
mechanical / electrical problem in the starting system and a purely
mechanical problem in the engine that prevents the engine from rotating
without tearing the motor down.  Ask them to clarify.

If the engine turns over OK then ask the shop what part of the above
equation (spark/fuel/compression) seems to be the problem.  Again, a good
shop should be able to roughly distinguish a problem in one or the other
area without tearing the engine apart.

You may need another shop, or you may need to understand (i.e., get a CLEAR
explanation from your shop) your problem better.

Let us know one way or the other.
