Great subject for research in the archives, as many methods have been 
posted here in the past, most several times.  The most popular seem to be 
renting a pilot bearing puller, the grease-packing method, and breaking 
apart/hacksawing.  Since the bearing is already broken apart, you might 
consider the third method which I almost always use.  Buy a new sharp 
fine-tooth hacksaw blade, clamp it in a vice grips and carefully cut 
through the pilot bearing from inside to out.  When you are nearly through 
it will weaken enough to be pryed out with a screwdriver.  I've done this 
several times and to me it is the least frustrating method, although I am 
going to try the grease method next time since it just sounds too cool.
