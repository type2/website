Hopefully you have your ignition wire (terminal 15) hooked up to your coil
and not the starter. Terminal 30 (fat and Red) is always hot at the starter,
while solenoid terminal 50 is switched hot (Red/White) from the
ignition/starter switch. (In other words I'm just teasin' you about your
having ignition going to a place it doesn't go).  Have any of the starters
you've put in been genuine new Bosch rebuilts?  Could 5 *used* starters be
the problem? What shape is the starter bushing in?  If you jumper terminal
30 via starter or battery to terminal 50 (solenoid) and the starter doesn't
work, she most likely be a baaaaaad starter.  Quit messin' with crappy used
starters and get a genuine Bosch rebuilt...which in many cases is brand new
stuff.  I have seen a bad fuel pump dump so much fuel into an engine it
wouldn't crank, but not on a 78 Westy ;-)
