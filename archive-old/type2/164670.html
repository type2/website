Ned,

Kelly at Metwiz_dot_com recommends using KlassicFab replacement panels. There is
evidence they are of higher quality and design. This will help with the body
work. Buses are not that difficult to repair the metal, but being unibody,
expect more rust than you see.

Paint chip is a good investment and there are no downsides unless the
prep-work under the chip guard is poor. Truck bed lining is really fantastic
stuff. The only downside is it will add weight if sprayed on too heavily.

My advice is be present as much as possible. Additionally be sure that no
more than a 1/16" of Bondo or plastic filler is applied in any area (when
reasonable). Make sure the windows/trim/bumpers/lights/doorseals/pop-top are
removed before paint is applied to get the highest quality of work. Also be
sure all seams between panels that are disturbed are re-seam-sealed.

5K is very reasonable for the amount of work you are describing, my '68
Double Cab was three times as much to fully restore and I did the majority
of the work.

Good luck!
