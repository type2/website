> I performed a full tune up on my 77 van before going on an 800 mile road
> trip. This included adjusting the valves which I have done on other
> vehicles many times myself but this time had a friend helping me and I
> used a book from the library which I had not used before to guide us
> through the procedure. I drove 400 miles and then camped out. When I
> restarted my van two days later I noticed a loud "tapping" sound just
> above idle speeds that went away at about 2000rpm. I thought it sounded
> like loose valves but it was only in the low RPM's so I drove back home.

Check to see if someone sometime in the past switched the engine to
hydraulic valves...they are adjusted different but can get to taping
when they aren,t right or haven't built up presure...

  I would do this first since it is fairly easy before going anyfurther.

easiest way to be sure is to remove a lifter, so what ever cyl is easiest
to get to, remove the valve cover, remove the rocker arms, remove the
pushrod, then remove one of the pushrod tubes, then take a magnet on a
stick and pull the lifter out of the engine...a hyd lifter is in several
pieces, a solid lifter is well solid...this is the only way to be
absolutely sure you have solid lifters.
