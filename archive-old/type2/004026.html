> Take a pair of channel lock pliers and brace them against the engine
> block and pull your tubes out.
> After  you get them out they may be a little bit crushed. Take a 3/8"
> extension with a proper sized socket on the end and drive it through the
> tube to get the compression out. Examine the ends of the tube and make
> sure that they are in good  shape.
> When you put the new O rings on they will hang going into the head. Use a
> screwdriver to push the top portion of the O ring down and it will slip
> right in. Use oil on the O rings to make them go in easier. I use a 15mm
> socket on an extension to give me control over the angle and guide the
> tube in.
>
> Clean all residue of silicone, dirt, grease out of the O ring seating
> areas on both the head and the block.
> O rings don't need any other sealer if they are on a clean mating
> surface. They could leak a little for the first fifteen minutes that you
> run your engine.
