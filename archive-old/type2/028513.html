Flack alert! ;-)

In the many VW cables I've replaced I noticed this:  The German cables last
longest. They last even longer when sufficiently greased with moly.  Longer
still if adjusted properly. Many if not most failures result from incorrect
handling, maintenance and *adjustment* and that goes for gas pedals as well.
My 71 Bus original gas pedal and cable are still working perfectly.  Not
only do gas pedals and cables break under excessive stress (no slack at the
throttle valve under full throttle or missing bowdens etc.) the throttle
shaft will eventually eat into its housing.  Too much return spring pressure
aggravates this as well.  Grease and adjust properly and even a Brazilian
cable will last a good long time.  Chincy design work?  For shame!!  ;-)
