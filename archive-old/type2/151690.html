Joe,

You didn't mention how you measured with the multimeter temp sensor...or 
where?

Just for grins, beg, buy or borrow an infrared temperature taker and make 
the engine it hot.  Then measure the surface of the oil filter at the 
bottom(where it is easy to get to and see.

Also the temp on the bottom of the case...say at the oil change plate.

Tell us what you get.

It sounds like you have done everything....but!

You didn't mention verifying that the thermostat works and that the 
flaps inside the air dist "case" work correctly....they are counter 
intutitive(at least to me) and I spent a lot of time when I rebuilt mine 
making sure that they operated free and correctly and that all of the 
meeting surfaces were even and tight.

Are your heat dump pipes and flappers in place and working correctly?
see these here  http://www.shockwaverider_dot_com/newbus/picture-005.html

Can you actually measure the temperature at the oil cooler(probe next to 
it) that shows the same heat at what you are reading on your gauge?

My oil temp ran 226 at the oil filter after 23 miles in 90 degree weather
at 75-80mph.  Next day it was 216 on the same run.

The fact that your heads are "too" cool and your oil "too" hot is very odd 
and I understand your bewilderment.
