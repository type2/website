I had a friend in eastern washington not so long ago who couldn't get 
warm with his catalytic wall mount heater. It is radiant heat so it 
heated what was around him, but not HIM. His windows still had ice, and 
he was cold. It was about 0 to 10 degrees outside.
On the other hand I have a radient heater, and a friend has a gas bus 
heater. When his heater is on, a warm 70 degrees all around the bus is 
norm, and when mine is on the bus get so hot, I open windows even down 
to way below freezing. Think twice about discounting the gas heater, or 
radient heaters.
