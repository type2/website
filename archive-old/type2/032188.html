I'm torn between two submissions, so I'll mention both.

- A small, flat pry bar with a right angle bend in one end.  There's a
special name for one of these, but I forget what it is.  One about 8" long
is great for prying down those spring bails on the valve covers, and for
getting them back on, too.  Forget using the ends of a couple wrenches
like Muir says, this takes about a tenth of the time.  You can also use it
for general-purpose prying, saving your screwdrivers from getting bent.

- Sports-type halogen head lamp.  No, not for your bus...the kind that
straps on *your* head.  Sure beats holding a flashlight in your teeth.
The one I have has a rubber bump guard around the lens and an adjustable
beam angle.  Awfully handy when you can't use a trouble light.
