>itself...except via tales of modification, etc...
>  I'm wondering if there's a common visual inspection that might offer a

Rachel,

There are a few simple tests you can do with with your hands and eyes as
the only tools. If you have a vacuum distributor you can take the small
rubber hose off in the carb end and put it in your mouth, when you suck
into the hose there should be some movement in the plate where the points
are mounted. If air is leaking trough the diapraghm it is time to put a new
vacuum regulator in. The points plate can sometimes just be a bit dirty,
some penetrating oil can be sprayed to help.

To test the mechanical advance mechanism, try to turn the rotor. You should
feel some spring resistace, and be able to turn the rotor a little. The
rotor should return to the same position by spring power. If not tru to
spray some oil in there too.

The bearings at the distributor shaft can be tested too. When you have set
the points to 0,45mm (or whatever your religion tells you). Try to push the
shaft towards the points an try to measure the point gap at the same time.
If there is a big difference in the points gap now, it's time to get a new
distributor. Don't know if the bearings are changeable. 

Have fun!
