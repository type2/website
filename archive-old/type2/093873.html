>When you actually drive  the bus, the engine begins backfiring around 
>3000-3500 rpm. At this point you cannot do anything other than take 
>your foot  off the gas to correct the problem. Do you have any ideas 
>as to what might cause this?

First thing I'd check is intake leaks.  Intake leaks cause backfiring under
steady cruising or light acceleration.  With the engine idling, spray a
little carb cleaner (use it sparingly) around the runner-to-head gaskets and
around the runner-to-center rubber boots 1 at a time.  Any variation in your
idle speed indicates an intake leak.

Other possibility that comes to mind is exhaust leaks.  Exhaust leaks cause
backfiring on deceleration.
