> Sunroof: My experience is that if a sunroof leaks it's because the
> drain
> tubes are plugged. There are 4 drain tubes -one at each corner of the
> opening- and they tend to fill up with mud and organic material and
> get
> plugged. I've never unplugged the ones on my 82, but have on a
> beetle.
> I'd use a piece of solid insulated #12 electrical wire 6 or 7 feet
> long
> and just gently ream out the holes. I do worry about pushing off the
> drain tubes from their attaching points in the body metal and the way
> to
> avoid this would be to snake the cleaning tool up from the underside
> thru the hoses. 
> Does anyone on the list have more type 2 experience on this?
> It helped on my 67 beetle which is similar.
> Al Brase
> 69 double cab

I have owned 3 sunroof busses and the late 73-up have a better system
then the pre-73. I have looked for the seals and have had little luck,
I did find one for $400, but just laughed at the guy. I then got a
pop-top from a Westy and solved the problem. You must clean the drains
or it will leak. The 68-71(or 72) have a long tube that extends into
the engine comp. and it is next to imposible to clean, the latter bus
had a short tube that can be cleaned from the engine intake vent holes.
