On Tue, 19 Mar 2002, Mike Visket wrote:

> FWIW -
>
> HP is is a (loosely) calculated figure based on Torque _at_ RPM, the formula
> being:
>
> HP = Torque * RPM / 5252
>
> A dyno will measure torque and RPM, the rest is math. You will notice that
> on any Torque/HP chart, the two will meet at... 5252 RPM (HP = Torque * 5252
> / 5252)!
>
> There are some ambient factors such as temperature and humidity, etc.. that
> will factor in, but not to a great degree.

These are corrections that attempt to make all tests comparable dispite
the fact that conditions did change while the engines were being swapped,
etc.

find power.
