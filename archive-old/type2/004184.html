Since it's Friday, I thought I'd vent on supporting local stores.
Last week I burst a water hose on my wife's Vol^o, losing water I made it to
where for the last 40 years there was a FLAPS they had shut down! I filled
the car with water from another store (pet shop!) & limped to another FLAPS
they too have closed down both within the last 2 months! As I limped down
the road I found why; a HUGE car part/accessories super store, did they have
my hose? No, but they could order it in, in 10 days! Both of the other store
would have had the part I needed.
Here in the UK, DIY Superstores, Food Supermarkets, mail order & the
internet have just about killed all the smaller outlets. You might pay a
little more locally, but when you NEED a left handed thingamybob, that mail
order shed is 2 days delivery away & even then they don't stock it! Most of
these outfits are only interested in what sells in quantity.
I think the cost is even, 1 item to send back & gone is the saving or
unnoticed damage after you signed for the package - DOH!
In the end a big DIY store wants to sell 10 6" wood screws in a nice plastic
non bio-degradable pack for £3, where as my local hardware store will sell
the 2 that I need for 50 pence & they come in a brown paper bag! For the
sake of  pocket change use your local stores, it's good for your local
economy & community. Just visit any area where most of the local stores have
closed down, the local area begins to degenerate as jobs are lost & passing
trade in other store reduces. Just my rant (and all over a hose!).
