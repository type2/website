In fact this makes the '72-'79 design a major PITA in my opinion, but Sean
is right, no way on earth it will fall anywhere unless you pull the two
"special"
bolts holding the carrier in place.  Funny that, Sean and I have had the
discussion, if you know busses and see that bolt laying about, you know
instantly what it is, nothing else quite like it.

>The '68-'71 transmission is NOT supported independently of the engine, and
>it WILL fall to the ground if you take the engine out without supporting the
>transmission.

A trivial addition is of course the Vanagon 091 has no rear carrier and will
indeed drop, a glorious design as it allows you to easily pull the trans and
engine as a unit taking out only 8 bolts (OK not counting the CV's but they
go quick with an air ratchet) or to leave it in place and pivot the tranny
down
to make alignment a snap compared to the evil '72-'79 design.  For either
the '68-'71 or '80-'92 I rec using a small hydraulic jack under the
bellhousing
to allow you to easily adjust the angle during in/out or just a regular ole
jackstand, milkcrate, lump of wood, whatever.  It isn't that heavy, even the
autobox.
