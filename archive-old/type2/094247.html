on 7/11/02 7:07 PM, don miller at don_miller1_at_hotmail_dot_com wrote:

> 1) My 1975 bus has a 0231 181 012 distributor, this disy uses a speed
> limiting rotor.  Do I need to use this expensive rotor or can I use another
> cheaper one like 04 033.

You can use the cheaper one, I do.  I've seen the 5400rpm rev limiting 04016
for as little as $22 but you don't need it.  FYI, the 914 rotor is not the
same, it's 5800rpm.

> 2) My motor has about 10,000 on it and I have not done a compression check!

> holding on to the fitting, I end up trying to twist the hose.  Is there a
> gage out there that will clear the sheet metal?

Some gauges have a hose you can thread into the plug then connect to the
dial gauge with a quick release fitting.  Other types have a rubber end you
have to hold against the plug hole but they are a pain.
