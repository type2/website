You won't hurt a thing bypassing the CS valve. There
should be no worry of air leaking, but if so, just
leave a short length of hose attache on each side and
clamp a bolt in there to seal the ends. Be sure to use
high pressure FI hose for your bypass piece. You could
alos do the same by placing a piece of rigid line for
the bypass, long enough for existing fuel rail lines
to attach to. Your air bypass valve will still work
(if it IS working) but all that does is speed the
engine up for a bit.

Check your aircleaner and your exhaust. Dirty
aircleaner will cause rich  and so will exhaust pipe
damage. As I typed that previous sentence, I got to
thinking about it - Neither of those items should
cause a rich condition because they would both lower
the airflow through the AFM and therefore the mix
should remain the same, Good to check them anyway,
tho!

Do you have a throttle body switch on yours? Is it
loose? (Kind of a low-tech TPS)

How's the resistance at the CHT sensor? Should be
around 2400 ohms at around 70 degrees. More resistance
would cause rich mix. -BaH
