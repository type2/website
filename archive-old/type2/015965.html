>What I know: I have a small hole or two in my exhaust, you can't see them ,
>but if you block the tailpipe while she idles, there comes a hissing sound
>>from below.
>	at speed (60mph), with my foot half depressed on the accelerator, I ascend
>a hill. Naturally the power drops off.....I put-foot to get more and....
>
>  this is where I need help:
>It sounds as if someone is rattling stones round my exhaust, and I push my
>foot THROUGH the power band, but with no increase. This only happens at high
>speeds in top gear.

Sounds like preignition, a.k.a. detonation/knocking/pinging.  Don't let
your bus make this noise as it will destroy your engine in short order.
First thing I would try is running higher octane petrol.  If the problem
goes away, then you just learned something new about your engine - that you
have either too much timing, too much air (relative to fuel), or too much
compression.  You should continue running the higher octane, or take other
steps to alleviate the detonation, such as retarding the timing, richening
the fuel mixture, or reducing the compression ratio of the engine.

As for accelerating up a hill in 4th gear with a 1641 type 1 engine in a
campmobile, well, you can just forget about that.  ;)  I'm happy if I just
don't have to downshift!
