You should first go to www.type2_dot_com and look through the archives.  Most
of your questions have been answered before.  Since you're new, I'll
summarize a few of them.

DON'T use an easy out.  Try to unscrew the stud however you can.  If that
fails, I recommend drilling it out and tapping.  Go to a GOOD tool store
(i.e. not Northern Hydraulics or Harbor Frieght) and throw yourself upon
their mercy.  They will tell you what you need.  You can either go helicoil
with stock studs or tap the hole to 9 or 10mm and use stepped studs (bigger
on one end than the other).  Either way you won't have to drill out the
holes in your exchangers.  It can all be done from below without removing
the engine.  Clean the area, put a blanket down, maybe even an old pillow.
Get comfortable, you'll be there for a while.  Stock studs are 8mm, stepped
can either be 8x9 or 8x10.  I usually go for the 8x10 - bigger is stronger.
Most important thing is make sure you tap the hole straight.  Use grease on
the tap and clean it frequently.  Go slowly.

You should be able to get the tap/bits/studs/helicoil/whatever for under
$50.  You can fix it with that.  Don't overtighten the exhaust studs.  Use
lots of anti-sieze on the nuts.  Use loctite on the end of the studs that
go in the head.  Make sure the copper gaskets on top of the exchangers go
on straight.  Glue them to the exchangers if you have to.  Keep us posted...
