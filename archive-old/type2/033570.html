It won't?  My understanding of this is that the L-Jet AFM measures both air
volume and air temperature, but it doesn't measure pressure, and therefore
can't compensate for altitude.  I might be misunderstanding the function of
the flap in the AFM, though.  '79 California injection, with the O2 (lambda)
sensor could possibly correct the mixture at high altitude, at least
somewhat...

Still, that D. Turner guy with the nifty L-Jet modification site concurs:

(click the "L-Jet vs. World" link).

That said, Stephen had it right, I think: AFM measures the air, assuming
sea-level atmospheric pressure of, say, 15psi and dispenses fuel

Anyway, a system such as this could be used to lean the mixture, too.  I
