Hi James,

If I were in your shoes, I would seriously consider the "new" bus.  
Rust-free (or mostly rust-free) bodies are much more desirable than rusty 
ones.  Body work can be expensive, time-consuming, and difficult.  Given the 
choice, I'd rather replace the engine on a great body than deal with rust, 
holes, bondo, and welding on a Bus that runs like a top.

That said, I must echo the previous posts about inspection.  Make sure the 
shiny paint isn't covering lots of bondo, basket-case metal, and dangerous 
structural issues.  Make sure the "well-loved" Bus was well-loved at the 
mechanic, the dealership, or in the garage.  Shiny paint and a rust-free 
undercarriage won't make up for mechanical neglect and (worse) half-assed 
repairs.

I looked for a Bus for 6 months, searching all over the Southwest, and I 
finally found one about 2 hours away.  It runs great, the body is in great 
shape, and it was well cared-for.  They are out there, and they cost less if 
you don't have to bring them back from the dead.

Good Luck!
