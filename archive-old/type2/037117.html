No, dishwashing gloves are not the same...not at all.
Dishwashing gloves are made of latex and not immune to
any chemicals other than what is put it common dish
detergent. Sure, they'll keep your hands clean (from
the paint itself), but any and all solvents will pass
through and be absorbed into your skin! 

If you doubt me, try a pair on and use a little
lacquer-thinner/toluene/zylene/whatever on them...then
pull them off and smell your fingers. You'll smell
traces of whatever solvent you used, I promise.

True chem-proof gloves are made of neoprene or other
materials and they say "chemical-proof" (or something
to that effect)on them. This is an OSHA
requirement...so if it doesn't say it; don't trust it.


I learned this the hard way... I washed some
silk-screen frames with lacquer-thinner, using common
dish-gloves, and wearing a respirator (with the proper
filters <don't even get me started on *that* one!>). 

It took me about an hour to get the job done and I was
dizzy, nautious and short of breath for a couple of
hours therafter. This was a direct result of chemicals
being *absorbed* through the gloves and into my skin
(when it absorbs into your skin, it goes straight into
your blood veins and from there into your lungs,
brain, etc).
