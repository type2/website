You CANNOT static time a dual vacuum advance distributor to the specs listed.

You have to time it with a timing light AND have vacuum going to the retard side.

If you want to be close, then SUBTRACT the amount of retard degrees from 10 after. I believe the 925 is
supposed to be 16-18 degrees of retard at idle. Subtracting 18 from 10 after will mean you need to static time
it at 8 before. [if you look up the specs for any single vacuum unit type4 distributor, you will notice that
the timing is 7.5 before.....]

Set your timing at 7.5-8 before. Start the engine. Does either vacuum hose have vacuum at idle? If one does,
where does it go on the dist? It must go to the inboard nipple. Connect it. Does the idle slow down? It
should. If the engine wants to die now, then you need to do some carb adjustments.
