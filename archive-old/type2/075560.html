I can/have gone cheaper/quicker- on our 71, I got a free piece of 14
gauge house wiring about 5 ft long and pushed it through the grommet
through which the battery wire goes to the starter.  Unhook the upper
starter connector and attach this to the same terminal; I may have
stripped the wire a bit and soldered it, or found a double connector to
get the two on one terminal.  In the battery compartment I wrapped the
free end around the big battery cable so it couldn't touch anything. 
When needed to turn the starter, just touch it to the + terminal.  This
was left in place to do a one man compression check.
