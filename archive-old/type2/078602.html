Subject: Bet you didnt know....(VW Bus in Men at work tune)

In the song, Down Under, by Men at Work. The first 2 lyrics are..

1) Traveling in a fried-out Kombie (A VW Bus)
2) On a hippie trail, head full of zombie (Zombie was a slang word for a
particularly potent strain of marijuana grown in Australia in the seventies
or could mean Opium)

At the beggining of their video they are driving a late 60's/early 70's VW
bus(Baywindow- Panelvan) through the desert.

Heres another tilt on it....

According to Greg Ham (Vocals, Saxaphone, & Keyboards), , the song is about
Australians who are abroad and have come back home. As Bob Baker points out
in email, It is actually about a bunch of guys from Australian on an Opium
binge. Think about it; A den in Bombay (An Opium den), Travelling in a fried
out Kombie (Kombie is an VW Bus)on a hippie trail, head full of zombie
(zombie is slang for Opium) Buying bread (another slang word for Opium or
Heroin) from a man in Brussels

(PS. I think its the 1st one ("Fried-Out" Kombi?) :-)
