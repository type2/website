If your '80 Vanagon is a California model, it will not have EGR, so don't
sweat it.  If it is a federal model, the EGR valve is bolted to the left
side of the air distribution box that sits on top of the middle of the
engine.  There's a connection to it from the throttle and a pipe that runs
>from it over to the left side and down through the tin.


>5. The FI was replaced by a Weber progressive carb (see #1) which I know
>nothing about.
>
>6.  My spark plugs have dry black deposits on them... what would cause
that?
>

Dry black stuff = carbon = rich mixture.

>7. I have a mystery valve/vaccume thing at the front left of my
>compartment... on end (with rubber hose) is plugged with a screw, the other
>(metal one) is not attached to anything.
>

longer have.  See #1.

>Ok heres some background on what I have done/been doing....
>1. I put a compu-fire ignition in the dizzy because points scare me:)
>2. I have been using 20W-50 and I live in Florida
>3. Sealed a leak at the gas tank filler tube (which is the cause of the

>4. Keeping it timed _at_ 7.5 BTDC _at_ ~900 rpm

This timing spec isn't really relevant for you.  This was for the stock
distributor and fuel injection.  You now have neither.
The 009 distributor needs to be timed for total advance, not initial
baseline timing.  You are shooting for total advance of around 40 degrees
BTDC.
