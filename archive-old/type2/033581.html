Subject: Facet pumps

If a low pressure electric fuel pump is noisy, its because it is starving for
fuel and it is probably mounted to the firewall.  I wouldn't go to the trouble
of using rabbit radiator mounts, but i will use some rubber bushings of some
kind. If one of these square 'facet' style pumps is mounted metal to metal on
the firewall of a bus, then it will rattle pretty loud before it is pulling
its full capacity and even during normal operation it will run low and start
clattering sometimes.  
I think this has to do whith electric pumps in general being fuel 'pusher'
rather than 'puller' pumps; The pump will perform better (not starve, not
rattle) if it is closer to the fuel source (under the tank)- plus - if you
mount it to the frame on rubber, you won't hear it even if it does clatter. 
Could be a down side there (not hearing it), but if it fails completely,
you'll know.
