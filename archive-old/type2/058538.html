> I was once told that a stock carb on the 1835 would be starving it for
> juice and after recent threads about various jet sizes and engine temps I
> now wonder if this could have contributed to it's short life (more so than

    Starving it for power, yes, but as long as it was properly jetted it
would have *increased* the life, not decreased it -- reason being that
as long as it wasn't running too lean, it would (because of the smaller
air/fuel mix getting to the cylinders) be producing less power,
therefore less heat and stress, than if you had a bigger carb.
