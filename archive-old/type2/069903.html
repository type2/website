I routinely take mine to 45-50 in third if I'm trying to get any
acceleration done. I would also do this up hills if we had any of those,
but we don't. These boys need to stretch, it's the only way they can
breathe and the only way we can get any power.

In motorcycles, the sound is called "packed." It's loud, I guess, but an
aircooled can do it all day, and it's better off that way than laboring in
a lower gear. You won't hurt anything from revving it high, but you most
assuredly will from shifting too soon.
