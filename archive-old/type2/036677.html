first the bus has to be completely tuned. 
1 valves
2 check/re-gap plugs
3 compression check
4 carb adjustment/rebuild
5 check points/condensor.  these are the minimum steps to do.

THEN you can check the timing.  as to moving it to different settings to see
what runs better...that is a great thing to do to experiment and find out
what runs the best for your bus.  a good combination of power/ease of
starting/and fuel economy should be your goal.

the above mentioned steps are not to be taken lightly.  you could spend all
day trying to time it correctly when your carb is way out of wack.  and no
matter how great a relationship you have with the bus GODS the engine can
still suffer back-firing problems when a simple carb rebuild or adjustment
could cure it.  proper diagnosis and repair is ONLY done by a systematic
(which saves time in the end) inspection of all related components.
