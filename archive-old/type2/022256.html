How many times have to heard that??? (This is the friend's van with the 
second layer of metal on the U-pipes still intact). If you ever get the 
chance, drive a 70K mile bus that has been well taken care of. It quickly 
puts you own bus's real condition in perspective!!!

Our bus however makes ice all winter long. Our's was a bit draftier (okay 
alot).

We just replaced ALL of the the weatherstriping and rubber on our '78 Westy 
so I'm anxious to see what effect it has on our bus. Also switched to 
'72-'74 style heaters and I have to finish making all of the parts mate (I 
had to replace the OEM exhaust, it finally rotted off) and the desired 
replacement system (ala RMMW) required a '72-'74 heater system (which I 
think would be superior since less plumbing hanging out in the wind).

As for the wrap you mentioned. This will accomplish the exact same effect 
as the second layer of metal now missing to rot -- HOWEVER -- and you may 
not care (I wouldn't hesitate to see for myself) -- the wrap will hold 
moisture against the steel and will overheat the metal, and accelerate the 
rusting process of the steel. I'm told wrapped pipes may have to be 
replaced annually or semi-annually. The good side is you'll make more power 
(another hamster is worth something right?) - the reason the racers use it!

Another option would be to have a pipe, slightly larger in diameter than 
the OEM U - pipe, bent in the same U. Then split the new pipe and have the 
halves spot welded over your U-pipes. Or just buy the OEM replacement pipes 
- what do these cost?
