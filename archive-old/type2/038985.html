Subject: Pilot Bearing Removed!

Greetings,

I'm not sure if anyone will find this information useful, but hopefully it
will end up in the archives.

I was having a difficult time locating a Pilot Bearing puller in my area and
the "make your own" version out of bolts and washers didn't work either.
I'm sure I didn't have the right type/size of washers, but they just bent
when attempting to remove the bearing.

Here's what I did:  Used a shop grade paper towel (blue ones) covered in
clean multi-purpose grease.  Shoved it into the pilot bearing/crank hole and
used a bolt slightly smaller than the inside diameter of the pilot bearing
to pound it in.  Only took a few whacks and the pilot bearing was forced
out!

I'm sure others know about this trick and as usual I'm the last to learn!
Hope it helps someone out.
