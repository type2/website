Richard,
Germany and surrounding countries have much colder weather than most of our
states and the VW engine, in it's original form, was designed to take that
in mind. If all the factory componets are on the engines and working order,
you should be able to start in frezing weather and drive where no man fears
to tread. Up here in the PNW we have mild winters and have to drive up to
the mountains for our snow, but the trusty VW engine doesn't balk once, if
properly maintained. In fact, that's the last thing I think of, getting heat
into the cab is the first thing! Having snow and ice packed around your heat
exchangers cools off the ol' temperature!
