On Wed, 2 May 2007, Alan Beck wrote:

> When I was trying to remove my oil screen to check it, I removed a plate
> that had 10 mm bolts holding it on.

> I put it back on after cleaning it up. I used reasonable torque to tighten
> it, such as snug but not tight, using a 10 mm screw driver.

> Now when I run the engine I am losing about < Liter of oil per day.

Something isn't right.  Either you have a bad gasket, one of the gaskets
is missing, or the taco plate is bent.

Check the taco plate by putting it on a piece of glass.  Sometimes the
holes get dimpled.  This can be corrected with a ball-peen hammer.

You should use a gasket on either side of the oil screen flange.
Non-hardening gasket dressing like Hylomar helps to seal things properly.

Be sure to use the proper acorn nuts and copper washers on the screws.
The torque is quite small so make sure that you are not overtightening.
