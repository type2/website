You could do it that way. It might be easier to remove the old bushing from the
lower
shock mount though. That involves wd40, safety goggles, a cold chisel, and a 5
pound
sledgehammer, some good jack stands, a jack and wheel chocks.

Use the chisel to try splitting the bushing longways. I've gotten them off
before.

Your other alternative is to place the new shock in a bench vise and try
pounding out
the center ring, but I'd prefer to keep the new shock intact and remove the old
crap
on the spindle.
