I was hired by a fellow who owns a nice '95 Saab convertible to do a clutch 
cable replacement the other night. Being that I LOVE convertibles I asked 
if I could put the top down when I drove it home. It was 65 degrees so I 
left the windows up, ran the heater and then I discovered the button for 
the heated seats.

WOW! Very, very nice.

I have seen these seat heaters offered on the aftermarket. You slip them 
under the seat cover and then reassemble the seat. I plan to investigate 
adding these to at least the front two seats. Another option would be to 
investigate 12 volt blankets or heating pads. I prefer the heated seats 
though because with all the padding, the heat would more go (I think) to 
the driver rather than being cooled by the surrounding air like the blanket.

Sources for 12 volt warmers: RV and motorcycle catalogs. The motorcycles 
have very small charging systems so you can be sure your bus would power 
the motorcycle accessories just fine.

The RV style and heated seats could overpower the VW charging system if you 
ran too many at low rpms. Add up the loads before you spend your money.

Before anyone asks - because we cover it annually for the newbies - don't 
spend your money on the 12 volt heaters with the tiny fans. I spent that 
$20 and they are junk. I could not heat myself by blowing it on me alone. 
You can't power much from the cigarette lighter.

Think of it this way - try your hairdryer on low outside in the van on a 
cold day. Now imagine about 10% of this much heat.
