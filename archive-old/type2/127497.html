> I about to embark on some "wiring surgery" on some (many) cut wires in my
> engine compartment and under my dash. There are far too many unattached
> wires and ones with bad cheap connectors.
>
> My goal is to solder using original wire and cover the solder area with the
> original plastic color, giving it a nice clean original look.
>
> I have not solder in years so my question is what is the best way to achieve
> my goal and still get a good solder? Should the wires be placed side by
> side, end to end, twisted together, etc...?

not end to end.
you can twist them and then solder the two or
tin the 2 ends and solder them side by side.
either is fine.
the twisting is going to leave a bigger lump.

I dont know why you would need to cover the solder joints with the same
color. just use some heat shrink tubing any color. the spots will be
small. It is a good idea to use the correct matching wire to do the
repairs.
