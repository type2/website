It was my bay window bus that Henry DeKuyper showed, along with a friend's 
Vanagon.  We both had the A/c units at Busses By The Arch.  Many of us here 
in the North Houston VW Club are doing this on our camping trips.  I have 
torn up the mount that I used in the '79, as it was built on the spot in St. 
Louis.  I decided to take that van because 3 days earlier, my Vanagon got 
hit on the right side and was undrivable, necessitating getting the '79 
roadworthy rather quickly!  Essentially, you have to build a ledge that will 
sit in the window opening of the door, and keep any weight off the glass and 
window stripping.  As I recall, I used a piece of furring strip both in and 
out, and a 1X4 tying it all together.  A piece of 3/4 plywood is then cut to 
larger than the window cutout and it rests on the threshold piece mentioned 
earlier.  That piece of plywood will be cut in an inverted "U" shape, and 
the AC unit mounted to the plywood, and setting on the threshold.  A tie 
down strap then goes from the rain gutter to the wheel lip and holds it 
against the body.  Feel free to email me for more information.
