For those of you who have not been following this debate we have been 
talking about trying to convert a stock Fuel Injection system to a full 
mechanical system also made by Bosch. The mechanical system was developed 
after the stock electrical Injection was developed and as a result the weak 
points of the system have been eliminated.
This conversion offers the following advantages.
Way better fuel lines and connections, greatly reducing the risk of fuel 
line leaks and the consequences thereof.

Since this system does not use electrically powered Injectors, computers or 
electically powered air flow meters there is much less of a chance of being 
left on the side of the road.

This is the same system used on hundreds of thousands of Volkswagen 
Rabbits,Volvo's Audi's and even turbo Porsche models.
Yes it is true you can wack off the stock system and put in a carb or two 
but I think most people will agree that this really isn't the best solution 
to the problem. [is anyone building a car with a carb. anymore?]

who is interested.
