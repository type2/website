  Good basic concept. However, if you install a 12 VDC
"hard start relay" you get the same advantage without
risking life an limb (and your tie).
  With this installation you operate only a low amp draw
relay which shunts the more direct connection to your
starter. You use the key to activate voltage to energize
the relay. connected to the "normaly open" contacts on the
relay is your nice fat wire which is also a very short run
(2 feet?) direct from the battery to the starter instead of
all the way to the front of the bus and back... Make sure
your FLAPS supplied relay comes with wireing instructions
if you are not electrical... For the short, fat wire. I
would use a multi strand wire as opposed to single ... as
the multi strand wire will deal better with fatigue due to
movement.
