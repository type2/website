On Jan 20, 2004, at 2:48 PM, Faster-Laster wrote:

> BUT! consider this situation. typical hella relay setup w/ 12ga wire
> between batteries and relay. Camper kills battery listening to tunes
> while camping w/ friends at BBB and burning the cabin lights. The
> camper still has on all the accessories when he starts the bus. What
> will happen? I dunno. I suspect that everything will try to pull as
> much power as is needed through the little relay and wire, but I am
> not sure. anyone have any real input on this? i.e. actually have some
> books to back up everyones claims?

Put a 15amp (or whatever your particular relay happens to be rated at) 
fuse between the relay and the Aux battery.  Because the fuse will pop 
if the current draw exceeds your relay rating (assuming you properly 
matched the fuse and the relay), that should prevent damage to the 
relay in your scenario above.

It's probably useful to stick an ammeter in the circuit if you're doing 
any sort of repair (or fresh installation) work on your battery 
isolator set-up, because then you can see *exactly* what is going on.
