Subject: Re: [T2] when does the spark plug fire?

On Thursday, January 08, 2004, at 11:37PM, Mike Soultanian <msoultan_at_csulb_dot_edu> wrote:

>On a type4 engine does the spark plug fire on every TDC stroke (every 
>360 degrees of crank rotation), or on the TDC of the compression stroke 
>(every 720 degrees of crank rotation)?

Every 720 degrees, otherwise it wouldn't be a 4 stroke engine.

>Or even better, is the distributor timed to the crank or the cam?  My 
>guess is cam, because otherwise the valve adjustment procedure using the 
>distributor marks would be incorrect.

Both: the cam and the crank are indexed to each other. The distributor turns 90 degrees of rotation for every 180 degrees of crank rotation.

The pinion at the end of the shaft interfaces with the distributor drive gear on the crank.
