>I'll take that to mean that you are not disputing the "carry spare parts"

I dipute carrying spare parts so you can forget about maintenance, run
unto failure, they redeem yourself because you just happen to have the
particular part that failed. There are some people that carry 200 lbs of
spare parts due to their insecurity about the condition of their vehicle.
My premise is that if you ACTUALLY KNOW the condition of your parts, and
there is any probability that they are marginal, replace them BEFORE you
go. If I was heading out on a 5000 mile voyage, I would confirm current
condition of, let's say, the fan belt. Every tank of gas I would check
the oil and look at the fan belt. Oh, what's this??? That belt is becoming
glazed. In the next 1000 miles I'll stop at the most convienient place and
buy another one and continue to monitor the belt condition. At that point
that deterioration appears to be progressing up the curve to failure, but
before failure, I would replace it. Just one example.
