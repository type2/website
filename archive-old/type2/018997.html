Hello all,

I have an early '73 that has the same system.
I believe that you have a late 72 with the same system.
I'm told that this system is not the same as earlier or later models.
It's easy to get the wrong shoes.
The main pin which holds the lever (on the rear shoe) might be in the
wrong position because the shoe is wrong. I did have a problem due to
wear at the pins so that the lever would hit the hub before actuating
the shoes. I built up the holes with weld and filed them back down.
This was no where near the 1/2 inch that you are saying. BTW, you are
sure that the brake cylinders are fully compressed together when the
system is static?

Hope this helps

> > I have the drums off both rear wheels and the e-brake cables disconnected.  I'm looking at the passenger-side rear wheel.  The e-brake cable lever is an articulated two-piece arrangement, held together and also to the rear (on the passenger side wheel) brake shoe by a pin and a horseshoe clip.  It resembles an upside-down "L", with a pivot point where the two legs meet, which is pinned to the rear brake shoe.  The top leg of the upside-down "L" connects the two brake shoes and is the part that forces the front brake pad into the brake drum (when the e-brake cable is pulled).  The part of the lever that is pinned to the front brake shoe (passenger side again) is a sideways "U" into which a pin fits (also held in place by a horseshoe).  To actuate the spreading of the brake pads, the e-brake cable pulls the e-brake lever, which pivots on the rear brake pad (passenger side) which puts pressure on the front brake shoe and forces it into the brake drum (at least that is the way i!
