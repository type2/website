You always want to ground as close to your weld as possible.  Electricity
will always take the easiest route and if there happens to be something
electrical in the way, or a bearing, passing large amounts of current
through it might fry it, or weld it together.

Where ever you clamp your ground, always clean the metal with some sand
paper.  And if you are using a wire feed welder, you will also need to
clean the metal you are tring to weld.  Rust is an insulator along with
paint and undercoating.
