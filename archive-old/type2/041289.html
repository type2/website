>From Sami's Overheating text...
Running lean: Adjust carbs or, if you have fuel injection, check for false 
air. False air is air that sneaks into the intake manifold without first 
passing through the air-flow meter; hence the computer underestimates the 
amount of fuel to be injected.  A possible source of false air is an EGR 
valve that was not disabled, say, after the EGR filter fell victim to rust 
perforations; other sources are old, leaky rubber boots at the air intake 
distributor, disconnected or cracked vacuum hoses, loose valve covers, etc.
