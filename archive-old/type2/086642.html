> David hit the nail on the head here. Best engine configuration I have had
> to date is the 2.0 FI type IV. Putting one of these together right is not
> cheap though. Heads are critical, you need to have them done by someone
who
> knows these heads and can replace the seats. Thats about 5 bills there.
You
> will probably want a good web cam to improve performance and head temps.
> about 150-200. New pistons and cylinders 250-300. Balancing is extremely
> important IMHO to a long lasting smooth running engine. Then theres
quality
> bearings and seals, good exhaust and heat exchangers. The list goes on.
> Unless you have the skills or are willing to be patient and aquire them
my
> advice would be to buy a motor from Jake Raby or Boston Bob then do the
> install yourself. You can transplant one of these 2.0 Type IV's into your
> bus but it will require some mods including a late model tranny. Disc
> brakes are a must as well.
>      That said I would consider building a mild type 1 motor. Maybe 1776
or
> even 1835. External oil cooler (full flow of course) dual carbs. Good
> balancing, mild cam, good heads. Probably can be done for $1500. The list
> is probably your best resource on motor tips. If you  really want to go
> fast though you don't want a bus you want a sports car.
