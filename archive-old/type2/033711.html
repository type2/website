Ok relax your mind, as the gearbox is probably OK.  It is something
wrong with your clutch most likely.

First thing to check is the free play in the clutch pedal.  If it is
excessive, then it is probably something in the clutch linkage.  

Often a cable will partially break which makes things go way out of
adjustment so that you can't disengage the clutch.

Less often, the problem is in the throwout bearing, but those sort of
failures mean that you don't get any clutch disengagement at all.

If the clutch free play is correct, then you might have a seized pilot
bearing that is making it impossible to disengage things.  This will
require pulling the engine for bearing replacement, but at least the
tranny's OK!
