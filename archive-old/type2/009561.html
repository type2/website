My dad and I used to camp out all over south western Washington in
several Westies.  The last one was a '77 Overnighter(no pop top). 
In the box next to the ice box, we'd keep a load of good oak, alder
or other hard wood for a fire if the local wood was too wet.  If
you want to use a camp fire to cook, you need good coals which come
>from hard woods.  

One thing we always had stashed away was a supply of Franksenbeans
and other canned food.

I loved camping out in the Westies.  You just pull into some little
nook off of a logging road, make sure the nose is at an "up" incline
and go to sleep.  The only problems with a few of our choices were
when the semis go by at 70 MPH and rattle the camper. <G>

Another nice thing is we were NEVER broken into or trashed, EVER in
20 years of fishing!  Hey, who wants to break into a VW when there's
a $30K rig sitting right next to it?? <G>

Maybe this is getting towards the friday limit?  Nah, it's still all
about our type 2s!
