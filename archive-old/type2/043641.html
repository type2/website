> Brian,
> I can't imagine changing it with out removing the gray gas tank cover. I
> have a carb in mine so it is relatively easy to pull the carb off, unscrew
> the screws that hold the cover on (two hidden on bottom), and then I had
to
> bend my back engine tin in a little bit to pull out the cover (bottom of
> gray cover has to come out 1st). Then remove the round plate and access is
> granted. When I 1st removed the cover last summer I was unaware of the 2
> screws on the bottom. I just thought it was hung up on something. So I
ended
> up prying it out w/ out taking them out. More or less ripping the sheet
> metal at those 2 holes. I never re-attached the 2 bottom screws and not
even
> sure you have access to them w/ the engine in. If you don't want to rip it
> out like I did (took about an hour!) you should ask the list about the
> proper way to remove the gas tank cover.
> Having the back open also lets you check on the small evaporative hoses
that
> run over the tank. One of mine was disconnected causing fumes.
>
> The new elbow attaches like the old and clamp it down. I bought a straight
> piece of gas rated hose for the tank section. Greased it up a little on
both
> ends so it slid around easily on the filler tube and at the gas
tank.There's
> not a lot of room back there so you don't want to be struggling with tight
> rubber on both ends as you have to twist and slide it both ways to get it
> on.  Push the whole unit in through the back. It's long but it fits.  Fish
> the elbow end up and out the opening. I got it to hang up a little at the
> opening, walked around, and carefully grabbed it w/ a pair of needle-nose
> and pulled the outer lip through. At this point I'm not sure if it would
be
> better to screw the elbow to the body or not. I did it last and the holes
> were slightly off, but the rubber was flexible enough to compensate. I
> clamped both ends of the straight piece. Went and bought a new very long
> screw driver so I could really torque them down. Not having the bend at
the
> tank makes the fit less than perfect. No leaks yet though.
>
> I had my engine out which made it a lot easier but I did some
jerry-rigging
> on the old filler neck  last summer with the engine in and it wasn't too
> bad.  I imagine it might be a little more time consuming removing some of
> your FI stuff. But removing the back cover is the first step. I forgot
that
> with the engine out, the gray cover should go back 1st but I put the
engine
> back 1st and ended up having to bend my engine tin in again! Other than
that
> it was a smooth operation. Of course I'm comparing it to my 1st engine
drop
> that I was doing concurrently, getting the engine out by myself, fried
pilot
> bearing, shot starter.........I'm a teacher too. I teach art at Southern
> Utah University. There were a few moments of despair were I was
approaching
> tears and wondering what the HELL I was doing and why I didn't take it to
a
> mechanic! I'm a better Bus owner now. An education can be painful, but
> priceless.
>
> Good Luck,
> Brian Hoover
> Cedar City, Utah
> 1978 Bus
