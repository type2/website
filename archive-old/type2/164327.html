Eddie,

I don't know how much you know about wire-feed welders...so this is just my
opinion.

You are right, flux-core welders are really difficult to use in most
situations.

I have a Miller Pulser MIG dual process welder and a Lincoln 185 TIG. Both
are superb machines and I recommend both brands. I have been using welders
for years and a Dual voltage welder is going to get you the best results.
This will allow you to re-wire between 110v and 220v. 110v for the field and
220v for the shop. The best gas to use is Silver Shield AR/CO2 which is a
less expensive mix that works extremely well for mild steel. Straight argon
is typically used for TIG welding aluminum and mild steel.

As for your question, the Lincoln 175 HD has a good track record for simple
shop repairs as does the Millermatic Passport. Too high of a duty cycle will
blow breakers but gives better weld quality.

Obviously the best welders for sheet metal are TIG welders, but TIG is
neither quick or efficient. A possible solution is using a MIG welder with a
TIG spoolgun attachment. It does sheetmetal much better than a straight MIG,
but that is a different subject all together

Good luck!

Jon Stiles
