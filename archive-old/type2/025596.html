> Do compressor tools work that much better than electric tools?

Yes, and it's not just what you'd expect...

Take the auto polisher, for instance. They have electric ones and air ones,
and the electrics usually have a couple of fixed speeds. No just a little
faster or a little slower. An air powered polisher has an air valve--a
little faster or a little slower is just a matter of a little less or more
air.

I also like the die grinder for cutting things off. If you can do an
electric die grinder, I haven't seen one. (There may be such a thing, but so
far I haven't seen one.) Pneumatic die grinders are in most of the "top ten
air tools" packs.

Get good hoses and you're set.
