Subject: RE: Cylinder to Head Sealing Rings

The guy at bugformance said these were used to adjust the compression also, but
said that the correct way to do this is to shim up the cylinder, as the spacers
have a tendency to burn out, letting the cylinder to head mating surface leak,
which my old heads do show signs of.  This seems to be a low compression engine,
since it only had about 105 psi max when I purchased it newly rebuilt from ATK
in 1985.  I think I am just going to skip the sealing rings and put it together,
then check the compression.  As long as it is below 130 psi, I'll leave it until
I have a chance to get my replacement engine built.
