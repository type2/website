IMHO, the transmission is the same, all you need is all tin to match the
upright engine. If you are going to re-build, I would go for a 1641 kit. Not
much more heat, lots more torque. If you use a flow exhaust & manifold (e.g.
the 'extractor' manifold with a quiet-pack exhaust) you will get even more
torque & power. You can burn valves etc quite easily though with these
engines. The above set-up means you don't thrash the engine as you have a
better power band & heaps of torque.

However you _MUST_ re-jet to match the charitaristics of the modifications,
other-wise you will 'lean-out' the mix & burn away the heads where the plug
enters the chamber. With the correct timing & mixture this set-up pulls well
& is as cool as stock (+/- 10deg). In fact cooler up hills!
