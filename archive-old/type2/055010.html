Jen,
Here's how I'm usually shifting in my '76 westy:

first  0 to 10-15
second 10-15 to 20-25
third  20-25 to 40-50 (yes it does go that high)
fourth 40-50 to 80-85 (96 downhill :)

That's what I can remember off the top of my head.  What you gotta do is
feel the power range of the engine.  If you shift too soon, you'll
notice that the car won't pull very well in the next gear.  It's kinda
like trying to start from a standstill from idle.  Doesn't pull too well
>from 1000RPMs(that's the usual idle) does it?  But, when you give it
some gas, you're running around 1500-2000 (I don't have a tach either)
which has more power and is therefore able to pull the car from a
standstill better.

So if when you are shifting you're hitting those real low RPMs in the
next gear and it's not pulling well, then give it some higher RPMs in
the previous gear.  In other words, gauge how high to rev the engine
based on the how much power you want in the next gear.  If you're going
up a hill, it's going to have to be higher because the engine needs even
higher revs to pull the weight uphill.  If you're going downhill, you
probably notice you can shift really fast, and probably get to fourth
gear by 30.

Hopefully that makes sense :)
Mike
