I recall reading a few years ago, in the British VW Type 2 Owners' Club 
bi-monthly magazine (Transporter Talk), that the heat riser tube (i.e. 
inlet-manifold, exhaust preheat pipe) can be unblocked chemically, without 
having to resort to cutting or drilling holes.

The inlet manifold is first removed from the engine, an placed up-side-down 
in a suitable tray or bowl. The chemical solution is then poured into the 
exhaust preheat pipe and allowed to soak into and react with, the compacted 
carbon; topping-up as necessary.

For the moment, I cannot remember exactly which chemical is used, but I 
suspect it is a commonly available, domestic drain cleaner, such as 
sulphuric acid or caustic soda (i.e. sodium hydroxide). Hopefully, I shall 
be able to check the details, before my next e-mailing session on Friday!

I used caustic soda dissolved in boiling water, to clean all the internal 
steel components, of my air-cooled VW engine, which works extemely well.

Regards.
              Nigel A. Skeet

P.S.

Speaking of chemicals, do any of you folks use EDTA (i.e. ethylene diamine 
tetra acetic acid or its di-sodium salt), in your lead-acid starter 
batteries, to prolong their service lives, by inhibiting the sulphation 
process? We use it in ours, most of which typically last 6 to 8 years. The 
original one on my father's Ford Sierra XR4x4 lasted nearly 13 years.
