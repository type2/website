At 09:28 AM 03/31/2001 -0700, you wrote:
>How do i test the individual injectors? Is there a better way than just
disconnecting them while >running to listen for a change?

Pull them up out of the manifold and visually watch the spray pattern as
someone runs the starter. Do ground the coil wire for this test. They
should produce an atomized round cone pattern, very geometric. Any abscence
of symetry is indication of contaminated pintles/orifaces.

>I have heard of some method of syncronizing the amount they spray.  Is
this something that can be >done at home or need a shop?

But do check with the vendors who service big semi trucking diesel industry
systems. Many of them have the equipment and skills to balance injector
flow. Independant repair shops that typically service Bosch equiped auto's
sometimes have the equipment to measure flow rates with the bench testing
equipment, but not what is needed to adjust the rates for balancing. I
would expect any Bosch Authorized Service Center to have the test equipment
to verify the balance of your injectors. They can also clean the whooly
s**t out of them if we got bad cone structure. If they are in spec, you
have eliminated them as a possible cause of your problem/s. If one is
lean/rich and the others fine, you know to only to buy one injector. You
spend no more or less time/money than is necessary to resolve the issue for
REAL by confirming all facts by inspection.  

What really amazes me the the number of 15-20 year old Bosch injectors I
have seen function nominal. I have replaced only a few dozen in the last 10
years, both diesel and gas. If you can find it, Lubri-Moly US/Liqui-Moly
Germany make a products called Ventil Sauber and Jectron that can really
clean up and resolve dirty injectors and valves and combustion chambers.
