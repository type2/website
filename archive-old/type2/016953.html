Ray:
What, ain't it warm enough in Stockton? Wishin' I was in Stockton cause
2 weeks ago it was -22F here in Iowa. Much warmer now though.
I'd say 80% chance it's the CV joint. Changing the ride height and
turning corners (with its attendant change in joint angle proves too
much for the wear or possibly just dry grease). I agree with your local
guru nothing else hardly ever goes wrong. One other thing- you did get
the nut (BIG nut) tight didn't you-with only a 3-foot pipe cheater bar I
dont think you could get it too tight. Oh, I'm re-thinking what you
said, you didn't take the stub axle loose at first, right? Then I'll
stick with the CV joint. I've prolonged the life of dying CV's by 20-30
thousan miles by cutting a small incision in the boot and spraying
around inside with spray grease. I figured I had nothing to lose- I
think the joints usually outlast the boots in our climate.
