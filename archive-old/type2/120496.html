On Tue, Feb 24, 2004 at 12:27:42PM -0900, Jamal Floate wrote:
>  I am facing a dilemna on just what to do with my bus carbs.  I currently 
> have a '74 1800cc engine with the stock dual carb set.  Both carbs are in 
> serious need of being rebuilt renewed etc.  My mechanic has advised going 
> to a single carb conversion due to our living conditions.  Because I live 
> in a very wet, consistantly cool enviornment (Alaska), my mechanic says the 
> very small power loss by doing this conversion will be easily made up for 
> in reliability.

let me put this disclaimer out first: I do not own a dual carb bus. 

Moving to a single carb in that environment is not a good idea. carb
icing is the problem. There are no hear risers to keep the manifold at
a temp to help keep condensation from happening. This causes a bit of
idle problems. Do some searching in the archives for more information
on this. 

One other alternative is to go w/ a system such as here:
http://cbperformance_dot_com/catalog.asp?ProductID=1214

I have not seen or heard from anyone who is running this setup on the
t-4 engine, but have talked w/ one who was very happy with the results
on his t-1 engine. They have chokes too!
