Joe:
Your ground cable on the transmission nose to frame is either
disconnected or non-funtional. The return current is seeking any means
possible and the one it found is the clutch tube (not as good as a
copper cable).
I noticed you mentioned that the coil wire is disconnected. You should
not disconnect the coil wire without grounding it- it could cause the
voltage to build up and damage the coil. A better plan is to pull the
points-to-coil wire from the distributor. This wire is never hot (the
points just ground it), so you don't have to be vey careful to insulate
it.
