He may not like the pop-top now... but he will wish he had one after

The weekender is the version made without a pop-top. They are out  
there, but not as common as the pop-top version. A lot of people have  
made westfakia's using a westy interior and a passenger van.... the  
tip-off is that the x3xxxxxxxxxxx VIN indicates a true factory  
westfalia. The x2xxxxxxxxxxx VIN is a regular bus. The more obvious  
tip-off is that the factory hard-tops also had a wooden headliner,  
which couldn't have been borrowed from a pop-top camper.


> Can anyone give me a price range he can expect to pay for something  
> that needs a little bit of cleaning up, but otherwise is mostly  
> rust free and has a mechanically sound drive-trane?

Location, Condition and demand determine price. But the common  
guideline on this list is that a good runner is going to cost about  
$5K on average. You pay it up front, or invest it later in the year  
as unplanned maintenance. Prices usually drop in fall and winter and  
go back up in spring.
