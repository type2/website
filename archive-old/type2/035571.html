I'll have to put the flame suit on for this one. IMHO, $5,800.00 may not be 
too far out of line for this one. I've seen bays going for more than this 
that don't "sound" as good as what you describe. Garaged and the interior is 
in mint condition? A fairly rare find. How far did you drive it? The brakes 
may have been slightly rusty if it's been sitting a while. Mine do this when 
mine has been sitting for a couple weeks or more, but it goes away after a 
while. Or the rotor(s) may have a high spot. I'd take it to a mechanic and 
have the whole thing checked out. More often than not, low miles and little 
use are harder on a vehicle than high miles and frequent use. Sure, you can 
buy a rusted out beater for $1,000.00 or less, but is that what you really 
want? And if you buy the beater for $1,000.00 and plan to restore it, I 
guarantee you'll spend far more than $5,800.00 in parts and TIME. And, sorry 
Mark, but a one year warranty from a private seller is unheard of. Even a 
used car lot would MAYBE go 90 days on a vehicle this old. Use the brakes and 
the heat ( and whatever else a mechanical inspection turns up ) as a 
bargaining chip,and try to get the seller down some, maybe to $5,000.00. 
Final question- How many of you out there would sell a garaged Westy with a 
complete, mint condition interior for less than 5K? Yeah, I thought so.    
Good Luck,  Jeff   ( Who has been restoring a rusted out Westy for three long 
years!)
