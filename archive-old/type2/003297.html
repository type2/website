Karl-
for general reference, in "porsche" #'s:
group # 021 is 1.7 origin
group # 022 is 1.8 origin
group # 039 is 2.0  origin
Just one of life's little quirks, i guess- unless they had a plan .
As in vw, the earlier # points to the origin of the part, and the
suffix letter points to a modification, or update, or improvement.
just as in vw , where you will find a part start with 111, as the
origin of that part was a beetle ,even though it is being used on a bus.

bus 1.7L               210 mm-  021105 271
bus 1.8                215 mm -  022 105 271A ( *not  021, not H )
bus 2.0                228 mm-   029 105 271   ( * not  022 102 271D )
914( one size fits all 4-cyl )-   022 105 273A

The 914 flywheel differs from the bus part in that the flywheel teeth are
.125" closer to the transaxle. this will cause the starter to jam.
