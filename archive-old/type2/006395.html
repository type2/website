I'm not trying to be argumentative. However, the solution to your "problem"
is staring you right in the face. Remove the rear tin, and drive it. If the
airstream under the bus is at a lower pressure than in the engine
compartment, the engine temps will not increase. If the pressure is higher,
the hot air will flow towards the lower pressure (engine compartment), and
the temps go up.

So the question is answered when you remove your tin and drive it on the
highway. You will be convinced after the test, I'm sure of it.
