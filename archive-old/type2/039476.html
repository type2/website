>When backing the #2 plug out, there was a bit or resistance, and after being
unable to >get the compression tester threaded, I checked with a flashlight and
saw that that the >threads were basically gone.
>I was real discouraged yesterday. So what should I do?

Check the plugs you took out. There is an off chance that there will be a spark
plug insert
on the plug that went in that hole. If that's the case, cover the outside of the
insert
with loctite, then reinstall the plugs, wait for the loctite to dry, then try
removing that
plug again, this time without the insert.

If there is no insert you may be able to install one. There is a chaser/threader
you can
buy - like an installation kit. It would probably be easier with the motor out.
Worse case
scenario you buy a rebuild head and switch the head - also motor comes out.
