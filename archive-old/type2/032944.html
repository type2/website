Steven,

Years ago (more than I care to count) I dealt with the same problem in a '64
Econoline van:  I bought different-sized flexible plugs from the local hardware
store (I think this was pre-Home Despot .. yikes) and fitted them into the
holes as best I could; I made sure the flatter side of each plug faced upwards,
or flattened a plug by hand/knife if it was too domed.  The odd-sized holes
(ones that wouldn't take a plug) I redrilled to the next size larger .. then I
cut corresponding holes (where needed) in pieces of plywood subfloor to fit
over the plugs so the floor didn't end up all lumpy and uneven.  It worked
pretty well .. as I was stationed on the east coast of Fla, it was inevitable
that my plug job would outlast the rest of the van, which succumbed to coastal
cancer (rust).

Hope this helps.
