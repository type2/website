Chris, make sure the seals snap into place properly on the window frame.  Use 
a little spray silicone on the window opening and the seal (only the part 
that contacts the bus body).  You have to push the seal in in some places as 
necessary as you push the window in, so it is easier with two people - one to 
hold the window in and another to work the seal in where it is a problem.  
After the window is in you may need to lift the seal edge out in some places 
where it got tucked under.  I use a sharpened popsicle stick to protect the 
paint.  There is no cord like the other windows, the screws hold the window 
in, not the rubber.  Good luck.
