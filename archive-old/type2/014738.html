VW made a transaxle just like you are looking for, called a Syncro. They
used them in the Vanagon Syncros. These Vanagons are hard to find and parts
even harder, but they are out there. The price reflects the rarity of the
Van. However, if you want to make an all-wheel-drive VW, this would be the
easiest place to start, with a used, possibly wrecked Vanagon Syncro. They
had an output for the driveshaft to the front and a differential in front.
The trans is a lot like the 091, but I do not think they share many parts,
mounting may be the same though. It may cost a lot for Syncro parts, but a
lot cheaper than having someone custom make parts for a regular trans. Put
out lots of feelers and you may get lucky with a Syncro that has what you
need, but the rest is trashed. The Vanagon list will be very helpful in
locating a donor or parts for such a project. Please note, I do not suggest
that you gut out a good Syncro for its drivetrain, these are to hard to
find, try to locate a damaged one.
