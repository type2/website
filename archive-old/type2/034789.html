This is true to a point, but trying to use any car for a counterpoise
(ground plane) in the 11-meter wavelength is a tremendous compromise. 
Just get the antenna on top of the bus and you're 90% there.

> 23-channel;

Also true, but most communications does not take place in optimal
conditions.  I prefer to quote the realistic numbers so that the reader
won't be disappointed.

> George is very correct on the antenna length issue, and you might do
> well to get help there if you don't have an SWR meter (locals can
> borrow
> mine :).  The length of the antenna and cable should be physically --
> or
> electronically, through a "tuner", but that's less effective --
> matched
> to some multiple of the wavelength for CB (10 meters) to get a
> standing
> wave ratio closest to 1:1.

Actually, the best antenna is a quarter-wave on 11m, which is a
straight whip 108 inches long.  This is 9 feet long, and unfortunately
when you put this on top of a bus you end up with an overall height of
15 feet, a bit more than the 13-1/2 feet allowed.  This is why I
recommend the 6 foot helical Firestik.

The actual length will vary slightly from the exact 1/4 wave because of
the small ground plane, which is why the antenna has to be tuned to the
vehicle.
