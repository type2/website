Paul,

I think he is talking about lengthening the wires that connect the gauge
to the thermocouple wires. From how you responded, im going to take a
guess that you havent seen a VDO head temp gauge. If I am wrong about
this please correct me though. The VDO sensor I have has ~1 foot of
thermocouple wire that has a ring on one end that goes around the
sparkplug base, on the other end is a molex plug that from there
connects to two regular old wires that run to the front to the gauge.
The only thing I could see that would go wrong with lengthening the
regular old wires would be the increased resistance, but I don't think
that it would be large enough to make much of a difference.

actually lengthening the head temp gage's wires with anything but the
proper thermocouple wire will make it worthless most likely as it
derives the temperature from the cold junction of 2 different metals,
copper/constantine (red/blue type T) being the most common in my line of
work.  not a big deal though, go to a geek store and get as much of the
proper thermocouple wire that you need and twist the wires together then
solder them and shrink wrap over them.
