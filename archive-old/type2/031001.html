> I am having a problem with the fuel gauge on my '77 Westi.  Most of the time

> sender needs to be replaced and it would cost approx. $300.00 CDN to cut the
> access hole and fix it.  Is this the only way to correct my problem?  I

if it is a Fuel Injected type IV engine (stock on your vehicle) and you
have small arms (or friend) you can get the sender in without cutting
anything.  check the archives for details 
but basically you need to

remove the Air flow meter and air filter.  
       the double relay, and ballast
       remove the bulkhead between the engine compartment and gas tank
        (don't forget the two _at_#$&*_at_#& screws that come up from under the
              bus.)

reach into the opening and find the two wires on top of the sender, pull
them off.  turn sender 1/4 turn counter clockwise (very hard, and if it
isn't you need a new O ring)  wiggle, twist, curse etc. it will come out,
I find it best to go towards the drivers side of the vehicle then pull it
around the side of the tank.

 reverse the prochedure to install the new one, give yourself several
hours.
