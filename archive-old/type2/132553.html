On Jul 11, 2004, at 2:37 PM, Kim Kariotis wrote:

> I was out adjusting my valves today on my 1600 singleport and while 
> tightening the bolt after setting on the screw on the feeler gauge at 
> .006  the whole screw and bolt cracked off.
>
> so, it seems the stud of the screw is still in the hole.  I'm assuming 
> I cannot drive it.  any advice on how to fix it so I don't have to 
> pull it into the mechanic.  Not sure how to get the stud out....I've 
> never really worked on the engine parts except to set the valves.  Its 
> number 3.

you can unbolt the rocker-arm assembly and work on it from there.

Basically: there are two 13mm nuts which hold the whole rocker arm 
assembly (the things that the valve adjust screws are in) to the head.

Remove these nuts and washers, put them in a safe place, pull the 
rocker arm assembly off and look at the broken off bolt.  If the bolt 
protrudes from the other side of the rocker arm, you may be able to 
either screw two nuts on it and thread it out, or, get a set of 
vice-grips on it and unscrew it that way.  If you can't do that, the 
next simplest thing is probably to pick up a new rocker arm assembly 
from somewhere (or somebody on the list).  I probably have one handy 
that I can send you if no one else can.

Good luck!
