On Tuesday, September 20, 2005, at 06:36PM, Eric Arnold <ezgruv_at_cox_dot_net> wrote:

>So my ALT light came on while driviing on the freeway tonight. Very
>light at first, increasing brightness while slowing down and when
>stopped. 

That means you've got 12V on one side of the light powered by the battery and several volts less than that on the "ground" side of the bulb coming from D+ (blue wire) on the alternator. If you check D+ at the VR you'll probably see 1-2V or less.

This means that the output from the alternator that the voltage regulator sees is low. Possibilities include:

- bad regulator not reading B+ and powering DF correctly
- battery has become disconnected from alternator
- bad battery or alternator ground
- alternator brushes or slip-rings not making good contact 
- alternator had electrical failure (diodes/rotor/stator)
