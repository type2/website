Sounds indeed like a late splittie tent. It's basically the same as the late
model tents, but without a floor. Unless you are striving for perfect matching
of the accessories, I would go for a later tent, as the floor is very nice to
have.
But, if you can ever find an early tent, they are pretty cool, as you can set
up just the awning, for hanging out and such, and then at night attacch the
walls to finish up the tent.
 I have seen examples of all three of those styles, and other than for reasons
of authenticity, I'd choose the late model tent over the late split one. Of
course, if you can find an early tent, go for it! (It also requires special
bumper brackets.)
