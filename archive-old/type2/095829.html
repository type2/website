Hi Don,

I assume you have an electric gauge rather than a mechanical gauge (do VDO
even make a mechanical gauge?).

With an electric gauge the gauge does not measure the pressure but rather
responds to the change in resistance of the sender unit in the engine bay;
but instead of being calibrated in volts or Ohms its calibrated in psi.

These means that the gauge reading can be effected by a change in resistance
some where in the circuit eg a dirty /bad contact at the sender unit. I
would check this first, also check that a stranded wire is not breaking
where it is attached to the connector.

Its also possible for electric gauges to produce a different reading due to
a different voltage being produced by the regulator (on my '73 the oil temp
was 5 degrees C different with headlights on or off). So a changed reading
could simply be an indication of a different electrical load within the
vehicle, or it could be indication of a failing regulator in your
dynamo/alternator.

The pressure within the engine is regulated by one or two springs (depending
on exactly which engine you have) so higher than normal pressure may
indicate a stuck pressure relief spring/plunger. There are e-mails in the
list archive on checking the operation of these springs/plungers.

Of course it could be dirt causing a partial blockage of the oil gallies
too, a treatment with flushing oil (follow the instructions provided when
you buy it) may clear a partial blockage; but I would not consider flushing
oil unless all the above produce no results.
