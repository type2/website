Subject: Coil Autopsy

As I promised, here's my coil after cutting it open.

The coil was purchased just a couple months ago at Autozone in California
while troubleshooting a problem that ended up being the fuel filter. On
Father's Day this year I started feeling an occasional misfire that
increased in frequency to the point where I could barely drive the bus.
Swapping in the old original Bosch coil got things back where they should
be.

Here's the shiny relatively new coil:
http://www.donimages_dot_com/tech/deadcoil/coil01.jpg

First I drilled two holes to drain the oil. I set it so when I drilled the
vent hole the drain hole would be right over the opening in my drill press
platform. Wasn't much oil in there.
http://www.donimages_dot_com/tech/deadcoil/coil02.jpg
http://www.donimages_dot_com/tech/deadcoil/coil03.jpg

The coil after going around the top with a reinforced cutoff wheel on my
Dremel:
http://www.donimages_dot_com/tech/deadcoil/coil04.jpg

Well, I was disappointed to see just how this thing was constructed. The
windings are embedded in paper and only use less than half the available
space. There's an obvious scorch mark in the paper nowhere near the
windings. Odd. The windings are two separate sets as expected around a steel
sheet metal box.
http://www.donimages_dot_com/tech/deadcoil/coil05.jpg
http://www.donimages_dot_com/tech/deadcoil/coil06.jpg
http://www.donimages_dot_com/tech/deadcoil/coil07.jpg

That scorch mark may or may not be why the coil failed. I haven't unwound
the thing, and probably won't. The paper just disintegrates when I tug on
it. I would, however, be curious to compare this to a known good quality
coil. But I don't have one to cut up, so this will have to do for now.

I would have returned this turkey to Autozone if I could find the friggin
receipt. Grr....
