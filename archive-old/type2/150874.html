Glue cracking produces an effect on glass like a frosted window in late fall 
(well, in my area, late fall is moderate cold)  It is a most common pattern, 
and you would see this in hotel lobbies,  restaurants or bars.

The horse glue comes in a powder, and it must be heated or cooked in a glue 
pot, and applied hot / warm to the sand blasted area.  When it dries, it 
shrinks, and the operative word here is 'when'.  I was talking with a glass 
artist about this and he antidoted me that one late evening he was working 
alone in his 'studio' and he heard some noises from a back corner.  He 
investigated and found some panes of glass he had put aside in the corner 
and forgot about.  It took it's own time in drying, and really there is no 
target time you can use for drying.  I guess it depends on how slow the 
horse ran in its last race as Santa Anetia.  Oh, yes, the broken glass 
particles will fly off in all directions as it cracks, so it is best to let 
it dry in an enclosed space, like a refrigerator box for large pieces...

Also

I had some sand blasted work done on my VW's rear window.  This glass artist 
has a special process to recreate even a very small visible marking, like a 
dot, onto a two layered mat.  It is not laser etching, but a patented 
process he has. (The studio also does laser etching for putting names on 
champagne glasses at weddings, etc.)  He charged me $.25 per square inch for 
the 2 layer mat with the art inscribed, and $.15 to do the actual sand 
blasting.  (The rear window would not fit into his blast cabinet, and he had 
to tape a garbage bag onto the cabinet to close it off.)  Extremely 
reasonable prices, but the inches added up and it was almost $100 per window 
X 3.
