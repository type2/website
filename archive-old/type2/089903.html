In the paint department at Home Depot (disclosure: I sell lumber there)
you will find a product called Rust-O-Leum Industrial Enamel. It comes
in quart and gallon cans; the gallons cost $22. It's packaged in the
most delightful colors--safety red, safety yellow, black, a light blue
for making handicapped signs, and a couple other colors I'm not
remembering right now. There's also a Primer for Clean Metal and a
Primer for Rusty Metal. All of the topcoats are glossy. There's also a
special traction additive for making non-skid walking surfaces. 
I got a can of this stuff in Safety Yellow (Home Depot employees pay
full freight on all our purchases) and painted the catwalks on a
printing press with it. I used a brush. It flows out really nice and
looks good. Catwalks are already nonskid, so no problem with the gloss.
It thins with plain ol' mineral spirits. 
It ain't car paint quality, but it's a far sight better than a rattle
can job--and at $44 for a can of primer and one of topcoat, it's about
as cheap.
