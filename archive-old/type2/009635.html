I wouldn't want it that way.  Flexible fuel lines are used to make
connections and in areas where some flex must be accomodated.  Letting
connections dangle in the breeze is courting disaster.  Picture a rock
coming along and smashing that filter, then having gravity-fed
gasoline sprayed all over the bottom of the engine!  You should
shorten the lines and secure the filter in a more protected location. 
If the filter is light, it may be supported by the lines.

Keep in mind that putting a filter upstream from the pump is asking
for vapor lock problems, although many do it successfully.  If you
start to experience cutting out on hot days, keep this in mind.
