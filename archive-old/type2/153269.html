Yes, there is a temp sensor in the AFM I mentioned
yesterday. To test it, if you have a seven terminal
connector on the AFM, it is terminal 27. Connect
ohmmeter to term 6 & term 27 and at 68 degrees or
above, the resistance should not exceed 2500 ohms.

If it is a six terminal connector, the sensor is built
in to the potentiometer.

Tests: terminals 6&9=100 to 300 ohms;
7&8= 80 to 200 ohms

Other factors like a choke? Plugged air cleaner, the
vane inside the airflow meter stuck (someone mentioned
that earlier - I hope you checked!) Plugged exhaust.
How about some way the charcoal cannister is full of
raw gas and it gets sucked into the engine? I dunno -
but it is, I suppose, a possibility.

All the enrichening is done by either increasing the
fuel pressure when under load (low vacuum) or changing
the dwell of the injectors (the amount of time they
stay open). The wire from the coil to the ECU is the
white wire at the coil that should be connected to
terminal #1 which is where the wire from points /
condensor is connected. The black wire (12V+) is
connected to term #15 coil.

Anything else? -BaH
