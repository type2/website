Subject: CDI installed... almost works!

I spent today installing the CDI system that I bought from ACN.  Most 
of the day was spent rerouting all of the related wiring to a set of 
terminal blocks mounted on the firewall (I will post photos once I've 
written up the text to go with them).

I verified the engine runs OK with the rerouted wiring in the stock 
configuration (no CDI) with the new Compufire high-output coil I got as 
part of the setup.

Removed the "stock configuration" jumpers from the terminal blocks, and 
wired up the Hyfire to the terminal blocks (see attached wiring 
description and terminal block diagram).  Hyfire is grounded on the 
body, and has a 14 ga. power lead running from the starting battery.  
Set the Hyfire configuration switches to "4 cylinder mode" (1=on, 
2=off).

Still have stock rotor, wires, and plugs (I'm putting the pieces in one 
at a time and verifying proper operation at each step).

Had my wife turn the key, Hyfire blinks its LED as it finishes its 
self-test.  So, I have her crank it over.  Engine fails to catch and 
run.

Pulled coil wire from distributor, and held about 1/2'' away from coil 
clamp bolt, had my wife crank it over.  Verified I have spark (WOAH BOY 
do I ever have spark).

So, I think the problem is that the FI is not triggering properly.  I 
do have the Mallory tach adapter installed.  The tach adapter connects 
to switched +12v and the white "from points" wire on the Hyfire (the 
latter was determined as the correct connection by reading Mallory's 
on-line knowledge base; the instructions included with the tach adapter 
are for the Hyfire 4, not the Hyfire 6).

My FI trigger is connected to the yellow "to tach" wire on the Hyfire 
(along with my VDO tach).  I ran out of daylight, so I haven't hooked 
up a test light yet to see if the tach output from the Hyfire is 
working OK.  The manual says it produces a square wave, +12v, 20% duty 
cycle.

Has anyone else on the list hooked up a Hyfire with a stock FI system?  
If so, could you please tell me how you handled the FI trigger wire?  
Specifically, did you connect it to the Hyfire's white "points" wire or 
the yellow "tach" wire?  Did you use the tach adapter?  If so, what 
Hyfire wire did you connect it to?  Did you have to use the diode 
included with the adapter?

I am planning on documenting the whole thing so hopefully no one else 
will have to ask these questions :-)
