Chris:  A suggestion, but not an easy one to follow:  The high beams on my
'71 Westy would randomly go on during a drive.  In frustration, I wiggled
the cable bundle that runs into the steering column and found that the high
beams would go on  or off.

A look at the circuit diagram and reading the archives led to an
understanding. The high beams are toggled on or off when a certain wire
leading into the relay is grounded.  Touch that wire to ground once  -
high beams go on and stay on.  Touch the wire a second time  -  the high
beams go off.  No need to hold the wire to ground, just touch it. That's
the way the relay works.

I ended up moving the steering column away from the dashboard,
disconnecting the turn signal switch, following each wire by color code,
and sure enough the brown wire mentioned above had a bare spot where it had
been rubbing on the metal brace.  I covered it with heat shrinkable tubing
and cured the problem.

Not an easy job.  Not even the only reason for intermittent high beam
problem. But something to try.
