Most compression testers have a long rubber hose with
a screw-on end that stays tight. On the end will be an
0-ring to seal against the head. Hand tight is fine.
This Harbor Freight set-up works very well for the
home mechanic: 
http://www.harborfreight_dot_com/cpi/ctaf/Displayitem.taf?itemnumber=39224
The only drawback is the way the long hose is stored.
The hose needs to be laid out in the sun before you
use it to get the bend out of it. With the bend, you
have a tough time starting the threads on #2 and #4.
