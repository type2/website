George, I forgot the step about undoing the PO's efforts...  It is very
important that the steering box be centered.  Again, I should have been
more explicit :-( 

More explicit step:

After you set the toe, with the drag link disconnected, you should turn
the steering wheel back and forth until the steering box is in the center
of it range of motion.  The steering wheel *should* be level and upright
at that point, but it may not be.  Now is the time to re-level it if need
be (see below). When you have the wheel off, make sure that the cancelling
ring for the turn signals is in the right orientation.  I have had buses
where the ring/wheel was installed 180 degrees out. With the steering box
centered and after the wheel has been set level, adjust the length of the
drag link to reach the pitman arm and fasten it in place.

Note that if your wheel is not level, you can relevel it at any time,
*BUT* the way to do it is to set the steering box to neutral and level the
wheel.  Please do not randomly level it to cover up a misaligned front
end!  If the steering box is centered and the wheel is level, but the car
steers funny, then your front end is out of alignment!

Removing/replacing the steering wheel:  
There is a divide between early and late steering columns.  I *think* the
change occurred in ?74?  If you have the early column, the splines have a
slight taper to them and the wheel will need to be pulled/popped off.  If
you have the late column, the wheel should lift off.

The way I have always interpreted these figures is that I want the front
of each wheel to be in between 0 and 1/16" *from the centerline of the
bus*.  Allocate half of the toe to each wheel.  The way I did that was to
set the left front wheel to 1/32" in (actually, we went for a hair less,
but once you get to 64ths and 128ths of an inch, you stop calling out for
precision and going "a hair more in/out" :-) then adjust the tie rod on
the right front side to a total toe in of 1/16".
