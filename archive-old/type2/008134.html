It's not a washer, but a speed-nut.  This is a thin piece of metal
that has a hole slightly smaller than the rod with cuts radiating out
from the hole part way to the edge of the washer.  the cuts allow the
speed-nut to be forced over the rod in one direction but make it very
hard to remove in the other direction.  You can probably find suitable
speed-nuts at any well-equipped hardware store.  Take your rod with
you to figure out the correct size.  Don't push them on, though, as
you can't get them off again intact!

My rod is 8 1/2 inches long from the centerline of the bent end to the
threaded end.  The rod has a couple of bends in it, which means that
it is shorter than it would be if it were completely straight.  Yours
looks straighter than mine.

The normal problem is the opposite of yours.  If the fan shroud is
installed incorrectly it won't seat all the way down, resulting in
closed flaps or the inability to screw the thermostat onto the rod.

Perhaps your rod is missing a couple of kinks?
