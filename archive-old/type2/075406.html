Robert S. Whitaker sends:

> Head gaskets? . . What head gaskets? There should be no head gaskets. As for
> the long bolts. . . You must mean the cylinder studs, they do not get
> torqued at all, just snugged, but the nuts that hold the head on do get
> torqued. Torque the head nuts when the engine is cool and only once. Over
> torquing can pull the studs from the engine case when the cylinders heat up.

Only once? A lot of folks like to put the heads on, put on the head nuts,
then torque in stages. Take it to 10 lb-ft, let it sit for 20 minutes or so,
go to 15 lb-ft, let sit for 20 minutes, go to final torque, let sit for 20
minutes, then check the torque on the head nuts one final time and leave it.
This gives the engine time to accept the fact that it's going to have heads
on it whether it likes them or not.
