Anthony--

Actually the run remover is nthing more than a small piece of a body
file held into a piece of nylon block ( 2" x 2" ) by a magnet--and the
file is reversable--

You have to wait until the paint is bone dry---and in a small bucket of
water--add copious amounts of Dawn dishwashing detergent--to make the
water really slippery--
Then in small circular motions run this small file over the run---lots
of water, lots of soap---and go real easy---

Sometimes it works--sometimes it doesn't--

I don't use it anymore, because sometimes it snags on the run and will
gouge the paint down to the primer---and it will not work on metallic
paint at all---
It'll leave a zebra stripe where you have removed the run--

I now use my palm DA with 1500, and 2000 paper on it---get it almost all
the way out---and then block the rest of it with 2000--
Finesse the area--and your done.
The finished product looks much better than shaving it out, with the run
remover--

This can be found at any better body shop supply store--
