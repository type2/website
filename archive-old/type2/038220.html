Pat, sounds fine.  I run REAL ugly CV's all the time.  Falling out of the
cage is not to be worried about, minor wear that catches a finger on the
races is fine, I begin to draw the line at ugly smeared galled metal, but
even then just usualy reverse their direction.  I don't typically toss a CV
till it has wear on the balls, or ugly galling on more than 2 tracks on either
inner or outer race, or is actually making noise.

That is me, I've run CV's that would terrify most for 50k beyond ugly before
I broke down to replace them.  But I am prepared to change one in the dark
on the road, I always carry a greased spare.  They are relatively cheap from
Bus Depot, and they will stop you cold.  A little bright dip in a couple of
tracks
on a race is NO reason to change them.  Reversing them is a great trick, axle
to axle, changes the contact path that is being worn, I do that every time I
regrease a set, maybe every 25k or so.
