Subject: Re: [T2] Belt-eating crank pulley

Those pulleys can be a bitch to get off. I recently bent one into a banana 
shape with my vw crankshaft pulley puller, ruined the pulley and the puller, 
and it was still there. Mark Dearing suggested using a chisel.  Remove the 
bolt and washer, hit the central inside part of the pulley with the long 
chisel, right on top of the Woodruff key(that part is important). Not so 
hard that you damage anything, lots of little hits. It worked. Avoid hitting 
the cases or prying on them. That'd probably bitch what's left of the 
pulley, but it's not great anyway, right?
I bought a new Bruck pulley from CIP-it's nice.
