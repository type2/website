The EGR port is a perfect place to add another CSV.
I wired 2 variable resistors (potentiometers) into the
temp sensor circuit. I experimented with that quite
extensively. One problem I had was turning the knob -
when exiting from the freeway I couldn't turn it fast
enough, and when cranking back up I never knew exactly
where I was until after a few miles of driving. The
second pot was for tuning in a baseline which changed
from season to season. Eventually I used a slide pot
and from then on it was all so easy. With the slide
pot (like a balance lever on a stereo - but linear -
stereos use a different "audio taper") I never had to
look at it. I was able to just reach down and adjust
it totally by feel. I had it tuned so normal freeway
driving it was just past half way.

I also used a CB mixture gauge with an oxygen sensor
mounted in the #3 cylinder header pipe about 2" from
the exhaust port. (another complete chapter on that
alone!) ...and of course I had the quad CHT gauge
which I strongly believe should be a basic gauge on
any aircooled VW FI engine.

I had the slide pot and the CSV button where I could
easily thumb the button while adjusting the slide pot
- usually to full rich while I had my right foot to
the floor. Speeds of -the speedometer needle hitting
the zero stop after going past bottom! - were fairly
easily attained.
