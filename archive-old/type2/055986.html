Bob and the list,

I have pictures up on my web page showing the setup with the Thule Roof Rack
& Canoe (see bottom of post).

This setup is perfect for day trips if you do not want to pop the top.

If overnighting and need the top up, the front portion must be removed.
This is accomplished by unscrewing the knobs at the base of each riser then
lifting risers and cross bar off as one unit, re-installation is the
reverse.  I have found that when tightening the knobs it is a good idea to
pull the riser base away from the bus to get it to the outside of the rain
gutter.  This lessens the likelihood of having to re-tighten the knobs down
the road.

Thule does not endorse this setup, the only reason I can think of is that
the rain gutters on a Type 2 are weak.....

....but....

I have had the Canoe on top while driving on some pretty rough dirt roads
with lots of rocking from side to side and the rack never moves.  Haven't
had to slam on the brakes really hard yet though....

The Westy roof rack can still be used for low profile items like lawn chairs
and firewood with a canoe on top.  I plan on getting one more set of risers
and a bar so that I can put two bikes on either side of the canoe.  Prolly
not until next year though.....

I refuse to modify or butcher the Fiberglass Pop Top in any way.
