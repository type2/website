Since you have a 78 bus and that muffler is from a type 4 engine without a catalytic converter.....  do you
still have fuel injection on it?

If yes, did you put hose clamps on all the hoses that connect TO the big S hose and TO the decel valve?

If no, then they probably all blew off like they should when you have a backfire.

If yes, then you probably destroyed your AFM. The backfire comes back thru the air vane and bends it. Someone
a while had a picture of his AFM where the force of the backfire blew the AFM apart, broke the rivet holding
it together!

Yes, a spark at wrong time will cause this. If a exhaust valve is open it blows out the exhaust, if an intake
valve is open it blows thru the carb/FI.
This is why you never put hose clamps on the air/vacuum hoses except on the ends of the big S hose.
