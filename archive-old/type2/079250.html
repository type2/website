> They're not actually eliminating all

> the Bus.  This was not exactly unforseen.  When

> unlikely that they would continue Bus
> production along side of it.  Every
> consumer product has a life cycle, and
> certainly the Kombi has had a longer
> one than almost any other vehicle.  Ben Pon
> should be proud that his modest
> design continued production straight into the
> 21st centrury.
> 
> As for what it mean to us:  Short term,
> nothing.  For some time, replacement
> parts will continue to be made, to support
> those busses that were recently
> sold. When VW discontinued the German bus in
> 1979, it was several years
> later before they began obsoleting parts for
> it. For a number of years you
> could still get most Bus parts at your VW
> dealer (if, of course, you were
> willing to pay the price).  However, as was the
> case with bus parts here,
> eventually VW/Brazil will of course stop
> supplying replacement parts for a
> discontinued vehicle.  So in the long run, it
> will mean that some parts will
> either become unavailable, or available only in
> the aftermarket.
