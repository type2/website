On Thu, 2 Aug 2001, Roger Lemberg wrote:

> On a type 2 engine, how does oil circulate to the rocker arms?
>
> My best guess is that the excess flows back to the crankcase through the
> push rod tubes via gravity. Correct?

Correct!

> But how does it get out there and how does it get into the rocker arm
> bearings where I would guess it's most needed?

Oil is (a) pumped to the lifters, after which it is splashed out to the
heads and (b) oil is flung by the crank spinning thru the oil in the sump.
There is no pressurized delivery system to the rockers, just brute
violence done to innocent oil molecules :-)
