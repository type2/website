On Fri, 30 Jan 2004, Harry Hoffman wrote:

> The steering on my bus is fairly loose, the steering wheel turns ~ 4-5 inches
> before the wheels actually start turning.

> Before adjusting the steering box is there anything else I should be doing?
> Should I also replace the steering dampner? How does one know if it's shot?
> Is there anything else that would make the steering so loose?

If the steering box is shot, it will probably feel "crunchy" as you
turn the wheel, particularly at the center position.

Before you adjust with the steering box, check the drag link between the
steering box and the swing lever.  The ball joints in this link can and do
go bad.  Have someone wiggle the steering wheel while you observe the rod
ends.

Fiddle with the steering box as a last resort only.  More damage has been
done by well-meaning adjustments than by wear!
