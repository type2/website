On Mon, 3 Jul 2000, Mike Soultanian wrote:

> I got a question that's been buggin the hell outta me on my 76fi.  What
> I am trying to figure out is how the computer knows which cylinder is
> going to fire and then know to open that cylinder's injector.  I know
> that it's getting it's timing from the points, but the points don't tell
> it which cylinder is firing, just when.
> 
> So, do all the injectors open on every cycle of the points opening and
> closing or is there actually some way that the computer is able to know
> which cylinder is going to fire a particular time, therefore opening
> that respective injector.

The injector doesn't synch with the cylinders.  The open/closed time ratio
is varied to set the mixture by vibrating the valve open and closed, but
the fuel simply accumulates in the manifold until the intake valve opens.
