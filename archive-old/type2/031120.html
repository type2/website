early pancakes (TypeI) are pretty much the same, the later TypeIV engines
are very different, especially the Fuel Injected ones.   HOWEVER many
folks myself included think the improvements and advantages far outweigh
the disadvantages.

one of the problems is that the TypeIV is that it is larger and heavier
than the little type I engine.   that translates however into more power
and torque so it is worth the hassle.

I have a 78 with auto tranny and 2 liter FI engine.....I used to be able
to take my 60 engine out in an hour or so, alone.  with this engine I give
myself 2-3 hours with a helper for actually pulling it.   BUT I would
never go back to that little anemic engine, but mine is a daily driver
too, and much more can be done with the engine in the car than I could
with the older one.  makes it easier if the first time you pull it that
you put in some quick release connectors.   I don't think it is much
harder to work on, Just Different.
