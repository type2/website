> Hello Tim,

> I recently replaced the window scraper on one side and hated the job so
much
> I couldn't get myself to doing the other side. You need to strip the door
> *completely*, including glass, vent wing, and even the felt channel. You
> will also find that the aftermarket window scraper is of disappointing
> quality. The chrome trim will be bent from shipping and the scraper is
> actually too short, leaving a 1/2" gap for water to enter! I ended up
> reusing my old chrome trim, drilling out the rivets that hold the old
> scraper and for lack of anything better, riveting on the new --though too
> short-- scraper.
>
> But... I don't think the bad scraper is actually the reason you're getting
> water into the bus; some water will get into the door even with a good
> scraper. What you are probably missing is the plastic foil behind the
> carboard/vinyl panel, which is then tucked into the door to deflect water
> towards the outer skin and away from the cardboard. The water will then
> drain through the drain holes in the bottom of the door (as it is supposed
> to).
