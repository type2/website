You didn't say whether it's aircooled or w/c, so I'll assume a/c. As 
a comparison, there is currently an '80 Westy for sale locally for 
$5500. This is in CDN $, and also in an area where VWs command a high 
price, so subtract 35-40% for exchange rate, and then some. I'd be 
*very* cautious of a dealer who offers take off the repair cost, unless they
are a VW dealer, and know for sure what the problem is.
$800 doesn't go very far if you're paying for labour. By the number 
of small things you mention that are broken, it also sounds like it 
may not be in such good shape after all. The Westfalia interior is 
extremely durable, so is often not a good indication of overall 
condition. It's also easy to get excited by all the cool stuff in a 
Westy and overlook some of the problems it may have. (speaking from 
experience here.... ;)

Good Luck!
