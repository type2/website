Those transmissions can be rebuilt, just takes know-how.  I'm told
by reputable sources that it takes rebuilding several before you
actually get one right. 

Up to sometime in mid-'59, busses used a split case trans.

Not much can really happen to reduction box gears, since they are
pretty much locked together.  Bearings go bad, which are not all
that expensive.  The only thing you can do wrong is run out of
fluid.

If you have a "split-case bus" like my '56, you can get an adapter 
put in a later transmission, otherwise known as a "tunnel case."
