I'm a little behind on posting results of my trying to organize
the August 25-27th campout, and I'll try to do something more in-depth
soon, but just wanted to let you all know it's definitely happening.

With the amount of vehicles we are expecting, we will fit into
the campground that has been recommended to us on BLM land at
Kings Canyon, just west of Garberville.  I'll post directions
to it soon.  The name of the campground is Wailaki (pronounced
why-locki), and is at the southern end of this reserve.

The place is pretty primitive - they have I think some pit
toilets, but is not the type of place with hook-ups, ac, etc., so
truck it in!  Also, I"m not sure that civilization is going to
be all that accessible with the type of roads we may have to go
in on, so check to make sure your car is in order, tires good,
bring gas, bring lots of water, food, propane, whatever you need, etc.

Here's a suggested packing list:

CB radios (channel 19 please)
map/directions
reservation/confirmation/number info or tix
beer/NO2/whatever
firewood (you can sometimes buy this on location but at double cost)
the weirdest object you can thing of to burn/explode
folding chairs (1 per person in your group, plus a few extras for the 				chairless or the forgetful) to sit around and watch the explosion
sun glasses to reduce the magnesium glare
fire extinguisher to try (ha!) and put out the fires we start
ice
extra batteries
cords/adapters as necessary
heaters/coolers
BBQ stuff if you need it - grill, stove, etc.  
food (please supply your own 						containers/pots/utensils/servers, etc.)
LOTS of paper plates, cups, towels, etc.
games/sports/hobby equipment/gadgets
sunscreen
extra gas
extra water
Tarps and bungee cords
light/cook sources:  candles, lanterns, stoves, ovens whatever, plus their fuel
music/guitars/harmonica/keyboards etc.
Parts and tools
First Aid emergency kit
weather gear
Camera, film, video camer, batteries, panoramic camera
Bedding/tents
Autoclub, health insurance, and credit cards plus emergency $$
Proof of insurance, ownership, and registration
Health certificate for pets
cash/currency
snacks:  popcorn, appetizers, nuts, smores, prunes, rhubarb, etc.
duct tape - just don't advertise what you do with it
something to feed the resident dog to try and test their "tolerance"
