Now, to add my own statistic:

I have a *friend* with a westy that's been parked in a barn since 1962
with a small stack of firewood in the back.  Unbeknownst to him, a small
screw hole in the roof let a little moisture drip down into the bus, onto
the wood while at the same time a very slight opening in the vent-wing
allowed a sparrow to stop by the wood pile, bringing in a couple of
spores. So, my friend goes out to check on his beloved 1957 westy (reputed
to be the first-ever, experimental westy) and discovers, much to his
chagrin, a small pod of mushrooms emerging from the well-seasoned wood in
the back of his westy. He opens the rear cargo door, and tosses out the
wood, not paying much attention to the mushrooms. 

Right about that time, I arrived to help him start the restoration
...bringing him a vegemite sandwich. Without even thinking, he wolfs it
down straightaway. It was so good that he even licks his hands,
*unwittingly* ingesting a few spores.

As the restoration work begins, he crawls into the westy and begins a
perilous journey through the ether, into the land of sdrawkcab
gnilleps, over the trees of hysteria, on past Jupiter and the land of
knarly bigszwots and back through a hole in the Sea of Japan, logging
roughly 16 million miles in about four hours. ...4 million miles per hour
in a westy.

Of course, when he returned, the westy had somehow been painted with
pretty impressive hemp graphics...and being that Arkansas is a drug free
state, now he can't drive it, even though compression checks out at 124
on all four cylinders and the restoration was somehow completed in the
four hour time span.

My friend indicates that his travels have much enlightened him and he's
thinking of moving himself and his westy to Canada.
