Isn't there a safety device in there by which if the flame goes out then
the gas supply is cut? Thought I read that in the manual.

This doesn't mean I advocate using the propane when driving: my fireman
friend told me that the two most dangerous things are smoking in bed and
propane. Evidently propane is heavier than air, so it pools (e.g. in your
basement) until it finds a flame (e.g. the hot water heater pilot light). I
went home and moved my propane tank (barbecue) from basement to garage.

I have seen a propane barbecue tank catch fire: it's very much like the 2nd
world war flame throwers without the 'off' switch.

It seems to me there's a worst-case-scenario here that's far deadlier than
is the pleasure of a slightly colder beer.
