It was interesting to monitor oil temperature during my recent trip to the 
Canadian Rockies. In a nutshell, what I observed was that oil temp would 
stay relatively stable at 190 when I was on a flat highway driving under 65 
mph. However, with sustained climbing in a lower gear, especially 2nd, temp 
would quickly shoot up to 230 at which point I would pull over and give her 
a rest. Also, once I made it up a pass and leveled out or headed down, oil 
temp would drop quickly.

-Mark McCulley
