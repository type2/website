Subject: A Visit to the Emissions Station

Hi folks,

Well, I paid my annual visit to my FLETS (Friendly Local Emissions Testing 
Station) today.  The 71 passed with flying colors again, but there were a 
few moments of confusion that lengthened my visit.  It seems my bus was 
registered as a camper, but the computer has it listed as a transporter.  
This caused some delay as the testers tried to figure out if there was an 
error.  "Is this titled as a transporter?" the tester queried.  "Probably," 
I said, "VW vans have a lot of names."  "Yeah, that's a hippy bus" he 
smiled and said.  So I was given the green flag to pull up to the test 
location.

After a minute passed the tester comes to the window and tells me that one 
of my tailpipes looks like it's not working.  "That's the exhaust for my 
gasoline burning heater."  So he says "You mean this is a diesel?"  "No, 
it's a heater that runs on gasoline, the engine burns gasoline too."  "Oh."

After another delay he returns to ask if the tailpipes are a dual exhaust 
system or if they come off the same muffler (I'm using a bug muffler). I 
tell him it's just one muffler.  He asks me if I'm sure cause the test 
procedure would be a little different.  Yep, I'm sure.

So I pass and as I'm paying up he tells me that the bus looks good for a 
thirty five year old vehicle.  35? hmmm.  Yes, thanks...  And out of the 
bay I rolled, feeling like an alien who just brought his flying saucer in 
for an emissions test.  We come in peace!...
