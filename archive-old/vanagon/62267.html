> I know there's someone out there with a handy dandy formula for calculating
> top end for different gear ratio's and rear ends. This is for my `82 Westy
> Diesel. Anyone?

I though this one out a while back basiclly speed in mph

speed = (desired RPM * 60) * (circum  tire in miles)
              ----------------------------------------------
                     (gear ratio) * (final ratio)

so say at 3500 RPM for a diesel with a 5.43 final and in 4th (.87?)

speed = (3500*60*.00127)/(5.43*.87)=56.5 mph

oh circumference of tire in miles

circ (in) = [   (width * percent sidewall series * 2)  +  wheel size  ]  
                  ------------------------        
                              25.4 

circ (miles) = (circ (in)) * Pi
                     -----------------
                       (63360)

or for a 185 (80) R 14

circ (in) = (185 * .80 * 2)/25.4  + 14  =  25.65 in
circ (miles) = 25.65 * 3.1416 / (63360) = .00127

These of course are theoretical estimates, actual tire size will vary 
by brand, I think these formulas are roughly correct but can not be 
held responsible for my own idiotic mistakes.

Attached is a 123 sheet that calculates for different RPM's and gear 
ratios speed at RPM.  The ratios are across the top of the columns, 
final ration on top, tire size on top right.
