Living in Washington, I am glad our more-populated counties now have 
mandatory smog inspections.  Smog is composed of three essential 
ingredients:  Hydrocarbons, Nitrogens & Oxides, and sunlight.  Sunlight 
accelerates the intensity of smog.  We don't see much smog in Washington, 
except a minimal amount toward the end of summer.  We don't have much 
sun, so smog levels stay low.  However, with more and more people moving into
 this state, the smog would gradually become more of a problem even without a 
large amount of sunlight.
