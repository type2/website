You'd be much better off with an automatic-feed wire welder.  More
expensive, but rentable.  I've done a lot of welding with a smoke wrench,
cause that's what I have, and it's a PAIN to weld sheet metal, because the
lower operating temperatures for oxy-acetylene than spark wrenches causes
sheet metal to warp.  You can compensate for this by "hammer welding" but
that requires a lot of practice (I'm pretty good with the 'wrench, but
still haven't gotten THAT down too well).
