Timothy D.F. Maddox writes:
> 
> Seems the word I've gotten is go for the deep cycle to run accessories. I
> haven't had a chance to do so yet but am also curious as to others
> experiences. I noticed that camping world sells a single battery that is two
> batteries in one, a deep cycle and high amp--for posts. This may be the way to
> go if I ever get another gas heater.

I wouldn't recommend buying any type of battery from Camping World.  You
will be paying 200% of retail.  There is no need for a separate "hi-amp"
battery - the deep cycle is just fine for starting gas heaters or any
other load you are likely to put on it in a camper.  If you insist on
running a welding rig from your batteries, you may need a "hi-amp"
battery.  :P

I would recommend getting a pair of 6V Golf Cart batteries to use as an
auxiliary power source.  They will put out more power and last longer
than a typical 12V deep cycle battery such as the Nautilus.
