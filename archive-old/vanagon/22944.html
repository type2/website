> 
> I was driving on a rainy day after it had been rainy all week.
> 
> Do you know the Bayview extension in Toronto?
> Down on the Don River flood plain?
> 
> Anyway, the lower end of it had become part of the river in places.
> As I was driving south, I saw a Ford Aeroscar stranded in a puddle
> (really about 18 inches deep, but a puddle to a VW  :) ).
> Well, I thought I'd show off.
> I pulled up beside her and asked if she wanted to be pulled out.
> She accepted the invite, so I tied a rope to her back end and 
> my van's back end and dragged her out the way she had come in from.
> 
> After untying her, I coiled the rope up on the floor to let it
> dry and was kind of mad at myself for stopping, because I didn't
> want the wet rope draining on onto the floor, getting things wet.
> 
> Anyway, off I went to cross the "great puddle" (she couldn't get
> her aerostar to aerostart, but I didn't care, I was already much 
> wetter than I wanted to be, And she was out of the river.
> 
> So I drive out to where she was, and continue across the water.
> The waterlevel gets just up to the floor boards, and I think to 
> myself, this is that last low area/dip, and I'm going to start going up 
> again soon.
> 
> Then I hit the low area/dip....  It's a lot deeper than I had expected.
> The water is right up to thetop of the wheel wells, and is pouring in
> around the sliding door...  And the engine stalls!
> 
> First thought, At least I made it further than the Ford.
> Second thought, No-one's going to pull me outta here, I'm
> the onlyone stupid enough to try somthing like this.
> Third thought, well, the wet rope doesn't matter anymore.
> 
> The water was about five inches deep over the floor.
> 
> So I put it into first and cranked the starter, and the 
> damn thing just kept cranking and cranking for the last 70-80 feet,
> and got me to a shallow spot.
> 
> I openned the door and let the water out faster, but I was stopped with 
> the right side higher than the left, so the water mostly pooled up near 
> the left wall.
> 
> I tried starting it again, but no luck, and I could hear that the 
> battery was getting tired.
> 
> So I go to the back of the van and get everthing off of the engine lid,
> piling it on the seat to kep it out of the water.  Open up the engine lid
> and take a look... Holy steam in the face Batman... That was stupid.
> Now all the windows are fogged up, but at least the Aerostar lady can't 
> see me.  I wipe out the inside of the dist. with my t-shirt, Not too 
> dry, but better than most alternatives.
> 
> Put the engine lid back down, splash my way back up to the front,
> Yes,  Vanagon comes through again... only three cyls. feel like they're 
> firing, maybe only two... but it's trying... I sit and rev it for a 
> minute in the hopes of recharging the batt incase it dies again...
> the remaining cyls kick in...  I'm gone!  Then the stuff on the back
> seat (sleeping bag, mini-compressor, engine lid carpet etc. falls
> into the water...  Ugh... that was stupid.
> 
> I drove home...  Last I looked the ford was still immobile.
> 
> Thank VW for clearance.
