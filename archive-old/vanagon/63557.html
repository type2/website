>To use it you specify rim diameter, tire cross section (185, 205, 195,
>etc), aspect ration (70, 75, etc.), max RPM to calculate, 1st through
>4th gearing and the final drive. The defaults on the gearing and final
>drive are accurate for my '71. Then clicking the calculate button you
>will get a table of speeds in each gear starting at 1000 RPM then
>increasing 250 RPM up to the maximum RPM requested.
>
>This was helpful for determining how different tire sizes impacted my
>cuising speeds. I have BFG 27x14 All-Terrains on my Westy and originally
>created this so that I could check the difference from the stock size.
