This would be fairly simple.  Most (if not all) major insurance companies
are public companies.  Look at their record of dividends... these are their
profits, divided up amongst their stockholders.  As insurance companies
don't have much in the way of R&D costs, the dividend multiplied by the
number of shares gives a fairy close approximation of their profit for the year.

(High-tech companies often don't pay dividends, since all of their profits
go into the next year's R&D.)

>From what I've heard, the insurance industries profits have been climbing at
