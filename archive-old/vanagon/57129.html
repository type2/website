Gasoline grades also vary among brands and with elevation.

In my '91 Westy, standard trans, I averaged 20-22 mpg on 87 octane. While
out west this summer in dry, higher elevation areas, I found that 'regular
unleaded' was only 85 octane. Being VERY CAREFUL not to purchase any gas
that was "blended", as many of the midrange octanes were, I found that I 
actually got 22-25 mpg on the 85 octane! And the engine purred. On the way
back east, you could see the octane of the "regular unleaded" go up as you 
lost elevation.

I ran "regular unleaded" no matter what the octane on the entire 8500 mi trip
with absolutely no problem. I plan to do the same in my "new" '91 Westy when
I get her.
