>The first step on the agenda for my new 73 bus is removing and welding the
>brackets on the front torsion tubes.
>
>Getting the front end out was been a little more complex than I
>anticipated.  As someone else mentioned, taking these things apart is a
>little like peeling an onion.  Fortunately - and suprisingly for a 20 year
>old vehicle -  nothing was impossibly rusted.  To get the torsion tube
>assembly out I had to disconnect the drag link to the steering arm, the
>shift rod linkages, the clutch cable, the master cylinder and the wheel
>calipers.  I also removed the rotors before dropping what remained of the
>front end.  In any case, a lot of this stuff needed overhauling anyway.
>For example, the shift rod busings are completely worn - the bus has that
>'omnishift' feeling.
>
>So now the front axle is sitting on my workmate.  I have removed the tie
>rod arms and 3 out of 4 ball joints.  The bolt on ball joint number 4 is
>very stubborn - I have to find a longer pipe!  My bentley manual has not
>yet arrived from JCW so I have a couple of questions for the experts:
>
>1.  How do I remove the sway bar?  The nuts came off OK, but the bolts are
>intractable.
>
>2.  How do I remove the torsion arms, bars, and bearings.  Muir indicates
>that the torsion arms should just slide out after loosening the set screws
>in the center of the torsion tubes and on the torsion arms.  I did this and
>(suprise!)  - no sliding.  Is it time for Mr. Sledge Hammer?  How about the
>needle bearings - do I need a special extractor to get these out?
>
>Thanks for any and all replies.
