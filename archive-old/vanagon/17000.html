> Dear Tracey,
> 
> There are many things that could be wrong here, but I think you should start
> with a thorough valve train check.  Make sure all the pushrods are properly
> seated in their followers within the engine and out at the rocker arms.
> Then go over the valves again very carefully, check for seized, or loose
> rockers, and anything else goofy.
> 
> Yes indeed, get a timing light, or at least a continuity tester so you can
> set and know where it is, very important over the life of your engine.  Was
> this a 1600 engine?  I thought '72 had a type IV.  If it was a 1600 you've
> raised the compression on your engine (with the 88's) and should be using
> premium and may need to retard your timing a pinch or something.  Us desktop
> detectives need more information here.
> 
> Rattles don't sound like they have anything to do with, like a wobbly fan or
> something?
> 
> Didn't forget something deep in the engine like piston pin keepers or
> something, eh?

> Shooting in the dark now,
