Batteries will charge at any where from 12 to 15.5 volts. But you are right
about the implications of higher charging. A way we used to condemn
batteries was to put a heavy charge to them and if they went above 15.5
volts the battery is suspect to being bad. If you use a maintenance free
battery you don't want to charge it at a higher rate because you could boil
out some of the acid. A 13.5 volt charge is good to keep the battery alive.
You can charge it at 14.5 volts but keep an eye on the fluid. HTH
