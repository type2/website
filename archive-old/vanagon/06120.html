 The rebuilders of air-cooled VWs usually refer to a short block as an engine
 without cylinders, pistons or heads. A long block includes those items but
 still doesn't have sheet metal, alternator,etc. A turnkey engine would be 
 the one that is complete with all accessories.

 Now, in the water cooled world things maybe different. If you were buying a
 short block for a Chevy, you would get pistons and rings installed, but no 
 heads. a long block would include heads.

 So, even after all this verbage, I don't know what you would get with a 
 wasserboxer short block, it's kind of in between. I think you can be certain,
 though, that a long block will not be a complete engine, but it would include
 heads.
