Although the 80-83 years are fine vans..you might reconsider looking at a '71
bus. That was the last year they made the bus with the type 1 engine but
it's the improved version (doghouse cooler, dual port) and you can increase
the engine size (I'm going to put in a 1776cc). These engines are simple,
very easy to work on, cheap to rebuild & easy to get parts for.  The '71
was the first year the bus came with DISK Brakes which is a vast improvement
over the drum years.  With four grand you could buy a '71 with a bad engine,
put in a new 1776 engine and give it a new paint job to boot...and you'd 
have a new looking & running bus. The only down side is yes...you do have to 
adjust the valves...up side...you DON'T have to adjust dual carbs.

Martin  (my 2cents worth) '71 owner
