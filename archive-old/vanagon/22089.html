     I have experienced many throttle (and clutch) cable breaks in my
     various VW's over the years. I have found that one of three things
     happen, and here were my solutions without replacing the cable on the
     road:

     1. The swedged-on "hook" end that goes into the pedal assembly pops
     off. Solution: Use a small pair of needle-nose Vise Grips to clamp on
     the end of the cable. Remove shoe from right foot and grasp Vise Grips
     between toes. A little painful, but works. This solution was used on
     Bugs. Type II's, this may not help much since the cable is "below
     deck"

     2.The other swedged-on end, at the carb falls off. Solution: Use one
     of those cheap "Throttle cable extenders" to temporarily replace it.
     This little gizmo sell for about $4 at VW shops.

     3. Cable breaks somewhere in the middle. Usually where it was rubbing
     against engine tin, due to improper bowden tube seating, or a missing
     guide tube. Solution: Profanity. Then, as Bob mentioned, bump that
     idle waaay up. I usually go to the end of the screw travel, which
     seems to get me almost 2500 rpm.

     I guess I could always carry a spare, but even then there is not
     always enough time to replace it on the road.
