Hi again,

Earlier today I inquired about engine substitutions for type 2s. Since
then, I received reports from people who actually did install Porsche 911
(six cylinder) engines. That's great news! So let me ask a few more
questions:

1. what about the transmission? Is the bus' gear ratio suitable? Can the
transmission also be replaced by the Porsche's 5 or 6-speed gear box?

2. Is the 6-cylinder engine significantly heavier than the original 4?
If so, should the shock absorbers be replaced by stronger ones?

3. I spent many years in Germany and heard (second-hand) about someone who
wanted to "upgrade" his old Ford by replacing the 4-cylinder (1.7liter)
engine with a V8. That attempt failed because he needed the approval of
the "technische Ueberpruefungsverein" (tuv), i.e., the inspection
bureaucracy. Not only would he have had to pay higher insurance
premiums, but they also demanded that he also put in bigger breaks. I
don't suppose there would be similar problems in the US? Do insurance
companies care? Does the EPA? (just kidding!)
