Subject: Vanagon Heat Vents - Update

Hi all,
Last week there was a little discussion about getting heat from the dash
vents (not the defroster vents) in Vanagons.

I stated that 87 & earlier vans could only produce non-heated air from these
vents, and that heated air from these vents was a feature that began with the
1988 models.

A couple of people wrote to prove me wrong.  I've gathered a little more info
and can say with a little certainty that this change was made in mid 1987.
 Here's what I know about some 87s and their dash heat

My (Jim Davis) 87 Syncro: VIN 007589, No heat, USA model
A friends 87 Wolfsburg: VIN 050426, No heat, USA model
Dave's 87 Wolfsburg: VIN 060438, Has HEAT, USA model
Tim Smith's 87: VIN 069457, Has HEAT, Canadian model

>From these four data points it appears that between #50426 and 60438 they
made a change to allow heat from these vents.

Any other 87 Vanagon owners want to add a few more data points to test this
hypothesis?  

If so, just send me the last 6 digits of your VIN, the country of original
sale and whether or not your 87 produces heat from the two vents on the face
of the dash.
