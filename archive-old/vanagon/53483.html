My experience has shown that blown head gaskets on these engines are almost
always caused by a case that is no good. The case is either warped or twisted
due to heat or the center bearing web wearing out or the cylinder seats are
worn out.  In either situation, the cylinders do not sit perfectly level so
the head will not seal properly for any lenght of time.  I learen this the
hard way a few years ago.  Place straight edge over cylinders before
installing head. Same edge should rest on both inner and outer walls of both
cylinder at same time.  If there is any clearance on one wall, block is
basically junk.  Proper repair includes machining case center flange,
cylinder seats, align bore etc.  Factory rebuilds excel in this area.  Hope
this helps.
