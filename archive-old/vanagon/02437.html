On Fri, 29 Jul 94 14:50:20 CDT Alistair Bell babbled:
>	Anybody have any opinions as to the effectiveness
>	of the vanagon chin spoiler?

i took mine off the 88 that i have now. it kept hitting curbs and things.
i don't think it hurt mpg any, because i still get an average 19.4 mpg
(as opposed to 18.6 on my old 1980 bus ... which did NOT have a spoiler).
i think it was just for looks (to match those bumpers and the side moldings).

>	And what about the the big pan that covers
>	the aux. gas/diesel heater under the van, do
>	you think it improves airflow under the brick?

one of my thoughts was to smooth in the bottom of the bus, to make it like
the beetle and the 911. but the size of the aluminum sheets and the noise
it would likely make, and what sort of fasteners (DZU's? expensive!), etc.,
etc., etc., just kinda made that a non-project. but from what i've seen of
the big 18-wheeler experimental trucks (trying to get better mpg), they
think it helps. even to putting fender skirts (which some of you might be
old enough to remember) on the rear wheels. what it would do to cooling of
the engine, transmission, and fuel pump, i don't know.
