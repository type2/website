Subject: I shift; bus don't go...

Coming home for lunch today, I turned off the major street onto my own, 
downshifted and released the clutch petal, only to hear a little clicking 
and feel a little coasting. Rev, Rev, Coast, Coast.

My bus has an uncanny knack for not leaving me stranded. I coasted to my
neighbor's house, where he was out in the yard listening to the
boom-a-boom-a-boom-boom-boom of his 8000 giga-amp stereo and I motioned in
Hillbilly sign language that maybe he could help me push, which he gladly 
did. I think we both have experience in pushing. He seemed to be very 
skilled; if there were an Olympic event, we might be contenders.

Other than installing a stereo like my neighbor's and turning the speakers
backwards, any ideas on what to check out first to get the bus going?

Note: I have had some pretty stiff shifting lately and in the absense of
any better advice, I'll probably start by examining the linkage near the
tranny--I've noticed this is very sloppy and replacement parts are very
near the top of my "things to buy for the bus" list. This may not be
fruitful, though, as the bus feels as though she's moving into gear, even
though she's not moving when I release the clutch.  She's never popped out
of gear before (in any gear), so I'm hoping this is something simple and 
inexpensive.

Any ideas greatly appreciated! Joe
