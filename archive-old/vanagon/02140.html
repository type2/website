]hmmmmm. the man has a point. :(  i guess it is true that while the transmisison
]end OUTER shell doesn't move much, the inner shell has to move with the axle
]and so, must match angle for angle with the wheel end (of the axle).

Cousin Billy from the Olde Country is correct.  The loads on the inner
and outer CV joints are virtually identical.  FWIW, the angles are
actually slightly greater for the inner ones than the outer because of
camber changes allowed by the semi-trailing arm, but the difference is
negligible.
