Semi-serious suggestion about bus cooling:  ICE makes aftermarket air
conditioning systems for VW vehicles.  Rather than adding the compressor
to a bus engine's already heavy load, mount the compressor on a lawn-mower
engine on the roof.

Incidentally I just talked to ICE about their Vanagon airconditioning.
About $1300, but a "difficult" installation involving relocating the radio
so the airco ducting can pass through the dash there.  At least you don't
have the "airco of Damocles" hanging over your head as on the VW system.

ICE system is set up for R134 refrigerant, supposedly more reliable than
VW, and I think has option of a second evaporator for the rear seats.

ICE is at 1 800 845 0424.  Don't tell them about the lawnmower engine idea --
they'll think Vgon people are wacky.  (But why shouldn't it work?  Set it
to run low but continuous -- probably get pretty decent "mileage" out of
it.)
