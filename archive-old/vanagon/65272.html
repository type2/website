If I recall this started after a tranny rebuild correct? When I had to
replace third gear in mine I remember there was a shim under the reverse
idle gear. The size of which required some math to calculate. If the
clearance is a bit on the tight side the gear will spin a bit longer
after pushing in the clutch. Since reverse is not synchronized it will
not match speeds with the main shaft and grind. Counting to ten gives it
time to stop spinning. In my case I do not think it is any thing to worry
about.  Two years later I only count to four. If you are still worried
run this past the fellow who rebuilt it.
