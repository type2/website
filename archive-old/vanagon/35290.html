I presume a few things in my answer:  the battery terminals are clean, 
the ground straps (2 of 'em) are in "good" shape, along with the positive 
cable, and that the battery is not too old or has one-too-many complete 
discharges under its proverbial belt.  

Now I go into uncharted waters (nope, I haven't done this yet):  Do you 
have a hygrometer?  Then you can test the individual cells.  Maybe one 
went bad.  

Your local battery shop should be able to do a load test on 
it (for free) and then determine its condition.  

Good luck!  Hope your bus gets back on the road soon.
