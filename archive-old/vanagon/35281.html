> 	I am still a greenhorn and I know just enough to screw myself and
> my van.  (Like reinstalling the thermostat only to find that it is not
> contracting when cold, that is failed flaps closed.) I can not find
> anything in Bentley, Haynes or Muir about retorqueing the heads.  Is it
> possible to do this with out dropping the engine?  I can see getting
> access to the lower head nuts but the upper nuts are under the tin. 

It is possible, but it's a major pain.  You will have to remove the
upper tin pieces to get at the eight upper cylinder head bolts, and to
do that you will have to remove the fuel injection.  I have not worked
on a FI bus engine so I don't know how hard this is, but I'm sure it's
not easy.  I'd estimate you would pay a professional mechanic at least 6
hours of labor to retorque the heads.
