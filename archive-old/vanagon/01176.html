My '86 had all the same symptoms and it was simply that the radiator
was clogged.  Found a shop here in Raleigh that had the equipment to take
it apart and "rod it out" for $50 (I removed and reinstalled).  Now the temp 
gauge rarely climbs above the mid point.

Just take the hoses off and run a garden hose into the rad.  It should be
real easy to tell if this is the problem.  The flow through mine was
barely a trickle.

 >The O2 sensor seems a might pricey, ie dealer$, Disconnnecting it is the 
 >right first step, not great for your lungs however. Your water problem is 
 >due to the idiocy of VW putting the radiator cap at almost the lowest point 
 >in the circuit. What is happening is that even though the water level is OK 
 >when you check it parked wih the engine off there is an enormous air bubble 
 >inside your heater core up front, the high point of the circuit. Once the 
 >water pump starts spinning, it builds up enough pressure in the circuit to 
 >compress the air bubble and your coolant level at the pump drops down below 
 >the pump inlet level. Voila, no real pumping, temperature rockets up, you 
 >warp your heads and/or cook those big expensive head gaskets. It's all down 
 >hill after that! You can check for the air bubble by turning on the front 
 >heat. If the windshield air is warm/hot  no problem, but if it is cool (and 
 >floor air is hot) your heater core is half full of air and you need to bleed 
 >the system, see Bentley. Leaking head gaskets can also let air into the 
 >coolant system, aggravating the problem. Once  the pump starts leaking 
 >around its shaft the coolant level will drop below the pump inlet etc. etc.. 
 >This is what got me, and now, one pump, new gaskets on one side, it runs 
 >again, but the other side is dripping. An air cooled vanagon is tempting 
 >indeed.    tjs
