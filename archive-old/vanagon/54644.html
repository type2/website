Had a similar experience myself.

Last spring I was visiting my parents and my father commented that his Sears lawn tractor 
would not start.

I could not get any spark from the ignition, so I took the the tin surrounding the cylinder 
off to check the ignition.

I found that all of the spaced for air cooling the cylinder was filled with material from a 
mouse's nest, includig a dead mouse (it seems my father put some poison out last fall to 
keep mice out of the shed).

The engine wouldn't start because the mouse chewed through the ignition wire.

I figure my father was lucky that the mouse chewed through the wire, because he would have 
cooked the engine if it had started.
