Just did mine on my 86GL.  I used air tools so I wouldn't know about
modifying wrenches.  But, you will need to loosen the hose clamps on the
small pipe on the right in order to remove the pump.  My clamps were
facing in the wrong direction to access, ended up cutting them and
replacing the piece of hose. DON'T TIGHTEN ANYTHING UNTIL YOU HAVE IT
ALL TOGETHER.  I made that mistake and had a bitch of a time putting in
the last hex bolt on the larger flange.  Also, you don't have to remove
the AC but it will give you more room to work if you do.

Best of luck, you will scrape knuckles.
