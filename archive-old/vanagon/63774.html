In general terms a Westfalia is any VW van converted by Westfalia to be a
"Camper" (VW's term) by the addition of a pop-top and other features.  Some
Westies had the complete camping package including sink, stove, etc.  Others
were more basic models and had a pop-top but only a simple fridge and no
sink, stove, etc.  The Multivan is very similiar to the basic Campers, having
a pop-top and a cupboard, but is usually not called a Westy.

Your GL and Carat both had the "Weekender Package".  The "Weekender Package"
consists of a bed that folds down and combines with a cushion over the engine
to form a bed.  On the GL (depending on the year) the Weekender Package was
likely an option.  On the Carat it was standard.  The description I write
here is VW's terminology for Weekender Package.

Many people call the basic (non GL) Camper (Westfalia) a "Weekender".  This
is often confused with the Weekender Package as described above.  VW never
called any of it's Vanagons a "Weekender", but many owners have.
