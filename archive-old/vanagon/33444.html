If the '71 is a single carb then I might have a possible answer to the flat
spot problem.  The heat riser tubes that channel heat from the exhaust up
along the intake manifold (to preheat the gas/o2 mix) may be plugged up.
They can get filled with carbon from the exhaust and once that happens you
will get that awful, annoying flat spot.  You can try to unplug it but
you'll probably have to replace the whole manifold.  And...by the way...
this can happen over night, without warning.  You can test it by removing
the manifold and blowing into it. If air dosen't flow freely, it's plugged.

Another possibilty is the way you're trying to time the engine.  Try timing
it statically via Lord John Muir's book.
