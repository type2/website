I would definitely remove the tank - it's easy to do & makes things 
easier to get at. You only need to remove two of the four nuts 
holding the tank, then loosen the other two & drop it down. (after 
removing the two gas lines at the regulator of course). Not sure 
about recovery, since I did mine when it was empty. If you can get 
the right adapters, most of the gas could be transferred to a 20lb 
bbq tank. You can pour it in by holding one tank higher than the 
other.
