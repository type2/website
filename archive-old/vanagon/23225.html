> rebuilt engines. The question would be: what causes premature breakdown of

> simply luck of the draw? Are those with high mileage type IV engines doing
> anything special? It was mentioned earlier that the engine should not be

     Probably not.  Lugging isn't good, but shouldn't cause that -- more 
like wear on bearings than valves. Valves -- and valve seats burn from 
too lean a mixture, or air leaks causing a lean condition, or too short 
of an exhaust pipe (not a problem on street vehicles). Valve seats break 
however, because, primarily, they were put in badly or shoddy materials.
