> 
> Well one reason is that the viscosity extender additives, that keep the 
> oil from thinning under higher temps, aren't really very effective 
> lubricants (I think they're some sort of polymer), and in the extremely 
> wide range multi-vis oils can sufficiently dilute the oil to the point 
> they impair efficiency.  I've read some stuff to the effect that you 
> should avoid products such as 15W50, 10W40, etc. for this reason.  Of 
> course there's potentially a trade-off.    Operating out of the recommended 
> temp range also impairs lubrication:  Too hot, the oil thins and doesn't 
> coat adequately to protect;  too cool and the oil can't penetrate the 
> tighter clearances.  Personally, I find that with 4 changes per year 
> (3,000miles / 3 months) I can avoid using the extreme multi-vis products.
