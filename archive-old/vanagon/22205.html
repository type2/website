Joerg Rieger writes:
> 
> Does anybody know whether isolators actually connect both batteries when 
> starting (this seems to be one of the problems of relays and may not be 
> good for your alternator)?

What is this "not good for the alternator" stuff?  And who is the
mechanic that is willing to admit to saying it?  You cannot hurt your
alternator by connecting two batteries together, unless your alternator
is *between* them and you are "connecting" them physically with a vice.
Even then the alternator will probably be okay.  You cannot hurt your
alternator by hooking it to a dead battery.  You cannot hurt your
alternator by hooking it to a battery that is "too large".  An
alternator is merely a bunch of wire spinning around (inside of a
magnetic field).  It is very hard to hurt it.  If you're going to worry
about something, worry about your cylinder heads.  :)
