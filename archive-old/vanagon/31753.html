> Whats "specific heat?"

Specific heat is a measure of how much heat material can absorb.  For
instance, water has a relatively high specific heat (you have to apply a
lot of heat to it to raise its temperature) while something like wood has
a much lower specific heat.  This is probably expressed in units of BTU
or joules, as in the number required to heat 1 mole 1 degree Kelvin.

So, an oil with a lower specific heat will not be able to carry as much
heat away from the engine parts it comes into contact with, and
therefore the engine will run hotter.
