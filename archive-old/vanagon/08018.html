> They gave me the case. It has a small crack in the bellhousing above

Those old cases are magnesium, and they can't be repaired.  As they age,
the cases become weaker and weaker.  The best bet is to get a new one, so
you know that your motor has a long life ahead of it.  If you get a used
one, you will probably have no idea how many miles are on it, and it could
crack tomorrow.  That's one of the few benefits of the type IV motor - it
has an aluminum case and can be "welded, and welded again, and welded some
more!  Good thing too...".

All of the above courtesy of my favorite HP book, "How to Rebuild your Air
Cooled VW".
