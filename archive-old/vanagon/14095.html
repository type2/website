Subject: 1990 Truck Dependability Survey

I saw this in the Data section of the March 27, 1995 issue of Autoweek.
It's interesting that the Vanagon (I assume they're talking about Vanagons)
is a truck though not imported as so.
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
SURVEY SAYS TOP 5-YEAR-OLD TRUCKS ARE TOYOTA AND NISSAN

1990 Vehicle Dependability
Nameplate ranking of 1990 model year light trucks.

Nissan     109          [vertical bar graph here]
Toyota     109
Mitsubishi 103
Mazda      101
Ford        95
GMC         95
Chevrolet   92
Volkswagen  92

Source: 1995 Vehicle Dependability Study, Light Duty Trucks

Nissan and Toyota are tied for best overall truck line for dependability for
model-year 1990.  That's according to a new J.D. Power and Associates survey
of nearly 12,000 original owners of 5-year-old trucks.  As the chart above
shows, their score was 109, 17 above the industry average of 92.  Nameplates
below the industry average are (in alphabetical order) Chrysler, Dodge, Geo,
Isuzu, Jeep, Oldsmobile, Plymouth, Pontiac and Suzuki.  Land Rover was not
included because its sample size was too small.
