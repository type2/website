I bought a '67 Westy this summer with similar fiberglass
problems.  My intention is to sand all the PO paint off,
clean with solvent and apply several coats of fiberglass
resin without additional glass fibers, essentially a "gel
coat".  If the surface has deteriorated to the point that
fiber is showing through and mold is growing, all those
crevices need to be filled and sealed with something that
will adhere permanently.  That seems to me to be the
function of the gel coat of resin.  Once properly sealed, a
color-gel coat might work, or application of primer/color
coat paint would do.

Other opinions?
