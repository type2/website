The way I do it is that you install your new flywheel and then start
to install the engine until you can't go any further.  Then take a
socket wrench or cresent wrench and turn the engine over using the
crank pulley on the front of the engine.  Then remove the engine,
the flywheel has now scratched an area on the bellhousing of the
tranny and you can see where you need to clearance.  I use a dremel
moto tool but you can use a air grinder or electric grinder.  just
take it slow and don't grind through the bellhousing.  If I remember
right, there will be two areas on each side at the bottom and one
area up near the top on the starter side. Every tranny will be a
little different. Hope this helps.
