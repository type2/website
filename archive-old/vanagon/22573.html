I was just about to write a trip report.  After spending 4 wonderful
days around Acadia National Park and Maine in general,  I got back to
work on Wed., and am finally catching up with mail.

Due to a last minute surprise the '87 weekender served us, we had to
take the EV for the trip.  This meant having to buy an extra tent, and
sleeping in it rather than the quite comfortable bed of the weekender.
Oh well, the tent was not so bad.

When passenger side coolant hose that connects the cylinder head to
the plastic (yeah, Joel, you're right)pipe that goes all the way to
the radiator blew on the '87, there was less than 24 hrs to find the
part and put it in, among all other stuff I had to do, so, the EV got
the nod.  

In short, the EV eagerly swallowed the 1,450 miles we drove on (2x650
plus driving around in the region), got 20.5 mpg (with two bikes
strapped on a front carrier and speeds usually around 75 mph and the
stop-and-go yuppie traffic around Freeport (LL Bean Land), where my
wife insisted on stopping at least once :-(

During our stay there, we took to some of the many trails, the kids
tried their climbing skills on the rocks by the shore.  We went whale
watching once (not as great a show as saw a few years earlier, but it
still felt great to be on the ocean), and also did some shopping at
the local tourist traps.  

I'd like to thank to all of you who replied for the great bunch of
information -- also those of you who said 4 days were not enough were
right -:(  .  There was a sign on I-95 on the way back that read:
  "If your Business were in Maine, You'd be home by now"
Hmmm... maybe they have a point.  As things are, I think we'll try and
go back there again for a longer stay -- not that many
software/hardware/Telecomm companies in Maine yet :-(


This weekend we camp in PA -- Oh well, you take what you can get...

Have a nice weekend everyone....
Cetin




In response to the query, I think the only thing to check for is the
excessive "piston slap" problem some of the earlier EVs had.  This is
very noticeable when the engine is started from a cold state -- it
sounds like a diesel engine.  Other than that, the EV seems to be a
veryreliable, powerful and utilitarian vehicle at 33,000 miles.

Oh, also, in one of the problems you list
in question #2:  The rear A/C somehow failed in my EV also.  It was
replaced while under warranty.
