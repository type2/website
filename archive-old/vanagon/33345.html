> Also, I'd like to quiz fellow diesel owners on their personal cold start and

> light to go out, I use the cold start lever (which is in fact hooked to the

This is probably not your problem.  I once had problems starting the
Vanagon (diesel) in 15 degree F weather.  I finally noticed where it says
in the owner's manual "at outside temperatures below freezing -- depress
accelerator pedal while cranking and release pedal slowly as engine begins
to run."  I did that and continued to run the starter 30 seconds or so
until the engine fires on all four.  I used the cold start lever and let
the glow plugs go until the light went out.  This procedure really made a 
difference.
