i absolutely agree with this.  When i had Gus, a 59 panel converted to
riviera camper converted to 70's naugahyde custom, i had no gas gauge and
kept detailed records of fueling up so i wouldnt run out.  My average MPG was
~31, which i think largely reflected the gearing and type of driving i did.
  I once got 40 mpg,  while driving UP the Sierras.  i never could figure
that one out.    Then one day i suddenly got a measely 16mpg,  striking a
chord of terror through my heart.  When i got home i did a compression check
and found 65 in #3 (surprise! well, it had always been weak), the rest
110-120.   

Funny thing was that performance had not changed, and neither had the sound
of the motor.  All he needed was rebuilt heads, new rings, and i threw in rod
bearings for good luck.  :)
