One of the things I remember about R&T from when I read it in the mid-70s
was the Stan Mott cartoons. There was one where the pipe-smoking, capped
and scarfed character was driving along a country road in his extravagant
open sports car. I think there was about 2 pages of panels of the car
racing along, with the final panels depicting the airbag inflating (due to
some trivial reason), and the now dishevelled driver saying, "Thank goff
for Ralph Nader", or words to that effect. Those were the days when car
manufacturers were lobbying furiously against mandatory airbag laws.
A decade later and airbags are now a sales point.
R&T took a decidedly anti-bag position, I don't read the mag now, but I
wonder if they now approve of supplimental restraint systems ( ie.
"automatic" seat belts and airbags).
