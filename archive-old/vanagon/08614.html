> > ----------------------------------------------------------------------
> > I'm going to take a non-traditional and perhaps controversial position
>  on cylinder #3 over-heating. I don't think it has anything to do with
>  the location of the oil cooler. #3 seems to run hot on Type IVs, which have
>  the oil cooler adjacent to cylinder #4. It runs hot on doghouse cooled 
>  uprights where the oil cooler isn't blocking air to any cylinder. I think it
>  gets hot because it gets the least cooling air due to it's location. It's the
>  furthest from the fan, it's the farthest from the cooling air exit, it's just 
>  plain out-of-the-way, and the cooling air doesn't want to go up there on
>  it's way from the fan to the exit. Picture an upright fan engine from the 
>  left side: There's the exhaust side of the head up by the flywheel, the fan
>  is blowing air down between the cylinders, the exit is back at the rear of 
>  the engine. The "path of least resistance" definitly doesn't include an 
>  excursion up to the exhaust side of cylinder #3. Same thing for the type IV,
>  again, cylinder #3 is furthest forward, farthest from the fan. The cooling
>  air exit is also at the rear, requiring the longest - and least probable -
>  path for the cooling air to take. 
> 
>  Anyone else have some experience or thoughts on this? 
> 
> 
>  Dave Kautz
