>Very funny stuff.  Your list is as nerdy as any of the science
>ones I read.  My feeling is of course to throw it out since it is
>so cheap, and at best would taste a little stale  (if it has been
>opened).  If never opened, it is OK.  Forget the stuff about the
>mold that one person mentioned -- there is a great scare about a
>fungus which produces carcinogenic aflatoxins and which grows on the
>paper thin skin on peanuts.  These skins are removed for pnut butter
>production, and all large companies measure the resulting aflatoxin
>content in their processed product, and keep it at safe levels.  The
>worst that can happen, once the jar is opened is that O2 in the air
>will make the peanut oil rancid, and the butter taste bad.
>
>The post about stablilizers is also misleading:  these compounds are
>emulsifiers.  That is, they act like soap -- they do not allow the oil and
>water components to separate.  They do not "stablilize" the product
>in any other way, such as making it more resistant to spoilage.
