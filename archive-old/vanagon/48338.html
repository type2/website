You don't mention the mileage on the engine, or the severity of the 
lifter problem, so I'll assume the diagnosis is correct. If so, 
beware of rebuilds involving just rings & top end work. My uncle 
spent $2600 on new heads & rings on his '88, only to have the engine 
throw a rod 2 weeks later. Obviously the mechanic screwed up for it 
to throw a rod that quickly, but my feeling right from the startwas 
that he should have at least checked the bottom end, and probably 
replaced the bearings (it had 225k km on it). 
I'd find out how much more it would cost to do a proper rebuild with 
new main bearings, etc, since you will pay for most of the labour 
gettting the rings done.
