I've not been to Wailaki but I have been to the Kings Range/Lost Coast
area and its stunningly beautiful.  We drove the loop road from Ferndale
down to Petrolia and camped overnight at the Mattole Campground right on
the beach a mile or so above the Punta Gorda lighthouse.  Quiet, isolated
area, though there were several other campers scattered along the beach.
Took the Mattole River road out, via Honeydew, picking up 101 near Weott,
as I recall.  Roads are mixed pavement/gravel, narrow and sometimes
potholed, winding on occasion in a fairly precipitous fashion.  This,
coupled with the locals predisposition to drive at high speed, all over
the road, can make for some interesting moments.  The last few miles of
the road from Honeydew to 101, were like a fairy-tale;  winding through
the redwoods, over pine needles, the road so obscure we weren't sure at
times we were really on it.  The Petrolia area (and road from Cape
Mendocino, just north of it) reminds me of what I imagine the Big Sur area
looked like before Hwy 1 was pushed through (remember, this area is called
the "Lost Coast" because they COULDN'T get the road thru).  Haven't been
to the Shelter Cover area (the area Wailaki is in), but based on the
Tourist Info center in Garberville, its MUCH more developed (and
accessible) than the Petrolia section - modern beachfront condos and golf
courses!  According to my map Wailaki is a couple of miles inland, about
half way down the Shelter Cove road.  Based on my traverses of the Kings
Range just north, it'll probably be a beautiful, wooded valley.  Its a
gorgeous area in any case.  Let me know what you find.
