>When you want to check to see if your charging system is working, check for
>voltage at the battery.  It should be holding between 11.9  and 12.5 volts.
> Then start the engine and rev the motor very briefly to about 2000 RPM to
>set the alternator to charging, allow the engine to idle and check the
>voltage at the battery.  The voltage when running should definitely be
higher
>than when sitting.   The charging voltage would ideally be 13.8 volts.  The
>lowest tolerable charging voltage is 12.8 volts while all electricals are
>being used.  Then, Shut the engine off, and check battery voltage at the
>exact moment that the starter is in operation.  The voltage should not drop
>below 9 volts.  If your voltage is lower than that, completely charge the
>battery with a charger.  Do not attempt to diagnose a charging/starting
>problem with a low or "iffy" batttery.  It is like beating your head against
>a masonry wall.
