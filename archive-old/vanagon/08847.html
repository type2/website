In my owners manual, it says for long drives on hot days or long uphill
drives, that you should leave the engine running for a couple of minutes
before you shut it down after you stop.  I think this help eliminate
hot spots in the system by distributing the built up heat into the
passing coolant.  I'm not sure if this is correct, but I've had no
problems yet (knock on wood) at 59,000 miles.  I do 2 minute idle thing
alot and it's not a big deal.
