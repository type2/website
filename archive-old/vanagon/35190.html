One of the tricks my Grandad taught me was;
before doing a compression test,
1. loosen all plugs by half a turn
2. start the engine
3. switch off, remove plugs and then test
(remember to keep the throttle wide open when testing)

The reason for this? Especially in older engines there is a carbon 
buildup around the plugs. If you just remove the plugs the carbon falls 
into the cylinder and when you test, this carbon can get stuck under the 
valves and result in "strange" readings.

If you start the engine, this carbon gets forcibly ejected.

Try it and see if anything changes.
