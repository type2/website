I opted for some Toyo truck tires (185-75 R16) which are 8 ply rated
on my '86 Vanagon.  They're a bit skinny where the rubber meets the
road, but they seem to handle very well.  They're pumped up to 45psi
now, but I may drain a few lbs out of them to take some of the bumps
out of the road.  My only concern is traction in the dirt.  I plan to drive
it in Baja this Spring and I'd feel better with a wider footprint.

It had Kleber 185-75's on it previously and they seemed mushy in
comparison.  It now seems to be easier to turn, more firm around
corners, and feels more solid.

I believe I paid about $75 per tire at Winston for them, and they're
supposed to go about 40k miles.  Sounds like some shopping around
may find a better deal.
