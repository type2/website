Most shops cut the rotors due to the experience of having them warp form heat 
spots developing while the pads get seated to an uneven surface. Personally, 
with the current brake materials and the long life most brake systems give,  
rotors are really consumable items and should be treated as such.  Figure you 
60K on  a brake job, measure the rotors, if okay turn them, if not or 
marginal, replace.  From experience, there is nothing like a full brake job 
with new drums, rotors, pads, and cylinders or calipers when needed.

Dennis.

PS, Don't forget to replace the brake fluid.
