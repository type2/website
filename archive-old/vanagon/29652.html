>Note that it's a little tricky to figure out the exact TDC using this
>method, as there's some tolerance in the mechanical parts involved.
>Besides normal tolerance, the piston will sit effectively still for a

Here's an old Indian trick:

Turn the engine clockwise till the piston touches the screwdriver, and
make a mark on the pulley next to the crankcase crack. Then turn the engine
COUNTERclockwise (backward) until the piston once again touches the
screwdriver. Make another mark on the pulley.

Split the difference between the two marks, and that's TDC.

Detail stuff:
Obviously, you must have the screwdriver inserted a little farther than
exact TDC. Doesn't matter how much, as long as it's "a little deeper".
But you must be careful not to move it AT ALL between forward and
backward crankings. Again, obviously.

Try this several times, to make sure. When your split-the-difference mark
comes out in exactly the same place each time, you're there.
