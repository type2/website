	Martha,
	Your Vanagon camper doesn't need the 'warm-up' like a carbureted
vehicle from the 40s or 50s- Once you've got oil pressure, the best
way to warm it up is to drive, but keep the RPMs below 3000. When the
oil is 180F or above, you can use full revs.
	Sitting at idle isn't, per-se, bad for a modern engine, but
it isn't good either. It doesn't really warm much until its used. Better
to put a moderate load on the engine and get it warmed up quickly than
sit sloshing cold oil around.

	IMHO, natch.
