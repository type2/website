It's been a while since I saw it, but the Spruce Goose has eight
28-cylinder radial engines in it.  The inboard four engines have two
counter-rotating props on them.  I'm not sure whether the horses of these
engines is as much as in the B-29, but they were impressive to stand under
and look up at!  By the way, radial engines have to be built in odd-numbers
of cylinders, or groups thereof, so as to facilitate the reciprocating
motion.  The Spruce Goose's engines were in four banks of seven cylinders.
Imagine a valve job on one of them!!
