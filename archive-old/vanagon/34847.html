You need to remove the "housing" that the ignition switch is in, in order
to really work on this thing.  I'm not sure if "housing" is the 100% correct
term, but I'm referring to the black, somewhat triangular shaped piece that
the ignition switch mounts in and that the steering column passes through.
You removed the hidden screws, and I think that's all there is.  What you
are trying to do is separate the plastic piece with the wires coming out of
it from the rest of the ignition switch.  If there is another screw holding
it in place, though, it should be fairly obvious when looking at it.  Once
you've got the screws out that hold the plastic part of the switch in, you
just pull that part out.  There isn't much room, and the wires are kind of
in the way, but you can do it.  You don't need to get the rest of the switch
out or worry about the chrome ring.  Your problem is most likely in the
electrical portion you just removed.  (Was in my case, anyway, with similar
symptoms.)  I think someone posted instructions on how to remove the rest
of it, though, and that there is a COMPLETELY hidden button on the blind
side of the cylinder you have to press with a hook-shaped wire.
