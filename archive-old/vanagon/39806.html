Stroker cranks are available in about any stroke you want similiar
to the type I.  But be careful with the stroke because if you are
using stock rods, then the rod angle will be increased.  Are you
going to stay with the stock size pistons and cylinders?  Aftermarket
pistons and cylinders are very pricey.  

You can buy 96mm pistons and cylinders for about $300, then buy a
78mm crank, and that would be a nice 2258cc stump puller!

Most people tell me to stay away from Bernie Bergman's cranks.
I havn't tried one, but they say the quality is very poor.
Pauter machine has good cranks, and so does Scat.  Berg has
awsome cranks, but who can afford them?  Anyway, hope this helps.
