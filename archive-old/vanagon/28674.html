	Strength for wieght is about the same for aluminum and steel, you
get about three times the volume for wieght with aluminum, so for some
applications its better (airplanes) because the thickenss makes it stiffer.
You could make a 747 out of stainless steel, but the skin would be so
thin you'd have to add lots of extra stiffeners. But steel can be harder
than aluminum, so can be made springy, or ductile, over a wider range.
A physical chemist/metalurgist could tell us why.
