	On the subject of seam sealers, I've been working on new rain
gutters for my house, and having given up on soldering the 10' sections
together, I'm pop-riveting them and using plenty of "Aluminum Grey"
sealer goop. Comes in caulking gun tubes, medium grey color, just
about infinately sticky and seems to be butyl rubber and filler mixed 
with a mind-expanding solvent :).

	It seems to dry softer than the "Brushable Seam Sealer" I used
on my battery tray replacement, but that's $15/quart and the gutter
sealer is $3.59/tube. Its made to be applied to aluminum and galvanized
steel, and is probably easier to find. 

	It CAN be removed from fingers, tools, etc, using laquer thinner.
