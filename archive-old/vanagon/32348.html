I don't have a copy of my letter to LiMBO (a few years ago)
handy, but in it I described going to a VW dealer "garage
sale".  Take some of your worst visions of going to a garage
sale and apply that to watching the friendly local VW dealer
selling out.  Lots of tables with unidentified VW parts
going for a buck.  I assume it was the stuff they couldn't
send back for re-stocking, and a lot of used stuff, but I've
seen better selection at swap meets.  Sad.

Years ago, when the dealer was owned by "Hans" the VW dealer
was unquestionably the place to go to keep a VW alive, well
and beautiful.  What happened can be told easily enough by
the evolution of the name of the dealer:

University Volkswagen
University Volkswagen-Fiat
University Volkswagen-Lincoln-Mercury
Bemis Volkswagen-Lincoln-Mercury
Bemis Lincoln-Mercury-Mazda-Volkswagen
Bemis Auto World

Or something along those lines.  VW got lost in the ABC's of
alphabet soup, and so did the parts and service.  For
several years, instead of 3 miles, I had to go 30 miles to
the closest actual VW dealer.

Since then, the former service manager of University
Volkswagen started his own shop: "Chucks" and has recently
been "authorized" by VW to do something with VWs.  But it's
just not the same.
