Maybe water in the gas? Can happen overnight. When my vehicles act strange
for no readily apparent reason, and I feel it's not ignition-related,
I throw a bottle of gas line dryer in the tank. Costs about $1.50, hurts
nothing, and sometimes solves the problem.

Or maybe you got some bad gas, with lots of contaminants? Others on this
list have had good luck with Techron (from Chevron). It costs more, but
may be worth a try.

Funny, I just recommended this to another person on this list. Maybe
oddball weather causes a rash of these events? Or maybe it's my oddball
brain...
