I wouldn't dare post another timing question if I wasn't convinced that 
I've tried about everything. Obviously not *everything,* but at least 
everything practical for my budget.

My problem: the dreaded flat spot...but it's bad enough to interfere with 
my driving. I do have the 009/34PICT3 combination and I'm on the lookout 
for the proper vacuum advance carb. In the meantime, PLEASE, please, help 
me if you can. I'm getting worn out by this problem.

I did close off the hole in the lower plate in the carb (I think the 
lower one is the "throttle plate?"). This seemed to help with idle, but 
also may have exacerbated the flat spot??? I'm unsure...it's all blurring 
together.

My basic approach to date goes something like this:
1. Find TDC on #1. Verify rotor position.
2. Check dwell and set timing on 009 to 28 ATDC _at_ 3500 RPM
3. Adjust idle per Bentley. I corked off the vacuum line to the brake 
booster to assure no vacuum leak problems. I make sure the engine's nice 
and toasty.
4. Check timing again and adjust as necessary.

With this procedure, the flat spot is noticeable. I can fiddle around with
carb adjustment and lessen the flat spot, but I haven't even come close to
getting it to go away. The only time I didn't have "flat spot" problems
was with the carb adjusted to the point where the exhaust was smokey and
then I was probably getting 5 miles/gal and the bus wouldn't idle at a
stop sign...obviously not good. I thought I had the problem solved the
other day after blocking that hole in the lower throttle plate, but I
really only solved my poor idle problems. Once the bus warmed up, the flat
spot was worse than ever. 

I'm now contemplating taking the bus into a local shop and paying big
bucks to have them do the adjustment. I really don't want to do this, but
I'm feeling a little defeated by this problem.
