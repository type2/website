> I have 4 used cv joints I parted out from my old bus that check out fine (no
> blue balls, no pitting in the ball grooves or cracks in the cage).  The sad
> part is that I went through the trouble of storing them intact in four bags
> and after a recent move I noticed the joints are no longer assembled and I
> didn't mark anything (Dohhh!!).  This means I have no idea of the original
> orientation of the cage (not to mention ball placement).  If I use these as
> spares, how radically have I reduced their life by changing the wear pattern
> and disturbing the balance orientation??  Guesses?  Commiseration?  If I
> just kept my balls in their cage....

Ron, IMHO as long as they're in separate bags, you're probably okay. I 
think Bob Hoover's wise to suggest replacing the balls to the original 
slots, etc....but most other sources don't recommend that degree of 
precision.

The thing you might run into is getting the CV jammed by assembling it 
"backwards." If you do, there is a way to get it unjammed. I succeeded in 
unjamming one by strategically "punching" the center part out of the cage.
BUT<--- you have to determine exactly where to punch, or your efforts are 
pointless and if you got really carried away, using Thor's hammer or 
something, you could probably damage the CV.

If you're scared to try this, sell ME your spare CVs. ;) Joe
