Well, not inside a bus.

But in Meeneesoota, driving my '70 bug, I often engaged in the time-honored
practice (at least up there) of steering with the left hand while scraping
the ice off the windshield with the right. This created realistic-looking
"snowflakes" that fell all over me, passenger, map, book, and never melted
due to the absent rusted-out heater channels.

Well, OK, this was not actual weather as yours was-- I was just moving
the ice from one place to another.

Remember in the movie "Oh God", when John Denver got rained on inside an
AMC Pacer while the sun shone brightly outside?

Up in Thule, Greenland (halfway between the Arctic Circle and the North 
Pole),
a standard trick to show a newbie in Jan or Feb, was to get a cup of hot
coffee at the mess hall, walk outside, and pitch the coffee out of the cup,
up in the air while it was about 40 below. A huge burst of steam, a 
crackling
sound, and freeze-dried coffee fell all around you. Also, sometimes, the
empty mug would shatter in your hand. This tended to pi** off the cooks.
