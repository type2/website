Shawn,

Sounds like a lot of money for the '78 (what's the exchange rate, U.S. to
Canadian?).  However, '78 is regarded by many as the best year for
late-model buses (i.e., Baywindows).  It has hydraulic lifters, which means
never having to adjust the valves.  It's also got fuel injection, but so
does the '88.

The '88 has a lot of mileage (or kilometerage).  If you think late-model
buses ('72-'79) are expensive to rebuild, get a quote for a new ANYTHING for
an '88, especially a motor or tranny rebuild.  I don't know much about
transplanting a Jetta, Scirocco or whatever engine into it.  These
conversions can be really time-consuming.

Rust is a very important consideration.  Check the '78 really carefully,
especially the rocker panels along the side, front floorboard, wheel wells
and under the window seals.

Who rebuilt the engine?  Any warranty left?  How's the compression?
(Definitely run a compression test.)  How's the front end (e.g., steering
box, wheel bearings, ball joints, tie rods and ends)?  Bring up any
deficiencies of the bus; make the owner an offer considerably lower than the
asking price and dicker from there.
