2) I'm not completely sure I understand this question, but I'll give you an
answer.  Maybe it's the one you are looking for.  O2 is the chemical compound
name for the stuff (well the most important portion of the stuff) you suck into
your lungs at the rate of about 15 liters a minute.  O1 would be ozone, the
stuff that is created when fluorocarbons released from aerosol cans combine
with O2.  Ozone is much lighter than O2, that's why it rises way up there and
floats. Hence the much touted ozone layer.  So maybe in that sense it is not a
condensed version, it's the run of the mill oxygen that we all enjoy so much.
