>> A nagging problem I have had with my 81 Westy through several engine
>> replacements (see 81 Westfalia Blues, posting) has been the sticking
>> starter solonoid.  On long trips, the transfer of heat from the engine to
>> the starter housing seems to overheat the solonoid, causing it to fail to
>> engage.  The cure is to crawl under with a tire iron and rap on the case,
>> then cross the hot leads to force a revolution.  Then my baby usually
>> engages and starts.  Anyone else had this problem, and found a cure?  Also,
>> if you know of a good retrofit air conditioning compressor fit, I would be
>> grateful!  Last rebuild my boat anchor disappeared.
>
>I had this problem for years in my '79 bus as well.  What I eventually did was
>have an aftermarket Ford solenoid put on IN ADDITION to the VW one that is
>in there.  The advantage is that the Ford solenoid can be attached far 
>enough from the enging area that the transfer of heat is avoided while the
>VW is attached to the starter and so is limited to being next to the engine.
>It has worked great so far, but I've only had it on there for 2 months or
>so, so you might get a second opinion from some of the other list members.
