My fresh air fan switch caught on fire once.  I'd check the switch/wiring
first before replacing the fan.  The vanagons tend to fuse electrical 
components closer to the ground side of the circuit than I'm used to.
For instance, on my '84, the fuse for the headlights is AFTER the switch
but before the lamps.  If you have a short before the fuse (near the 
switch) you get a fire until the circuit opens or your battery blows up.  
I'm used to Japanese wiring which puts fuses as close to the battery as 
possible.  Much safer.
