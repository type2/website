Sure, if you need it. Personally, I drove my first bus ('69) for three years
in Summit County, Colorado, mostly at 9000 feet and above, where they
average 160 inches of snow per year, and foot-thick dumps are routine.
In that time, I carried chains-- and never once had to put them on, even 
when
towing other (lesser) cars out of snowdrifts!

If you're going to do a lot of off-roading in rough country, then yes, you
may need 4WD. Other than that, I wouldn't bother if I were you.

I've never owned a wasserboxer, and have only the same info you have, from
this list, which as I've pointed out isn't a comprehensive evaluation.
I do have an '80 Vanagon, formerly air-cooled, but now it has a Chevrolet
2800 V6 engine in it, and a radiator up front. Keeps up with traffic nicely,
handles well, and doesn't have some of the problems that seem chronic to
VW watercooled engines (fingers crossed!). Try it, you'll like it. It's a
big job, but you get bragging rights afterward.

I didn't do this conversion, BTW-- bought it this way from the previous
owner who did it. He did some things well, other things a little sloppy,
and I'm cleaning up stuff as I go. But after some introductory pain, it's
been VERY solid and reliable, on two 1,000-mile-plus trips. Quiet, too,
and it didn't even notice the snowstorm over the Grapevine last Christmas.
