1991 was the last year Vanagons were made. There were no '92s and the =
Eurovan appeared in 1993. In my opinion, there's not a huge amount of =
distinction from the '87 through '91 model years. Same 2.1 liter engine, =
same drivetrain, same body style. Differences are mainly cosmetic, and =
of course an '87 is four years older than a '91. There are more options =
available on the later models, if that is important to you. Syncros are =
wonderful, but harder to find, more expensive to purchase ($2000 to =
$4000 more), and more expensive to fix if there are transmission =
problems. I have an '87 syncro Westfalia, and I wouldn't trade it for =
anything.
