>A quick note on how R-X works and why you may or may not like it.
>
>R-X is an anti-wetting solution developed for high airflow situations such as
>aircraft windscreens.  Yep, just like they say, the water beads up and blows
>or falls off (depending on airflow).  The downside is using wipers AND R-X.
>The fine structure analysis shows that wipers rely on a microscopic layer of
>water to slip across the glass.  R-X eliminates that layer and the wipers will
>skip, just like they were running on "dry" glass, which in fact they are.
>
>The key is to use "specially aged" rubber in the wipers (read "old wipers").
>
>The micro-surface on well used wipers is such that the rubber/glass interface
>is poor enough to allow skip-free operation with R-X applied.  New wipers will
>skip and deposit rubber marks at the excursion limits of the wiper arms as
>they quickly degrade to the desired "specially aged" condition.
>
>So that's how the stuff works and why you may or may not like its performance.
>
>It does seem to work well in snirt conditions (SNow,Rain,dIRT).
>
