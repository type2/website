My 1959 Pickup Truck (like a bus, but with a pickup truck rear) cost $1000
to buy and another $1000 or so to get running safely and well. Watch for
engine swaps and stuff. Mine has a '65ish engine and transmission. I had to
do the brake cylinders, swing lever bushings, new tie rods, new master
cylinder, a gas tank to replace the homemade 40gal tank, lots of new
wiring, a new battery, new (well, junkyard) wheels, new tires, a new rear
drum 'cause the old one had to be cut off, etc, etc. I still need to do a
lot of rubber and body work and everything else.

Maybe it was more than $1000. I think after a while, I didn't really want
to keep track! Pickups are kind of rare, so the initial high price was
probably ok. I could still spend thousands of dollars getting the body
perfect, but it's a truck, and I want it to be rough so I don't have to
worry about it.

Don't know if my broad answer covers your broad question.
