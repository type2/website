Oh come now -  I had a Dewer flask 3' high ahd about 16" in dia. full of LOX
in my home for about 2 years.  Had a service truck come by about every 3 weeks
 to re-fill it.  When it was quiet in the house (rare) you could hear it
boiling away.  Course, we were kinda parinoid about open flames, no fireplace
usage, big sign on the front window, and we had to be kinda careful when
filling the portable "bottle" from the big tank, 'cause when you unhooked it,
the stainless steel spigot was kinda frosty cold, and if you didn't disconnect
the two tanks right away, they froze together (and so would your flesh if you
were foolish enough to touch it )!

Was a real "treay" to hoist the big tank into the Vanagon when we went away
for more than a half a day.  Used to get my teenage son to help me heft it
into the spot behind the walkthrough, and then used a nylon cargo strap to tie
it down.  Wonder why I never thought to pipe a little of that back to the
little 52 hp diesel that grunted and groaned to push all of us and our stuff
down the road and worse yet, up the hills ?

Seriously - as with anything else, it is a matter of knowledge, respect,
training and 
                              ^^^^^^^^^                                  
^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^
proper  usage.  Remember during the 70's gas shortage (for those of you folks
old
