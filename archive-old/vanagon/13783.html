Sorry, but I had to ask.  I didn't do that once and had similar problems (plus 
my e-brake didn't work.)  Oh!  That's a good question.  How well does your 
e-brake work?  That should tell you how well your rear brakes are adjusted. 

If the e-brake works well, but your pedal doesn't, first I'd bleed it 
thouroughly.  Check to make sure that the fluid isn't clogged up.  You may need 
a new hose from the rear tube to the wheel.  Those are designed to clog up 
before gunk gets into the wheel cylinder itself (at least I heard or read that 
somewhere.)  So first, bleed, if not fixed, replace hose, if still not fixed, 
replace wheel cylinder.
