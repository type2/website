In the realm of tire selection you need to know how much your van weighs on 
a per wheel basis.  Not what it says in books or on various stickers but what
it weighs loaded as you use it.  If you carry spare anvils, chainsaws, bridge
timbers or such you need to factor that into the weight.  Best way to do this
is to visit a public farmers scale and simply weigh the rascal loaded with
people and stuff and gas and water and dogs and kids and...

Then you'll know exactly what the ground pressure requirements for front and
rear really are.  Now start looking for tires.  

If you have a westie syncro and you carry more than your credit card and a
package of ramen for your outings you may be surprised to learn that the 
rear wheels support slightly more than 2900 lbs.  An easy division gives you
the load of ~1500lbs per wheel.

If the tire you own is rated at 1400 lbs per wheel at max (MAX..Get it) you
are under tired and potentially in jeopardy of tire failure.  

If I recall, the UniLaredo is rated at 1325lbs max per wheel.

The Y370G is rated 1710 lbs /wheel _at_ 65psi.

I returned my laredos to Price/Costco with regrets as they were otherwise an
acceptable tire.  The Y370Gs are head and shoulders a better handling tire
for MY purposes (snow, offroad, loaded transport).  

If you're hauling across US50 outside of Austin NV on a downgrade loaded in
summer temperatures and you lose a rear tire, the $150 you might have saved 
won't even pay the towing charge.  

For my nickel, I'd rather have a little extra margin and YMMWCV.
