>I need new tires for my '72 Safare.  Primarily for this 3000 mile trip.
>What make/model tire should I purchase that works well on later model busi?


This question has been hammered and hammered and HAMMERED on this list,
both recently and in the past. If you check the archives, you'll find
a wealth of discussion about tires for type-2's of all stripes.

My own personal conclusion, based partly on the pretty-good passenger
car tires I have on the V6anagon, is that I would get reinforced or
Load-C tires as VW recommends, if I had it to do over. 185 or 195
width. Passenger-car tires don't have the load-carrying capacity that
most reinforced or Load-C (or D) tires do.

But the biggest reason IMHO, is high-speed handling (over 40 is high
speed for a VW bus), whether heavily loaded or not. I've found out the
hard way that I need a lot more pressure in the rears than the fronts,
to keep the van from squirrelling all over the freeway. A check of the
sticker inside the left front door jamb confirms that VW thinks so too.
But most passenger-car tires have a max pressure of around 35 psi,
which doesn't allow much room for a wide differential between front
and rear. I have to play with the pressures very carefully, to get the
V6anagon to handle like a real car on the freeway, and I run the
rears slightly overinflated, which is probably not a good idea.

I have heard from others on this list, that the reinforced and high-load
tires also give you less steering effort and lower rolling resistance.

A fellow at Discount Tire here in San Diego, recommended a Yokohama
reinforced tire in the 185 width, claiming that they see a lot fewer
warranty returns of this tire. If true, that's a good point, but keep
in mind that the suggestion came from a salesman working on commission.
I've never tried those, or any other reinforced tire-- they may be
excellent for all I know. Caveat emptor, y'know?
