This is a great idea, and solves 2 problems: bulbs burning out, and keeping the 
right color. Most LEDs seem to like about 20mA of current. That would lead to 
(14.4-.7) V / 0.02 A = 685 Ohms (or something close) for the current limiting 
resistor. Power dissipation works out to I2 * R = 0.27W, so a 1/4W resistor is 
cutting it close - this is one of the larger SMT resistor packages, 1206 or 1210
I think.

> electronic components slightly smaller than a gnat's ass, the task is
> trivial.

> as long as your great-grandchild owns the vehicle.
