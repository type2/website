A normal engine will always produce some water vapor.  It is a byproduct of 
the combustion process.  That is why it is important for engines to get hot 
enough to get the water out of the oil.  Sludge and all that gooey stuff.  In 
addition, all fuels contain some sulfur.  Add a little heat, some water, and 
now you have sulfuric acid in your crankcase and exhaust system.  Did you 
originally fail on Hydrocarbons or CO?  CO is mixture related,  HC is ignition 
or valve problem.  Lean Miss-fire will also cause excessive HC.  Also, over 
advanced timing at idle is a problem.
