All--

Another perspective on the tailgating issue, in the form of a story 
recognizable to all of you with daoist leanings.

When you are crossing a river and another boat strikes yours, you stand, 
shake your fist, and scream with anger at the helmsman.  But when a boat 
strikes yours, and you notice it has no one in it, you say to yourself, 
"empty boat," shrug your shoulders, and row on.

When someone treats me stupidly on the highway, I think of this story and 
say to myself, "empty boat."  It helps with the anger.
