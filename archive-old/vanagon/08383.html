At the risk of digressing, I have a quick story of an '85 Golf.  It's oil 
pressure light flashed and the engine made a horrible clattering noise. 
The oil change guy (a specialist, no doubt) put 10W30 in the crankcase.  
This was in the summertime (avg temps 90F); at any rate the replacement 
of this oil with 20w50 fixed the problem immeditately...

On Fri, 16 Dec 1994, Donald Baxter wrote:

> This warning system (although hearing it referred to as a "cold" warning 
> system is news to me) is the same as on my '85 VW Jetta.  I had problems 
> with the system after of all things, washing the engine.  Apparently I 
> was not allowing the engine to cool down enough before rinsing the 
> engine cleaner off and the cold water was frying the oil pressure switch 
> that was mounted on the oil filter flange (Jetta, remember).
> 
> After wasing the engine, and tooling down the road, the buzzer would go 
> off and, looking in the engine compartment, I'd find a steady stream of 
> oil running from the oil pressure switch.
> 
> Anyway, this system does not seem to have so much to do with cold oil as 
> it measures an unusual difference of pressure between two oil pressure 
> switches.  In the Jetta, it measured the difference between the switch 
> at the oil filter and the switch on the cylinder head.  The system only 
> activates itself above a certain RPM (2,000 to the best of my 
> recollection).
> 
> It was also my understanding that in the earlier model years, the 
> circuit boards that are mounted behind the speedometer were rather 
> buggy.  I ended up replacing one of them after it decided to start going 
> off for no reason and it never gave me another problem.
> 
> On my 92 Passat, occasionally this system will sound for a split second 
> when starting the car, I don't know whether this is an occasional test 
> or a bug in the system--it's never gone off when I've been driving the 
> car.
> 
> The are diagnostics in the Bentley manual for the Jetta and the Passat, 
> and I think in the Vanagon manual, I haven't read that far yet, but I 
> think I saw the subject headline in the contents list. --
