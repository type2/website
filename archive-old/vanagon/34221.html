My knowledge is nowhere near Hoover's, but I believe these engines
were the Pratt & Whitney Twin Wasp. Turbocharged, 2800 cubic inches (!),
2,000 horsepower each, driving a propellor at a whopping 900 rpm at cruise,
through a reduction gear assembly at the front.

The engines were impressive, but trouble-prone. The B-29 was known
by pilots and aircrews as "an aborting son of a b*tch", since every time
a thousand of them took off from Tinian Island in the Pacific to bomb Japan
in WWII, more than a hundred would have to turn back with engine trouble.

Is this the engine that was used on many American fighters, such as the
Chance-Vought F4U Corsair, Republic P-47 Thunderbolt, Grumman F6F
Hellcat, and Grumman F7F Tigercat (which had two!)? Any others?
