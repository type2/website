On page 27 of my 1984 Vanagon owner's manual it reads in bold type after
the explanation of the oil pressure light:"An occasional flickering of the
oil pressure warning light when the engine is idling after a long
high-speed trip is no cause for concern if the light goes out upon
acceleration." Cold/dampness should have no effect upon the oil
pressure(except the pressure will be higher the lower the oil
temperature),but will have effect on the electrical connectors involved.
