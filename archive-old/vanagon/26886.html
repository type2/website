John,

I had an '86 that had the inside of the catalytic converter disappear.  
The dealer swore that I must had done it to get better "performance".
Where is their brain, I don't think I would have noticed that MASSIVE hp 
increase.  Anyway, in this part of FL we have emission inspections and I 
obviously could not pass without a new catalytic converter.  The dealer 
wanted near $900.00 for the part only!  I went down the street 1/2 mile 
to the Midas, they had one in stock.  I ran me $225 installed and I was 
outta there in about 2 hours. It has worked perfectly for about three 
years now.  I'm not complaining. 

Regarding your fuel tank implosions, my tank has, on an on and off basis, 
made a distinctive clunk.  Especially when near empty, it's like the 
whole thing is sucking in and then boom, it expands out.  But, I've never 
had it completely implode.

With all the similar problems posted on this list, it sure sounds to me 
like VW really screwed up on the fuel system design in the Vanagon.
