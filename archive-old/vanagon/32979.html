This just will not work (nose down) since there is no way for the 
coolant/air bubble to drift readily DOWN from the engine then UP to the top 
of the rad.  Your pump will push things along, but mother natures bouyancy 
effect really helps. If you really rev the pump you may move more bubbles 
along, more likely blow more coolant out the bleed screw, unless you use the 
'purge bottle' approach that I like. Saves coolant, makes the operation 
truly a one-person job.

In this regard...  how many people would like to buy a small brass adapter 
to screw into the bleed valve  and slip a length of vinyl tubing over?  I 
made one for myself, could get into 'mass' production using an idle piece of 
equipment we have here in the machine shop, price <<$10, ideally $3-$5 since 
that's all I figure it's worth.  Any interest?
