On Thu, 17 Aug 1995, Dyer Lytle wrote:

> I am also wrestling with 6 volt to 12 volt conversion question.  I'm thinking
> of leaving it 6 volt for now and changing later.  I know I'll have to drop
> the engine when I finally do convert so I can change the flywheel.  Anyone
> have any information on the bottom line cost of doing the 6 to 12 volt
> conversion?  Also, does the transmission bell housing have to be clearanced
> when I convert?

I would say do the 6V -> 12V conversion when you do the engine.  The crank
is differnet on the 6V, so the 12V flywheel requires machining to fit on a
6V crank.  Or, I'll sell you a 12V flywheel already machined for a 6V
crank.  :) If you don't do it now, then when you do, use a 12V solenoid on
the 6V starter to avoid chewing up the teeth on the 6V flywheel.  

And if you have the original transmission, it WILL need to be clearanced 
for a 12V flywheel.  

When I got my bus it was 6V, but had a 200mm flywheel, so it was already 
clearanced for 12V.  

Does Hoover have a sermon on 6 -> 12 volt conversions?
