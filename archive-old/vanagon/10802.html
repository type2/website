  I checked the data panels of both a "Road & Track" and a "Car & Driver"
  road test. They refer to the ring and pinion ratio as the "final drive".

  There must be lots of equations to reach a "revs/mile" depending on which
  bits of info. you have to start with. IMHO, an easy way is to start with
  the wheel/tire diameter since this is easily measured with a yardstick.
  Multiply the tire diameter in inches by pi and divide that product by
  12 to get circumference in feet. Divide 5280 by the circumference and
  you should have wheel/tire revolutions per mile. Multiply wheel
  revolutions by the final drive and the gear ratio that you are interested
  in and the result will be engine revolutions per mile.

  I think it was you who is interested in what the "Mountain ratio" is? I
  have Haynes for 1600cc buses in front of me here:

		   Final Drive Ratio
		      Normal ratio................5.375:1
		      Mountain ratio..............5.857:1

                   
		   Identification code
		      CA..........................Normal
		      CB..........................Mountain ratio
		      CC..........................Normal w/ limited slip
		      CD..........................Mountain w/ limited slip

 
 For 185-R14 tires <25.5 inch diamter> we get 791 revs/mile at the wheel.
 791 * 5.857 * 0.82 = 3799 revs/mile. The wear index for this combination
 would be 1720 ft/mile, BTW.

 I think you said you were running 185/70-R14 tires though. The calculatd
 diameter (yours may actually be a little different?) for 185/70-R14
 that I get is 24.2 inches. Going through the procedure described above
 results in 833 revs/mile at the wheel and 4001 revs/mile at the flywheel.
 At "a mile a minute" we end up with the 4000 rpm you reported. Well within
 1 rpm anyway. Incidentally, the wear index for this combo is 1811 ft./mile.
 You may want to keep an eye  on your oil temperature at freeway speeds,
 at least until you get your transmission ratios sorted out.
