Bob actually had it wrong.  Terry had it right. Be careful, get this wrong
and you could, at the very least ruin your batteries, if not the alternator.It can also cause fires.

+ parallel :  positive to positive, negative to negative, with a diode
              or an isolator in between.  Overall voltage is equal to
              that of a single battery's.  The CCA (cold-cranking-amps)
              is potentially doubled, assuming identical batteries.

+ serial : take battery A and battery B, attach B's negative to ground (body),
           B's positive to A's negative, A's positive to vehicle's positive
           terminal.  Doubles the voltage (6V+6V--> 12V, 12V+12V-->24V),
           halves the CCA, assuming A and B identical.
