The Problem at Hand
-------------------

One thing I noticed today that concerns me, is that on deccelleration, I'm
getting that "power burp" I described earlier. That is, a slight, sporatic
loss of power, like one of cylinders is failing to fire and then starts
back up. Seems to only happen once the bus has warmed up well. Say, after
a couple of miles of driving. 

Since I "rebuilt" this engine without splitting the case, I'm constantly
wondering about things I couldn't check...like the distributor shaft. I'm
going to check valves again (hopefully tomorrow) and adjust timing and
carb one more time. I haven't done any of this since I got the preheater
hose on, and I imagine that with it in place there may be a noticeable
difference in the way the carb adjusts out. 

I seem to recall reading that if the distributor shaft is worn,
timing adjustments won't remain stable. Is that true? What else can I look
for? 

I'm kinda hoping that I was somehow compensating for the missing pre-heat
hose and that now the system is out of whack. But as always, I'd rather be
correct than foolishly satisfied. 

Also, the distributor I have on the bus now is old. It squeaks a little
when I start the bus (the shaft against the plastic side of the points). 
Come to think of it, I might need to add a little non-conductive lubricant
to keep this from wearing the back of the points...effectively throwing
them out of adjustment. 

Comments? Suggestions?
