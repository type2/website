I have some of the same problems.  Removed the sliding mechanisms to save
headroom (I'm 6'0).  The seats I chose were from a Porsche 924.  Black
vinyl with tall integrated headrests and thick side bolsters.  I chose
these seats for two reasons.  First, they were thin.  By that I mean
that the seatbacks and bottoms are very flat (not thick) much like the
original bus seats.  I sat in Sami's Volvo seats, and while they were
very plush I lost a good deal of visibility out of the side window, and
since I spend a lot of time looking out the window when traveling, I
decided I needed a "flat" seat.  Installed, the Porsche seats are
provide roughly the same ride height as the original seats.  For people
over about 5'10" I would definately recommend these seats.  The second
reason I chose these seats was price.  I stumbled across them in a
junkyard, and they happened to be on sale.  I walked out with the pair
for $45.  Since then, I have been keeping my eyes on the 924's in the
junkyard, and haven't seen a pair of seats in decent shape.  I guess I
really got lucky that day.  I spend a lot of Saturdays wandering around
junkyards hot, dirty, and tired and every once in a while it pays off.  :)

The one possibly negative thing about these seats is that they are deep.
The bottom of the seat is "longer" than it is wide.  This is slightly
less convenient for entry and exit, and might be uncomfortable for
some people under 6 feet tall.  Shelley and I were perfectly comfortable 
in them, though, for all of GNATT (8,000 miles in 4 weeks).
