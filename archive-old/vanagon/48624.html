Dave,

In regard to your querry about whether or not the list owner can own an 
address...the answer is plainly NO.

What is involved here is a trade mark or brand name. The concept is rather 
simple....you can't get trade mark protection on your home address because 
it identifies a particular geographical location. An internet address is a 
geographical identifier, rather than identifying terra firma, it identifies 
a specific location on a network.

So there is no ownership. 

I'm sorry that the list owner of "type2" is incurring expenses in running 
the list. That was his choice, not the choice of the list members. In fact, 
I don't recall any of the list members of V_at_L getting any say in the matter. 
We were told 24 hours prior that the split was happening....and boom it 
happened. No discussion...no vote...nada.

Tim Smith has a point in his response....do any of the owners of the 
web-pages ask or demand payment....no...so why should we have to "render 
unto Ceasar" or in this case the list administrator because he feels that 
the list name is his property. Well it isn't, period.

If it were, then every time we sent a posting to the list, we would be 
violating his rights by typing "type2_at_primenet_dot_com", and therefore we would 
have to seek his permission before sending a post to the list.

If anyone has ownership in the list names, it would be Volkswagen, 
considering "TYPE2" and "VANAGON" are protected names under trademark law.
