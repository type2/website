>What causes are there for backfiring (mine's starting to do it on the
>down shift).  I know incorrect timing can cause this - why exactly?  Is there
>residual compressed gas in the cylinder during exhaust stroke followed with
>late ignition?  What other causes can lead to backfiring other than the
>timing disorder?

Another possible cause for backfiring is a leak in the exhaust system. During
the downshift, vacuum will draw outside (oxygen rich) air in through the leak
and cause a backfire.  It will be even worse if your engine is poorly tuned (ie,
late timing or over-rich mixture). I have found this a problem with some of the
header systems for the type II and early type II engines that used the little
"doughnut" behind the heat exchanger. The pipes would shift and vibrate, and
cause a leak. On the last set I had on my '66 Bug (God rest it's soul- It gave
it's life so that I might live!) I bought a set of flanges from the local bug
shop and welded them on the back of the heater box and on the end of the header
pipes.They bolt together using a gasket, and never leaked up till the very end.
Before that, I used Hi-temp silicone and muffler patch compound, but it never
lasted. Of course, if I hadn't lowered the car so much (Mufflers dragged the on
small speed bumps,etc), the problem would never of happened.Just a thought.
