Can't speak from experience but, it sounds like there could be something to 
it. I can't speak for the combustion chemistry but any time you are 
increasing power you sacrifice something. The hydroboost thing is a perfect 
example you may get more power when the gas is realeased but the energy 
consumed makeing the gases is more than what you gained in power. In essence 
you are storing energy at a less than 100%efficiency and releasing it all at 
once for more power.

Moral of the story... you may be able with this fan to get short bursts of 
power at little cost because the time when it's not "boosting" offsets the 
lost energy. Using it for continuous power increase, however, could only 
pull down the efficiency of the engine.
