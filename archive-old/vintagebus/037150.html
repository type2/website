And where is it stated that people have a "right" to a junk vehicle?

People do not realize that having a project car comes with a responsibilty
to keep things somewhat neat.  The basic rule of thumb is to keep
your house no worse than your neighbors.  If all of your neighbors have
junk cars on the lawn, then you can too.  If they don't, you shouldn't.
If you do not like conforming to the de facto standard of the rest of the
neighborhood - MOVE.  Find a neighborhood where everyone has their cars on
blocks and feel at home.  Or rent a shop in an industrial area,
where you can rip stuff apart any time of day or night and nobody will
give a damn.  Go in on the rent with some buddies, and you can have a grand 
old time.

A project also brings with it the responsibility, although somewhat vague,
that you'll actually do something with it.  To collect a bunch of 
"cars" and not do anything with any of them is more along the lines of
"hoarding" than "restoration."
