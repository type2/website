>What is the recommend air in the tires of a 67 bus.

NOS Hanover air at 20 degrees C.  You can buy cylinders of it from
Wolfsburg West or BFY Obsolete.  :)

See pages B-11 1 and B-11 4 of your '63-'67 Bentley manual.  If you don't
have one, get one.  This info is also in the owners manual, but those are
a little harder to come by... prowl the swap meets.  Some later Buses will
have a sticker somewhere that lists the tire pressures, but I don't think
VW did this on splits.

>The kid that put them on put too much air in and I felt like I was
>floating .. I WAS .. let some air out and it is much better.

Another classic VW trick is to not fix the air pressures when rotating
tires.  Most aircooled VWs specify more air pressure in the rear tires
than in the front.  When you put the stiff ones in the front, it makes
the handling squirrelly.
