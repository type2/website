I would almost recommend getting a brand new thermostat.  I had two used
ones sitting in my basement and for some reason they were both stretched out
to far and did not retract enough to close the flaps. They should provide
about 1/2" contraction to fully close the flaps.  BTW they do fail open,
this was a question a while back.  Again way too much free time and a
controlled basement stove to kill a thermostat :)  Also do not use a tight
spring on the heat flap connecting rod in back of the fan shroud because
that would effect the thermostat performance as well, as little as needed is
great.
