I thought I'd relay an interesting story one of my friends told me last
week.  You AZ guys can probably figure out who this is.  ;)   We were
talking about a Squareback he used to own back in the 80s when everybody
wanted a pimped-out Squareback, some of you may remember those days.  Ah,
the memories...  He had taken it upon himself to build the *ultimate*
Squareback.  Starting with a 1964 "N" model (rare car, to you Bus guys, that
would be like starting with a ribbed bumper 15-window), he proceeded to
convert it to RHD, install an elaborate selection of original EMPI Type 3
goodies, and so on and so on.  So we're talking for a while and he says:
"Yeah, that was the one with the Barndoor [Bus] ragtop."
"What?"  I replied, "What do you mean?"
"This guy had a Barndoor Deluxe in his yard, I paid him $100 to cut the
ragtop section out of it.  Then I took it down to the local body shop and
paid them to shorten and narrow it, adjust the frame and sliders, and cut it
into the roof of the Squareback."
At this point I was pretty much laughing at the insanity of it all and
managed to spill out:
"Oh my god, did it work?"
"Yeah, it worked perfectly," he answered, "That car is still around in
Flagstaff, AZ, the guy asks me every once in a while if I have found him
some RHD pedals for it yet.  As soon as Barndoors became popular, I went to
go get that Bus but it was long gone."
