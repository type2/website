i just dont soak any rubber parts in sovent if I can avoid it.  A solvent 
tank is nice for cleaning parts (knock the dried grease and dirt chunks off 
first) but barring that you can get by with primitive methods.  Like I have 
been using carb clean in a can to clean out the engine case for a motor I am 
building (very low miles, but it caught on fire and stuff fell into the 
motor- its a newer case with the plastic cam plug-  when dd they start using 
those?)   or after getting the crust off with a screwdriver/wire brush, you 
can spray it all with simpe green (much nicer on you than toxic chemmies, and 
it does cut the grease)  Anyway, are the link pins rusted with pits?   are 
they worky in the bushes?    should fit snug , not have ovalled area.   like 
the needle bearing s in the later front ends, a lot of times they just fall 
aprt when removed, thats bad.     Do you have  a bd workshop manual?   it is 
pretty much the same procedure as in the 63 manual, same way to adjust link 
pins, set the shims,  remove stuff, .   You just want the pins to fit nicely, 
if they can wiggle back and forth then so will your wheel.    simpe and 
straightforward.  then just clean, reassemble, and grease.  Or you can get 
stuff powdercoated .  what ever.
