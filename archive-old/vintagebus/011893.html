The 195/75R14 Load range C tire has a capacity of 1435 pounds, which
is a bit too low for my tastes.  I have a pair on the front of my bus
(followed the tire man's advice, bad choice!) but I wouldn't put them
on the rear, and certainly not on the rear of a Westy.  My theory is
that the lower-profile tire has to use more of its strength to
maintain its unnatural "flat" shape, so less strength is left over to
support the load.  The "rounder" 185 tires are more flexible and
therefore carry more load.  Note that this is a theory only!

> As always you are not going to pop tires like this no matter what
you do to
> them.

The major problem I've had is with tread ply separations in standard
load 185R14s.  Remember that the load ratings are the _maximum_
allowable and that you should run a comfortable (say 30-40%) excess
capacity.  Run the tires around at near their maximums and they will
give you trouble.
