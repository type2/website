Chip,

        If your cooler says "Campmobile", it is from a Westfalia....no
question about it.  Now...whether or not the cooler is original to the rest
of the interior or not is a mystery for you.  I have pix posted of my SO-42
('65) Westfalia interior at:
http://ourworld_dot_compuserve_dot_com/homepages/JJRodgers1/library/so42.htm.

        One thing that I have noticed about the E-Z's is that they "all"
seem to be bench seat models.  Of course, that is based on a very small
test field......I mean....like maybe 5 buses.  Also, "all" of them where
originally PanelVans (VIN 21.....).

        On your sheet metal question, there are three parts to make up the
area that you need.  First, and obviously, the "outer rocker panel" (app.
$40).  It has square holes in the middle of it with tangs that stick up and
go thru the second part, the "outer ten inches of the cargo floor" (app.
$45).  The floor will have the "check plate" that you are looking for
prewelded on it.  Usually, when you replace the "outer rocker", it is
necessary to replace the "inner rocker" (app. $15).  This piece is simply a
long, skinny piece of channel-iron-shaped sheet metal that tees into your
frame cross supports.  

        You can do all this work with a grinder and a mig-welder quite
easily.  Sometime, maybe we could get together and I could give you a hand
with it.  My labor is pretty cheap.._dot_usually in 12's or 24's.  All parts
prices are quoted via Wolfsburg West, but Das Bulli Haus makes heavier duty
sheet metal parts.
