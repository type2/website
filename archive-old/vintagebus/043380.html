I looked into this earlier this year when it looked like I was
going to need to dial in (modem) to work from a remote spot in
Texas.  You need a cell phone with the capability and a modem
card for the laptop that matches the cell phone (the modem you
already have probably won't work).  You can buy the phone and
the card as a package for $300 or so.  Then there's airtime.
Also, you only get around 2400 - 9600 bits/sec on the cell
phone modem, so your pictures are going to take awhile to
upload.

If you drop your real-time requirement it gets much cheaper
and more doable.  Pictures can be uploaded if you stop in
towns big enough to have public 'Net access, like at the
library, an Internet cafe', office supply store, etc.  Or,
modem them back via payphone...still requires some equipment
but the phone rates can be cheaper than cell if you do it
right.  If nothing else, set out with a box of floppies and
a stack of FedEx mailers - mail a disk home each night to
a support person who will put it up on the Web.
