That brings up a new thought now that we're on "Everything You Ever Wanted
to Know About Sales Tax, But Were Afraid to Ask."

For those of you who own stores and sell at swap meets and such (in CA), St.
Board is starting to get a little wierd on these transactions.  They are
requiring you to fill out what is called a "Temporary Business Location" and
of course you must charge the tax where the show or swap is set up.   Your
accountant should be able to give you details.

This is not a new rule, but I've been getting hit lately.  I do the sales
tax reconciliation for the largest swimwear supplier in the country.  My
client travels to about 150 swimmeets a year in CA, and St. Board wants me
to do business licenses and seperate forms for each location, even though
they may only be there once.
