On Thu, 17 Oct 2002, ronnie chase wrote:

> looking into putting together the 36hp motor for the 57, and I've run into
> two different schools of thought. Builder number one(factory trained in
> germany many years ago when 25/36hp was what there was) says to oil the p's
> and c's when assembling the motor, builder number two(many years experience
> building stock and race motors, one of the few local builders who's work has
> a good rep) says that oiling them helps make an oil burner out of your motor
> and that they are best left dry. Opinions?

This has to qualify for the "provocative subject line of the year award!"

I think that your local builder is off his nut!  Starting an engine with
no lubricant isn't a good idea in any case.  It _might_ seat the rings
quickly on a race engine, but in terms of longevity I wouldn't take the
risk of scoring the cylinder walls before the oil film arrived to cushion
the parts..

Oil the parts while assembling, crank the engine to get oil pressure, then
hook up the ignition and run it.
