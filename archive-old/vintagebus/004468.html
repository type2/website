Well I checked my shifter plate because my shifting has been rough. Turns
out, it's upside down. After flipping it right side up, I couldn't get into
1st or 2nd at all (reason I had originally put it in upside down). I crawled
under and checked the shift coupling and found that one of the screws was a
home-made job that I had ground a pointy tip on years ago (for lack of a
real one). The point wasn't exactly centered, which was slightly misaligning
the coupling to the tranny shaft. I picked up a new screw, put it in, and it
shifted pretty good, but not perfect. So I crawled up front and found that
the front (shorter) shift rod was rubbing on the steering damper bolt
because the shift rod bushing is gone. As a temporary fix, I wrapped some
thin cardboard around the shift rod where it enters the tube, secured it
with some duct tape, and it shifts so smoothly now, I can't believe it!

Now I've got to replace that home-made cardboard bushing :-) Is there
supposed to be a bushing at the other end (rear) of the tube too?
