As far as the inverters go, I have seen DC-DC converters in several
electronics catalogs.  The ones I think would be useful for a Bus
have a 5v input, 12v output, at anything from 1 to 40 watts or so
(0.083 A to 3.3 A).  If you can find a suitable one in an electronic
surplus catalog, they are generally cheap, like $5-$15 or so.  New ones
are available but have silly prices, from $60 to $100 or more.

I have thought about installing a small 6 volt sealed lead-acid
battery (gel cell) in series with the vehicle power to power 12v
accessories.  Ideally, the setup would include a relay and charge
itself somehow when not needed.  Otherwise, you'd have to keep
taking the battery out to charge it, or have two batteries that
you could switch between.

A Bus-owning friend who is an amateur radio operator just keeps
a huge marine deep-cycle 12-volt battery in the back of his Bus,
just inside the cargo doors, and runs all his 12v stuff from that.
He puts it on a 110v charger whenever he's at home.
