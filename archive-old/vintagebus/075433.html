> Damn! The new one I have is off by about 1/16".
> How do you tell if my old one (bolt holes in line)
> Is stiff enough to be good to reuse?  I don't want to send couplers back and forth in the mail for the rest of the year!

Take a vise grip to each end and twist - there should be no play. If there
is play with hand pressure, you're losing torque and it'll be sloppy or
not go into gear. Probably also ready to break. This is critical - ask me
blah blah... The reason 1/16" is critical is that the shifter knob is two
feet away  from the axis of the shift rod. When you move the shifter from
the 1-2 position to the 3-4 position, you're moving the knob about four
inches or so. That's about 9 degrees of rotation.  Down at the rod, if you
give the rod a radius of about 1/2", the 9 degree angle of rotation works
out to 1/12" of an inch, a bit more than 1/16". Neat, eh? Ask me what I
went thru alone, decades before the list existed to figure this out.
