Gary and all,

    When I replaced the motor in the Ghia I used to own, I bought a new
Brazilian AS-41 case from Gene Berg that had no numbers from the factory.
It was kind of funny because at the time, I was living overseas in the
Philippines where engine numbers are recorded on import paperwork at the
time your car has it's entry inspection.  When I went to export my car back
to the States, the inspector spent almost an hour looking really puzzled and
pouring all over the engine looking for the numbers that I knew he would
never find :-)  When asked about where the numbers went, I just shrugged,
put on my best "Chevy owner's face" and said ,"I dunno, I just put gas in
the thing. . ."  :-)  He finally gave up looking and rubber-stamped my
papers.  It's a good thing he did too, 'cause that would have opened up a
HUGE can 'O worms for me trying to explain where the old engine went.
