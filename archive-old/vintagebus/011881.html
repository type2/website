I wasn't going to mention this, but...

I have replaced a generator without loosening the fan shroud...

1.  Fan off front shaft.
2.  Pulley off rear shaft.
3.  Loosen two long screws holding generator together, let nuts holding
backing (fronting?) plate fall off front end.
4.  Looosen generator clamp.
5.  Slide generator out without dropping shims.

Reverse to replace, except you can't put the nuts back on.

I have done this, but:  it was in the rain, on the side of I-85, and I
fixed it right, by pulling the motor, when I got home.  Any person who
leaves it this way is a lazy slacker and deserves whatever catastophe
befalls them when the generator slips and chews up the fan and
housing...but that's my opinion.
