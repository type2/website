Greetings and Felicitations Honorable Bus List Members:

Forgive my impertinence, but I must defend the "Gauge" users on this list,
or, at least my use of them. Living in the desert, I have found that an oil
temp, oil pressure and tack can help you understand what your motor limits
are (Eric & Barb was very helpful in advising me to know where my RPMs are
at each shift speed and "see for yourself."). I want the fullest motor life
I can get while at the same time, enjoying trouble free miles along the way.

What have been my experience with the gauges? Well, I found that I was over
shifting 500 RPMs before I installed my tack (flickering lights at stop sign
or just around town driving on a warm-hot day). Brought my RPMs down by
observation and the result: Stopped the flickering light in town or after a
hill run in hot outside temps. Valves not tight at oil change.

I found that for me, over 3000 RPMs on a hot day that the oil temps go way
up and oil pressure goes way down. Buy adjusting my RPMs based on my gauges,
I know how far I can push my motor when traveling up a long grade or when I
have to back off on a long stretch of road. I feel like I'm in control now,
knowing that I can adjust to whatever me and my vw my find on the open road.
The gauges react to all variables, load, grade, head wind, outside temps,
which changes moment to moment. With this information, I adjust and keep the
oil temp where I feel comfortable.


<<if your rev's are mysteriously too high, SLOW DOWN.  oil temp too high-get

With respect, the only way to know these things with consistency and
accuracy are with gauges.
