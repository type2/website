Paul,

By mentioning use of the the rain gutter, I am assuming that your bus is
as straight as mine is... you basically need to find what you consider to
be a true horizontal body part to use as your reference plane. The rain
gutters are, to my knowledge and personal experience, a good reference,
as they, and the body in general may be assumed and expected to be
parallel with the ground, ie horizontal, when the bus is properly set up.

The rain gutters are usually undamaged and unmodified, so they are flat,
and may be properly used as reference plane. I always compare the gutter
and the bottom of the rocker panel for agreement.

> Will definately use the anti sieze on both ends - the fact that they were
> seized caused most of my problems

Yes, that has always been the main problem here too. Both sides are now
easily removable for service, as are my rear brake drums, etc. They all
come off by hand, no tools needed.

HANDY TOOL TIP:

If you buy a 12-16" range turnbuckle ($10) with a hook on one end, and an
eye that fits the shock absorber bolt on the other end, it makes a fine
tool for raising and lowering the spring plate as needed, without any
other 'special tools', or multiple jacks. Grease the threads well before
use. I keep two in the bus at all times for this repair. Graingers and
McMaster Carr both sell this item. Turn it with your 17mm wrench or a
screwdriver.
