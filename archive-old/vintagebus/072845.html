Subject: Re: [VB] 65' brake system question

On Mon, Jun 30, 2003 at 09:37:28AM -0700, Tracy Bonin wrote:
> Hi, I have a 65' Splitty that I'm restoring. When I was taking it
> off the trailer that we transported it on, the brake pedal gave way,
> and went from having pressure to none at all. The pedal just goes to
> the floor, and will stay there unless I flip it back up with my shoe
> or something. Now I know that this could be a couple of things,
> master cylinder, wheel cylinders. I plan on replacing all the
> hydrolic parts of the system in the near future, but don't have
> enough money for them right now. I am placing a order for parts
> today, so I can work on it on my vacation next week, and was hoping
> that someone might have expierenced this before and could tell me
> what it could be so I can order the part.....

well like you said, could be the master cyl, a wheel cyl, even busted
brake line. I would just guess master cyl. What you need to do is go
around & pull the wheel hubs off. Check each wheel cyl. If all are dry
& the flexable hoses going to them are not leaking there is a very
good chance that it's your master cyl. 

I suppose you could check the fluid level & then just pump the pedal a
lot & see if the level goes down (would indicate a leak) or if the
system just never creates pressure to begin with (indicate a master
cyl problem). I personally wouldn't drive it any untill I found the
cause of the lack of pressure, but I'm sure you agree with this.
