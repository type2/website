Six fuses, right?  The leftmost one (hot all the time) should be 
for horn and interior lights, and the second-from-left (hot with
key on) should be the brake lights and turn signals.  If you can
start the engine, the feed end of both of those fuses should be
getting power.  Grab a test light and check both ends of these
two fuses.  If your high beams and tail lights work, the right
four fuses in your fuse box should be OK, so you can substitute
them for testing purposes.
