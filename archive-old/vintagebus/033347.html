I have rebuildt brakes with success.   You do need to have wheel cylinders 
that do not have pits.  Use a brake hone.  If they have pits the fluid will 
seep past the new seals and cause brake problems.  if yours are original they 
are probably in this state.    If you are unsure about how to do this, buynew 
ones.

make sure your backing plates all have the spring tabs that hold the brake 
star from turning.  If the brake stars self turn, your brakes self deadjust.  
 :(    You can get the pieces off bay window bus backing plates.  I have 
happily transfered the bit with the springs off rear bay backing plates onto 
split backing plates.   Dunno about the fronts.

I'd get them powder coated semi gloss black.   then again, I haven't 
bothered, and just rattle canned mine.  But I woud like to have mine powder 
coated.  It would be neato.
