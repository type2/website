Copper's softer than the aluminum alloy the head's made of.  I use a
dental pick, get underneath it and work it up gently.  If that doesn't
work I'll try and deform it in the same radius as the manifold port with
a screwdriver, using my hand as a hammer and making sure I don't contact
the head with the screwdriver.  It's not hard, just be careful of what
you're trying to save.  It also helps to have a rag in the port if the
head's on the engine so you don't accidentally drop something inside. 
The port should be shiny silver.
