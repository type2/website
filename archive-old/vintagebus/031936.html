after starting it up  get out of the bus, open the engine lid and look at the
manifold just under the carb.   Is it getting ice formed on there?   This is
called carb icing, very prevalent in high humidity at temps just above
freezing to somewhere in the forties.  Which is the PNW except in summer.  It
makes the engne sputter and run funky and even stall, until things have warmed
up enough  in the engine coompartement to counteract the icing.    Do you have
a preheat tube?  This tube runs off the air cleaner down  through the rear tin
and picks up warm air from next to a cylinder head.THis preheats the air
through the caarb and manifol and counteracts the icing.   You can pick up a
new tube for circa $3, andeven if you dont have the little metal pickup that
bolts where the muffler bolts on, just send the pick up end of the flexible
preheat tube to the area abve the under tin but below the push rod tubes and
you will notice an intstantaneeous improvement.
