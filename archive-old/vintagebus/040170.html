JUDSON:
These are some facts that I've learned about the supercharger kits from 
Judson: The Judson kits were supplied with three CRITICAL small parts that 
were to be installed along with the lubricator,pump, pulleys, etc...these 
were: a stronger-than-stock fuel pump diaphram spring (to provide the carb 
bowl with a larger volume of fuel with each diaphram stroke),  larger MAIN 
and  smaller AIR CORRECTION  jets for the carb ( to provide the richer fuel 
mix for the supercharged engine).

FACTS:
Now for some details, I purchased the bus from the second owner,who 
originally spotted the bus and purchased it. He got the engine running and 
in doing so one of the things he replaced was the fuel pump. The original 
fuel pump was included in a box of parts that came with the bus when I 
purchased it. Of course, as the proud new owner of a cool bus I was anxious 
to log  some miles on the bus, but on the first outing I knew that something 
  was just not right in the engine, the primary sympton being the bus 
running very hot. It cranks, idles, accelerates beautifully but at high  
RPM's- just before shifting or anything above 50MPH (remember it is a 36HP 
in a pre-'59 type2) I got lots of high pinging sounds, rattling, and a 
significant flat spot. My gut feeling was that the engine was running very 
lean.

QUESTION:
Now for my questions: I was getting pretty upset not being able to figure 
out the overheating problem. I had been basically doing only short jaunts ( 
about 20-30 minutes) then parking it because it was very hot! Well, I got 
little longer, it got a little hotter, oil light on--I shut her down.
Next, I did the research on the Judson kits mentioned previously and it 
clicked, I remembered seeing that old, original fuel pump from the bus in 
that box of parts. I dissambled that pump, cleaned it up well and installed 
it--PROBLEM SOLVED. I mean what a difference! This thing sounds wicked now, 
and much less noise (pinging) and she cruised beautifully at 50-55 MPH 
without all that straining.
BUT,BUT,BUT, This is why I am crying, this is why I am posting this: Now

The noise is not really loud but it is present. Engine performance is good. 
The oil light does NOT come on while driving or at idle.Should I continue to

MORAL OF THE STORY:
For the List- If you intend to run a Judson, be aware of the fact that a 
change in carb jetting and that special stronger fuel pump spring  will be 
required in order for the Judson to perform as designed.I believe that some 
of the bad rap on Judsons may be due to the fact that INOMPLETE  kits have 
been installed.
