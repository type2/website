Cheapo jobs can be done with headliners available from your local flaps. But 
there's a
lot more to it than meets the eye. The cheapo jobs, if you want to try it, 
can be had
for about $50 ($150 for a bus) and you install. It's best to get a VW shop to 
do it, however, in which case the cost is about $200 and takes about one day 
to complete. Buses can run up to $1,000 to $1,500. And, convertibles and 
rag-tops are maybe $300 to $400. And most VW shops will try to justify your 
not installing the headliner even when torn - their reasoning being that the 
headliner is a bitch to install. But most independent shops that specialize 
in upholstery can install them; albeit for a fee. The last one I had made 
cost me $400 for a 1974 Beetle, and I got gypped. So, go with anything 
between $200 and $400  for a beetle or $1000- $1500 for a bus and you choose 
the material. About the other material for the interior, you can get by with
standard panels for busses.
