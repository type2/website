bodywork takes time, and the better you want it the more it'll cost.   Sounds
like you know a fair bit about bodywork, take a close look at the bodies and
paint jobs at the next show for  attention to detail or the lack thereof.
When I planned to get some paint and rust repair done on my 65, my goal was
just make it look tidier and keep the water out- no big deal.  I learned about
bodywork doing it and now some people say I should show it,  though I can look
at it and point out a billion of things wrong with the paint and body,
although it does look a ton better than it did before (and new window seals).
It would have been better looking if it had more time spent on it, but there
was a limit.      Who knows- ask the shop what the usual customer does- maybe
they just drop it off and say paint it such and such a colour.     A lot of
people don't look beyond a coat of shiny paint.
