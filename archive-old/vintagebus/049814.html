They are supposed to step down by themselves.  I remember that the new ones
did, though maybe thay don't do it well on most of the old stuff we're used
to.  Since you have a new carb, it's working.  There's nothing to 'force'
the choke cam to rotate to the next step, but if you look at it you'll see
that the side opposite to the steps has a large round shape, just like the
counterweights on a 'full circle' crankshaft.  This causes gravity to
rotate the cam in the direction of the next lower step.  The vibration of
the engine, or blipping the throttle, allows the cam to rotate.
