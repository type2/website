This is usually caused by holding the brakes while stopped after a long
brake application. It eventually will go away with more long brake
applications not followed by a stop. Not that you would go out on Saturday
and run down a hill with your foot on the brake all day. But normal driving
with slightly more aggressive braking for awhile will do it. If you turn
them to get rid of it, you take off large amounts of material on the narrow
sides and none on the stretched sides and end up with a useless drum that
will oval anytime you lay into the brakes. I have had ovalled drums several
times over the years after mountain camping trips and it has always gone
away in fairly short order by just braking.
