okay, a guy from NORWAY hears 'rust' and gets a mental picture COMPLETELY 
different that what they cal 'rust' in Arizona.

they salt the roads in some places, which creates salt rust which can make 
the frame unsafe.    THis does not happen in Arizona.   the rust tends to be 
from paint flaking off from excessive sun.  A little rocker rot.  maybe.  you 
would drool at the solid buses in the  desert.    Well, I would.  Look... 
solid rocker panels, I thought those were a fairy tale.   In areas where salt 
rust happens I see the point of fixing it first - it can be structural.  

rust in the PNW tends to be holes in the front floors and rockers from window 
seals leaking.   sometimes the outriggers and jack points are gone.  but the 
PNW rust does not make the vehicle unsafe to driver, it is not structural.  
It rains a lot here, as in often and relatively constant high humidity.   
Just cause it may not be raining now (well, it is) doesnt mean it wont in 
half an hour.    Lewis and Clark wrote of over wintering at the mouth of the 
Columbia, and how for 40 days it had not stopped raining for more than 30 
minutes at a stretch (and of how they were tired of camas root and dryed 
salmon, and had all picked something up from the local Indian ladies)

here I do brakes first, and major steering problems, window seals come before 
new paint.

I think people approved of Brian's gumption hell for leather get the bus 
going from pulling it out of the weeds.   I didn't think it was unsafe, nor 
rusty.  I heard it had new brakes.    I didn't see why the cooling system was 
disconnected though (no fresh air hoses, no air cleaner, no apron) some 
things which are fairly quick to install.   


I can see the point,  me and my ex went and drove 500 miles in a 67 westy
