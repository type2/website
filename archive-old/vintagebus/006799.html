Overheating has never been easy for me to solve, there can be a lot of
causes.  I do major changes like swap out spare carb (or one of a known
non-overheating engine)... same with a good running distributor.

I try to focus on one KEY branch:

  -- is it producing TOO MUCH HEAT -- intake mixture, timing (including
dwell), etc, etc.
  -- Is it just not getting enough cooling

The list always seems to focus on cooling, but I don't -- there are a lot of
buggies and stuff out there with minimal cooling tin -- and they don't
overheat.  My observations in Arizona confirmed a lot of this.

If you are heating up that fast, if it is cooling -- it is something major
-- like the oil cooler not working -- or oil pressure relief or major tin or
fan problems.

Is it a new cooler?  Are you willing to re-do the gaskets and stuff under
the oil cooler just to make sure they seated correctly?

Then focus on the "producing too much heat" -- head leaks, manifold leaks,
carb leaks -- timing, improper advancing of dizzy (check with strobe), dwell
off (too short or too long), spark plugs messed-up (even fouled).  Area all
4 firing equal?  Triple-check the valves, make sure you distributor isn't
installed wrong, exhaust is right for carb -- etc, etc.

It isn't easy, the list of things to eliminate is at least 3x longer than
what I just posted!

Good luck.  Tell us what you have for a carb, manifold, dizzy, oil cooler,
whatever you can.  I'll say that being able to run the engine on a bench can
save a hell of a lot of time; too bad I never have that option!  Is there
someone who can get you a bench with starter on it?  That way you can play
with all the intake, valves, timing, etc and carb a heck of a lot easier.
