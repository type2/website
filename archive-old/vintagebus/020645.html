Preflight Checklist                          (1) Have
2 of the newest biggest fire bottles on board behind
front seats.         (2) Make sure gas lines to carb &
fuel pump are secure and the metal tubes they hook to
are seated tight in carb & fuel pump.Metal tubes have
popped out with gas lines still secured to them with
gas pumping onto hot engines...FIRE!!            (3)
Lug nuts can work loose and reports of wheels coming
off while bus is moving have been reported.Check after
any shop has done any work on bus.                    
       (4) Use only GL-4 fluid in tranny.         
(5)Grease front end every 1,200 miles       (6)Repack
front wheel bearings every summer (7)Thom's mix for
leaky steering boxes.     (8)Replace the 4 rubber
brake hoses.        (9) Complete flush of old brake
fluid from brake system every 2 years.

I flew to LA to pick up my '64 Sundial, which the
seller assured me was in fine running condition and in
plenty good shape to drive back to Hood CA. He even
took it to an LA VW mechanic for a tuneup and lube.

around LA a bit, and indeed it seemed just fine.
Compression, oil pressure, etc. was all good, and I
thought I had nothing to worry about. But less than
100 miles outside LA disaster struck: rod through the
case. Why? When I finally got the bus home (trailer)
and started tearing into the deceased engine, I found
the spaces between cylinders to be just packed tight
with a hardened mixture of motor oil and sand.
Rust-free desert bus, you see. Had I trailered the bus
home in the first place I could have saved that
engine. I had a hellish journey home (hitch-hiking in
