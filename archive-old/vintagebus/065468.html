What sort of rpms are you getting now?   Is the rear axle nut 36mm or 46mm?  
The 46mm rear axle nut means you have a 1.26 reduction, and the 36mm 99% of 
the time means a 1.39 reduction.  ghias don't have redux, so if a ghia was 
going 70mph with the 
1.39 it'd be 70/1.39=50 mph
1.26 it'd be 70/1.26=55.5mph  
( ya, I'm ignoring tire size here)
Buses and ghias came with .82 4ths, but bugs came with .89 4th (lower)   If 
you have a bug 4th, it limits your top speed (but you can pull hills!) 

The 66 came with the 46mm, but it could have been swapped sometime.   The 
other thing I have noticed is that people new to VWs tend to shift up earlier 
than they should, as newer cars are quieter.  You may want to borrow or 
install a tach to find out some actual numbers.

Re tires:   I have 195 75 14 tires on my 65, I wouldn't run anything smaller. 
  You may want a 205 75/14, or even find some 15" bus rims and use 205 75 15s.

How fast are you trying to go?   Is 55-60 mph way too slow for you?  Or is it 
whining the p&ss out of the motor at 55mph?  Is your son happy with the 
gearing?( I mean, it's his bus, right?)
