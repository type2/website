Well, the general methods are:

* jam your knees under it and whack the shaft with a hammer (the steering shaft, that is, can you dig it?)
* a real steering wheel puller
* get lucky and have it pop up the second you take the nut lose (it happened to
me once)
* remove the nut, and take a long leisurely drive down hwy 1 or some other
curvy road - GUARANTEED to remove the wheel.

up the threads pretty good getting the wheel off.  Wolfgang was actually able
to fix it.  I was impressed!
