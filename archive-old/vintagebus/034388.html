Hi,

Most likely culprit is the link pins.  If they have been left ungreased
 the bearings within can become *very* rusty and sieze.  Probable effect
of disassembly is destruction of a brown rusted mass meaning that your 
bus will be off the road until you get replacements.  But a replacement
link pin kit for  a post 64 buses are cheap at around US$50?

Many places/books advise that you note how the 8 shims (per link pin) were
arranged (at each end) and replace the same way.  This is not technically
correct and
the shims should be arranged so that you get the correct offset on
the torsion arms (offset is around 8.XXmm - I never have my books here
at work).  The shim stack is actually how you sorta do set your camber
on the front end of a bus...........
