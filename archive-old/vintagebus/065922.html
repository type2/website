Subject: RE: [VB] batteries and cold

Batteries, and much else besides superconductors, don't work well when cold
because chemical reactions are slowed way down. That's what makes storing your
dry cells in the freezer great. The natural chemical reactions inside the
battery are slowed way down extending it's shelf life. Of course you have to
let them warm back up to become useful again.
