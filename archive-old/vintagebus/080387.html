Richard, don't feel isolated. I have seen this with more than one T-4
motor. This problem was real prevailiant on the 411 and 412 and the
early 72-74 busses befor they finally got a handle on it. It still
occurs on high mileage T-4's. The problem is mostly from poor "O" ring
seals on the push rod tubes. The early ones were terrible and the newer
ones were better but still tended to dry out, crack and leak. The busses
that had the most problem were the ones who saw the most heat under the
heaviest loads. Those are air cooled head and that little black "O" ring
is weaker than the heavy T-1-3 push rod tube seals, although those can
get hard and crack too.
