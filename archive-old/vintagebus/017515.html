"Adam Crosier" <acrosier_at_completebuserv_dot_com> wrote:
:  Whenever I hit a decent bump, or jerk the clutch, there is this hideous
:  knock in back, reminiscent of when the nose cone mounting bolts came loose

Could be shocks, tranny mounts (front or back), loose engine bolts
(although I'd expect some trouble shifting, since that would make the
clutch not fully disengage), or a dying torsion bar.  The shocks would
be most suspect if it only happened when hitting bumps, unless you're
really getting the engine revved *way* up before dumping the clutch.

So, I'd check the engine bolts and the tranny mounts.  Of course,
tranny mounts can fail in such a way that you can't see the
failure...trying lifting the tranny a bit with a floor jack (don't
lift the entire vehicle, the idea is to see if the mounts give any,
not rip them apart so you *have* to replace them).  The tranny has
three mount points - two next the engine, and one up front.
