Well it's official.  After paintstakingly installing the remote oil filter
"just so" and test firing the bus, I've still got that popping noise.
Getting underneath the bus and switching off the trouble light while
reaching up and bumping the throttle shows me a ring of fire and soot around
the #4 cylinder to head seal.  Damn.

So my options for BNNTA are to take that old standby 1600 and "help" it out
a little bit with my dual 34ICT's, and 1.25 ratio rockers or get the 1776
pulled, tore apart, and new heads put on.  Funny but I might do both.  I'm
going to do a little research tonight and see what kind of heads CB could
send out to me.  My brother says for a good reliable bus engine I should
keep the compression at 6.9:1 (where it currently is but with new heads I'm
going to have to yank the cylinders and do the CC work all over again) & go
a ported head with stock valve sizes.  I know some of you have emailed me
with good suggestions for heads to run so I'll go back and re-read that
information.

I hope to order the CB heads tomorrow and in the meantime, start dismantling
the engine tins on this 1600 and get it powerwashed and cleaned up just in
case I have to use it for BNNTA.  I'm still optimistic but I must admit that
this first time engine rebuild is starting to turn into a pain in the ass.
