In a message dated 5/31/2002 9:28:11 AM Pacific Daylight Time, Nathan writes:
> Tonight while removing the axel from my 1960 parts bus we decided to remove 
> the steering box also, unfortunately after removing the four bolts on the 
> box it would not come all the way out to separate the steering wheel from 
> the box. How do you separate the two? 

Hi Nathan,
Rebolt the steering box back in place.  Remove the nut under the horn button 
and either use a hammer on a piece of 2x4 under the steering wheel to pop it 
loose of the tapered fit they have and/or use a propane torch to warm the 
inner part of the steering wheel to make its tapered hole swell slightly and 
loosen on the tapered shaft of the steering box.
