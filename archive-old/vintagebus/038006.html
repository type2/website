methods of bus ownership and maintenance (I can fix it and change a starter) 
vs. (buy all new parts, dont work on it as much).  Me I have a forever 
Schucks (same as kragens and checkers) starter in my 65.  It died a year and 
a half after I got it, while I was 450 miles from home.  Went to the local  
Kragens and traded it in, no big deal to change a starter for me.  Still 
going fine years later.    Got one in my bug too.  But since I can do the

generators out of my piles of mystery dead ones.  Any one want to buy a cheap 
generator?   get some space under the workbench.  I find electrical things 
die two weeks out of warranty, and the new bosch generators have a one year 
warranty (I think).  so I would rather have the forever warranty.
