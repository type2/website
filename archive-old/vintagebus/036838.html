Hi Paul,
The problem with this is two fold.  One, which size of pulley are you talking 
about?  Secondly, where is 0 degrees?  The only way to find out is to either 
feel for TDC #1 with a screwdriver with the plug out, and/or use one of those 
degree pulleys as a tool to show you where the marks on your stock pulley 
are.  For us, this blueprinting the stock pulleys is the only use for the 
aluminum degree pulleys.
Eric&Barb
PS.  Best investment in non-static timing lights is the type you can dial 
adjust.  In otherwords when you have it set for 0 degrees it flashes at this 
point on the pulley.  When set for say 30 degrees BTDC it again will flash at 
the 0 degree mark to show you that it is sparking at the 30 degree point 
without having to place marks all over the pulley.   Eric&Barb
