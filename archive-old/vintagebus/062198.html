     Anyway, I got a new battery, the old one was six years old and only kept 
9 V in it.   But still, sometimes click click, dash lights dim, no start.  I

switch to starter push on terminal.   maybe helped a little, maybe no change.

    The thing is years ago I had hooked up a wire from the push on of the 
starter into the engine compartment.  If the key didn't work, I'd open the 
engine lid and touch that wire to the hot terminal of the battery.   Worked 
all the time, unless the battery was low. well, that's easier than crawling 
underneath and crossing the starter with a screwdriver, and told me the 
starter was good, but I am still wanting to use the key .

the 4 brushes were all really good.  bench tested- started every time.   SO

like.   Annoying.  put it back in the bus with dielectric grease at the 
connections all clean.
