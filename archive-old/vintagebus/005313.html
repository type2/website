Rex:
2  formulas for rating various air compressors:

compressor rpm = motor pulley o.d. x motor rpm
                          divided by compressor pulley o.d.
pumping time= tank vol. (cu.ft.) x pressure differential
                      divided by 14.7 x scfm delivery

*pressure differential is the remainder derived from
subtracting the  "on" psi from the "off" psi, for instance,
if the compressor starts pumping at 100 psi, and shuts off
at 175 psi, the pressure differential is 75.

in general, compressors that turn  750 rpm tend to last forever
and run much quieter than the high-speed oilless type.
the fill formula will tell you how long you can expect to wait for
the compressor to catch up to your work.
