Dave, if it's just the crank shaft pin holes that are ovaled out the
best thing is to echange the crank because they are cheap. The next
thing you can do is to have the crank pin holes deep drilled. This is
not recommended but they do make extended pins for this. I have done it
for hard to replace stroked cranks and dune buggy/sandrail engines and
retorqued to 300 lbs. If it's just your flywheel I'd replace the
flywheel. There is also the option to oversize to the next larger pin,
but this makes the crank an unwanted core in many places.
