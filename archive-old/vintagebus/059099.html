Your oil pressure gauge and oil temp gauge can certainly tell you when
your engine is warmed up, but they don't tell you what your choke is
doing. For that you have to put down the phone, turn off the stereo,
and listen. You can hear the idle drop as it bumps down each of those
little steps on the fast idle cam. When it stops dropping you've
gotten all the work you are going to get out of your choke until it
cools down again. If you are not confident the choke has unloaded,
just run around back and look at the fast idle cam. If you really want
a gauge to tell you, you could get a tachometer.
Got carb preheater, thermostat and flaps? Those are vital to speedy
warming.
Warming up the car is a good thing, but why have you set upon this as
the cause of gas in the oil?
