Best way to figure out jetting in any engine is to install a much smaller 
main jet.  Say a 110 then experiment with smaller and smaller sizes of pilot 
jets till you find the size that runs the idle with the air/fuel mixture 
turned out 2-1/2 to 3 turns and shuts the engine off when this screw is 
turned in a turn or two back in.
  Then once you have an idling engine you can try experimenting with the main 
jetting.  If your accelerator pump is functioning properly and in the later 
34-3 PICT carb is adjusted for the proper injection amount you can then 
experiment with bigger and bigger main jets going from the 110 you just 
installed.  What you are looking for is a main jet that is just big enough to 
prevent that AWFUL bog  in the lower to mid RPMs.
  Once the pilot jet and the main jet are set properly you can experiment 
with the air correction jet.  Best way to do this is to install a air 
correction jet that is too big and then drive the Vw in the 2,500-3,000 RPM 
and see if the engine bucks/coughs.  Then proceed to install smaller and 
smaller air correction jets till this bucking/coughing in the higher RPMs 
just goes away and go one size smaller to keep it on the slightly rich side.
