Now that I've said that.....

You don't have to slit the gaskets to get them to fit.  Drain the trans
and expect a mess.  Loosen the axle boots and take off the nuts/
washers holding the flange to the side cover.  Slide the boot down
the overtube to clear the studs.  Determine the thickness or how many 
paper gaskets/shims there are and replace with new ones. Be sure 
that the sidecover area and studs are very clean.  I like Loctite #518 
($$$) for a flexible sealant and I think that Eric and Barb prefer
Permatex Aviation sealant (??  sorry if info is incorrect).  Make sure
that the gorilla that installed the flanges last did not overtorque and
dimple the holes where the studs go through.  You need a flat, clean
surface to ensure the reseal will work. I paint mine sealant on using 
an acid brush- try and get a coat over the shim surface (both sides) 
like a fingernail polish or a thin coat of paint.  It doesn't need to be
an inch thick to work correctly. I also like to coat all of the shims 
while others only do the surface that contacts the sidecover and the 
axle tube flange.  Reinstall and refill the trans and keep fingers crossed.
