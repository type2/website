I'm not sure my input is necessary, but I agree with the gentleman from CA.
I recently "retired" my '67 Kombi in the interest of safety.  I figured that
although it did run and the brakes were strong (even if the drums are a bit
worn), it was getting to where *something* was going to go, and I couldn't
afford to fix it correctly.  Not being able to stop is a problem worth
keeping a bus off the road, but also IMHO coming to a halt in the middle of
the interstate because the tranny locks up, or the engine does Something
Bad, is just as dangerous when the average speed of the other cars hovers
between 80 and 90mph.

I'm not interested in endangering my own safety and that of others to enjoy
my bus.  I skipped HCBF even though I probably would have made the trip ok.
What I'm interested in now is fixing up my Westy so I can be out of the
Che*y Spectrum I'm driving, and back into the vehicle that I love, safely.

Two cents from an infrequent poster.  ;-)
