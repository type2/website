My first instinct is, if it ain't broke, don't fix it.     You are just
beginning in the world of split dom, so check it out a while before you decide
on major modifications, which such a conversion is.    Drive it, and think
about what you want out of it in terms of performace and hill climbing
ability.    It would probably be a good idea to get your bus tached out to see
what rpms you are doing at what speeds/gears.  Then go from there if you
really feel a tranny swap is what you want.  You would find it easier to
switch from a 4.37 R&P (if that is what you have ) to a 4.12 (which I find
quite freeway practical) or a 3.88 R&P (higher top end good for flat cruising
but less hill climbing ability) than to an IRS if what you want is a bit more
speed without rev the gajeebas out of the motor.

My 61 when I got it had a little nut tranny the PO said (whined a lot)   That
got dumped in favor of a $35 swap meet unit which turned out to be a 3.88
center section with 1.26 (big nut) reduction gears.  With a fresh stock 1600
SP it is quite the cruiser, for an old splitty at least, though certain hills
I have t o make sure I am at a good torque speed (55-69) or it will slow down
noticably.    My 65 westy had a 4.12 R&P with big nut RGBs, and was geared a
bit lower, so I went a bit slower on the freeway (closer to 55 than 60) and
was better on hills.  I would have left it, had not it started popping out of
gear.   Replaced with a 3.88, which is not as nice as in the 61, which has a
fresh motor.   The 65's motor has many miles on it, who knows how many, and is
a bit less that perky anymore.       And has a wierd oil leak now, thoughit
has served quite well.   To get a faster bus, you really need to put a lot
into it, and i still say if it aint broke, dont beat yourself on the head.
My 59 truck (whic may be sold  :(   has a little nut (1.39 RGs) with I think a
4.37 R&P, and is low geared.  I can haul stuff up hills like you  wouldn't
believe, but I aint going 60 mph, keep it about 52, that's the sweet spot.
Taching it notwithstanding, I like 52 in the truck on the freeway, other
drivers, however, may not.  Sucks to be them.      (geez, what a b&tch I am).
I like the slow truck.  I am not sure I would go with a 3.88 R&P next time.
Anyway, a tranny swap just isnt so fun, you may want to wait until it is
warmer, then decide.
