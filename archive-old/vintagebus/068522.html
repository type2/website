Hm were you using the tar to lube the seal?   I paint/smear a thin layer on 
the bottom edge and corners of the windsheild metal area, but not actually on 
the seal.

I've ripped the seal when installing too.   To prevent this, go slow. and 
careful, and the friend who is applying gentle pressure with spread fingers 
of both hands on the glass should press only gentley.

Then the breakthrough idea! oil the channel for the string!  After the seal 
is on the glass, I use the squirt can and with my little finger run the oil a 
gentle squirt every so often, all the way around.  this makes it easier to 
get the string in the channel, and easier for the string to slide past the 
lip (or the lip past the string) when you pull the string.   yes, a warm seal 
is more flexible. 

Yes, it'll be safe to drive to the DMV with a split lip.  In fact, It'll 
probably be safe to leave it like that, though it'll look funny.   Just as 
long as the split isn't on a corner.
