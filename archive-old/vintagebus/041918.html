When i start a 4000 mile summer trip, I pack 6 quarts of oil.  This is
enough for two oil changes.  The used oil goes back in the bottles and
goes along for the ride.  If I have a mishap that drains my oil, I'm not
going to be very picky about the fact that the oil I have already has 2000
miles on it!

The oil and drain pan live in the engine bay, opposite the battery.  With
a roll of paper towels, fresh gaskets, and a pair of latex gloves, I can
get through the oil and valves in about a half-hour with little cleanup.
The owner of the "Cactus Motel" in Thermopolis WY recognizes me each year
as "the guy who's always working on his car."  What he doesn't know is
that
oil changes are about the only work I do on the road and that his concrete
parking lot is a particularly nice place to work, so I fudge the mileage a
couple of hundred miles one way or that other to do the work there.
