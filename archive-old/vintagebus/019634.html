You need 3 things to run an engine - air, spark, and gas.  The trick is
to get the right amounts of each at the right time.

The fuses should have nothing to do with the bus.
 
Do you have an idiot guide?  If so, you can follow the procedures in
there.   Think of it in terms of getting the juice to the gas.  The
juice comes thru the ignition thru the switch, to the coil, out the coil 
to the condesor, across the points, thru the plug wires to the plug, which
spark and *bank* - there you have it.  Follow that path and make sure
each of the pieces is working correctly.  You didn't mention the condensor,
for one...  Take one of the plug wires off and set it close to something metal,
like the engine tin, whilst your buddy cranks the engine.  Are you getting
a bright blue spark?
