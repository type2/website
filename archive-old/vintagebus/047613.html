depends on how much you want to spend. and whether you want a stock or fat 
motor.   

I have a 69 beetle that I got 9 years ago for $100, its the greatest.  Cut 
wheel wells and apron (cheap baja!!!!), ratty funky paint, pretty solid (no 
rust holes when you look under car)   I have a mystery motor that I dont know 
the history of in there, works great.  Seems to have a mild performance cam 
by the sound of it, is full flowed (big plus), 8 doweled crank, runs fine, AK 
case, DP, swivel foot valve adjust screws, Doghouse, thermostat stuff 
installed, have a 30/31 pict carb on it, can get g forces with it, 25mpg, 
stock muffler- put a new german one on there a year or so ago.   I 'm happy 
even if one of the spark plugs is stuck and I don't take it out. the motor 
was pretty much free. three years ago.

if you are building a motor, best to stick with a later case in terms of the 
bigger oil passages and the beter alloy, any of the later A cases, the 
factory installed case savers ones are my favorite.   try to avoid anything 
that has been machined a half dozen times.   you definately get more pep out 
of the same motor in a bug than a bus.  I personally prefer SP, and would 
recommend a less than 8:1 compression ratio for longetivity.  berg says what 
6.5 : 1.   A new stock 1600 sp  feels very powerful in a bug.  

are you sure you need a complete rebuild?   Have you had a compression check? 
  do you have crankshaft endplay ?
