Real experience should probably take precedence over book learnin', but
here's an interesting tidbit...

M 175  Rammschutz  211-244, 271-274
M 176  Rammschutz  261-264  (Exportlaender)

A literal translation of "Rammschutz" is "ramming protection".  What's
interesting to me is that M 175 is supposed to be for panels (Kastenwagen),
standard (Neunsitzer), Kombi, "Samba" (Neunsitzer-Sondermodell), and
ambulance, while M 176 is supposed to apply only to single-cabs.

Another tidbit...forty-one years ago Friday (5 Jan 1960), from 554 147,
all Buses were supposed to get rear bumpers standard.  If your Buses
straddle this line, that might have something to do with the codes...
by default, a Bus built before then might not get bumpers, so over-riders
are called out on his Bus (implying bumpers too).  If yours is after
that date, bumpers would have been standard, so no code.  If they don't
straddle this line then all this goes out the window.  :)
