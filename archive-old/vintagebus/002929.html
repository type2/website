Hi folks,

After reading through the owner's manual for my '67 Westy (thanks Thom -
excellent reference), the only thing really new to me was the paragraph on
the air cleaner intake flap:

"The weighted flap in the air cleaner intake pipe must be free to move in
the winter and the cool seasons. If the average temperature is above 50° F
(10° C), the flap should be fixed open by jamming the lever under the ridge
on the intake pipe."

I've been driving this bus for almost 10 years, replaced the long block with
a rebuilt one 2 years ago, and never knew this. Before the bus I had a '73
Super Beetle (my first car - auto-stick...ugghhh!), then after I totaled it,
I put its engine in a '64 beetle body (wish I still had that!)....anyway,
paper filters until I got the bus in '91.

After reading this, I went ahead and "jammed" the flap under the ridge. I
haven't noticed any difference in performance, but if that's what VW said to
do, that's what I'll do. Is this a "warm-it-up" thing? If not "jammed", the
thing opens as soon as you give it gas, so I assume it must be something
about keeping the air warmer while idling?

Anyway, after all the literature I've read on air-cooled VW's, I've never
heard of this. Not even from Muir. Then again, I don't have Bentley's guide
(yet).

I would bet there's a lot of people out there who don't know this, but
then - is it really important?
