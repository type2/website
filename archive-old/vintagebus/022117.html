But in an auction, there is no "transaction" until the last swing of the 
gavel.  That last swing is the instant the auction is over, and all bids 
are accepted until then.

As a side note, there have been a few auctions where I was bidding against 
list members & I recognized their names and emailed them to see how bad 
they wanted the item.  If they were going to have a final bid higher than 
my final bid, then I did not drive the final price up.

Thom & I did this for each other a couple of times and I think we both 
benefitted from it.

I often bid on literature & am amazed at how much higher the prices go than 
you can get the same piece at a show for.  I usually bid $5 less than what 
I could get the piece at a show.  I have only won a couple of times...

Don't you guys have literature sales guys at the shows you go to?  Stuff 
that goes for $30-40 at shows I have seen listmembers bid $70-80 for!
