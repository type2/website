The JC Whitney article you mention works ok.  I had a small alominum
block machined for fittings for the oil cooler lines and had it drilled
to fit the sensor, which I epoxied into place.  The block is small
enough that it heats up to oil temperature quickly (don't mount it
directly to a large heat sink like a fender well unless you inulate
it).  Gene Gerg offers a complete setup, I've not seen one.  The
downside to this is, of course, yet another pair of fittings in the oil
hose assemblies.
