Well, we are talking about swingaxle reduction box VW bus transaxles...

People go out & buy a "Freeway Flyer" tranny and wonder why they don't 
go much faster than their original tranny...  There are 2 ways to build 
a "Freeway Flyer":  you do it correctly with a taller ring & pinion, or 
you do it with a taller 4th gear.  If you do it with a taller R&P, you 
retain the same 1st-4th gears and their spacing.  If you do it with a 
taller 4th gear, the rpm range between 3rd & 4th changes & when you are 
climbing that big hill & drop to 3rd, you have to slow down even more 
to keep your engine from racing.

Here's where the big lie part comes in...  If you buy a cheap "Freeway 
Flyer" for your bus and do not specify a bus 4th gear, guess what you 
get?  A beetle tranny with a flipped differential, which means that 
with a beetle 4th gear, is not much better than the original bus tranny.
Most bus trannys have keyed pinion shafts & most beetle trannys have 
splined pinion shafts.  To use a bus 4th gear with a beetle pinion 
shaft requires machine work that you will not get with a cheap tranny.
