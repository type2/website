Yes, fuel tanks (of any type) are more dangerous empty then full.  The
reason is that an empty tank can have a mixture of air and fuel present
at a ratio that might be immediately combustable.  The whole mixture
goes off at once, releasing a bunch of energy.  The military uses this
effect to create fuel-air explosives, the most powerful explosives in
their arsenal short of nuclear.  In Vietnam, FAE were used to create
"instant landing zones" in the jungle by blowing down all trees for a
radius of more than a hundred yards.  Whoooom!

I'd disagree here.  It's never a good idea to put a tank inside a
closed vehicle, and all recreational vehicles put their tanks either
outside the vehicle or in compartments vented to the outside.  If you
put the tank inside and you have a leak, you will create a lovely FAE
bomb out of your entire bus.
