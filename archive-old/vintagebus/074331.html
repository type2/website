Just from raw experience with dozens of dune buggies and performance engines,
the stock oil pump in "out of the box" configuration will deliver adequate
pressure for a stock oil system. Once you full flow you have to increase the
volume to keep your pressure up. I tried blue printing factory 26 mm oil pumps
and got good results, but they always lacked in full flow applications, mostly
because the large filters are just restrictive enough to kill the pressure. I
use blue printed Melling 30 or 32 mm pumps for full flow and I use the
filtered 30 mm pumps for non full flow apps in big blocks. You usually have to
sand down the body to get the 2 thousands max clearance with the cover on but
after that they seem to work pretty good for open engine applications like
trikes and dune buggies. I like to take my oil from the oil cooler holes and
route it to the external cooler and the oil filter. The fittings for most of
these adapters are 3/8" and I have never had a dangerous pressure drop. Plus
the oil filter slows the oil down in the cooler an additional amount of time
to extract a little more heat.
