the nose come on a split case trannyis different that a tunnel case tranny.  
Both where it bolts onto the tranny case, and where it attaches to the bus.   
  

What this means in putting a tunnel case tranny in a split case tranny bus is 
that you need to remove the front tranny mount from the TCT and use an 
adaptor plate.  You can buy this from someone, or make one out of a sturdy 
piece of metal by drilling four holes in the right place- evident when you 
look at the SCB, so the mount bolts to the SCB and to the TCT holding the 
nose cone from bouncing up and down, messing up the shift linkage etc. 

You may also find that the bus is very loud.  I recommend using the sandwich 
kind shift coupler with split case tranny buses (mid 59 and earlier), or with 
this set up at you do not have the rubber mount which helps insulate the 
noise and vibrations.   Switching to the sandwich kind of coupler lessens the 
noise.

You do not have to grind or make any permanent modifications.   The rear part 
of the tranny bolts up the same, the RGBoxes bolt up the same,
