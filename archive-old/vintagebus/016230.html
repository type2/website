That'll work fine (well, as far as mating up is concerned, I wouldn't
run an engine like that).  The other way around fails, because the
fresh air tin on the muffler interferes with the engine cooling tin.

If I remember your original posting correctly, it sounds more like the
J-tubes from the front exhaust ports are either too short, or are too
large in diameter.  If the former, they've probably simply burned away
some.  There are "repair kits" available which are installed by
cutting off part of the old tube, sliding the new one on, and putting
a clamp on it.  If the existing tubes are too big around, then you've
probably got such a kit, but it was installed incorrectly (or someone
with a welder got over-enthusiastic).
