Additional info to add to the general discussion:

1.  Cable modem technology may or may not be available in your local area.
If you have Time-Warner as your cable TV company you can check if it is
available to you by going to http://www.roadrunner_dot_com.  You can put your
zip code in and it will tell you.  If you have TCI as your cable provider
the Cable modem service is called _at_home.  You can check into availability by
going to http://www.tci_dot_com and entering your zip code.

2.  DSL stands for  'digital subscriber link'.  I wont go into geeky
details, but the end result is that it gives you fast internet access and
runs over your existing telephone lines.  You need t check with your local
phone company to see if it is available in your area.

3.  As far as computer systems are concerned, Compaq, HP and IBM (and
Gateway too!) all make excellent systems for home use (if you do the
go-to-the-store route).  All are about equal  in price and performance.
Your best bet often is to see who has the best prices on any given day.  If
you do the on-line thing, you might want to look at Dell (or Gateway if
there is not a local store around you).

4.  You dont have to give up AOL if you get a cable modem or DSL connection,
_but_ you will still have to pay the $19.95 a month in addition to the cable
modem/DSL access fees.
