I queried Coleman about their claim that these heaters are OK in enclosed
spaces.  Their e-mail reply said they recommended an opening of at least 6
sq. in. for ventilation.  That's a hole only 2"X3", and I'd personally be
pretty leery about sleeping with one of these heaters going and only that
tiny bit of ventilation.  They are sending me some documentation and a copy
of the operating manual, but I don't have it yet.

The trade association for these devices still says a flat "NO" to using any
catalytic heater in an enclosed space, especially while sleeping.  I asked
Coleman about the contradiction and their response was the comment mentioned
above.

FYI, Coleman also makes a version of these heaters with a battery-powered
fan to aid in circulation.  I'm considering one of these for our camper, but
want to inspect one before making a purchase.
