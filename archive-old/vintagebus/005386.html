Hi,

Basic thermodynamics says that efficiency increases as the compression
ratio increases. Diesels use this fact. I just read an article in an
old book that suggests that increasing the compression ratio of a VW
engine from 6.6:1 up to 8:1 will increase the horsepower by 8%.
Increased HP with negligable change in displacement means more power
from the same amount of fuel.
Of course, high compression ratio's require high octane fuel to
prevent detonation.
