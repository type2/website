I have to agree with these observations.

Many assume that any inaccuracy in their speedometer is duplicated in their
odometer.  This is rarely the case.

The odometer is driven mechanically, through a gear train in the speedometer
head that's, in turn, driven by the speedometer cable.

The speedometer needle is driven by a magnetic field.  There is no direct
mechanical connection between the speedometer/odometer gears/innards and the
speed indicating needle.  This is one reason why speedometers lose accuracy
over time.

For example, the speedometer error in my '70 bus is ~ 7 : 5.  The odometer
error is .2 miles in 10 measured miles.

With my current tires, true 60 MPH is an indicated 82.5 MPH.  I have the
corrections at various speeds memorized, and use them instinctively, but for
the benefit of uninformed passengers I've posted a table of speed
corrections in a prominent place on the dashboard.  If nothing else it keeps
the screams of mistaken terror down to an endurable level.
