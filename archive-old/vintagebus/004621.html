>The stock transaxle in your bus has substantially higher gearing to take

>What type of distributor are you running? I hope it's a stock vacuum
advance unit. They make an enormous difference in the bus's ability to start
from rest and pull up steep grades. The ubiquitous "009" distributor is
totally unsuited for a transporter/bus in "normal" circumstances, let alone
the rigorous conditions you are encountering. If you are running an 009, you
need to rev the engine up and slip the clutch to get going on a grade and
that's not conducive to long clutch life. I speak from long and painful
experience driving a Westfalia in the mountains of the Pacific Northwest...

Hum, I did not know this.  Yes, we have the 009 and I wonder if it would
help to change it out.
