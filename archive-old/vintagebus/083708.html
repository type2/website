On Sat, 11 Jun 2005, C. Dreike, Advantage Automation, Inc. wrote:

> George and anyone else,
>
> I was trying to come up with a way to test for belt slippage short of taking
> a direct rpm reading of both the crank pulley and the alternator pulley as
> rpm is increased, and then taking the data and checking the speed ratio of
> the two.

Pretty tough.  The only way I can think of is to use a digital strobe
light to determine the RPM of the two pulleys while the engine is held at
constant RPM.  Digital strobe lights aren't exactly household items, and
the cheapest run $200+.  Perhaps if you find a friendly physics teacher to
help you out!

Now, in the unlikely event that the pulley ratio is close to 2:1, you
could use a conventional timing light against a mark on the generator
pulley to see if the mark apparently advances or retreats.
