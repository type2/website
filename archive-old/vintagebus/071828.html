Hi Jim,
Yes, we just hammer out the upper left pressed in nut plate and use the 
earlier bolt washer and nut.  Then when installing the doghouse engine leave off 
the exhaust tube and exhaust shell till you have the upper left nut and bolt 
tightened.  The problem with the later doghouse type bolt,nutplate setup in an 
early VW is the rear clutch cable arm is curved in the way and installing the 
later straight arm requires replacing the rear clutch cable boden tube holder 
(Plus there are two versions (Long or shorter.) of the straight arm and thusly 
differant rear boden holders!!!) to aim the rear end of this boden tube exactly 
at the eye in the clutch arm.  Not doing this results in 6,000-10,000 mile 
clutch cables due to rubbing/wearing out on the missaimed end of the boden tube!

OK we are guessing you have a doghouse shrouded engine.  For this type of 
shrouding you MUST use a 33, 34, or 35mm wide fan measured between the discs that 
make up the body of the cooling fan.  Using any smaller width fan will cause 
a doghouse shrouded engine to have cooling problems!!  If you have an earlier 
non-dog house shroud you can use the fans up to 29mm, but using a larger fan 
you can find is just going to cool the engine better.
