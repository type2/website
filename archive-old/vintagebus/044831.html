That might work!  With carbs the best thing to do is try it in steps and 
remember that the pilot jet and air correction screw are controling the idle. 
 While the main jet begins working just above idle right after the 
accelerator pump gets it started moving up in RPMs.  Then the air correction 
jet should lean out the mixture by allowing bubbles of air to be sucked into 
the fuel going to the main jet in the higher RPMs to prevent the main jet 
from over enriching the air/fuel mixture.
