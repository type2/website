As long as it is a 356 or early 912 engine (not a 76 912) it will bolt right
in. You will have to modify the tin and also fab up some throttle linkage.
Porsche used solid linkage (no cables) and have a bell crank on the back of
the fan shroud where the throttle rod connects. Try not to destroy the
original engine tin as it is rare $$$. You will also have to modify the
exhaust to fit, don't destroy the original muffler if it still has it, I
might have a used header in my Porsche stash. Be prepared for some great
RPM's, depending on which engine you have the red-line is anywhere from 5500
to 7000 and you can cruise all day at 4000 without a problem, but watch out
for the lack of low end torque.
