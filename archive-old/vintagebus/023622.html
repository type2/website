when a clutch cable breaks,  it tends to be that some of the strands break, 
and the cable seems to stretch out, so you have to adjust it.  You could 
probably find underneath with a flashlight where it is unravelling.  If you 
dont want to drive w/o a clutch maybe you can bum rides for a few days.   No 
it is not a weird cable.   

211 721 335 B   Cable - clutch (length 3116mm) (LHD) 
up to chassis no 469 505 and from chassis no 835 705

211 721 335C Cable - clutch (length 3110mm) (LHD)  
from chassis no 469 506 up tp 835 704

I think you can use whichever of these, 6mm is not that different.  If you 
had a RHD barndoor, those take a 3200mm clutch cable, so you have to use a 
longer one.

Ya- get a new clutch cable.  It;s a good idea to carry a spare.    I have had 
clutch and accelerator and brake cables go, which is a PITA,   So I started 
carrying spare clutch and gas and even sometimes brake cables, and that has 
kept me free from  a single cable breaking in about 6 years.  grease the new 
one when installing.  

therrre could also be a problem with the TO bearing assembly, but if that arm 
breaks, the pedal would suddenly go flat, no clutch action.
