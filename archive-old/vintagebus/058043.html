somewhere in the wires between the positive terminal on the starter, the ig 
switch, and the starter on push on terminal the wires are losing the 
electricity.  I think the click is the solenoid trying to do its thing, but 
stopping cause there isn't enough voltage to turn the engine.  the 
screwdriver bypasses the wires, so you get the volts.

Well, first I'd clean the battery terminals and ground, and the terminals on 
the starter that you screw on with the 13mm or 14mm nut.  Then clean the fuse 
box, the light switch terminals, everything in the starter pathway (check a 
wiring diagram if the light switch does not make sense.  The big fat red 
wires on the 30 terminals are what hss the problem.  undo the battery before 
cleaning the terminals up front, as they are always on.  This can really help 
the purity of your voltage flow.   check it with a volt/ohmeter if you have 
one and feel like it.
