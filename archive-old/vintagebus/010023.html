Well I went back out there and readjusted the brakes again.   Success.  I
_thought_ I had adjusted them before; they were really not even close (live
and learn).

I just took it on the 'victory drive' around the neighborhood, and was I
ever surprised at how much better it handled.  Before there was a bit of
play in the steering; even the slightest movement of the wheel would make
the bus veer to the left or right (we're not talking a hard left into
oncoming traffic, but some definite movement).  Never having driven a bus
before 8 weeks or so ago, I presumed that was the nature of the beast.  I am
guessing that the replacement bearings made all the difference in that
one!!.

As far as the brakelines were concerned, I never thought about replacing
them until I was into the job.  I am going to tackle the rear brakes on
Thursday and Friday (I've got vacation time to use or loose in the next
couple of weeks); I am going to replace them then.

Thanks again for all the encouragement and the advice;  I would be lost
without all of you.
--Rich
