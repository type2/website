Who cares, I have had buses for sale and nobody in the states wanted them,
so overseas they went (England), had 1 go to Japan and the majority of the
ones I have sold stayed in the states and are being used here. But I also
know of buses that were bought by white folk for overseas shipment under the
premise of "Oh cool dud this is the bus I've always wanted and will be part
of my life forever" as it's being loaded on the flatbed for the docks. Does
this mean that we all should run an FBI background check on anybody who buys
a bus to make sure it doesn't go overseas? We all should be thankful that
buses are popular, regardless of where they go, with all this activity a lot
of parts are appearing, lot's of parts never made are being made now and
this isn't because of the few of us that have had buses for a while, but
because there has become a huge demand for the parts because of all the
buses being resurrected we all have benefited. Look at how many of the
listys only had 1 bus, now they have 2,3 or more. Popularity fueled by the
fire of demand. Then if we don't like buses going overseas, should they take
the same attitude and quite shipping parts over to us. Remember that very
few parts for our buses are made in the U S and all come from overseas (as
did our buses originally),
