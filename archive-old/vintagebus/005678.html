The tires you have are D rated which means that they have an 8 ply
construction. You should definatly run them at 59 to the max of 65. Any more
and the center line will bald, and any less and the edges will bald and this
is a fact. The tires were designed to have this higher psi in them. The old
manual or shop psi ratings for the bus tires dont apply in the least because
the tires are completly different.

> No, in the Ford/Firestone case, Ford's recommended pressures were
ridiculously low, on the order of 25PSI.  VW's pressure specs for
> radial tires are much more realistic.  The cause of tread separation (way
oversimplified) is excessive tread deflection.  The maximum
> pressure printed on the tire is sufficient to limit tread deflection at
the maximum load specified on the tire.  D-rated tires will handle much
> more load than they will ever experience on a bus, so the pressure may be
safely reduced without causing excessive deflection.
>
> Tire manufacturers publish charts that specify the optimum pressure for
various loads.  Savvy tire dealers will have these, but such dealers
> are few and far between.  You need to have the actual load on each tire to
use these charts, since the load will be different for front and
> back tires.  I have a feeling that the tire manufacturers specs will be
very close to VW's recommendations anyway.
>
> > Plus with higher pressure and stiffer tires you will have better

not
> > automaticly change lanes inadvertantly!
>
> I really haven't noticed any difference in handling with a 10PSI reduction
in pressure.  The stiffness of the thicker sidewalls, not the
