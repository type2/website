During my welding classes ( I will admit it was almost 20 yrs ago ) they told 
us that any time a fuel tank was to be welded, fill it with sand first. 

How do you get the sand out?  

When I was working at a radiator shop in California some years back, we used 
to hot tank gas tanks to clean them and we cut an "X" in one corner to get 
out the caustic liquid.  After that, we just used this huge iron (soldering 
iron - heated by a torch away from the tank) and soldered the hole shut.  
Much safer than welding if it's feasible for your repair.
