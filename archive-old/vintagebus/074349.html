Hey Cal,
sorry it took me a day to get back to you. Unlike E&B who e-mail from the
comfort of their home and have all their info in front of them, I e-mail from
work and don't! This is my CHT head and sensor info that I have red lined into
my trusty Bentleys:

I use a NAPA part, TS5550 _at_ $38, since it is widely available and it lists the
following numbers it replaces:
0280 130 012
213-758
25174210
022 906 041
022 906 041A
311 906 041A

As you can see, it covers Porsche 70-73 and 75-76. 1974 was a weird year for
VW, Porsche and Saab/Audi while they were trying to contend with the newly
released pollution control laws put into effect in July of 73.

VW T-1 and T-3 were covered 1970-1985 all years.

The sensor is a variable resistance part with a M10 X 1.0 thread. The
resistance is 1700 ohms cold and 190 ohms at 212 degrees. The temp sensor
location is a threaded boss above and slightly to the rear of cylinder #4 and
that is why the temp range is so low. The  cylinder head shroud had a 1/2"
hole in it facing aft and the wire came through a small hole in a rubber
grommet. I used a 1 ma gauge with a trim pot and a paper face that I had drawn
a temp scale on and copied off into my Bentley book. Lot of work but easy to
reproduce now 20 years later.
