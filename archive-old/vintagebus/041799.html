To further this lovefest, I'll add that the VW, unlike "modern" cars, was
designed without air conditioning in mind.  The fresh air vents are
generous and the side windows allow good airflow.  The hot bits are in the
back, so you don't get hot air from the engine heating the floor.

My '71 even has side wing windows that can be left open without allowing
access to the inside door locks.  My bus is always much cooler than
"modern" cars after sitting in the sun for a while.

Also, VW didn't paint the top white for style!  If you want to be even
cooler, a roof rack with a deck of plywood will lower interior temps by a
few more degrees.

The engine?  No worries as long as the cooling system is up to snuff.
Unfortunately, most of the buses I see in distress suffer from bad seals
and missing hoses/tin, etc.
