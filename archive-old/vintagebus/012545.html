Child safety seats are generally required to be attached through the
existing restraint system, not directly attached to seats, so your
strap-on idea won't work.  How could you ensure that some bozo
wouldn't attached the thing to the non-latching folding front seat of
a '50s 2-door car and proceed to crush their kid.

There is a 4-point child harness that attaches to a lap belt on the
market now, so that idea is gone too.

As far as patent data is concerned, I suppose that you should consult
with a patent attorney.
