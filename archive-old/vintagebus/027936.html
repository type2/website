That appears to be an early to mid '50s "Allstate" trailer frame.  These
trailers were made for and sold by Sears & Roebuck Co. from the late '40s
thru the late '60s both as complete trailers and kits... price ran from $12
for base wheel kit to $60 for complete trailer with canvas cover.

Anyplace that has old Sears catalogs on microfilm( I know Dartmouth college
has a set on microfilm, Sears Co. archivist told us that...) can show you
these rigs...
    My buddy has one fairly complete trailer with a steel body and a later
frame and wheel assembly.  He paid $250 for the complete trailer (swapped a
new utility trailer to the painting contractor who had it hooked to his ford
pickup) and $60 for the frame/wheel assembly.

The big thing when using these on a Bug or KG is to be aware that they put a
heck of a strain on the bumper brackets when hauling any kind of weight in
the trailer.  My buddy has broken 2-3 brackets on his '70 Beetle even with as
little as 150# in it...  These weren't meant just for VWs (altho they were
really ideal for them), and on the huge Detroit iron of the times the bumper
hardware was substantially heavier duty...
