actually, I find that my bus cools better at 3K than at 3500rpms, (seems to 
cool best around 50mph (2600).   I don't know that this means your motor has 
the same optimal cooling rpms, but it would be interesting to know.  

you can check for yourself by driving on the flat at each speed after the bus 
has warmed up, stay at say 50 for ten minutes, then go to 55, ten mins, then 
60, ten mins, 65, what are your temps?  so you know where your bus runs 
coolest.  Find your optimal cooling speed.  There is a curve between heat 
produced by engine, which goes up with rpms, and cooling from fan, and at 
some place the extra heat produced is more than the extra cooling produced by 
the higher rpms.

do you have a guage, or just one of those dipstick things that flash when the 
oils over 225?
