I have not done this, but I don't see why there would be any problems.
AFAIK, the axle shafts are the same 55- 67, and the tubes bolt on the same, 
and the RGBoxes bolt to the spring plates the same.

The only thing I would be careful of is the fulcrum plates.   They were 
changed in late 59 (after the change to synchros tranny) and the earlier ones 
if you lay the curved side on a flat surface do not rock end to end.  The 
later ones when checked the same way do rock end to end.  Just make sure you 
have the same kind on each side of a particular axle shaft spade end.  I 
mentioned this as it's usuall to keep the best FPs to put in your tranny.

r/p gearing of the split case is slightly higher than 4.38, if I remember 
right.
