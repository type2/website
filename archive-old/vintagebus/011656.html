Originals are of a wood composite material, like a very thick
masonite.  They have a tendency to delaminate if wet for any
length of time.  I considered making replacements of solid nylon
block--which would slide easily and not be affected by water.
Turns out mine weren't so bad and I just put them back in.

> 3) Has anyone installed a sunroof cover?  The one I got from
> W.C.M. has no instructions, and the pictures in the Bentley are
> kind on lacking.  Any tips would be appreciated.

I had an upholstery shop actually attach the cover to the front
bow. I did the rest.  There is one key element in attaching the
cover to the front bow.  On the bottom/front lip of the front
bow, there should be a thin bead of rubber weatherstrip that goes
between the metal bow and the cover fabric.  When the sunroof is
closed, this hidden W/S forms the seal at the front of the
sunroof to keep water from blowing in as you drive.  Most
crucial.

From the front bow back, it's fairly straight forward.  Install
the rear bow bolts loosely at first.  Then close the roof and see
if it's too tight or too loose.  The holes in the rear of the
fabric should be oval (or at least round/big enough to slide it
back and forth under the rear bow that screws to the rear metal
roof.  You may get lucky and have it fit the first time, or you
may have to fit and adjust until you're satisfied.  Each time
you'll have to remove the top of the front bow so you can fold
back the cover to expose the top of the screws in the back bow.

Once that is done, the small screws/brackets on each side are
fairly simple.  There are two pieces of metal sticking out there;
one is the bow itself, one is a thinner and shorter piece on top
of each side.  This top piece fits into the cover, so the cover
has to have a slit cut (about an inch).  There should be two sewn
seams on the outside of the cover.  The slit should be cut
through ONE THICKNESS ONLY from the underside about 1/4" from the
inner seam.  Make sure you know what I'm talking about before
cutting the cover!

Once all pieces are in place, the screw goes through:

1) Finish (cone) washer
2) Top layer of fabric
3) Short bracket
4) Bottom layer of fabrick
5) Into bottom bow
