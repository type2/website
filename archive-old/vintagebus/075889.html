sometimes when the shoes aren't centered one edge will be caught up on the 
drum.   If you can get the drum on, stomp on the brakes a few times and see how 
that is. That'll also center the shoes, shift them over, like.   Does that 
change the drag on the shoe/drum interface?   If the drum won't go on, I tap the 
end of the shoes to center them so the drum will go on.  You have to look at 
the end of the shoe to see where it drags, and tap it slightly the other way.  

So ya, though you are supposed to adjust first, just adjust where you can at 
first, fill with fluid, bleed, and them adjust again.  Bleed again in 50 miles 
or when the pedal gets more space, whichever is soonest, as the shoes will 
still center a tad, plus the edges will wear in quick at first.  I avoid getting 
drums turned unless they are badly grooved.

Don't forget to adjust your brakes and grease suspension at every 3K tuneup.

carefully and make sure the end of the shoe is in the slot in the wheel cylinder

 Because of this being easy to do, I usually put the shoes on with the dust

cylinder with a little screwdriver, making sure it is settled on all sides.
