from my experience with lowered buses (not that I have owned
one, just from 
being a passenger and observations) it does not seem to make
sense to lower a 
bus if you do plan on hauling loads with it, unless you dont
mind the 
scraping.   The scraping can be the bottom of the steering gear
box and the 
lower part of the muffler.   this can happen on going into/out
of driveways 
with an unladen (1 person inside, no extra gear) lowered bus
(with the 
wolfgang lowered spindles/straight axle rear end).   Also, if
you load it, 
the tires can scrape in the wheel wells, eating up the tires.
  If this seems 
okay to you, and you dont mind the scraping  (wear on tires,
more frequent 
muffler replacement), carry on.  If your tow set up is one that
does not hang 
weight on the rear of the bus, that really should not make a
huge difference, 
and may be the best way to go for hauling if your bus is lowered
(small 
trailer).
