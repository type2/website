Okay, this is a Me Too on the cleaning battery connections. 

When I lived in Logan, UT, temps would be below 0 deg. F. most mornings
throughout the winter, with a 2 week period that would be -20F or less.
My winter prep routine was oil change (lighter weight), complete tune
up, and pull/clean battery cables on both ends. By doing this, I could
reliably start my Bus w/1600cc & 1647cc DP engines, 12v system down to
-15F. Below that took about 3 mins with battery charger to warm battery
and blow dryer to the carb.

Making this part of the autumn routine is ideal for cooler climes, since
the starting system is more stressed in the winter *and* it's a more
miserable time to be stranded. I've gotten less diligent since moving to
Salt Lake, as winters are milder. I think I'll do the winter routine
today.
