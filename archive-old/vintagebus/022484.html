At 10:29 PM 3/9/99 -0800, Chris Poage wrote:
>I have a 64 sundial conversion bus  with the 2/3's 1/3rd seats up front and
>There is nothing left worth saving except the frames and springs. So my
>question to you guys is whats the cheapest way to recover/cusion theese
>seats and still have it look some what similar to stock (I like that look)
>What do you guys suggest? What did you do?

I bought mine from Lenny, and it's not cheap, but it's so close to stock
you can hardly tell the difference.  As a cheaper alternative, you can use
sewfine or TMI.  Sewfine's stuff is made well, but doesn't look stock.  TMI
is one step lower.

For the '55, I put a layer of denim directly on the springs, then a layer
of this soft brillo-pad like substance, then a layer of 1" foam.  On the
bottoms, I put a layer of 3/4" carpet padding between the brillo and the
foam.  These seats are the nicest I have *ever* sat on, and the cushiest!
