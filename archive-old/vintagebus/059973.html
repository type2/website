I think all head light switches get hot... even in newer cars... like my 93
ev.

To clean electrical connections, I'd simply use hot water, soap, and small
toothbrush to clean up those wire ends and screw terminals.  Things like
switches can be carefully wire wheeled but be careful not to chew up the
bakelite bases.  Wire wheels do big damage to wire insulation.  I actually
prefer using a sisal wheel (or hemp or other natural fiber bound wheel) to
clean up delicate parts like switches and the like.  That's how I did my
speedo recently
http://www.vintagebus_dot_com/gallery/image/2000390.JPG

My friend that does electronics says that coin collectors prefer to use
Worcestershire sauce to clean old coins as opposed to vinegar.  That foamy
stuff is designed to neutralize acid on a lead post.  Your copper wires and
terminals only need cleaning of dirt and the small oxide layer.  Once you
cinch down the screw terminal, the cutting action between the screw and the
wire breaks that oxide layer and produces a gas tight seal.  By the way, as
far as I can tell, wire ends for screw terminal connections are either
tinned (soldered) or use the small copper barrels that slip over the wire
end and then into the screw terminal.  Note that these metal ends are not
usually soldered (but you could just for that added help in reducing
electrical losses).

Loosen the dimmer switch from the floor and spray some electrical cleaner up
into the switch and work it a bit.
