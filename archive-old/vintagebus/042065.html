Good.  Anti-sieze is exactly that.  Keeps the threads from sticking.

> I can't remember who wrote about this - my intention is to just have a
> clarification on this.  It seems to come up often.  Any input on what I might
> be missing would surely be helpful.  Thanks in advance!  Flames welcome.

A conductive paste is of use when trying to make an electrical path
between two hard-to-connect materials.  If you connect copper and
aluminum, for example, you must exclude air and moisture from the joint or
you will invite corrosion and a bad connection.  At low voltages this is
important, but on the high-tension side of the coil the spark will jump!
