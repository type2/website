Do you have the bulb numbers, from the packaging or the bulbs
themselves?  Without the numbers, you could measure the current
through one with an ammeter and estimate the wattage.

I have a Wagner bulb catalog and it lists the following.
Dia is maximum diameter, len is overall length; both in
inches.  For the "Euro" bulbs, the 12V xW designation
is listed with the base type in the catalog (ie SV7mm(12V 5W)).
These bulbs also have pointy end caps, just like Bus
fuses.

These "American" part numbers aren't right for a Bus
but are commonly sold in US parts stores.  They
either have wire loops sticking out of the glass at
each end, or a metal cap with a flat end, like a
US-style glass auto fuse (3AG, SFE, etc).
