If you can do bodywork, you can do the wheel.  It is the same process.  Take
your time and be a perfectionist.  You'll be staring at it quite a bit!  Use the
epoxy...it will last longer.  Use quality primers, sealers, and top coats and
you will do as good if not a better  job than  Kotch.   Only you can decide if
the time + money equation works for you.

If you decide to do it you might want to get the book that eastwood sells.  It
has a few tips for piecing together really thrashed wheels.  Good luck.

Steve
