On Sat, 4 May 2002, Dan Kinsey wrote:

> Looking for opinions on the bottom tin...the pieces that attach to the
case and the heater boxes.  The local shop owners believe them to be
gravel guards to protect the push rod tubes from flying rocks.  Others
think you have to run EVERY piece of the tin in order for the complete
system to work.  I'm running the type 3 or "Cool tin" on the bottom of my
cylinders.  I wanted the air exiting the bottom of the cylinders to blow
across the oil sump, theoretically cooling it (while stopped) so I made
"shorty" bottom tins.  basically cut them at the first case attachment
screw tab.  Figured that would be good enough to keep the gravel out.

Why do you want to blow hot air across the sump?

the goal of the original tin is to get the hot air out of the back of the
bus as quickly as possible.  If you just blow it out the bottom of the
engine, it has more of a chance to recirculate.
