Nope. On a 1600, it comes in from the passenger side and exits on the
drivers side. If I remember correctly, other mufflers had other
arrangements (mebbe the fresh vs stale-air 40hps?).
The gaskets tell the tale: the gasket with a LARGE hole goes on the
heatriser leg that lets IN the exhaust gasses... in the 1600 case, the
right (FIF) side; the gasket with the SMALL hole goes on the 'exit' side -
this creates backpressure  designed to "hold" the warm gasses in the riser
to better enable the heat to warm the carb.

Inlet side cokes up first.
