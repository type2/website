        << The way I picture it, there is only two times the #1 cylinder

 only reached twice....the dizzy will only have the rotor bug pointing at

 least, this is what I thought.  I have found when I rotate the engine to

         Why is this such a common confusion?  I mean...I understand why we

Well you only need the rotor to point to the notch in the distributor when #1
cylinder is at TDC on the end of the compression stroke for that is when you
want to burn the air fuel mixture in the #1 cylinder.  When #1 cylinder is at
TDC at the end of the exaust stroke the rotor in the distributor will be
pointing at #3 cylinder when it is at the end of it's compression stroke.  How
this is done, is that the cam gear is twice as big as the drive gear on the
crankshaft so the crank has to turn fully twice around to the distributor
point at #1 cylinder at TDC at the end of the compression stroke.  If the
distributor pointed at both TDCs of the compression and exaust stroke you
would have a real backfiring problem from your VW because both of the valves
are open during the end of the exaust stroke.     Eric & Barb
