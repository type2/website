Michael Evans <MichaelE_at_limon.co.za> wrote:
:  Can anyone tell me why both of the rear brake shoes that have the e brake
:  lever attached to them would have worn out completely and both opposing
:  shoes have not. The worn ones have about 1mm of material left while the less
:  worn ones still have at least 7mm.
:  
:  Also, the worn(e braked) shoes have worn at a slight angle ie. more on one
:  edge than the other.

Something to check is, with the hubs off, watch the shoes while
someone else steps on the brake.  Do both shoes move, and if so, do
they move the same amount?  If not, I'd be very suspicious of bad
wheel cylinders, or possibly of the non-worn shoes hanging up on the
backing plate or at the fulcrum/hinge at the non-wheel-cylinder end.

Also check to make sure that both shoes are seated down properly.
That angular wear sounds like the piston/shoe connection wasn't done
straight.  That seems less likely, because it makes it really hard to
get the hub back on.

:  At first I thought that it was clogged flex lines but I figure that that
:  would affect all the rear shoes. Is that assumption correct?
