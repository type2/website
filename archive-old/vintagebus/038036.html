Hi Steve,
You might want to check the clutch disc just inside of the clutch material 
for any signs of rubbing.  If nothing there you should try replacing the 
pressure plate and see if that makes a differance.  If no improvement then at 
worse you have a spare pressure plate on hand for the next time.  It could 
also be a bent clutch disc or it's splines are too tight on the input shaft.  
The rear tip of the input shaft could be worn too much causing the end of it 
to wobble in the FW gland bolt.  We have had problems with a slight amount of 
oil getting to the clutch and creating a vaccume between the disc and FW.  
This tended to affect the VW only at start up at first and sometimes second 
shifts and then would go away.  Found that scoring by hand the FW disc 
surface about every inch or two radially measured at the OD with the side of 
the tip of a drill bit relieved this vaccume.   Eric&Barb
