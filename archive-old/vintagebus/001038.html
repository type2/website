OK, I've checked the archives and sermons, read my Bentley and Haynes and
started in on this job, but need a little help.

Previously I asked about solutions to my broken redux box mounting bolts and
from those responses determined that I need grade 8 or better bolts.  The
12mm-1.5 bolt with a 19mm head had 10K on it and several other places (but
not the reduction box section) Bentley mentions use only 10K bolts.  When I
went around trying to find replacement bolts, the help I got ranged from the
clueless, "I don't know what grade that bolt is" to the more knowledgeable,
"That's a hardened bolt, I know we don't have those."  Is grade 8 good here,
or do I need to get something more specialized?

Next, I tried to easy-out the broken bolts with the axle still in the bus
and ended up with a broken easy-out, so I have come to the conclusion that
the box must come out.  The best clue I got from my manuals was, "use a
special puller that grips between the inner and outer races" to remove the
lower, outer roller bearing before removing the box cover and use the same
or similar to remove the upper outer.  I used to have one of these special
pullers, but can no longer find it and it has been so many years that I
can't remember clearly how it worked (I only used it once).  So my next
question is where can I get one of these pullers?  Does Grainger have one
that works, or do I order it from Harbor Freight?

Finally, the responses I got and the archives indicate a couple different
approaches to my ovalled out hole.  One is to drill out for a 5/8 bolt, then
grind off the 5/8 bolt flush and drill and tap for a 12mm-1.5 bolt.  The
other is to drill out for either a 12mm or 1/2" helicoil.  If the ovalling
isn't too bad, I think I'll go for the helicoil approach, but my first
problem is to get out the broken bolts.

My last resort is to replace the whole assembly if I can find one, and save
the repair for the future.  This approach is probably the most likely to get
my bus on the road soonest, so I am pursuing both approaches.  In either
case, I will need those hardened bolts and the special puller, so those
searches have the top priority.
