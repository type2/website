Vacuum Tuning . . . when you think about it, it's obvious . . .

You got a set of duals for example , , standard procedure says use
a "Uni-syn" or some other device . . . what is this uni-syn device
reading?   Flow rate would be a good answer but Vacuum is more correct. 

 To read an air-flow rate directly, you would have a vane
propellor right in the flow path . . not cool so it is read indirectly
with a pitot tube or in our case an orfice on the carb side versus
an opening to the atmosphere on other side of ball thing . . 

So we're looking for max flow rate . . strange as it may seem, that's
where you are drawing the max vacuum . . .

 So you do one side to  max and then match the other. . . and you have
graduations of at least 30 inches vac. . . .

 Some, by now, see that you can also tune a single carb this way . .

 In this case, you're just looking for the max vacuum . . .

 I leave finding a source of manifold vacuum as one of those "student
exercises" . .  :-) anywhere below the throttle plate but as long as 
you're just looking for a "max", it shouldn't matter where you get it.
