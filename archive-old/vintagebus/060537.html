The forty HP engine is a good one for durability due to being such a low HP, 
but the more you hot rod it the less durabilty you will have.  The other 
problem is that you could install big bore HP P&Cs with a 69mm crank, but the 
40 HP heads and intake manifold will not breath well enough to make it 
worthwhile.  If you want a 40 HP engine that is a little bit more HP either 
go with a set of big bore P&Cs or the 69mm crank (Preferably 
counterwieghted!), not both.  The latter will require 40 HP rods machined to 
fit 1300-1600 connecting rods.
