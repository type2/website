Everything is bigger in Canada, eh?

Make sure the shoe "tangs" are fully seated, wobble the shoe back and forth
to see if it's really in and the slot of the piston isn't canted to the
side, and then open the bleeder screw and "squeeze" the shoes in.
Sometimes, if you've been playing with the pedal while they're apart, the
pistons will come out and not go all the way back down.  

Also check to make sure you have the ebrake setup on correctly, if applicable.
