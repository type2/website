> Do you have one of the repair manuals?
> 
> The front mount is a rubber-centered, sorta rectangualr thing.  The
> transmission bolts to it, then it bolts to the body.  The rubber and
> adhesive deteriorate over time, and it comes unglued.   When it does,
> the weight of the engine on the transmission causes the front of the
> transmission to lift.  This puts strain on the coupler, and nose cone.
> If left too long, it will crack the nose cone, bend the shift rod,
> and possibly damage the shift selector.
> 
> To replace it, you will need to slide the transmission back about 3"
> or so.  Ideally, you should replace the coupler, front mount, and the
> two rear mounts all at the same time.  You could also do the axle boots
> while you're at it.  Yes, it's a solid afternoon of greasy work, but
> you won't have to worry about this stuff for a while!
