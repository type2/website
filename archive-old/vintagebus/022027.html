Both the Splitty Bus and Beetle shared the same style
of transaxle housing so its a fairly easy swap.  You will
have to pull the diff out and install it on the other side of
the pinion as you stated.  You will also need to remove
the large diff support bearings and swap the shims from
side to side as well.  Those are what centers the diff in
relationship to the pinion head and if you simply flop the
diff over you can either cause the pinion backlash to be
too tight or too loose.  No big deal there either- those
bearings will come off either with a large puller or if you
are careful you can knock them off the diff section with
a hammer.  Oh yeah, you will need to use the Bus nose
cone and shift selector rod to line up with the coupler
and shaft from the shift lever.  The Beetle nose cone's
set up is too high and the angle of the dangle for the 
selector rod is incorrect. 

As far as the proper width goes you will need to use a
redux axle set up with brakes from a Bus or (oh no!  here
we go again!) fabricate a straight Beetle type axle w/o the
redux assy. and use Type 3 backing plates and drums.
If you plan on keeping it stock (and I *recommend* that you
do) then go for the orginal style reduction gear box set up
and do not consider the Type 1 straight axle way.

The later Baywindow post-'68s use a completely different
style of transaxle than the earlier Split windows.  My advice
is to stick to stock with some minor modifications to upgrade
it to a more modern useage requirement.  Much, much easier
than trying to shoehorn a later 002 transaxle into that Split
and properly set up with different factory gearing than it came
with orginally the redux set up will give excellent service and
driveability.  Or even easier than trying the dreaded Beetle trans 
and no redux box set up.

Thats why probably over 2/3rds of the early Type 2 redux
transaxles that I do are actually converted Beetle boxes.
For the most part they are identical but do have a different
3rd and 4th gear ratio.  You want the Type 2's ratios as
with a late Bug 3.875 ring and pinion (vs. the 4.375 or 4.125 
that your Split has) and the post-'64 redux box ratios you
can cruise down the highway at 3200 +/- rpm at 65mph
all day long.  Once you've been in one of those converted
Type 2s you'll never want to got back to the old 'stock'
ratio'd gearboxes- its as different as night and day.  Have
you been in an Split-window and actually passed someone
on the highway without worrying about punching a rod 
through the case??  You can with these gearboxes.
