From E&B...
>The thing is you do essentially the same thing by hooking a hose to the tip 
>of the bleeder valve.  Sticking the other end of this hose in a jar with a 
>little brake fluid in the bottom that is enough to cover the open tip of the 
>hose.  Then when you pump the pedal the old fluid with any air is pushed out 
>the hose into the bottle.  When you release the pedal the hose sucks up a 
>little of the old fluid, but no air.  VW has had a special bottle/tool for 
>this for decades.  

You can get a plastic bottle, magnet, hoses and clamps from most FLAPS and UFLAPS. Idea is to attach it higher than the bleeder so the air bubbles flow up and purge faster. The crappy clamps won't, I use a ty-wrap to make a better friction fit.  Makes it a one person job.
 Whatever you do, don't use a beer bottle. And if you do that, don't have an audience to witness the inevitable.
