Subject: Lowering my bus...

Yeah, well that's a load of...
...bricks!

Went to HD today and picked up 100 paving stones to make a small driveway.
I pulled up to the loading area, gave the guy my receipt and told him what
I needed:
HD kid: OK, where's your truck?
Me: It's right here.
HD kid: "No, I mean where is *your* truck, so we can put these in it."  
Me It's right here!
HD kid: You're going to need a truck to carry these home.  Did you bring one?
Me: *Yes*!  I'm putting them in my bus.
HD kid: You can't do that.
Me: Why not?
HD kid: Because they won't fit!
Me: Why not?
HD kid: (now with that peeved 'I know what I'm doing attitude) Because you can't!
Me: Watch.  Bring the bricks 'round and I'll show you.

So they bring a pallet of bricks, and they fit, of course.  I'd already done
the dimensional homework in my head.  A small crowd watched us load the stuff,
most of them wide eyed in shock.

When I got home, I weighed a few of the bricks.  They weigh about 19lbs each,
so that's 1900lbs of payload.  They bus was a big sluggish, and I would have
done well to have the tires up to proper pressure first.  Braking was a challenge,
but I took it slow and had no issues.
