Subject: Re: [T2] Torquing large nuts w/o torque wrench, was torque dude

It's not geometry, it's physics - simple reality. In particular, it's
the mechanics of rigid bodies. Force at a distance. Torque is the result
of applying a rotational force. This force is normally applied at a
specific distance from the center of rotation, and the torque, measured in
foot-pounds in this case, is simply equal to the magnitude of the force
multiplied by the 'moment arm', or the distance from the center of
rotation to which it's applied.
