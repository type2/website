Hi Clara, (and everybody else too)
Reading your post, something clicked regarding my bus troubles, wanted to
ask your opinion.  Regarding the advance sticking on a bus, I'm assuming
that you're checking the timing with a strobe light to find this problem.  I
don't have one so I've never done it but I'm thinking that maybe this was
the source of the overheating I've been experiencing with my bus.
I do know that when I brought the bus home for the first time, it started to
run really badly somewhere around Montana, stalling and generally running
crappy, but good enough that I just kept going.  (I was by myself and really
wanted to get home)  When I took the bus in to my (aircooled vw) mechanic,
he said that the distributor had been "sticking" and was the source of the
problem.  I guess he'd done something to make it stop sticking; the bus was
now running fine.  He's a crusty old German man so sometimes I don't really
understand what he's saying.
I also know that, in my quest to get the motor to run at less than 220 - 240
degrees, the only thing that seemed to make any difference was retarding the
timing.  If it makes any difference, I'm running a 009 distributor.
The only other thing that makes me suspect the distributor is this nagging
feeling I've been having that I should get a new one.  Now, you may think
that's crazy but I often rely on intuition when fixing the bus and it's
usually been helpful.  For example, this spring in Colorado I had a nagging
bad feeling about the fuel pump.  I suspected that maybe the rubber
diaphragm inside was torn and that it might be leaking into the motor.  So I
bought a new one and went to replace it.  It turned out to be fine
internally, but when I went to take off the fuel line from the gas tank, the
brass fitting pulled right out of the fuel pump quite easily!  Basically it
was a big fire waiting to happen.  Coincidence?  Maybe.
Since I'm currently in the process of taking the motor apart to rebuild it
(for real this time - you may remember that I just replaced the pistons and
cylinders and put on new heads at the beginning of the year, but I didn't
split the case) finding out why it was overheating is a major goal right
now.  I believe that 11 000+ miles worth of extended road trip driving, with
the motor running super hot the whole time, probably caused some of the cam
lobes to self-destruct.  And who knows what other damage.  Presently I've
stalled at the flywheel removal point: I've never done it before.  And it's
freakin' cold outside.  But I digress...
Thoughts, advice, ruminations welcome,
Diana
