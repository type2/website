Hi

Please Excuse me if you have heard this before....

I have a PDF (in French) that explains the air pressure compression
theory of the early type 2 engine bay. Many people have tried (most of
them in vain) to get more air flow thru the engine compartment in order
improve cooling. The tests that were done in this document state that no
matter what speed the vehicle traveled at air would only pass the
cylinder heads at a maximum speed of 15.5 liters per second. Unless you
are willing to radically modify the compartment.

Meaning that if you travel fast with a powerful engine at some point the
engine itself will produce more heat than the airflow can disperse,
causing gradual overheating.

Besides...busses were made to go slow...with a 1300cc engine my 66 will
would only do 48mph fully loaded (downhill)
