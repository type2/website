> Hi Wink,
> Right!  With the tubes sealed off to the heaters the pre-1972 bus heat
> exchangers just heat up and up.  They are designed so that cooling air goes
> through them not only when you have the interior heat on, but even when the
> interior heat is off.  The valve that turns on/off the heat to the interior
> of your VW is a double flapper valve that also opens (When closing off the
> heat to the interior.) a cooling opening in the back of the head that is
> shaped kind of like a shell of each heater cooling the exhaust.  Without this
> cooling of the heater tubes the exhaust gases are not cooled and condensed.
> Instead the gasses stay hot and continue to expand causing extreme back
> pressure against #1 & #3 cylinders which in turn causes more heat to be
> transfered to the heads and into the engine.
