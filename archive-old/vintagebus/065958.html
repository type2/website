Subject: RE: [VB] soldering fuse box

When I had my 71 westy and 65 DC I had a couple of tabs break off because of
age. I used 95/5 solder and my jewelers tip for the propane torch to solder it
back on. Regular solder is more lead than tin and is too soft. 95/5 is hard
after cooling and has a better tensile strength for the springy ends of the
fuse clips. It is standard code water pipe solder. Be sure to use the proper
flux.
