At 05:35 PM 4/6/99 +0200, Christian Figenschou wrote:
>At 4:11 pm 2/4/99, David Easterwood wrote:
>>The socket you need is 42mm or 1-5/8".
>
>I haven't seen anyone contradicting this 42mm claim, but I think it should
>be 41mm. I've done several redux box rebuilds and the socket I use for the
>inner axle nut is a 41mm (at least on big nut trannies).
>
>A 42mm socket may loosen this nut, but it is not the correct size and could
>slip and damage the nut (which should be replaced anyway, as you "damage"
>it when you lock it by hitting the shoulder into a groove that rbns across
>the threads). I would suggest that unless you are going for a similar
>imperial size, get the correct 41mm socket.

You may be right and I may stand corrected.  I use a 1-5/8" 6-point socket
because they are readily availible here in the US.  But go try to find a
41 or 42mm and IF you can find one, the price is almost double the 1-5/8".

A problem I have run into is about half the inner axle nuts in reduction
boxes that have been into before have suffered from the Muir inspired
"field repair" of beating the nut on with a hammer & chisel.  Not a pretty
sight, and hard to get off (or get a proper size socket on).

In a perfect world we would all use the right tool for the right job, but
sometimes we have to compromise or improvise for those who compromised
before us.
