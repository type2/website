Boat folks have been dealing with this issue for ages.  A good quality marine bedding compound ought to be just the ticket.  Marine bedding compounds are designed to stay flexible and watertight at all temps. while exposed to deluges of salt and fresh water.  Check with the marine supply of your choice, tell them what you're doing and ask for suggestions.  

I'm skeptical about the wisdom of rivets combining dissimilar metals, as mentioned in another post.  Seems like a setup for electrolytic corrosion [AKA 'electrolysis'].  Actually 3 different metals when you factor in the steel of the bed.
