Subject: Body shop prices (ouch)

I stopped by the only VW restoration shop in these parts (Minnesota)
yesterday, to see of he had anything interesting for sale, as I'm in the
market for a '68-'70 Beetle.  I saw about six cars marked SOLD, including a
somewhat rough but rust free '68, which was claimed to have gone for $3,500.

Anyway, in his 'showroom' was a freshly painted '67 21 window.  It was a
rolling bare shell, painted inside and out, original blue/white colors.  I
was sighting down the sides and boy was that sucker straight.  Probably
straighter and shinier than when new.  Just then the owner approached, and
I half jokingly asked "Did you replace the rockers in this one?", and he
said "yeah, both rockers, the rear corners, one dogleg, the outer floor
inside the side doors, and the lower pillars.  Look inside and you can see
the seams."

Sure enough.

"I could have made the inside look like original too, but this is a driver,
and those places will be covered up anyway."

OK, so the guy does good work.  Even the seams which are supposed to show
on the outside were perfect.  Now to talk turkey, as I have a '66 Westy in
need of rockers.  Last fall this guy had quoted me $600 per side to do my
rockers.

Me:  "How much do you get to do rockers in a bus?"

Him:  "Including paint?"

Me:  "No, just the body work."

Him:  "$1200 per side"

Me:  "You know, that's double what you quoted me last year.  Business must
be pretty good."

Him:  "Well it all depends on what you need.  Most will need crossmembers
underneath, lower pillers, etc.  Anyway, it's $75 per hour."


Maybe the problem was that I was wearing jeans on my last visit and chinos
this time?  :-)

Anyway, what kind of prices have folks paid for near-perfect rocker
replacement in a bus?  And/or what kinds of hourly rates have you'all paid?
