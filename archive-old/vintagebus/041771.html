> << What has the better chance of getting a seized plug out and saving the
>  threads...   Cold engine, warm engine, hot engine??? >>
> Hi Andy,
> Probably best to just have it a bit warm after starting and running it about 
> 5 minutes.  Aluminum of the head and other parts of the engine swell in size 
> faster than the steel parts.  So the threaded hole will probably be bigger 
> than the spark plug when just warmed up a bit.  

OTOH, the head will also grow in the axial direction of the plug faster
than the plug itself, so it will be tighter when hot. 

>   If that does not work try a hammer and punch to shock the plug into 
> releasing the head threads.  Next time you remove your spark plugs use 
> graphite (Pencil lead) to lubricate the threads of the plug or at least some 
> good quality anti-sieze material.

Good advice. be very careful to keep the anti-sieze material away from the
electrodes and insulator.

If you have a plug that is stubborn about coming out (gets harder to turn
while removing), don't try to force it.  Turn it in a bit, turn it out a
bit more, in, out, etc.  This will wear down the carbon deposits a bit at
a time and save the threads.  This doesn't work retroactively, though!
