No offense taken Dave.
 On one hand I agree with you totally.  I love all the things that are
unique to my bus that make it more than "just a car" and something that
no-one else understands.  I enjoy people going "Oh wow swinging doors"
and "Look at those cute little tail lights".
But on the other hand I enjoy a few of the "mod-cons".  I no longer have
the original radio (it wasnt in there when I got it anyway), I replaced
it with a kick ass Blaupunkt system (at least I kept it German), I have
an alarm and immobiliser with dual stage shock sensors and microwave
movement detectors (I could have used the door pin switches instead if
there were some - another special v.w. feature) to keep what's mine as
mine, the interior has been reupholstered in crushed velour.
There is no way that I could do anything like alter the ride height, or
drop in a bigger engine or anything like that.
I do enjoy my bus for all it's peculiarities, but I do enjoy some
luxuries aswell
