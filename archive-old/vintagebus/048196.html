are your shocks attached?    I had one unattached in my 59 when I got it and
it made a clunking noise.   Also check your steering and jack it up in front
and grease the suspension and check the kingpings by wiggling at 12 and 6 on
the wheel,  there should be no play, also wiggle at 3 and 9.   If wheel
bearings are loose it will cause play.  If youa re worried about the front
wheel bearings pop off the wheel while you have it jacked up and clean and
regrease them, replacing if there are bits missing or shavings.

take the handles off and to a locksmith (door panel off, two allen heads
screws to undo) and get a key cut to code-  which should be something like
T1234, usually on the little pin screwed to the inner end of the lock
cylinder.   Cut to code is better than cut to a worn lock- a local locksmith
here charges $15 and gives you two keys. Actually odds are the handles haven't
been removed, so you could just take one off.  Or take both off and while the
panels is out take the door mechanism out, clean it and grease it and see how
much nicer the doors open and shut.    (While the door is open, push the latch
thing vertical (like when the door is fully shut).  When you press the handle
button it should pop out enthusiastically.   If it doesn't I would take it
out, clean and grease it)
