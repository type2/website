Greg Banfill wrote:
> 
> Here's another variable for you whilst adjusting your valves....
> This is how I've done mine for years with no problems. I call it the 3-1
> method
> 
> Get engine to #1 TDC
> Adjust #1 intake and exhaust , #2 intake (3 forward most on right side of
> vehicle) then do #4 exhaust (rear most on left side)
> 
> Get engine to #3 TDC
> Adjust #3 intake and exhaust, #4 intake (3 forward most on left side) then do
> #2 exhaust (rear most on right side)
> 
> Much easier than turning the engine over and over bouncing from one side to
> the other.

> Greg

This is a great method if you have a brabd-new and stock crank.
Otherwise, you should really use the exact tdc of each cam lobe. The
sides may not be the exact same on either a hot-cam with non-stock ramp
or a cam that has been used/reground a couple times. 
I can do it the old way in about 8 minutes. Why take the chance that you
are setting them wrong?
