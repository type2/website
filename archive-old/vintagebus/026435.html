Thom Fitzpatrick wrote:

> Is there any way to electrically warm the water in a particular vessel?  I
> have a harbor freight parts washer, which uses a water-based solition, and
> if I could warm the water, it would be MUCH more effective!
>
> Is there something you can immerse into it to do this?

Thom,
there used to be a coffee heater that was made that could be used.  It was a
solid heating element that came out of a handle, made a couple of loops and
back into the handle.  The idea was that you plugged it into the wall, stuck
it into the coffee cup and a few minutes later, had hotter than hot coffee.  I
think the lawyers may have gotten them off of the market but I have seen them
at garage sales.  Unfortunately for the parts washer, it might take all night.

Another option is one of those heating elements that were used to start bbq.
Its about the same shape, but about 10x larger.

I was recently at a surplus place and they had a whole section of heating
elements that could be bent to the proper shape and immersed into liquid.
Might be worth checking into if you have any of those places up there.

In ANY case, what ever method you decide upon, do not connect it up and drop
into the tank without first going through a GFCI receptacle or circuit
breaker.  Your bus will thank me for enabling you to keep working on it if the
element happens to liven up your parts tank.

GFCI == Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter, used to be $150+, now $10 at Home
Depot

Scott
