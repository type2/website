	I have found from e-z outing many bolts at work (and having a fair
amount of e-z outs break off) that all that is often needed is to apply
some heat from a small propane torch prior to trying to drill through
it.  After getting it nice and hot, let it cool off on it's own.  By
doing this you've in effect "de-tempered" the e-z out/tap and now you
should be able to drill through it as easily as any normal steel with a
good drill bit ("easily" being used VERY loosely ;-) ).  I've never had
the pleasure(?) of drilling out a tap before, but the process I
described should work the same since e-z outs and taps are virtually
made from the same type of kryptonite.  I personally use carbide drill
bits.  While they are initially pricey, you couldn't pay me to go back
to those standard ol' black things.  As always, YMMV  Good luck.
