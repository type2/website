did you try greasing the moving parts?  My 59 has the same set up with the
metal tab on the support, and it does not screetch.   It was somewhat greasey
when I got it but I gave it more grease.  hey just jack it up, grease the
front suspension, and use the old grease that comes out to grease all the
moving parts on the pedal  and brake stuff.  Older style brake cables have
grease points too, and the older (63 and back) tie rods and drag link, and
some of the shift rod tubes even.  Grease it all!!!!!   Grease is good.  I
like grease, and so do VW s. 

Oh, the lock out plates are interchangable- so that should be great.  It is a
good idea to dump the old worn ones.   But all the stuff where the shift stick
is in the ball and socket joint and around the shift plate should be greased.
Did you put the new shift plaet in correctly?  Although reverse is back and to
the left, the reverse portion on the plate should be forward and to the right,
as it does a reverse mirror image thing at the pivoting point.   (meaning when
you pull the shift stick back, it pulls the shift linkage forward)

I haven't interchanged shift parts, and some will not interchange.  I'd put
the original shifting stuff back in with the new shift plate and grease.
But then again I guess I'm a vintage pervert.
