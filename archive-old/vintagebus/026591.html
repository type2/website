Joshua,
My heat works with the same effeciency as Eric and Barbs.Though below 10F the
stock VW heating falls off dramatically.
I do have a set of curtains dividing front from back(makes a huge difference).I
have all the heater tubes
insulated and I repaired all the leaks and holes.Also make sure your door seals
are actually sealing out the cold air!!!
Is your belly pan in place and all the seals for the pedals ?Is your overhead
vent leaking?
Also make sure your heater cables are adjusted correctly so when you turn the
heat on you are actually opening the vents in the heater boxes completely!
Another way of keeping warm is to have a nice wool blanket over you while you
drive.When temps. dip below 10 degrees F, which they often do here in Chicago,I
run a electric heater on a timer out to the bus and have it timed to kick on an
hour before I leave for work.This gets the cab temps up to about 50 and my butt
doesn't freeze on the vinyl seat.I also have a leather steering wheel cover to
keep my hands warmer.
        Good Luck,
                            woody

ps. I have also found that the bus stays the warmest if you have the defrost on
just a little.This also allows you to point your fingers over the outlet while
you are driving,hence a built in finger warmer!!!
