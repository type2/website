The SR-71 and X-15 did not get their black color from 29 cases of rattle can
paint from Schucks Auto Supply. Yes, they were black, but that's about all
they had in common with paint you and I can buy. The special heat absorbs
ion and dispersion coatings on the Titanium and stainless steel were
extremely porous and very flexible when the metal underneath expanded and
contracted. Besides the heat shielding coming towards the plane, not from
inside out, they also, on the SR-71, F-117, and other countermeasure planes,
provided radar absorbs ion capabilities, not required on the VW block. The
only thing that the coatings for the afore mentioned craft and your common
off the shelf paint have in common was color.

Back to the physics lab, compare apples to apples, not watermelon.

If my memory of high school and college physics serves me right, the color
