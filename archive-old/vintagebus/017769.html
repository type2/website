Hi Arron

Sounds like a vacuum leak, stuck choke
or a sticking valve.
Get a fire extinguisher handy, attach a tachometer 
to the distributor (or tune up your ear)
With the engine running at idle and not warmed up;
spray carb cleaner at manifold to head
and manifold to carb joints as well as any vacuum lines.
Does the rpm pick up? Then you have a leak.

fix it

Spray around the choke pivot inside and out.
You may consider disabling the choke 
all together if you live in a tropical / desert area
(look up "Carburetor procedure" and under  "about chokes"
in the idiot book for a simple explanation of this)

Still nothing solid?

Engine still running? OK
Pull a plug wire at the cap with a insulated tool
and note any change in rpm.
Do this one at a time for all four cylinders.
the cylinder that results in the least rpm drop is 
doing the least amount of work and may have
stuck  or sticking valve.

get a vacuum gauge 

$ cheap tool $

and refer to my notes at 

http://sites_dot_netscape_dot_net/labmission/vaccum.txt

Berymen's chemtool carb cleaner is the best for the above check
