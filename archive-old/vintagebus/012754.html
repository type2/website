IMO, there are no "P-metric" tires that should be put on a bus.
P-metric tires are way too low in capacity.  These tires were designed
for cars that use a much large tire size for vehicles in the bus'
weight range.  185-section tires were put on compact sedans.

Be sure that the actual load capacity (in pounds) is equal to or
greater than the specification for the original tire (usually around
1520 pounds)  The load capacity is the only load measurement that
doesn't change unpredicably with tire size.  Would you believe that a
195/75R14 load range C tire has _less_ capacity than a 185R14
_standard_ load tire?

You want the tallest, narrowest tire you can find, as that was the
shape for which your bus was designed.  Low tires spin the engine too
fast, wide tires cause steering box failures.
