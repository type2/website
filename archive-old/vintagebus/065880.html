When the prototype was originally finalized they knew what the growth of the
engine overall was and the final torque the head bolts became. There must
have been a lot of trial and error, but the VW engine block metallurgy was
similar to some of the aircraft engines they were also designing. Aluminum
and aluminum/magnesium cases grow at different rates and to a slightly
different point. The head torque for a type-4 on it's pure aluminum block is
different than a type-1/3 on the aluminum/magnesium block because of that.
