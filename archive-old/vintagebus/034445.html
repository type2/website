putting a new roof/sunroof roof section onto a bus is, of course, a lot of
work.  All must be carefully measured.    Probably cut it so that the top of
the window frames stays with the old roof, which means careful fitting so the
windows go inproperly.       With the new roof section on, the belly pans must
be added.   All the sunroof buses , and also the double door panels came with
these for added structural support, and any newly made sunroof bus should have
them too.   Das Bullie Haus sells the subfloor pans (at least the outer ones-
I woud check about the midde one).   I haven't seen them,  but hear that they
are perfectly servicable, even if not identical to the original.

The idea of cutting the skylights out is interesting.     I woud take a good
look at a sunroof roof before doing that.    VW made sunroof buses w/o
skylights (standards and panels) and they have the inner skin and
reinforcements around the skylight holes, though no outer skylight holes.
I don't believe they made them w/skylights and w/o sunroofs, though.   You
would want to make sure the reinforcements across the roof side to side stay
intact.    The reason for the small poptop, AFAICS, is that it is cut where
there are no reinforcements, and thus takes away less integrity than cutting a
huge hole in the roof w/o adding reinforcements.  It is just a covered hole to
stand up in.     I think the splitty sunroof is one of the largest, if not the
largest, sunroof, next to a convertable.
