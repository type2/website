Subject: Re: [T2] Re: [VB] Questions about the metal fuel line to fuel
  pump in 1600DP

I used a 3 inch piece of fuel line that was sliced down one side.  I placed
that over the metal fuel line and slide it through the tin.  I then placed
hose clamps in front and behind the tin to keep the assembly in place.

> > adequate fixes for this (Bob Hoover had one involving lamp parts and, I
> > think I recall that veewee111 recommends wrapping the fuel line w/
> > bicycle inner tube).
>
> Actually, that one is mine.  A 1 1/2 inch strip of inner tube wrapped
> around the line until it is just a bit larger than the hole in the tin.
> Makes a nice friction fit and if it starts to fail it will take a long
> time to split the entire strip.  The original ones I did with a strip of
> truck inner tube are still doing well over a decade late.
>
> I've never seen a pre-bent tube.  The tube comes from the hole in the
> front tin, passes between the manifold and cylinder tin, and then curves
> around to get a straight shot at the fuel pump.  When I replaced mine I
> just got a length of steel brake line (from a Chevy pickup, IIRC) and bent
> and tweaked it until it was the right shape.
