Subject: Re: [VB] hard starting

Have you checked whether your automatic choke (assuming you have one) is
working properly?  If it is broken or mis-adjusted and the butterfly
valve on top of the carb is wide open when you start the engine in cold
weather, this can result in hard starting.  This valve should be
adjusted (by adjusting the auto choke) to be open just a hair for
starting in cold weather.  See Type2_dot_com library for auto choke
adjustment procedure.  If the choke is broken (as is mine--I recently
discovered that the first owner broke off the tang that hooks into the
choke coil), a workaround until you have time to fix the choke properly
is:

1. pump the accel pedal a couple times for priming
2. open the decklid
3. turn the stepped cam on the left side of the carb to almost close the
valve, & hold it in place with pressure from the idle adjust screw
4. go back in the cab and crank until it fires (don't touch the accel
pedal until it does)
5. put pressure on accel pedal to warm engine until it will idle
somewhat on its own
6. close decklid (important final step before leaving!)

You may have to repeat this procedure once or twice, but it will fire up
quicker without flooding the engine.  I am doing this myself until I
have the time to rebuild my 30-PICT-1.
