By "frame is done" I assume you mean before you start putting outer body 
panels on. It is probably easier to get to blast some of the spots without 
the body panels.  Is there a cargo floor in it now?  Blasting now would also 
mean there is less cleanup required at welds.  Blasting later means you can 
get primer on it faster to prevent microrust.  

>>* Areas that are exposed, (ie underside of the front floors) would be
exposed and get a proper blasting and painting.<<<
Wouldn't the cab floor be exposed in either case?  Does this '54 have belly 
pans?  From the "belly pan to third outrigger" thread that was going, I seem 
to remember it does have pans.  Of course, these would definetly get in the 
way for later blasting.

>>* all parts will receive same primer treatment<<
So you mean you will leave the primer that comes on the replacement panels or 
old paint on clips?  I have always wondered about the repo panel primer.  As 
for Alan Schofield's, some of the black primer comes right off easy, and some 
is a real a bear to blast off.  I finally ended up torching some of the 
panels (carfully not to warp them) to get the paint off.  Don't know what to 
do inside the doglegs where I couldn't get the primer off.  I don't trust 
that black primer.
