 As far as quality of rubber, I'm not all that impressed with German
rubber.
  Fuel lines that I replace every two years generally have cracks in
their
 ends, where the SAE lines I put in my C*evy 5 years ago look fine.

I went to my local Aeroquip shop and found some hose that is high
quality neoprene with a SS braid inside and a cloth cover. Have to cut
it with a hacksaw or cutoff saw and it can be used with hose clamps.  It
cost around $2/foot (probably $4 these days).  It's been on the vehicle
for 20 years with over 200,000 miles and still going strong.  The end
near the carb, which is actually about 6" from the carb, has minor
cracks at the very end, but is still flexible. I have used the same
stuff for oil, trans, heater & P/S cooler lines and have never had a
failure.  The ends at the engine crack and get stiff from the heat, so I
just trim a little off the end when needed. Personally, I wouldn't use
any hose that has "fuel" on it, although I carry some for road repairs
'cause it can be cut with a knife.
