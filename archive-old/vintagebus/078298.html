Jamie, the diode in the coil circuit is prolly in parallel with the coil
for transient suppression purposes. My rec was for putting one or two
diodes in series with the coil. Each diode will drop about 0.7 volts, thus
raising the threshold at which the relay would activate. IE, if the the
relay required 8 volts to pull in, then two diodes would raise that to
about 9.4 volts. You could keep adding diodes or use a zener diode with
appropriate voltage rating in series, to set the threshold where you
wanted it.
