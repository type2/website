The hugest differece with a split is the handling.  Splitties have a firmer, 
nimbler ride.   They also tend to have a bit more pep than a bay, like if you 
compare one w/ 1600 to one w/1600, because the splitties are lighter.

That, and the aura is just more old school.   Funny, bays have always seemed 
newer to me, but they are getting old too.   Anyway, even a good running 
splitty probably has some delayed maintenace.   Myself, I'd go do the 60,000 
tuneup just to make sure.  Check the tranny oil.  (there are three fill 
holes, but you only check it on the side of the tranny) and the tranny boots, 
check the wheel bearings, adjust, and bleed/flush the brake system.  Grease 
the suspension including the king/link pins.  Adjust the link pins.

usually I end up removing the cab door opanels, & cleaning and greasing the 
door mechanisms, so the doors open and shut nicely.

If you have a upright bay, you're familiar with the motor, and probably what 
to bring for a trip.   You know, fan belt, spare oil, generator bruches, lug 
wrench, good spare, and jack, misc tools, etc
