> I need someone to tell me how I can supply power to the sending unit and

Well, the gauge/sender unit is rather simple.

+ into one side of the gauge, the other side goes directly to the sender
lug.  Sender is groundded.  Thats it.  a few wires on the table, and you
should eb able to move the float around by hand to check it out.   move it
to the "full" position, then the "empty" position, and in between.;)


Going to use the 6v gauge with that drop circuit that whatshiname posted?
I'll warn you, output voltage of the simple circuit is a dead steady ~5.6
volts or something like that.  So the gauge always reads low. (ie, when
full, it only hits the top hash mark, instead of pegging out...it just
means when you hit R you still have a few miles more then you normally
would.;)  I'm speaking using the 6v system as a reference, using the same
gauge and sender.;)
