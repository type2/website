>Unfortunately, for the most part stainless steel fasteners are not available
>in high strength grades. 18-8 and 316 refer to the stainless steel
>composition and only describe the corrosion resistance. 18-8 is the basic
>designation for 300 series stainless, roughly the same as 302. It has good
>corrosion resistance under normal conditions. Tensile strength of these
>fasteners is about 100,000 psi, slightly less than an SAE grade 5 alloy bolt
>(medium strength). 316 stainless has a higher corrosion resistance, it is
>intended for applications with exposure to organic acids, specifically
>hydrochloric acid. The strength characteristics are the same. It is possible
>to find stainless fasteners in the 400 series, which can be hardened for
>higher strength, but the corrosion resistance is lower as well as the
>availability. The price would  make your jaw drop. In short, use plated SAE
>Grade 8 fasteners where strength is an issue, otherwise, 18-8 stainless
>steel will serve for other situations. Be sure to use some anti-seize
>compound when you put them in to keep them from galling and seizing. Use
>copper or brass nuts on the exhaust system. HTH
>
>Craig Sauls
