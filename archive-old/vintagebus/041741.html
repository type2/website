Asking someone about their gas mileage is a bit like asking them about
their sex life.  The only person who is likely to tell the truth about
either subject is a priest.  I'll pretend I'm a priest, but I'll only tell
you about my mileage, not the other thing.

I get a truthful 18-20MPG in my '71 bus running a fresh Mexican engine.  I
got about the same with my tired original engine.  This is over typical
Western terrain of flat deserts and mountain passes and cruising at
57-60MPH (actual).

IMO, it is possible to get better mileage from a bus, but it would take
good tuning skills and careful driving.

If you were to go to a well-engineered closed-loop fuel injection and
ignition system, then 30 would be possible.  The cost would pay for a lot
of wasted fuel, though!
