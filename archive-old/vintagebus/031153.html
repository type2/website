Indeed, this is starting to look like a Friday topic...but before Thom kills 
it,I would like to mention that I think it is a mistake to comment 
despectively on the hippie lifestyle and their history with VWs, if nothing 
else because many of them had and have a great apreciation and knowledge of 
the VW bug and bus, and that is a lot more that I can say for most people in 
our society nowadays. I live in one of those  "hippie towns", and thanks to 
that culture I am able to enjoy a big vintage VW scene, availability of parts, 
mechanics, etc. If it wasn't for that,this would be another generic town with 
no vintage buses in sight. Hippies kept them running and away from the 
junkyards (in most cases). Please don't rush to generalize, many hippies value 
their VWs and get the principle for what they where built, enjoying them every 
day, camping in them, fixing them themselves, etc.
And yes, I do approve their principles, idealism and lifestyle, but that is 
another story...
