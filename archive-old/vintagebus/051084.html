We were all just ribbing you, we knew what you meant, it just gives a little
chance to get someones goat!

I had an odd one on a Brazilian 1500 block that the previous owner said the
shop could never figure out. He had it replaced at 45K and the new engine
ran great. I picked it up from him complete for $250 and It seem to run OK
on the stand, but had excessive oil pressure. I tried about everything and
then tore it down. Nothing was apparent with any of the oil related
componets and the oil galleys and crank and rod oil slots seemed OK. It had
me stumped for awhile untill a friend of mine wanted me to build a full flow
stump jumper for his dune buggy. When I took the block down to drill open
the galleys, the machinist called me up to come down and look at something.
I always have front and rear of the galley drilled and tapped and when he
showed me the galley down #3&4 side I couldn't believe it. There was enough
casting flash and lodged debri to increase any oil pressure. I had him drill
out both galleys and as far as I know today, that's the same engine still
sitting on the back of his dune buggy. Really wierd.
