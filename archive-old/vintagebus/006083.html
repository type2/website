Hello all....

I would like to say that I'm happy to be on "the list" again.   And happy I
am to have a good reason for it!  There was this ad, you see, in the local
(Santa Rosa, CA) paper, which read:
 1967 VW Kombi Van, 0 mi. on rebuilt trans. & reduction boxes. New 1600
balanced. New brakes & susp. Registered. Restoration in progress. $2500/obo.

Well, needless to say, that particular beast lives here now (he got his
$2500) and I am in the process of  seeking and destroying all rust or
possibility of future rusting.  I will have a report soon on the quality and
effectiveness of POR-15 from Resto-motive Labs....it's another of those
rust-killer-transmogrifier-sealer products, and I have only found one that
seems to really work ("Rust Neutralizer" from Proven Solutions does make a
great primer primer).  This leads me to the second part of the subject:
Check your vents!

I knew that I had to take the vent access plate off when I felt the way the
handle was loose and the flap seemed sticky, and I was aware that I might
find something nasty lurking up there, as that is a channel for cold, wet
air, so I was not surprised to find quite a bit of rust forming on the
underside of the roof and in the little pockets and crevices that are
created by the vent housing.  My advice to you, if you have not done so
already, is to dismantle your ventilator system and clean it out!  I'm
really glad that I caught the rust in there now, as the roof of this bus is
rust-free (at least from the outside), and it would be a shame for it to
develop holes from within!  I'll be trying the POR-15 on this and other
areas that are hard to reach and/or replace.

When the former owner and I finally got the bus to my house (we towed
it...no lights or windows), we had a great deal of fun taking it for a brief
and very windy drive...it runs/feels great!  I'll tell ya, the existence of
wind-shields is never more appreciated after a ride like that! Maybe they
should also be called "bug-shields"!
