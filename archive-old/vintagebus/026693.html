Matt,

Both you and Jim are correct.  There are two styles of this "C" channel tent
mount.  Jim is correct in that the Westfalia company did mount their tent to a
channel which was attached to the rain gutter as Jim described.  The aluminum
railing clamps to the rain gutter and is about an inch and a half tall.  This
helps offset the tent higher for more (not much more) head room.  This style
went along with the SO42/44 interiors (64-67).  After that, they dumped the
aluminum channel in favor of a rubber molding that griped the rain gutter
directly.

The tent you have does mount directly to the roof of the van.  You will have to
drill the mounting holes.  I'm not sure exactly how high the channel should be
mounted.  I think you will need to set the tent up and judge where the best
point will be.  I think you'll need more than two hands to accomplish this.

Did you look at Joe Clarks tent page.  I'm sure the sundial and Westy tents are
displayed there.  Note, there are I'm sure more than a few more iterations of
these two.

Steve
