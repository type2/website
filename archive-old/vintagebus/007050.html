Moving right along, I hit the old Weber book this evening and did some
learning on just what exactly makes these IDF's work.  It's still tough for
me but I have identified various parts of the carbs and found out that
according to the book, the jets are off their mark for a 1776 mill.  I gonna
need to order new jets (which ain't cheap I guess), carb kits, and I'd like
to check out CBP's replaceable venturi which nets about 8 to 12 more HP at
5g's - might be a nice little addition for power if I can afford it.

First of all, here is what the Weber tech book says you must run for an
engine with a 1776cc displacement and dual carb arrangement:

40IDF Jetting Guide Dual Carb

1600cc to 1835cc

Venturi = 28mm (mine is labeled 28)
Main Gas = 140 (mine says 115)
Main Air Correction = 210 (mine says 170)
Emulsion Tube = f11 (mine says f11)
Idle = 55 (mine says 50)

These carbs were rebuilt for a 1600cc engine so it does make sense that some
of the figures read low.  If I had the jet reaming tools and guages, I could
probably open them up myself but alas, I do not. :-(

Will there be any correction needed for my being 1100 feet above sea level?
