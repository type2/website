Valves do not go much out of adjustment on a normally running motor.   maybe 
have to adjust a few just a tad.   If the motor is breaking in from new, or 
the rocker arms have been off for some reason (such as retorquing the heads, 
or replacing a cracked push rod tube with a two part one which means push 
rods ust come out) the valves will go out of adjust mean as things set 
together.   If your motor got hot, they tend to get tight.

what is the knocking problems like?  If you rev the motor and let your foot 
of the gas do you hear it distinctly more like a rattlesnake about to strike, 
but not really hear it as you rev, but definately more of the decel ?  That 
signifies a rod knock, which means DO NOT DRIVE!!!    
then again, an exhaust leak can make a knocking sound.  let your bus idle, go 
around to the motor and feel around wher ethe heater boxes and muffler meet.  
air should not blow out from there.  Air will blow out from around the 
cylinder, and the under neath tin will aim the air flow to wards you when you 
are behind the motor, so ignore that and feel around the joint of the exhaust 
system.   and if you have a stock muffler, at the tailpipe etc connection.   
do you have a hole somewhere.   I know someone driving around with a hole in 
a heater box .  not a big one, but enough to make a noise.  I use that 
coppery high temp goo with the donuts.  seems to help.

you've read the idiot book procedure?   it is pretty good but me myself I had 
a real hard time visualising it - and the first time i did a top end rebuild 
I had help/ was walked through it.  any one locally who you could get to do 
that?
