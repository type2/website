And now for the best of both worlds...

First sandblast.  Then wipe a layer of the ospho onto the newly blasted
surface.  Then after a few seconds wipe it back off.  This gives the acid just
enough time to make that inert layer ospho is famous for.

If you don't use both, you're not getting it all.

By the way, for areas that recieve a lot of water such as wheel wells, check
out

http://www.zincrich_dot_com

The por15 is good, but 95%zinc galvanemetric paint is better because you are
actually setting up an electrolysis reaction.  The web site will explain
everything.  No, they don't know me, I'm just passing along info. 
Here, it would probably be better not to use the ospho so that the
electrically conductive paint will be able to electrically connect with the
metal to be protected.  Don't want an inert layer in there.  Otherwise, use
ospho.

And use por15 on everything you don't galvanize.
