>What are the cotter pins? Could someone explain it to me?=20

It's a long, thin piece of steel that is folded over into a U shape.
The legs of the U go through a hole and are bent outwards to hold
the pin in place.  A cotter pin is usually used on a shaft or axle
to keep a nut from coming lose.

http://wow.atlasta_dot_net/~mriluvu/oacdp/5054part/170.jpg has a picture
of how it is used on a Bus.  The pin is number 17.  There is a hole
drilled in the axle (number 15) for the pin to go through.  It keeps
the nut (number 16) from coming off the axle.=20

http://www.switchgearmusic_dot_com/~bluedawg/vespafaq/body/rbrake/rbrake10.jpg
has a photograph and a drawing.  In the drawing, the cotter pin is red.=20
