   In Vic Edelbrocks vintage race cars he has cool suit systems in all of
them. All it consist of is a smaill ice chest with a copper coil in them. To
that is an air pump with pumps cool air to his and his daughters  helments
and driving suits. These systems are available from most good speed shops.
You could adapt this to cool a seat by running coils of flexable tubing
throughout the seat and pumping the cooled water instead of the cooled air.
The flexable tubing ahould not be felt under the padding of the seat. Kind
of like radient cooling.

The problem with the peltiers, is that you have to directly couple a 
mechanism to move the heat.  Big hunks of metal, aluminum heat sinks,  not 
all that comfortable to sit on.  You could fabricate yourself a metal 
formed seat, mount those things all over the backside of the seat then 
connect up your heat sinks to the back of the peltiers.  Instant cool or 
hot seat, but not too comfortable.  I guess kind of like a racers seat.

If Harry's water cooler works, he could keep the water cooler on the cold 
side with a big batch of peltiers used to cool the water.
