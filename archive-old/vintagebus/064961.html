On Fri, 25 Oct 2002, ronnie chase wrote:

> If you want to be safe you should get a radial spare. radial & bias ply on
> the same axle means an accident and possibly death. Most bias ply makers
> tell you to not even mix them on the same car, but under no circumstance
> should you use them on the same axle

Well, if you're slow and careful you can run just about any combination.
However, at highway speeds mismatched tires can handle strangely, as I
found when I put passenger 185R14s on the rear and truck 195-75R14s on
the front.  Every little crosswind resulted in an entirely new diretion!

BTW, the "donut" spares on modern vehicles are bias ply!

Weirdest tire story I have is driving down the 5 freeway smelling rubber.
I came up behind a toyota with one rear tire smoking away merrily.  I
waved the driver over and we looked at the tire.  The Bridgestone tire was
missing its tread.  That's the _complete_ tread including the belt.  She
was running on the two relatively stiff sidewalls still seated on the rim!
The whole works was too hot to touch without gloves!
