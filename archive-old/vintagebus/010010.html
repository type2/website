I agree with Karl.  The vehicle _illustrated_ in the brochure is definitely
a 1970 Campmobile.  In fact, it's identical to my '70 Campmobile except for
the hubcaps.

However, the text is very clearly in reference a 1972 vehicle.

On page 2 of the brochure the text says: "And for 1972, engine rating has
been increased to...".

Page 8 gives engine specs for a 1700 engine.

Page 9 text says power front disk brakes are fitted, and also mentions steel
belted radial tires [185 SR 14ZX].  Interesting that "Reinforced" isn't
stated here.

Page 9 also lists 5 seat belts [2 front, 3 rear], yet in the photos on page
6 the kids are unbelted and no belts are in sight.  The kids are shown
lounging on the bed, etc., while the vehicle is driven in traffic [note also
the compass on the dash to the right of the man driving].

I believe a frugal sales/promotion department simply recycled a nicely
illustrated '70 brochure for the 1972 model year, and updated the text as
needed.

Case closed?
