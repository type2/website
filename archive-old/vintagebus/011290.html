Do not use sealant there!  MKG 2.0 or 14 Ft. Lbs.
 
 <<3.      The Workshop manual says to use a little "hi-melting point graphite
 grease" on the brake adjusting stars for lubrication when re-assembling
 them in their housing.  What is a modern-day brand and descriptive name for
 this grease?  If it's unusual stuff, where do you get it?>>
We have just used old used stuff from the front axle grease sessions.  
Graphite mixed would be a very good idea!  That way when the grease dies the 
graphite goes on and on since it is a dry lubricant.
 
 <<4.      Am I correct in assuming that the only parts used out of the 
generic rear axle seal kits when used on a 1964-'67 "big nut" Bus tranny are 
the two paper gaskets, the smaller "O" ring, the cotter pin and the axle

kit).  The larger "O" ring and the large flat, thin washer included in the 
kit can be saved or discarded. Does that sound correct?>>
Correct!  The washer is for pre-early 1964 and the large O-ring is for type 3.
 
<<5.       Finally, do any of you experts out there have any wonderful tips
 to share concerning these procedures (bearing seal replacement and rear
 brake replacement)?>>

pressed in it, it is a good idea to have the steel spacer/seal race seated 
inside the axle seal.
