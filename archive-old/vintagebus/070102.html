  The Brazilian latch delema never ceases to amaze me. I have had some in my
own bus for 5 years and never broke one. Have sold probably 1000 of them in
the last 5 years and have never really had a high return/broke ratio.
  Granted the Brazilian ones are cast base and the German ones forged or use
a much stronger alloy, but all you have to remember is to don't pull on the
latch hard to close. I just close the wing vent then squeeze the glass with
my hand and latch it. You MIGHT have to grind the nub down on the latch if
it is to tight.
  If you have all new seals you will more then likely break a German latch
also if you use to much force, that's why the OG ones broke in the first
place.
  I just went back to see which latches I have now and noticed that they are
a different manufacturer then I had previously. The last batch had a
guaranty 6 anos on the bags and the new ones only say guaranty but no year
anos now? hummmm
   You can also try and find a used wing vent from a bus with a good latch
and swap them out (latch that is)

ronnie
