>Fix for this:
>
>Clean both the tube and the hole with spray carb cleaner and make  sure
>that it is clean and dry.
>
>Use a flat file and roll the metal tube back and forth on a flat surface
>to put a bit of knurl on the part of the tube that goes into the hole.
>
>Mix up a bit of JB-weld according to the instructions.
>
>Smear this lightly on the knurled end of the tube.
>
>Gently tap the tube into the hole.  The JB-weld will form a bead around
>the tube where it goes into the hole.
>
>Let it cure for a couple of days.
>
>I do this if the tube can be pulled out of the hole with pliars.  Better
>safe than sorry


Follow with a small punch. Hit the aluminum of the pump where the tube goes 
in. There might even be a mark where this had been done before. The idea is 
to put a dimple that holds the tube in. Do this while the JB weld is setting 
up.
