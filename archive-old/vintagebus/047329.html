behind the passenger seat is a little 2 " x 3" tag,  write down everything 
just as it is on the tag, and go to that website to decode it (someone help 
here with where you can decode it?  vintagebus?  NEATO?)   on your VIN, the 
second number is the model number, if your VIN is 236 xxx xxx , the 3 means 
kombi, which is what westys tended to be made from.   If it is 216 xxx xxx, 
it means you bus was originallyt a panel, which most of the US camper 
companies usede to make into campers.  The non walk through panels have three 
vents up by the roof right at the back on each side.  the third digit of the 
VIN (find VIN on tag on side of air vent) is 6 for 66 model year buses.    
THe first digit is 2 for type 2s.  Hope this helps.
