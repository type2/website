After searching for such information,
I ran across Car Care Specialties
many months ago.
They finally have a good website.
http://www_dot_carcareonline_dot_com/

They don't just promote one product,
but explain the differences between
various ones, and under what conditions
one is better than another.

Overall these people do what I consider
a very good job.  I was thinking about
doing something along these lines, but
he's doing a great job.  Spend some time
checking out the recommendations. You
can get some of the products locally,
but usually at a higher price.

They cover vinyl, tires, rubber, paint,
car covers, and a whole lot more.
Why one should not use silicone is covered.

The product you were referred to is called
303, and is a decent product, but there
are better ones.  I have some and it seems
to work, so I'll use it on the S*b*r* but
not on my bus or the Q*e*t.  You have to
try several FLAPS to find this stuff or
the other recommended ones.  Different
places stock different stuff.
