I pulled the fuel tank out of the Westy a few weeks ago.  When I pulled the 
bolt out of the hose, some of the vilest goo I've ever seen came out.  But 
only a little.  It was enough to make me flee the garage in terror due to 
the smell though.  Gasoline that sits for twenty years isn't too great.

It was clogged up pretty badly.  The filter screen, which promptly
disintegrated upon removal, was a fetid brown mass...a new one is on the way.
I pressure washed the tank inside and out, and now the funk -- that is, the 
fuel flows freely.  There's some minor rust inside the tank though.  The
outside is fine, it was preserved by a thick layer of dust.

at me like I was crazy when I started talking about fuel tanks and making broad
sloshing gestures with my arms.  If I would have found something, I would have
hauled off and just done it...but it looks like I have to mail order whatever

to seal the tank, or maybe if I should even bother with it at all.
