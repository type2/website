I finally found a local outlet for the Mr. Heater Portable Buddy and checked
one out.

It's a bit larger than I expected.  I think it would fit in the aisle
between the front seats of a baywindow bus, but it wouldn't leave room for
much else.

Even on the lowest heat setting it was really blasting everything around it.
Perhaps that's on purpose, so that your "enclosed space" gets so hot you are
forced to open windows and provide the necessary ventilation for safety.

The built in safety device is a sort of pilot light.  Assuming everything
works properly, a low oxygen situation is supposed to douse the pilot light.
When that happens the unit is supposed to shut itself off automatically.
Things mechanical and electronic being what they are, I'm not certain I'd
stake my life on that.

I asked the manager of the store, a gas-heating device specialty place,
about the disparity between the manufacturer's safety claims and the trade
organization's stern warning.  He said it's just a "liability thing" to
protect against lawsuits by those misusing the heaters.  If that's the case,
I would expect the manufacturer to be the one seeking liability protection.

Your "sauna" comment is on the money.  A lot of moisture is released into
the atmosphere by these devices, and some ventilation is needed to prevent a
soggy bus interior.  Another point in favor of the externally vented
heaters.
