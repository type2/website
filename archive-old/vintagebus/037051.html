called splittys barndoors cause the cargo doors open up on hinges "like barn 
doors".  Even an older German guy who had a bus in the late fifties.   It's

about buses and agree that a "barndoor bus" is the pre March 55 kind, doesn't 
mean that everyone knows our codes.  I mean, who thought up that word anyway, 
 it really isn't so descriptive.  Splitty is descriptive, its a split wind 
sheild bus.  It makes sense.  Barn doors dont open up like a barndoor engine

newer ones that slide like sliding cargo doors.
