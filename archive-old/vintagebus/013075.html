Hey, I had the same thing done to my panel. It had tar thick enough to
cover the ribs in the floor, and 3/4" plywood screwed to it every 6" or so.
I think I made more or a mess trying to get it out than anything. the floor
was completely rust free and undented till I started prying the plywood up
with a crow bar. I did find some stuff that gets the tar out though. It was
at the local parts store, I can't rmrember the name right off, but it had
an orange scent, and came in a silver can with black lettering. It was in
the engine degreaser section. I chiseled most of the tar out, then this
stuff totally dissolevd the rest. I also found that gasoline works really
well, but it's way too dangerous to use for this. A little heat is good to
get the tar scrapable with a putty knife. I'll get the name of the stuff I
was using tomarrow if you want. Maybe this helped a little.
