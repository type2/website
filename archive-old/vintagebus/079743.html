> flywheel is still attached to the engine (boy aren't

> normal or should I go through more of a engine rebuild

5 mm worth of play? that's...like um..... bad. That could very easily
be the reason your rear seal is leaking. spacers will not take out
this endplay. if you want it fixed I feel pretty sure you will be
doing an rebuild. I suppose it's possible for the flywheel to be
actually installed incorrectly and not seated on the crank, but in
either case you will be giving that engine a good looking at.

> My other question is about the oil.  Would the oil
> sneaking by be caused by the warped flywheel or should
> I replace a seal?  Any pointers toward a good resource
> for doing that task?  I haven't looked in my Idiots
> guide or my Bently yet for this info.

it "could" cause a warped flywheel, be letting the clutch spin a lot
creating extra heat, but if this engine is as far out as it sounds
from the endplay, it might have just been warped to begin with and
never resurfaced.
