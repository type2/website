Fuel valves can be had at aircraft suppliers and RV supply shops.

You might also want to check out the "dry break" quick-disconnects that
allow you to disconnect the fuel lines.  The connectors automatically
seal themselves when you disconnect them.  They are available from
racing suppliers, AFAIK.

My problem with the disconnects, valves, etc. is that I usually replace
rubber lines while the engine is out.  No matter what kind of valves I
have in the system, I'm still going to have to open the lines to
replace them.  The only solution I can think of is the old-style
reserve valve on the tank.
