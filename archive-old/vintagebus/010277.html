On the "non-standard but functional" tent front...

There are some truly outstanding [and inexpensive] modern tents out there
that would be terrific for a camper tent.  What's lacking is a
connector/adapter to tie them into the side door opening on a Westy, etc.,
to provide weather protection and privacy as is the case with the standard
Westy tents.

I think such an adapter could be connected to the tent with the addition of
some velcro strips/fasteners and the possible addition of a zipper.
Connection to the van could be at the gutter, or to the roof with a
traditional rope hem and molding or even velcro or magnetic strips.  I think
I'd use a heavy-duty velcro strip attached to the van roof close to the base
of the pop-top.  This would provide added head room under the adapter, and
the roof attachment would be concealed and protected when the pop-top was
lowered and secured.
