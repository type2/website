NQ,

If you have the money, you might invest in a copy of 
"Desktop Dyno" a computer program that let's you adjust
engine parameters and see where the torque and hp come out.

I have used it extensively when trying to decide what to build and
found it to be a fairly good measure of what to expect.  I started
out by putting in(as closely as I could) a stock engine configuration
and then seeing if it matched some of the specs for a stock vw engine.

It matched failry well and I then went on to change the stock
configuration and see how it affected hp and torque.  

It is really meant for the big v8 guys, but I got what I considered a good
feel for what certain engine changes meant in terms of hp and torque.

The best part is being able to plug in the specs for different cams
and see where your hp and torque go....extremely interesting!

I picked an engle 100 based on how I was going to drive and the top
rpm that I wanted the engine to "see".  I am very pleased, even though
I run 1776 with engle 100 and heads almost the same as yours.

The next one I build, I am going back to 87's and an old 3/4 race 
cam that I used long ago.  Stock heads and stock stroke.  I drove
one like this many years ago and could turn in the 15's on the
drag strip and still get 33mpg with a Holley bug spray.

Read the dyno results from all the tests in the HVW and magazines,
listen to how people feel about their 1776 or 1910 or whatever and 
then make a selection.

I personally like where the torque falls from the engle 100 and 
don't expect my 1776 to ever see over 5,000 rpm.  Plenty of umph
left at 75mph! 

As the man said....there is almost no substitute for more cc's...

Your mileage may vary<grin>.

Cheers, dave
