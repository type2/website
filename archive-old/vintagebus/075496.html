Maybe there's a more specific meaning for "205" that I'm not aware of,
but every single VW part number for a distributor found on
http://www.oldvolkshome_dot_com/ovhignbo.htm is a "205".  The letters after
the 205 are important too.  There are three 205M distributors listed:

111 905 205M   '65 Beetle, '64-'65 Bus
113 905 205M   '68 Beetle MT, Bus
025 905 205M   '86-'90 Vanagon

On a '68 Bus, the 113 905 205M would have been on a 1600 sp with 30 PICT 2.
On a '64-'65 Bus, the 111 905 205M would have been on a 1500 with 28 PICT 1.
Either will probably work, but the '68 Bus application is closer to what
you have.  According to http://www.oldvolkshome_dot_com/ignition.htm , the
advance on the 113 905 205M comes in sooner:

113 905 205M  17-19deg _at_ 1.3 In. Hg, 32-35deg _at_ 3.2 In. Hg
111 905 205M   5-11deg _at_ 1.3 In. Hg, 23-28deg _at_ 3.5 In. Hg
