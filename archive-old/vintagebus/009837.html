The fuel fittings for outboard motors are the "dry break" type, and are very
inexpensive.  When the connections are locked together, the hose will fail
before the connectors will pull apart.

Different manufacturers have used different designs over the years, but
they're all very durable and you can use whatever setup you want if you're
building your own system.

The older Mercury/Mariner fittings are metal, and very robust.  Some other
systems use plastic or nylon for their fittings which would not fare as well
in a high heat environment.

A valve at the tank outlet would allow flow to be stopped while lines are
replaced.  I asked quite a while back about a fitting that would allow a
valve to be mounted directly to the tank, but never saw a response.
