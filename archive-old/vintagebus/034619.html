Well yes and no.  The overflow tubed tank uses a non-vented cap and relies on 
the tube to keep the tank from being pressurized or being vaccumed.  The bus 
tank has to use a vented cap to prevent this from stalling out the engine 
and/or damaging the tank from flexing itself to death since it's drain tube 
in the bus body does not prevent overflow or tank pressure problems.  
While the net effect is the same it is kind of like the differance between a 
tricycle or bicycle or between a lathe or milling machine.  Just try to enter 
a tricycle in a competition bicycle race or mill a crankshaft out of billet 
material.
