Radius....diameter....what's the difference? Nothing, except a factor of two. If you want a diameter (or radius) to use in your calcs, mark the spot where the tire touches the ground, like you did, load the bus up how you like (1 passenger, 2 passengers, dog, picnic baskets, etc.), and roll it until the spot touches the ground again. Measure that distance and you have your true circumference (for that load and tire pressure). Now if you need a diameter or radius value:

diameter = circumference / pi
radius = circumference / 2*pi

This is your actual rolling diameter (or radius, yeesh) of the moving vehicle.

Hope that helps :-)
