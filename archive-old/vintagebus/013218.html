This is where you lost credit on the problem.  "The wheels on the bus
go round and round" so it's the circumference that counts.  You have
to divide by the value of the diameter multiplied by pi (3.14)

This gives a value of 703RPM for the wheel.  You have to multiply this
number by the reduction box ratio, the ring-and-pinion ratio, and the
gear ratio of the gear you are in (probably 4th!) to get the engine RPM.

Another possible source of error is the difference between static
circumference and rolling circumference.  You measure rolling
circumference by marking the sidewall of the tire and the ground, then
rolling the bus forward for one or more revolutions and measuring the
linear distance on the ground.
