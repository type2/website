I also like to keep up with traffic.

The 912 E engine is the same as the late 914 2.0L except for a different
fuel injection.  The FI on the 912 E is better in my opinion than the 914
but neither would be easy to fit in a bus.  Otherwise the engines are
identical.  The only difference between the 914 engine and the bus engine is
the dipstick location and slightly different heads.  The late bus engine
also has hydraulic lifters.

I have a 67 westy that I put an upright converted 912 2.0L engine in.  It is
carbureted as making the stock FI fit with an upright shroud would require a
ton of fabrication.  I was able to do this without any modification to the
engine compartment of the bus.  I am happy with the conversion but it is
probably the last I will do.  It was a lot of work for a little more power
and still having webers to deal with.  If someone comes out with a kit to
fit the stock bus FI on an upright conversion I think it would be worth it.

In all of the different engine setups I have put into buses my favorite is
still a single port 1600 with kadrons.  Great power up to 70 and cheap!
