HERE'S THE DEAL, IF YOU DON'T READ ANYTHING ELSE
		READ THIS!!!

A database system was placed in service about 3 years ago. It's
called the National Driver's Registry database.  Most, if not all 
states in the country now have loaded record data WITHOUT
verifying data validity or integrity.  If you got a ticket in the 
past, you NEED to check the database.  Most of the problems
I have found concern tickets more than 10 years old.  

I found the same thing with a ticket I got in '86 (I was on the 
road about 100k a year back then) in Louisiana.  In the process of 
clearing this off my record, the manager who was finally able to 
help me told me that they had about 95% of the entries were
wrong.  In my case, the ticket I got was in '86, I took care of it
promptly. I moved to NY, and then back to Austin, TX, in '89.
Texas told me I had a ticket that needed to be cleared up before they
would issue a license.  At that time, I still had the receipt where I
had paid it, but had to take it back to Louisiana to clear it up.  That
was in '89, it had been cleared the second time.

As I was researching this Alabama problem, I found a Louisiana entry
as well.  It was the same ticket.  It took going all the way up to the
governor's office to get someone to help me.  Every single person in
the DMV said I had to PAY $100 to get this off, even though I had
taken care of it over 13 years ago.

It's a scam for the states to collect money.  The logic behind this is
you took care of it years ago, you surely didn't keep the records for 
10 years, but the state kept the charge.  And you got to pay to get it
off.  So the state collects twice for one charge.

By the way, My wife has had 1 ticket in her life, in Lago Vista, Tx.
That was in '91.  It was also in the system, even though she had paid
the ticket 7 years ago.

Moral:  Keep the thumb on the authority, or the authority may put
the thumb on you!
