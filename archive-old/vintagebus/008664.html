I have a 1641 in my '67 Deluxe.  I could use a little more oomph getting up
the CO mountains, so I've done some investigation into the possibilities.
(There's nothing like tooling along in 2nd gear on I-70 while the Lexii
blast by at 75+ MPH!  Fortunately, the semis aren't going any faster, so
I've got some big company in the climbing lane.)

The advice I've gotten, from two experienced (pro) builders, is that if
you're going bigger, go all the way to 1914 (or is it 1915?).  (And go for a
good CW crank & good balancing, while you're at it.) Apparently the 1914
P&Cs are more robust than those for 1776.  However, both builders
recommended staying with the 34 PICT on DP that I have now.  (BTW, neither
had anything but contempt for the 1776, which was my first thought, too.)

What you really want is more torque (esp. low-end).  According to these
guys, most people who think they want more horsepower really want more
torque.  The 1914 with stock DP components is supposed to be a cheap (w/good
gas mileage), powerful, reliable combination for the bus.

I've seen opinions online saying that the 34 PICT will run out of gas at
high revs with such a big displacement, resulting in potentially bad
leaning-out.  But these guys dismissed that possibility as very unlikely in
sane bus use.  The overall combination would probably be more driveable than
my current 1641 with the "mild" (110 clone) cam, 34PICT, DP, 009 and
extactor:  currently there just isn't enough flow to make optimal use of the
more open intake and exhaust.  Especially in the thin Colorado air I live
in.
