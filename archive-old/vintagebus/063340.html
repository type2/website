Keep in mind that your VOM accuracy affects this too.  Rat Shock's
$25 DMM has a DC accuracy of +/- 2.0% of reading, +/- 0.2% of full
scale, +/- 1 in last digit.  Their $90 DMM has a DC accuracy of
+/- 1%, +/- 2 digits on the 40 V scale.  The two VOMs they sell
have a DC accuracy of +/- 3% of full scale.

If your tools say things like "Snap-on" on them, go get a Fluke
digital multimeter.  Their model 77 has an 0.3% DC accuracy.  It
costs $200 but you will have it forever.  If you prefer analog
meters, get a Simpson 260 for about the same money, with a 2% of
full scale DC accuracy.
