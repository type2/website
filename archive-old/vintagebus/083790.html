On Wed, 7 Sep 2005, Matt Roberds wrote:

> The "usual suspects" for goop include 3M, Permatex, and Loctite.
> Digging around, it looks like "3M Scotch-Grip Rubber & Gasket Adhesive

The problem here is that most of the goops are adhesives.  This
translates to "a bear to remove and clean the next time" which leads to
more leaks.  Better to use a non-adhesive goop when the goal is simply to
help a gasket seal.
