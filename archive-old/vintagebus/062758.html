Gene;
Been there done that last winter (right around Christmas).  I used JBWeld (a
two-part epoxy-type substance you can pick up at  any FLAPS) to plug similar
size holes; it actually took a couple of coats to build up enough matter to
fill the "larger" ones.  The important thing was to get all the rust out (I
cleaned it out and hit it with Naval Jelly to make sure everything was
nuetralized), then sand/prime/paint.  After going thru a rainy summer in
Florida all 7 windows I did are dry as a bone.

Another thing to consider as you put the rubber back on is to use roof tar.
I used it to coat the lip where the rubber and the bus come together as well
as in the channel where the glass goes into the seal.  This helps with
making sure you get a good seal and the roofing tar on the metal lip will
also keep rust from forming in the future.

I did both windshields (removal, fix and replace) over the course of a
weekend; it would have been quicker but it takes time for the JBWeld to set
up.

Check the archives from Mid December thru early January for more tips as
there was quite a discussion about this and using roofing tar as a sealant.
Good luck!!

--Rich
