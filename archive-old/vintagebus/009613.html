I've got a starter problem I just haven't been able to figure out.  When I
first bought my bus, a 67 Westy, I soon encountered the failed starter.  In
my inexperience, I just went out and bought a rebuilt starter.  After about
3 or 4 months this starter developed symptoms similar to the old one by just
clicking and not turning over the engine.  I wiggled the solenoid wire and
she took off so I thought I had the problem licked.  Soon this didn't work
anymore.  I took off all the leads and cleaned everything.  It worked for
awhile,  but soon this too failed.  I pulled the starter and tested the
solenoid on the bench and it worked fine.  Eventually I came to the solution
of crawling under the bus and banging on the starter and/or solenoid and it
started.  Well, it's been about 2 months since I've had to bang on it cause
it's been starting fine.  But, today it failed.  I banged and nothing.  I
wiggled and nothing.  I had to get a push to start it.

My question is what is going on in the starter when it responds to banging
on it to get it to turn over the engine.  I wiggled the starter shaft in the
bushing when I had the engine out and it wasn't that bad.   Is something
stuck and gets freed by the banging?  I could unsolder the leads to the
solenoid and look in there,  but I've never been in there and I don't know
if I could fix anything unless it was just dirty or something making it
stick.  Thanx for any suggestions.  This thing has been buggin me since the
first of the year.
