Lot of talk about transmissions over the last year so I though I would throw 
my 3 cents in (inflation) 

For a trans I run a 3.44 with big nut reduction boxes.  For an engine I have 
a 1776, 110, dual 34 webers and a Thing fanshroud (no heater snouts, better 
cooling)   

I really like this setup a lot.  I generally drive around 65mph and don't 
slow down much on small hills unless pulling a trailer.  Can go faster if I 
wanted but what is the point.  You want to go fast get a motorcycle, you want 
style get a Bus.

Anyway, there seem to be a lot of OPINIONS out there as to what trans setup 
is "The Setup".  All depends on how and where you drive, what you haul, and 
how fast you want to get where you are going.  The setup I run IS NOT the 
setup you want if you live in a hilly area or have an engine smaller than a 
1776 or you haul heavy loads or live in Death Valley (or other hot area)  

If you live in a fairly flat area, haul light to medium sized loads, want to 
drive 65-70 in a stock height bus without spinning it so fast she will 
explode, a 3.44/big nut is something to consider.  Since I take a trailer to 
just about every event I am contemplating a bigger engine though I think I 
would be fine if I didn't pull a trailer.
