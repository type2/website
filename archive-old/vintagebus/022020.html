Again it depends on what you are going for in a engine.  You could even do
both and have the H case for a back up engine and/or show engine.
<< I know that the later case has the dual oil relief valves.  Is it that much
better than the 1500 H?>> 
The AE case should have dual relief valves and we have seen that this type of
case gets about twice the oil pressure as a single relief valve case will at
higher operating oil temps.  You could in theory could bore the main oil H
case passages as large as the dual relief case, counterbore larger cooler
holes in the case to bolt a doghouse cooler there, and install the later
larger dia. oil sump pickup tube.  We are doing this to a 40HP engine to see
if these changes will work.  The AE case has a alloy that reputedly is harder
and thusly more durable than the H case especially for racing purposes.     
<< BTW I plan on making  the motor a 1641 and I still want to use the stock
carb and vac dizzy. >>
We are using a 30PICT-1 carb on a single port 1641cc and a 010 distributor.
Getting 24 MPG average doing 64MPH at 3,000RPM with 1964-67 reduction gear
boxes on our 1960 panel with full roof racks and camping interior and gear.
