Hi Kevin,
Then it sounds like you only have the engine case as a good rebuild part of 
all of the above.  Engine cases can be bored in the main bearing bore four 
times .020" (.5mm) for a total of .080" (2mm).  So unless your engine case 
was really badly run to the point is was beat to death and/or badly machined, 
it should be still good.  Since new engine cases are over $300.00 and there 
is a good possiblity that VW is soon going to shut down ACVW production.....

You mentioned that your rebuilder was just regrinding the used valves.  
Unless you know there is less 40,000 stress free miles on them it is not a 
good idea to reuse valves especially exhaust ones.  Valves are exposed to 
high tempertures (Again especially the exhaust ones!) and lots of 
tension/hammering.  This work hardens the valves and eventually the head 
snaps (Like a coat hanger sudden failure.) off the stem and the piston pounds 
it into itself and the head.  Often putting a hole in the piston and 
releasing bunches of shrapnel into the engine case and sucked into the 
bearings in a moment!
You can take a normal steel valve after 100,000 miles.  Put it in a vise and 
try hammering it into a L-shape.  It will snap off before bending much.  
Stainless steel valves in the same condition/mileage will bend bend nicely 
all the way into the L-shape.
  Your cam can possibly be regound and rehardened. Best to have the cooler 
cleaned.  Eric&Barb
