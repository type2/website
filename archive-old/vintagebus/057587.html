On Sat, 25 May 2002, ncbus wrote:

> I have ordered and an oil temp and oil pressure sender with the
> accompanying gauges. When I ordered them, the Counter Guy at the
> FLAPS recommended the following set up: adaptor with T-fitting
> replaces stock oil pressure switch, new pressure sender goes on one
> side to stock oil light and VDO pressure gauge  and temp sender on
> the other side of the T-fiting.  Is this a good place to get an oil
> temp reading? I will also be running a Gene Berg dipstick sender just
> to make things complicated :)

Chris,

IMHO, this is a *terrible* place to get oil temperature!  You want a place
where the oil is moving past the sender, and at the top of a tube, off to
one side of a "T" fitting is not it.  The positively idea place would be
in the stream entering or exiting the oil pump or cooler, but it is damned
difficult to plumb it in there.  Most people go for temp in the sump, as
Berg's dipstick sender does and the drain plate sender does.  Both of
these have the advantage that they read the temperature of the oil right
after it has come off the bearings with a slight disadvantage that the
temperature is affected (slightly, IMHO) by the temperature of the case.
