From your description I would say you have several things wrong with
your steering system and possibly something wrong with the king pins
and link pins.

1. Check out your steering gear box. It may be set too tight. This
could be an indication that it is worn out and the PO set it tight to
try and compensate.

2. Check the swing arm bushing. It may be worn out causing slop.

3. Check the shims on the link pins. Wrong setting will cause squirley
steering

4. Maybe your king pin bushings are worn out also.

Whooeee. This sound worse than it may be. Good luck.
Others may be able to tell you more.
