That is a good price for the westy, it would be well worth the money, even
if it needed work.

I have a number of split and bay busses (about 35).  I love to drive the
splitties for their coolness (is that a word?)  The splits have an "air"
about them that nothing else can come close to matching.  They look great,
are easy to work on, parts are fairly inexpensive and easy to get.  To put
it in a nutshell, driving a splity will just make you smile from ear to ear.

But the "big top" bay window westy is wonderful for camping and long trips.
With disk brakes, a larger and longer life engine, ball joint front
supension and IRS rear, the bays will travel so much easier and faster.
You can't beat that huge bed on top!!  The engine are more expensive to
work on, but will out last an "upright" type 1 engine twice as long.  I
think the bay window busses are classics and I do like the looks.  You will
get the ear to ear grin driving a bay too.

I do have a '64 deluxe that has all the paint and body work finished, I
need to do the interior, that I will use to run around town, go to club
meetings and close to home shows.  But the '76 westy will be for camping
with my daughter and long distance shows.

Both the bays and splitties have their good and bad and both have fairly
low insurance rates and property taxes, so why not have 1 of each?  They
sure look good under the carport!!
