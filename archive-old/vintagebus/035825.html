I painted my 65 westy a year ago, with much help from a couple friends.   The
paint has some rocks chips now, some rust coming through in a couple places,
and one or two bubbles.     No show car, but it does look 5000% better than it
did before and I am sure will rust less for the protection.    It's a driver.
The key to a good paint job is prep.   In this case it meant da sanding and
bangin dents out and a cool hydraulic thingy to push out the side where the
bus had laid over and mushed in just a tad at the drivers pillar by the
bulkhead some rust repair and using bondo and spot putty and time and energy.
I spent about $500 in supplies- paint and various chemicals and filters for
better breathing and sanding supplies and rust repair pieces and new window
seals, used ppg paint- they were very nice about mixing stock colours to code
for me, and remixing the bluewhite which was off (I took it back after
spraying the bus and having a fit that it came out too green).   I had never
done this before and it was very nice that someone could show me how it works.
Not that they were experts.  It did make a big difference temperature and
humidity when painting- it was raining outside when we did the green and it
came out better than the white which was done when it was dry outside.
