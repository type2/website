Subject: re: [VB] condensation?

Whenever you decompress a gas (i.e. create a vacuum), you get a cooling
effect. This is precisely how air conditioning works. This is due to the
fact that the energy of the molecules is spread more thinly, so the
relative heat is lower per given volume. The opposite is happening in
the cylinders, where the gas (and the energy of the molecules) is being
compressed into a smaller volume...

Enough physics lessons...

The heat riser tubes coming from the exhaust are there to counteract
this. If you notice icing on your intake, then maybe you should make
sure the heat riser tubes aren't clogged...
