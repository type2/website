sounds like you have a doghouse as you mention 4 seals.  usually its the
upright kind that leak, though it's not impossible that yours does.
were the
old seals crunchy?  they should be supple.   in the middle where the oil
flows through is where they crunch first.  once I found one bent into
the
middle so it would block the oil flow!!!!!!!!!!!

puzzling.   pressure check wouldn't be bad idea.  hm which seals did you
use?
 the ones that are higher in the middle than the edges?  or the tubes?
how
high?  what number case do you have?   if you have a small oil passage
case
and use a dighouse (larger passages) you need the seals to fit that are
bigger one side than the other.

you said it looked clean around the main seal- does that mean you pulled
the
FW?  If you pull themotor again do yourself a favor and swap that main
seal
and o ring just cause you don't know how old they are and the parts are
cheap.

when you look at the engine tin to the left of the dist near where it
goes to
the fan housing, does oil drip there?  usually it is oily there with an
oil
cooler leak.   And all over the left side cylinder (look from under
neath at
those)

is it leaking from a push rod tube?  does it drip at idle?   watch with
a
flashlight and try to narrow down the source?
