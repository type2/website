 thread, I do not claim to be the lowering guru (please no!) but I have seen
how some were lowered,  and some impression of what does and does not work.
Willing to learn more.   I know what works for me is stock height.    I have
also replaced the torsion leaf springs on a bus where they broke, making the
wheel fall off (luckily, no one was hurt, which is pretty lucky, to be
repetitive and redundant), and so am really wary of people taking them out.
As well missing some leaf springs makes it ride really jouncy, and have
replaced leaf springs on two buses. ( I have some extra if anyone else needs a
set).    I would not lower a bus.     I dislike what people do to them a lot
of times, especially when they say, oh it's a VW lets modify it.  For example,
the radio area in my 59 was cut out a couple years ago to put a radio in it.
fairly recently.   Some jerk put a later style shift coupler, which hid
reverse.  (it was there, but unreachable)   Why not just get the correct part?
There was a reciept for $151.xx for the shift crap done to it that I had to
undo in order to back up.   Such a PITA.  Also drilled several dash holes.
And welded one up very badly so it warped.  I guess I'll cut it back out-
it'll look better  with a guage in it than that warped pile of rusty slag.
It wasn't through not wanting to spend money.  There were a lot of receipts,
but I don't think the knowledge was there.    I don't think the guy(s?) who
cut the dash,  or had the coupler done,  or did the weird wiring that I was
reorganising getting closer to the correct colour coding today,  or removed
the front turn signals,  or spliced the brake cables togther with nuts and
bolts but they are fraying,  thought they were doing anything but something
functional and cool.    Most of it I have to fix as I want it to be a safe,
well running bus, but some of it I want to fix to put things back to stock.
I won't keep Mr. Truck forever.    But he will be a better truck then than
when I got him, barring misfortune.
