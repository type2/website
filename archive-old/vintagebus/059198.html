Do these go in place of the headlights or do they have their own
enclosure for mounting on the bumper?  If they mount on the bumper,
they only have one beam.  One prong is for the supply voltage and
the other is for ground.

In the Bentley manual, VW recommended that these lights be wired to
take the place of the high beam filament in the regular lights.
Unplug the high beam wires at the fuse box (white wire and white/black
stripe wire) and use the two terminals you just freed up to wire the 
new lamps. 

If these bulbs go in place of the headlights: if the bulb has a metal 
plate on the base, that might be the ground.  Most all headlight bulbs
I've ever seen -- sealed-beams, old Euro halogens, new American/Japanese
halogens -- have a terminal for ground, which means two-terminal bulbs
have just one beam and three-terminal bulbs have two beams.

If you can get the bulb out of the reflector, you can tell for sure by
looking closely at the filament(s).  If it has both high and low beam,
it will have two filaments.  If it's only got one filament then it's
only low beam.

Looking in a Wagner lamp catalog I have, the only two-prong halogen 
bulbs (for separate reflectors, not sealed-beams) they list are:

H3, 12V, 55W, 2.5" overall length, 0.45" diameter bulb
H7, 12V, 55W, 2.4" overall length, 0.59" diameter bulb

If you have the H3 bulb then there's not much you can do.  If you 
have the H7 bulb, you can _probably_ replace it with a bulb with
two filaments and the same mounting.
