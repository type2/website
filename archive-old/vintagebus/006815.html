If you are useing Nason get a couple gallons extra, you'll need it in a year
when the paint chips, peels and fades out.  On my 67 Sudial it took a quart
to paint below the beltline (this was before reducing) and a gallon to do
the roof, down to beltline and interior, I used PPG's low end OMNI, the
sundial has been gone for a couple of years now, but I still have my DD
which I used the same paint, great paint for a resale vehical, but I will
never use it again on a bus I want to keep, it holds up OK but I am not
really happy with the strength of the paint. When I paint the 59 single I am
going to use PPG DELSTAR 3 times the money, but will pay off in the long
run, this information was told to me by my PPG supplier, OMNI is a great
selling product but it is low end and will show in the long run. If useing
Nason, step up and have your paint supplier move you up to a better Quality
Sherwin Williams paint (Nason is their budget line)
