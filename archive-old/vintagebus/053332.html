I recommend using the original 'pod' sound dampers and the flexible metal 
tube with the fiberglass insulation round it.   At one point I had one of 
those bay window plastic tubes on my 61, and a road trip to the SF Area in 
December I broke a fan belt and thrashed the right side paper fresh air tube. 
 Replaced fan belt, bunched up fresh air tube so it blocked outlet as I 
hadn't another as a spare.  Ten minutes or less south on I-5 there was a 
horrible stink- the plastic tube between heater box and body was 
melting!!!!!!!!!!!!   So I pull over, crawl under and rip the POS off.  Drove 
to the next town- Grants Pass and got a replacement fresh air tube.   Now I 
use those not as pretty but sturdier plastic fresh air tubes from fan shroud 
(at least on right side) and only the stock heater box to body tubes.
