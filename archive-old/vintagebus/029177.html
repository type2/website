The classic sign of a front oil seal that's gone bad. When the engine
stops running, the oil can drain out past the seal easier, so you get a
little rush of oil until the seal is drained.

You need to pull the engine, take off the flywheel, and replace the
seal. Replace the o-ring in the flywheel while you're in there, if you
have one. Of couse, this is the time to do all that "engine out" stuff.
Check your clutch disk (it's probably soaked in oil), engine comp.
seals, axle boots. I'm sure you'll get lots more suggestions. 8^)
