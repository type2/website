yes- and I'd like to also say that the bus in question has a VIN only 36 
numbers away from the recorded change VIN.    So if the bus has a vin close 
to the change,  E&B have it on the nose, the recorded change may by slightly 
off, especially if you have a model that is not exactly standard, which could 
be pulled off the line for something, such as 23 windows or double doors or 
extra M codes, then put back on in a different place.  But seeing a 62 bus 
with a pointy speedo pod dash, I would be hard pressed to believe it was 
original, and would look for evidence of a nose job and/ or dash replacement.

re: whether the parts will fit pretty much you do need to pay attention.   
Stuff like doors and aprons and shift linkage stuff will not fit if it is the 
wrong version of the part, unless hokey alterations are done.  I found a late 
box type shift coupler on the 59 truck I had.   Some one had drilled the rear 
shift rod and used the later version.   However, it did not go into reverse.  
I installed the correct shift coupler and voila, I got reverse.  The 
modification for the wrong part did not work properly.  I have seen a pull 
handle door on a 67 bus, but it had been put on by a DPO with hokey mods for 
where the mechanism latches.    Of course some stuff will, you can change 
transaxles, put a 67 version in a 61 bus and vice versa. I have later front 
suspension and transaxle in my 61 hardtop deluxe, no hokey mods needed, but 
If I didn't know that and went to get brake stuff and ordered by VIN I would 
get the part that didn't fit, as it has the later brakes now.  But if you put 
an early suspension in a 64-67 bus, where do you attach the steering damper? 
- the early ones have one end attached to the body of the bus.   And some 
stuff is cosmetic, do you want green or beige or hardboard sunvisors?  They 
will all fit the same w/o mods.
