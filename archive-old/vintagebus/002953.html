TAKE HEED of Ronnie's advice. FIRST, remove the cancel plate from the
bottom of the wheel.  Get yourself a good gear puller with long teeth
and remove.  Hitting the top of the steering shaft can bung up the
thrust bearing in your precious steering box. If you have a 5/16-18
tap and drill set, you can put a couple of threaded holes into the
casting, put two screws through a plate that has something like a
1/2-20 threaded hole in the center, put your big bolt in and  use that
to pull up the wheel. Sort of like a gear puller except instead of
arms reaching around to the bottom of the wheel, you are grabbing the
hub from the top.
