Sure sounds like a Beetle switch, and maybe even a Beetle wiper motor.
A '65 Bus should have had a single-speed wiper motor stock, with one
wire from the switch to the motor.  A '66 would have had a two-speed
motor, with four wires from the switch to the motor.  Most of them
would have had 53 numbers instead of 54 numbers.

Looking at the '65-'66 Bug wiring diagram, its wiper motor and switch
matches what you have more closely.  There are three wires going to
the motor and four connections at the switch: 54d black/purple to
motor, 54 hot (two black wires - one from fuse box, one to motor),
31b brown to motor, and a ground that was probably made by the nut
or screw that holds the switch to the dash, hence no tab for 31.

>My replacement harness says  53blk,  53a redblk, 31b  brown.

53 and 31b make sense but I don't know where they got 53a (red/black)
from...purple/black and yellow/black are the more common striped colors
for earlier wiper motors.

I don't know if this answers the question but I hope it sheds a little
more light.
