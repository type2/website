The problem is at what engine RPM at 75-80 MPH in fourth.  3,000 RPM seems to 
be the bus best RPM for best engine longevity and MPG (LPH).  Which both of 
these factors will save you alot of $$$ if you can make the engine last long 
and not suck fuel like crazy especially since you are in Europe.  Sounds like 
using a 1960-67 bus tranny with RGBs, 3.44 R&P, .82 fourth, freeway flier 
tranny and either a 1776 type1/2 engine or a 1800 4 engine.  Even with this 
you are most likely going to be more likely a less than 70MPH bus.  While 
trailering we would never push it over 60 MPH.  Anyhow going much faster than 
70 MPH will result in dangerous handling due to areodynamics, most likely 
resulting in a big accident.      Eric&Barb
