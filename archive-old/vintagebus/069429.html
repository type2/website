> A 50% "derating" seems like a lot.  This formula would say to use a
> 35 A fuse for 2 100 W lights.  35 A in a 10 gauge (4 mm^2) wire is
> pushing the limits for that size of wire, at least according to

30 is pretty close to 35!

The NEC is designed for wires being loaded continuously to their maximum
current by being hooked up to several different devices.

The headlight circuit is a dedicated wire, so the fuse is really there
only to guard against a short circuit.  A short will put a very large
current through the wire so you can use a relatively large fuse.
