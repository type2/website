On Sun, 18 Jun 2000, Thom Fitzpatrick wrote:
> A reserve price isn't necessarily secret.  Some sellers want it secret,
> some don't.  There are reasons that a seller would prefer it one way or the
> other.
 
> In my case, I'd like to use the ebay community to establish a valuation.
> If I were to put the reserve price up there, it would "taint" the results.
> Some sellers reveal the reserve to separate the men from the boys up front.

Often, if the buyers knew the reserve price they'd say "that's crazy" and
not put in a bid at all.  The reserve allows the auction seller to try and
get a bidding frenzy going without actually having to risk selling an item
at a low price.  

In the old days of face-to-face auctions, sellers would
employ "shills" to perform the same function as the reserve. They'd bid
the item up a bit at a time until the real buyers got sucked in.  If there
was only one "real" bidder, the shill would bid the price up until it was
high enough to satisfy the seller.  If the "real" bidder followed the
shill up, they would end up paying a lot more.  If they didn't follow the
shill, the seller wouldn't have to give the item away cheaply.

For some reason, people are more likely to raise a low
bid to a higher amount than they are to simply bid the higher amount to
begin with.  Ain't human nature wonderful! :)
