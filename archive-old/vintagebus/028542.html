Well, you broke one trying to tighten it to 14 ft. lbs, so that should
tell you something. 8^) Just remember that the size of the fastener
tells you a lot about how much torque it's meant to take. Small
fasteners like M6 are only meant to hold small loads. It has to do with
the cross sectional area of the fastener. Higher torque = higher load. 6
ft. lbs is plenty for an M6 fstener. Avoid using a 3/8" ratchet on the
M6 nuts and bolts. A 1/4" ratchet makes it a lot easier to feel how
tight they are without breaking them.
