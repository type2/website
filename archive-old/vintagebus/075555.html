A 40 W pencil will probably take forever on the fuse box clips.  I'd
use at least a 100 W iron for that job.  When I soldered the big brass
terminals on a headlight switch, I had a 140 W iron and that wasn't
really enough.  Higher-powered soldering irons often let you do _less_
damage to the materials that surround the metal you're soldering,
because you can get "in and out" quicker.

>Then there is the area of contact where the two metal pieces "squeeze"
>into the black fuse box thingy...should I solder here as well after
>reconnecting everything...

Any place that you have two pieces of metal that conduct electricity
touching one another, solder them together.
