There used to be governor eliminator pipes sold.  I saw one on Ebay a
couple of months back.  It is necessary because the type 2 intake manifold
is shorter than the type 1 manifold, to give space for the governor.  If
you try to bolt the carb directly to the type 2 manifold, the throttle
cable will not line up properly, and you might also have interference
between the carb and the generator, particularly if your carb has an
electric idle jet solenoid.

If you don't like the governor, you can strip the guts out and fill up the
holes with JB Weld or bondo, or you can try to find an eliminator pipe, or
you can find a '67-'70 type 1 intake manifold (best).  In your case, you
should be able to trace the strange shape of the governor onto a piece of
galvanized sheet metal, cut it out with a tin snips, punch or drill bolt
hioles, and then use some silicone and screws to seal your new cover in
place.

I missed getting a NOS governor on Ebay a while back, but I did get a used
one.  Unfortunately, the safty wire is gone and I think this thing has been
monkeyed with.  I'm running a '74 type 1 engine at the moment, but I plan
to build a stock 1500 (well, maybe 1600 or 1641, or would be really nice to
build a 1500S) H block to make evcerything original again.
