> Hey Conrad,
>
> for the stick shift:
> 211 711 121 gear shift lever up to ch 501 706
> 211 711 121 A  gear shift lever from ch 501 707 up to ch 216 028 111
> 211 711 121 B  gear shift lever from ch  216 028 112
>
> the first version is straight, and the ball has no pin for orientation
> the second and 3rd versions have a bend near the botton. They also  
> have a pin that goes into a slot in the socket, to keep the  
> orientation of the stick right.
> the distance from the ball to the cup that holds the spring is  
> different on versions A compared to B
>
> much better to cut the front shift coupler than either of the shift  
> rods.  I have had good luck with heat and then quenching with a  
> squirt bottle, and only once had to cut the coupler... a lengthwise  
> slice or two allows a fat sccrewdriver to pry it apart ... then I  
> was able to seaparet teh front from rear rods.  But hindsight is 20  
> 20, oh well.
>
> For the forward shift rod:
> 211 711 155 up to ch # 501 706
> 214 711 155 from ch # 501 706 up to 835 087
> 211 711 155 B from ch #  835 087 up to 216 028 111
> 211 711 155 C from ch # 216 028 111
>
> looks like WW made a repro shift rod to use in place of either the B  
> or C originals.
>
>
> When installing the shift stick, I do it as a two person job, with  
> one person holding the horizontal rod up from underneath, and the  
> other putting the ball in the socket and putting the bolts in.
> Without a person (or block of wood) holding the shift rod up, it  
> will just sink down out of the way.
>
> use grease for the ball and socket joint.   grease is good.  ;)
