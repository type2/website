Not as bad as it sounds.  Someone had cut a VDO sized hole in the dash of my
67 bus long ago and didn't too bad of a job of it.  Since I can't run a Berg
temp dipstick with my T3 conversion (it runs WAAAAAAY too close to the belt
and pulley), I'm going to run a VDO sump plate guage.  2 years ago I
cabbaged onto a nice little set of various VDO guages in an older T4 wagon
and have been sitting on them for awhile.  I plucked out the VDO Oil temp
guage and hooked it to a new sensor using a battery I keep charged on the
bench.  I then took a lighter and held it on the sensor and sure enough, the
guage started moving.  As for accuracy, I have no idea but if anyone had any
good ideas for how to run the Berg dipstick sender on a T3 to T1 conversion,
let me know.  The dipstick convertor thing comes out too close to the crank
pulley and even though the tempstick guage goes in without hitting anything,
you can bet with a little vibration and air whipping around that it will rub
or get caught up in the belt.

I installed the guage, hooked up the wires, and the backlight seems to work
well with the rest of the dash lights.  I just have to run a wire back to
the engine bay and I'll be in business.
