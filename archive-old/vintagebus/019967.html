On Fri, Apr 27, 2001 at 07:19:37PM -0700, Thomas Fritz wrote:
> So my question is... what is the real scoop on the subject?

I figure the engineers know better than the "experts" that weld them open, 
remove them, etc.  If you press 'em beyond "they're not needed", they say that
your engine will burn up when the 'stat fails.  Bull, they fail open.  Punch
one with a nail and see if you want.  Flaps stuck closed?  Don't leave your
engine out in the rain, and lubricate the rotation points with lithium grease
every time you pull the engine from the vehicle.  Rattles?  You're probably
missing the spacers.

As for the "not needed" part: sure, it'll run fine without them.  But your
cooling will be erratic and will cause the engine to take longer to warm up,
never warm up all the way, cool the wrong parts, etc.  At the least you
probably aren't going to see the same durability that you see in an engine
with a properly functioning thermostat assembly.

Best place to get the parts, cheap, is from a mechanic who advises against

009 was favored for so long.  The local VW joints around here have seemed to
notice the demand now, though, so the swaps are cheapest for 'em again.
