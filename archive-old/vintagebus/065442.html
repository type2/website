well, you won't get electricity out of the coil if you aren't getting it into 
the coil.  The black wire on terminal 15 of the coil brings the electricity 
from the second to right most fuse on the fuse box in front of the drivers 
knees, which gets volts from the black wire from the ignition switch when you 
turn the key on. (black means hot with ignition on.  red means hot all the 
time)  So if you turn the key on and that fuse doesn't get electricity, it 
won't get to the coil (you can check the black wire there too if you like, it 
only gets volts with ingnition on) and the motor won't spark.   But since the 
starter was wimpy, sounds like you have problems getting electricity to 
anything.

One neat thing Muir shows how to do is take the wire off the center of the 
dist cap, turn key on, out of gear, turn engine round with wrench while 
holding the wire a hair away from metal (I use the edge of the carb the thing 
the spring goes to) and as the engine just about reaches TDC a spark should 
jump the gap and you hear a click.  A blue spark is great, and orange spark 
is wimpy.  I hold it with a clean dry rag so I don't get sparked.

Did you clean up the battery connections?  That's the first place to start 
when there's electricity issues.
