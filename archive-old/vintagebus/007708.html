There are just as many kinds of white powder (?!) as
Eskimos have words for snow. A color match request
can be accomplished by asking for a test panel and
comparing against the original.
This gets confusing as to whether you want the original
or are trying to match to the antique it has become.
Often a color shade can be influenced by the amount of
gloss, and powder is universally rated on a scale of 0 to 100.
A color match may be photo-analyzed for a perfect match,
same as paint suppliers offer, and an available product can be
adjusted for gloss with the appropriate clear-coat, or a new
batch mixed, which usually gets more expensive because the
chemist has a minimum order for this custom service.
The best inexpensive solution is to work with paper samples,
then request a test panel of the actual product for direct comparison.
