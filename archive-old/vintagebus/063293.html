I don't know of one that's out there already, but here's a shot at
one.

First, a word about bulb numbering.  The VW literature lists bulbs
by their "Normteile" (standard part) numbers and also by their
designation under DIN 72601.  For example, the license plate light
bulb is N 17 718 1 and G 6 V 10 W.  Here in the US, we usually buy
bulbs by their ANSI numbers, which are simply assigned to each new
bulb design as it comes along.  For example, 1157 is a common
stop/tail bulb for older American cars.

Different bulb vendors have approached the problem of integrating
European and US bulb designations in different ways.  Wagner seems
to assign five-digit numbers to European bulb types, while GE has
an "international series" of bulbs that are given designations
similar to the DIN 72601 numbers, like P21W or W5W.

There are a few bulbs on a VW that I haven't been able to find at
the FLAPS.  These include the non-sealed-beam headlight for non-US-spec
Buses, the instrument panel warning lights, and the dome lights.
You'll probably have better luck finding these bulbs at your FLVWPS
or from one of the mail-order vendors.

If you're looking to upgrade, halogen headlamps work well.  Blue
and "superwhite" headlamps are illegal and don't work anyway.  You
can sometimes find a brighter bulb that will fit a certain application,
but be careful - brighter means more heat.  For something that's not
on all the time (brake lights) this can work well; for something that
runs all the time (license light) it can melt lenses.
