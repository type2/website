Man, I really don't want this to sound insulting... as I'm sure you must
have thought of it, but it needs to be said :)

Why not just get a 1971 bus?  The ball joint front ends is a lot nicer ride,
poptops are a lot nicer, you can get poptop canvas that is 2x as big for 1/2
the price, etc, brakes are awesome, etc, etc.

In fact, about the only thing I can say negative about a 1971 is that they
are expensive to find in good condition (more so than many splits!) ...
especially a Westy.  As they are so versatile and great for just USING and
not collecting.  People will gladly pay $6K for a good one that needs
paint.... because people appreciate the value of a Westy, even if they
aren't into VW's :)

And maintenance is a lot simpler on these as you point out... especially the
1971 still using the Type 1 engine.

Now they didn't offer propane until later, but pretty standard kits that
would retro into a '71... or find a later Loaf and just convert it over to
Type 1 engine.  Keep your split what it was... and keep the loaf for your
trips.

Of course, I respect your decision as it is yours, but it is sure hard
having owned a 1971 bus to see why anyone would want to go through all that
trouble with a splitty when VW already built what you are attempting.  Just
to have that front windshield?

And the trend toward unique models like Westy's turning into really
expensive collector items is starting to happen with loafs too.  I know many
on this list might laugh at that, but go try to find a rust-free Loaf Westy
or Truck.  They are actually more prized than Splits by many... for the
mechanical and camping kit reasons that Ronnie is hinting at...
