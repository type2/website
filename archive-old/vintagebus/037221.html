From what I understand the oil loses its lubricating properties about 235*F 
(or is it 225*F?) So I dont want to be there.   I figure 170 to 210 is fine 
and dandy.  higher than 210 and I'm keeping an eye on the guage and /or 
driving at lower rpms. (as 2600-2800 seems the optimal cooling, but generally 
I drive faster than that)  If the temp climbs above 210 at all, the motor 
gets an early oil change.  And that oil often as not that had got hot has an 
inconsistant consistancy, not all one uniform , but has gloppiness to it.  I 
don't think that is good.

As the cooling system is the heating system, it is interesting that about 
anything I have done to make better cooling has also made better heating.   
Like engine seals everywhere, thermostat, etc etc.
