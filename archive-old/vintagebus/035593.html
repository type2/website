yeah- that letter confirmed that the air cleaner I had was right for my bus, 
not from a later one- the oil bath version was standard not till 54, and the 
one I had is nice and evidently orginal to the bus, and also confirmed the 
engine had the right # on it.    I had a mere 3 m-codes.

Also the engine # thing-  some places used to register cars with the engine 
#, for a vw that gets a engine transplant  but later someone buys the bus and 
the title number is nowhere on the car this can be a real problem.  So 
official documentation of that engine number (the number on the title) being 
with that particular car (by the vin) can be really useful to get the VIN on 
the title to sort out paperwork.  A friend of mine is going through this with 
a bus he bought from a state that used to register vehicles to the engine 
number, and to get a title in his state he has to jump through these hoops to 
get the paperwork sorted.
