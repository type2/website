This may be funny to some of you...

Recently, I sold a 1/2 rack and agreed to ship it via Greyhound to Florida.
 Greyhound has 2 maximum sizes - 34x34x24 and 54x74x24 (don't take this
to heart, I'm going on memory right now)

I measured my rack and came up with 50x70x16.  Ok, I'm good to go.  I mounted
the rack on my car and took it over to Greyhound last Friday night.  Side
note - don't ever go to Greyhound on Friday night, it's a total nightmare
situation.  People everywhere, phone ringing off the hook, etc., etc.  Naturally,
I got the new guy, to which I explained what I was doing and then told him
how to do his job.  About 45 minutes later, we had it weighed and tagged
and ready to go.  Cool, I think, and head home.

1/2 hour later when I get home, there's a message telling me to come pick
up the rack, it's too big.  WTF?!?  I call up Tim, the guy who "helped"
me and he says, "My boss says it's too big, you'll have to come pick it
up."
"But it's smaller than your maximum size of 54x74x24," I argue.
"My boss says it's too big, we can't ship it."
"Fine, I'll come pick it up tomorrow morning, I can't make it back there
before you close."  I say
"Ok, there will be a $2 storage charge," Tiem replies.
"What?!?  I said I can't make it back before you close, I'm not paying you
to store it."
"Sorry, there's a $2 storage charge per piece per day."
"Fine, I'll see you tomorrow, I'm not paying the $2."

The next day I head back over there at 8 am, right when they open.  The
boss man is there and I explain to him how it's not too big, I called 3
Greyhound stations to make sure before I brought it down in the first place.
"But it's 50x70x16, that's bigger than 54x74x24," he says.
"How is that?"  I ask
"Well 50 is bigger than 24"
"Huh?  50 is less than 54," and I point to their size chart to show that
50 is less than 54 and 70 is less than 74 and 16 is less than 24.  "It's
less!" I state.

He looked puzzled and went to measure it again.  "Oh, you're right, it will
fit." he mumbled.

There was no mention of the $2...
