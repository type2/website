I also took out the wiring on my '67 Westy and had to clean up some breaks,
etc. A couple of hints:

- make sure the tailights are grounded to the body... use a test wire on
each taillight to effect a ground on both sides and see what happens.
Consider a permanent ground to the taillights. Screwing around by trying to
get a ground on the stock tailight set-up is exhausting and usually only
works short term.
- 9 prong box - I tired four of these before I got one to work. Even if you
have an NOS one moisture apparently gets inside and does strange things.
- the wire running along the gas tank and the drivers side ( heading to the
front?)sounds like the power source to the interior lights which is
Westfalia only. However this usually is attached to the +ve pole of the
battery and also has an in-line fuse.
