Looking at it again, maybe this should be parsed as:

(Sliding door)   (front turn signals until 1/60)

In other words, before 1960, M 164 meant front turn signals.  After
that, as you noted, it meant "sliding door".

The 1960 section of the book bears this out a little.  Apparently,
before 1960, German buses had semaphores.  So front turn signals
(US style) would have been called out as an M-code.  From June 1,
1960, all "Inland" models got front turn signals instead of semaphores.
No need for an M-code after that if the bus happened to be going
to the States.

Trivia: The German term for these (bullet/nipple) signals is 
"Warzenblinker", which Babelfish translates to "wart turn signal".
The later, flatter style, which I think is called "fried egg" here,
is called "frog eye" or "fish eye" in German.
