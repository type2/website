Brake fade is a condition caused by the lining melting and turning to gas.
The gas prevents the friction material from grabbing the drum/rotor.  In
*addition*, with extended brake heating, you can boil the brake fluid,
which would cause a soft pedal in addition to the fade.  The boiled fluid
is essentially ruined, so after your brakes cool, you are still stuck with
a soft pedal.  That's why people bleed their brakes several times during
the day at track events.  

In performance cars, and bikes, they employ "gas slotting" or drilled
rotors to dissipate the gas.  It's a religious issue as to which to do;
some do both.   I have drilled rotors on the 911, and yes, it does make a
big difference.

What we need on buses are finned drums to help dissipate the heat!
