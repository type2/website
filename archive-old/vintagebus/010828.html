re senders and guages it seems that guages themselves rarely fail, while senders do.  I have been worried about my oil pressure the past week or so, as my guage would read 0 at idle and the light come on at idle above 80 degrees C (175 F).   actually paid a shop to hook up a guage to my bus, it read 12psi at idle, not 0.  I bought a new vdo sender, comes with a 2 year warranty.  The old vdo sender was dated 4/78.  I had found it on a case probably 5 years ago.  think it lasted longer enough. The new one is dated 9/99.  Okay the bus was working fine, but I was getting wrong info and feel much better to really know what is going on, don't want to throw a rod due to lack of lubrication.  Now my guage reads just over 10psi at idle when it has warmed up.

I like the idea of calibrating a sender, but then you need the tools to check it out, which unfortunately I don't have.

So buying a new sender instead of using one you find on a case is a good idea.  Wouldn't mind calibrating the old screw terminal ones for my 36hp though.
