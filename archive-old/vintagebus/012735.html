It cracks me up when we VW folks rave on about good old "German" steel. I
own a collection of Foreign Car Digest Magazines <featuring VW's> which
roughly covers 1960 through 1965 inclusive. Somewhere within this collection
there is an article or small blurb ala Reader's Digest style margin drivel
which gave a statistic concerning the amount of U.S.S. steel that was
exported to Germany for the production of the Volkswagens of that era. I
believe that at one point in time the United States was providing the
majority of the sheet steel. Unfortunately I dont think that I have the time
to glean through the five years of issues to find this quote. This sounds
like baloney but I was amazed as well. Possibly I can find the issue and
give a proper citation. A scan of the article would be possible at that time.
Conversely, I chuckle about the "Buy American" bias against the VW products
of that era based on the same quote.
