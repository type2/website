think I've got the CR fiasco nailed down.  I'm going to balance the CR
across the 4 cylinders by doing 2 things.  #1 - It's going to require a set
of .080 shims (I guess now this is considered a spacer) on all 4 cylinders.
Next off, I'm going to flip flop the heads side to side.  This ends up
requiring that I shave .5 cc less off of one cylinder and tends to match the
deck height a little better.  It still worries me that #1 is so off.   No
matter what, I have to hog out 4.5cc's yet out of this combustion chamber so
I guess I'll just start with the Makita die-grinder until I think enough is
enough.  I checked the 2 cylinders in question once more and got the same
results.  When I'm done, the CR across the cylinders will be
6.91/6.92/6.95/6.93  I would like to have the extra power that 7 or 8 gives
