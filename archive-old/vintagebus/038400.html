We made it!  I'm in my living room writing this letter.

The list of Thank You's is so long that I have to organize it first.  Thom
did all the work at his place, so correct all the "we" references in his
letter <grin>.

We drove 51 to 56MPH the whole way, 60MPH when in city traffic (Portland,
Olympia, etc.).  Ran great.  I tried 60 to 65MPH one 20 mile stretch and
pulled over and found engine WAY HOT, so I kept it at 56MPH or under the
whole rest of the way.

Only two problems, both were the kinds we anticipated.  Looks like the bus
had a poor 6 to 12V conversion and fuel guage isn't working (or hooked
up?)... ran out of gas in Oregon mountains where we were using 2x normal
fuel (so caculations were WAY OFF for a 50 mile stretch).  No biggie, as we
had a 2Gal gas can on-board and filled it up and were off in less than 5
minutes.

The starter also failed right as we entered Washington.  I looking at it, it
too looked 6V and must have just been run too long at 12V, but I haven't
really researched this to be sure.  But that was easily overcome by careful
planning when stopping.  Make sure you were pointing downhill or on flat
surface and a simple push start had us going after gas stop and rest breaks.

I will be sleeping and catching up on other stuff, so won't be near email
until Monday morning!
