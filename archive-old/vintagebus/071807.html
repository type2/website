Actually, with RPMS too low or too high, the engine runs hotter.   as rpms go 
up, cooling air blown increases, and so does heat produced, but not at the 
same rate as each other.  work goes up with the square of the speed.  It all 
boils down to that 2600 to 2800 rpms is the coolest speed, according to the 
several buses I have checked this in, from 1600 sp  with early 64 gearing to fat 
2000cc motor in no rgbox bus.    below 2600 the engine runs hotter  the slower 
it goes.  above 2800rpms, the engine runs hotter the faster it goes. 
I don't tend to drive 2700 rpms, though.

Check this out for yourself, drive along at 40, then 45, then 50 till the 
numbers level off (will take a few minutes), then 55, then 60, then 65. etc  
write it all down.   If you have a tach, even better.   I'd love to get info from 
a range of people.   It would be interesting to try to compare temps w/ diff 
gearing too, but you have to take into account outside temps, humidity, etc, so 
that could be tricky.

no, I dont run  a berg dipstick, I have oil temp guages.
