You might want to have the oil pickup tube hole in the case drilled and reamed
for the later larger sized pickup tube that has the bolting leg on it to
connect to one of the sump plate studs.  While you are having this done you
will have to have the end plug removed and you might as well have the passage
into and out of the oil pump enlarged.

production.  Unless you are only going to drive your truck in town at 50MPH or
less.  You might want to look at rebuilding a 1600cc engine to look stock and
modify the tranny to do lower RPMs at higher speeds.  Especially since you
live in Arizonia were the last time we where there the folks on the highways
were doing 80-90MPH instead of the posted 70MPH.  At 54MPH in our 1960 panel
camper with 25" dia. tires on the front and 29" dia tires on the rear at
3,000RPM in fourth.  It felt like we were running down range with missiles
being shot at us.  Now with the modified tranny that has 1964-67 reduction
gear boxes (Plus changed the front suspension at the same time to keep the
brakes from 180 degreeing the bus during panic stops.), 25"dia tires in front
and 27" dia. tires in the rear we speed down the miles at 66MPH at 3,000RPM in
fourth gear.  If you are interested in this tranny modification we would be
glad to email it to you.  EEE&B
