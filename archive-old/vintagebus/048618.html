The back of the tent has a rubber molding that slides into the channel on
the drip rail.  I know the earlier tents would unsnap the molding from the
tent which would make putting the molding onto the bus much easier.  It
would seem a lot of work to slide the tent on the channel before you set it
up, but I guess that is what has to be done on this style of tent.  Sewn
around the back of the tent are flaps that will fit around the cargo doors
when they are opened in order to form a seal between the bus and the tent.
The back flap is zippered on 3 sides and rolls up when you are ready to
pass between the bus and the tent.
