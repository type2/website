I have used a 'heat gun', sort of a heavy duty hairdryer to get the same
results.  An advantage is you can apply heat as you insert the trim. I haven't
used glue- although that may help if the groove in the cabinet has splinters
broken off it.   Using the heat to remove the trim helps with reducing
splinterage.  But, the idea of hot water may be good to clean the trim up-
using laundry detergent or whatever.   The heat gun is helpful for removing
the plastic trim from the cabinets, as well.  I have replaced missing trim
from baywindow westies in junkyards, also.   Though some of it is a thicker,
so beware.   And I have heard you can get new plastic trim from RV places,
think Van Bondo was saying that when he was finding bits for the Resto
FromHell.   It may just be my OPBO, But Ithink the white is a nice contrast,
better than chrome.
