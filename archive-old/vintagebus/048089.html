Hi John,
If you lower the rear you must lower the front or look/feel rediculous and 
have slight steering problems because you are riding on more of the rear of 
the steering tires.  While lowing the bus would help handling we have found 
at least equal handling improvments by installing stiffer shocks and proper 
wieght range tires.  Lowering the bus most likely means using smaller tires 
that are much below the stock 1530Lbs. wieght range.  Also if you are talking 
about using the type 3 axles the brakes are less powerfull than the 1955-67 
ones and we have seen lowered type 3 rear drums crack due to changing thier 
geometery in relation to the pavement on the much lighter type 3!

<< I would like a little better speed on the freeway without winding out the
