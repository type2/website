I built one of these about 15 years ago for my 67 21 window.  Don't
believe all the hype, it is all in how you build it.  For a long time,
1835cc was what everybody ran, then 2180cc when 82mm cranks became the
mainstay.  After about 50K miles, I sold the motor to a friend for his
71 westy (hmmm, haven't talked to him in ages).

I spent some time hanging around Gary Larsen when I was a teen and
learned quite a bit from watching him build drag VW engines for my
friends.  They all drag raced, but I had a bus!  His ultimate street
motor was either a 2275cc or 2332cc.  As I recall, the 94mm cylinders
are just as thin as 92mm ones.

My friends all drove their bugs to the races, raced them (12 to 14
second cars), then drove them home.

Rather than be concerned with engine size, I would be concerned with the
engine builder.
