Joe wrote:

John is right. The AE case, all things being equal, is a far superior case. It
has larger oil passages, dual pressure relief valves, and is made of a better
allow of magnesium than the H case. Since you're not building a stock engine,
count your blessings and carry on. 

You wouldn't, in my opinion, want to build an H case up as you have described.
The H case was designed for the smaller oil cooler, and even though the
doghouse cooler can be retrofitted to the H case, you'll never get the full
benefit because your oil passages in the case are smaller.

BTW, the AE case was intended for dual-port heads. I don't think it makes any
difference to the case, but I would consider going back to DP heads. I
*believe*, but do not know for sure, that they will give you better power and
better cooling of the heads (equalling longer life).
- - - - -
I'd stick with the AE case you have rather than find an H case, unless you are
doing a resto on the beast.   because 1)  you have it, 
2)  bigger oil passages
3)better alloy
4) dual oil relief, as has been said

But I must say that I have happily driven around in H cases for years.  Well,
I wasn't in the H case itself, I was in the car the H case was in.  Ya.  okay.

But keep it SP.   SP heads are sturdier.   Dual port heads are notorious for
cracking.  A little bit more power, but not worth it.
