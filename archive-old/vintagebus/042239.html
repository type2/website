On Tue, 26 Jun 2001, Ev Rein wrote:

> 1776cc 
> single or dual port heads (whatever tickles your fancy)
> single heavy duty valve springs

If you're not planning on running high RPMs, you don't need these.

> stock cam
> stock intake/oil bath/stock exhaust with heater boxes
> vacuum advance dizzy
> solid rocker shafts/swivel feet adjusters

If you aren't running high RPMs and the requisite stiff valve springs, the
stock shaft/rocker/adjuster will do fine.  The stock adjusters are easier
to adjust properly and it's hard to screw up the stock geometry.

> doghouse 
> compufire drop-in module

It's a religious argument, but the stock points are pretty darned reliable
and don't tend to die in unfixable ways like the electronic gizmos.

> full flow with Berg oil lines

Keep the lines short and mount the filter upright so that you can fill it
before installation.  The HP-1 filter is a good idea unless you make
provisions for a pressure bipass.  Myself, I just change oil at reasonable
intervals.

> balance it, too

Always a good idea!

Of course, if you simply want a reliable engine, the Mexican longblocks
are pretty hard to beat.  bolt in and go!
