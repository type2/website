Jamie Auch advised all of us of a great source (Home
Depot) for 5' x 5' White Baltic Birch panels.
This is great but two things:

1.> Double check the actual sizes needed for the
headliner prior to purchasing wood.
2.> I have found two kinds of Baltic birch panels.
First is the kind that is all wood <no masonite> with
at least a 1/16" of the Birch wood on both sides.
Supposedly popular with the "scroll saw hobby craft"
crowd. These folks need both sides to show for their
cut outs of animals or whatever. The other product
that I have seen and bought from HomeDepot is masonite
with a microscopic paper thin veneer of "Baltic Birch"
on one side only. It's great if it has been found in
5x5's I have only found it in 4x8's. Try to use all of
the same kind of material as I have found that the two
types as described above will stain differently. Do
tests of stain color to match between products.

Splitties use the 5'x5' and I don't remember if I used
the same size or the cheapie 4x8 stuff to redo the
interior (headliner)of a '83 Vanagon full-westy
camper. I think it was the 5x5. I have no experince
regarding the width span measurement of the bay window
headliner.
Good luck!
