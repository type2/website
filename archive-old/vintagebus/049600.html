beetle ones like that have a plastic (or better, metal) tube that goes up
front. not too far on a bug.
many old bus dudes mounted the gauge on the rear window or back seat,
facing front; you'd look in the rearview mirror to see the pressure.
electric ones generate a signal in the can you screw in the oilpressure
port; this signal is what the gauge sees.

FWIW: if you use the tube type, get a metal tube; plastic can stretch,
distorting your readings... they can also break, spilling your oil.
having said that, the electric ones are generally not as accurate.
