I'll stick my nickle in here as it seems to be a recurring thread. I've got
the large green coleman thats been in the family since the 60's. Like
Michael said it adjusts from 3-5000btu's. We first used it in a tent camper
and now I use it in our westies. I just recently found the red 3000btu here
in Victoria, from what I've seen the small one was only sold in Canada.
Things I like, I have small kids and really dont want them to burn
themselves, the original coleman lit tells you that you can set a piece of
paper on it and it wont ignite. Experiance shows me that thats pretty close,
you wouldnt want to leave your hand on it, but you wouldnt get instant 2nd
or 3rd degree burns.
I have mixed feelings about propane, I dont like haveing empty canisters to
throw away and I'm not real sure I want a gas that seeks low places in the
camper.
I like simple, add white fuel, tip, and light and youve got heat. I set it
on the sink, crack a vent and just let it sit, it'll turn itsself off when
it runs out of fuel.
Call me a throw back, all my camp gear is white gas, dont even ask how many
lanterns I own, but camping in the bush, towns are kinda scarce and a gallen
of white fuel goes a long way. I've been told but cant verify that white
fuel is only unleaded gas, might be why coleman sells a duel fuel product.
