sounds like a sticky clutch to me actually.   If it was my truck I'd pull the 
motor, check the clutch (check pressure part of clutch too- place on floor, 
stand on it and bounce up and down to make sure it springs correctly), and 
see if it is the main seal or tranny shaft seal that is leaking.  
you can pull the tranny tooo and replae the nose cone seal, but the truth is 
that on at least 95% of the old VWs out there that seal leaks and there is 
ooze and it keeps things from rusting.  You might top up the tranny oil.

freeway flyer you have a 3.88 r&p and keep the RGBox (generally).  stock R&P 
is 4.12 or 4.37.  lower r/p means higher gearing.
type3 tranny means no rgboxes with the swing axle center section.   gearing 
may vary.  the idea is you get higher gearing over all as you don't reduce it 
by 1.39 (little nut) or 1.26 (big nut)
