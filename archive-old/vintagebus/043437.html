Complete combustion of most (all?) hydrocarbons gives you carbon dioxide
and water.  For propane, C3H8, it's

   C3H8 + 5O2  ->  3CO2 + 4H20

If I remember how to do molecular weights right, this means that
44.1 g of propane will combine with 160. g of oxygen to give you
48.0 g of carbon dioxide and 72.1 g of water.  In other words,
burning a pound of propane puts 1.6 pounds of water (about 3/4 of
a quart) into the air.
