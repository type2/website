I see a LOT of stuff advertised on ebay as being barndoor that is NOT.
Barndoor is one of those magic words that people search for, like
"NOS" and "Empi", and cojures up dollar signs in people's eyes.

Furthermore, there is widespread misinformation about what really is "barndoor" - people need to learn the difference between "barndoor," "wolfsburg"
and "hannover" buses.  There's also "European" parts which go up
to '60 or '61 (I think it's '61 upon futher reflection, to
coincide with the end of semaphores)

To me, a "barndoor" part is a barndoor-body specific part.  Ie 
"barndoor" engine lid, apron, pedals, dash, windshields, switches,
taillights, front beam, transxaxle, front seat, steering components,
and probably a couple others I missed.  These pieces will ONLY fit
a REAL barndoor. Spare tire tray.  Gas tank.   Bck seat top half.

Even the early barndoor-era pickups and ambulances had an engine lid 
which was significantly different than the later, non-barndoor style.

A *wolfsburg* part is in a set that *includes* barndoors and early '56.
Examples of this are bakelite dome lights and six-leg middle seats.

*European* parts consist of such diverse elements as semaphores and
symmetrical bulb headlights.  These went to '61 on euro-spec buses,
yet are *constantly* being touted as barndoor.  Ribbed semaphores
are 53 and earlier barndoor and split bug.

Hannonver parts are just about everything else, mid-56 -> 67
