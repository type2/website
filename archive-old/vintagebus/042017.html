The forks were welded in two places and they seem to fatigue after a mere
30 years or so.  I usually weld them all the way around and I notice a lot
of replacement forks come that way.

The other breakage point on the old-style forks is the half-circle cutouts
where the bearing rides.  These get deeper and deeper until the end of the
fork breaks off.  You could weld up the opening, but a new fork is much
easier to do!
 
> The arm looks usable if it were welded back, but I'm going to see if the

> welded up real good-like?  This is a stock vehicle.

Actually, the dune buggy folk have much stronger arms built for stiff
clutches.  They also have wider arms with the spring clip sitting in a
hole rather than a notch.  Definitely worth it if you're replacing the
arm.
 
> > I dug around and found some better clips (the kind that are like a 
> torsion spring instead of just C shaped) and am going to wire them in 
> place when I put it back together.

good.  The wire can go in the notch around the bearing.

BTW, if the bearing has been sitting loose on the shaft, it would be a
good idea to replace it.  They seem to get beat up and you probably don't
want to remove the engine again!

I also safety-wire the snap ring that goes around the throwout fork shaft.
If the snap ring comes out of its groove it can bend the fork another
removal hassle.
