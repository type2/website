On the topic of remote car disabling...this probably works in one
of two ways.  I know that some of the proposed OBD standards (not
OBD-II, but I think OBD-III or later) include a "phone home"
mechanism for your engine computer...when the check engine light
comes on, it transmits the fact that it came on, any codes, and
maybe your VIN, probably over the cell network, to the Correct
Persons.  The Correct Person may be the dealer who bugs you to
bring it in for service, or the DMV who sends you a nasty-gram
about getting your smog equipment fixed.  Given that you have
the "call out" capability, it would be reasonably easy to extend
this to include a "call in" capability that would allow the
Correct Persons to cut off your fuel remotely.  This scares me,
because I am sure that the remote-cutoff capability is advertised
as being "encrypted with a code that can NEVER be broken."  NEVER
always turns out to be a lot sooner than they think for these
kinds of things, so imagine the fun on the highways when you
can buy a box out of the back of Popular Mechanics for $100 that
can shut down any nearby car.

The other way wouldn't require built-in support in the car
computer.  I think some companies have been working on disablers
that are essentially powerful, directional radio transmitters.
By aiming a powerful burst of RF at your car, they hope to
overload the electronics with spurious signals to the point
that the fuel delivery is affected and your car stalls or at
least slows down.  However, there's nothing that says your ABS
computer won't decide to lock up one wheel while you're flying
down the road at 70, either...

I don't think the bad guys will be running away in Buses,
but I think the smarter bad guys will try to use cars with
points ignition and as few electronics as possible if they
contemplate running from the law.  (BTW, I didn't know that
the Supremes had further lowered the standards on search...)
