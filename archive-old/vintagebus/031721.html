The first thing I would do is check the wires (ala Clara) but the biggest
problem I have heard you mention is your trying to set things with 3 fouled
plugs. VW's in my experience DO NOT like fouled plugs. I have had many a
cars come through the shop with the symptoms you described, with the owners
having changed everything but the plugs, the answer was I just put plugs in
a few hundred miles ago, I answered by saying "Here's the deal, I'll install
new plugs, if it doesn't help you can have them for free", I always got
paid!

valves dont seem badly out.   the compression seems on the low side, but not

horrible.  But really, all 4 spark plugs should look the same   #1 is not 
burning the same as the other three.  this could be a bad plug wire, needing

new plugs, or something in the motor. if you start it up, then with a rag 
pull one spark plug wire off the dist cap, then replace it , how does the 
sound differ?  check all 4.  pulling each plug should changg the way the 
motor runs exactly the same, no favorites or bad guys.  If 3 make bigger 
changes, and one very little or none, it is probably #1.  And as the 
compression in # is not 25psi or something dramatically lower than the 
others, I would check the spark plug wires.  they should be flexible, and
not 
have cracks, etc.
