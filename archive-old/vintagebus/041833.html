Keep in mind that this is a low-compression engine desigened for Mexican
Nova (regular) grade gasoline.  It's a great utilitarian piece of
hardware, which is what I bought it for.  Performance folk will probably
want to do some internal mods before hanging those dual carbs on it.

Mine looks great with the stock pulley and sump.  I did switch to a sump
plate with a drain plug to make oil changes less exciting.

Unless you plan on doing something like autocrossing, deep sumps don't do
much for you.  In normal usage, the stock setup is better.

If you're drilling the hole for the thermostat stud, _don't_ drill a small
pilot hole first.  The pilot hole will allow the full-size bit to grab and
pull itself in too fast.  Just centerpunch the hole location and drill
with the full size bit the first time.

You'll also have to replace one stud on the top rear of the case parting
line with a longer stud to accomodate the dual port manifold.  You will
probably be able to pull the one out of the old engine unless some
gawdawful threadlocker was used on the old stud (<blue word> GEX <blue
word><blue word>)
