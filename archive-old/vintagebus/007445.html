> The valve in the 1500 is called the oil pressure relief valve.  It
performs two
> functions.  When the oil is cold and thick, it forces the plunger down and
> exposes two ports, one to the main galley and one to the sump.  As the oil
> warms and thins, the piston is forced, by the spring,  past the port to
the
> sump and oil flows through both the cooler and main galley.  In a hot
engine,
> the plunger rises above the main galley port and flows all of the oil
through
> the cooler and then to the cooler.
>
> Oil, like water, is not very compressible.  A higher volume forced through
the
> same size passages should create a higher pressure.  This higher pressure
will
> hold the plunger open to the sump port and the main galley port for a
longer
> period of time and at higher oil temperature/lower viscosity.  Your oil
will be
> hotter before it flows through the oil cooler.  This should raise the oil
> temperature, and operating temperature of your lubrication system, crank,
> bearings, rods.  Gene Berg recommended using the stock 26 MM pump in a
single
> relief case.  (and a 30 MM pump in the dual relief case.)  Based upon his
> explanation in his book of technical articles, I'd go with it.
>
> In your position, I'd install a new stock 26 MM oil pump.
