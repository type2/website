>>I've read before about the '67 air cleaner pre-heat hose running
forward through the front tin to the heat exchanger, but I remain
unclear on what is exactly meant. (My '67 has a '73 beetle 1600DP in it

>>Does the hose (1) actually run to the heat exchanger itself, which
must have a non-standard port on it for the hose to connect to? In other
words, does it actually take warmed fresh air from inside the heat
exchanger? <<

The paper/foil pre-heater hose runs to the heat exchanger to a "port"
which is just above the header pipe coming from #1 cylinder exhaust
port, just below the clam-shaped heat exchanger vent (where the heated
air goes when it doesn't go through the tubes to heat the bus).  Because
I have three '67s, I guess I've come to look at it as standard. 
Actually, both of my heat exchangers have the port (both left and right)
and they came with a cap to tap on the one that didn't need the port for
the pre-heater.  Mine are Dansk, and five-plus years old. So, yes, it
does actually take warmed fresh air from inside the heat exchanger.

>>OR does it (2) run forward and connect to an intake pipe that bolts to
the heat exchanger and is in the engine-cooling airflow?<<

I haven't spent much time on pre-'67s, but I do understand that there is
a pipe that bolts/screws to the exchanger for picking up pre-heated air.
  Exactly where I don't know.  I take it to be the part you describe to
Tom below.
