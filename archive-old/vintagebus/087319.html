A standard method to make an exact copy of a boat- make a mold and plug.

Theoretically simple. Flip boat over (square it up in to shape)
2 apply release agent
3 apply polyester resin and fiberglass to make an exact copy of the 
shape
4 separate.
now you have an exact copy of (the hole the boat makes in the water)-
you see where I am going,
then you use this plug as your mold. then lay up glass and resin to 
make a copy of the hull
THOUSANDS OF BOATS ARE AND WERE MADE THIS WAY. people would copy wooden 
boats to be made in fiberglass.

Personally I have been thinking about building a small dingy for a one 
man shop
It is not a skilled labor job . Once the first one is made you are set 
up to do an indefinite amount.
Your demand will be hard to calculate-there is the initial investment 
probably at least a few to several boats worth.  The more you make and 
sell the less the percentage
your initial cost is in the final product.
Then once you have the bottom you copy the "top"

A lot to check out but you might find a good business
