     the *best* system is a true full-flow oil filtering system... where the 
outlet
side of the oil pump is plugged, and a special oil pump cover installed that
diverts outlet oil out of the case, through the high-quality oil hose, 
through your
spin-on filter adapter.... through another high quality oil hose and back 
into the
engine case through a fitting installed in the main oil galley, left case 
half in a
boss almost halfway up the edge of the case .... that is true full flow. all 
of the
oil, all of the time.
     *** there *is* a variation on this style.... a banjo-style return 
fitting that
will bolt in place of the oil *control* valve on the front (front is front) 
of the
bottom left case half.... this way you don't have to drill and tap into the 
case,
but the downside is that you have an inch or so less effective clearance 
between
engine vitals and the ground, etc.... only for engine cases having *both* 
oil
pressure relief AND control valves, though....
     some people use the top-of-the-engine-case adapter thing which bolts 
over the
former location of the original oil cooler to plumb in a larger cooler and 
also a
spin-on filter adapter... this only filters part of the oil, part of the 
time... i
have also heard it said that cooling is impeded, since the air restriction 
offered
by the oil cooler which was designed into the system is no longer there...  
dunno
the technicals on that one, though I have heard of people fabbing a 
cooler-shaped
piece of sheet metal to accomodate the shape of the blockoff adapter and 
still fill
out the silhouette of a real cooler... but if you are going to do that, you 
might
as well go to the trouble of installing a real full-flow system.
     If it was me.... i'd replace those oil lines as well as the fittings 
that
attach to them... IIRC that's either a 45 or 60 degree flare (for most of 
the
better pre-man hoses and systems out there...)... a lot of leaks like this 
are
simply due to overtightening... mangles the sealing areas, etc....
     the last full flow setup i installed was on a friend's '69 type I... i 
located
the filter mount under the left rear fender, and attached a shield in front 
of the
filter to protect it from road debris and so on.... you might need to do 
something
similar in your situation.
