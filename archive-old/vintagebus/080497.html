Congrats on the new bus!!!!

I've found that a large axe comes in handy for rescuing old buses.    loosen 
lugs, Jack up bus, brace securely.   Log rounds are good.  Remove rim, pound 
on outside circle rim of drum with blunt end of axe.  pound in a few different 
places, like front and back.  You want the axe with the long handle, not the 
puny hand axe.   An 8lb mallet with long handle also works.   Lift the large 
pounding device with both hands, legs braced, aim carefully (miss your own legs, 
are you wearing sturdy jeans and boots?, and miss any friends nearby) and  
aim through the drum, yelling loudly:

  "Live!!! you son of an unwashed she-goat!!!" (thwack!!) 
"YOU! (thwunk!) lousy! (thwunk!) rusting chunk! (thunk!) of crusty rust!!!! ( 
thwunck!) 
"Churning urn! (thwuk) of burning funk!" (thwunk)
"I'm doing this for your own good" (thwunk!)  
"Sweet little bus, now be nice!" (diinngggg!!!!!!!!!!!) 

When the sound of the pounding changes from a dull 'thwunk' to a resonating 
'diinnggggg', the shoes have come loose from the drum.   Kinda loosens up the 
upper back as well. 

I think I have some spare drums if you need them
