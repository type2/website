> Hi- On my 61 standard bus I was going to replace the shift rod guide
> bushing (or sleeve).  When I tried to undo the coupler with the two
> square headed screws, one of the square heads was broken off, I guess by
> a PO.  After struggling to loosen the other square headed screw I had the
> great pleasure of breaking it off as well.  (It was a team effort between
> me and the PO =o).  So my question is, where do I go from here?  Is there
> some way to get the broken pieces of screw out or have they permanently
> welded themselves to the shift rod?  Thanks for any help you can offer.

A Dremel with a cutoff wheel can cut a screwdriver slot in the remaining
bolt. With the addition of a good non-WD40 penetrating oil, the vibration
and heat generated will usually allow you to back them out with a
fingernail. If not, try a screwdriver. Hope this helps.
