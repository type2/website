Halfway through the arc of the rocker arms pushing the valves are the 
adjusting studs in parallel to the valve stems?  If not the valve guides will 
wear out sooner to too much side thrust pressure.  Just guessing your 
pushrods might be too short or even too long for your engine and thusly might 
be partly the reason your #3 cylinder valve running out of adjustment.

You might want to get one of those CB oil filter/pumps which do need a cut 
and rewelding at a different angle the neck and slight redrilling through the 
oil passages between the pump cover and where the oil filter threads on to 
function with a stock muffler with its left heat riser tube on the front of 
the muffler.  This will clean the motor, oil cooler and help extend the life 
of the engine bearings.  30 MPG (IMP) _at_ 55 MPH _at_ 3,000 RPM is great for a 40
