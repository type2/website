Rex,
  It sounds like the problem I had. The bearing 
had "wallered" (Texan speak) out the arms on the fork 
(mine had "sprung" one of the springs). Replace the 
shaft/fork and put a dab of grease where the throwout 
bearing meets the arms, to keep it from wearing so 
quickly.
  Be careful to get a good fork- many rebuilt ones have 
crappy welds (even I could do better!). Also take your 
old one to make sure the grooves for the circlips are in 
the correct location. Do all this and make sure all 
parts/bushings are in place before mounting that d*mn 
spring! Ask me how I know...
