funnily enough, when I think about getting an engine to run cool, the list of 
things to check has a big overlap with things to getthe heat working, because 
the heat and cooling systems all all interrelated.

engine to body seal good?
gaps in tin are bad
good engine compartments seal
plug holes in battery tray/alt batt tray (for air flow in engine compartment)
tune the bus up :)
check fresh air hoses are good, 
and good connections from fresh air hoses through into the heat exchangers
have the thermostat installed and working (flaps direct air for better 
cooling)
a doghouse is better than an upright cooler.,
a typeIV cooler mod is even better
have the L deflecter aka Hoover bit with your dog house
have the little air deflectors under the rear edge of the cylinder tin 
installed
I like my oil temp and pressure guages
oil temp really should be under 230, I feel uneasy if it gets over 210F 
(oil degrages with too hot a temp)
