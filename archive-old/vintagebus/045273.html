Hmm, we find that most folks who say this have not realized that thier third 
gear in the freeway flier now covers almost the same RPM/MPH area that the 
old stocker fourth gear did.  Third in the freeway flier with the 1964-67 
reductions is only 2-3 MPH lower at 3,000 RPM as the 1960-63 stocker tranny 
with it's stock reductions.  The freeway flier compared to the late part of 
1964-67 tranny is about 10-11 MPH lower MPH at 3,000 RPM so it actually 
climbs better than the stocker fourth.   The freeway flier fourth in essence 
becomes a fifth gear.  We are looking forward to see how Steven G.'s new 3.44 
R&P tranny does.  It might be the way to go by moving up the third and fourth 
gears just a bit more.  Allowing the use of smaller diameter rear tires to 
attain the same high MPH at lower RPMS as the 3.875 R&P with larger rear 
tires.  This would be very helpfull for our pre-late 1962 buses that have the 
lower, thinner rear wheel wells that do not allow bigger radial tires than 
205/75R15 to be used.
