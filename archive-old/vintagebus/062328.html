With the heaters closed, extra air is vented out of the top of the heat
exchanger stove pipe through those little holes, or slots on later busses.
The hot air around the heat exchanger is dead air for the most part, venting
slower than the exiting air past the heads, but enough to get the hot air
away from the pressed on aluminum fin heat radiators inside the exchanger
tin preventing overheating in that area.
