 Next, I want to add the cover plate to the oil pump that allows a

I go with Jason  S. on this.  Try the doghouse out first before adding the
second cooler.
You can always add the lines later.   For the filter use AN fittings and
Teflon lined SS braided lines.
I've had trouble finding 1/2" ID line for AN8 fittings and have the smaller
ID lines on my vehicles.  Most of the hydraulic shops around here don't want
to mess with the larger lines.
The AN8 fittings are going to restrict the flow down to that level anyway
but there would be less restriction with larger lines.  Maybe you can check
with the local aircraft mechanics.
I mounted my filter as I had seen on the Vanbondo RFH at about a 45 degree
angle using one of the bumper bracket holes.  The filter is a little inboard
of the rear tire and I haven't had any problem with rocks etc.  Use either a
Fram HP-1 filter or the K&N equivelent or something like a System 1 filter.
I've been using Berg pumps with 30mm gears thinking that maybe this would be
good since I'm running through all that extra plumbing, but it isn't really
necessary.  If using a big pump, it might be good to use a pressure relief
cover since the oil doesn't see any relief until it's gone through all the
add on plumbing.

Make sure your gauge is reading what you think it is before you start.
Stranger things have happened.

>sump,
I run sumps on my vehicles as well.  They won't do much for the temps,
except keep the oil from warming up quickly in the winter.  Ground clearance
is something to consider if you like to go camping in the boonies as well.
I haven't had any trouble, but it does reduce your clearance and if you
knock the sump off in the boonies be sure and have your hiking boots.  I
have to admit I get a little worried about that sump being on there
sometimes.

,second cooler.
A second cooler can help alot I think.  I would think it would need a
by-pass set up so if you are running in the winter you won't be cooling the
oil too much.  There are automatic temperature controlled switches for this
but I was thinking maybe a manual valve with dual ports?  Another
possibility I've considered is just switching the lines over for summer and
winter configurations and capping off the cooler.  I've been thinking on the
cooler for some time now and it's more of a question for me than the rest.
Everything else bolts up nice and easy.  The cooler means either I punch
holes in the driver's battery tray or I run a bunch of extra tube under the
bus.  I worry about oil starvation on startup with all that tube.  Might not
be a big deal, but then again, if the oil is cold it might take awhile to
get going through all that?  I also don't want to cut up my bus, but it
should be possible to do the louvers without really making an ugly mess?
