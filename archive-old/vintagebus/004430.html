Maybe if it had been VW factory built :) .....

This raises an interesting issue, however.  For years VW made much of the
fact that all "improved" or "revised" parts/components could be retro-fitted
to an earlier model without modification or adaptation.  If your fender, for
instance, got banged up it was replaced with a new part that was of the
latest design but fully compatible with the older vehicle.  It was not
uncommon to see a Beetle owned by a frugal person that had mismatched bits
hanging all over it, including in the engine compartment.  This probably
explains some of the anomalies we find on early VWs.
