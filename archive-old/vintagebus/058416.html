It seems a waste of time and effort to weld a cam since stock new ones are
so cheap.  Also why are there strict "rules" for trade in cams that need to
be a "rebuildable core", if a lobe is toast then it is a bad core.  If each
used cam was to be welded and ground again then the trade in core rules
would be much more slack.

Like reground lifters.  I know no one welds these and grinds them.  When
resurfaced the lifters will be a little bit shorter than before.

Unfortunately I do not have a reground stock cam to compare against a new
stock cam at this time, but when I do I will be sure to mic them out and see
if a difference does exist.

Normally it is just a better investment in my opinion to get a new stock
cam.  

Addressing the V-8 crowd, yes the cam "bottoms" can be ground off more to
provide lift at the top.  There was a great article about this in Car Craft
a little while ago and it was a good read.  Again hydro lifters are more
forgiving on this issue than solid lifters, just as Ray pointed out on the
pushrod geometry issue.

>reground cam (web-cam), <

With a reground cam the outer diameter is smaller than a stock cam.   Lift
can be higher than stock on the lobe side, but be "thinner" on the off-ramp
side.  Basically lift and duration/profile have nothing to due with the
outer diameter of the cam.  A lot of the V-8 cams are shallow re-grinds like
this, but with hydro lifters it really does not matter since manual
adjustments is not a factor. 

This could effectively cause the lifters to sit farther into the case due to
the narrower cam.  I would suspect at worst just a couple mm's.  Nothing
drastic or it should have been a "used up" cam where the hardened lobes
would have been worn through.

Of course it could be that lifters are shorter than normal as well... Or
just a combination of both scenarios compounded together. 

Granted WebCam has a great reputation so I doubt the cam is ground down too
much.
