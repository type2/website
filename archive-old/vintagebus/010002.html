I received this from Erin last Thursday.  Here it is in its entirety:

> Somehow James, I've been put back on the vintagvw list.  I was unsubbed
for
> a while and got the official signoff notice and then it started up again
> today for a few messages at least.  Is there like a big campaign to get me
> back on the list or something?
>
> Here's the latest - I got a call at work while I was unloading a semi of
> lumber.  It was Ron Barrett of Barrett Enterprises.  Somehow he got a copy
> of a message from Kevin Gallagher which was the original post I made about
> all the crappy parts I got.
>
> We talked for a good 15 minutes and he sincerely seemed like he wanted to
> make amends for the problems.  First off, he said the lifters are ground
> with a slight curvature on a 'DPR?' machine but not as curved as a new
> lifter.  As for the heads, they get them from GEX.  We've all heard enough
> about GEX to know why I'm having problems.  Here's something interesting
> though - Ron Barrett told me that the typical industry standard term
> 'rebuilt' for VW heads usually means that everything is checked to see if
> it's within spec.  The intake valves are reused, the exhaust valves are
NOT,
> seats reground, and guides replaced only if out of spec. (usually the
> exhaust guides end up being replaced.)
>
> It was totally Ron's suggestion that to remedy the situation that they do
> the following:
>
> Send me a new set of 4 intake valves and 4 exhaust valves...
> Send me a new set of 8 guides
> A set of new lifters (not reground)
> A check for $50.
>
> I told him I thought that was more than a fair deal.  He said I wouldn't
> have to send the lifters back but I'm going to anyway because I don't need
> them.  Ron seemed apologetic and said he's been in the VW business for 30
> years and wanted to make sure I was happy.  He was concerned about his
> reputation and simply wanted to make amends for this whole thing.  When he
> told me not to give up on the bus, that was also another tip-off that
> someone on the net had notified him.
>
> I'm kind of happy that I'm getting this stuff but I feel like I don't
> deserve it.  It ain't my style to bitch and scream to get freebies and it
> seems that is what I've managed to do. :-(  Right now though, I'll at
least
> go through the motions and send in the cores and whatever else is supposed
> to go back and at least get the parts Ron is offering me.  I'm still
pretty
> indifferent about the whole thing though.  I'll still have to decide
whether
> or not to continue working on the bus.  I had a couple of friends come by
> tonight that really want to go to California in the bus - I'd hate to
> disappoint them but at the same time, I'd hate to have to file bankruptcy
> over it too.  Time will only tell.
>
> On the lighter side of things, I took my 2 friends, Brian and Melisa for
an
> airplane ride tonight (rides for charity) and they both thoroughly enjoyed
> it.  It was Melisa's 2nd plane ride and Brian's first and we all had a
good
> time. I call them kids - they are aged 19/20 respectively whereas I'm 28.
I
> paid for it all (despite being broke already and spending emergency money)
> and felt better by treating them out than I have in the past 2 weeks.
>
> And one more thing Jim - Glen Hadley has offered to come help me build an
> engine - whether new or good used.  He lives in Iowa City, IA and is going
> to work about 70 miles from here and commute weekly.  On bad weather
> weekends, he said he'd come over and help me out and is entirely insistant
> on me not selling off the bus until he looks at it.  Good ol' Glen.
>
> I'm gonna carbon this message to the vintagebus list so they know what's
> going on.  Take care James.
>
> Erin
