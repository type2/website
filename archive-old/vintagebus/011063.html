Also you can just replace the pin itself only.  The steel pin rusts, and 
flakes off into a smaller diameter.  The bushings being made of brass or 
bronze though not harder are tougher than steel, and do not corrode as much 
so they wear out less.  This for us has taken out 70-80% of the slop in the 
steering several front ends.  Making the steering in them very acceptable.  
This is a good quick fix, and especially if you take a new center pin among 
many other parts for those who are going to recover an unknown condition bus, 
and drive it home!  So having a source of low price pins without all of the 
other parts is not totally a bad idea.
