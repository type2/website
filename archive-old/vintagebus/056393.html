I have wondered about this Hoover claim. It seems to be the density of
the metal and air surface presented would be the only variables having a
direct relation on an objects ability to dissapate heat.
I see that black surfaces can absorb more radiant, heat, like black cars
in the sun. I tested this with my new non contanct infra-thermometer.
Black cars were 113 on a 90 deg day, and white ones were 102 .
But the engine heat is internal, and is product of combustion, not
radiant light absorbsion.
Now if the paint itself was thick enough , I could see this as a thermal
insulator, causing the cases to remain hotter.
I too would like to see a naked case vs a white one, vs a black one, if
this theory is correct the white should be the hottest, and the black
the coolest.
