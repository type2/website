Perhaps, but we have seen fully loaded Buses show up at VW shows and swap
meets equiped with our lowering kits and they seemed to be working fine. No
Bearing problems reported. The Bug rear wheel bearings are very durable.
People have been using swing axle Bugs off road for decades and the bearings
seem to live a long time. The transmission is usually what will give out
first in a high strees or off road situation.
You might also take a look at how small the front wheel bearings are
compared to the rear.

Our kit is designed to keep the rear wheels straight and not with a
posititive or negative camber (when installed correctly).
As for the drums, we have not heard of anybody cracking type 3 drums used on
a Bus. If there is any concern about this, then the modified 1963 and
earlier Bus drums might be the better choice (although these are getting
hard to find).

As for reduction boxes, these made their first appearance on the Kubelwagen
alond with special Kubel-only front spindles for additional ground
clearance. A few years later, reduction boxes were used for the Bus for
ground clearance and to allow the tiny 25hp engine to push it along with
heavy loads. The final appearance of reduction boxes was on the 1971 and
1972 Thing (not sold in the U.S. for those years).
