ya- never got the thermostatic thing on an air cleaner figured out, though I 
think it is a nice idea.  the way i understand it, when the thermostat is 
closed, the air cleaner pulls air ONLY from the pre heat system.   This is 
only on aircleaners with he 2" diameter preheat tube (68 and late).  2" 
diameter in cross section is 4 times that of the 1"  dia. tube.  think it 
would not be good (air starvation) to have  this set up on a air cleaner with 
a 1" pre heat.  anyway, if it is sucking in the warm air (do you have the 
pick up pipe, think that helps, but should work anyway) it should de ice the 
carb.  I'd try staring it up, watching it, and when it starts icing let it 
run at about 2K rpms and watch the system.   does the air cleaner etc start 
warming up?   you may need to get the accessary firewood chunk to lean on the 
gas pedal.   It does de ice faster/better above a normal idle speed.

The thermostat theoretically keeps the flaps nearly all the way closed when 
the therm is closed, and the engine warms up faster (& more completely) that 
way.  it helps in winter as well as in summer.   I have had to waller out the 
hole on the bracket or not screw the thermostat onto the rod as much to 
adjust the thermostat to close stuff all the way.  the bit on the flaps where 
the rod goes on gets mushed up, so if you have that fan housing off, check 
that out .  Eric says you should fit a popsicle stick under the curve (not 
the thin way, but the wider way).  Factory spec = popsicle stick width.  :)   
Think I have some extra flap bits, if you are going to be at spring meet I 
could bring them.

The mystery motor in my 65 had that extra hole in the rear tin, and a metal 
tube (small) from the intake manifold going through that hole, no clue what 
it is, I swapped out the tin and blocked off the hole in the manifold.  think 
the guy said it was from a 73 or 74 bug.
