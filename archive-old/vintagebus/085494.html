I had a similar problem.  An adapter you need. Assuming you are not in a 
Metric Zone. Buy a piece of 5/16 square key stock at your hardware store. 
Most likely in the specialty bins.  You may want to cut a 1/8 or 1/4 inch 
off of your existing shank (you will need to decide if this is necessary or 
not). If need be, drill the hole a little deeper, with a 5/32" drill, and 
rethread using either an M5 or a 10-32 tap. The two are so close that they 
are virtually interchangeable for this purpose. Cut off the desired length 
of key stock and drill a clearance hole, 7/32", thru the center. Best done 
on a drill press or at a machine shop. Use a slightly longer screw, again 
either M5 or 10-32, and assemble your handles. The adapter will slight into 
the square hole and into the inside handle.

I did this once with a piece of brass without cutting my old handle.
