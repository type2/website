  Using a late bug master will work but give you real spongy brakes. They
will lock them up and stop but I really don't feel safe with them being so
spongy.          We did a long term test in a 64 21 window and the master
(bug) failed in 6 months. We placed the blame on having to press on the
brakes so far to stop the bus. When the 67 bus masters came around we
changed to that right away. 
  When people complain about the price of the duel circuit 67 only master I
always cringe, I have always felt my life is worth more then doing the
improper late bus conversion or using a bug and is worth enough to do it
right.
  The 67 masters are readily available and at a decent price now and should
be around for years to come. I am trying to locate kits for them now.
  They are a lot of other parts you can cheapen out on that doesn't
sacrifice the safety of your bus. 
  
Ronnie

ps. On the Porsche 64/65 disc brake cars we would upgrade to a early 911/912
master to get the duel circuit system (if I remember we also tried the 924 S
master, those I think are the same as a Audi 5000), straight bolt in
application, just a thought for those of you upgrading your bus to 4 wheel
disc.
