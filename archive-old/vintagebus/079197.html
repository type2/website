Coincidentally, there is a thread today on the Type 2 list about a new (to
the U.S.) shock that I highly recommend, from AlKo Suspension.  In a
nutshell these are Boges only better. They're oil filled like the Boges, so
they have a smoother ride than KYB's (which are sometimes criticized as
being a bit harsh). But they're noticably beefier than the Boges, and are
European made (and T.U.V. approved) whereas the Boges are now all Mexican
made.  These are probably the closest thing to real German O.E. shocks that
you can get today. I initially started selling them a couple of years ago
for the Vanagon, based on the recommendation of Just Kampers in the U.K.,
who had received glowing reports on them from their customers. After
receiving similar feedback from my own customers, I added the Bus
applications, and they have been received equally well. To quote from the
today's Type 2 List thread ...

> agree that the ride is probably what the factory had originally
> intended for the bus. What a difference in "Drive-ability".

and...
