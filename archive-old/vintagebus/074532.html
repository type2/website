I live in the pacific northwest I share your issues with sogginess.   In Oly 
we get about 60 inches a year.  To fix the problem of sopping dash tray,  
which leads to the problem of flintstone floors, you must reseal the windows.  

So remove the glass and old seals, 

then clean up the windsheild area so it is smooth.  This may involve welding, 
or merely wire wheeling off old rust, and re primering, spot putty, paint to 
get a new smooth painted surface.   a few vehicles just need cleaning of the 
metal with a soapy cloth, but those tend to be desert buses.  You can't tell 
till you take the windows out, though.

I like the polish the windsheilds, which helps get rid of the blurs from road 
grime.  use engine oil as a lube when putting the new seals on the glass.  I 
use a thin stripe of roofing tar on the lower windsheild edge and corners of 
the windsheild ledge. Cleans up with rags and WD-40.  Plan a weekend to give 
time for paint to dry.    Next go round and do the rest of the seals.    Get the 
heat working as well as possible for drying out the sogginess.

Put the windsheilds in my 65 westy 5 years ago and the stuff on the dash tray 
stays dry.  More details are in archives.

wiggling connections helps a bunch.  there may be corrosion wher ethe bulb 
holder plugs into the speedo.  Or  where the speedo is held on.  So try loosening
