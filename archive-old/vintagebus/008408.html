Hi Chuck,

    You didn't say how much experience you have as a weldor, but just
remember the old saying, "A weldor is only as good as his welder".
    With that said, did you look a Lincoln TIG 175? I have had one in our
shop for about a year and a half now, and I just love it. It works off of
common 220 current (like your home a/c or drier). It will weld any thing you
are likely to come up against, from a piece of 1/2 plate to two razor blades
edge to edge (the machine will, I haven't tried it yet). In, cast iron,
chrome molly, stainless, aluminum, mild steel, etc. It is an A/C - D/C,
stick, or TIG combination machine. It is the cleanest weld you will ever
see, you could weld in a dress suit (not recomended) if you had to. With the
foot amp control you can spot sheet metal with little or no warpage!
    Its not a Wire feed machine, so its not as fast as wire feed, and that
is my only disapointment, but the machine won't hold you back! You wont go
to weld someting one day and have to go borrow a machine. Its perfect for
sheet metal (such as body work), as well as welding Volkswagen case's,
heads, or almost any automotive, air craft, marine need, etc.
    I do have a Miller wire feed machine, I have had it for fifteen years, I
bought it used from a muffler shop that was closing down, and it was well
used then. It is a very good machine, fast, reliable, etc. and it makes a
perfect compliment to the TIG 175.
