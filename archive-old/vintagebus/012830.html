 just throwing my $0.02 in:)
 Years ago when I drove a big truck (89 International
 Eagle w/Range Rover 10 speed, 6V 92 Detroit engine)
 the only time you use the clutch is when taking off
 from a dead stop:) No matter if your bob-tailing or
 over-loaded. When trying to explain to folks how to
 to shift w/o using a clutch, tell em to listen to a
big truck. Shift _at_ low RPMs, diesel engines, as with
 VWs, create power _at_ a low RPMs, its one of the
easiest
 vehicules to "power shift".
  To this day I catch myself in the B*g power shifting
 without thinking about it, until i miss a gear:o At
 least ya aint gotta come to a complete stop in a VW
 to get another go at it:)
  Your right tho... every person that drives ANY
 standard trans auto needs to learn to power shift &
 do it correctly. In my opinion if done right it gives
 more life to your drivetrain, less wear on clutch
 componets, less jerking around of trans mounts, ect.
 People tell me "That'll wear out your trans in no
 time..." they dont believe me that a person can get
 well over a million miles out of a big truck trans
 if properly maintananced:) I dont do it in the B*g on
 purpose, just out of habit:)
