Bear in mind that in the early days of radial tires in the USA they ALL
ran tubes.  Michelin (and others) _required_ the use of a special "radial
tire tube" that they happily sold for 2 or 3 times the price of a
convential tube.

Lots of brand new cars are running tubes inside tubeless radials right
now.  Outside of extreme continuous high speed work there's no
significant downside to running tubes in tubeless radial tires.  Granted,
the unsprung weight will be very slightly increased but that's of no
practical concern on a Type 2.....
