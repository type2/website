According to the diagram above, there are two fuel things: the pump
and a fuel cutoff solenoid (sort of like the idle solenoid on
later model carburetors).  Early models have a brown hot wire to
the pump and a green hot wire to the solenoid that both come from
the same place.  Both pieces ground through their mounting.  The
later models have a green hot wire to the pump, a green hot wire
to the solenoid, and a brown ground wire for the pump.

Also, it looks like the fuel pump has the ability to turn itself off,
or something.  See the contact inside the pump on the wiring diagram.
Maybe this implements an "x minutes on, x minutes off" thing - on
the BA 6 wiring diagram at Thom's site, the fuel pump is actually
run by a cam off the combustion air blower, so it probably wouldn't
run all the time either.

I think it's likely that if you disconnect the wire at the pump that
doesn't go straight to ground, and power it, then the pump should run
if it's working.
