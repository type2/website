Hi Jason,

As I went through his whole topic a few weeks ago, I can give you an answer
:)

IMO, and in the opinion of others -- your best investment is the "type1
automatic starter".  From what I understand, this starter was never used in
the type2 -- but it is a great replacement.  It is "self supporting" and
does not need a bushing in the case.  It also seems to have a bigger motor
and I find it cranks harder.

Telephone your local parts store and tell get a price for a "starter for a
1973 automatic (auto-stick) bug".  If I remember correctly, I paid $47 and
$12 core for a rebuilt one (Exide).  This was at a flaps that offers a
lifetime replacement on the part as long as I use it only on the one
vehicle.

Anyway, if you want to go 12v on an old bus -- my recommendation.

If you want to see photos of starters, go to http://www.i405_dot_com/vw/stand/
<-- I took photos of my starters when I was trying to figure out 6v vs. 12v
vs. automatic.

Also check the archives at http://vw.i405_dot_com/ == you will find all you want
to know about starters.  Start with searches like "starter near bushing" and
other variations like "starter near auto*".
