A good idea!  Most of my tank-building info comes from aircraft
homebuilding sources, and they usually have an altimeter handy for use as
a low-pressure gauge.  You blow a 2000 foot altitude difference into the
tank and then watch the gauge over a period of hours.

Altimeters are perhaps a bit scarce among the VW croud, though!

The important point is that the pressure be kept low.  Anyone who has seen
the "crush the gallon can" science demo knows what havoc can be wrought
by a few PSI over a large surface area.  Quite a few perfect tanks have
been ruined by overzealous testing!
