A few tips:

  o   Separate each word by a space on body and subject searches, do not
use commas, special words, or other punctuation.
  o   Try a few variations of your search.  Enter "brakes rear" on the
[Body] field, and if that doesn't find what you want, try "brake rear
drum" or other variations (notice that "brakes" and "brake" are not the
same!)
  o   If you aren't finding what you want, try LESS words.  Try just
"rear drum" instead of "brake rear drum" -- too many words will restrict
the search too much.  Remember, there is no automatic word variation
matching, try both "rear drum" and "rear drums" -- the person who wrote
the message you want may have only used pleural!
  o   Once you get to a message you like, on the individual message,
there is a link to show all of the same messages with the same subject
(thread).  You can also see all the messages from the same author (as
long as they didn't change their e-mail address <grin>).

From most places in the search, you should be able to bookmark and/or
paste links into e-mail to get to the same place.  That was a primary
goal of the archives... so that you can post messages pointing to
individual message or a thread.

  ========= Example 1, Individual Message =======
Example 1:  Simple example, you can jump right to a specific message.  I
used the [Subject] field and put "Hooper Christmas".  I had the [Mailing
List] pull down on "Type2 (December 1996 - current)".    I then read the
message and pasted the link here...
http://www.i405_dot_com/vwarchive/sm.asp?ID=14108&ml=1

  ========= Example 2, Search Link =======
Example 2:  Here is a bookmark after a search.  In this case, I used the

Once the results came up, I pasted the link into this mail message.
http://www.i405_dot_com/vwarchive/default.asp?body=clip+brake&sub=&ml=1

  ========= Example 3, thread link =========
