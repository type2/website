Anyway, the swap area was proportionately tiny. But
poking around I came across some things I thought I'd
ask the list about....

I saw 2 diffrent kinds of deluxe coathooks. One kind
was sort of pointy on the ends, and another more
rounded on the ends. Which is early and which is late?
What year did they switch?

Does the door/window post grabhandle mount under these
hooks, using the same screws?

I have a rear seat w/ original carmael brown
upholstery. That places it in the '58 - 61 standard
bus interior range. I want to sell it. What years will
it fit? It was in my '67, so I figure it will go that
late, but how early will it go? TIP: the driver's side
leg is bent in (towards bus center) at the bottom back
before it rises up in front of the
hump/firewall/slope/thingy. What;s up with that?  I
need to know so I can tell potential buyers what year
will go. (anyone want it? $200)

also: SPLASH PAN WARNING!!! I picked up a set of
splash pans, only to find out they were '68 and not
split bus (thanks Wolfsburg West booth guy). Luckily
the swap seller was cool enough to take them back. But
check out your pans before you buy them. On the
correct (split) ones, there is a notch in them about
3+ inches before the inner end (where the overrider
goes) and the mounting bracket nearest the end of the
bumper has a nut welded in it. The later one has an
oblongated hole and a carriage bolt where it bolts to
the bumper near its end. Make sense? Just beware if
you are a stickler for correctness. The late ones will
fit with some mods. (further reading, see: vintage
nazis)
