Subject: Re: [VB] Run the thermostat or not?

Running the thermostat and flaps does a few things for you.
One thing, is they warm up your engine a LOT quicker,than without.  Anytime
your engine is not running at operating temperature, there is accelerated
wear.

The flaps, when in place, cool the engine better/properly.  Without the
flaps in place, the air coming from the shroud is going to find the easiest
way past the motor.  It's easy to see, that the cooling air is going to be
going past the cylinders, more than the heads.  With the flaps in place,
they divert  force air to go through the head, where it is needed.

But of course, a ton of people run without them, and of course they say they
run fine without them.  And that's true.  There is no seat of the pants
difference.  They just reduce wear, and help the motor last longer.
