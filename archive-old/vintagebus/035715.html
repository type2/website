Wow! I just returned from a week's long business trip where I hadn't been able
to read my P-MAIL. What a brain dump! I felt that reading complete threads at
one time gave me quite a different perspective than reading them as they are
posted. At any rate I wanted to first thank everyone that responded to my
request for any VW happenings with the show in Pleasanton on the 15th. Jim and
I will be there and I hope that I run into some of you there. Also, Gary
LaVere do you plan on setting up a booth there? I want to get that hatch
repair kit we talked about. If not I will hunt you down at your shop.

FYI. The Bus Depot www.busdepot_dot_com is just a stones through from my house. I
stopped in to check them out and they are good people. Jim and I ordered some
brake parts from them and I snaged two of the early bus tail-light assemblies.
Good quality and good price!

Well renovations have begun on our "new" 64 camper. Jim and I have begun
pulling out the interior and fortunately have found very few surprises. Anyone
who feels that husbands and wives should share a hobby have obviously never
attempted to restore a splitty with his or her significant other. The first
battle is over, Jim has won! I was in favor of belt sanding the cabinets down
to bare wood and doing a showroom fresh resto. Jim insisted on a light sand to
remove watermarks and a fresh coat of varnish. I felt that the resto shouldn't
have "beat up" cabinets Jim thought a little age was more dignified. The
argument ended when he reminded me that I am always dragging him into antique
shops to buy stuff for the house so why should the interior of the camper be
any different. I had future visions of us driving PAST interesting looking
antique shops in the fully restored camper while Jim reminded me that I didn't
"like old beat up things" I shut up and must admit that the cabinets are
turning out nicely. Our next challenge is to find a good stain color. I
imagine that the interior was originally natural and has darkened with age.
Since we are going with the darkened finish, we will need to match it for the
few doors that have to be reproduced and the headliner.

As far as hardware and plastic parts, thanks for the lead on the Outwater
plastics company (www.outwater_dot_com), I sent away for it and feel that it
should be a standard volume for any bus enthusiast.

I am considering getting Eastwood's (www.eastwoodco_dot_com) brass plating kit to
replate my cabinet hinges has anyone done this? Please advise.

I would also like the lists opinion on another matter. Our 64 has grey
pressboard in the front cabin. The camper section is all birch except for the
two door panels. The left has the fold down shelf and the right has the toilet
cabinet. the panels here are white pressboard. Jim thinks it would look better
with birch plywood like the rest of the camper interior. I agree but am afraid
that if we ever show the camper we will be slammed by the judges for not
restoring the white pressboard. We have a 63 camper catalog that shows these
panels as birch and not white for that year. What do you all think?

I would also like to comment on a couple of  threads.

1) lowered, chopped, frenched etc. Personally I would never do it! I like the
original automobile too much. But I must admit I do admire the Cal looks,
customs and retro customs that I see in Hot VW sometimes. I would like to
suggest a volunteer guideline based on the the Hippocratic Oath. "Above all do
no harm" If you go out and find a factory mint early VW in a California Barn?

Crystal Chandeler. (Sounds like what Martha Stewart would own if she were into
VWs)!
