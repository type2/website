If you can afford it, I would try the Tie-Coat stuff.  I used the Dupont
Prime-n-Seal, and although I have yet to test it on the road for durability,
I have two comments.  That stuff is NASTY!  You MUST wear supplied air while
using it, it seems to be concentrated evil.  Also, you have to spray your
topcoat within 12 hours of spraying the primer/sealer, else you've got to
scuff it before topcoating.  That can be an issue, but not too bad of one.

I am a sucker for good advertizing, and if the Tie-Coat/POR-15 combination
is as tough as they say it is, (or even half), then I would go with
that...You'd "never" have to go below your topcoat to refinish or repair!
"Impervious to rust" and all that....

Speaking of rust, anyone got an opinion on whether to use "One Step" or Rust
Mort in rockers, doglegs, gates, etc. to kill and prevent more rust?  I'm
gonna get one of those wands to squirt the stuff in with...."One Step" is
about $100/gallon.....
