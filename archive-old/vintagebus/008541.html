An earlier version of the Washington State law on Historic Vehicle plates
specifically restricted vehicle use to "shows, exhibitions, and parades".
Some jurisdictions were pretty lenient on this restriction, but others
[including the Wash. State Patrol] held to a very strict interpretation.

On the flip side, however, was the specification that ANY motor vehicle 25
years of age or older could qualify.  This resulted, for a time, in some
pretty peculiar combinations of vehicle and Historic Vehicle plates.
