Well, I'll throw in my two cents since I have been rebuilding engines for
twenty five years and have had only two brought back in all that time, both
for defective components.

I generally run my engines for two or three hundred miles with standard
non-detergent oil, pull it, retorque, and put in 30 weight detergent oil for
the rest of its life. Depending on the application, I can usually get a
reliable 65 to 80K out of them, but I make them work for it. My old 65 DC
hauled garbage, gravel, trees, you name it. My 71 West failure took us
camping all summer long every year and not on the paved roads either! My
current project 67 SC hauls as much or more than the 65 DC and I hardly ever
give it a rest since it is my daily driver. But, as with all my motors, I
pulled it around 400, retorqued, and run 30 weight detergent. It runs what I
would consider normal temperatures, but I don't have a gage on anything, I
know what a hot motor feels and smells like!

Good luck
