The 66 standard my then bf was driving wouldn't turn the front wheels one
winter morning.  The water in the steering box had frozen.  Okay, that bus
had sat in the low part of a field the previous winter, so it was sort of
in
a seasonal pond.

I didn't change the steering box oil in my 65 till I had had it a couple
years.  Good God! There was water in it!.  Never sat in a soggy pond AFAIK.

I think draining the oil is not a bad idea, actually.  Ya, you undo the 4
bolts on the bottom of the box.   I'd spray some lube through it to help
wash
all those crusty things out of it.  Let it drip a while.  Clean up the thin
metal shims real well.  I glue them back on with aviation sealant, same goop
I use for engines.

I heard mixing  grease and gear oil is good goo, and you can squirt it in
with a plastic squeeze tube.  Like a ketchup container, with the pointy top.
I'd filled mine with straight 90 wt, and still have a drip years later, so
next time it's the mix.
