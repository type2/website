Here is my $0.02...

Take a look at this diagram from the 1958 VW Spare Parts List Type 2:

http://www.mindspring_dot_com/~deasterw/bastard/bac.jpg

This shows the 261 129 611B Air cleaner (oil bath, suspended)

I have 2 of these (one of which came from Neil Pickett).  They have the
part number stamped in them as well as "LB 610" and the manufacturer name
"KNECHT" stamped within a diamond.  The lower bowl for the oil bath clamps
to the upper lid of the oil bath via 3 short cam-clips.  The air enters
the oil bath via vents in the side of the lower oil bath bowl.  The lower
oil bath bowl is also stamped with "Olstand".

Now take a look at this diagram from the 1959 VW Transporter Workshop
Manual printed in 5.59 that features (guess what?) the bastard 40hp
engine:

http://www.mindspring_dot_com/~deasterw/bastard/bac2.jpg

The oil bath pictured appears to be the exact same air cleaner that is in
the 1958 Spare Parts List.

I have 2 other air cleaners in the shop, and I think they are both 40hp
air cleaners.

On one 261 129 613 is stamped as well as "LB 625/1" and the manufacturer
name "KNECHT" stamped within a diamond.  This one has a snout for the air
intake into the lower bowl & the lower bowl attaches to the upper tube via
3 long cam-clips, sandwiching the lid & filter element in between.

On the other 261 129 613A is stamped as well as the manufacturer name
"Mann".  It is almost identical the the last one otherwise.

I have another 36hp I could look at in my '57 Kombi for another data
point if we have not already beaten this one to death...
