I agree with ya.  Even with NOS parts, there are sometimes fit
issues; why?  Because the dies to make the stampings wore over time,
so a piece made at one point in time would be slightly different than a
piece made two years later.  Look at some of the stampings that are
identical for a large range of buses, and if you have the luck of
ever having used such a piece, you might see a fitment issue.  I installed
an NOS 1/4 panel on my '54, and definitely saw spots where it wasn't
*perfect*, but I had a Schofield panel to compare it against, and it
was defintiely better.

OTOH, the repro pieces are usually patterned after a very good
used or NOS piece, *but* a) the means of fabricating them are totally
different; b) there's errors introduced by the "human factor" in
reproducing them.  

The more complex the part (ie doglegs) the harder it is to repop.

The pressed used to make the original parts were *HUGE* - check out the
History Channel segment on VW's some time, where they show body sections 
being stamped.  The guy puts a big sheet of metal on the press, and *wham* -
a second later it's a bug 1/4 panel.  The repro guys don't have the same
machinery available to them.
