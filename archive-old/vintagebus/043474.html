  it with an 'e'

Give the online ones a shot.  Seriously.  In these latter days when you
can buy 1 GHz processors at the local computer store, automatic translation
works fairly well.  Yes, they will miss some of the idiomatic expressions.
If you have any clue about what the source document is about, you can
salvage about 75% of these yourself by looking at the context.  Write
down the idioms it misses and look them up in a good German <-> English
dictionary at the library.

If you decide that only a human will do, try your nearest college or
university with a German language program...either ask the office or
just put a couple of notes on the bulletin boards in the building...
you can probably get a student or a professor to do it for beer money.
:)

Good luck!
