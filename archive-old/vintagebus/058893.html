On another note, the later master conversion was dreamed up as a replacement
for the then UNAVAILABLE 67 only master (thus using the 72 and later so the
reservoir would fit) and not as a duel circuit replacement for any bus, it
just progressed that way.

  On another, another note. When I did my friends 64 deluxe, we tried many
different masters on it trying to find a safe alternative to the 67 only.
This included 3 different type 1 masters. A 64 Porsche master and the 72 and
later bus, we even installed the antilock piston kits and proportioning
valves to try to get good smooth even braking. Our test track consisted of
hauling ass up and down the alley behind my shop and locking up the brakes.
This how we discovered what really happens. The antilock pistons did work
with the 72 master but had a slight spongy feeling in a panic. The 68 and up
bug worked but no matter how we adjusted and bleed the brakes the pedal was
real soft and with the antilock kit would go to the floor. After a few days
of doing this we installed a 67 only master ($250.00 dollars NOS) at that
time and had all braking problems solved.
