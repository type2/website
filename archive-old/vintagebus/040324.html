If the driver's side trailing arm comes
off you could try pulling on the passengers side
arm to see if you can pull it out with
torsion leaves attached.  You can't really bash too much
on the end of the torsion leaves (exposed if drivers side 
trailing arm comes off) cos you will probably 'spread' them and
them you'll never be able to pull them through
the beam (the fitting in the middle they pass through
is quite tight.  You could try gently to see how 'firm'
things feel......

But I don't think it will work.  The fact the passengers
side wont move suggests the trailing arm is surrounded
by crap and stuck to the inside of the beam.  You need a 
penetrant rather than grease. If the drivers arm comes off
you could put the beam on its end and pour penetrant
in .....might be problemetic now its full of grease.

You might have to drill a couple of holes closer to the stuck end
and and try and introduce some penetrant there.  You could
always pull the zerk (grease) fittings and try and squirt
some in through those holes.  Squirt penentrant in every night for 
a week ot two and then see what happens by gently tapping on the other end.
