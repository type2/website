Several people asked me where I got the idea that there was a standard way
of mounting heaters in the rear seat of a Crew/Double Cab.  Here are the
archive messages that pointed me to this...  I went back to the archives as
I was writing this message and actually came up with a great message, that
describes one person's install in a 1964 Double Cab!

 === How to install in Double Cab ===
Mark Chambers wrote in October 1996:
 http://www.i405_dot_com/vwarchive/sm.asp?ID=57183&ml=5
 === How to install in Double Cab ===

The question is... was this rear seat mounting something the factory ever
did?  I like the idea of keeping the left side of the engine bay free for a
second battery (to power the heater when camping).

Here is some further research on general installation.

Dean Aukee asked a year ago if anyone had seen a factory install this way.
From what I can tell, there were no (public) answers on the list.
 http://www.i405_dot_com/vwarchive/sm.asp?ID=69262&ml=5 

Thom talks about "stock" gas heater in a Split, but not specific to
Single/Double cabs:
 http://www.i405_dot_com/vwarchive/sm.asp?ID=30619&ml=5

Jim Bryant adds to Thom that having them mounted below the dash is something
he has seen:
 http://www.i405_dot_com/vwarchive/sm.asp?ID=30628&ml=5

Matt Henricks is the one who says that Crew Cabs have them mounted in the
rear seat, but no details of air ducting or fuel lines:
  http://www.i405_dot_com/vwarchive/sm.asp?ID=30703&ml=5

So that should tell you how I reached this idea...
