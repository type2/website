Contact a local kayaking business, the older the
better. It was common a few decades ago to copy a boat
to make a mold and make your own from the mold.

The newer kayaks are roto-molded thermosetting plastic
rather than fiberglass, so you want to find a shop
that knows and understands fiberglass. Some of the
ocean, surf, and scuba support kayaks are still
fiberglass. 

Perhaps check with a kayaking club, there may be some
folks that used to be involved with making homemade
kayaks who might be interested in such a project.

Newer materials (carbon fiber) could result in a
lighter and stronger boat than the original!

Since you are in the SF bay area, at least one older
kayak shop should be in the area.
