Well, if you've checked the timing, the battery, points, plugs, valves,
distributor cap, rotor, wires, ignition coil (by the color of the spark, a
white/blue spark is great, a yellow is kinda weak), and that you're getting
fuel (get one of those temporary semi-clear fuel filter, and if it stays
full, or mostly full while running, you're probably getting good amounts of
fuel).  I would the do a compression check, and if you have the means, a
leakdown test, and see what those numbers are.  As our little engines get up
in the years, the rings tend to get weak, and the valves don't seal quite
like they should, we lose power, and it could cause harder starts.

Charles
