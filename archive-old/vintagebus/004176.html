Ok, so it's been six months...but the scanning is complete.  749 pages all
online for the world to see.  The SO-42 parts list supplied by Dave
Easterwood is also online, oh, three or four months after he "loaned" it to
me.  :-)  I'm afraid that upgrading the motherboard in this machine made the
scanner decide not to work with it.  I eventually installed Win98 on another
partition on my Linux box and did the scanning using that machine...that was
yesterday :-)  Here's the addy:

http://home.cwru_dot_edu/~agw4/docs.htm

are now hosted at members.nbci_dot_com, not members.xoom_dot_com.  Did anybody else

And, hello list, it's been a while :-)  The reason being that I've been busy
with school, and also no longer driving buses :-(  I pulled my Kombi off the
road after nearly running beneath a tractor trailer, trying to stop going
downhill.  I decided at that point that in the interests of my own safety I
shouldn't be driving until I could afford to fix up the Westy.  I'm now
driving a...a...Toyota (cough)...a non-air-cooled VW for the first time

unit in the Kombi.  Also the engine.  Probably several electrical
components...who knows, the camper hasn't touched the road since 1979, a
year before I came into the world.

I recall some interest in the fact that my Westy has a full belly pan.  I do
not have the M codes handy, but I did look them up.  It has the one for a
belly pan.  Also has the one for locking ignition, chrome hubcaps, and a gas
heater.  I've still got the locking ignition, and while cleaning out the bus
I actually found the bakelite plastic cap that goes over the electrical
connections -- perfect condition, not even any scratches.  Cool.  Funny
thing is, when I found it I just tossed it out of the bus since I had no
idea what it was...realized what it was...and shouted excitedly at my friend
to grab it.  I think I scared him a bit!

An interesting thing about the documentation page...people from four
