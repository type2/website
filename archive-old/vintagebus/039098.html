I know exactly how you feel. When I built my first Must_at_ng, a 66, I drove it
all over. One day I went around a corner at about 20mph and I saw a sliver
streak rolling away from my car(the wheel). At the same time the brake pedal
went to the floor and the steering locked up. I spun around a few times and
landed on a center island. Turns out the spindle just snapped. bearings were
fine when I checked them in the wheel(which was about 1.4 mile away) and it
was cool to the touch so it wasn't heat. Mustang guru I showed it to guessed
at a stress crack from age. I got the car fixed but was scared to drive it
for a while. When something happens like that with no warning or way to
prepare it freaks you out. You'll get over it but it will be in the back of
your mind for a while. VW content= I had a similar thing with my 67 ghia
vert. While towing it I lost a wheel. That wheel managed to make a curve
onto an on ramp for a highway and rolled down the road for a long time. Can
you imagine driving down a highway in the right lane and having to yield to
a wheel trying to merge into traffic? That though made me laugh as I ran
across 3 lanes in rush our to get the wheel off the divider.
