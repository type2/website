Subject: Realign Dizzy Drive Pinion

Hi, all,
I keep reading about distributor drive pinions being out of alignment.
It looks like everyone _lives_ with it. Maybe I missed the post, but has
anyone proposed pulling the pinion and taking another shot at it?
Forgive me if this is a repeat.

I build engines so infrequently that I expect to take a couple stabs at
the drive pinion before I get it right. It's not hard to pull it back
out and try again, especially if the engine is still out of the car.

I start two narrow, straight bladed screw drivers. Insert into the dizzy
drive slot, and then move the handles past each other so that they're
crossed in an "X". Apply pressure to the sides of the drive slot and
pull the drive pinion out. Once you get a feel for it, it is a piece of
cake.

Look down the hole to make sure the two washers at the bottom of the
hole are still aligned with the pinion... probably need a flashlight. If
they were coated with grease when they were installed they shouldn't
have moved. Use a screwdriver (non magnetized) to realign them if need
be. Make sure they don't get lost in the engine, or this will turn into
a big job (can you say teardown?).

You may be able to pull the pinion with a strong magnet, but I've never
done that. There is a bit of friction you would have to overcome, and
the magnetism might pick up the two washers.

I hope this is helpful to some.
