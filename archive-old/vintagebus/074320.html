While admitting that Lycoming and Lincoln engines have much closer tolerances
and higher compressions and require a little pampering before a heavy work
out, VW air cooled is not the same.

You will notice that when you pull your engine down, even after it has been
sitting a few weeks, that there is a fine wash of oil remaning in the
cylinders. Scouring a dry wall is a non issue with these engines unless you
assemble it dry, that's a different matter. Oil is pumped to all points of the
engine within one minute or so and the bypass valve ensures high pressure oil
to the bearings and rockers.

The way I learned it was to grow your engine from the head to the block to
ensure a tight seal and good compression. Ring break in was always remaining
up the motor to 2500 or so for a couple of minutes to break the glaze and seat
the rings. We did that with all of our Formula Vee engines and they ran
great.
