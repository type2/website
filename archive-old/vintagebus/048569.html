Get a can of starting fluid in a spray can.  Don't spray while you are
cranking in case of backfire, but shoot a little in the carb with the air
cleaner off, then crank.

If everything isn't in top notch shape (compression, ignition, carb, clean
fuel), any engine will have a tough time in the cold, but I've started VW's
in -30 before, no problem.

If I remember my history correct, wasn't cold weather usage a must when
designing the first VW's?  And from what I have read, the VW's on the
Russian front were at times the only things that would run in the cold.
