get out your test lamp.  turn headlight on halfway. open the engine lid.  Is 
there power going to the right taillight- it should be the white/red striped 
wire at the taillight.

if the wire gets power, but the bulb isnt getting it, take hold of the wire 
where it is puished on to the spade connector and wiggle it.   I have been 
having the same problem with my 61, it is corrosion, and what I need to do is 
take the light bulb out, and clean (lights off) the little thingys that 
contact the bulb from the light, and wher ethe bulb ground to the holder.

if the wire doesn't get power, you check the fuse box.  blown fuse? check 
your wiring diagram foir which fuse is the right rear taillight.   Check 
power at both sides of the fuse with taillight on.  twirling the old fuses 
can help, if there is corrosion building between the fuse and fusebox.    
This happens often in buses that sit, especially in wet climates.  and in 
bugs the fuse box is under the hood, I used to have to clean the fuse for the 
wipers every year, until I moved to washington where the rain is lighter

See- you just trace the power to find where it stops, and generally just use 
some fine sandpaper to clean the connections.   using a small screwdriver, 
and loosen and tightening the screwterminals will help the electrical 
connections.  But every connection has to work for the bulb to come on 
bright.  

Its not a bad idea to take some time and clean the electrical system from on 
end to another.  Including taking apart the horn button assembly, and 
cleaning that.  I like a nice loud horn.  BRRRRAAAAPPPPP!  That and a crusty 
bus with stuff hanging off the edges of the roof rack the people driving new 
cars give you lots of space on the freeway.  They dont drive up your 
tailpipe.  And let you merge (I use an assertive lane changing technique, as 
seen in caravan to New Brighton State Beach last weekend) when they see your 
bright lights.
