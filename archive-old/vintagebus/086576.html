But they don't come in 6 V.  :)  Also, while these would work fine for
general illumination and the indicators for turn, high beam, and oil
pressure, I wonder how well they would work for the generator warning
light.  The original lamp is specified at 1.2 W - this is 0.2 A _at_ 6 V
or 0.1 A _at_ 12 V.  An LED draws more like 0.02 A _at_ 12 V - about five
times less.  The reason you care is that the voltage regulator may be
using the current coming through the warning light to help start up the
charging system.  Installing a lamp that reduces this current greatly
may affect the charging system.  It probably won't totally fail to
charge, but it might cut in later and cut out sooner - no problem if
you're on the highway but not so good in the city.
