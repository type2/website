  All aftermarket door seals come longer then needed and have to be cut. WCM
add approx 2 inches on there seals and WW are pretty close to the correct
length, except they only come in one profile and there should be 2 to 4
different profiles depending on the year.
  WCM come in the 67 bus profiles (4 total). When questioned on this Lorenzo
stated that their seals were copied from original (NOS) back then they were
still available from the dealer and that is what the dealer would have used
in a door seal replacement in the early 70's.
  There is an overlap of the post seals at the beltline. I have seen these
cut (OG seals) 2 different ways at the belt line. 1 way was with the
connecting points (belt line) cut at 45 degree's with the upper seal over
lapping the lower seal. The other way I have seen it is with the overlap
notched square with the pieces fitting together.
  I have not found that in the last 6 or 7 years of seal shrinking (either
WCM or WW) so I wouldn't be to concerned on them shrinking up on you to
much. I always put the 2 post seals in first and then the seal that goes up
and over the door. That way I can trim the rear most first and cut the over
lap correctly on the front post. The bottom seal I install last with a
little crush to get it to press against the 2 post seal nice and tight.
  Depending on what seals you are using, they might require some trimming in
the thickness to get the doors to shut properly or even some shimming (WW
front door post seal) to seal properly.
  On the WW front post seal, you will need to shim approx. 1/8 to 1/4 inch
to get it to seal correctly. This can be accomplished by putting a thick
bead of black silicon and pressing the seal in to the correct height.
