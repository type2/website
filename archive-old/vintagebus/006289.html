> I'm posting this to the List as a whole because I've received so many
> individual inquiries.
>
> The bike rack I found at Target a few years ago is called:
>
> CONCORD III
> Premium 3-Bike Carrier System
>
> I bought it at TARGET, a national department store chain.  TARGET is not a
> membership store.  As I recall I paid around $35.00 to $40.00 for the
rack.
>
> The box copy says "Fits virtually all vehicles (including Mini Vans,
Wagons,
> 4x4s and RVs)."   When used on a sedan it allows access to the trunk.  It
also
> claims a "Unique new design...", and with that I agree.
>
> I'm no good at ASCII art, so I'll attempt to describe the design...
>
> The weight of the rack and it's cargo is borne almost entirely by the
> vehicle's bumper.  very stout straps with coated hooks attach to the rain
> gutter, front trunk edge, or some other suitable metal body part above or
> forward of the rack, depending on the vehicle.  Similar straps with coated
> hooks attach to the vehicle tow hooks, body, or bumper below/under the
bumper.
>
>
> The rack (which is built from round steel tubing) is a rectangular closed
loop
> when viewed from the rear.  From the side the loop is shaped rather like a
> shallow letter "L".  The base of the "L" is heavily padded with thick
neoprene
> foam.  This is the only portion of the rack itself that is actually in
contact
> with the vehicle.
>
> The rack is installed by resting the base on top of the bumper with the
top
> (on a van) straps hanging loosely from the rain gutter or hatch top edge.
The
> lower straps are hooked to the frame, tow hooks, etc. and tightened with
> cam-lock buckles.  The aim is to have the upright portion of the rack
angled
> back around 20 degrees with the top straps tight and the bottom straps
very
> tight.  The final step is to draw the upright portion back to vertical by
> further tightening the upper/forward straps.  The design incorporates
special
> protective pads so the lower straps won't mark or chafe the bumper.
>
> This may sound cumbersome and complex, but in practice installation on the
> back of my Campmobile takes about a minute.  When installed on the front
of my
> van, the upper/forward (now actually "rearward") straps are anchored to
the
> front-mounted spare tire carrier.
>
> The bike support arms are attached near the top of the carrier frame.
They
> have a wide range of angle adjustment, in small increments, and the
locking
> assembly is really stout.  The arms have an upturn at the ends for
increased
> security.  They include custom-sized bungee cords to lash the bikes to the
> rack.
>
> This rack folds flat for storage, and can be broken down further if space
is
> at a premium.  All adjustments, etc., including installation on the
vehicle,
> are done by hand.  No tools are needed.
>
> Someone put a lot of thought and some good engineering into this rack's
> design.  I'd cheerfully pay 2 or 3 times what it actually cost me to buy a
> replacement if that became necessary.
>
