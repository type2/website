I am looking for a prop rod for an engine lid for a 60 single cab. (Cargo
door prop rod is the same as engine lid)

When I had to remove my front shift rod coupler on my pickup, I tried
everything: heat, cold, oils, pickle forks, hammering. I finally gave up
trying to save the coupler and used a 9 inch grinder to slice a groove into
it from the front to back. This split the coupler open without damaging the
rods.

When putting it back together, I learned the plastic bushings (badminton
birdie looking things) can actually be changed with the rod installed and
the trans in place. If you remove the front rod, the rear rod can be slid
forward all the way to the front of the bus, which just allows the rear
bushing to come out of the tube. The bushing is split, with plastic dowel
molded into it, and it can be slipped over the shift rod. Don't force the
bushing back into the tube, or you will shear the plastic dowel off. The
open ends of the birdie face the center of the bus, so front bushing faces
rearward, and rear bushing faces front.
