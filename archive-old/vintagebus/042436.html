but if you use a 3 spring pressure plate you need the ring...  if you use
a pressure plate with all the fingers you cant use one with the ring, if
that makes any sense...  so if you have an old school 3 spring pressure
plate in there, you NEED the TO bearing with the ring on it or it wont be
able to disengage fully... the ring is a bearing surface and a spacer of
sorts, and is neccecary.

> It seems as if the cable isn't being pulled far enough to disengage the

Dunno how kosher this is, but i was working on a guys bus where the clutch
tube had been crushed and a section replaced with a piece of pipe.  The
pipe was cut short, so the clutch cable was too long, and wouldnt actuate
the clutch fully.

We put a couple washers between the wingnut and the clutch lever arm to
get it to disengage fully.  I have since heard you can put those washers
between the bowden tube and some braket, but im not sure about that.

that will fix it in 30 seconds flat i bet, but may not solve the
problem...
