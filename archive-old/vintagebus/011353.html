I am loathe to disagree with experienced mechanics, but now and then
their wisdom doesn't ring true.

The torque on one end of a shaft is equal and opposite to the torque
on the other end.  In a static situation (such as a torque wrench) it
doesn't matter if the shaft flexes or not.

Extensions do add inaccuracy to the torque, as they add to the
opportunity to change the length of lever arms, input secondary
torques, etc.  If you must use extensions, be sure that they stay
straight, at right angles to the wrench, and that you don't apply any
twists to the wrench or extensions except for that through the handle.
