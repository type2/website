It can very easily be the linkage.  When I first got my Bus, 1st was OK,
2nd was OK, but when trying to go for 3rd, sometimes I got 3rd and
sometimes I got 1st again.  I was lying under the Bus one day waiting for
oil to drain and started idly poking at the accumulated crud under there.
In doing that, I noticed that the rear shift coupler bolts were safety-wired
to one another (instead of to the shaft) and that they were quite loose.
Tightening them up (and re-doing the safety wire) helped a lot.  Other
things to check include the front shift coupler and the plastic bushing
around the shaft right under the gear shift.  Taking out the gear shift
and greasing the ball-and-socket joint at its bottom end can also help.
You can also adjust the plate that goes between the gear shift and the
floor (it's got a rib on it for the reverse lockout) to change the way
your shifter works.  Also, look for beat up or split rubber blocks on
the transmission mounts.

You can replace the front coupler and bushing without pulling the
tranny, but if the rear coupler needs replacing I believe the tranny
has to come out.  You may have to pull the tranny out (or do most of
the steps for it, then just drop it down a bit) to replace the mounts.

Also try "shift coupler" as search words at
http://www.type2_dot_com/archives/search-vintagebus.html , and look at
section H-9 of your Bentley manual.
