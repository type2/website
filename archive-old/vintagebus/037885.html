Back in 93 I hitch hiked  and hopped freightt trains around the country for
a year.  Now during the east coast part of the journey, i remember being
picked up so much by buses that it always seemed like a guaranteed ride.
I'd be sitting under an overpass for hours on end just watching the cars
whiz buy and suddenly...  put put put put put... and away we go.  So much
that i would get overly excited about seeing them.  Now out here on the east
coast where im from I can count all the buses in my city on both my hands
and almost all the owners of them are throwback deadheads or newschool kids
waiting for the next phish tour.  On the west coast theres buses all over
the damn place!!!  :)  So, to relate the story together...  I hop of a
freight and land myself in Seattle.  I probably stayed theres for about a
month before it was time to head to portland.  Once out on the highway i put
my trusty thumb up and no more than a minute later i see the savior vw comin
on the horizon.  Im thinkin, Wow this is gonna be great...  nope.  I proceed
to sit on the side of the highway being passed by more buses in 6 hours than
i think Ive ever seen in my life on the east coast.  I felt betrayed.  I
finally did get a ride eventually by a nice woman in an old volvo.  Almost
got to the state line too.  I wish i had, cause not only was my next ride
NOT a bus again, but a police car to the station.  (Hitchhiking is very very
illegal in washington it turns out.)  As for the freak in the bus, he
probably wanted the nicest looking podium that exuded the most Peace Love,
happiness, Karma, and good will to men, to damn everyone to hell from.
alas, he too fell for it like i did. =)
