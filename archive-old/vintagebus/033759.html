Karl and I tried to do the above on a junkyard 66kombi-camper. Karl recently
found a great junkyard engine on half-price day that was blowing ~135
compression after we fixed the PO botched generator installation, loose
exhaust studs, missing "dime" in the carb, etc. It's a stock doghouse
singleport from a '71 bus with the weird left heater duct. 
So it was time to pull the rod-knock dual port from the bus. I crawl under
to put a socket on the upper left bolt that threads into the insert of the
AH case engine. Can't get the socket on. Arms wrapped around the oily
transmission. Karl tries and finds the tranny has the D-bolt hole with a hex
head bolt jammed in it. So I pulled the doghouse tin to find out whether it
still had the insert. Yup, and a nice 17mm nut behind that! Did they drill
out the insert and put the nut on to pull the bolt? No, it was threaded in
the insert. Bad-word-Bad-Word. We had to remove the left side heater box to
get a wrench on the jammed in bolt head. Finally pulled the engine after
hours of this in 100+ heat. Luckily we have a half barrel from the local
brew-pub on tap and that the PO's whereabouts were unknown.
