Hi volks, 
Good point!  All too true for any engine, tranny, or other major part that 
you have to have shipped to you.  
  All new engines are nice , and if you go that way Ronnie is probably the 
best way to get that.  But remember that these engines are being built by by 
assemblers in Mexico who probably are getting $.80 or less an hour so they 
may not do a good job.  Which is why we know of a few of these engines having 
prolems, including having the cam installed 180 degrees out!  While having a 
engine rebuilt by a small shop does not mean you will not have a problem with 
it, it means you should have much less of a chance of having problems due to 
good shops will inspect the parts, and take more time to assemble them 
correctly.  We have heard rumor that the new Mexican cranks are cast not 
forged.  Which unless the alloy process has hugely improved since the 1970s 
means cranks that might break more often.  Plus you can not order internal 
modifictaions to improve the engine, but have to dissassemble it, and install 
them when you get it.  Which of course will void any warrenty you had!
