take all the cylinders and pistons off, marking which is which.  Heating up 
the cylinder with a propane torch after removing the circlips (use circlip 
pliers or needlenose pliers) will help the pin slide out easily.  check for 
grooves in the cylinders.   Check fit of a used ring in a piston- Muir says 
how.   what is the gap in the ring in there?   how bad or good is it.

you should also pull of the rods  (mark them too- I use a hammer and punch- 
knock 1 dot for #1, two dots for #2, etc.  ) and look at the rod bearings.  
rod bearings are only about $10 a set (us) you should replace them at this 
time, the size is marked on the old bearings (they are like half rings) - you 
must get the right size.

how to decide whether just to replace rings and rod bearings, or more- well- 
are there grooves in the cylinders?   is there a noticable ridge from the 
swept area to the unswept area? The unswept area will have the hatch marks 
still- are there some left in the rest of the cylinder?  the chip may not bee 
a problem. You can re-use ps and cs, or just replace one, if same 
size/weight, but you may want to get a new set, which will also some with new 
rings, wrist pins, and circlips.



also- how is your endplay- with the FW still on and bolt tight, push pull the 
crank pully- it should only move a hair.   if it thunks you may want to 
rebuild the bottom end, but then you may be able to get away with resetting 
the endplay.   You should replace the main seal at this time too.  do you 
know how many miles the motor has?   How dirty/stained are the heads from the 
side the rockers go on?   Did any studs pull out when you took the head nuts 
off?
