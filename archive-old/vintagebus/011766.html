From what I see, you have two possible routes.  

If you get the crank and flywheel redrilled, then you can just bolt
everything back together and have whatevever quality engine existed
before the failure.  Since you didn't have it then, you are really
dealing with a "pig in a poke" here.

If you decide to replace the crank and flywheel, you will have to
replace all the bearings to match.  Since you're into the engine this
deep, it isn't much farther to a complete rebuild.

If you can afford it, the second route is the way to go.  You're
looking at a few hundred dollars difference, but you'll have a great
engine when you're done.
