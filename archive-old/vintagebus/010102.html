After losing 3 relatively new wheel cylinders in as many years I now carry a
handy little kit to seal off the brake line in situations like this.  It
fits any _female_ brake line connection.

To securely seal the fitting/connection you need a 3/16" steel ball bearing
and an M 10X1 bolt.  The ball bearing seats nicely in the taper inside the
fitting, and by tightening the bolt on top of it a large amount of pressure
is applied to the ball bearing/internal taper interface.  No sealer or joint
tape [Teflon, etc.] is needed.
It will not leak.

In a pinch a larger ball bearing could be used, but that would only allow a
few threads on the bolt inside the fitting.  My experimentation showed that
a ball bearing smaller than 3/16" _might_ be forced into the orifice in the
fitting, damaging the fitting.
