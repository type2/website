I've sprayed POR-15 with a gun...it cleans up easily with laquer thinner
(the economy pro stuff).  It did not need thinning to spray, but if you have
to thin it, Restomotive recommends "their" thinner....not sure why, but I
don't mess with chemicals enough to know what should work.  Yes you can sand
it, if you're a glutton for punishment!  It's better to spray on a mist coat
when the POR-15 is tacky.  This mist coat can be primer, or whatever you're
going to topcoat with (non-visible areas only, recommend), but hear this:
The mist coat should be very light, even, and dry.  I have noticed that when
I get it on too heavy, uneven, or wet, the POR-i5 cracks or bubbles.  Not
good.
