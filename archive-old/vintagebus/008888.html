Anyway, to put a little VW content into this message, I've been researching
ultralight aircraft that one can build.  One of them I've looked at is the
legal eagle ultralight at www_dot_usastores_dot_com/betterhalf.  It uses a Team
Minimax wood construction wing and for power a VW engine heavily modified
for ultralight use.  I ordered the plans and the video and it was really
neat watching and listening to this plane.

The engine is modified so that you are run on just the #2 and #4 cylinders.
The #3 and #1 spiggots are not utilized and on this particular design, the
case is not cut in half.  The carb is an updraft carb and the manifold runs
up through the oil sump area and into the front half of the engine case.
From there it splits and comes out each of the cylinder bores and into each
half-head.  The valve covers are sectioned as well as the head so you have
just a 2 cylinder VW engine.  The sound of this engine with it's chrome
plated brass sink drain exhausts is deafening! :-)  I personally have a big
problem with using an engine modified like this because I know the
weaknesses of a VW engine.  I would opt for the engine I already have - a
K440 with prop redrive unit.

Anyway, that's the latest.  The bug and bus sit under snow while I ride the

car that drives on it.  For those of you who might have an inkling of
wanting to fly for fun, the legal eagle is a great little plane.  You can
also check out the www.affordaplane_dot_com site for an interesting craft but it
hasn't been flight tested yet.
