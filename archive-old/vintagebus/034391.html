An associated problem as recently noted by Bob Hoover
is that the door drains via the hole in the top of the dogleg.
The inlet is a honkin great hole (well comparitively speaking)
while the drains are much smaller.  Thus 'big' stuff (flakes
of rust from the inside of the door, gumnuts, whatever) gets
into the dogleg (often blocking the drains) and can't
get out no matter what you do.

For the non-purist, it is possible to weld in a tube that
goes straight through the dog leg and 'connects' top the inlet
hole at the top.  Thus all the big stuff (and water) passes
straight through rather than accumulating in the dogleg.
Structurally, a welded tube does not compromise
integrity.  

A lot of work?  Not really and after a couple of dog leg replacements
it seems like a minimal amount of work......

Original?  Nope.
