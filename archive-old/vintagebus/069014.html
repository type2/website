On Mon, 10 Mar 2003, Curt Conklin wrote:

> While My Sweet Bus was up on the jacks, we replaced the tired old shocks.  I
> put in KGY gas shocks, KG5529 in the back.  Wow. really raised her and the
> rear end lifts on standing normal take off.  We have KG4521's for the front
> but havent installed them yet.  The rear wheels are really cambered in too.

Define "really cambered in."

Chances are you're just seeing normal rear wheel camber at no-load.  These
busses have single joint (swing axle) IRS rear suspensions and have a lot
of camber change through their travel.

Grab a few friends and fill your rear and middle seats.  Roll the bus a
bit to let the suspension settle.  Now compare the camber to your no-load
camber.  Look better?


> Uh, is this a bad mistake?  Will it wear the tires and is it unsafe?

It will wear the tires, yes.  But so does any driving. :)  The
seemingly-excessive positive camber doesn't put as much edge-wear on the
tires as one would expect.

> Fortunately we do have a '72 Westy that could use them, if we need to go back

vary.
