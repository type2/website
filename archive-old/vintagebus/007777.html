I have to disagree with the this,, assuming that the starter motor isn't
completely stuck and won't turn ,,, then a good , fully charged 6 V battery
should not drop below 4.5 volts for at least 1 minute at room temperature,,
now, those are my figures,, I am not sure of the exact data,,
But what I am trying to say, is  *I* think from what you have said,, the
Battery has no Reserve left..bad battery
A lead acid battery has approx 2.4 volts per cell..  that is why a 12 volt
battery is fully charged at 13.8 - 14.4 volts
A 6 volt is the same..2.4v/cell .... it should be 6.9 to 7.2 volts... and
load tested it will not fall much below 5 volts
Road to a solution......
Try and get a charger that has a BOOST output ,, and connect it to the
battery in the Bus,,, and then hit the Key!
If it turns over, it proves the battery has no uumph left in it.
barring that....,, boost the car with a 12 volt battery....YMMV,
If it turns over real fast,, then you know the battery is bad!
