  What I was taught by the panel beater GERU"S here in town (they build
complete bodies by hand) is when you are fitting a panel to butt weld is to
leave little tabs every 10 or so inches (really depends on the size of the
panel), bend them down at a 90 degree angle then bend them back 90 degrees
so the tab will fit under the piece you are connecting it to. Use some
kleeco's to attach the tabs and hold the metal in place. After making the
welds you use your cut off wheel to it the tabs and continue welding. 
  Anther trick I learned from an old school body guy was to take a strip of
brass or copper sheet (brass works better)kleeco it to the bottom of what
you are going to weld (will also hold the metal in place) and do your butt
welds. The brass acts like a heat sink and welds won't stick to the brass.
Also you really need to hammer the welds as you go, this will flatten the
weld and help shrink it back to shape.
  I have a set of photos of wheel well openings being installed. When he was
finished NO GRINDING was necessary, a good metal file took off any excess
weld and the weld was undetectable.
  I personally have lost the touch, but I do use .023  coated wire and stich
weld (not constant weld) sheet metal, with the biggest problem being the
BRAZILIAN CRAP
metal that is available, thin, inconsistent and just a PITA to weld.

ronnie
