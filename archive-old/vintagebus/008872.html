I'm on the list (Aircooled.Net Inc.).

If it's "OK" to e-mail the lists, I'm all over it. I run a VW parts company
myself (Aircooled.Net Inc.)

However, I am the guy that bitched about getting SPAMMED; we have enough of
that already.

Perhaps the lists can provide a forum for list members to be added to
mailing lists where they have invited solicitations from VW parts vendors,
rather than having them forced on them (the argument of "just hit the delete
key is #(&#(^&.

This is the whole issue of SPAM, and why it pisses people off. These lists
are information forums, not a place to get free and easy commercial
advertising, IMO. If it becomes known that we (the commercial
establishements) can freely e-mail the list members, the lists will
eventually become virtually useless as more and more small businesses (I am
one, right now) use them to try to "catch up", and the "big guys" use them
to pepper the list members with "deals".

Now, on the other hand, I am a VERY active participant of the RAMVA Usenet
group. I think the solicitation and information is much more tolerated by
CONTRIBUTING members of the lists or NGs, since they are members of the
"family" and not just coming in for a quick SPAM job, never to be heard from
again until the next one. If Wolfsden is an active contributor, then I may
have crossed the line. But, from the look of the list addresses, this wasn't
limited to one small group.

Now, I know this issue is going to stir up a lot of controversy, but it's
better to err on the side of conservative marketing, IMO.
