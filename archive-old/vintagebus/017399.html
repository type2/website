I'd be interested too.  Nobody seems to know.  Best
bet is to weld in an oxygen sensor in your exhaust and
read it out, I guess.  I did some reading in Bentley
tonight, and read about a cool altitude compensator
that used to exist.  It replaced the carb main jet and
consisted of some bellows attached to a jet such that
the mixture would automatically get leaner at high
altitudes.  I'd sure like to find one of those!  The  
book says they're very specific to carb model, main
size, etc.  Then too, I remember that JC Whitney used
to sell an adjustable main for solex carbs.  A big
brass knob on the side of the carb;  set it how you
like it.  That could be an answer.

I know my '67 bus tends to run too rich with the
34-PICT-3.  Although flat-landers will tell you that
that carb is too lean across the board.  My motor also
failed emissions, with the 34-pict-3 and 009; 
although I tend blame the cam (mild, but not stock). 
Mine failed for excess hydrocarbons.
