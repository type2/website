I don't think SAE bolts have decimal points in their grading system.  It's
just whole numbers, like grade 2, grade 5, or grade 8.  Grade 5 bolts
have three lines on the head and grade 8 bolts have six.

Metric bolt grades do have decimal points: 8.8, 9.8, and 10.9 are common
on cars.  Sounds like you need grade 10.9 metric bolts.

Trivia: "The first number represents ~ 1/100 of the minimum tensile
strength in N/mm**2, while the second number is 10 times the ratio
of the minimum yield point (or 0.2% yield strength) to the minimum
tensile strength."  (Bosch Automotive Handbook, 4th edition)
