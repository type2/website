I can finally write about this.

I met him 3 years ago at High Country Bus Fest.  He was in the company =
of 2 very lovely ladies but he caught my eye even more than they did.  I =
remember thinking 'Wow - he's beautiful'.  I tried to get to know him =
but he remained aloof, preferring the company of others.  I met him at =
several other campouts, always with the same 2 pretty ladies.  He seemed =
wary of me for some reason, why I didn't know.

About a year ago I went to pick up a bus from one of the aforementioned =
pretty ladies and there he was again.  I left busless, but he came home =
with me.  He seemed uneasy during the ride, moving around and looking =
out the window.

We got to know each other slowly, as most good relationships start.  He =
stayed at the farm while I commuted back and forth from Charleston and =
work to my little postage stamp farm in Lenoir, NC.  Every Friday when I =
walked in the door there he was to greet me, always happy that I was =
home and never mind what kind of week he may have had.

If nothing else in the whole wide world, he liked to ride in the buses.  =
I'd ask if he wanted to go with me and he'd be at the bus before I was.  =
He tolerated the baywindow camper, but his true love (besides me) was =
the splittie.  He'd sit tall and proud, and I know other people would =
look at him riding in the bus with me and they'd marvel too.

Many of you east coast bussers may have seen him either with Andrea and =
Chris, or with my family and I.

You see, Rocky was much more than a dog, he was a great bus person and a =
great friend.  I have other dogs, but it just isn't the same.  I know =
there are other bus dogs out there and have had the honor of meeting =
many of them.

Driving the bus just isn't the same without him.
