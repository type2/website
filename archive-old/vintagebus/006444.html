    The seals... no biggie, IMHO... the last time I went to the bearing

produced that will work... I have faith in that.
    Baum Tools sells a jig for pinning (actually repinning) main bearing
saddles where the dowel pin holes have worn excessively. The kit comes with
dowel pins that are OS on half of their length... other half goes into the
back side of the bearing as usual. So surely we could pin the bearings..
(judges won't think to look in there). But some new bearings would have to
be made up or turned down from something.
    How odd to have the thrust bearing anywhere but up front in the case...

a dead DD series 60 we inspected had a main thrust bearing buried somewhere
in the engine. Didn't look like there was any provision for adjusting end
play w/o replacing the bearing...
