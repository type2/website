I expect many people with operating flaps and an engine that seems to take
forever to warm up and run properly blame the flaps and thermostat, when
their real problem is a blocked heat riser and/or an improperly adjusted
carburetor and choke.

The flaps may be assisting the engine in warming up quickly just as they
were designed to do.  But it takes a long time for the heat from the heads
and crankcase to migrate all the way to the carburetor mounting flange and
into the carb body.  A working heat riser assures that the carb and the main
mass of the engine warm up at the same time, just as they were designed to
do.
