Gene always told us, like warming a race car, to rev it up at odd RPM's for a
minute or two and you won't risk scouring dry cylinder walls and the heat will
build up quicker. The only problem is....
Nobody likes to hear their neighbor reving up his engine for what seems like
an eternity!

The electric choke on my center mount carb is fine for what it does by setting
the RPM up a little while the temp rises, but after the temp is up on the
choke and the idle drops down, the engine isn't really up to temp yet, not
until I'm a mile down the road. Also, at night I have a higher current draw
and the generator isn't charging at idle.

So..... I just fire it up and take off. So much for planning!
