Sure it isn't the throwout bearing? Otherwise, when I changed out the center

in the singlecab for a 3:88 Rancho, I got a thunky noise from the right

axle. With the bus up on jackstands I ran through the gears and when I hit

third it started jumping around and clunking. Put it on the ground and it

didn't do it at all. Figured it for the new fulcrum plates. I had set out

for .006 lash as in the Bentley, but got .004 on the right side which was

supposedly acceptable. After 100 miles I put it back on the stands prior to

dumping the oil, and it did not do it anymore. Probably took a minute of

driving to work the oil in good. Van Bondo and myself changed out his tranny

for a 3:88 Rancho here a week or so ago, and on the advice of AZ. Transaxle

went for .008 lash on the fulcrum plates. Got .006 on one side and .008 on

the other. No clunkies at all to my knowledge. Seems to like a little lash

to let the oil in. To tight and it'll bind when the axles aren't dead flat.

So, if your rear is high or low and the axle tubes are at an angle, it's

probably the fulcrum stuff getting to know each other. If it hasn't gone

away after a short bit, you better tear in there again.
