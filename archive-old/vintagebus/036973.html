Hi Jerry, 
It would help to know what dia. are your tires and what year is this bus?  If
you are running a 25" dia tire on both front and back you should get a speed
of 56MPH at 3,000 with the 3.875 R&P or 58MPH if you install 1964-67
suspension.  If you increase the size of the rear tires more you increase the
top speed, but keep the same RPMs.  For stock 4.125 R&P here are the results
one attains when increasing the rear tires over 25", but keeping the fronts at
25"dia..  For the 3.875 R&P double these increases in speed.  
1. 26.5" dia = 2MPH more.
2. 27" dia.= 3MPH more.  Unfortunitly at this time one can not use this size
radial in pre-1963 stock bus rear wheel wells.
3. 29" dia.= 6.4MPH more.
  Eric & Barb
PS What Todd says about trying to manufacture new reduction gear boxes is
exactly what we found out ourselfs.  Instead if you have a pre-1963 bus with
stock wheel wells you might want to look into a special made .79 fourth gear
from one of the tranny shops in S. Cal., but expect to spend about $200.00+
for just this one gear.  Otherwise you can get the right sized rear tires, the
3.875 R&P, 1964-67 suspension and be able to do about 63MPH at 3,000RPM in a
pre-1963 bus.   EEE&B
