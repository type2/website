>>In the book "how to hotrod volkswagen engines", the author
>mentions, in the blueprinting chapter, a method for cleaning
>up mating surfaces on the engine case, particulatly around
>studs and bolt holes, to make them flat.  The method is
>'stoneing' or 'stoneing down' the surface.
>
>My question is, what are these 'stones' and where can they
>be found?
>

It's a machinist thing. They are stones just like the ones used for
sharpening knives. For this purpose, it is helpful if they are not all worn
and uneven. A new, large 'bench' stone, which is about 3/4 x 2 x 6 inches,
is nice for this sort of thing. You can get a cheap one at Harbor Freight or
the like, with different grits on the opposite sides. Just make sure you
flush/blow out any grit after you finish.
