> This depends entirely on which years of parts you are putting together. If
> your oil holes are different size (as in early small passage case to the
> larger passages of the cooler adaptor), you need the reducing seals, which
> are green. They are not included in an engine seal kit. They look like the
> large diameter thinner red ones in the kit, but they are different
diameters
> on each side. Ordinarily it would go large size on the adaptor, small side
> on the engine. The seal you put in, did it have movement on one side or
the
> other? If so, it is wrong. It should slip easily but snugly into the holes
> on the block with no forcing, and it should not slide around when held up
to
> the holes on the adaptor. They should not be tight to get in either side,
as
> when you tighten it down you will close off the passages and blow
something
> up, be it cooler or seals. This is the most common error when dealing with
> doghouse coolers, pardon if it is not the case.
