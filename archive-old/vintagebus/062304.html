I've found that an old chuck of thick glass, (about an inch), or any
surfaced stone that thick or thicker with a piece of wet/dry sand paper
works good to resurface something. I use a piece of quartz glass and 420
grit wet paper for sanding down my carburetor shells flat. I have used
similar methods for larger pieces. If a person could come up with a broken
chunk of pool table slate, that works good also. Goes hand in hand with
lapping in valves or cylinders.
