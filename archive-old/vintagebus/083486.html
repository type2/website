Subject: Re: [VB] link pin zerk won't take grease

you can take the zerk off with a wrench (dunno what size), and poke a  pointy 
thing in it to clean it out, and pump grease through it.   see  what's 
clogging under the fitting...reinsert fitting.  It's probably not  the zerk, but the 
crusty hardened old grease in the link area
 Adjust your link pins (VW says to do this every 3K or is it 6K  miles)  Try 
pumping grease in while you loosen the link pin up a  bit.  Don't know how?... 
it's in your Idiot Book and your Bentley.   Real easy too.  While the bus is 
jacked up, check the spindles for wear ala  Muir.
 
if the links are stuck and don't want to turn after you loosened the pinch  
bolts, you need to take them apart and inspect them, possibly (probably ?)  
replace them.   Or since spares are hard to find, clean them up,  manual grease 
them with clean fresh grease,  and reinstall them if they  aren't rusted to 
bits with the needles falling out of the needle bearings.
 
grease your suspension every 3K miles (1200 if you drive on bad roads) to  
keep these parts in good shape.  
 
grease is good. 
 your bus loves grease. 
yum grease yum
