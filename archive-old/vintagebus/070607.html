I'm not going to assume there really is a 912E motor or some type of mega
power type 4 conversion. That aside, there is no 'motor' brace, unless we
want to count the transmission. The trans mounts to your torsion tube at the
front with a big mount, and onto a cross piece with two isolators.  The
front one toast should creat shifting problems, and life isn't good if the
rears are shot too in shift land.

Your engine mounts to the tran with two studs and bolts, and 2 large bolts
with two bolts into nuts, one nut is captured into the case in later stuff.
THERE ARE NO OTHER BRACES UNTIL BAY WINDOWS CAME OUT. These buses had
another brace across the pulley area of the engine and a splitty doesn't
have or need it, even if it is the rare 6-12v converted 1967... Not that
adding one would hurt, but it's not required to prevent a case from
'flexing'...

You also can't FIX a cracked or damaged anything by reinforcing it, that
won't undo any damage. I am uncertain as to how your case is damaged, I
suppose it is likely it is cracked about a mounting point/hole if forced to
guess. To 'fix' that, you need a new case, which bascially means rebuilding
the entire thing again, if it is to be done right at all.
