Well,

I know that buses in Australia (built from CKD kits)
were painted (or repainted) in country in our 'own'
local variants of VW colours (acrylic laquers made by an 
Australian paint company called Dulux).  Hell
of a lot of original beryl green splits here  (beryl
green was supposed to be a type 1 colour).

I've never seen overspray on other major parts so
it looks like empty shells were painted and then
built up.  I would assume that major components
(engines, tansaxles, beams) were imported and installed
as units rather than individually assembled, because
this is the way it was for beetles here.

Oz is sorta unique in that we did have manufacturing
set-up in country for beetles.  (really beetle bodies
 we weren't forging cranks or cases or anything
else - these were imported and installed as units).

I've found it difficult to get any info on the assembly of 
CKD buses in this country.  

Were buses built from CKD kits RHD?  Could you
get LHD CKD kits?  Seems that all CKD assembly went on
in RHD countries?
