There have been lots of questions directed my way about the use of silk
thread on the case halves.  If I'm not mistaken, this is from Bob Hoover's
Sermons.  I believe it was him that used a thin strand of silk thread on the
case halves with <non-silicone gasket sealer of your choice>.  I want to be
sure there are no leaks so I'm gonna try it out.  I've since been told that
dental floss works too but of course, I found out after I spent $4.75 for a
spool of thread about 20 minutes ago.  I have dental floss on hand. :-)

Another question people have had is "what do I mean by 'sealing nuts' on the
6 main engine studs?"  Well if you are familiar with the oil pump sealing
nuts, it's just like that only bigger.  These are dark (if not black)
looking nuts that have a red ring of a soft plastic material on the face
that goes towards the engine.  As they are torqued down, they form a seal
against any wonton oil seepage.  This T3 block had that style of nut on the
6 main studs so I was wondering if should use this kind of nut or not.  I
would feel better using them but if others have had success using nylocks,
well then I won't argue about it...
