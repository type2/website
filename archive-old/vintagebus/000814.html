  Interesting to hear about the reverse polarization problem.  When we burned 
up two generators one, after another we were not running voltage, or amperage 
gauges.  Just the idiot generator light, that went out after installing each. 
 Apon polarizing the third the generator did not burn out, and we never have 
the same problem since we always polarize each new/used unit we install for 
the last 19 years.  From what we understand is that if the generator sits 
around long enough the iron magnets loose thier strength, and the generator 
will run somewhat, but soon will over heat from not working properly, and 
burn the insulation.  Maybe someone else with more physics knowledge could 
explain it better.  What we do know is that Bosch the ignition parts supplier 
of not only almost every year of VW since WW2, but many, many other 
automakers vehicals from the factory all over the world insist on 
instructions to polarize thier generators.   Eric&Barb
