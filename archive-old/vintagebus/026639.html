    I'll add here that it is *very* important to get it cut & aligned properly.
The '66 Westy I hacked was done in a bad way (my fault)... and turned out to be
a big headache... hack begetting hack and so on.
    Luckily I had enough spare shifter stuff to get it roadworthy again...
    Even then I have to wonder if it has the right hockey stick in it. It was
exhibiting some of the same symptoms as Ellis's bus, less after a little
geometry-tweaking.
    Scat makes a little weld-on thingy that lets you adjust the length of the
shift rod and the angle between the socket up front and the set screw dimple in
the back... I have never used one of them, though... would require tranny
removal I think, to get all aligned properly for the weld.
    I am thinking of a shift-by-wire system... sensor box under the shifter,
different signals for each gear, built-in interlock system, 'lectric servo box
on nosecone... hm. something for the MEs to chew on. For the right price it
would eliminate the shift geometry problems of most vws, as well as the
midengine apps and so on... and it'd sell, too...
