Subject: Re: Bus Shifter Difference

>From: "Aaron" <veedubs_at_valkyrie_dot_net>
>Date: Wed, 8 Jan 2003 02:41:02 -0500
>
>Can someone please explain the difference between a '60-'65 Bus shifter
>and the later '66 and up versions.

Look at pages H-9 2 and H-9 3 of the '63-'67 Bentley.  Also note that
the "'60-'65" style actually persisted through the early 1966 models,
until approximately 15 September 1965.

>Does the earlier version extend further below the cab floor?

Yes.  The shift rod under the Bus is also supported differently.

>Might sound silly at first, but what would I need to do to get
>a '66 and up shifter to work on a '60 Bus.

You'd probably need to get the later-style front shift rod and weld
on the support for it under the Bus.

Note that the Bentley says "Parts of previous type will still be
supplied as replacements."  Usually this means you can't interchange
the old and the new.  If you can, it will usually have a note to that
effect along with "parts of previous type will no longer be supplied."
