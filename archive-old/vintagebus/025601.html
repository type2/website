http://www.sonic_dot_net/~sdelanty/6to12to6/

JC Whitney and a number of other places, sell a voltage drop device that is
used for the windshield wipers and has a variable control on it so that you can
speed up and slow down the wipers.  It sells for around $38US.  This device
would work for the radio also if you are not the wire and solder type.  You
need a volt meter to set it properly for the radio.  You could apply 12V to it
and adjust the control for 6.0 to 6.5V output.  Once you have it set, you might
devise a way to fix the knob in place so the voltage does not change on you by
accident.

Now the regulator described in the link above will provide
6V _at_ 1.0Amp   $6.20US  good for gauges and dash lamps that you do not want to
change out.

adding a bypass transistor will get you up to
6V _at_ 6.0 Amp with peaks to 10amps  great for radios.  Use a bigger heat sink
and the unit will provide 10amps continuous.  That should take care of most
radios that you care to throw at it.

Everything should be less than $10-$15 and a bit of elbow grease, AND the unit
is a much sturdier and dependable regulator than the $40 one from JCW.
