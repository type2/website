RESERVED and PREPAID site.

Our experience at the check in was not exclusive. Most of the Campers in our

if they are intruding or somehow incovieniencing the Rangers by arriving to

IT GOT WORSE!

The Rangers were constantly patrolling the Campsites of everyone in a

enforced. I do certianly understand now the importance of adherence to this

tool of harassment by the Ranger/s.

The Ranger/s was also constantly approaching the campsites, uninvited, and
threatning several Campers with a "FIFTY DOLLAR FINE" for non-specific and
even better non existent rule infractions.

THEN IT GOT DARK.

The ranger/s approached several campsites, one specifically several times
under cover of the night and the woods. Then when he was heard, he would
turn on his flashlight and walk away. This is BIZZARRE behavior, and
unacceptable from any perspective.

The next morning, Saturday June 17, The rangers resumed their harassment,
abeit with slightly less vigor. By Sunday morning, they seemed to have
calmed down. We, collectively as a group, and myself individually, did not
change or modify our camping behavior over the entire weekend. Actually, to
placate the Rangers we were forced into a policy of appeasment for the
duration of the Weekend.

My wife and I camp on average twice a month, year round. We have camped as a
couple and with groups all over the Southeast. I know we understand how to
camp responsibly. In fact all of the campers in our group pride themselves
on being responsible campers.

Nearly all of the campers in our group had prepaid reservations. We were not
by any strech of the imagination a Horde.
