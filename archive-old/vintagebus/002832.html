Red paint is then only paint I know that is more expensive than other paint.
The main difference in paint cost is the quality. PPG, DuPont, Glasurit are
the best, in my opinion. Metalic paint is definately harder to paint than
non-metallic, especially gold or silver metallic. If you're letting someone
else do it, let the painter pick the brand. That way, he or she will be
familiar with how it behaves.

Solder is great. A lot of high quality restoration shops will only use
solder. The problem is finding someone that can do it, it's a dying art. If
you can spare the bucks, go with solder.

Some high quality shops are using metal filler now. It has the same
water-proof substrate as fiberglass, but it has aluminum particles in it
instead of fiberglass. My paint supplier swears by it. They told me that
when it sets, its as hard as metal and can even be grinded like metal.
