I'll head this one off right now:

DON'T PUT LED REPLACEMENT "BULBS" IN YOUR EXTERIOR LIGHTS.

The reflectors that are on there now are designed to work with the
size, shape, and location of the filament in an incandescent lamp.  If
you install a replacement lamp made of clustered LEDs, you will
drastically REDUCE the total light output from the lamp/reflector/lens
assembly.  Guaranteed.

Yes, I know there are production cars with exterior LED lighting.  In
this case, the LED/reflector/lens assemblies have been ENGINEERED FROM
THE GROUND UP to work together and produce the correct light output at
all viewing angles, input voltages, etc.  Slapping an LED-cluster lamp
in a reflector that wasn't designed for it does not count as
"engineering".

Yes, there is even a 2008 Audi that will have white LED headlamps.
Again, these were designed from the ground up.  I am waiting for the
aftermarket to produce a cluster of white LEDs with an H4 or Sealed
Beam base and sell it as an "LED HEADLAMP!!1!" that "FITS ALL CARS!!!"
They won't mention the fact that at anything over 10 mph, your stopping
distance will be further than you can see with these lamps.  After

the sale of these lamps.
