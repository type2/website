 << Small Nut / Large Nut has NOTHING to do

<< The center section is the "split case" part --

Yes, however, the split case ended in '59 and the 
tunnel type started in '60

<< Is the change from  "split case" to "tunnel" the 
same as the Reduction box change?  Or am I
 confused on center sections (yes) ? >>

Yes.  Barndoor is barndoor. Later split case and 
pre-'63s were one style (small nut).  Post '64-'67 
were the larger 46mm nut style. 
 
<< Small Nut is '63 and down, Large Nut is '64 - '67.  
Small nut has smaller  brakes and uses 1.39 ratio 
reduction boxes.   Large nut has 1.26 ratio.>>

For the most part yes.  There were some redux ratio
options for the pre-63's but they are somewhat rare.
AFAIK you could only get the post-'64 in the 1.26
ratio here in the States.
 
<<You can put the center section of a '64 - '67 on a 
'63 and down?  What the  heck is the difference, if 
any?>>

Yes, you can.  Mainly internal improvements.  I only
use the post-'65 1st and 2nd gears for example.  Later
Swingaxles came with O-ringed sidecovers and I try
and use those as well.  So on and so forth.  A good
general rule of thumb is the later the better.  There are
some exceptions though!

<< It wasn't until I went over this again this morning

 worry about locating a correct core for a center

Thats correct.  The earlier reduction gear box trans
used a variation on the Type 1 center section.  Very
similar to the Beetles but with a different 3rd and 4th
ratio.  I will machine down some splines on the late 
3.875 Type 1 ring and pinion to accept the early 1.217
3rd gear from a Split window Bus.  Then I will get a 
.82 ratio 4th from a 002 IRS Type 2 transaxle to round
out the build.  This is the same ratio as the earlier Split
but it is splined to mate with the 3.875 ring and pinion
instead of being retained by a key like the earlier 'stock'
Type 2 ring and pinion. (4.125 or 4.375)
