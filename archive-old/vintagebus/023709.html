I am not sure about Thom's story...but I sent dues to SOTO in early `94.  The
check was cashed and nothing received...ever!   Mine is a tale of endless phone
calls...photocopies of a canceled check, and even a personal plea to Mr. Heard
himself at the  `94 Annivesary SOTO meet (the one which I meet you at jim).   This
was also  the meet in which I traveled nearly 6,000 miles round trip to in my `67
Westy to get to.  I was a complete gentlemen in this matter and was very friendly
in my pursuit.  Well after nearly a dozen phone calls with no reply, and nearly  a
half dozen letters with copies of my canceled check ALSO with no reply.....I
finally made a 6,000 mile personal visit to the SOTO Anniversary show in `94 and
spoke with Mr. Heard personaly. He knew who I was and was shocked at the distance
I had drve to this show.  Mr. Heard was very nice and said there must be a mix
up...he took my vitals and said he would fix it right away.  I STILL never
received a single newsletter or heard another word form SOTO or Mr. Heard again.
Needless to say I have never been to another SOTO meet or supported any of its
functions.  After a couple years of trying you just give up!!

I did contact Mr. Heard via email this past year (again) bringing this problem to
light (having heard he was "fixing" all of SOTO's problem's).  He was cordial and
said he would give me a full years membership if I could send he a copy of the
canceled check from `94!!  Well that check is long gone!!! and I am still out $20
and a lesson learned.

I feel I have $20 worth of personal slams at Mr. Heard at my disposal.  No
appologies here.  Sorry Jim.
