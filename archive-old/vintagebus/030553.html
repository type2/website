Subject: My rear saved by Zip Ties

I was at a stop light this afternoon on the way to my daughter's swim meet when
disaster struck.  I put the DC in gear and the linkage came loose.  Figuring that
it had popped out, I got the truck pushed over to the side, rolled out my
"emergency repair mat", slid under the DC and found that the rear shift coupler
had exploded into two pieces.  Everything was still there, but wire would not hold
it together to allow shifting.  I cut the wire off and aligned everything and
tried to figure out what I could do to make it operational.  I hit on the idea of
using some of the zip ties in the tool box.  One that went between the two set
bolts to hold everything together and then a half dozed wrapped around the
circumference of the coupler to hold the first one in place.  Ended up working
like a charm and I made it to the swim meet ON TIME and back without further
incident.

On the way to the meet, I was a little apprehensive about the quality of the
quickie repair, so I left it in third all the way.  Light DC + the 2L type IV =
amazing lugging power when needed.  On the way back, I gingerly went through the
gears like normal and it worked fine.

Question:  The coupler that was down there was red urethane.  Why aren't they made
out of metal?  Does that much vibration get absorbed by the non-metal couplers?

Looks like I'll be out coupler shopping in the morning.
