  There are numerous under pully tins. I have 4 different ones that I can
think of off hand and whenever putting an engine together I always match the
pully tin to the breast plate. 
  I personally use the early 1500 tin with the narrow pully tin (like you
appear to have) and a breast plate to match. 
  I have piles of the wider breast plates like you also appear to have and
the corresponding tin.
  One of these days when I have time (ya right) I can post pics of all the
different styles and let the VW gurus out there date them all.

ronnie

ps. In other words you either need a different year breast plate or a
different year (easier to find) pully tin
