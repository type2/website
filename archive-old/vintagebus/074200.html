On Wed, 21 May 2003, Heath, Raymond E wrote:

> Did physics get replaced with fuzzy logic? Small pump, large lines, low
> pressure. Volume is still volume, the larger the volume for any given
> pump size the lower the pressure. The same principle applies to a little
> hand fan blowing in a wind tunnel vs the large wind tunnel fan.

No, big difference here.  Oil is incompressable.  The main restriction to
oil flow should be the bearings and the goal is to deliver sufficient oil
flow at sufficient pressure to the bearings.  Running smaller hoses causes
a pressure drop in the hoses resulting in less pressure at the bearings.
Larger hoses reduce this pressure drop.

With incompressable fluids, if you put one cm^3 of oil in one end of the
hose, you'll get one cm^3 out the other regardless of the size of the
hose.  You can't lose volume unless you have a leak or _very_ elastic
hose!


> At

I would be more worried about their bearing clearances than the line size.
