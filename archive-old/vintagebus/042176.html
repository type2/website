Before you get carried away on this concept, do a bit of math.  It takes a
lot of miles to reach the breakeven cost point where the fuel savings
equal the cost premium of the vehicle.  My students did a cost comparison
of the Honda hybrid versus a Honda Civic using EPA mileage numbers.  At $2
a gallon, the breakeven
point was 700,000+ miles.  To get a breakeven point of 100,000 miles the
cost of fuel would have to go up to $12 a gallon.

Better to restore a bus, and with the money you saved from not buying a
new car take a few trips around the world!
