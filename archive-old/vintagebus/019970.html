I can tell you what NOT to do...
Have done a couple of things that I regret regarding
tents and truck tilts.


1.> Do NOT use bleach.
2.> Do NOT be aggressive while cleaning.
3.> Do NOT use a 3M scrubbie or strong brush.

Unfortunately have violated each in the past.

I suggest Woolite and gentle scrubbing/soaking.

Obviously, that the stitching is old and probably
weaker than it was when new. Tread lightly. Remeber
that you can always do more but cannot go back.

Back in the day, I got agressive with my first truck
tilt. Went after it with hot bleach water and a scrub
brush in an attempt to make it "whiter". Ranks right
up there with slamming suspension into the ground, up
dating oldEuro TypeI's from semaphores to incorrect
year turn signals and anything related to stinger
exhausts...
The bleach and agression weakened the stitching and
the whole thing literally shreaded the first time that
I drove it down I-10. <dummy> People kept passing me
staring until I realised that all I had was a pile of
canvas flags furiously flapping behind me...
