Hi rex.
What a blast !!! Man I had the coolest time in the US. Apparently, so the 
girls tell me, my accent is 'Kick Ass' !! Whooo !
seriously, thanks for everything. I only had 3 'break downs' in 2,680 miles.
1. The top pulley (on the generator) broke. I had stopped to check out a 
pawn shop for a guitar and noticed a nasty engine rattle..... the center of 
the pulley had sheared ! I found a guy who welded the center back in place 
good enough to use. With this all refitted I looked for a VW on the road 
.... In about 2 miles, BINGO ! a 60's bug. I traced the owner to the 
journal office where she worked and her father had an old pulley that I 
bought and fitted for $10. Its still fitted right now !
2. A tad more serious this time. Brake failure !!!! I sailed through a 
junction, but no collision ! Phew! Now it was the turn of AIRS ... A real 
nice guy Steve Pryble came to my rescue. stayed at his place for 2 days 
until the local 'Foreign Car Parts' came good with a wheel cylinder. 
Rebuilt and back on the road !
3. You know what the 3rd one was ? well it just wouldnt start, and the 
battery was getting more and more flat..... pulled a spark plug ... scratch 
head ... I had knocked the FUEL PUMP SWITCH !!!!!!!!!!
So, bar these small dramas, she ran like a dream. Congratulations on the 
engine build, it goes like magic. I rode at 55 but i reckon I could pull 
70+ when im back here in UK and have good brakes !
Now it should be on the 'Jingu Maru' !! thats the name of the ship ! - I 
think I might call the bus Jingu ... kind cool eh ?>
