Hi Dworkin,
Okay, that is not an over fill tube as we consider it.  That is connected to 
the metal bib on the bus body that goes around the filler neck.  Thus this 
bib collects the fuel that leaks out the around the gas cap and sends it out 
below the bus instead of all over the right side of the engine compartment.  
Interestingly our 1963 SC does not have this drain tube or even a bib that is 
designed to collect leakage from the cap.  So the fuel just leaks out into 
the compartment the tank sits on.
Overfill tubes are what the post-1960 type 1s have.  Which is a tube directly 
connected into the neck of the gas tank.  So when the fuel level gets to be 
just below the gas cap the fuel instead of leaking out around the gascap and 
into the trunk gets bled directly out under the type 1.  This helps to avoid 
getting just the right fuel/air mixture and blowing off the front hood when 
switching on any of the dash electrical switches and of course rids the 
interior of gas fume smell pre-1961 type 1 often had.
