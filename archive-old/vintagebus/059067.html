The redrilling of the holes by hand is the hard part.  Use extremely light
to
no pressure pushing the the drill bit into the material.  The drill bit is
going to want to grab into the material especially since it is soft
aluminum.
 If the drill motor is strong enough and geared low enough it will either
break the drill bit or give a good try to break your arms!  The hardest part
is to get the holes straight and that depends highly on how good your hand
eye coordination is and checking often at from two 90 degree points of view!


> Should I use locktight on the insert?

Not needed.  The Time-Sert inserts have a unformed final bottom interior
thread.  When you install it on the inserting tool and seat it the inserting
tool is driven though this unformed area of thread spreading the insert into
place into a press fit.  So you have to form the threads inside the repaired
part for the full length of the insert.  You could use locktight, but in
exhaust areas of great heat we doubt the lock tight will survive long!
