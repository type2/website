if your engine has done so good , I wouldn't add gauges, that could create
some bad karma with your engine, very interesting survey, maybe I'll use the
syn oil on my new 1776 single port, I'll know after I tear the 1600 new VW
mexican engine down after the swap, it'll have about 35,00 hard miles by
then.

As far as heads, there are 3 long reach duel port heads made with VW
stampings, not counting CB's own brand and MoFoCo's so you have a choice of
5 different castings, stay away from the cheap Brazilian, CB's have valves
from Isreal, the rest I don't know where the valves come from, but a lot of
the valves used today are made in Taiwan, I use only Manley valves when I
have my heads done, 200.00 dollars per head on my single ports, open for
90.5's, deck and CC, install new valve seats (40 and 35.5), 3 angle valve
job, match all spring tensions to specs, hifh rev springs, cromalloy
retainers and keepers, silicon bronz valve guides, seems like a lot of work
but the last 2 engines I had heads built to these specs are still going
strong after much abuse and many miles.
