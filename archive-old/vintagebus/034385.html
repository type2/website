	Good idea.  Nothing wrong with building a 'spare' motor from parts
	that are still within spec (measure carefully).  However, I would be
	very reluctant to re-use bearings unless you put them back with 
	exactly the same components from whence they came......

	Things like bearings and related components establish wear patterns 
	very quickly, and unless you put them back with the case, crank or
rod 
	from which they have established a wear pattern will get 'false'
torque 
	readings on the fasteners, *fast* wear and very early failure....(
and I do
	mean very early.....)

	Gotta put in original spot or replace.  And if replacing the other
components
	must be pretty perfect before deciding not to linish, grind or align
bore - or you really are
	still faced with the same problem.  Nothing wrong with putting an
'original' 
	low wear (as measured) bottom end back together (esp. in a 'spare'
motor).
