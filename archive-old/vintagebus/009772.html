Another possibility is that the tubing could be inside the bus!  If one
could get a hold of this leak-free, flexible tubing in a FLATTENED shape to
maximize heat transfer (same theory as in oil coolers: flat, not round), it
could fit in the grooves in the floor underneath the plywood or whatever
that is a typical camper floor.  I do not think that attempting to heat
underneath the rear cushion would be very successful, as the rear cushion
would insulate, and you'd feel very little of the heat.  Again, insulation
underneath the floor of the bus would be vital.  Heating the sides is also
an option/addition.  The insulative material could be placed next to the
skin of the panels, with the tubing running right next to the interior wood
(or whatever you're using.)
