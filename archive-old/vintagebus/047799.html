> found out about the gear shift while I think about it.  A shifter from a 66

> reason is that the piece that sticks down in the socket is shorter on a 66

a 66 and 67 shifter is the same, as long as the bus came with the later style of front shift rod where the front shift bushing pokes into the front end of the shift rod and slides onto a peg sticking back at the front there.  The earlier kind the bit the shift stick bolts to has a circle that goes around the front part of the shift rod and the bushing goes in that circle around the shift rod.  this was changed early in the 66 model year, so an early 66 came with the one kind, and maybe the 66 you got the piec eoff was an early 66 and so had a differnt piece than the 67 .  I hope this makes sense, it is hard to describe clearly.  The early kind it really helps for reassembly to have someone under the bus holding the bit the shift stick bolts go into, the later kind you can assemble it w/o help easily.
