Hi,

If you push with 10lb against 1 sq inch, that 1 sq inch will have to press
back with 10lb/sq inch. If you push with 10lb against 1/2sq inch, that 1/2
sq inch will also have to press back against your 10lb with 10lb, but the
pressure built up will have to double so that you have 20lb per sq inch of
pressure behind that 1/2 sq inch to develop 10lb to counteract your 10lb.

Therefore, a smaller diameter cylinder will produce higher hydraulic
pressure than a larger cylinder given the same foot pressure applied.
