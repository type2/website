Are you sure you want the .77 fourth?  You will definitely need to change the 
lower gears to prevent an even more bigger MPH/RPM gap between third and 
fourth.  We use a 3.875 R&P with .82 fourth in our buses with a stock VW but 
differant from bus third gear to get only a 1 MPH gap between 3,000 RPM _at_ 44 
MPH in third and 2,000 RPM _at_ 45 MPH in fourth gear.  Below 2,000 RPM in 
fourth seems to lug the engine. Cruising with same size tires on front and 
rear axles results in 58 MPH _at_ 3,000 RPM.  In our SC truck we can carry a 
load with a big bore 40HP engine on the freeway.  Gojng N. to S. in our W. 
Washington State area only one looong steeep hill in S. Puyallup and one 
through Tacoma makes us down shift into the top of third.    Eric&Barb
