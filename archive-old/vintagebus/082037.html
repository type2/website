On Mon, Mar 07, 2005 at 01:09:49PM -0700, Eric Allred wrote:
> I would think they would cancel each other out somewhat. The power pulley
> slows the fan down, then the 356 pulley speeds it back up.

yes, but depending on the actual diameters of the pullies it will make
a difference. I found some measurements from someone on the samba and
make these assumptions.

stock crank pulley 6.75" 
power pulley 5.75"
std gen pulley 4"
356 pulley 3" (this number was extracted from known ratios)

these measurements were not belt groove, but OD. Making the huge
assumption that they all ride down the same amount in the groove the
proportions should be somewhat close (i think).

crank pulley | gen pulley | ratio
    stock    |   std      | 1.67:1
    stock    |   356      | 2.25:1
    power    |   std      | 1.43:1
    power    |   356      | 1.92:1

these numbers are very much up for interpretation since I don't have
all the parts to make real measuerments, but I'd love to have some
real measurements from someone with all of the parts.
