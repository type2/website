Sounds to me like the starter is screwed up, maybe the bushing that goes into 
the flywheel. It's just a small fix that you can get to by removing the 
starter and feeling up in the hole what shape the bushing is in. You can back 
it off with a screwdriver and replace it so that the starter doesn't drag.

A dragging starter will sound like it's about to quit whenever it turns over. 
Once fixed, it will turn over fine. I have a Type 34 Ghia that acted that 
way. I was using a 12-volt battery on a 6-volt starter system. It dragged all 
the time. After replacing the bushing that the starter fits into (about a 
1/2" x 3/4" bushing, which was wallowed out) the starter now turns over 
smoothly and rapidly. Don't worry, the 12-volt battery on a 6-volt system 
just turns the car over a little faster, has been that way for two years, and
is the prescribed method of fixing the starter on a Type 34 Ghia (otherwise 
one would have to replace the flywheel, if he could find one). For the length 
of time that the starter is engaged, there is no bad effect.
