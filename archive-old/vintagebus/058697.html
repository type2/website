>How's the starter BUSHING in the trans case? The
>starter relies upon it for support to keep things
>centered, and if the bushing is worn even a brand-new
>starter will drag and have less power. Simple to
>change with the engine out or you can remove it from
>the starter side (either with the "correct" tool or a
>tap - tap through the bushing and when it bottoms out
>against the engine case it'll climb right out).
>There's also a correct tool for driving the new one
>in, but you can improvise with a bolt and nut. Not
>feeling that adventurous? At least see if putting a
>dab of grease in the bushing helps any.
