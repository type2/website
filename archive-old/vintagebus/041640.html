The buyer is definitely under pressure to complete the deal.  The seller
is the one who is under less pressure in a "first money" situation.  Since
it is the seller that has the "rare" object, it behooves the buyer to act
as rapidly as possible.

The original topic of this thread was the ethics of someone selling a bus
after it had already been verbally promised to another buyer.  If the
seller wishes to operate under this basis, then the seller should be
honest enough to tell all buyers that the vehicle goes to the first person
who comes up with the cash.

Keep in mind also, that I live a little too close to LA, and that this
proximity has probably tainted my opinion of the random caller.
