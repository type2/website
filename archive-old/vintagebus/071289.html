My Stewart Warner is rated to burn 0.3 Gallons of fuel an hour while
burning. With the adjustable thermostat, the burner does not burn all the
time (it cycles), so fuel useage is even less than that.

According to the math, if my burner was going non stop all the way from SLC
to Las Vegas (it takes me about 7 1/2 hours in my bus), I would burn around
2 1/4 gallons of fuel during the whole drive. I've listened to the burner
(on the hottest setting) cycle on and off on a 25 degree morning, and it
seemed to burn for about as long as it wasn't (50%). So if during the entire
drive to Vegas was sub freezing temps, I would still only use about 1 1/8
gallons for the drive. If it's in the 30's-40's during the daytime, it would
be a lot closer to the 30% cycle time as stated above. On the lowest
setting, i've listened to it on a 40-45 degree day, and it seemed to cycle
probably closer to 15-20% of the time.

I would say, fuel consumption when using the heater is anywhere between 2%
and 6% on my bus. Not too bad. Worst case, my mileage is going from 24mpg
down to 22 1/2mpg. I can still go almost 340 miles to a tank of gas (bay
tank), so i'm not complaining at all. 20,000 BTU's is keeping me toastier
than my stock heater ever did, and small loss of gas mileage is sooooo worth
it!  :-)
