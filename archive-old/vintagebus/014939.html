Well, if you take the number of turns at the crank, divide by the 4th
gear ratio, divide by the ring and pinion ratio, and multiply by 2 you
will get the number of turns at the wheel (with the other being held):

(CRturns/FOURrat/RPrat)*2 = WHturns

If you are accurate about your angular measurements of the crank and
wheel, there will probably be only be one combination of 4th and R&P
that will satisfy the equation.

There is no direct solution, since you have two variables and only one
equation.  Since there are a limited nunber of values for the gears,
you should be able to figure out what you have.
