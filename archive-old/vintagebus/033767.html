OK, here is my proceedure

1. I find TDC then set the rotor in the center of the mark for number 1.
2. I start the engine, rotate the dizzy left till it runs ruff, make a mark,
then rotate it the other way till I get the same.
3. I then set the dizzy in the middle (between the marks), lock it down,
then drive it up a hill and feel the how it runs, I will generally advance
the timing from there till I hear a ping, then retard it a few degrees from
there.
4. After that it's all driving and minor adjustments from there.
5. After all said and done I will put the timing light there so I know where
my MARK is at for future reference.
6. All this is done with the engine at OPERATING TEMP, not just sitting
idleing.
