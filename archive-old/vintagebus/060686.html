Nice deal!!!

so check the fulcrum plates and crown gear for wear- and the spade ends of 
the axle shafts- use the best ones- and even swap them side to side-- so the 
not worn side is the push side.   

wear at the crown gears/FP makes a thunk when you step on the gas, or let off 
the gas, unless you are real careful.   

So check RGBearings for wear as well.  Some folks might say buy new, but i 
say see if they are okay first.  

replace shift rod bushings in bus
"""  shift tube boots (WW has repros)
"" front tranny bushing- where hockey stick goes into nose cone- a usual drip 
source
--new tranny boots
--drain old oil. put fresh oil in the tranny.  Change oil after about 300 to 
500 miles.
--If you want to get fancy you can paint (or powder coat) the axle tubes etc.

myself I'd remove the side cover and count the ring gear teeth on the old (no 
4th) and new (used) tranny,  divide that number by 8 - for 8 teeth on pinion 
shaft for tunnel tranny-   to get the r/p ratio.  Like a 4.125 is 33/8.  If 
35 teeth the ratio is  35/8 is 4.375.  Just FYI.
