Hi Micheal,

I believe the 'different' engine air ducting was an option available
on all 'utes', but mainly built into buses being
sent to what VW perceived as hot dusty countries
(why does every pre 56 Aus bus have factory safaris? 8-)).

However, what we have here is a little different from what
you describe.  Here, the air is basically taken in through vents
under the brow (above the windscreen - sorta as per usual)
ducted through a 'twin skin' cabin roof and then through further
ducting to the engine bay.

The M code may have been 010?  Anthony Surie
has some pictures of a single cab with the 
option on one of his web pages at

http://www.geocities_dot_com:80/Baja/Trails/7509/ozsc.html

I have not personally seen a double cab with
this 'complete' set up here - but have seen
a few with a twin roof skin  in the cab as if they
were prepared for the option?
