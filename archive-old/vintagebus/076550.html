HC is hydrocarbons: fuel that is burned.
CO is carbon monoxide. Too little CO and the engine misfires because it is too lean, HC goes sky high. Too
much CO and the engine is too rich and the HC goes sky high.

At .20 CO at idle, you are way too lean. You need to get the CO up to 2 to 2.5%, that will bring the HC down.
Either you have a massive vacuum leak or your carb is way screwed up.
