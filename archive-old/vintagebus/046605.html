first I would go check that the bolts and nuts holding the tranny in and the 
ones at the RGBoxes are not loose, I personally torque the RGBox bolts to 
70-80ft lbs, no less, no more.

you really should use new rubber mounts when installing the tranny, 
especially the front one, which is a hassle to change, the back two you just 
have to pull the motor, pretty much.   crawl under bus to check to make sure 
they are not cracked and fubared, if they are you must replace them.  NOW.   
or things will bend and break in your shift linkage and it will be a bigger 
hassle. ( I have seen not only the front shift guide broken, the front shift 
rod messed up, and the rear coupler messed up, but the hockey stick in the 
tranny bent and the nose cone cracked. )  One way of checking this is to lay 
hands on the fan shroud, and pull it back.   If the engine will tilt back, 
this mean the front of the tranny will tilt up and the front mount is broken. 
 At least it was all apart recently.  If the engine does not tilt back, this 
means that the front mount is securing the front of the tranny to the bus, 
which is it's purpose and it should do.
