Amen brother Jim! While I appreciate the fact that this bus was apparently
cobbled together from a bunch of buses that would normally have seen the
crusher I still couldn't see it being worth more than a couple grand as a
novelty. I'm sure the work is fine, I'm not criticizing what I haven't seen
in person, but we all know that to get what you want out of a bus in this
price range (i.e. as an investment) you have to be either bone-stock, or
front cover material. Otherwise, you've thrown a lot of money into a hole.
It's might be a hole that's a labour of love, but if so, then why sell?
Sure, sometimes you have to scrape the money together, but as the saying
goes "an emergency on your part doesn't constitute one on mine". I hate to
see people get into the position where they HAVE to sell a bus, but if you
think you're going to ever be there, you can't expect to get crazy prices
on something. The Campulance was a different beat altogether. I can't
believe it stayed in Jim's driveway long enough for the ink to dry on the
For Sale sign!

I sincerely wish the guy selling the bus good luck. But, if it were at a
show...I'd keep on walking by it personally.
