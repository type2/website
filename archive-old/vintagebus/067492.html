Subject: RE: [VB] broken clutch bolt

Old clutch pressure plate bolts are a pain in the petudy. Over the years I
have had many snap off. The main reason was that in the past they had been
over torqued and stretched, weakening them. Back in the early 70's I learned a
trick from an old VW mechanic. Since the clutch pressure plate bolt holes go
all the way through it is better to take off the flywheel and do you carbide
drilling from the back side and use a reverse easy out with the hole sprayed
liberally with LPS or WD-40. Carefully rock it back and forth and it will come
out. Since one broke all are suspect and I would buy another set. I get all my
hardened metric bolts and case savers at a local establishment called Tacoma
Screw. Maybe you have access to something like that where you are. Don't
forget your gland nut, if it's old, replace it!
