We had to finally just torch the coupler in half to get things out enough to 
work on them.  Then replaced the coupler with a used one Clara had in what 
was left of another 1960 panel that we had to cut what was left of the front 
to get out the rear shift rod and the forward coupler that were rusted 
together and still took three hours to seperate.  We would suggest slicing 
your coupler lengthwise with a thin grinding disc.  Then insert a slot tip 
screwdriver in the slit and twist the driver to spread slightly the coupler 
open to loosen the coupler and allow in penetrating oil.  Afterward you can 
weld up the coupler and reuse it.
