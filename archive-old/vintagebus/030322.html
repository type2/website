I found a "Campmobile 80" interior in a junkyard that had a full-width 
bed (and lots of nice birch!).  As I was tired of the view-blocking 
cabinet in my 71 westy (Campmobile 70) anyway, I swapped the beds out.

Now I've got a spare 3/4 width bed and I'm thinking of doing something 
similar to what you describe in my 65 bus, which came without a rear 
interior.  It will take a bit of tweaking, as I'd like to make it full 
width and the right-side (cabinet-side) brackets aren't made to mount on 
the wheel well like the other side is (the full-width version does). 
Can't use it as-is, anyway, as the floor of the bed unit conflicts with 
the heat log location.

My plan is to use the bed hardware, but cut new plywood to fit the 65's 
layout and make it full width if possible -- or I'll do what I did in my 
71 before I found the full width bed (pix at 
http://www.supernet_dot_net/~jclark/vw/71westy/page2.html
).  Should be pretty straightforward - I'll post pix if I ever do this.

Other than the heat log conflict, I think a bay bed will fit - mainly 
depends on height of the rear tray, slope down to cargo floor, and wheel 
well config.  In fact, seems like I already tested it once.
