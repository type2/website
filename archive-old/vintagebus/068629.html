> does the 65, 70. 75, (hate to sound so stupid) make a difference?

195, 205, or 215, etc. is the width of the tire tread in mm.  The /65, 70 or 
75 is the sidewall hieght in percentage to the tire thread width.  In other 
word 235/75R15 is a tire with 235 wide tread, and sidewall hieght that is 75% 
or 176.25mm tall, R stands for radial, and 15 is the rim diameter where the 
tire seats.

> LT is what I want to side walls rated at!?!

Some LT (light truck) radials are softer and lower rated than some P 
(passenger) radials.  Thusly you have to inspect the sidewall rating of the 
tire to make sure it meets or surpasses the 1525 Lbs. factory minimum.
