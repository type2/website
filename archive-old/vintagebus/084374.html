227 may be ideal, but I don't know that it is realistic on a bus. I
hear a lot of 235 and even 265 on hot california central valley days.
I experienced thermal breakdown of the oil at 280 with Valvoline 20-50
racing oil.

You mentioned head temps of 300, which is really mild. 425 to 450 is
considered normal for a bus. Bear in mind the temp calibration of the
gauge which made to read at 70 ambient temp.

You can run synthetic oil to help your oil run cooler. Type 4 oil
cooler and larger pulley (or toothed belt/pulley system) can help cool
you down. Yes, get your Hoover bit and do all else you can to seal
your engine compartment and cooling system. Make sure your fuel
mixture is not lean, but that would affect your head temp most.
