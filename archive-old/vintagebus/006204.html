My experience with this is problem fresh enough that my blood pressure is
still recovering, so here's my tale of woe:

Bad News: An anonymous neighbor complains to the county about my junk
vehicles ['66 crewcab on jacks in the garage, '73 insect-type VW in the
driveway]. S/He didn't come talk to me, say something like "Hi neighbor,
nice to meet ya, how's about movin' your POS's?" Went straight to The Man.
So at the end of another 10 hours chained to my cubicle, I come home to find
a WARNING taped to my door, giving me 10 days to remove the "abandoned
vehicles" from my property before the 60 days in jail &/or $1000 fine per
day kicks in. Bummer mas grande.

Good News: The next morning, I call the inspector named on my guvmint
warning. "Look," he says, "if somebody complains, we gotta do an inspection,
and if there's something there to write up, we gotta write it up. Sorry."
But he is surprisingly sympathetic to my problem. I ask him what I can do
short of selling off my VWs that will make him happy, and he says that if a
vehicle is tagged and *appears* to be operable in the inspector's opinion,
wink-wink-nudge-nudge, then it's not a junk vehicle. The insect is tagged
and running, so my only problem is getting the crewcab kosher. After a day
spent in tag lines, several days hunting desperately for a set of bus wheels
[thanks BFY and Vintage Warehouse], and several very late nights of
reassembly, all is well.

Conclusions: This could've turned out much worse than it did. In my
area --Atlanta 'burbs--my new inspector buddy tells me, the zoning rules
prohibit practically any automotive work at a residence, up to & including
oil changes. The philosophy seems to be to outlaw everything, so when they
do decide to throw the book at a guy, it's a hefty book. I do agree with
Thom that we don't have the right to an eyesore that offends our community,
but if a neighbor has to go out of his way, even trespass, to find my
eyesore, then it should be _his_  problem. But that's not the way the laws
are written. What offends me most about this whole episode is that my
[assumptions follow] yuppie, new to _my_ neighborhood [I've been here since
'65] neighbor couldn't take the time to introduce himself and make his
problems known to me. If he'd done so, I would've happily done all the
things I ended up doing anyway, and we could've left the guvmint out of it.
Maybe we'd even be friends by now. Stranger things have happened.

Ultimate Conclusion: The world has too few volkswagen people and too many
yuppies, with disastrous karmic consequences for the planet. Old news.

I feel much better for having gotten this off my chest. Sorry for the
bandwidth.
