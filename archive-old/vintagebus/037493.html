 Also, the shell I bought had safari's at one time.  Anyway to tell if the
 brackets are original or aftermarket?
 ---------------

if the windsheild glass is one, look at the top of the window area- the ledge 
hidden by the glass/seal at its top.  if they were original safaris it would 
have some nice painted round holes, through which you see a plate with 
threaded holes for the safaris frames to bolt to.   Aftermarket tend to be 
installed with holes and screws thread into the sheet metal of the bus.

look at the brackets welded to the window frame sof the bus- how are the 
welds?   Original have nice smooth welds around the brackets, and also latchs 
on the dash (or holes where they had been) and the one eyed duck on the nose 
to hold the wipers (or holes where it had been)

also- M codes M113 and 132 on the tag is hinged windsheilds (aka safaris) 
though you could have original safaris w/o it being on the tag.
