Along these lines, http://manual.gimp_dot_org/manual/GUM/Part_four.html and
especially http://manual.gimp_dot_org/manual/GUM/Prepress.html deal with
a subset of this: how to get an image that looks good on your computer
monitor to come out of a printing press looking approximately the same
way.  The basic thing I get out of all of this is "it ain't easy".

TV manufacturers faced similar problems when color TVs first came out.
Put a color TV in a room and ask each of ten people to adjust every
knob until it "looks right" and you will end up with 11+ different
sets of settings.  They tried all kinds of tricks but even the TV
you bought yesterday still has "color" and "tint" controls.

Also in the direction of Bus painting, see pages 15-16 of "VW Bus"
by Keith Seume.  The OG paint process was approximately: acid dip
(cleaning), phosphate dip (anti-rust), hand rub the entire body,
spray primer, spray the first color, and (if two colors) mask off
the bottom and respray.  (The top first/bottom first/dash same color
as top or bottom is a whole other thread).
