well, its like this.  dont bang.  This is a fiddly procedure, so find a 
comfortable place to sit.   You want the flywheel to have a little movement 
there.  otherwise it will bind up , your motor will seize, and generally be a 
PITA.   But you dont want too much slopp in there, or the crank will slopp 
back and forth against the bearings and case and generally bash itself into 
the scrap pile.  You want it just right.  And VW says use 3 shims,   so use 
three shims,  of the right thickness.   Sounds like you need to get some 
thinner shims.   The end play should be .003" or .004" to .006"

so you put two shims in, put the FW one, torque it down, remnove the FW lock, 
check the end play.  Install a 3rd shim to makes the space about .005"   If 
it is too tight, to put a 3rd shim in, you have to get thinner shims.  This 
is why they come in several sizes.  just have to do some math.  Basically you 
need to find the amount of end play you have, and figure what 3 shims work to 
make the end play .004-.006".  dont be attached to the shims it used to have, 
if this bottom end was rebuildt, its a whole new number, and you change the 
shimmage to fit the motor.   You also have to be really anal about not 
getting any grit in the shims when you install them.  clean clean clean.  
After you get a combonation of 3 installed and they check out the right 
endplay, then you take the FW back off, and install the main seal and new o 
ring, put them back on, Voila!!
