I don't have any experience with "ALL METAL", but have plenty with a couple
of similar products.

When I was building jigs and templates at Boeing back in the '50s, we did
revisions and repairs using DEVCON "Liquid Steel".  This is a 2-part product
with a very high metal content.  We'd plug holes in drill jigs with this
stuff, drill new holes in it after it cured, and press in steel drill
bushings.  The bushings stayed tight and true in production settings where
they were subjected to very high heat and torsional stresses.

A similar product is "MarineTex".  This is also a 2-part product that is
used often to repair outboard engine crankcases, etc., along with pump
casings, exhaust manifolds, etc.  It's extremely tough and can be machined.

FWIW.....
