Non- bus (but related...)

As someone that once worked for UPS in college for 2 years, I give the company
credit.  No, I am not replying coz I am offended, and I am sure that offense was
not meant.  Thom is right...  They move an enormous amount of material.

For example, when employed there 15 years ago, I was required to sort 35 pieces
per minute, and I would do this for 4-6 hours (per day) during the regular
season, and 9-12 during the holidays.  And there were 10 of us sorting in a rural
California city (Salinas).  We used to have 10-25 bob tail trailers a day coming
into our hub, loaded with packages.  Special handling was given to "odd" shaped
material, and we were "written up" by non-union supervisors for mishandling the
packages.  Packages could weigh up to 70 pounds (then).

By the percentages, some damaged packages are inevitable.  But believe me, they
work very hard to prevent losses and balance this with high productivity.  As
they ultimately pay for the losses and the inefficiencies.

Finally, I again agree with Thom in the key being in the packaging.
