 This same thing happened to the guys who came to BBB V from West Virginia.
They had a new clutch cable installed before they left on there 3000 mile
journey and made it all the way to Havasu when the symptoms you described
happened. Upon removal of the NEW? cable it was found to be totally frayed
in the middle of the tube. They replaced the cable here, cleaning the tube
out thoroughly and NOT greasing the cable, this is one place where grease
can be bad. After rerouting the new cable through and greasing the BOWDEN
tube, all was fine and to this day 3500 plus miles later, clutch still
smooth as silk.
