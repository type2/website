.... I think Matt is pretty right on the money even tho he's only 29!!  As
an old fart (53) who was there during the og phases he described, I can
relate.  However, I think the one thread that runs thru all these eras where
buses are cool is that buses have always been DIFFERENT than the present
cultural values.  I think this is why the latest group that is thinking out
of the box, whatever that box is, is attracted to the type 2!  At first,
there was nothing else like a type 2.  Then GM came out w/ the Corvair to
copy it, but alas, they didn't have a clue, just like now.  Then  the Big 3
came out w/ vans and vans became part of the establishment, so the out of
the box people lost interest in them for awhile.  Then the Big 3 came out w/
minivans which became very mainstream, and volks saw how the og buses really
were ahead of their time, very different from the minivan, and thus became
cool again because they were DIFFERENT.  Then cars became very expensive as
safety features such as abs, crush zones, air bags, leather upholstery,
computerized fuel injected engines, "mag rims" as standard, etc. and those
out of the boxers again saw the type 2 as DIFFERENT, economical &  a
statement that they saw things DIFFERENTLY from the mainstream.

Buses have always appealed to the out-of-the-boxers because they were
DIFFERENT.  Now VW is trying to capture that market, but unfortunately they
have not come up w/ something that is DIFFERENT, so I think they will miss
the boat like they have since the 70's, unless they start thinking out of
the box again.

IMHO,
