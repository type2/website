Bummer seriously.  yes, definately go to the doctor if the eye's hurt.   good thing it'll be ok.

 I've never bothered with goggles while pulling a tranny myself either.  Then again, I don't have an air gun to zip things off with.  But I always do when using a grinder.

Yes you have to split the boxes to take the axles off the center section.

with the tranny still attached to the bus I'd take the rgbox covers off.   (you can do it out of the bus, but easier if it holds still, your choice) (you already have the brakes off, even the backing plates right?) Put a oil drain pan underneath when you pull the center seal covers off the RGBox, and when you pull the box half off, as it will drip gear oil, even if you drained the tranny already.  Your wife will like you better if you don't smell like gear oil.

with cover off,
take off the circlip at the end of the axle shaft.  The upper outer bearing is a press fit, meaning you should be able to take two screwdrivers and pry it off. Sometimes they are stuck, and so I made a wierd puller thingy for when that happened to me, which has come in useful since.

  Then (with nuts around base of thingy around axle tube undone, and RGBox undone, heck, tranny out) pull the axle tube./RBOx inner half off the tranny.   give it a good pull. Then you can knock the inner upper bearing out with a stick or long pipe.  

under the base of the axle tube flange is the daisy wheel aka flower aka kaiser sphere.

you can now get out your big circlip pliers (and goggles) and remove the big circlip and take the axle shaft/fplates/crown gear out and inspect for damage.
