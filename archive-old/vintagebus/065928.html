I ran thin wall 88's until they blew up and later had the block/heads
punched for thick wall 88 or 90.5 (went 88) on my old 71 westy. I used stock
exhaust and heater boxes with 041 heads being the only difference. I had
some mis matched 375 heads on the engine when I bought it and it ran pretty
rough for a dub. When I tore the engine down the first time I noticed that
one head was new and the other was a rebuilt that had also been flycut. A
definite cc mismatch. Anyway, I never noticed any heat problem or exhaust
restriction. I kept the thermostat and flaps in place.
