Using DOT 5 will do more than "gum up switches", it will also destroy seals,
though the process will probably take longer with bus brake systems.

This discussion comes up quite often in MTB circles, as half the brake
manufacturers use one type of brake fluid, and half the other.

DOT 3, DOT 4 and DOT 5.1 are compatible

DOT 5 is totally different, it will almost certainly cause leaking seals
(the seals are compatible with either type of brake fluid, contact with the
other type will cause them to degenerate), which will all have to be
replaced AFTER the system is properly flushed and cleaned, and it will
probably create a gluey mess within your braking system if mixed with DOT 3,
4 , or 5.1 fluid.

The primary advantage of DOT 5.1 over DOT 4, is the higher heat rating. This
is an issue with limited volume systems used in high performance Motorbikes,
and the small hydraulic systems used on MTBs, but a bus's brake system
should have sufficient volume, that the fluid expansion (due to heat) should
not be sufficient to create any noticeable problems.

IF anyone wants to use DOT 5.1, the easiest place to get the fluid is bike
(IE motorbike) shops. it's used for Ducatis, later Sports Hondas etc.
