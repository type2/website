I buy my plasti-gauge at a NAPA parts supply store.
I would think that any above average auto parts store would have it behind
the counter -vs- having to order it special.
Make a note of the tolerances you are shooting for prior to buying it.
Then you can be sure you get the right gauge first stop.
Each package covers a pre-set band of measurements.  It also comes with
more than enough material to do one motor.  It doesn't take much to a rod
or main journal.
I use a razor to cut them into smaller pieces and let the ends that have
been cut overhang outside of the journals so that the actual "cut" doesn't
get into the squish and taint the readings.
Good luck.
