I think it's a dang site better than a) chemdip b) carb cleaner in the
driveway

The best parts cleaning solution I've had - which could be extrapolated to
engine cases with the right bits - was a small harbor frieght parts cleaner
with a small amount with laquer thinner.  I sealed the lid with  good
quality, closed-cell weatherstripping, and duct-taped the holes in the
sides.  The vapors *alone* will strip an emblem to bare metal in a few
hours!  2 days stripped an old tranny nosecone to near-nos condition.

The secret - you don't have to immerse the part - the vapors will work as
well or better, if you trap them in there with it!  Seal it up and let it
stew.  Caution: it's going to stink real bad when you open it!
