I did not have much time to work on the bus tonight-but I did get it to
start- I think the problem was in the relay connections. I took the relay
apart, and polished((with my dremel) any connection I could get to, and the
ones I had difficulty getting to-I scraped clean with a dental pick...hooked
it back up and the bus started.


I have just looked in the bentley guide pg E-10 1. I guess I have the
connector that is not in the photo-as my bus was built prior to august 1
1965. I think it was built on march 29. My connector was improved upon.

As for the spring loaded connector-I mean the plastic thing right on the
back on the sealed beam. It is black and triangular shaped. They are
basically three push on sockets in a plastic housing-but where the wires
from the loom connect it is a spring loaded connection. I don't have a
digital camera handy-but I will try to describe it...basically there is a
piece of copper bent over in a u-shape. The tinned wire goes under this
u-shape piece. I think it is tinned-or soldered someway or other so all the
strands are stuck together. Now the bent piece of copper feeds through a
slot in the plastic and a small spring holds it in the plastic housing. It
is quite a complicated little piece. Now the problem in the design-there is
nothing holding the wire tight to the copper connector-just the spring. I
think there is too much slop in there. In other words I can actually see
space between the connector and the wire.I think tomorrow night I will try
circumventing the connector and just use some aligator clips between the
headlight and the wires of the bus...if this works ~ the problem is in the
plastic connector,right? therefore I will go out and get some new
connectors.(I guess to answer your question-yes it is possible to remove the
wires fromthe push on connector-no problem, actually very easy)
