We take "commercial" stuff on a case-by-case basis.  For instance, if you
have some NOS widgets and put them on ebay and tell the list, that's OK.
If you feel the need to re-inform the list of that auction several times,
that's not, and you'll quietly disappear from the list.

The finer line is with the "real" commercial vendors, ie, the ones with
real shops.  So far, none of them have crossed it.  Posting a "we sell that
piece" when a question comes up about a particular widget is fine.  

What *isn't* fine are completely off-topic posts about laser printers or
surf-for-money schemes, and "little jimmy has terminal rust of the colon
and wants to receive 1 million emails  before he goes tot he junkyard in
the sky" stuff.

If you cross the line, you'll know.  You can bank on that one! :)
