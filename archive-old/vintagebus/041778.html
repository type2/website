OK, if you decide to go with the Mexican engine (I'm happy with mine) you
will need:

oil cooler adapter
Doghouse fan shroud
wider doghouse fan
front (FIF) tin with hole for doghouse outlet
Two pieces of ductwork for doghouse outlet.
Hoover bit that goes on oil cooler
cylinder tins
manifold ends for dual port
manifold center section for dual port
Carburator adapter to adapt 30 carb to 34 manifold (unless you can
scrounge up a rare 30mm center section)

The hard parts to find are the front tin, which was a '71-bus-only part,
and the manifold center section which has a fitting for the brake booster.
Both of these can be made from bug parts in a pinch.

The best way to go is to find a complete set of tin on a junker.  Check
the tin (particularly the cylinder tin) for cracks and braze them before
installation.  OEM tin is markedly better than the aftermarket stuff, and
the wrecker's price is usually a lot more reasonable.

I've probably forgotten a bit or two, but the conversion isn't all that
hard to accomplish.
