I've only done this with a pump not installed on engine, but I think it
should work either way. Cover an fitting with your finger and operate
the pump a few times. Remove finger. If you here a little "pop" of
vacuum being released, then you have identified the inlet. No "pop"? Try
the other fitting. Still no "pop"? Pump is bad, probably the check valve
flapper inside. Can't rebuild the Brazilian pump to my knowledge. Old
German two piece pump is a breeze to rebuild, kit is readily available.
Instead of a finger you could attach some clear tubing to the fitting
and drop the other end of tubing into a can of gas. See gas being drawn
up the tube? That's the inlet. I've never had to prime a pump, but I
could just be lucky because I have heard some references to it
somewhere.
