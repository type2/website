As long as we're not talking about oil, I thought I'd throw my two cents
in.

I read that article and thought it was really good. I was especially
pleased to see that the author (who's a Mechanical Engineer) mentioned
the *superior* heat dissipating characteristic of synthetic oil as
compared to dino oil. The idea that synthetics don't dissipate heat as
well as dino oil just doesn't seem reasonable. If that were true, the
oil would never get hot. The synthetic oil in my engine gets plenty hot.
Gene Berg got a lot of stuff right, but that wasn't one of them.

If we had archives, I would tell you to go read my engine autopsy posts
from last summer, but we don't, so here's the short version. I use
Castrol Syntec 5W-50 and a Purolater filter and change both at 10,000
miles. When my engine dropped a valve (my fault) and I had to rebuild
it, I found so little wear on the parts in the bottom end it was
amazing. I checked my bearing clearances with plastigage. I didn't find
a single bearing worn out of the new engine specs. I could have reused
my old rod bearings. The cam and lifters looked like new.

I'm using synthetics from now on.
