Subject: Great BNNTA weather and event update...

Hi all,

I just got back from attending the Eureka Springs VW event down in Arkansas.
I caravaned in my '87 Westfalia Camper GL with DBG member Brian and Dawn
Holcomb in their awesome '63 Westy.  He was "robbed" of what I (and others)
believe to be a First Place award (a story to be expounded upon around the
campfires at BNNTA this weekend).  The event was quite interesting...  They
definitely need to move to a more participant-friendly event location,
otherwise, it was a great event.  Thanks to owner Bruce Braun, Brian, Connie
(Brian's Dad) and I got to ride all over town in the event parade in his newly
restored 1944 VW Schwimwagen!  What a gas that was!

As an update, "the Weather Channel" is now forecasting a cold front to move in
on Wednesday, and that the daytime high temps in the BNNTA area ought to be in
the mid-80's throughout the Labor Day holiday weekend!!!!!  We may be getting
another great weather break for the third year in a row!  They also mention
the chance for isolated t-storms.

The Corps of Engineer rangers at the Indian Creek Recreational Area "group
use" campground have again graciously offered to open the gates for early
arrivals sometime mid-day on Thursday (a day early!), saving any early
arrivals a campsite fee over at the main public campground.  Everything is
shaping up to make for another memorable VW camping weekend.  Just thought
you'd want to know the latest info on this weekend's VW festivities.  Also,
all makes and types of VW's are welcome, not just Busses!

Hope to see you soon!
