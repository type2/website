The engines are the same except for the heads (bigger valves for high end)
and camshaft.  The reason the Porsche type 4s rev higher is because the cars
are lighter, no bottom end differences.  

FI can be a PITA to trouble shoot and fix $ if it does not work properly
from the beginning.  You still need to get an adapter for the transmission
from Kennedy Engineering.    The tinware most certainly will not line up
right, so you will butcher the Porsche stuff or end up using some of the Joe
Cali tricks or Oregon Performance parts.

It may be cheaper in the long run to go with a used VW type 4 engine, a 1.8
would be super cheap/free, and get the conversion kit for $400ish.  Put in a
WebCam camshaft and lifters and you are good to go.  

One day I hope to drop a 1.8 or 2.0 type 4 conversion in my bus so I wish
you luck, but I do not think the 912 E engine is the way to go because of
the extra hassle.  Save the rare stuff for a Porsche Nazi anyway, the VW
crowd steals enough crap from them already.  Leave the Fuchs on the
Porsches!
