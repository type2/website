If VW thought in their infinite wisdom that DP were worse off than SP heads
they would have discontinued their use on production vehicles and went with a
modified SP that could produce the same HP. Problem is, the production SP
heads are as good as they get. The 30/31 carb set up on the SP vs the 34 carb
on the DP do not compare. Even when they tried dual 32's on the 66 T-3 SP they
got no more useable HP. The intake on a DP is larger and optimum for longevity
in its application. Speaking of application, they only tried it on the 71 T-2
and then went to the T-4 1700 because there wasn't enough power for the weight
with the geared up trans. Still worked good in T-1 and T-3 to 1979.

Hi John,
SP will deliever more torque, while DP will result in more HP at much higher
RPMs.  For a bus the SP and its resulting increased torque over DP is
absolutely needed.  At least this seems to be the rule of thumb for engines
up to at least 1776cc size.  For even more torque go with more stroke of the
crankshaft and less bore.
