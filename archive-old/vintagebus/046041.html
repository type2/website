first, seal work best when they have a nice smooth surface to seal against.   
grit and rust bits (holes ?) create a non optimum sealing surface.   I tend 
to clean it up - at least the bottom shelf and the corners, wire wheel 
completely to metal, rust fix stuff, primer, spot putty to fill itty pits, 
sand smooth, redo 1st steps, paint, all these have to dry .  if really bad 
welding needs to be done, 

anyway when a nice smooth surface is there, I use roofing tar on the 
windsheild ledge, and oil in the seal where the glass goes.  The oil helps 
get the seal on the glass easily, as well as repel water.  anyway, the tar 
does not tend to harden, but can mush and reseal.   any extra after 
installation can be easily wiped off with rags and WD-40.  yes WD-40.  of 
course the tar can stain fresh white paint visably if left in place for days, 
but just use WD-40 and wipe it clean after install.
