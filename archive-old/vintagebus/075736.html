> 3.The heater channels running from the exchangers to the cam were encased in

McMasterCarr sells a closed-cell elastomer sleeving, the inside diameter
of which should fit your existing ductwork. You can cut it, and tape/clamp it
around the ducts as needed. I replaced the entire line from heat muffler
to cabin duct-entry with a single run of silicone impregnated hosing and
covered with elastomer insulating sleeving, cut into parts to accomodate
the framework. I POR'd the sleeving as well. The heating upfront is
excellent now. The materials are a bit pricey, but it sure worked! If you
choose to go this route, measure your frame/hardware/etc, and make sure
that the ID/OD's that I picked below are correct for you. With a full
replacement, the measures below I believe were correct for me (1994).
Make sure the ID of the sleeving is firm on the OD of the hose (you have
several to choose from in each catagory - McMaster catalog).
