If the clutch cable breaks, the pedal stays on the floor, and you can easily 
lift it up, and drop it down.  But it won't do anything to the actual clutch, 
as the cable connecting the clutch to the pedal is kaput, ya?
   You can check the cable at the back- if you can pull it back it's broken.
      If the return spring, which is located at the at the side of the 
tranny, (around the arm the cable goes theorugh that has the eye the wingnut 
is on the rear side of) if broken, the pedal would pop up partway.    

    Sounds like your pedal is stuck? The pedal is hard to push in/pull up?  
In that case you need to disconnect the clutch cable from the pedal, remove 
the pedall, remove the ivot assembly, and grease all moving parts.

Haven't done this for a while, but I think I used a drift and hammer.    It 
is fairly sturdy.
