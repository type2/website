  I wondered about this a lot.   I got some parts of a dead 62 that had m code
195.  I purused various lists of m-codes, published my VW and also published
by other people and confusingly paraphrased form the lists published by VW.
I consulted with D Easterwood who was compiling the neato m code survey and he
had listed I think about 8 with m 195 all f which were around 61 62 or 63.   I
thought it was the sliding drivers seat on the 62 walkthrough, which was odd
to me when I saw it in the 62 M195 parts bus.   Contributing to this theory is
the tendency of "like" modifications to be grouped with each other, and the
swivel drivers seat is M199.    But some of the buses with m 195 in the neato
survey were bench seat buses, or even single cabs or double cabs.    And then
found out that a sliding drivers seat was standard on walkthroughs from july
of 61- a year before non walkthroughs got a sliding drivers seat. - with it's
own unique seat july 61 to july 62, so though it was nice to solve that puzzle
it still left the m 195 unsolved.   Bs seemed like your basic mouse grey/white
37 year old standard micro that had had a custom homemade wooden highroof aka
methlab buildt in it, then been pushed round a field with a bulldozer and
finally  left in the rain to me.   I put the kick panels in my 61, cause they
were the right colour.
