Subject: busted shock mount!
Date: Tue, 21 Mar 2000 17:13:22 -0800

Pulling into my driveway last week, I cut the corner a little short and
hopped the curb with my right rear wheel. I heard a clunk, but thought
nothing of it. So today after work I'm adjusting the brakes and after
pulling the right rear wheel (I have those white "baja" style rims with no
adjustment hole, so I've got to pull the wheel off to adjust them) and I
can't believe what I see! The upper mount on the shock absorber is not
welded to the frame anymore! It's still bolted to the shock, but just
floating there in mid-air! And it's the cleanest break - the weld stuck to
the mount and the place where it mounts is smooth as hell...errr...smooth as
a bell. Actually, it welds to the outside of the engine compartment, not the
frame. What a bummer :-....(   Guess it's time to find someone who can weld.

BTW - speaking of adjusting the brakes, an old German VW mechanic in Santa
Barbara once told me: after adjusting each shoe, hop in and give the pedal a
good stomp or two - kind of resets everything - then go and check again and
readjust if necessary. I haven't noticed much difference when doing this on
old brakes, but after installing new ones (or changing anything inside
there) it makes a difference. It kind of sets everything back into "working"
position.
