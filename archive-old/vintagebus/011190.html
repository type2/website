Hi AL,
Your local hardware store carries home door sealant foam tape.  We use the 
stuff that is about 1/2" thick and 1/2" to 5/8" wide, a dark grey color that 
is almost black with large bubbles in it bigger than a pin head or three in 
diameter and is very soft/plush feel to it.  There are foam tapes with much 
smaller foam bubles and much harder, which will not conform as well to the 
tin.  The big secret in installing this foam is to not just tightly pull the 
foam tape over the top corners of the cooler.  Instead you want to back off a 
little slack (1/8" to 1/4") at each top corner for a square shoulder look to 
prevent a sloped shoulder look which will allow some of the cooling air to 
slip by the outside of the cooler.  Also apply two strips of this foam tape 
to either end (Left and Right sides of the cooler in a verticle postion and 
connect these at the bottoms with a horizontal stip between them.  Then 
install one or two more strip vertically between the first two vertical 
strips.
