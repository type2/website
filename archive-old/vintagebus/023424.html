Hi Lee,

I too am struggling with overheating and need to pull my engine and track it
down... recently had to cancel a 5,000 mile trip due to it running 250 when
it should have been running 230 or less :)

Anyway -- you seem primarily focused on your distributor, that is pretty
easy to tell.  Go out and purchase or borrow a strobe light and watch it
advance with the engine running.  That will totally eliminate your concerns
in this area, as at 3500rpm you should be fully advanced (with most any
distributor).  You could always rev (at idle) to 4000rpm to really be sure
-- if you can stand being that close to the engine :)

At full advance, where it will go no further, you should be at or bellow 32
degrees.

If you have a stock pulley instead of a degree wheel, just mark some spots
as a point of reference and you can look at them more closely after you stop
the engine.

You can purchase a strobe for $30 or less if you shop around, I advise it --
as total advance is often a much better point of reference than the "base"
advance at 0 RPM.

What is your carb, how is it jetted?  How is your muffler?  Make sure none
of those carb to dizzy hose lines are leaking air, as that would be a cause
of overheating too.

Good luck.
