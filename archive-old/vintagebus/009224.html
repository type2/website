You know Erin, I am right there with you on this one, as I am seriously
contimplating the sale of my '67 for the same reasons.

A couple of weeks ago I took my '67 to an appraiser to get the necessary
paperwork for my insurance company (it apprasied for just under $6000;
egad!).   He asked me what my restoration plans were for the bus; that's
when the same sinking feelings hit me as well.

18 months ago, I realized I needed a hobby.  When I was a kid in high school
(the stone age if you ask my teenage kids) my friends and I  used to work on
POS cars out of necessity.  I used to enjoy doing it, and decided this is
what I wanted to do to escape from my 'day job' as a geek.  I decided on VWs
because they were relatively easy to work on, didnt need alot of spacialized
tools; finding the '67 with a solid body (in need of serious mechanicals)
finally sent me on this path after a brief stint with a '69 ghia (which is
on its way to the crusher soon if I cant find a buyer).  In the past 10
months I have rebuilt the engine, bearings, brakes and a bunch of other
stuff (electrical, etc; no AM radio _yet_) that I can play with after work
and on the weekends.

I am not a body guy, nor do I really (I think) have serious desires to
become one.  I dont have the skills, space, tools (I tackled the majority of
the engine work with the Muir book, my general mechanical knowledge and a
set of wrenches I received for Christmas on the floor of my garage) and most
importantly TIME to devote to this kind of effort.

I feel the bus (very original with a few rust spots; looks alot like yours
except in blue) deserves a major body overhaul to be preserved for the next
33 years.  I could drop a zillion dollars and turn it over to a body shop,
but that would mean a) I would not have the pleasure of driving it to work
every day (and I smile every time I turn the key)  and b) it would cease
being a hobby and become something that I am throwing money at.

I too dont know how to proceed. All I know is that I am going to drive it
every day (smiles feel pretty darned good) and keep plugging away at
mechanicals.  I guess that if the right price comes around, I'll sell it and
move on to another VW (Things look like alot of fun; a camper is something
else I would like to work on as well).

Oh, and I am playing the lottery every week as well.  I could then quit my
job and move to a double-wide trailer with a 7bay garage located in the
middle of nowhere.  Then I would have time to try body work on the '67 while
driving a Thing to the parts store.
