Many people do not realize that not all Dansk heat exchangers are the same.
Dansk makes two different quality levels.  These low-end Dansk boxes are a
definate example of "you get what you pay for."  You'd might as well save
your money and just buy J-tubes. I won't even list them on my website. The
heavy duty Dansks (which I sell for $99) are reasonably decent quality,
however.

But by far your best deal are my N.O.S. German VW heater boxes for $179 a
pair, while I have them left.  These blow away even the "good" Dansks by a
wide margin, and for less money.  But there's a catch.  The tab for the
heater cable is set up for a '68-71 bus.  To make the heater lever work
correctly in a splitty you'd have to do minor modification, i.e. weld or
bolt another extension tab onto the linkage similarly to what Dansk's heat
exchangers (which are "universal bug/bus") have. Or if you're lazy like me,
just wire the boxes open in the winter with a piece of wire, which was what
I did on my '63 deluxe, figuring that during an east coast winter there
would be no situation where I _wouldn't_ want my heat on; in the spring I
just snipped the piece of wire.  In either case, modifying the linkage is
well worth the effort because you will not find anything else approaching
this quality for this price.
