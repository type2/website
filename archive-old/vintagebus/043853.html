 I made this same point in the archives years ago, and no-one could give
me a definitive answer. I drew all kinds of ascii art, and explained
leverage.

Unless the force is centered between the bearings, the bearings will
actually see more force due to leverage and torsion as compared to a long
axle where leverage is not such a factor. If you look at a bus lower stub
axle it is much longer away from the outer lower bearing than it is
between the bearings.  Did you ever wonder why the lower outer bearing
always fails first? If VW had designed it so they shared the load  
perfectly wouldnt you see an even distribution of failures between the two
lower bearings? 

If you can tell me *exactly* why with even forces the lower outer bearing
almost always fails before the lower inner bearing, then I will believe
that you truly have a psychic connection into the minds of the VW
engineers and do know exactly what they intended.

Otherwise, I'll stick with logic and figure the forces must be uneven for
such a lopsided failure mode to occcur.
