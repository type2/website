That in itself should be setting off flashing red lights and klaxon  blaring 
in you mind!!  Even slightly resting your foot on the brake  pedal will 
prevent the relief valve/s in the MC to stay closed, and with each  braking increase 
brake fluid pressure till brake shoes burn up or wear down  and blow out one 
of the wheel/slave cylinder.  So without a brake pedal  return spring the 
brake pedal pushes agains the MC!

plunger seems like its slightly engaged (i.e. slightly pushed  in).  I assume

Eric when in the USAF got a MC for our 1963 SC from the VW  dealership in 
Abilene TX and had the same problem you describe.  Turned out  the pedal stop 
that keeps the brake pedal from coming up had to be VERY slightly  bent forward 
to allow for the proper spacing between the MC and brake pedal push  rod.  
Without the spacing you will have the very same affect of using the  brake pedal 
as a foot rest!
  We hope you have been using a dual MC all this time!  Otherwise  you lost 
all braking ability each time the wheel/slave cylinder blew out.
