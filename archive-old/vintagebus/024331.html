Treating the oil strainer gasket with gasket shellac 
may not be such a great idea, as it tends to become 
hard with time and apparently hard to remove at overhaul time. 
Apparently it works for Kyle though, YMMV. 

If you are using a full flow external oil filter, you won't 
need to remove the cover for long period of time and a 
different approach may be in order, such as an elastomeric 
sealant of some sort, such as a spray. 

However, what I've always done is much simpler, and
much more . .uh . . tactile . . . .
learned sometime long long ago . . . . . . 
maybe I wanted to continue playing with mud . . 

I apply a thin layer of grease to both sides of the gasket. 
Skooge a little onto your thumb and index finger, place 
gasket between and CAREFULLy pull/turn the gasket through 
your greasy finger/thumb, it feels so good. Keep the 
layer thin and let it sit for awhile to soak in. Rub 
off any excess and install. 

Rather than just say "this is what I do" 
I'll include a "why I think this works" too. 

Paper gaskets absorb oil over time and eventually ooze oil. 
Applying a thicker material (grease) blocks 
the thinner oil. It also helps hold the gaskets in 
place while getting gasket/strainer/gasket/cover 
in place while reaching for the out of reach nuts.
