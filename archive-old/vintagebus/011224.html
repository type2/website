We just take a large washer in between the left thumb and forefinger and slip 
it on the bolt.  Then do the same with the nut giving it a clockwise rotation 
to engage the threads.  This can be done by one person, but is alot easier if 
you have a helper under the bus to push the bolt rearward.  With the engine 
out we weld a locking arm to the bolt head to go over the tranny bellhousing 
to lock it from rotating and drill and to tap a bolt into the bell housing 
and attach a spring to the head of the engine mounting bolt and to this new 
bolt to spring load the left upper engine bolt.
