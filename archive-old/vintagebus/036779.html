Hi Thom,
Wel we wanted it to be as easy as possible to remove and replace in case of 
repairs on the road.  With the old 1960-63 reductions we replaced a set of 
reduction bearings twice while on long trips.  The second time at a rest stop 
we did the whole job in just under two hours!  So when we went to the later 
reductions we drilled the center of the plugs, and filed a hex hole in them.  
Then simply welded a half thickness nut into the plug with a sheet metal 
cover welded on the inside of this nut.  Thus making it possible to just 
thread in a reduction box bolt, and using a claw hammer to extract the plug 
with one easy tug!  So far no leaks there for about 40,000+ miles, and three 
years in our panel camper, and the same time but alot less miles in our 
friends Sundial.
