IF you look on the underside of the engine, you can see where the casting
was made thicker to accommodate the stud.  It's really quite deep.  You
can easily go 15mm deep without a problem.

Hint:  if you're using standard twist drills do _not_ drill a pilot hole
in the case.  Just use the tap drill and do your best to keep it centered.
The magnesium alloy is brittle and will grab the tap drill if the pilot
hole is present.  I had nighmares the first time I did this (with pilot
hole) but managed to avoid breaking the casting.  The second case (without
pilot hole) was very easy.
