Hi AL,
Charge the battery up whislt you remove the fuel pump and check the pump 
pushrod for proper hieght.  Then if the pump is one of the early types one 
can dissassemble take it apart.  Look for damaged diaphram, stuck valves, 
etc..  If the pump is one of those types you can not dissassemble or you put 
back together the type you can dissasseble, take a plastic fuel filter and 
cut off half of the filter to work as a funnel.  Try pouring a couple of 
teaspoons of fuel into the funnel hooked to the inlet of the pump.  While you 
turn the engine over see if the fuel gets sucked into the pump and begins 
coming out.  If the fuel goes in use the funnel made out of a filter to pour 
about 1/2" cup of fuel into the inlet to the carb.  Hook all the hoses back 
up properly and try to start the engine.
