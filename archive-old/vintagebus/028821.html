The limiting factor in all VW engines is the crank shaft. 

Bill Fisher's book "How to Hotrod Volkswagen Engines" claims
that the stock crank can take continuous operation up to
5,000 rpm. It also claims bursts to 5,500 for shifting gears
is ok.

It continues to relate that in tests ran by Gene Berg, use
of a stock crank limited to 6,500 rpm wrecked an engine in
2,500 miles.
