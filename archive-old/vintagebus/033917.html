well, That double door (yup that's what people call em with the cargo doors
both sides) if kinda nifty.  Tends to be worth a bit more than the regular
style.  But beware, they have a double floor, for reinforcement, and this
helps catch and hold water and rust out.  Look underneath-- there should be
panels welded in under the regular corregated floor.  

Alot of panels were made into campers, either by companies such as Riviera,
Sundial, etc or by individual people.    In the seventies it was unfortunately
common to cut out the cab partition and also cut the wheel wells (not to
mention shag carpet, extra windows, and painting everything root beer brown.)

I would say look behing the pass. seat for the m code tag, but as the
partition is gone, you are out a luck.

Well, it being solid is important, esp in Michigan where I hear cars
disintigrate in about 5 years due to salting the roads.   Partitions can be
replaced.  What do you want to do with a bus?   Sunday drives?  Picnics?
Summer driver?   Camper? Show car?  Something to tinker with on the weekends?
Do you like this one?   Does it have a good vibe?  Will you give it TLC and
accept it's limitations?  Are there many to choose from?
