>So I'm going to tighten up my rear wheel nuts and I thought I read somewhere
>about how to figure out how much torque I am applying.  That is, by knowing
>my body weight and the length of the pipe I have welded to the socket ... ya
>know?

Torque = force * distance from center force is applied

If you weigh 175 pounds and are putting all your weight on the pipe
a foot and a half from the socket, you are developing 263 pound feet
of torque.  150 pounds at nine inches would be 113 pound feet.  If 
you've got both feet on the pipe, I'd say you'd be safe to take the
center point between your feet as the place you're applying the force.
