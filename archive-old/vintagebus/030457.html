Try checking out www.movecars_dot_com for a nationwide listing of auto
carriers' websites.  This site also contains some points to consider
when choosing a carrier.  Some of the linked websites will give online
estimates.  Note also:

1.  Some of these companies are only brokers, not truckers.  Using them,
your vehicle may pass through many hands (making progress difficult to
track) by the time it reaches you.  Which could take a couple of weeks,
all told.
2.  Covered transport is more expensive.  If you go uncovered, plan to
give your bus a good degreasing when it arrives (diesel soot).
3.  More risk on your part = less cost
3.  Based on my own experience of 2 years ago, I roughly estimate a
typical coast-to-coast shipping of a VW bus to be $1000 now.
