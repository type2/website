    It appears that Maisto (the company that makes the R&T models) bought the
original body molds from Matchbox/Lesney of England...  I have a NOS Matchbox
#34 in the original box from the late '60s and it is identical to the new R&T
model (as far as the body goes).  The undercarriage of the Matchbox model has
no detailing at all, so Maisto did their own thing there. The "hinges" are
solid where the Matchbox actually has cargo doors that open...

    What's kinda neat is Matchbox came out with three different versions of
the Splittie Camper under the #34 model number...  A flat panel roof
"Caravelle" in a kinda puke green with green interior in the late '50s, a
silver Camper (with low raised roof, no windows) in the early '60s, and the
camper (again in silver) with high raised roof with side/front/back windows
in the the late '60s...  I have primo examples of all three...  The Maisto
version sits right next to them on the shelf and it fits right in...

    The Golf/Rabbit GTI is also a Matchbox in disguise...
