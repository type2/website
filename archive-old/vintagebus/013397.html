No, the Department of Defense (who owns the satellites) adds error to
the signal to stop folks from using the "consumer" signal for
targeting their weapons, at least that is the theory.  The "military"
signal has an accuracy measured in fractions of a centimeter!

You can buy "differential GPS" units that correct the error with an
additional signal from a ground-based commercial transmitter, but
these only work where you can receive the additional signal.
