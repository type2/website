Hi Sam,

But are these LT tires?  Are they 6 ply?  Anything that is 1/2 the price --
sounds like passenger tires (4 ply) to me.  What is the max PSI, 35 or 50?

Which raises the issue of Splits:  especially the older 3/4 TON models as
opposed to later models that are 1 TON.

There may be 15" tires out there that have the load capacity but not the
stronger sidewall.  It would seem that we don't have the double whammy
issues of the 14" VW tire users... 

Especially on the late loaf westy, there are few tires out there in proper
size at 14" that can carry full load.  On my '71 Loaf I have 195/75/14 and
they are 6 ply but not near the load capacity.

In other words -- passenger tires or "non 6 ply truck tires" may actually be
able to carry the load on a 15".  But as David Raistrick pointed out -- for
those of us spending more $ to deal with driving in cross winds (or being
passed by large trucks), we need the 50PSI tires.  A Honda SUV may hold up
with 4 ply tires, but are those things square and take wind like our beloved
VW bus?

My experience is that you pay $10 to $40 more to get stiffer 50 PSI tires.
