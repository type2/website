If you can afford it, I would try the Tie-Coat stuff.  I used the Dupont
Prime-n-Seal, and although I have yet to test it on the road for
durability,
I have two comments.  That stuff is NASTY!  You MUST wear supplied air
while
using it, it seems to be concentrated evil.  Also, you have to spray
your
topcoat within 12 hours of spraying the primer/sealer, else you've got
to
scuff it before topcoating.  That can be an issue, but not too bad of
one.

I am a sucker for good advertizing, and if the Tie-Coat/POR-15
combination
is as tough as they say it is, (or even half), then I would go with
that...You'd "never" have to go below your topcoat to refinish or
repair!
"Impervious to rust" and all that....
