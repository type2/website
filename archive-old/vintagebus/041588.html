To clarify, I did not comment on the quality of these shifter extensions,
which I assume is excellent.

What I was attempting to get across is that many folks advertise that
their parts are made of "aircraft aluminum" and bandy about the grade
numbers as if this somehow made that parts better.  Unless you happen to
know what the grade numbers mean, all you really know is that the part is
made of aluminum.  The range of strengths is tremendous, and you don't
always want the strongest material, because the strongest is also the most
fatigue-prone.

I didn't mean to offend, just to educate.
