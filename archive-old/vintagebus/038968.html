I've got a '67 Kombi, which is apparently the first year they had
an "emergency flasher" feature.  This is the switch on the dash that
supposedly makes all four turn signals flash at once to warn that the
vehicle is a road hazard.  Useful feature for a 31-year-old Bus..

Turning on the emergency flasher promptly blows a fuse -- but the turn
signals work fine.  Both are controlled by a big blue relay box labeled
"Blinkgeber" (trans:  "blinker") with about a dozen wires coming into
it, including feeds from the turn signal switch.

I've started to trace the circuit and the wiring (the Bentley has a page
describing the how the "new" circuit hooks up), but has anyone already
done this and constructed a schematic of the Blinkgeber's guts?  It
looks like a fairly straightforward system of relays and a couple of
transistors, perhaps to control the relays.

BTW, the E-flasher switch simply grounds a lead into the Blinkgeber box
that energizes a multi-switch relay which reconfigures the whole
circuit, presumably into emergency-flasher mode, so the switch itself
shouldn't be blowing a fuse.  The light bulb circuit inside the switch
is fine.
