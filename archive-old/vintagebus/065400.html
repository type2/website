On Mon, 6 Jan 2003, Scott Fraser wrote:

> When I first got my DC, it sat somewhat low and I checked the adjustment
> ala bentley also.  Got the degreed level out and went to town.  It still
> seemed to sit somewhat low.  I think I went back and added one or two
> "clicks" and was happy with it.

By "clicks" do you mean splines?  I so, one spline would be around nine
degrees, a heck of a jump!  Much finer adjustments can be made by moving
the bar one spline in one direction on one end and one spline in the other
direction at the other end.
>
> I did come across a lowering web page once where they were messing with the
> rear torsion bars.  I vaguely remember something about heating the bars or
> something to allow them to droop more.  I also wonder if with age, these
> bars develop a slight twist to them?

Bars do develop a "set" which is why you never switch sides with them, but
once they settle they tend to stay that way for a very long time.  Heating
springs for lowering is done with coil springs by hack "customizers".
Since VWs have built-in adjustment splines, and you would have to pull
the bars out anyway to heat them, even the densest customizer would not
be likely to resort to heating VW springs.
