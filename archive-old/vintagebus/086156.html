While you can do a straight up install of the 62-67 tank in a no gas guage 
bus, I would definately keep the reserve tap.   It is nice to have that 'there 
is only one gallon left' notice, as gas guages are not always exactly accurate. 
 :)  Plus you can shut off the gas for engine pulls or fuel line replacement, 
which helps to avoid fuel pouring on you and fire risk.   The fuel tap will 
attach to a later tank just like an earlier one.

Personally, I find the lack of gas guage in 61 & earliers no problem.   I 
also keep a record book with fill-ups in each bus.   And the reserve does give 
you pre-run-out notice.  I have considered retrofitting a fuel tap in a gas 
guage bus, actually, just never got around to it.

If you really want the guage, I'd keep the tap, and install the guage in a 
bracket rather than cutting the dash.
You need to pull the motor to replace the tank.
