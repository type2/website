>  << 1) Should i use Mig or oxy acetylene( I have both) can you also

>  acetylene for brazing results in paint bubbling in a year, or two due to

I mostly use MIG with 30 mil wire.  I also do brazing with a 0 tip on heat 
sensitive and delicate areas (read thin metal), but the flux problem in new 
to me, and seems to make sense due to the acid.  I sandblast and clean with 
solvent after brazing. E&B think this will help with the flux problem?  I 
think Bob Hoover was mentioning that he was doing a lot of body brazing, 
somethin about experimenting with brazing.

>  <<3) do you use sandblasting for rust removal>>
>  Yes, and sandpaper, wire wheels, phosperic acid, and lots of elbow sweat.
If I can see it, I sandblast with Dupont StarBlast, try it.  Found the black 
beauty too corse.  I use a pressure blaster from www.tiptools_dot_com, very good 
product at fair price.  I have had no problem with panel distortion. Nozzel 
size is completely compressor capacity driven, and I use a fairly small 
nozzel (3/32).  Tip Tools make a nice unit for this size, and I don't need 
much more for sheet metal.
