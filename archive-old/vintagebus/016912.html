Hi Isaiah,

Run a compression check
That will tell you if you have one...(said hole in piston)

If the MoFoCo engines are as bad as you say, maybe the ring gaps are 
lined up all in a row during assembly rather than spaced 1/3 of the
circumference apart from each other and he engine is pumping oil into the
cylinder...

If the valve clearances are set loose this oil pump up will occur and
foul the plug and spit oil into the exhaust.

The engine will not smoke until the heat from the other cylinders
increase temps to flash point.

If all four cyl  are good with in 10% of each other on compression check,
pull spark plug wires individually with an insulated set of grips and
notice any RPM drop at idle. No rpm drop indicates a non functioning
cylinder.

Because it's not your engine don't trust plug readings as they may give a
false history
