Why not take this one step further and make the heating system
recirculating?  Run a duct from the back of the passenger compartment
into the engine bay.  Connect this duct to an electric fan and the
output from the fan to the heat exchangers.

This way, the exchangers get already-warmed air from the passenger
compartment, rather than having to heat cold outside air.

The intake for the fan could be located behind the spare tire, where
it is out of the way and unlikely to be blocked inadvertantly.

You would also have to provide a way to keep air flowing through the
heat exchangers when the heat is off.  You could just leave the fan on
"low" speed all the time, or you could work out some sort of "flapper
valve" that would allow air from the engine fan to go to the boxes
unless the electric fan starts blasting away.
