First of all, shoot some WD40 on the adjustment threads. Get someone to
depress the clutch pedal, then jam the clutch arm in place with a
screwdriver or something, and let go of the pedal (carefully). Now you
have some slack in the cable to work with.

I''ve got a dandy little tool for adjusting the clutch from RMMW. It's a
small cross shaped wrench (about 2" square) with a slot in it that fits
over the wingnut. Works much better than fooling around with vice
grips...
