> How does one bend the tubing to create perfect arches for corners and 
ladders?

TIP: Using a tube bender will help to prevent flatening and kinks when 
bending tubes.  By "tube bender" here I am not talking about a hydraulic 
press used for mufler pipes, etc., but rather the tool which looks like a 
spring and is designerd to fit over the tube at the bend.  It is often used 
to bend copper tubing for plumbing.  They come in different sizes, so you 
need to use one which fits the tube to be bent.  Since you are bending 
aluminum, which has softness similar to copper, it should work well, although 
I have never used one on aluminum tube.  This tool does nothing to control 
the radius of the bend, just prevents kinking.

NOT a tip:  I usally just find something around the shop that has the 
approximate radius I need and go at it!  There is probably a good tool for 
controlling radius, but I don't have one.  I have found that the kinks are a 
lot more important to prevent rather than the tube radius, especially with 
soft metal which you are going to fix in place anyway.
