Subject: On the road! As always carb questions.

Picked up my bus from Lehamns' Auto in PA on Rt100. Tim did good work, got a
bus running that I couldn't. Seems all my carbs were clogged to bad
internally. I am going to ultrasound them to try and fix that in the future.
Got a new 30/31 (there goes original stock I was after). Engine is running
now, drove it 30 miles home, and to work today, good power and cool running,
I could hold dipstick forever afterward. Will get empirical readings asap
with laser infra thermometer. Gentle driving and lots of oil changes to
follow before long drives.

The low compression/high quench and ENGLE 100 cam resulted in huge
Hydrocarbon levels at idle 3000ppm. CO is 3.8, and tailpipe calculated AF
ratio is 17-18. Jets are stock 120 60 65 and I think a 135z? I'll have to
tear up my outher 30/31 to check the ones they come with. Throttle screw had
to be bumped to get a 900 rpm idle speed. Air cleaner is stock 66 bus.
I am think ing I need a larger air correction jet to get more atomization of
charge for the 1600 with 7.1:1 compression with this engle 100 cam, to help
get a cleaner burn at idle, with more conrol by the bypass screw for idle
speed, and closer to the magic 14.7 AF at the tailpipe. After some breakin,
I want to try going for this.

Questions:
If an idle speed produced by bypass screw is insiffcient for an engine with
this cyl volume and cam profile, does that entail I need more air AND fuel?
Could a 30/31 be too small to be ideal? I am strongly considering that a
larger air correction jet is in order, perhaps to allow for larger jets as
well. Seems I need a leaner/more atomized idle burn to bring CO and
hydrocarbons down, and for a naturally faster idle, with better/more control
by bypass screw.
Running of the main on thottle seems just wonderful, so I am close to a good
set up here, as hydrocarbons decrease into the high 1000ppm, and CO drops

Power and climbing hill at 37mph in third is good, a queit, responsive and
cool running engine.
