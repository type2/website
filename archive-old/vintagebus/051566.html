I had the same issue, concerns aboput the 'hole' and the grease fitting.
I was refurbishing/rebuilding OE 66 spindles, lucky enough I was reusing
all the parts. Did a soak in thinner, and used a pressure grease gun to
blow out and check each fitting, completely disasemmbled (but no with
bushing pressed out), got out nasty grease, recleaned the bores,
regreased, and assembled. The hole doesn't have to line up, as long as
grease gets around. Pumping it through disassembled to check, clean,
flush out the old crap worked well for me, also gave clean grease to
prelube to assemble.
Since you have wasted (maybe?) needle bearings, you would need new
bushings anyway to match, so you have the change to line them up real
well, I imagine, mine were all very close from factory (guess they had a
good day).
*I agree, don't drill.
I know 63's had a lot of specific parts, but am no help on brake
happiness and spindle fits. I do recommend double checking the shimming,
and replacing any bushings that the needle bearing is toast on. If they
are good, make sure not to move them around, or they wonj't be good
long.
You might want to do (rebush, replace) all of them, but I know that is a
lot more work.
Al
