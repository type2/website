Rick Taylor wrote:
> 
> This does sound like a good idea and I might suggest something that may
> work even better.  Around the rural areas, we are building large metal
> barns that have a wooden frame.  In order to attach the metal to the frame
> we use a hex headed metal screw that has a drill bit tip and on it's head a
> rubber washer.  Using a cordless screw driver, we drive this into the sheet
> metal and they drill their own hole through the metal and into the wood.
> The rubber washer seals the screw (there is a shoulder on the screw head
> that acts as a washer) against the sheet metal.  These screws do have a
> powder coating on them so they won't rust.
> 
> You can find these screws with the self drilling tip and rubber sealing
> washer at most well stocked lumber yards.
> 
> >These were actually #12 sheet metal screws with hexhead tops so I could
> >tighten them down (from underneath) with a mini socket wrench.  They
> >held quite well in this wood.  By pre-drilling, I meant that I drilled
> >smaller-diameter guide holes into the undersides of the slats, to
> >prevent splitting the wood with the screws.  Hope that clears things up
> >for you.
