Hi Joe,
CAREFULLY use an Easyout to remove the insert.  If it does not remove easily 
drill it out and clean out the remains.  You can buy 31/64" drill bits with 
turned down shanks at specialty shops or hardware stores or have you drill 
bit turned down at a local machine shop that does small jobs.  Though we 
suggest just buying a low speed drill with a 1/2" chuck.  We just bought 
another vintage one for $25.00 at a drive in swap meet and bought a new chuck 
key for it at our FLHWS.
  Then drill the hole deeper with a 1/8-1/4" drill bit then use the 31/64" 
drill bit with drill motor or drill the hole with the biggest drill bit you 
have for a 3/8" chuck and then drill by hand with the 31/64" drill bit.  When 
ever drilling anything over 1/2" it is alot easier to first drill with a 
small bit and then the bigger drill bit does not have so much back pressure 
from it's larger cutting center tip.
  Bet you just did not drill the hole deep enough for the tap to full form 
threads all the way down and the insert hit these and stopped before seating 
all the way down.
