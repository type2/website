 Also talked about why the buses have reduction gearing boxes.   WHy is that?  AFAIK, it is because when they designed the bus, they used as many parts as possible that they already had.  to make it as cheaply and quickly as possible.   The same motor as a bug.  The same trans and rear axles as a kubel wagon.      The RGB set up gave more ground clearance and also lower gearing.  It was designed for a kubel, which was roughly the same weight as a bug (?) anyway it is a type 1 (I think, not being sure about wartime VWs) for higher clearance and lower gearing for off road use.  They put that in the bus for ground clearance and the lower gearing for pushing a laden delivery van.
 
 Looked up what bearings were used, and the barndoors used the same earings as a later little nut tranny in the RGBoxes.  So the axle bearings in your 63 with little nut were those used in a kubel, which was designed for not so much weight.
 
 Then looked up some weight capacities for buses, and the permissable axle load, rear for a was 1015 kg (2238lbs) for all models, this from a 59 workshop manual for post splitcase buses with little nut rear axles
 
 The 53 barndoors permissable weight load in the rear is 925 kg for all models except pickup, which is 1000kg.

I believe the geometry is also differnt for the barndoor rear axles.  well the axles themselves are the same, though the rear torsion bars are different.   The stub axles are differnt. not sure about the lengths.

 so how does this all work out?  I don't know   Any one have bearing load info?
