The thermocouple itself is a junction of two different metals that
generates a small (millivolts) voltage when heated.  They get used in
pairs - one reference junction and one sensing junction.  Usually in
commercial instruments that use thermocouples, the sensing junction is
in the probe that you attach to the item of interest, and the reference
junction is inside the meter somewhere.

Tip: If you're getting flaky readings, check the connectors.  Thermocouple
probes often have flat ceramic or plastic two-pronged connectors and these
usually have screw terminals inside.  The screws loosen up when the cable
or probe are banged around.
