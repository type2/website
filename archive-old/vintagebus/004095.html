    If you are a restorer then you are stuck with it, at 55 you will go. I
felt endangered almost always in modern traffic and did the following to my
66:
Kept the nice tight 1600, put a doghouse cooler and a mechanical advance
distributor on, spent money on decent extractors,then (all restorers forgive

complete.
Now I have IRS 14" wheels and a top speed of around 70 something MPH  (no
tach yet)
I Love it . It goes, it handles and with the 1970ish bolt in disc brake
front end it stops too.The old hubcaps fit and I can still climb the kerb if
need be without tearing the underneath.
Saving up bux to get it painted and de-rusted next. Heaven.
