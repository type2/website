David,

Bleeding any hydraulic cylinder is easy. But first you need to top up the
fluid first. Use jack oil (do not use brake fluid) that is available from
any FLAPS but don't fill past the top of the piston that you can see in the
fill hole. Once it's full, jack it up as high as it will go, put one of
those kids on it and let it down FAST. You will hear the air leaving the
cylinder at the bottom. Repeat until you don't hear any more air. Rebuilding
will only be necessary if it is leaking. Your FLAPS will have someone
available to do this for you. Good luck.
