Beware! This sounds frighteningly familiar.
I flew to LA to pick up my '64 Sundial, which the seller assured me
was in fine running condition and in plenty good shape to drive back
to Hood CA. He even took it to an LA VW mechanic for a tuneup and
lube. The mechanic didn't see anything amiss. I drove it around LA a
bit, and indeed it seemed just fine. Compression, oil pressure, etc.
was all good, and I thought I had nothing to worry about. But less
than 100 miles outside LA disaster struck: rod through the case. Why?
When I finally got the bus home (trailer) and started tearing into the
deceased engine, I found the spaces between cylinders to be just
packed tight with a hardened mixture of motor oil and sand. Rust-free
desert bus, you see. Had I trailered the bus home in the first place I
could have saved that engine. I had no idea and there was no obvious
sign. I even pulled the engine myself in LA to put in a new generator,
and didn't see the problem. Be careful, if you are going to drive it
home maybe you should pull a little engine tin and get a better look
at the motor you expect to be carrying you home. I had a hellish
journey home (hitch-hiking in USA just plain sucks) and wouldn't wish
that upon anybody.
Good luck, and congratulations on your new bus,
Joel
