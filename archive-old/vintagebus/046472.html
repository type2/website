I like to carry a couple two part push rod tubes and some push rod tube seals 
in my repair kit.    A couple times I have used them- great for doing repairs 
when there is a crack in a push rod tube or the seal is leaking, yoou can 
pull the valve cover and the rocker arm and remove the push rod, then yank 
the old push rod tube pout with a pair of pliers, install the replacement, 
put the push rods back, in go the rocker arms, on with the vlave cover, 
voila, so much easier than removing the head, just check the valve adjustment 
again in a hundred miles or so.  I haven't bothered installing on a motor 
when buildt, just as a time saving repair.
