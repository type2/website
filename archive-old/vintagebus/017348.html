Ahhh....just so you know, there is a big difference between MIG and TIG especially in price.  A quality MIG (metal inert gas) welder from Linclon or Miller will run you around 600 bucks +.  I like my Miller XP130.  It has metal guide rollers that most of the cheaper brands don't.  It's never let me down or jammed once.  

Yes I'd love to have a TIG too, but those run about 1500 and  take a bit more skill to run.  Nothing too hard but still takes more practice than zapping a mig bead.  Still though the quality of a TIG weld is far superior plus the benefit of far less heat buildup and excess weld to grind.  

Stick with the quality machines...stay away from the 299 POS's.
