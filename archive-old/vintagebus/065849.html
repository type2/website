The 8mm square headed screw.  This is often known to be rusted to sh&t.  I 
hope you have been using penetrating oil daily for the last couple weeks, 
having seen how things rust in the UK., it's even worse than here in WA 
state.   The thing is, you might break it off, and have to drill it out, 
while the collar is a softer metal and you don't want to mess it up.   I
know 
the earlier (61 type) collars are available repro, but I don't know about
the 
later collars.    Sometimes I use a propane torch and alternate with a
squirt 
bottle of water to shock the rust loose, then more penetrating oil.   

I have found  that you can mash a 12 point 10mm socket on there, use a short

extension, and it usually works.  If you can find a 8mm square socket you're

stylin.

the square head used to have a wire through it, to wrap around the shift rod

and keep it from undoing on its own, check to make sur ethe wire's gone 
before undoing the set screw.
Always grease liberally when assembling.
