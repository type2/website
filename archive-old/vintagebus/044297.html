I recommend disconnecting the push on wire from the sender and grounding it.  
have your wife or someone turn the ig on (not start motor) and look atthe 
guage.  does it read all the way to full?   if so your guage is working fine 
and your sender is having issues.  

One thing your post makes me wonder if if you have a 15 gal tank and a sender 
for a 10 gallon tank.   If so, once the gas level is below the sender, it 
will read empty, but you will still have maybe 5 gal in the tank.  THis was 
how the 59 truck I had was- the guage only read for the top 10 gallons.    
The sender works fine for 6 or 12 volts, doesn't matter.  6V guages do often 
keep working fine on 12V but again I have heard it shortens their life.

Meanwhile, I recommend keeping a small spiral notebook in the bus, and 
writing down fill ups- milage and how much.  you can calculate your MPG and 
know when you have to put gas in it by.   and also use the book to mark when 
you do tune ups etc.

Gas guages are accessories, you dont really need them.
