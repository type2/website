Stock, your Bus wouldn't have had the "9 prong box" - it would have
had a four-pronged relay, item J1 in the wiring diagram.  See this
link for more:

http://www.type2_dot_com/archive/vintagebus/Pine.LNX.4.10.10104270106490.9611-100000_at_birdbird.birdbirdbird_dot_com.html

>Some odd effects, when the ignition is off but the hazards on, and
>then the brake pedal is applied, the low oil / generator lamps and
>the fuel gauge flash along with the hazards. I think I know why but
>don't care to do anything about it.

This is inherent in the design and there's not much you can do about
it.  A strategically placed diode would fix it, but would also introduce
another 0.6v to 1.0v voltage drop to your brake lights.  If your Bus
is still 6 volt, this means your brake light brightness would be reduced
by 30% to 50%.  A 12 volt Bus would fare a bit better and "only" lose
17% to 30% or so.  Since you need your brake lights more than your
hazard flashers, it's a reasonable trade-off.
