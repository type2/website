> BTW, I noticed some needle valves are stamped 1.5, some 2.5, one I have

Memory says that this is the travel of the valve pin..but i may be off
here.

This is the valve that SHUTS OFF the fuel pump (effectively) when the bowl
is full of fuel. (of course, this depends on the float height being
correct and the needle valve being shimmed correctly.)

If you have to much fuel pressure THIS VALVE STAYS OPEN. (ie: the fuel
pressure forces it open) This causes the bowl to overflow and fuel goes
EVERYWHERE that it can.
