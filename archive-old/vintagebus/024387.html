All,
	Stephen Gutknecht wrote quoting me as saying that 50 psi air pressure
in the front tires on a Bus can create understeer.  If that is what I
wrote(?) what I actually meant was the the 50psi tire pressure will
accentuate an oversteer problem that rear engine vehicles have.  I
recommended to Stephen that he run the front tires at less than 30 psi
to compensate for this oversteer issue.  Try to create a little less
oversteer.  I'm sure that listees with racing experience will wonder how
much I understand about this issue hearing that I think higher pressure
in the front will cuase understeer.  I don't, it won't.  Lower tire
pressure in the front is important for keeping your bus literally on the
road.  Gary Gibson
