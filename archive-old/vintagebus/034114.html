A lot of times the leaks are from the studs or case savers, many people fail
to remove and reseal the studs when rebuilding an engine, another learning
experience for me, about 5 years ago I did an engine, tore it down 3 times
looking for the oil leak, finally ran it on my test stand without shroud,
let it get warm, then saw the leaks!! from the upper studs between the case
and the case savers, LESSON learned. Also I always use high temp rtv sealant
under the jugs, only place you'll ever find silicon on one of my engines.

>Well hey Diana - sorry to hear about the oil pouring out of the engine.
The

>part about the pushrod tubes leaking...did you "extend" the tubes before
>installing them?  They need to have a certain amount of "crush" when you
put

>the heads back on.  If you don't, they never seal quite right.  You fix
this

>by pulling the head, removing the tubes, extending them (very carefully!),

>and cleaning the pushrod tube holes liberally (spic and span!!!).  Upon
>reinstallation, use some kind of gasket sealer on the seals and tube ends
to

>prevent oil leaks.
>
>As for the oil pouring from the cylinders - just before you install them,
>you should put some RTV around the base of the cylinders and then install
>them.  BUT, before you even do this, you need to make sure that the
>cylinders don't slop around in the case.  If it's been bored out
previously,

>there is no way an undersize cylinder is going to seal no matter what
>(besides an obviously creating a gap for oil to shoot from.)  If there is
>ANY slop between the case and the cylinder, they are the wrong size.
