On another vehicle list I am on this very discussion came up.
Being a bunch of seriously diehard motorheads, they discussed
it ad nauseum.  The conclusion is that some decades ago, some
engine builders deliberately left their cases outside to
weather for a while before doing the final machining.

Don't know what difference if would be for magnesium as opposed
to whatever version of iron they used, but it made the cases
more stable & less prone to distortion as a result of the machining.
