Well, they kinda get confused.  I took my bus next door to the Mr. Lube to
top up my tranny oil because my mechanic was way too busy and I didn't feel
like smelling like gear oil for a week.  I pulled in, they asked me the year
of the van, etc. etc.  for the computer, then the guy spent about 5 minutes
frantically thumbing through a big book before I told him that I didn't want
my oil changed and that there was no oil filter anyway, so he could quit
trying to look it up!  Then when I tried to explain the concept of reduction

kind of thing."  But they did manage to grease the front end (or so they
tell me.)  Moral: if you want it done right, you gotta do it yourself.
Although it is kind of funny watching the Mr. Lube guys get more and more
confused.

was an incredibly difficult concept.  And of course they were amazed by the
church key for the gas door.
