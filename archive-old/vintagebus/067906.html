Yes, and that leads to the interesting fact that a real 52X CD drive would
have to spin at such a high rate on the inner tracks that it would fly
apart at something like 30K RPM.  They weasel out of this by only reading
52X on the outer tracks.  There's an interesting page on the 'net
that shows the results of overspeeding a CD.  Google "exploding CD rom" to
find the page.

IIRC, most actual CD drives now spin at constant speed and buffer the
data.  They're cheaper to build that way.
