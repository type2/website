The only way to get any coating to with stand the rigors of the mother
nature especially on the bed of a truck is to use something that penetrates
the wood real well.  Many of the polyurathanes simply coat the surface.  Not
putting a perfect smooth surface helps some.  Oil based products generally
work better than water based ones.

I have a set of Roberts strips on my double cab that I used an oil based
urethane coating... since my truck hasn't left the shop yet, I have no
judgement of how well it will hold up to UV.  However, I have used the same
product on interior kitchen cabinets and it does real well with water and
detergents.  Nothing is gonna look as good after a year as the day you put
it on unless it's garaged or under a canvas top.
