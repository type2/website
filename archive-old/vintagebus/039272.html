I managed to find the time to mess with the bus
and did a partial cleaning.  Not wanting to apply
degreaser and have the resulting muck wash
onto my gravel driveway, I used just plain ol' water.

I got in there with the spray nozzle and sprayed the 
(&$%(& out of the engine bay.  I'm amazed at how
much stuff (non-sand) got cleaned up just by using
water.  The engine bay looks really nice, the years
of accumulated stuff on the ceiling just rinsed off!

I reached way back and flowed water around the 
tank several times, back and forth, etc.  I removed
the rear apron to better reach and provide a place
for the sand to get out.  The right side is clean and
straight, while the left side has had some repairs
done to it.

I'll re-do the cleaning with degreaser after I get it
running and go to a u-do-it wash rack, since they
properly deal with the resulting goo.

I'm confident that I got enough sand out to be able
to work on the fuel pump and distributor without
getting any sand into the internals.
