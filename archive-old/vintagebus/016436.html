Being fair to Lenny, what he is marketing is not
purely birch panels but "convenience and availability

amount of labor and sundry material costs involved,
even if you happened to 'cipher out the correct
dimensions of all of the panels. And no, the Westfalia
roof panels are not exactly square, nor are they the
same... Also go find yourself the long panel in one
singular sheet with the grain running vertically. Even
I didn't do that in the overly touted resto from hell
'67 Westy.

at Home Depot is mearley a micro-paper thin skin
veneer of Birch mounted on one side of a cheapish wood
substrate. (In fact bought some for the EZ camper from
Home Depot last weekend!) The grain on that sheet runs
lengthwise and, as Thom mentioned, will not work for
the headliner panels or properly for the longpanel.
The final appearence does not come up to the same look
as the good schtuff. The nicer stuff comes in a
different size (and thickness if you mic it out...)
and has a thicker surface of nicer White Baltic Birch
on *both* sides. The right stuff also has a better
appearence when sanded and finished with the final
sealer. Past that point I will not infringe on Lenny's
market position of offering a "convenient, complete
source option" for those of us who choose not to
research, skin knuckles, make mistakes and figure it
all out for ourselves. Contact Lenny for more details
