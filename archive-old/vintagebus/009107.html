From time to time a shop doing an oil change on my Type 2 with a 1600SP will
dump in a full 3 quarts of oil, rather than fiddling with precise
measurements.  When this happens, my crank pulley will sling a fine oil mist
until the oil level in the crankcase drops to the normal "Full" level on the
dipstick.  This is on a relatively fresh rebuild, with ~14,000 miles on it,
but I've had this happen on 1600SPs in various stages of their service
lives.

It's annoying, but sure beats a blown front seal.

And before I get flamed for actually paying someone to do an oil change...
