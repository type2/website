Wade,

Looks like you have a version of what is sometimes called a "stationary
55/56" Eberspacher heater.  Consider yourself lucky as it looks all
there and is a good factory install.  I've restored one or two of the
bus versions of these.  When I get some extra time I'll dig out what
manuals I have and copy them for you.   Someone down the line did it for
me and I'd like to share.  The black areas with the wires is the fuse
terminal.  Never seen one broken up in to two separate sections, but
yours is a bit different than the ones I've worked on.  All the parts are
the same, but in different places.  

I've seen a few pictures of one of the early ones installed where yours
is, so I know it is where they put DC and single cab heaters.

I'm late for work, but here are some pics I had Ron Vaness put up on T2
a long time ago.

http://www.type2_dot_com/library/heating/statstev.htm

Here is a brochure someone else posted...bus and not truck, but you see
the similarities.  Hey!  Just noticed this is your heater...look at the
fuse block.

http://www.type2_dot_com/library/heating/oldeber.htm

Read all the gas heater stuff as most of it will relate to the very
basic operation and restoration of the early heaters.  The fuel pump is
usually what does not work on these things.  Contact Rick as someone
else mentioned or the other gas heater gurus off the list.  As a last
resort, I might be able to help if you are desperate.  Cool to actually
see one installed like that.  If it was cold where I'm at I'd build and
install one.  

Steve
