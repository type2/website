Steve, Eric & Barb;
Regarding Seat Belts/Shoulder Belts,
I have no desire to be submarined, but on the other hand, a heavy webbed
belt running up between my legs is sort of intimidating...
My 66 SO-42 has heavy web belts, and non-retracting shoulder belts, the
heavy-web aircraft style belt with manual length adjustment. I have M-code
425 *Seat belts & Mounting Points*, the shoulder belts are anchored aft of
the entry doors with a plate/bolt/nut setup through the reinforcing area
around the door frame. I'm 6'3" and the shoulder belt anchor is just above
eye-level when I'm seated. There is a metal cap (looks original, matches the
body color) on the outside of the bus where the anchoring bolt goes through
the body. The seat belt buckles have the Wolfsburg castle on them and the
webbing is a light beige color. They give a very secure, snug fit, but being
non-retractors it takes some time to adjust for each passenger.
