Richard asked:
>Is it true that one should not use sealants or teflon

>tape on the threads of 
>temperature or pressure senders, as doing so will 
>cause the gauges to read 
>incorrectly?  (The cause being that the selant or
>tape will keep the sender 
>from grounding properly). 

===============

Personally, I don't think the sealant will affect the
ground enough to matter.  But it isn't a good idea to
use teflon tape in any situation where a loose bit of
tape could plug or block an oil passage, so I would us
Loctite teflon sealant or other teflon pipe thread
sealing paste, not tape.
