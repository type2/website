Hello all!

I've been on vacation for a week and I'm going through my back
list mail.  There's been some talk about how VW dealers were
required to throw out/destroy their older spares after some
time.  At least in the US, there is a probably a tax reason 
for this.

A friend of mine is a writer ( http://www.elvendude_dot_com ) and
in the book business, it is common to send warehoused copies
of unsold books to the shredder after a few months.  The
reason is a court decision that made companies liable for
tax on warehoused inventory.  This isn't just books...it's
finished goods, and car parts as well, I would assume.

After some point, the balance of "having items on hand to
meet demand" vs. "paying tax on items" tips in favor of
ridding yourself of them.  The book folks would rather
make a small print run that sells out and then do another
if the demand is still there, then make a big run and end
up writing part of it off.  This also affects how the
writer gets paid!  VW's accountants probably did the math
and came up with the time period for their dealers.  I know
that this law has existed at least since the early 1980s, so
some older parts may have escaped.

Switching gears, I was on vacation visiting my sister
in Germany!  She's in Stuttgart and I was only there for
a week, so I didn't make it up to Wolfsburg.  About the
only split-bus related events on the trip were finding

maybe a falling tree right over the cargo doors.  Either
both cargo doors were wide open and/or had been removed...
