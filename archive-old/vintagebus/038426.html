Sure, I'd love to get a replacement block.  Let me know what you are wanting
($)...  Although I'm still not sure I like how many devices hang off that
right-most fuse.  (Ignition, horn, wipers, dome light).  Makes one unhappy
when you blow the wiper fuse at night and can't see to change it with no
dome light :)  

As for the wire colors, I agree with you Clara, but I too haven't found an
affordable source of those wires.  In the case of what I'm doing, I am
keeping the original connectors on and using a male spade connector to
extend them.  My patch cables aren't correct, but I did not cut nor modify
the original wire.  So by tracing back to the original wire, the colors are
still there.

I do have the color wiring diagram, Thom's http://www.vintagebus_dot_com/ page
is great.

On another wire note, I was looking for solutions to run a neat package of
wires from front to back of bus (gauges). Barb and Eric have suggested that
people get trailer wires.  I haven't been that happy with the choices and
quality...  While at home depot, I found that they sell some wire intend for
underground burial.  Labeled as 16/3 and 14/3 wires.  These are 3 wires of
16 or 14 gauge (stranded) respective.  These are wrapped inside a protective
cover, intended to be buried directly in ground.  Water proof, also no worry
of running it through metal holes.  Sells for about 50 cents a foot, but the
home depot I was out was discontinuing a brand and selling it for 5 cents a
foot!  I purchased all they had (100 feet of each 14 and 16 gauge).  I
suggest listees visit their local Home Depot!
