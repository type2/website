I have a large stock of both kinds of couplers, early sandwich type (comes
with grub screws) and the latter pressed couplers (doesn't come with
screws). The later style ones are OEM VW manufactured and not the ruff cast
copies out there. When shopping for a coupler look at how the castings are.
If the castings are ruff and the VW numbers are barely readable avoid it.
The good ones are beautiful castings with very distinct numbers cast in.

Hey, I have had that happen myself.   Then I was stuck in 3rd gear. which 
actually was doable to get to the parts store.  Anyway, there are two kinds 
appropriate foir the VW splitty bus.   The older kinid is the "sandwich" 
kind, the end and metal plates are 'filled' with maybe half an inch of 
rubber.  These are harder to find new, but are appropriate for buses that 
came with split case trannys (may 19 59 and earlier)

THe later kinid have more metal around them, So when the coupler uncouples, 
the metall will still hold the pieces loosely together.   These are actually

available new with VW OOOO stampings on the- better quality - from a variety

of sources.   

If you use the later kinid on a split case tranny bus (DC?) it transmits a 
bunch of tranny noise through the shift stick.   Which sucks.  The tunnel 
case tranny buses are mounted differently, so you can use the metal covered 
coupler w/o noise.

there are also urethane ones, aftermarket.

I recommend investing in a 8mm square headed socket if you feel like you
will 
ever mess with shift couplers again.  Snap-on makes them.
