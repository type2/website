On Mon, 12 Feb 2001, Thomas Boyd wrote:

> In my 65 daily driver I have been having a problem
> with a "whine" coming from the speedo.  I have
> replaced the cable with a new one and even switched
> out speedo's.  Only to see marginal results.
> 
> Usually only occures in a.m. commute (i.e. COLD
> weather).  Can last for 5 seconds, 5 minutes or even
> 15 minutes.  Speedo needle remains fairly constant
> (maybe a 2-5 mph flucutation) during the "whining".
> 
> Any ideas, do I need to/can I lube my speedo???

Yes, although it's not a guaranteed fix.

What I did was to take the speedo out and immerse the protrusion where the
speedo cable attaches in a small cup of heavy oil.  This fix seemed to
work for six months or so.  There is probably a better fix, but I didn't
want to disassemble the speedo.

You might also lube the speedo cable from the top while you are at it.
