how about your rubber flex hoses?  these get old and are probably collapsed 
inside, even if they look ok on the outside.  and the hydraulic pressure will 
let fluid through the hose, but not back to the MC, meaning the brakes will 
stick.   If you don't know they have been redone in the past few years, 
better replace all four.  You could also remove the hose at the end closest 
to the front of the car-  get a 11mm line wrench and a 17mm open end wrench 
to break the nut loose.   after the connection is loose (apply penetrating 
oiil) pry or knock the U shaped holder clip out , then undo the connection 
with an 11mm open end wrench.  pull the rubber hose off.  watch itwhile a 
friend steps on the pedal- fluid comes out- blame your flex hoses for 
blocking it to the rear wheels.
