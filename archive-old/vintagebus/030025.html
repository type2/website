Don't use resistor voltage drop devices for radios.  PERIOD.

It will cause the voltage to vary as the volume goes up and down.  You may get
away with it but the performance will be subpar and you run the risk of it being
too small resistance and frying your radio.

Get a voltage regulator type device that will regulate the voltage right at 6
volts.
An LM317 in a TO-3 can is good for 3 amps.  Most of the old 6V radios are draw
less than 1 amp.

There are a number of web pages around with the circuit on it if you want to run
to your local electronic parts house and gather up the stuff and DIY.  Sorry,
don't have a link off the top of my head.
