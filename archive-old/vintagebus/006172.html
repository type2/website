Yeah Isaiah - I would guess that you have a combination burnt pressure plate
and shot clutch.  How long have you driven it without adjusting it?  I'm
sure you know that by just "barely" disengaging the clutch, has caused undue
wear on it.  My guess is that when you take it apart, you'll either find a
paper thin clutch or a skeleton of one.  I've seen worn clutches before - or
rather - what was left of one.  While you are ordering your new clutch and
pressure plate, add a clutch cable to your list of items to get.  I'm sure
you'll be glad you did...

One more thing - when you mention you didn't have any power in 4th gear -
was the engine bogging or revving?  Bogging would NOT be an indication of
worn clutch whereas revving would be.  I've driven a dodge pickup with a
clutch so badly worn, flooring it caused a slight amount of acceleration and
then 3 seconds later this putrid smell filled the cab, the engine revved up,
and smoke was everywhere.  Yep - bad clutch - burnt to hell.  A good clutch
won't slip at all.
