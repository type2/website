On my 58 single cab with 74 type 1 dual port and an alternator (not
generator), I drove around for 4 days with the G-light on, thinking, "I'm
gonna fix that tomorrow morning".  Finally stranded my stupid ass about 15
miles from nowhere and took me 2 hours on my cell-phone to get hold of a
sympathetic friend to come rescue me.

The problem (as I already knew) was a bad connection on the big red wire
that runs from the positive connection on the top of the alternator to the
positive terminal on the battery.  So I built a new cable and now
everything's happy again.  If you're running an alternator, you can test
this theory by (carefully) hooking up a jumper cable from your positive post
to your positive post.  If it's a generator, I don't know.

I hope your problem is this simple!
