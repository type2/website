you are supposed the do a compression test with the engine warmed up.  Now I 
know why.    they are all close to each other- dont worry.
 However, all the plugs should be the same colour.   I would inspect the plug 
wires and cap.  is it running evenly on all 4 cyls?  do the pull one plug 
wire off the dist at a time, then hold it close to the hole in the cap (use 
rag to not shock yourself) and listen to the diff sound as you let it run on 
4 vs 3 cyls.  do this for all four.   the sound change SHOULD be the same for 
all 4.  if not, you probably have a spark distribution prob- examine the wire 
for the one that makes the least dif (which is probably the darkest plug)   
and also a new cap can change this.   maybe you need new wires?

what I do with a 30/31 pict is adjust the big screw first.  turn it in till 
it starts to die, then turn it out 3 half turns. (or 1 1/2 turns if you like) 
 then take the little screw driver and turn the little screw in till it 
starts to die, then out 3 half turns.  then do the big again.  back and 
forthe till they are adjusted to each other and you dont change the setting.  
 this usually means the big screw is about 4 half turns out from closed.  I 
have runs a 30/31 pict on my bug for 9 years and it's fine. (and a 009- no 
flat spot)
