Subject: Re: [VB] Busses and winter

 Just did a 5,000 miles road trip.
 
The 6V bus started no less well in winter than in summer.  
The heat is fine as long as the outside temps were above 30F.  Below 30 I have to put the shoes and gloves on and wanted another layer too.   An eberspacher auxiliary heater would be advised for regular subfreezing driving.  I think they were standard equipment in Canada.
 
A windshield washer would be good for the salty roads that kick crud up on the glass.. but you really don't want to drive an old bus in te winter were they salt the roads.  It's going to the car wash tomorrow am to knock te salt and ice off the underside and everywhere.
 
You can find the heater things that bolt on under the drain circle that plug into a regular outlet to keep the oil warm to ease starting.
