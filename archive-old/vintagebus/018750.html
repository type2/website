As I understand it, you can run propane and gasoline
on the same engine - basically you have the propane
carb and the gas carb and can use either.  Using
two different liquid fuels, like gasoline and alcohol,
is more interesting but still doable.  I would guess
that because of the different energy contents of
different liquid fuels, you would need different
jet sizes and settings in a regular carb to run 
multiple fuels.  You might be able to rig up one
carb to be "switchable" in this way, but it might
be easier to fangle an intake manifold with two
carbs on it and valving that lets you use one carb
or the other.  Maybe not the best thing in terms
of flow efficiency, but it would allow relatively
painless (wrench-free) switching between fuels.

If you just want to run on one alternate liquid
fuel, you can probably re-jet your plain old Solex
to work just fine.  The other consideration is
the seals and rubber lines used in the fuel system
and their compatibility with the fuel you want
to use.
