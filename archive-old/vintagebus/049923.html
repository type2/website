>A word of caution about stainless: It is not nearly as strong as common
>steel fasteners, especially if the threads are cut instead of rolled. Most
>stainless bolts are about equivalent to a standard steel grade 2 bolt, and
>most standard steel fasteners used in the automotive industry are grades 5
>to 8.
>
>You can buy specially prepared stainless bolts that are stronger, but they
>are not widely available and are very expensive. Look to the suppliers of
>the airline and the nuclear industries for the exotic and strong stainless
>fasteners. The marine industry also likes them.
>
>So, be warned, and be thoughtful about where you put the stainless
>replacement. It will probably not corrode, but it may break. If the job
>requires strength, perhaps you should consider a coated  standard steel
>fastener. And broken stainless fasteners are very hard to remove because
>they work harden as they are drilled. Use the highest grade of drill when
>working on them.
