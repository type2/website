The way around this is to restrict the flow from the engine by placing
an orifice in the engine fan inlet to the flapper box.  If you put a
2-cm hole in the flow path, the electric fan will have no problem
overcoming the engine fan.

  Secondly the reason we prefer the partial
> recirculation system is that if there is monoxide leak somewhere in
the
> heating system.  The new fresh air will help to avoid the monoxode
from just
> building up to higher and higher levels that a closed circulation
system would
> cause.

I'd expect that normal leaks from the system would cause fresh air to
be drawn into the cabin.  However, a sizeable leak from the exhaust
manifold into the heat exchanger could supply all the "make-up" volume
and turn the bus into a gas chamber.  My feeling is that you would
certainly notice a leak of this magnitude, but I'd probably go for a
CO detector just the same.
