The advantage that air shocks give, is when you pressurize them with air,
they extend and raise the vehicle up. Normally installed on the backs of
trucks for when they tow a heavy trailer to keep the vehicle sitting level.

It's been hard finding information on them, but after searching for a while
last night, it looks like the Gabriel Sky-Jacker shocks might end up working
for me (if any airshock will). The shocks can be run without any air
pressure, which is great, so the ride will remain normal for most of my
driving.
