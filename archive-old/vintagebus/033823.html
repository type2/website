I use a very small adjustable wrench for stuck ones, with a vise grip on the 
solid (not cable) part of the cable.  I have bent some wingnut ears doing 
this.   when the threads are clean, and greased, you can do it by hand.  I 
use this technique:  Put on hat, scoot under bus from motor end, facing up,  
head to front of bus.   about as far so just my feet stick oout from under 
the bus.   reach my LEFT hand OVER the axle tube.  Then I can reach the 
wingnut without contorting.

I just adjusted the clutch on my 61 deluxe, after installing the motor.  The 
wingnut turned easily, I had cleaned it up and greased it about 5 years ago.  
   so they don't stick it slathered with grease.   I think it is easier to 
slide under and turn a wingnut than mess with two wrenches (two arms in 
there?) and realising you have the wrong size and climbing out from under the 
bus and getting another wrench...
 Definately easier to unstick it with the motor out!!

Some of the clutch arms where the cable goes through have a kinda pointy both 
ends oval twards the wingnut side of the hole the cable goes through.  This 
is cause the wingnut has a similar shape the side that goes towards the arm.  
so the wingnut does not loosen itself  I think the nuts are round, and they 
should be used with an arm with a round shape there, but i dunno how much it 
matters.
