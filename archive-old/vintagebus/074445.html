trying to remember whether or not there was such a thing as a steering
lock on a split bus.
..........
some have them.  Rarely did N American spec buses have them, but it seems 
that all splitties sold in Europe did.   
If the key goes into the painted pod the speedo is in, you have no steering 
lock
If it goes into a black (or white) thing under the plate (under the speedo 
pod) you bash your knee on when getting into the bus  it has a steering lock.   
on 66& earlier versions there is writing around the key hole 'lock, garage, 
fahrt, start' or some such.
The steering lock is activated when you remove the key in the lock position.  
merely turning the key to shut the bus off would not lock the steering.

 Now here is the question: what is the method by which the electric
 choke communicates with the stepped cam? 

..... hot wire from 15 terminal of coil should run over to the tab on the 
choke.   So when you turn on the ignition the coil warms up and relaxes.   To 
adjust the choke, turn off the ignition, let the choke be cold.   Loosen th three 
screws than hold the choke still, and remove the brace, leaving the choke in 
place.   you should be able to turn th choke by hand or with a pair of wide 
mouthed pliers.   If you pull the choke off you'll see how the coil in the choke 
moves a thingy in the carb that flips the top butterfly open/shut.  (have to 
remove tube to air cleaner to see the top butterfly.)  It also turns the step 
on the left side of the carb.  There is a notch on the choke, & mark on the 
carb body that should roughly line up.  I adjust it so it's just at the maximum 
closed with choke cold.   The accelerator arm should rest on a higher step.  
Attach choke brace/screws.   Reconnect everything, turn key on.    As the choke 
warms up, it relaxes and lets the accelerator arm rest on a lower step till 
it should be on the lowest step when its warmed up.    Sometimes you need to 
goose the gas (can do that at the carb) to get it to go to a lower step.  Adjust 
choke as necessary.   Remember, the wire to the choke is hot with ignition on.

 Here is another question: the tube which encloses the accel cable in
 it's passage through the tin is loose, and moves back and forth
 together with the cable. The grommet is split, so the rear (RIR) half
 is still on the end of the tube as it moves. What should hold the tube
 in place? The grommet?
...........
Earlier carbs did not have a spring between th top and bottom, there was a 
spring between the metal tube the cable goes through and the accelerator arm 
there.

I take a old piece of fuel line and give it one slice length ways and then 
carve a dent around its middle.  This goes around the tube th accelerator cable 
goes through, and also acts as a grommet through the front tin  (a little 
tricky to wiggle it into place, that's why I carve th groove in around the 
middle).  W/o a grommet eventually the tin cuts through the tube and can cut through 
the cable.    I also use a small hose clamp on the front side of the fan 
housing around th tube, so it can't slide through the fan housing to the back.
