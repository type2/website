I bet you a nickel it's an AH code engine.  My dad has one like that in
his '74 Bug, it's original.  The bigger breather tube goes with the
paper-element air filter and plastic housing that was used on those
cars.  The extra plumbing on the intake manifold is EGR, exhaust gas
recirculation.  Stock, there is a threaded fitting on the left side
muffler flange.  A filter screws into this.  A metal tube screws into
the other side of the filter, and runs up through the tin to the EGR
valve.  There is a small vacuum hose from the top of the EGR valve
to a port that sticks out of the carb (34-PICT-3) to the rear and 
about 30 degrees above horizontal.  The EGR valve has a square metal
base that bolts on to the manifold and a round top.  If you want
to get rid of it, just make sure you cap off the fitting on the carb,
and that it doesn't leak.  If it does leak, take it off the manifold
and you will see the two bolt holes with a bigger hole in the center
that goes into the intake manifold.  Make yourself a little cover
plate to fit over this, put some RTV on it, bolt it down with
shorter bolts, and there you go.

My Dad's Bug has one of these too.  The regulator is under the
back seat.  Stock, all the terminals are in a big round plastic
plug that fits onto the top of the alternator.  Look at
http://www.vintagebus_dot_com/wiring/bug-74+75-a.jpg
Three of the connections go to the regulator - one is also the
dash light connection.  The other connection goes to the battery.
You can probably make it work if you can find the late regulator
to go with it.
