Get the shipper to specify "Parts for vehicle 25 years and older" and you
will pay only GST, and lately PST were applicable....... no extra duty.

UPS tried to stick me with the "customs" duty from their $$ hungry broker
who also was loose with "the rules" to their benefit ( same part shipped one
month apart; vast difference in duty). Not only that the Canadian broker
firm at the time that asked to be paid in US dollars (great business
decision on their part). Anyway, several times I told them that Iwas to
clear it thru customs myself. When they refused, I told them to send it back
on more than one occasion........ the broker charge disappeared. Also told
them to put me on a list of future shipments that were to be cleared by
me..... assured they had such a list but never materialized with continued
hassle.

Anyway, don't need the aggravation anymore and get shipments thru USPS and
Canada Post. Cheaper duty procedure and the package gets there. And if it is
a high $ item, spend the extra bucks....send it express and traceable. Less
aggravation for buyer and seller....
