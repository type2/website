David,

I have seen these conversions installed in a few different  makes of
vans.  There is a 60's Corvair in a junk yard in So Cal that has one of
these.  I didn't see a maker tag.

There was a camper converter over in England that used these types of
tops.  They were called Slumberwagens and were manufactured by European
Cars of South Kensington, London.  The english refer to this type of roof
as a "Calthorpe elevated domed roof".

There are pictures and a small amount of information in a book titled"VW
Bus  *Camper*Bus*Van*Pick-up*Wagon" by Malcolm Bobbit.  It is published
by Veloce Publishing PLC.  And I quote

"the Slumberwagen, a conversion based on the early split-window
Transporter.   Available with a Calthorpe roof which, in the raised
position, was dome-shaped and incorporated sliding windows, the
Slumberwagen provided sleeping acomodation for a maximum of two adults
and two children.  Extending the roof was a fairly simple task and, in
terms of reliability - it was completely water and draughtproof, it was
one of the best of its type when tested by the Autocar  staff."

There are two pictures of this conversion on page 88 of that book.

Hope this helps.  Sounds interesting, to bad the interior is not there!
Good luck.
