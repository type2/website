Hello all!

Well, here it is...the complete list of special tools that VW forgot,
also known as VWX tools.  I don't plan on posting this again unless
I get lots of new submissions, so grab it now!  Thanks to everyone who
participated, including George Lyle, whose 'MX-Code' list inspired
this one.

Matt Roberds

=== VWX codes - The tools that VW forgot ===
JE VWX-000 - Zip ties
               any size, length, and color
D  VWX-001 - Duct tape
D  VWX-002 - Baling wire
               attach heater box cables
               fix broken gas pedal linkage
               attach license plate to front overrider
MR VWX-003 - Small wire brush 
               all electrical connections
MR VWX-004 - Magnet-on-a-stick 
               fishes spark plugs and tin screws out of shrouding
MR VWX-005 - Mirror-on-a-stick
JA VWX-006 - 20cm Hemostat 
               retrieves dropped spark plugs and sockets from Type IV tin
               retrieves dropped rotor from Type I pulley tin
MR VWX-010 - German/English phrase book
               for cussing at your Bus in its mother tounge
MR VWX-011 - BFH 
               Big F(ine) Hammer
MR VWX-012 - 1.5m cheater pipe
               rear axle nuts, flywheel
               See also VWX-024
MR VWX-013 - Stocking cap
MR VWX-014 - Socket set 
               used as drifts to install bearings and seals
MR VWX-015 - J-bolt with threaded end filed square
               used in lieu of church key (my Bus came with this)
MR VWX-018 - Rag for front window defrost assist
JA VWX-020 - 1m 6.3mm fuel hose 
               cut into 8cm-16cm lengths to install plugs
               also doubles as a stethoscope for finding unusual engine
                 noises and synchronizing dual carburetors
               can additionally be used as fuel hose
MR VWX-024 - 60cm monkey wrench
               rear axle nuts, flywheel
               inspired by Walt 
               See also VWX-012
CW VWX-049 - Lighter that no longer flames
               for lighting VWX-050
GL VWX-050 - Blue Wrench (gas torch)
GL VWX-051 - Friend with good hearing 
               for setting timing without lights or instruments
GL VWX-052 - Rope
               for securing items to roof rack, pulling bus out of sand, etc.
GL VWX-053 - Shovel
GL VWX-054 - Friend with small hands 
               for retrieving objects that fall into the cracks
CW VWX-055 - Wire 
               attaches items to roof rack (steel is real)
RR VWX-068 - Ice chest for beer
A  VWX-069 - Extended Ice Chest 
CW VWX-101 - Milk crate
               place to sit while pondering your engine
CW VWX-101dlx - Milk crate covered with shag carpet
CW VWX-175 - Old link pin 
               used as an Intermediary Pounding Device for straightening 
                 bumpers, guards, or wherever an IPD is needed
CW VWX-176 - Piece of railroad track 
               used as anvil
CW VWX-177 - Piece of I beam
               used as anvil
CW VWX-178 - Strong friend
               holds your vise which is breaking free from the workbench
CW VWX-200 - Small size cheap rolling floor jack
CW VWX-201 - Plywood base for VWX-200
               prevents sinking into mud or sand
CW VWX-202 - Short piece of 10cm x 10cm wood for VWX-200
               increases jack height to allow lifting front wheels 
                 off the ground
CW VWX-203 - Pieces of split firewood 
               blocks the Bus from rolling
CW VWX-204 - Round from a tree
               used to place transmission support under transmission
               supports Bus when the transmission is removed
C  VWX-250 - Golf tee 
               plugs fuel hose
CW VWX-601 - Test light (for local manufacture) 
               Made from an instrument bulb holder and bulb, a cable
                 with connector on one end (attached to bulb holder) 
                 and clip on the other, and a cable with clips on both
                 ends (one clip on bulb holder body)
               Note: fit a 6V or 12V bulb as appropriate
ME VWX-602 - 20 cm long rod with 15 mm hex nut welded to rod on flat
               side of nut
               used to remove and replace transmission fill and drain plugs
