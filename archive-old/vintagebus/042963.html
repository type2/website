Do you know what is "bad" about your MC? If the rubbers are just worn out
or broken, then rebuild kits may still be avialable. You want four 22mm
'cups' ( basically flanged "O"rings) - I believe they are the same item as
used in the wheel cylinders. That and a good cleaning and check out for
corrosion - maybe some honing with some super fine grit emory paper, and a
final rinse with isopropyl alcohol - 95% or purer. Be sure to note the
directions that the flanges go before removing the old items! There's
between 2 and 4 of them in there - can't recall at this point. 

Don't leave *any* trace of oil or water in the MC when reassembling - You
may use some brake fluid to lube the reassembly.

If you can find rebuild kits for the vintages wheel cylinders, they
probably do the trick.
