On Mon, 14 Oct 2002, Rocky wrote:

> Since the studs are mounted to or near the surface of the case,
> wouldn't the growth of the case be of minimal relation to the studs
> that retain the heads? It seems reasonable that the growth of the
> cylinders would be the major cause of stud stretching. Am I missing
> something?

The cylinders are iron and grow almost the same as the studs.  The
aluminum heads grow faster than the studs and result in pull-out or
breakage.  You're right in that case growth is a minor concern, both from
a geometry standpoint and from one of temperature change.  The heads get
much hotter than the case.
