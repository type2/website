> It looks like the Brosol 30-PICT-1 in my '64 is in need of a little TLC.
> A couple of weekends ago I decided to fire up the Bus and adjust the
> carb as it seemed to be running pretty rich per the plugs and the
> tailpipe.  Well, after my tuning session, I noticed a film of fuel on
> the sides of the carb near the throttle shaft, and also a bit on the
> left side of the float bowl.
> 
> I'm not as worried about the float bowl leak; it appeared to be coming
> from the air horn/body joint instead of the inlet tube.  When I put
> a stubby screwdriver on the air horn screws they were 0.25 to 0.5 turn
> loose, so that might explain that leak.  At this point I have to pull
> the carb off the engine and send it to the Berryman Spa anyway, so if
> there is a crack or some other problem up there I should find it then.
> 
> The throttle shaft leak seems to be coming from the throttle shaft -
> the bolts that hold the carb to the manifold are tight.  I also tried
> wiggling the throttle shaft (with the throttle closed) - it has maybe
> 0.5 mm of axial play (left to right).  If I hold the shaft all the
> way to the left or right with one hand and try to move the other end
> radially (fore and aft) with the other hand, it also has some play
> although about half as much.  I'm assuming this is the "classic"
> symptom of worn throttle shaft bores.  I have no idea how long this
> carb has been on the Bus; it seems odd to me that something that is
> obviously a replacement carb would be worn in what I'm assuming to
> be a relatively short period of time.  It could have been on the
> Bus as long as ten years, though.
> 
> So: it looks like my carb needs the throttle shaft rebushed.  It

> replacement?  (I know you can't get those new.)  It seems that new
> carbs only come in manual choke versions; is it possible to swap the
