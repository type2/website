Well, after my last whiney post, I figured I'd better follow it up with the
good news.

I got the gas tank and engine back in this morning with very little trouble.
The clutch had been getting really stiff, and Id' read a post on here about
lubing the clutch assembly.  I decided instead of slapping things back
together in a big hurry to get to a VW club meeting, I'd skip the meeting,
take my time and get some stuff taken care of.  I'm glad I did.

I took the clutch pedal assembly apart an took some 400 grit sandpaper to
the shaft and the inside of the tube, added a squirt of grease and voila!
The difference was absolutely amazing.  I've found that many older car
drivers, myself included, fall into that "it's old, that's just the way it
is" syndrome.  But usually that's not the case.  It shouldn't take both feet
to push in the clutch, and you shouldn't have to pull it back up.  It works
like a dream now.  Slicker'n snot on a pump handle...  If you have a sticky
clutch, take care of it.  It's a 10 minute fix, and well worth the time.

I took her out on the road just before dark, and everything is A-OK!  Now I
just have to get wipers on it, and work out a few electrical problems.  And
then there's the body work and paint.  Oh, yeah I guess I've got a bit of
work on the interior.  And the gas heater installation...  Other than that,
I'm almost done! ;-)  Like I'll ever be done.  I just keep telling myself
that it's the journey, not the destination...

Keep on wrenchin' and try to do some driving too,
