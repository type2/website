The problem with trailer tires is that they are speed rated for 55 only, if
they seperate on you and distroy your wheel wells and/or your bus I hope you
have good insurance as the tire manufacturer will not warrenty a trailer
tire on a passenger vehical. A trailer tire has a totally different ply
construction as a passenger tire as the are made to go straight and not take
the abuse of turning as on the front of a bus. I looked into trailer tire as
there is the correct size and ply rateing but after researching as to why
they are trailer tires I got an education from people in the know. Good Luck

Firestone makes a 6 ply 205 75/15 which is about as close as you'll get to
the original size. I do have access to 195/15 8 ply tires but the problem is
they are an export tire and do not have a DOT stamping on them and they are
tube type, but was told again by the person in the know that they will run
tubeless. In India and third world countrys 195.15 6 or 8 ply tires are very
common, now if we could only get some one to go to Mexico and get some!!!!
