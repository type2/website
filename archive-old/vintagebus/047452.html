 Michele,
 
 Driving an antique occasionally is surely better than letting it languish in
the garage.  However...
 
 The fact I have forty years experience with Volkswagens, a shop full of tools
and spare parts up the wazoo makes it practical for me to drive an old bus
literally any where I want to go but it has become obvious that my occasional
trip reports have given many a false impression of the durability of old
Volkswagens.
 
 Firstly, the fabled reliability of early-model Volkswagens is nothing more
than a reflection of the service they receive, and they are high-maintenance
vehicles, requiring ten times for preventative maintenance than the typical
modern-day vehicle.  Secondly, the skill-level needed to perform that
maintenance is about two orders of magnitude higher than any you'll find at
the local quickie-lube.  And finally, the price of brake and suspension parts
is rising steadily while many body repair panels are already unavailable.
 
 The bottom line is that unless you're qualified to do the maintenance
yourself, keeping an antique Volkswagen in-use and on the road is liable to
cost you more than a luxury vehicle.
 
 -Bob Hoover >>
