Way back when dinosaurs roamed the earth I used to work

spun up, of course.  Like I said, when the front counter was 
slow and you had that certain type of customer........
 
Anyway, one of the things I did was to check the back of 
the bearing catalogs for the cross-reference section.  All
bearings are made to an international standard and given
an universal number.  This number tells you the ID, the OD
the thickness, how many balls it has, is it shielded, or 
sealed, on how many sides, etc.  So for your viewing pleasure
these are the numbers that you can use at your local bearing
supply house to get what you need.  

311-501-283  is a 6306 ball bearing
211-501-285  is a 6305 ball bearing
211-501-283  is a N207E roller bearing

If there is no shaft tolerance number then it is a 'C3' fit.  
(Which is what you want anyway)  But if you are asked 
then tell them C3.  That simply signifies how tight the 
bearing will fit onto the shaft.   So, for example, if you 
have a '66 and need all the bearings for only one redux
box then tell them you need (2) 6306, (1) 6305, and (1)
N207E.  The counter guy (gal) will know exactly what
those bearings are and you don't have to try and explain
what they fit.  Also, specify brand.  If you don't then they
will just grab the first ones out of the box and you may
end up with 'Wang Chung' bearings.  Now, a 6306 is 
supposed to be a 6306 whichever brand due to international
standards but I'd try and stay with either the German or
at least Japanese.  There are also American brands but
the biggest supplier that I ran across was L&S.  We didn't
call them 'Loose' and 'Sloppy' for nothing!  If you stay 
SKF or FAG regardless of country of origin then you'll be
just fine and dandy.
