I've used Castrol 30W all year round in 1600SP engines for 22 years and
300,000 miles.

For 200,000 of those miles I was running an external oil cooler with an oil
filter in the circuit.  And for 143,000 of those miles the engine had a
heavy duty oil pump.  The filter used a standard "Ford" screw-on cartridge.
All the hose connections were the push-on type, with barbed fittings and
hose clamps. This setup endured all sorts of temperature extremes, including
innumerable cold starts in zero or even sub-zero conditions.  In all those
years and miles with this system I never had a problem with blown filters,
seals, or coolers, or with oil leaks of any kind.

Because of this experience I have a difficult time accepting that various
engine problems are due to "thick oil".  I'm _not_ saying cold starts with
high viscosity oil never cause blown seals/coolers/filters, just that such
claims puzzle me based on my own experience.

More dumb luck on my part, I guess.
