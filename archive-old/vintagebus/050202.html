I made a water trap myself for very little money and it works great. What I
did was buy some small PVC and some elbow joints. I run from the compressor
about 2 feet and then put a "T" joint. I go down from the "T" joint just
enough to connect a valve. I go from the "T" joint about a foot or foot and
a half and then an elbow. I go sideways again about a foot and then an
elbow. I then go back down about a foot and a half to another "t" joint
with a valve on the bottom again. Out from the "t" joint sideways to a
Wal-Mart water filter I already had. All of the PVC, joints, valves, and
adapters cost less than $10. And it works GREAT for me. I NEVER get water
in my line anymore. A paint and body guy told me to do this and I'm very
glad I did. The whole set-up looks kind of like this:

                                 ________
                                 I              I
                                 I              I
                                 I              I
Compressor---------I              I---water filter---hose
                                 I              I
              valve--->    I              I    <----valve


Hope that diagram came through OK.
