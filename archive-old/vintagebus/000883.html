> Ok here is the scoop. I am going to sandblast my 65 delux bus soon and I
would like some
> tips on what I should do before and after. I have never sandblasted before
and I heard it > is very messy.

First thing - don't use sand to remove the "stuff" like rust, previous coats
of paint, primer, etc.  Use either glass beads or walnut shells.  If you
can't find either of those (available at http://www.eastwoodco_dot_com). then
ask for "non-angular" media.

> Some things I need to know...what should I coverup? When do I know the
rust is really
> gone? What do I do after it is shiny clean metal?

Cover up anything you can...I would mask off any areas that you are not
specifically blasting.  Use an etching primer and then a guide coat to
figure out where there are low spots in the sheet metal.

> I know I need to primer it, do I use primer from a spray can or paint
brush it on like you > do when you paint your house.

Try not to use the brush on kind.  The rattle can is much more effective.
