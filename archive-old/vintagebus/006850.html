In my 61 side loader I used PB Blaster, I sealed the tip of the spray can
against the cable then slowly sprayed it in, took 2 days, also did the same
from the rear, 2 days later cable free, still need to pull completely out to
really soak the cable sheath. The problem is though that your cable sheath
is probably totally gone inside from rust and freeing up the cable will
leave you with a hollow plastic tube for the cable to slide. Inspect it good
from underneath and try to determine the shape of the sheath before spinning
your wheels trying to get the cable free. The cable is usually held in place
with bent over tabs and you could carefully pull it out with a guide wire
attached and then pull the new one through, even if you miss the hold tabs
on reinstall both ends are attached so it would still function alright.
