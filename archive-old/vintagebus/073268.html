> water for a while) and plumbed it straight to the burner. The heater

> out that the electric pump had to be powered where the coil is so
> the thermostat would turn it off along with the coil when it kicked
> in, it was functioning perfectly.

The literature I have found about the SW calls for the pump to put out
about 5-9psi.

> But, because this is the only first hand experience I have with gas

> black smoke (just faint, but it is there). It's not a lot, but you

After I got it in fine working order mine doesn't seem to really have
any noticable black smoke. It did have more smoke when the combustion
fan did not turn fast enough. Try drilling a small hole in the motor.
There is a felt spacer thing in there. Soak it in elec motor oil for a
day through the hole. it will help that motor a LOT.
