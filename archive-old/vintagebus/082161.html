>>  I'm also interested in your results with stroker crank.  IIRC the 72 is

Good to hear.  We were thinking of going to the 72mm stroke in  the hopes 
that it was not too much.  Not sure where, but there comes a  point with too much 
stroke and the pistons get shoved more and more into the top  and bottom 
sides of the cylinders from the change in the angle of the connection  rods.  
Which is not good for longevity.  Yes there are stock  engines that run bigger 
stoke without this problem, but then they run much  longer connecting rods and 
this in turn means moving the P&Cs and heads out  more from the crank.
