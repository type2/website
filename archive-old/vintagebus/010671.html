Hi Jason,
Never assume, you know what happens when you do that!  Our 1960 panel camper 
came with a DP engine with a rod through the top of the engine case.  We 
built up a nice big bore 40HP engine and could not get the bus over 45 MPH on 
the flats pedal to the metal.  Turned out it was doing 3,000 RPM at 35 MPH 
and 4,000 RPM at 4,000 RPM.  Traded for a used tranny that a friend said he 
and his brother drove at 55 no problem!  Turned out that one did the early 
stock 1960-63 47 MPH at 3,000 RPM and 55 at 3,800 RPM!  If you had a 4.125 
R&P with .82 fourth and late RGBs you should be able to do 55 MPH at 3,000 
RPM.  So unless you have a freeway flier 3.875 R&P .82 fourth with late RGB 
which would get you 60 MPH at 3,000.......  so far Clara is the only person 
who seems to be able to RPM an engine at 3,000 RPM in fourth gear by ear.  
Everyone else we have helped tach out, including us have had sheepish 
embarrassed grins on after finding out what RPMs in the higher gears to MPH 
really are!
