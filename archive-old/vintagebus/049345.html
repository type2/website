I'd try a plumber's snake.  You can get a small one quite cheaply at any =
hardware store.  If the mouse is too stuck in there to push out, the final =
1 - 2 inches of the snake is loosely twisted and resembles a corkscrew.  =

out.  Once you get the mouse out, use a rag soaked with bleach or vinegar =
on the end of the snake to clean off any baked on mouse bits and slime and =
disinfect + deodorize the whole thing. =20

against the odor.  I don't want to read a "[VB] Best methods for removing =
