>>What's the original of the term "church key" anyway?

>>Any info is welcome,

When I was a kid in the 50s & 60s, beer cans were made from fairly
tough steel, and required the kind of opener that punches a small
triangular hole in the top of the can.  Same kind of opener that's
used for large juice cans today.

People called this type of metal opener a "church key", as a kind of
irreverent slang.  I think that this is probably the origin of the
term being used for the tapered keys that fit in the square holes of
engine lids & gas tank flaps on splittys.

Bill
