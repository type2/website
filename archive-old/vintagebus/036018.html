When I have been in or driving a bus with a bad tranny mount there is a 
noticable lurch as the bus is shifted into another gear.  LIke the bus jumps 
forward slightly, and then is thrown back.  It is not comfortable, and is 
immediately noticable to the non mechanic as "something is wrong"

so there is a clunking when shifting, it could be in the tranny, it could be 
in the clutch, it could be in the fulcrum plates- if that areais worn you 
will feel/hear and clunk whe you step on the gas- if you drive carefully you 
can avoid the clunk, which is more wear happening in there.  say you are 
cruisng along in third gear, then take your foot off the gas, then step on 
the gas- if your FP areas are worn you will tell that clunk which is at the 
spade ends of the axles.

I had a 59 truck with severely worn FP areas, in fact when I first changed 
the gear oil I was concerned the tranny would work, as there were noticble 
pieces of FP that came out in the oil.  Anyway, it did work, and I drove it 
around for months,  and had to remeber to be careful with slowing/ 
accelerating as the thunk was not good.   I did mean to take it apart and fix 
it, but it was functional that way, and I was fixing some more important 
fuinctioning bits on it, and then I was persuaded to sell it (I warned the 
Next O about the axle shaftFPs).

anyway, that would not thunk when shifting exactly, but when you step on the 
gas right after shifting-  

You can heard this thunk in most buses to a certain small extent.

If it is during shifting I would check to make sure the shift linkage is all 
in good shape,  otherwise it is probably internal to the tranny.

when did you last change you gear oil?

I hope this makes sense, it is difficult to communication thunks and crunches 
through words and the situations they occure in, over a written medium
