> For the hydraulic lifters... If it has been more than a week since the
> engine last ran, I would say just take it out and drive it around for a
> while, locally, on surface streets, at low speeds, for like 1/2 hour.  If
> you have any that have not pumped up after that amount of time, they are
> dead and should be replaced.  My theory on this is that a collapsed lifter
> is allowing way too much clearance and too much clearance does not damage
> an engine (in 1/2 hour) despite how it sounds.  It is too *little*
> clearance that will damage an engine. (I await the various flames I will
> soon be receiving :-)
> 
> On the too rich problem when you start it, again, let it warm up.  My '80
> stinks of gas when cold, but is just fine once it warms up.  I think this
> is "normal", tho I don't like it much.
