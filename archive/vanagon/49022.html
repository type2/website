Before you start replacing the computer , try checking your electrical
system again. Try to swap a new ignition coil and eliminate this as a
possible cause. Your described symptoms seems to indicate some
intermittant loss of electrical power or possibly momentary fuel
starvation due to fuel pump problems or clogged filter. 
Also some engines that uses the Hall effect system uses a transistorized
module that goes bad quite often. This also causes the kind of problem
you described including poor starting. The vanagon engine has one
very unique feature. If everything is in good condition, the motor
will kick in almost instantly under most weather conditions. You can
credit German engineering for this. If you have to crank the engine like
some autos , something is wrong somewhere.
