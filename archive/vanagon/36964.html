The removal of the EGR valve can potentially be dangerous to your valves.
What Dennis H. was talking about is that if you remove the EGR, and don't
plug the holes and retime your engine to compensate, you may burn your
valves due to the excess air causing a leaner air/fuel mixture.  I believe
this can be defeated by plugging the left-over holes and adjusting the 
timing a little to richen the air/fuel mixture.  This will take a little
experimenting, but it can be done.

Now if you're in California, the may keep you from passing the emission in-
spection if the technician is knowedgable enough to look for an EGR valve.
