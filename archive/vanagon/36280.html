    Wow, that may be a mileage record!!!  price is very good for an
    '88 Westy otherwise.  Find out about that rebuild (full
    details/parts list), and who did the paint too, no point having
    it blister off in 2 years.  I prefer to see repainted cars that
    have had two+ years on the paintjob, as the rust bubbles will be
    coming through if they are going to.  A freshly painted vehicle
    if not done by a reliable place is a gamble.

    Check the engine for water leaks!!!  Check the PS rack for
    leaks, and at pump too. Check condition of exhaust system, both
    of these are big $$$$ items. Check steering bits, cheaper to fix,
    so not so worrisome. Front brakes will cost $350-$400 complete,
    so look at the rotors. Dicker with the dealer, nice to get OEM
    fixups as part of the sales price.  Check the front heater fan
    for screeching/binding, it's about due. Listen for clunking CVs,
    a rebuilt axle is $140 here, cheapest/best fix.
