On Tue, 13 Dec 94 15:03:00 CST, Stuart Hutton <hutton_at_poly.oscs.montana_dot_edu> wrote:
> Hello,
> I was just told of an interesting idea for remote engine warming.
> Apparently, this has been used in Alaska with success.
> The idea is this:  take an iron pipe and stick the end of a propane torch
> in it.  Light the propane torch and place it under your engine.  
> I was told that in about an hour, the engine will be ready to start.

  I doubt you'd get much out a propane torch -- what we use for cats and 
skidders is a BIG torch or the burner out of a gas hot waterheater, stuck
in a couple sections of 6" stove pipe with a elbow on the end so the heat
goes up to the bottom of the oilpan (actually to the skid plate, then 
slowly to the oil pan) and fueled by a good sized propane tank. What you 
probably heard was that, but something got lost along the way. Believe me,
a little propane torch won't cut it. 
   Oh yeah -- people do manage to burn up some expensive equipment that
way too.
