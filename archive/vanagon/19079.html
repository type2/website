Hi Chris!

We're bussing from Connecticut to Tennessee starting Thursday in our 71
Westy.  We're bringing along spare CV boots/grease, fuel pump, fuel hose,
generator brushes, manuals, points/condensor, plugs, accelerator and clutch
cables, generator belt, precious automotive fluids and tools, tools, tools
for that remotely possible engine pull somewhere on the Blue Ridge Highway. 

I'd do a routine maintainence before leaving--adjust valves, change oil,
check timing etc.  Checking my maintainance records, I've used the same
plugs for as long as 15,000 miles (Bosch (nonplatinum) W8AC).  I checked the
gap at intervals over the 15,000 miles but it didn't vary significantly.  Is
your plug crossthreaded in there?  I can't offer any advice for quick fixes
as I usually opt for the long, painfully anal repairs that entail removing
and disassembling everything.

I wish you a fun, safe trip with no repairs!
