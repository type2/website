Does it run poorly at all, when you smell that hot smell?

Air cooled engines do get hot.  I remember the first time I started driving
an air cooled VW around.  It took me a while to get over the nervousness
everytime I smelled that "hot smell."  It is a "hot smell" that is quite
differnet from the "hot smell" of a water boxer.  But the Bus ran well, got
good gas mileage, and never missed a beat for tens of thousands of miles
with that "hot smell" that eventually faded into the background of
familiarity.

When I fried the engine of my last Bug, the "HOT SMELL" that emanated from
that poor engine was very alarming, and very different.  I smelled like
something mechanical was going to die, and it did.  Qualitative, yes, but
true.
