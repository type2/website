Ever driven one of those Ford 2800 V-6's? It is the most gutless
engine (for its displacement) on the market, even when it was still
in the Ford. Was this the first of the "smog" motors? Depends on the
year, perhaps. But a few Fox-body Mustangs I've driven with
that engine... the four-cyl engine is actually quicker, and gets
better gas mileage. Now, the 2600 Ford V-6 that was in the '72 and '73
Capri, is a whole different animal-- it hauls!

OTOH, I've heard good things about the Buick 231 V-6, and one I
drove seemed pretty peppy. It was still in the Buick.

Other engines KEP makes adapters for, include the Pinto 4-cyl, the
Corvair air-cooled flat-6, the Mazda RX-7 rotary, and the Porsche
air-cooled flat-6 (triple the cost of your bus! :). Check them out--
their adapters sound interesting and workable (speaking as one who
has never put his money where his mouth is by actually TRYING one).

I'm not affiliated with KEP... yet.
