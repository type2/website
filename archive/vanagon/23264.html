>Speaking from experience, the pilot bearing as it wears out caused me
>the following symptoms. First, when one of the needles jumped out of
>the rusty bearing, the tranny input shaft spun with the crankshaft.
>That's like having a broken clutch cable. You can't shift at all.
>That did't last long. The needle ground itself to dust. Eventually
>all of the needles fell out. Then shifting and driving was just fine.
>The second symptom is a good sized tranny oil leak, but only at high
>speeds.  That one is a real mystery unless you know what a pilot
>bearing is.  I replaced the rear tranny seal twice before somebody
>told me about the pilot bearing.
>
>Moral: inspect and grease the pilot bearing every time you remove the
>engine.
