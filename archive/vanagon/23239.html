Sounds like you're running rich when warm.

Could be:

1. Stuck or broken electric choke (unlikely, since they usually stick
open, making cold-idle shitty but warm starting/running fine)

2. Misadjusted carb idle air and/or idle screw. St. Muir has a good
procedure for adjusting this.

3. Wrong float level in the carb. Several possible causes-- a saturated
float (replace it), a loose needle valve seat (tighten it), a wrong
adjustment (this one never bit me, so I have no clue, but may be
pretty simple).
