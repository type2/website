Just let'em be!, they'll pump up again in a minute or so. DON'T rev the 
engine to help this out! Just let the 20-W-50 build up some heat and 
pressure to flow back into the lifters. My '78 also did this, especially 
parked on side hills. If it persists for more than 5 minutes without 
diminishing in fury I'd consider doing something. The Bentley manual covers 
bleeding (the tappets, not yours!).  Tilted parking also may get some oil 
into the cylinders, which is hard for the measly starter motor to churn up, 
especially in colder weather. I don't think I've left my  busses still for 
longer than 4-5 days at a time, you should run it every 2 weeks just to keep 
the batteries alive, and mice out of the heater boxes!   bye,    tim s.
