Jim: I'm not big on guns, beign Quaker so I can't shoot you.  I'll give 
you a suggestion, though, and more conservative than replacing the 
thermostat, which is do-able (and probably original, like mine and 
probably needs to be replaced) but a hassle getting to.

Try replacing the temperature sending switch.  There are two sensors
located on the top of the thermostat housing (it has the top large
radiator hose leading to it) the one with the rather intricate looking
connection is the sensor for the Electronic Contol Unit (ECU) or the
"computer" the one with the rather simple looking connection (push-on) is
the temperature gauge sending switch (if you are looking down on it, it's
the one on the right--and I guess there is no way to look at it but down). 
Before replacing it, clean the connection, it may be just caked in so much
crud that it works unreliably.  If that doesn't work, replace it. 

The thermostat is mounted on the bottom of the thermostat housing (the
part that the two switches are screwed in to, remove the flange that
connects to the lower radiator hose and the thermostat is mounted between
that flange and the thermostat housing.  I wish I could describe this more
accurately--will be glad to copy the appropriate pages out of Bentley
manual and send to you.  E-mail me your address.
