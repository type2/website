I don't think the moisture caused your problem.  The switch is incredible
simple and I don't think it could be harmed by a little water.  The function
of the switch is to signal the Digifant brain when there is a no-throttle or
full-throttle condition.  At no-throttle, the idle stabilazation is engaged.
 At full-throttle the Digifant richens the mixture.  I don't see how either
of these conditions (if they were engaged at the wrong time) could cause the
symptoms you describe.  I don't have a good suggestion on where the problem
lies, but my inclination is that the switch is not it.
