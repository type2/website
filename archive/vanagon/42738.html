> >   Second question will I need any special tools above Open/box wrenches,
> >  feelers, srewdrviers etc? If so what?

You will need:
    - new valve cover gaskets
    - sealant for the gasket if you like (apply *only* to the valve 
      cover side!)
    - Medium sized flat head screwdriver to pull the bail off
    - short (3" or so) 3/8" drive socket extension (as leverage to help 
      get bail back on--a trick David Schwarze showed me)
    - small flathead screwdriver to actually turn the adjusting screws
    - 14 mm box-end wrench to do the locknuts
    - 0.006" and 0.008" feeler gauges (set valves to 0.006" intake and 
      exhaust on the 1976 2.0L engine)
    - rags to clean as you go.

If this is your first time adjusting the valves, check that the fan 
pulley is marked *opposite* the timing mark so you can set cylinders 2 
and 4 to TDC easily and accurately.  If not, get some white paint and dab 
it on in the correct spot.  Measure accurately: Muir has a good 
description of how to do this.

I'm assuming you have solid lifters otherwise you wouldn't be asking.  
Solid lifters were stock, but a DPO could have installed hydraulics at 
some point.
