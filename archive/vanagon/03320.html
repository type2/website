Newer rads (including Vanagon's) are plastic and aluminum.  Instead
of soldering, the tanks are crimped on and a rubber gasket is used in between.

Some rad shops won't deal with them.  It takes a special machine to crimp
the thing back together and then you run the risk that the "ears" will
fatigue and the crimp won't hold.  Shop carefully for a good radiator shop!

Also, I imagine that this maybe a one shot deal.  The next time you'll
need a whole new radiator.

- Frank
