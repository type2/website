     This is something I've wondered about as well.  I, too, have an '85 that 
     I've noticed the needle rise close to the limit without the fan going on.
     
     I've checked the 2-position switch on the radiator and it turns the fan on 
     when shorted.  I've also looked in the Bentley at the wiring diagram, which 
     shows that the temp indicator is wired to the switch at the engine while 
     the fan is controlled at the radiator.  In addition, the cooling system 
     bleeding procedure suggests that the engine needs to be turning above 
     2000rpm for adequate circulation of the coolant.
     
     All of this, in my mind, indicates that when the temp needle starts to rise 
     at idle, we need to rev the engine and let it "idle" at +2000rpm.
     
     Any comments?
