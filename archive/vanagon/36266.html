When I saw your post I looked in JCW and was surprised not to see the CD
ignition I bought from them.  It was inexpensive and "universal" in that
it is triggered by the points, but puts only triggering voltage across
the points so it has nearly the "set it, forget it" characteristic of 
photo or hall-effect triggering.  It also has a reversible plug that lets
you return to normal points-and-condensor operation, for diagnostic pur-
poses.  (Except that the tach sender connects one spot in CD mode, another
spot in normal mode, I found.)  I used it on the 2.2l Porsche engine when
that was in my 81 Vgon, so I know it's good for six cylinders, presume it's
good for four.
