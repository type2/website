Well so much for the "do it yourself" spirit! ;-)  

It's really not lock rebuilding that's the issue here.  The lock barrel
breaks over time as a result of trying to force the worn/broken spring into
action.  We're not talking about the tumbler springs in the lock barrel or
the small spring in the handle button.  The spring that gives the trouble is
part of the lock release mechanism mounted on the door itself.   

I would recommend modifing the tumblers on a spare lock barrel to fit your
key if yours ever breaks.  It is easy to do and it will save you some spare
change to help buy that pop top seal or parts for the engine!!
