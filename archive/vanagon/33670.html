I understand your problem.  Same on my '84 GL.  You can beat
the system the way I do...go out the passenger side and cry
when you see your keys in the ignition.

Actually, this 'anti-lock-out' feature is an improvement
over the older buses for which you could only lock both
doors with a key once you were outside.  It is only an
improvement when you remember your keys.  Granted, this only
applied to the "front" doors and cargo doors.  You could
always crawl out the rear hatch (if you had the special
low-tech inside rear hatch opening mechanism).

I do have some familiarity with the handles, and the locks,
and I suspect that even if you switched handles it would
make no difference because that feature is built into the
actual lock inside the door.
