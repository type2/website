Your puzzle intreges me.  I've had similar problems but I've figured them
out.  I jumped into your string late so if you could give me some
background on the bus and probelm it would help me troubleshoot.  Flat
spots are more likely to be a fuel delivery problem than timing.  Is the
flat spot always there?  Or does it go away after the engine is warm.  And
is it always off of idle or anytime you hit the accelerator? Have you
rebuilt your carb?  Is it stock and original or from unknown origin?

As for timing, the engine will work just fine timed statically.  You don't
need to time it a 3500 rpm to get it right.  Remember, the distributor
advances.  Of course it SHOULD also work setting it dynamically but in your
case it may not be.  John Muir has a whole section on static versus
dynamic.  It's worth reading.

I hope I can help you shed light on this.
