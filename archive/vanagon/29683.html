>Derek,

>The output of most alternators can be adjusted.  The chip in the typical
>Bosch/Motorla internal regulator has a range of about ten volts.  On
>alternators with external regulators, such as the old Bosch I'm using,
>voltage is adjusted at the regulator by simply bending the relay contact.
> This provides a range of about +2 volts, more than enough to compensate for
>the 1.2 volt drop if your isolator uses garden variety diodes (Schottky
>diodes have a drop of only .7v.).  For a wider range of adjustment there is a
>wire-wound resistor that may be changed, a smaller value giving a higher
>voltage.
>
>I strongly recommend against using a single battery circuit.
>
>Some off-road racers mount a second alternator just to power the lights.
> This principle could be adopted to charge your auxiliary power circuit.
