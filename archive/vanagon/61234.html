I've seen 3 modes of failure on a odometer.
1) primary drive gear split, common, can be fixed with string and 
glue.
2) same gear shatters, need to replace with one from junker, hard to 
find un-broken
3) shaft gear is on slides out form housing and disengages gears.  
Tried several things, but ended up replacing speedo housing.

You'll have to take speedo apart to figure out this one, different 
versions for different years and models.

I spent $55 for two broken odo instrument clusters, and with mine, I 
ended up with 2 functinal ones.  Holefully cheaper where you are, 
Sacramento Vanagon used parts are pricey.

The voltage stabilizer on on the instrument panel can affect the gas 
guage reading.  I replaced both that and the gas guage with no 
change, so on mine, it's the sender and I haven't gotten around to 
dropping the tank.
