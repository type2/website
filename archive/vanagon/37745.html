You serious about trying some of my suggestions?  Well, here goes...

Your looking to remove something that was deposited with a solvent as a
vehicle.  Undercoating can be more easily dissolved than worn away.  Try
Easy-off oven cleaner.  It should dissolve all of that stuff.  It may leave a
chemical residue, though.  But blasting afterward may remove that.  You might
also try gasoline (dangerous), Zip-strip aerosol (residue again), or you may
heat the undercoating with a heat gun and scrape it off with a putty knife
(round the sharp edges of the putty knife with a grinder) just like you would
remove varnish from a valuable old piece of furniture (no residue).

The problem is that you're trying to blast something away that is absorbing
the energy of your media.  It is deflecting the grains of sand like a beach
ball would repel a baseball if thrown at it.  If you check, your undercoating
is probably getting warm from the energy, getting soft from the heat, then
eroding with the sheer overwhelming flow of media.

Just a thought.  Good luck!
