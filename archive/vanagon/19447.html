I can't imagine what the man's race has to do with it. In those days of
extremely wide doors there were countless people of all races, religions
and professions (even lawyers) who were quite content to ding your car
because of the extra effort it might cost them to open their door
carefully. In addition there was a very cavalier attitude to cars in
general, as they quickly rusted and wore out. Recall the practice of
(parallel) parking by ear.

I agree that the man's behavior was unconscionable, but, again, I don't
think race has anything to do with it. Nor should it be reported by the
narrator.
