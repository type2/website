> Excellent question!  Originally the "bird" flip (originated in Italy in the
> late 1500s) consisted of the thumb, pinkee, and index finger, thus
> resembling a bird's "foot" and hence the name.  As generations used and
> reused this great tradition various derivations arose, leading to much
> confusion, especially among fellow bird watchers and early orinthologist
> clubs.  In late October, 1788, a select group of European diplomats gathered
> in Paris to universalize this profound expression of anger.  After much
> bickering between the Italian and German delegates a physical fight ensued
> between Alfredo Romeo and Gustov Grundig which resulted in Grundig cutting
> off all of Romeo's fingers except the middle.   The gathering was postponed
> till later the next year, but events in Paris prohibited a second meeting.
> So that's how the modern usage of the middle finger came about.
