Ron Van Ness writes:
>	I was thinking of replacing my front engine seal on my '71 
>Westy.  What I've read tells me I must pull the engine first.  It looks 
>possible with much luck, lubrication, and aggravation to do it without 
>pulling the engine.  Despite the ease of engine removal on my bus I would 
>like to avoid that process in this weather and under my current working 
>conditions. If anyone's done it before and could make any suggestions I 
>would like to hear from you.  If it isn't possible I guess I'd like to hear 
>about that too.

You *cannot* replace the *front* seal (front is FRONT) without removing the
engine, then removing the flywheel from the engine.  If you mean the REAR seal
(which early Type 1 engines don't have, per se), then you can probably replace
it easily with the engine in the car.
