Bzzzzztttt!  Wrong.  The visual inspection is just as valid a criteria
to fail a vehicle as the tailpipe emissions.  You've hit a nerve.  
I'm in the process of getting my '73 camper with a '78 engine inspected.
There are 3 major points in the smog inspection.  1. Tailpipe emissions,
2. Visual inspection and 3. Functional testing.  That's right, not only
does all that smog plumbing have to be there, but it has to be *working*.
If you fail any one of the 3 you FAIL the check.  Last time I was inspected 
they failed me for not having an unleaded restrictor in the fuel filler neck.
The tailpipe emissions were well within the limits for a '78 engine.
