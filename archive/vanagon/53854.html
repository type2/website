When the engine heats up, the bad spray pattern from a fouled injector
is masked by the vaporization resulting from the gas hitting the hot
cylinder walls. So I would expect a fouled injector to only run better
as the miles roll by.
On the other hand, when the engine is cold, a fouled injector, that does
not atomize the fuel, will not ignite properly/evenly. So I would expect
a fouled injector to cause trouble starting and running smoothly in the
first few miles. (watch out for fuel contamination in the oil - test by
smell).
