  Unless I'm mistaken, the flaps and thermostat control only the lower limit
  on operating temperature - An engine without them is no less likely to 
  overheat. Furthermore, oil that does not reach operating temperature is 
  bad stuff to have in your engine. Acids are formed as oil oxidizes and 
  heating it up to "boil" them off is the only way to get rid of them, other
  than changing the oil. I suspect that there could be a relationship between 
  above average wear and oil in poor condition.
