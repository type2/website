	I've been watching this thread with some bemusement... especially 
	the worrying about oil pressure instead of moving part lubrication.
	IF you are using a high-quality synthetic (Mobil1, SYNTEC, etc)
	fluctuations in oil pressure should not be cause for alarm. HIGH
	oil pressure should NOT be a HolyGrail...Even *better* oil pressure
	shouldn't be cause for rejoicing unless your engine *needs* it!

	It's also interesting to think about the VW OEM filter bypass valve
	and why said valve isn't built into the engine like in some other
	vehicles. Having an oil FILTER, at all, in a horizontally oppposed
	VW was, after all, a significant forward step!

	My Villager mini-van has a ball-valve on the engine side of the 
	oil-filter receiver, for example, allowing me to use those cheap 
	FRAM/Purolator/TARGET/etc filters with its Quarterly (~8-10Kmiles) 
	Mobil1 change.

	I'll bet most of you DIY oilchangers cause more engine damage after 
	an oil change by NOT filling the filter (OEM or not) BEFORE 
	installation than you might incur with perceived low oil pressure(s).
