	Reading this, I suddenly remembered that I inherited a '55 bus in 1974
	for taking care of a friend's dogs for a few months.

	It was painted like a big American flag and it had a 1300 engine in it
	which required the teeth to be ground off the flywheel making for
	the only hand-crank start '55 for many miles, I'm sure. 

	And, it was a sure bet to impress folks
	sitting near the windows in cafe's in Alfred, New York to see me
	open the rear hatch, flip a toggle (ignition), mount the homemade
	crank through that stock keyhole and make it start on first try.

	Needless to say, it was a tad cool inside in that upstate NY winter.

	It lost its' brakes on a downhill and was transformed into a chicken
	coop.
