I'm writing from memory here, because it has been a couple of years
since I had to fix one of these.

There is (was) a small spring that hooks in a small hole in a lever
that is mounted in the up-down plane. The other end of the spring
fastens to the mechanism housing. The moveable end of the lever gets
stuck if the spring is missing or broken. You can coerce it out of the
way by pounding on the door above the handle, or you can replace the
spring.  To replace the spring, the lock mechanism must be removed
from the door--not a fun task. That's why I've lived with the problem
for over a year on one of my doors even though I know I can fix it.

Maybe I'll get to it this weekend and post better instructions on the
fix next week.
