If I understand you correctly, you mean to say the fan draws less
current at higher rpms.  This is true (provided that the Voltage drop
across the motor is held constant).  However, before cavitation
occurs, at the 'working range' of the fan, bigger pressure
differential at either side of the fan means lower rpms for the fan,
which would mean a higher current draw.
