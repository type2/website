To remove large, rusty Phillips screws, I just use plenty of Liquid 
Wrench #1, then put the screwdriver to it.  Push it in hard, turn left, 
then hit the back end of the screwdriver with about a 16 oz ball peen 
hammer a few times while maintaining the torque with your other hand.  
(Don't miss!!)  this most always does it.  

You can pick up one of those "hand impact drivers" at most cheap tool 
stores for under $20 (or JCW has them, too).  They work pretty neat, I 
wouldn't be without one.  They come with a giant Phillips head driver.

Good Luck! :<)
