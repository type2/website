  Jeez, Dave, you ask the tough questions. A straight and solid '71 is hard
  to pass up. I'd be a little nervous about taking off on a long trip with a
  motor that has only ten miles on it. And, although I am a big fan of '71s,
  the pancake motor makes for more pleasant <and more rapid, if that matters>
  freeway driving. On the other hand, the upright motor in the '71 has a lot
  less plumbing attached to it and should be simpler and cheaper to repair if  
  required during your trip.  Here in the S.F. Bay Area, $3500 would be a  
  high price.
