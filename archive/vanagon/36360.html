Sean,
I think you're barking up the wrong tree.  I haven't been following the
thread but it doesn't sound like a ground prob to me.  It sounds more like
a positive wire problem or a bad relay.  Are you sure that juice is getting
to the signal switch and relay?  Use a test light to check the current flow
from the fuse to the switch and then to the relay and then to the lights.

Possible problems are a broken wire, bad fuse, corroded fuse holder,
corroded wire connector, or a short.  Since nothing works, I would guess
that the lights, sockets, and gounds are okay and the problem lies under
the dash.  Actually, I'll go further, I'll bet that there is no juice going
to the switch and the problem is between the fuse box and the switch.
Good luck.
