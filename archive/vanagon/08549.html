i think it was just an attempt to solve the old beetle engine problem of
#3 exhaust valve breaking. and they found out that the Type 4 engine didn't
really need them (and therefore some costs could be eliminated by getting
rid of the more expensive valves). it may be that fuel injection came along
about then, and with more control over the mixture (i.e., idiot-owner won't
be dorking with it as much), they could keep the temperatures more closely
under control.
