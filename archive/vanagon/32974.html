Subject: Re:  synthetic oil

Synthetics (Mobil-1 at least) have been known to cause rubber seals to 
either swell or shrink in some cars.  In some cases the problem goes
away, in others it just leaks some for the life of the engine.

My SAAB with aircooled turbo, ran for 180k miles on synthetic.  It leaked
some, but not enough to ever add oil between changes.  As some other
list member attested, this car "fell apart" around a strong, perfect running
engine.

I've run Castrol Syntec in my '86 since breakin with no trouble.
(I've also heard that synthetic is too slippery for rings to seat
correctly on a new engine)
