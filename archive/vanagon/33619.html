Well, some people came to look at my '71 bus over the weekend,
and naturally it chose to run like sh*t that day. They left, saying
"I'll get back to you" and other politenesses, which at least sounded
better than "You lying, scumbag hose monster", which was maybe
what they were thinking after I'd told them it ran fine but had problems
with overheating and oil leaks.

I hadn't driven it for a while since getting the V6anagon, and hadn't
done a tune-up in a while, which became my first suspect. Popped off
the #2 plug wire, and it broke in my hand. Hmmm, maybe a leetle problem
with plug wires here... put them on my shopping list.

Put my wrench on the #2 plug, and that sucker did NOT want to come out.
By putting all the torque on it I could in that cramped engine compartment,
I could get it to turn ssslllooooowwwlllyyyy, fighting me every inch of
the way. Yes, I AM turning it in the right direction.

I'm about to put a cheater pipe on my wrench handle, but thought I'd
ask a few "saner heads" for advice before assaulting those soft aluminum
heads like that. The engine is stone cold, I haven't run it since the
weekend.

Anyone have any advice on coaxing a reluctant plug out of a VW? This
plug is actually the easiest one to get to in a bus-- #1 will be tougher,
being farther from the hatch and under the air cleaner mount (I've already
lifted out the air cleaner to get more room). Hope #1 doesn't decide to
display solidarity with its brother.

Frankly I'm not too far from yanking the engine and stuffing a correct
dual-port one in, in its place (this one is a '70 single-port 1600, with
Solex 30PICT-3 carb and standard VW bus muffler). Only the $$ are stopping
me, as usual. Ever notice how few truly rich people drive old VW buses?

Frustrated in San Diego,
