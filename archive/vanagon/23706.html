Hey, guys, he's looking at a TORN-DOWN engine, meaning he's got
just a bare case. Cases were almost identical between type 1 and
type 3, despite the huge differences in stuff bolted TO the cases.

I think the only visible differences in the case themselves (please
somebody correct me if I'm wrong), is the area where the fuel pump
and/or generator stand would mount on a type-1. On a type one bare
case, both of these areas have holes for pushrods, oil breather, etc.
On a type 3 bare case, one or both of these areas is solid metal.

(Somebody on this thread also mentioned that the oil cooler mount
is different? The cooler is certainly in a very different place!

Many moons ago, I ran into a guy who said he built a type-3 pancake
engine aout of a type-1 bug crankcase, by fabricating a cover for
the open area on top of the case and bolting it in place.
