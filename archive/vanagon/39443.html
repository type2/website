Is there such a thing as an engine running TOO cool?

In light of what (sorry I can't remember who quoted this) Gene Berg says
about fixing the problem, not the symptom, it struck me that it might be
possible to overcorrect.  If an engine is well-tuned and running fine,
would it make sense to add an oil-cooler as a precaution?  Or could you
bring the temp so low as to put the motor in a constant state of "warming
up" and never reach proper range.
