OK.  I run a 1600DP w/ 009 at 5000' in a '70 #230XXXXXXX with a setting of 
12.0 degrees BTDC per the recommendation of the FLARS.  10 seemed to make 
it run hot, 7.5 was "el sluggo" off the line with reduced power.  

Most of my travelling is to them thar hills (6000'-10000'), so a bit of 
extra timing advance seems to be to my advantage.  BTW the valves have 
yet to get tight (even the venerable #3!), compression is just fine,  and no 
overheats (clunk, clunk, clunk--sound of me knocking for luck 
on my padded dash).

The point, you ask?   12.0 BTDC _at_ 5000' = OK; 10.0 _at_ 1200 = hmmmm, maybe 
set it back a little.
