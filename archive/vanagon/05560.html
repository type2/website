What you want is a porta-potty.  The one we have actually came with the '74
hardtop camper that we previously owned.  It's great because my wife has to
go fairly often and I hate stopping so frequently.  She just closes all the
drapes and takes her seat.  The one we have is kind of small but adequate.
Easy to clean.  Just dump the refuse into a toilet.  Rinse it out and put
in the chemical and water and you're done.  They make bigger ones.  I don't
see any reason why you'd have trouble using one.  I go on a trip without
it.  It stays in the camper.  If your camper is equipped with a bed, toilet
(porta-potty counts) and a water supply, then it is eligible for an RV
tax deduction.  I found this out from a from of mine and have used it
for a couple years now.  Sears carries a variety of the porta-potty's
in various sizes and features.  I'm sure you could find one to 'fit'
your needs. ;)
