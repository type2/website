Ethanol has a specific heating value of 29.7MJ/kg, compared with gasoline's 
47.3MJ/kg. But diesel is 44.8MJ/kg. Diesel engines use less fuel because 
they run at much higher compression ratios than gasoline engines, and 
operate (at least in theory) on a constant pressure cycle which is more 
efficient than a constant volume cycle. Thermal efficiency is directly 
proportional expansion ratio. You get more bang per drop out of diesel 
because you can squeeze the heck out of it before burning it and then expand 
it more completely.
