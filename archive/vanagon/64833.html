Tony, don't worry about the heavy duty cables.  You only have to run one
over to the main battery and ground the other anywhere handy.  It is
worth it, 'cause I guarentee you will have occasion to want to crank
with that 2nd battery.  For most of a year I had a misterious starting
problem that required the help of bat #2.  I put a marine switch on the
back of the main battery case, but you don't have to go that far: Just a
cut out switch will do.  Plus all the other times bat #1 goes dead.
