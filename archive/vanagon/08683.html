> As the former owner of a '72, I will chime in here. Access to the carbs on
 a '72 is not great as you are already aware. You can work on them , the
 fuel lines and tee if you remove the air cleaner. To get it out, you will
 have to remove the paper hose that goes from the heater booster fan to the
 engine tin on the right hand side. The air cleaner is held to it's mounting
 plate by over-center bales which you can't see, but will have to feel. When
 removing it, remember that it has oil in it, you really don't want to turn it
 upside down. Once it is out, you will be able to see the fuel lines. If you
 want more access, you can remove the aforementioned booster fan and the
 carbon canister from the evaporative emissions system (make sure to put it
 back before you start the car though, otherwise gasoline vapor from the 
 tank will be vented into the engine compartment, not a good idea from an 
 explosion prevention standpoint.)

 As for the tank, my '74 sat for 18 months before I bought it. I cleaned the
 "sock" filter at the tank outlet and found some rusty sludge there, but I 
 haven't had any trouble, knock on wood.
