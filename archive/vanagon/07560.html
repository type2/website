and to Donald, who asked what the overflow tank was for...
When the coolant heats up it expands, taking up more room (that's why 
you open the bleeder screw when the engine is getting hotter)
In the confines of the coolant system it is restricted from expanding
therefore building up pressure.  When the pressure gets greater than
what the cap is set for, the cap allows it to flow through that little
braided hose into the overflow tank.  When the engine cools off, it sucks
it back into the engine.
