The (*bloody*) pins in every connector anywhere on the Bus (& on all other
vehicles that I'm aware of) are swaged onto the wire, but never soldered.
Works for 100k miles or the extent of the warranty, whichever is shorter,
but for various obvious physical reasons they have a tendency to loosen &
either cause an increase in resistance, or outright failure.  Remove the
pins from the connector housing, re-crimp, apply some paste flux and solder
(if you're a *really* tweaky type, use 5% silver solder - cheap & will still
be conducting when the rest of the vehicle is long gone)

(BTW, sometimes those pins were never seated properly in the housing in the
first place - it'll drive you nuts trying to track down an intermittant
caused by this)
