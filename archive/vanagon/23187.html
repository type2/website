Apparently one of the reason that 3-wheelers such as the Morgan were popular 
in the UK, was that 4-wheeled vehicles paid tax as "motorcars" whilst vehicles 
with fewer wheels paid a lower tax rate as "motorcycles".  Thus, three-wheeled 
cars were technically motorcycles "within the meaning of the Act".

Also, the tax was based on displacement of the engine, with the rate going up 
something fierce, thus encouraging smaller cars. 

I don't even want to think what the tax on a Bentley 8-litre would have been...
