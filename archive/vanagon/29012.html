Oooh, goody..how to figger out height (or rolling diameter as Al mentions) from 
the tire specs...this is fun!

Given the two sizes,205/70 and 195/75....

The first number is the tread width in Millimeters. The second number is the 
"aspect ratio", which is derived as the percentage of height the sidewall has in 
relation to the tread width.

For the 205/70, the width is 205mm and the sidewall height is 70% of 205mm, or 
143.5mm. For the metric impaired, that would be 5.64". So, add (2 x 5.64) to the 
rim diameter to get total height.

Similarly, the 195/75 is 195mm wide and the sidewall height is 75% of 195mm, 
which is 146.25mm, or 5.75". In this case, add (2 x 5.75) to the rim diameter to 
get the total height.

Looks like the two are pretty close in height.

Just my $.02 worth...
