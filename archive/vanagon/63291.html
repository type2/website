Scott,

For what it is worth here are some tips from my Dometc experience.

First of all, there are two possibilities for the buzzing noise.  If it is
the fridge burner don't worry.  My fridge does it and it is my
understanding they all make some kind of noise (buzzing, roaring, rumbling,
etc.).  If it is the ventilation fan (thermostatically controlled), and it
is on too frequently, then reduce the thermostat on the fridge.  Also, try
not to park the drivers side of the van (fridge burner vent side) in the sun.

As for cooling it on AC or DC, you should check that the AC plug is plugged
in to the Vanagon AC socket under the sink.   Open the cupboard to the
right of the fridge and look at the back wall.  You will see it.  Also
check the fuses in the small black box on the wall behind the drivers seat.
 My fridge works great on 110V AC.

On the advice of several listees, I tried running the fridge on propane
while driving.  Works great!  So fire the fridge up before you leave, fill
it with pre-cooled food, and never switch modes.  Works for me.

Happy trails.
