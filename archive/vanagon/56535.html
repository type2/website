Randy--CHANGE MECHANICS

The O2 sensor has little effect UNTIL the engine's warm.  Get it changed
and change your mechanic while you're at it.  Whatever he 'adjusted'
probably needs to be readjusted.  And the O2 sensor WILL affect your
mileage.  You can probably find a post from me in the archives
explaining how, but you can replace the O2 sensor with a generic and
save about $80.  You'll have to cut and splice, if you can't find the
information in the archives, let me know.
