I just got done debugging all this on my '86.  The fan is controlled by
the radiator temp.  The guage back in the engine compartment at the
thermostat housing.  If the temp goes up, and the fan no go on, bleed the
system per Bentley.  If this doesn't help there is a flow problem.
(Assuming the fan SW and relay are good as well)

In my case it was the radiator.  After 150k miles it didn't allow enuf
H2O thru to cool the engine at idle.  This slowly got worse until it
would overheat anytime the ambient temp went up >70f, even on the
highway.

I was doing the rev the engine trick for a while as well.
