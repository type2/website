	Someone said the catalyst accounts for .1% HC, someone
else said 50% of their engine's actual output. both could be right- .1% HC
is 1000ppm, in other words, a HUGE ammout. .1% *of the limit* would be a
small number, of course. The modern three-way catalyst is a marvel
of applied engineering. Works great, has no moving parts. Only problem
in in lamda systems, with oxygen sensors, the oxygen sensor has
a murder-suicide pact with the cat.- if the oxygen sensor fails, engine
goes to full rich and the cat may melt down, blocking the exhaust flow
and taking a while to troubleshoot. If I ever had time on my hands
I'd probably try to retro-fit my '70 with catalysts, and it probably
wouldn't work, what with the relativly sloppy mixture control even
a good carb has. I figured one-per-bank rather than the cheap two-to-one
deal of the later type IV... Like I have time for this ;)  Maybe I'd just 
have to spring for a type IV with FI :)
