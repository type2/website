Specifically, check the dipstick.  Someone famous (Muir?) once said that
if the dipstick is hot enough that you can't hold it for more than a
second, your engine is running too hot.  Seems reasonable.  I've
verified that with my temp gauge.  At 180, the dipstick is cool as a
cucumber.  At 230, it's getting pretty hot.
