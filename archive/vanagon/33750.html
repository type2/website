I thought that in the VW FI system, the operation of the cold-start
valve is electrically controlled by the thermo-time switch.  The only
thing that pressing on the accelerator does is move a butterfly valve in
the intake air distributor to let more air into the engine, hence the
name Air Flow Controlled Fuel Injection.  The accelerator pedal has no
effect at all on the cold-start valve.  The thermo-time switch operates
that depending on the temperature of the air around the engine.  The
engine computer automatically computes the right mixture and all that
for cold starting, making pushing on the accelerator unnecessary.  So
say Bentley, Muir, and the VW owner's manuals.
