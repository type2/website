The thermostat

Simply said, the thermostat decides whether the coolant will be cooled
by the radiator at the front of the car or not.  Therefore, it governs 
whether the coolant will circulate in the "small" or "large" coolant
loop (see page 72). 

Of course the thermostat controls the coolant flow dependent on the
coolant temperature.  That happens via a container filled with a special
wax and the container's valve plate.  When the coolant warms up the wax
softens and expands, with the larger volume forcing the valve open.  As
long as the coolant temperature rises, the thermostat increases the cold
water inflow from the radiator.  If the coolant temperature falls, a
spring in the thermostat pushes the valve into the "closed" position
and seals off the cold water inflow until operating temperature is
reached again.

Note: While on the road, if the coolant starts boiling due to a
defective thermostat, only towing the car or a complete replacement of
the thermostat at the location of the breakdown will help.  If you
decide for replacement, you should wait until the coolant temperature
has dropped to acceptable levels.  Otherwise you run the risk of burning
yourself!  In the meantime, get containers to capture the coolant.

Removing the thermostat

Thermostat removal is identical for gasoline and diesel engines:
* let coolant cool down, otherwise danger of burning yourself
* partially drain coolant (see page 74)
* remove lowest coolant hose at the thermostat
* gasoline engines: remove protective heat shield from the hose
* loosen two six-edged screws at bottom of thermostat housing and
  remove the lower housing lid
* take out thermostat
* when replacing, orient the thermostat so that the spring is on top
* use a new o-ring for the housing lid.

Testing the thermostat

You can check yourself with a thermometer if the thermostat opens at
the proper temperature.  Here are the data:  starts to open at 85 C,
completely open at 105 C, the full displacement is at least 8mm (gas
engine) or 7mm (diesel).
* remove thermostat
* hang thermostat in a pot with water, and heat water
* check when the valve starts to open and when fully open
* check displacement of valve
