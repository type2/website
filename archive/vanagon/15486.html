>3M makes an excellent seam sealer for this purpose, available

3M makes several differnt types, two of which I've had experience with.

There is a tan sealer, that is very thick and rubbery, and when you put 
this stuff on, you _know_ nuthin's gettin thru.  Its a pain to clean up,
though - laquer thinner seems to work best.  Its great for filling the void
betwixt panels though.

The other stuff is bright white, and is much thinner.  Its a lot more 
manueverable into tight areas, but the jury (me) is still out on how effective
it it.  I bought it by mistake - I'd intended to get the tan stufff...
