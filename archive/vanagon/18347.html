  Those pesky oil galley plugs are known for popping out during
          periods of high oil pressure, like when first starting the
          engine on a cold moring. They can be replaced with
          threaded pipe plugs, though. This would mean removing the
          engine to get to the front (FIF) one, and removing
          the fan housing for the rear one. This is highly
          recommended during a T-4 rebuild, and can be done while
          the engine is together. Just be careful to suck out any
          metal shavings that may be left in the galleys, and use
          thread locking compound on the plugs
