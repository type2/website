On Thu, 30 Jun 1994, David Kao wrote:

> This is what I believe about hydraulic lifters.
> 
> They provide some cushioning effect to reduce impact and wear on rockers, lifters
> and valve tips. Tappet clearance is totally eliminated. The hydraulic lifter also
> self adjusts. The engine will run quieter and the valve train will last longer.
> It eliminates the need of valve adjustment. Valve opening and closing is more
> accurate. Engine performance is always at the best.
> 
> It works like a pump. when it is fully pumped up it will work almost like a solid
> lifter. It will firmly press against the valve tip but not firm enough to push
> the valve off its seat, which would cause the valve to overheat and warp.
> 
> However, if the car has been sitting around for too long (sometimes could be just
> a few days or even in one day) the lifetr might collapse. When the engine is
> stationary half of the valves are compressed and partially opened. This causes
> the compressed oil inside the lifter to leak out. Cars with a lot of miles tend
> to have worn lifters and will leak faster.
> 
> This is not a problem though. Once the engine is started oil will be pumped
> back into the lifter again. It might take up to 30 minutes to fully recharge
> all lifters. Do not drive the car if lifters are still clicking loud. the noise
> will go away eventually. Replace dirty engine oil with fresh one if it takes a
> long time to recharge the lifters. Once the noice is gone take a 5 minute driv
> to fully recharge the lifters.
> 
> Lifters can go bad if they do wear out and can no longer withhold preasure.
> This seldom happens really. Kepp enging oil clean is the key to keep them alive.
>
