Hey - I just did this!

Rebuilding the calipers is an easy job.  Pop the pistons out one a time - I
used a bicycle floor pump and just wedged the rubber tube into the caliper.
Make sure you put a block of wood between the pistons to protect them.
When the piston comes out it will sound like a gun shot.  Clean up any
corrosion/goop on the pistons and reassemble with the new seals.  I used
brake rebuilding gel from the local FLAPS as it made everything slide
together easily.  I also cleaned everything a couple of times with this
gel.  To get the seal around the top of the pistons I used a high
technology tool made of a large C-clamp and a jam jar lid of the
appropriate diameter.

If the calipers are scored you are in trouble.  I believe they are chrome
plated and cannot be honed.  Probably not cheap to replace.
