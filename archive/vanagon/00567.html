NONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONONO!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!
	A 100apm load from a 12V device is huge!!!
	Use at least 4ga wire and I would recommend running 2 4ga wires
	in parallel for anything this HOT!!! The amount of current a run of 
	wire can support is related directly to 2 things: most importantly,
	the cross sectional area of the wirepath, and seccondly, the length 
	of the run. Power = Current*Current*Resistance; with such a high
	current, reducing the resistance is critical. I presume the length 
	of the run is fixed, so the only variable remaining X sectional area.
	Make it as big as you practically can! The Power consumed is also 
	somewhat constant, so if you are drawing Xamps across Yfeet of cable
	run, then for a small cable (8ga-) you may overheat (excess resistance)
	the cable to a dangerous level while at the same time nuking the 
	battery. Drawing big power from a small source will tend to ruin the 
	small source.
	1200W is an increadible amount of power to be removed from a 12V 
	source and any plans to do so must be very carefully thought out!!!
 
> 	Second, make sure that your battery is capable of producing that
