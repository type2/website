I agree with the two others. I've never even seen Rustoleum stop rust
effectively. I've heard of chemicals that convert rust to something
inactive, which sounds great if it works. I've never tried that.

My favorite trick, is to get some motor oil and a teeny paint brush.
dab oil on the rust in the seam, and let it spread along the seam. It's
messy-- you'll wind up with oil streaks coming from every seam you treat.
Don't wash the bus for two days afterward, or let it get rained on if
you cn avoid it.

Oil stops the rust COLD. I did this to a Datsun 610 in Minneapolis,
and two years later the rust had gone no farther than when I bought
it. Amaze your friends!

The bad news is, the oil wears off after several months, so it's a good
idea to re-do this every 4-6 months or so. But you can buy a lot of
30W and teeny brushes for $1200.
