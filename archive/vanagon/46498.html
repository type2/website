Mine doesn't have a reset button, but to the right (rearwards) of the 
filler attachment, their is a small bleeder knob. Apparently on most 
car systems which use this type of filler, the bleeder only needs to 
be opened a bit. The first time I tried it the thing too $.20 worth 
of gas & stopped. I told the guy to try opening the knob all the way -
this did the trick. It then took about 11 litres worth of gas (tank 
was right empty at the time.)
