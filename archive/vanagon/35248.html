I'm no VW guru so I could be wrong, but I hav had this problem in other 
makes of car.

It sounds like the Gearbox "layshaft" bearings.

In a "normal" (read rear wheel drive/front engine) the input and output 
shafts are in line. There is a load of sliding gears and syncros on these 
shafts and they mesh with a gear (the laygear) off to one side.

In first gear the laygear (and hence layshaft) do a lot of work changing 
the ratio and hence the loud noise in first. As you change gears the 
Laygear/layshaft does less work and so gets quieter, and in top gear it 
does nothing and is almost silent.

The upshot of all this is it sounds like a gearbox rebuild. But dont take 
it as gospel, as I'm no expert in VW's.
