The later engines are a bolt-in item (between 911's)  with the exception of 
fuel injection issues.  The later engines were either DME or Jetronic, so 
Its doubtful that someone who has a car with a 2.7 is prepared to handle
the FI without some work.  The shop I takemy car to (my Porsche) recently
converted a '71 911 from the 2.2 to a 3.2.  The got about 125 hours of
labor for it, too.  The guy supplied his own engine.  The labot figure
is a little high 'cause they had to do some tranny work to - his was 
going out.  This guy obviously had more money than he knew what to do with...
