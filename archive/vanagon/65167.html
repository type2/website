Why was this so hard.  Surely someone out there must have had similar
experiences, I suppose it is cause if you don't have an Ox reset problem, you
don't think about it.

Here is what I've found:

o It sounds like Canadian vehicles don't have an Ox Sensor light.  I believe
Bently mentions something about "US" only, or maybe the '89 owners manual, I'm
not sure which, it was a looong weekend.

o My '89 SyncroBeast has the Ox light, but doesn't have the reset.  (No wires
past the fuse block, connector C, pins 2 and 11)  Stickers on the door jam
lead me to believe that mine is NOT Canadian, but US.

o My '90 Vanagon Carat did not have.  Not even mentioned in the owners manual.
 Same situation as SyncroBeast, but w/o light in dash.  Wire from dash to
connector block B on fuse block, nothing else.

o Bently shows you where it is if you have a reset box for the Ox sensor
light.  It's on the left side (Drivers side) frame rail, inside, just above
the spare tire.

o Be aware, not all vehicles have the Ox light reset.  If you have it, it's in
the stock location Bently shows, if it's not there, then you shouldn't have a
reset problem.
