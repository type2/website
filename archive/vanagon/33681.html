With a carb, stepping on the acellerator before starting the engine squirts
some gas into the manifold which makes the mix fuel rich.  This aids in
starting and is similar to the way a choke works.  The choke cuts off the
air supply temporarily, making the mix fuel rich.  Once the engine warms up
the choke opens to allow the proper amount of air.  See John Muir's book
for a full explanation.
