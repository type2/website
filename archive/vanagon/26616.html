No engineering input from me, I'm afraid, Bill... even though I are one.
Just the experience at both low (<30) and high (<55) speeds, that
my engine started pinging and smelling hot while towing (mostly on freeways)
with the hatch closed, but stopped fairly quickly once I opened the
engine hatch.

My opinion on why (yes, guesswork now), is that an overworked engine
starts heating up the air in the engine compartment, and doesn't inhale
it into the cooling fan and out the bottom of the bus, fast enough to
get it replaced thru the upper vents with cooler air. So you get warmer
air flowing over the cylinders, which causes the engine to run even
warmer, and around and around in a vicious cycle that results in
significant overheating. Opening the hatch scavenges that hot air out,
in my opinion.

Yes, this isn't proof. But it works for me.
