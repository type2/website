My 300D is a 1981 with 75k miles.  It's non-turbo, so I control the amount 
of smoke with the throttle.  That the capacity to burn diesel is limited by 
the amount of air being drawn into the engine.  If I start at full 
throttle, it puts out quite a bit of smoke.  If I start at 1/2 to 3/4 
throttle, I achieve the same speed without the smoke.  The neat little TDI 
takes care of that for you.  So you don't have to know how to drive a 
diesel.

I love the TDI, but I've got to wear out my MB first!
