Subject: Used compressors and air tools


Peter asks about air tools and you replied: 
 
> Peter, look for a used compressor, 3-5 hp, 20-25 gal tank, this would be
> minimum. In finding one used, you might get some used tools with it. The air
> tools are a lot less expensive that electric, they don't weigh as much and
> folks don't tend to steal them as often. You can use air for lots of
> different things, blowing out the shop, filling up tires, powering air tools,
> etc. I've got an air drill, air rachet, die grinders (2), impact wrench and a
> few other specialty tools. Changing tires is great with the impact wrench. I
> picked up a few hundred feet on air hose and I can sandblast outside (did my
> house/foundation this summer) and when I do my roof, I will rent an air
> nailer. Just having compressed air to blow/clean things is worth the price of
> the compressor. 

In the case of the used compressor, I would highly recommend that people
do *NOT* buy a used compressor. Compressors have a nasty habit of rusting
from the inside out. All the compressed air causes a lot of condensation
in the tank and condensation == rust. Rust weakens the tank until finally
BOOM. shrapnel everywhere. No matter how good a compressor tanks looks
from the outside, you don't know what the inside looks like.

I once worked in a machine shop where we had a compressor tank incident. 
Fortunately in that instance, there were no injuries.

However, I feel that there is nothing wrong with used air tools.
