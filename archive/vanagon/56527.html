Subject: Re: Lighter tyres for lighter steering? 

As a general rule, this statement is correct for comparable tire designs.

But, the tire's contact patch "area" does not remain constant for different 
tire designs.

The contact patch area is a function of the spring rate of the tire for a 
given inflation pressure.

Contact patches can have different contact pressures for different tires 
designs.  And, for a given load, different pressures result in different 
contact areas.  Reinforced tires tend to have a higher spring rates and 
smaller contact patch areas than comparable standard tires under the same 
load.

Also, the contact patch is dependant on the kinematics of the vehicle's 
suspension.

Different suspensions will induce different tire camber changes, which 
directly relates to the shape of the tire's contact patch.  For example, you 
do not want to have a wide tire on an old VW with rear swing axles.
