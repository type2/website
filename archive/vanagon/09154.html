The "1835" engine has the same case as a 1600, just with bigger pistons and
cylinders (and a stroker crankshaft?  Damn, I can't remember!).  In other
words, the mounts would be the same.  In fact, you wouldn't even have to
get a different engine, just buy the parts and install them onto your
present engine.  I have a friend with a bug set up similar to Lee's
description (except it is around 2100cc) and it gives my Mustang a run for
it's money from 0-60.  Veeeeery impressive what you can do with a type I
motor...
