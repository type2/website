	My sainted mother got front-ended in our '68 and no one
in our vehicle was injured- someone pointed out that most cars
are lower than your bus, and this is a very good point. Hit
a Honda with your bus and the odds are you'll slide over its
hood. I wouldn't want to be in the Honda...
	I've seen, and my brother has, pictures that a Swedish
magazine did of a Vanagon and a Volvo station wagon in an offset
front end collision. The Vanagon rode up over the hood of the
Volvo and stove-in its front windshield. Both cars were instrumented
and had dummies in them- the dummies in the VW were ok...
	My mom's accident was an offset, someone mis-judged and
she was hit while trying to make a left turn across traffic. Because
of the difference in height and the shape of the sheet-metal, I think
busses, even older busses, are safer than one might at first think. 
	Think of it as the Patton or Red Army theory of energy absorbtion:
You use the OTHER CAR'S crush zone!!!
