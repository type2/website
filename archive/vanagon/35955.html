just so we are all clear....
you ARE *opening* the bleed screw before filling (and expecting 
propane to gush out when full)???  the bleed screw must be open prior 
to filling.  

that bleeder is easy to check.  when you know you have some propane 
in the tank (light the stove to check, not assume) just open that 
thumbwheel and see if propane hisses out.  if not, probably dirt has 
packed into the unprotected opening.  with the valve closed, insert a 
dental tool into the end and check for obstructions.  scrape dirt out 
if necessary and open the valve to blow out the pickings.

Manchester supply has rebuild kits avail. for the auto-stop valves.  
they include a few o-rings, a SS intake screen and a miniscule amount 
of grease.  the kits are a whopping $4 or something and did fix my 
auto-stop from a leaker to a smiler  :)
