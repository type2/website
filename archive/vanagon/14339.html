In response to a dimininshing number of requests...

A Form Factor 41,42 or group 41,42  battery shape is the only widely available
battery that will fit easily behind the driver seat in the secret spider well.

The 42 needs ~10" in the long dimension for installation, the 41 needs 11 3/4".

The 41 will have more capacity for the same grade of battery.

Delco does not currently make a thick plate (deep discharge) cell config. in 
this form factor.

Considerations:  An inexpensive auto starting battery will give you ~100 
minutes _at_20A.  A delco group 41-60 lead calcium will do a bit better than that
and will give more deep discharge cycles than a non calcium technology battery.

Or so sayeth the oracles of lead-calcium-sulphuric acid.

So on a $/use a moderatly priced series 41 seems to be the ticket.

If your westy has a relay with an included diode  wired between alternator blue
and ign switch you can rewire this "refrigerator disconnect" circuit to deal
with the charging issue and isolate the additional battery.

Do remember a 50A FUSE in the hot lead from the new battery.

For those of you with custom battery access and the right FSN you could plop
in a high volume NiCad radio battery and its custom charger at taxpayer
expense, I suppose.  This sort of lash-up will retail in the $750 range and
that's one hell of a long extension cord.
