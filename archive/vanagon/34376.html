>   First, yes, Vanagon diesels are just like a rabbit diesel, most of the
>parts are interchangeable.  They came with a 1.6L and were offer from
>'81-'83. In 83 you could get a vanagon with an air-cooled motor, diesel or
>the new water-cooled flat-4 1.9L engine. Yes, they are slow, the engine
>only produces 48hp. The trans is geared much like a split window bus.  But
>they get great fuel mileage, average about 30mpg. Most parts are still
>available, and are not to expensive.
>    When I bought my Westy diesel the engine was shot. The bearings had
>worn out do to antifreeze in the oil and lack of oil pressure.  I had the
>choice of either rebuilding the diesel, with new crank and rods to match,
>or replaced the diesel with a used gas rabbit motor.  I went with the gas
>motor. It was cheaper, and at the time I didn't have a lot of time to do a
>complete overhaul. Besides, I heard that it could be done and I'm a sucker
>for trying new things. So far the motor has been great. Right now I having
>problems with the fuel injection. But baring that I think that the gas
>motor has been a good choice. Anyway, the diesel, is a good motor, maybe
>slow but it's reliablity and relatively cheap to maintain makes it a good
>choice in my opinon.  If you do decide to get a diesel vanagon you could
>always do a gas transplant. In line-4 water cooled motors are quite
>plentiful in the salvage yards. I hope this answers your questions.  Good
>luck finding one.
