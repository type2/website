On  our '90 Westy I've got 6" Boston Acoustics in the front (approximately
the same position of the original ones), and 5" Boston Acoustics in the
rear, and sounds pretty good, with quite good base and very crisp sound.
 You don't need to cut any metal to put the front ones in, but you do need
to study the existing hole carefully.   The speaker is partly under the
edge of the dash when the door is closed, but I picked speakers that don't
protrude much.   You do have to bend the handles up about 3/8", which you
can do easily in a vise (need to bend  the clearance in and then correct
the bend to have the knob at the right angle).   I've put 6" speakers in
both our vans and never had a handle break.  Great sound.

In the rear on the Westy (with A/C) I  found that there are large openings
on the side that allow sound to escape (especially on the passenger side),
and hence degrade the bass response.   I sealed these with lead flashing
applied fron the inside with double sided foam tape (and stuffed stiff foam
into the holes where the A/C plumbing comes out on the driver's side).
Made a MAJOR improvement in the bass .

Dave
