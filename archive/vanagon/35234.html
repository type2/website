Been there and done it on my 73.  Had a general dribble leak coming from the 
gas tank comparment somewhere when I got the van.  Removed the fire wall at the 
front of the engine comparment and found a rubber elbow conecting up to the 
overflow tube of the fuel filler had rotted out.  Even had a part number on 
it and surprise the dealer actually had one in stock.  Sticks out the rear 
upper face of the tank and is easy to fix and see once the firewall is out.  
To take the firewall out you should know that there are 2 screws that you get 
at from underneath the car on either side of the transaxle on the lip that hold 
the bottom edge of the fire wall in.  I thought it was worth fixing since the 
transaxle is magnessium and I don't like dribbling fuel on it, especially with 
the starter right there (ie source of sparks).
