I was in England and Europe in 1971, back when "bigger was better"
in America. The other side of the pond was a surprise-- a VW bug
was one of the larger cars you could find there!

I saw a few different varieties of three-wheeled cars-- some with
one front and two rear; some with two front and one rear. Smaller
was obviously better in Europe/England, since gas cost about $1.90
per gallon while it was $.30/gallon here (remember those days?).

There weren't a great number of three-wheelers there. OTOH, they
weren't considered unusual-- just small.

My $.02.
