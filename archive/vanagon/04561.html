I'm re-transmitting ...

I have the original Goodyear tires on the EV. I have approx 28,200 miles 
on them and I try to rotate as near to 5,000 miles as possible.

The last trip was about 6,500 miles. I rotated the tires just before the 
trip.

The term "cupping" was exactly what the service person at the VW dealer 
used to describe what was happening.

Not being even a "backyard" mechanic, I did not know what the term meant 
nor how significant it is.

In layman's (uh, layperson's, sorry Martha) terms ...

There is a very obvious (can be seen and felt with the hand) uneven wear 
all over the road-surface of the tires. There are surface areas where the 
tread depth is much less than in a segment immediately preceeding or 
following in the same linear path.  Adjacent treads show a similar 
pattern but not in any consistent arrangement. There are small cracks 
where the sidewalls join the tread.

When I rotated the tires, the ones from the back (now on the front) 
really made a difference in driving and handling. I had the tires replace 
like they were on the trip.
