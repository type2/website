Derek wrote:  
My alternator (the 90 amp model that comes with mid to late model Vanagons)
puts out about 13.9 volts. A few months ago, you wrote to me email saying
that this voltage seemed too low and that it ought to be higher. But I
checked Bentley and it specifies that the regulator is working properly if
it is operating anywhere from 13.5 to 14.5 volts. 

I would love to turn the voltage up higher, especially since I am running a
marine battery as one of my batteries. These marine batteries require higher
voltage than normal automotive batteries to charge properly so I have been
advised I will never be able to charge them enough. 
============================
I have a Bosch alternator (in a non-VW) that was not putting out a high enough
voltage to keep a Delco Freedom battery (the kind with the green dot built in
charge indicator) charged according to the "green eye".  The alternator tested
fine, but the output voltage was kinda low, around 13 volts if I remember, so
I was able to replace the stock regulator (that mounts on the alternator case
and carries the brushes) with an adjustable model.  This has a little hole for
a screwdriver to adjust the output voltage setting.  Got it an a
starter/alternator repair shop.  I set the output voltage for around 14.3 with
a load on it and the battery charged up nicely and stayed that way over time.
