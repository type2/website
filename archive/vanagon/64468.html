This was recommended by the repair shop that fixed my fridge.  They said

>Regarding the lighting of fridges at high altitude, here's what I've done
>(live in Fort Collins, CO, ~5280', with regular trips much higher), on our
>81 Westy.  Those of you with significantly different systems may not be
>able to use this advice.
>
>Turn the toggle to gas, turn gas ON and set the thermostat at it's lowest
>setting.  I believe this to be in the pilot light range as there is a
>detent when you come off this lowest setting which I believe to be the
>main burner enable switch.
>
>With the thermostat at it's lowest setting, go through the normal routine
>to light the fridge (10 pumps, hold in safety relay, fire ignitor,
>repeat).  Leave the fridge on, but at it's lowest setting for 20-30
>minutes, at which point you can move the thermostat to a colder setting.
>
>This fridge has always been hard to start, since purchased from the PO.  I
>was planning on pulling the fridge to check the burner, etc, when I
>stumbled across this technique, which so far has worked as high as 8500'
>feet.  YMMV, of course, but if it works for you, let the list know.
