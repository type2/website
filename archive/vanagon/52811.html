I'll second this.  Anybody else find this true, too?  My singlecab was so
stiff in the suspension that it would only really handle well with about

Not so funny about big loads -- or loads at all:

I had a friend, a Park Ranger in Yosemite, who was killed driving his bus
from Yosemite to Fresno.  He was returning a VW core engine, which he was 
carrying in the back of the bus.  He went around a curve, and found the road
blocked.  Braking hard, the loose engine took flight, and removed the back of
his head.  Moral: secure the load.  VW did not provide factory tie-downs worth
crap on the trucks, and nothing in the Kombis and panels to speak of.  I have
seen none with "headache" panels -- I got mine from a Dodge van.  I suspect
we've all carried something heavy enough to kill us loose in the back.  Think
about getting whacked by a connecting rod, even.

Loads are dangerous -- secure them.  Well maybe not 50 yards of sod.
