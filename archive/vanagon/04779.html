This happened to me a while back. (Way back music begins to play...)
After getting real greasy and messed up squirming around on the ground
under the car, examining linkages and estimating how long it would take
me to drop the engine and trans, I discovered the real problem.

The shifter bracket under the gearshift up front had come a little
loose and drifted to the right about 1/16' of an inch, preventing me from getting
the extra leverage to make it into reverse. 2 minutes with a 13 mm wrench
fixed that problem. Make sure you line up the two small holes when
clamping it down. I still had to fiddle with it for a couple of days
to get the shifting real smooth, as 1/64 of movement in the plate can 
make the difference between an easy shift and a hard one. Again, it is just
under the rubber shift boot at the bottom of the stick.

Good luck.
