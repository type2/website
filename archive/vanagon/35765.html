>I want to share with the list my correspondence with VWOA 
>about the deficient heating/defrosting systems in my new 
>Eurovan Winnebago camper.
>

>
>My letter to VW ...
>
>
>Dear Sir/Madam:
>
>re: Defrosting/Heating System Eurovan Winnebago VIN 
>WV2ZB0252JH048419
>
>We are writing to express our dissatisfaction with the 
>defrosting and heating systems in our new Eurovan Camper.  
>The systems are so deficient in the Canadian climate that we 
>believe that they constitute a safety hazard.  The vehicle 
>has been evaluated by OWASCO, and the Service Department 
>there has confirmed that the defrosting and heating meet the 
>factory specification.  Accordingly, we are writing to you.
>
>In our experience, despite careful attention to the 
>directions in the Owner's Manual (pp. 78 - 93), it is 
>impossible to demist the windows when the ambient 
>temperature is -10ºC or less.  In more severe weather such 
>as -20ºC, the inside surfaces of the side windows are 
>actually covered in a layer of ice. When outside 
>temperatures are -20ºC temperatures at foot level, even in 
>the front, are as low as 0ºC.  Any passengers in the rear 
>seat are typically wearing a sleeping bag – this in a 1995 
>vehicle!
>The driver is armed with a windshield scraper.
>
>We note that, unlike the non-camper Eurovan, there is no 
>rear auxiliary heat exchanger or fan,  nor is there a floor 
>mounted air outlet.  (We anticipate that the air 
>conditioning in the camper will be as deficient as the 
>heating.)  Furthermore, the outlets shown at the base of the 
>windshield in the graphic on Page 84 of the owner's manual 
>are missing.  
>
>Our primary concern is safety.  Being unable to clear 
>windows is very dangerous.  Our second concern is comfort.  
>No one should have to huddle for warmth in a vehicle costing 
>in excess of $40,000.
>
>We believe that Volkswagen's cold weather testing of this 
>vehicle was seriously flawed.  A van of this size clearly 
>requires an auxiliary heat exchanger.  We would like your 
>response to our comments as soon as possible.  
>
>
>Yours sincerely, 
>
>Dan Herbert
>
>P.S.  Our two previous vehicles were VW Westfalias.  The 
>1988 Wasserboxer had a terrific heating system (and leaky 
>head gaskets).
>
>
