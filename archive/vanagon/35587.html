 F.Y.I.   About Isetta cars

In particular for Martha (Busgirl), and  Sam (Sglw_at_aol_dot_com) and others
interested in Isettas --

Somewhere I recall hearing that early Isetta's did not have a reverse gear at
all, and not until later did one become available.

Any of you intested in Isetta basic info, the Autust'95 issue of the BMW Car
Club magazine , "Roundel" has a series of articles about the Isetta in
conjunction with it's 40th birthday.  There a quite a few pictures as well,
including a full color glossy 3/4 (right front fender) shot of a bright red
one.  

Apparently, the roots of the Isetta predate BMWs production, going back prior
to 5 March, '55. "Iso Automotovielto SpA in Milan designed, built, and marked
the original Isetta, or little Lso.  Iso, owned by Renzo Rivolta and son Piero
was a major motorscooter manufacturer with models like the Isoscooter,
Isomoto, and Iso Diva.  the company also produced Isothermos refrigerators. 
Could this have been the inspiration for the front door design?"

To be certain to have some VW content, compared to 36 hp VWs, etc - ..."the
Iso Isetta used a 236cc, two cylinder, two stroke 8 horsepower engine." " BMW
Isettas used an R25 morotrcycle-based , four stroke, single cylinder..."

Any one interested can contact me, and if not too many of you are interested,
maybe I can copy and mail or fax some of this .
