I take issue with the following statement from WHellmer_at_aol_dot_com:

> the change in magnetic strength in your alternator
> does not dissipate horsepower from the engine or produce 
> signifigant additional "drag" as some seem to believe. it is 
> important to understand how a bosch alternator functions in 
> order to further explain.

The force on a current carrying wire in a magnetic field is proportional
to both the magnetic field and the current.  I.e. you increase the torque
of the alternator by drawing more current or by increasing the field.  Adding
a load to the alternator increases both.  When the current draw is higher
you need more force to push the wire through the field and this force must
come from the engine, slowing it down.

your stereo.  You do agree that the energy has to come from somewhere, right?
And, since the alternator isn't all that efficient, it probably is more 
like 1.5 or 2 hp.
