The Clone (my suicidal orange '75 Westy) had this on the dash, and my '64 camper
came with one above the wardrobe that has a neat little flap to cover it to
avoid curious fingers and errant screwdrivers.  The nearest I can figure, since
the air pump that came with The Clone fits this socket very nicely, is that the
engineers in the Vaterland figured that a cigarette lighter (which was not
standard equipment on my '64, and I can't remember if The Clone had one or not)
was not the optimal design for an accessory 12V outlet, and thusly designed this
little socket for that purpose.  When I was in Deutschland (sp?) a few years ago
visiting the in-laws, I had them take me to a VW dealer in an attempt to obtain
plugs to fit these sockets.  We were referred to an electrical supply house,
where I picked up a couple of the plugs and a spare socket so that I could
attach this plug to any accessory I might decide to use in my camper.
