Subject: Loose bearing in my head!(f)

I spent the Memorial Day weekend tweeking the '57 single cab. I have to keep
it going as a daily wifey driver until we complete the '67 Westy Resto from
He**. Last week I participated in the eternal exhaust manifold 10mm clamp
loosening ritual. Found that I had the header shoved too far forward on the
heater box so I was trying to tighten it up on the narrow part closest to
the box. Backed it off and solved the leak. Cool!
Last Tuesday evening I drive up to the house and get a warm glowing feeling
inside as I approach the Singlecab thinking " Boy I'm glad it's running
great. At least one honey do chore that I can scratch from the list." Maybe
it was just the 7-11 coffee. Just inside the door my beloved wife mentions
in her best Marge Simpson voice (as I heard it) "Ya know Rusty, there's a
new knocking kinda noise coming from the truck." Grrrrrmph! So I fire up the
truck and hope that I didn't burn a valve or $omething major. Whew, "only"
sounds like the alternator is loosing a bearing or about to throw the
doghouse fan! Next step is to adapt the wife's short commute to the job.
Drop her off/pick her up until the holiday weekend.
Weekend comes and I spend part of Saturday yanking the, fuel pump, alterator
stand, alternator, backing plates and attached fan. A little end play so I
grab a UFO alternator off the shelf and check the watch. It's into the four
oclock hour so I begin a new episode of play "beat the clock" and drive it
20 miles to the parts joint. The guys are even looking like they are half
way out of the door as I arrive. Success in conning them into testing the
UFO and removing the huge nut on the rear of the "bad" alternator assembly.
Five oclock, door's locked, money is counted and I'm outta there. 20 miles
back home. 
Installation completed early Sunday morning. I stand silently feeling proud
of myself. Gene Berg's guardian Angel lays his hand on my shoulder. I hear
the royal trumpets from the scene in Ben-Hur when the chariots first entered
the Coliseum. Fire up the truck... Arrrrgghh! the same noise exists!!! Next
step logic. No wait, cigarette then logic. (I know, I know but get off my
back would ya?). OK logic. Isolate the noise. 
Laying down on the driveway I "look" for the noise. What do I see? Next step
stand up and bend the apron away from the header that it's banging against,
clean up the tools and have a nice day. Duh!  Mental note: add it to the
list and swear never to tell anybody.
