dan;
How are your wiring skills? A couple ideas come to mind. How about a
relay to disconnect the charger from the battery? Two things to keep in
mind, the contacts must be rated greater than the charger output. I
would go with twice the current rating. Second the  coil voltage . I
would use 110Vac and hook it up in parallel with the input to the
chargers transformer. That way when there is power to the charger the
coil will be energized and its contacts closed connecting the charger to
the battery. when the charger is off the contacts would be open and NO
battery drainage would result.If something went wrong with the circuit
hopefully the chargers fuse would blow also disconnecting the relay.
