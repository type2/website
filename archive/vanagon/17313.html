Al Knoll reminds us that miscegenation (aircooled engine in watercooled body
or vice versa) means you get no heat.  I like the idea of a six-cylinder
Synchro, but I think aircooled upgrades to aircooled bodies, waterpumper
upgrades to waterpumper bodies, is a good rule of thumb.  In this connection
note that the Porsche engine, with good heater boxes, makes *great* heat.
Maybe bus and early Vgon people could use this in justifying the conversion
to skeptical spouses.
