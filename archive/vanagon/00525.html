How about running an air conditioner but with the condenser in the cabin
(instead of outside) and the evaporator (which is normally inside) in the
engine compartment.  Essentially, you have a heat pump here.  Heat pumps work
great anywhere that the temperature (outside) is above 40-45 degrees.  What
you are doing - for those who don't understand an what a heat pump is - is
trying to cool down the outside (helps on global warming ;-) ) and trasferring
that heat inside.  Think of putting that home window A/C in the window
backwards.  A/Cs don't create cold - they just transfer heat from one place
to the other.  Reverse the direction of the transfer.  I would guess that a 
VW A/C unit would probably be rated at about 20KBTU (average for a midside
american car,  van is much bigger that that).  Run in reverse, you would get
about 20KBTU of heat in the car - about the same as a 4000 watt 
(333 amp _at_ 12 volts) electric resistance heater.  By putting the evaporator
in the engine compartment, you should have temperatures above 40 degrees even
in the dead of winter.
